> I won't be going. I don't feel the same this year Ian.

Brian,

I used to think a bit the same way - I perceived Anzac Day as too much 
glorification or celebration of a tragic event, that symbolises all the 
horror of war.

But its not. Its a celebration of the courage of all those who 
sacrificed for something they believed in, and a poignant reminder to us 
all that we must never let be that sacrifice be diminshed or forgotten.

The fact that wars continue is undoubted, but the fact so many revile 
it, question it, and protest it, is a direct tribute to the men and 
women who died during war so that we had the right, the freedom, and 
ability to protest, think, debate, and try to stop war.

My girls go to the dawn service every year, and this is what I have 
explained to them its for. Its not a public holiday, its a day to 
remember, be grateful, thankful to those who sacrificed.

Lest we forget.
