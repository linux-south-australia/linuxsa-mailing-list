Re: masquerading spam

Ben Kramer <ben@voicetronix.com.au> wrote:
> I have just received a lot of rejected email for our domain over night
> and the returned mail is spam! It looks like somebody is using our
> domain name and pretending to be us while spamming through an open
> relay.

I think what you are seeing is a new way of getting past anti-spam
measures.  Send a message with a forged sender's address to almost any
site and, assuming the mailbox you specified in the to address doesn't
exist, that site will forward the email, as a rejection notice, to
your target.  Naturally, when the person who's address was forged sees
a rejection notice, they'll open the returned email to see who and what
they sent, and thus they'll view the spam.

Et voilla, you can use any site as a relay, and get past the target's
psychological spam blocks, too!  Whoever thought of that is some kind
of a genius.... unfortunately.
