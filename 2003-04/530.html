"Crunchy exterior with a soft chewy centre".

It's interesting to note that there has been incidents of one of the
(many) IIS worms spreading when a person caught the worm while at home
connected to the Internet, suspended the laptop then resumed it while
plugged into the "protected" network.

Several places I've been working at recently are reluctant to install
VPNs into corporate LANs over ADSL connections for home users for
similar reasons - the home PC is barely protected, and if compromised
can completely sidestep the firewall.
