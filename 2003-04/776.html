The quickest and dirtiest way to do this would be to:

grep -10000 username@domain.com /dev/hda2 > possiblemailbox

Change /dev/hda2 to whatever partition the mail spool is on, the grep 
-10000 means get 10000 lines before or after a user's email address 
(presumably in the To: field of a deleted message). 'possiblemailbox' 
will then be a easier target for finding lost data than grepping a whole 
partition every time, although you might need more than 10000 lines if 
you're trying to recover mail attachments.

If this is a busy mail server, you might want to research what the 
effects of running such a command are before doing it.
