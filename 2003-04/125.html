Re: strange nforce2 and mandrake 9.1 problems

Hmm..sounds like you've already made up your mind as to what it is, so
why ask ?

Re-assurance & agreeance from other parties perhaps ?

Anyway, you could try 'renice' & 'nice' to give your burning process
lower cpu priority & your program that is queuing a higher priority.
The queued program will open but just stand back while those toasted
coasters fly.

Btw, having a shit hot CPU doesn't mean jack if the motherboard handles
the data poorly. The same CPU on different motherboards will behave very
differently.

Having said that, it may also be a misconfiguration in the kernel that
could be causing a performance hit. Note that the nForce2's IDE
controller on linux, works in compatibility mode by default (no DMA) but
needs a kernel patch for nearly full performance. This will give you one
massive performance boost. Check your current HD speeds with a
'hdparm -Tt /dev/hd(X)' - X being your hard drive letter.

Then apply the nforce2 patch available here ->
http://home.t-online.de/home/Johannes.Deisenhofer/nforce2ide.diff

Re-compile your kernel, boot into it & then compare the previous speeds
with another 'hdparm -Tt /dev/hd(X)'

Currently the patch will only take you up to UDMA-100. The chipset
supports up to UDMA-133, and this is being worked on apparently.

Good luck,
Regards,
Rick
