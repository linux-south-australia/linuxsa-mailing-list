Ryan,

> What exactly does RH9 do that RH8 doesn't?

As per the URLs others have posted reviews on the difference, a fair bit is under the cover so to speak.

> Screenshots I've seen of RH9 look just like RH8 - Gnome 2.2 
> with bluecurve.

Yes bluecurve is default whether you choose Gnome or KDE, however this is easily changed :-) 

So far my impressions of Redhat 9 are...

As a server it seems rock stable as ever, a few little things have changed. Although this is only testing, production is always the key, users can do strange things to a server!! 

As a graphical desktop machine it's not too bad, the first thing I did was to get rid of bluecurve. The novelty has worn off since Redhat 8 :-) I still find some frustrations with the default layout of the GUI.

As a drop in Windows replacement I still think Mandrake 9.1 is streets ahead at present. Especially in machine configuration, mounting network drives, installing peripherals etc etc. Maybe that's just me :-)
