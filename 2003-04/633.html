I like beaches, 
the view,
the water, 
the sand,
any beach will do,
any beach is grand.

I like to dig,
to build, 
to mould the sand,
to be able to make a masterpiece,
with my own hands.

You can build a structure,
You can sculpt a scene,
And then, with one wave,
It is gone, washed clean.

The next day dawns,
No evidence remains,
You get your bucket,
You get your spade,
You start to dig,
You build again.

I love the freedom,
To do all this,
But most of all,
the best thing is,
our beaches are safe,
for visiting.

There's no barbed wire,
between me and the sea,
no landmines hidden,
preventing digging.

No soldiers firing,
requiring me to duck,
no blood spilling,
turning all into red muck.

No young men hiding,
Hugging the dunes,
dodging bullets,
praying it all ends soon.

No terror,
No horror,
No screams,
No lives ending.

Did we forget,
Or don't we really care,
Why on April 25th we stop,
Why we pause in prayer.

Is it because,
So many years ago,
When the next day dawned,
The waves came in,
wiped clean the bloody beach,
Carried the bodies away,
Removed the evidence
Of that horrible day.

Just like when we build,
Our small sandy art,
To watch them disappear,
in the next day's tide.

Is this why,
On April 25th,
We forget to stop,
or remember,
other beaches,
far away in time and place.

On the beach where I go,
The sun shines down brightly,
The breeze blows gently,
The children play,
And I thank God,
For this every day.
Lest we forget,
And the beaches we know,
Become ANZAC beaches themselves.
