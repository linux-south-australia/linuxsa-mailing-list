I think you may just have refuted your own argument -- you can't name
one platform that is perfect. Each platform has its' own strengths and
weaknesses, and the diversity is a Good Thing. To rephrase your own
words, one could easily say that:

---
... the best that Microsoft and Intel and a cast of two or three others
could do was to create an environment that was insecure and unstable,
quite lacking in inter-operability, locked the opposition out of "their
own" customers, and generally kept computing in the dark ages.
---

Yes, there would be advantages in homegenity (sp?), and in the ideal
world, our IT infrastructure may well be homogenous, but in the
meantime, we have to deal with the imperfections that the real world
introduces, and that means that diversity is an asset worth fighting
for.
