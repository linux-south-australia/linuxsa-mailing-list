[OT] ANZAC Day, April 25th, Lest We Forget

ANZAC Day

 On April 25th please take a moment out of your day to stand
 alongside us as we remember those Australians and New
 Zealanders who gave their lives in the First World War and
 in subsequent wars so we could live in freedom.
 [ ANZAC = Australia New Zealand Army Corps ]

http://www.defence.gov.au/army/
Select the item "Traditions" from the menu to the left of the page:
These items can then be retrieved.

 History of the Dawn Service
 History of the Last Post
 History of Reveille or Rouse
 Why do we have 2 Minutes Silence?
 History of the Ode of Remembrance
 Origins of Saluting
 History of Remembrance Day
 Why is the Parade Ground sacred?
 Why are Australian Soldiers called "Diggers"?
 Significance of the Red Poppy
 What are the soldiers' ID discs for?
 Full text of the poem "For the Fallen"

 The Returned Services League has taken their Ode of
 Remembrance from the 4th stanza of
 "For the Fallen" by Laurence Binyon (1869-1943) 

"They shall grow not old, as we that are left grow old;
Age shall not weary them, nor the years condemn.
At the going down of the sun and in the morning
We will remember them."


 Also visit this site, http://eternity.medelle.com/sun.html
 for a poem by a young person, "On war and the Anzacs,
 written after a visit to the Australian War Memorial."

LEST WE FORGET
