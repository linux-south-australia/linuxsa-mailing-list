postfix vs sendmail solution

Hi.

Well, after trawling the help and google groups, I think I have the
answers. We'll find out for sure when it goes live in a week or so.

The mapping is handled quite well with canonical, so that:

user1@our.domain	username@remotehost.our.domain
user2@our.domain	othername@otherhost.our.domain

and:

mis-spelled.name@our.domain	othername@otherhost.our.domain 

(handles people who repeatedly stuff up someone's address)

Works well in concert with a transport map:

remotehost.our.domain	smtp:[192.168.3.6]
otherhost.our.domain	smtp:[192.168.4.5]
etc.

and 

masquerade_domains = our.domain

Hides all the internals in the visible outgoing mails.

We catch dud email addresses rather than bounce them, and luser_relay
will forward them to the dead letter bin.

I still have to check, configure and test that relaying is denied from
all except our internal hosts. After that I just need to build a new box
to run it. (the old one is sendmail/uucp on Redhat 5.1, and it's sooo
tired)

Thanks for everyone's suggestions.
