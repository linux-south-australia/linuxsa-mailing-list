Re: Honeypots get stickier??

David,

I don't get your first paragraph at all... You seem to say firewalls can 
make people complacent and therefore firewalls are crap.  This is not a 
problem with firewalls, it's a problem with ill informed admins.

Not everyone can/needs to do everything to protect their network.  A 
firewall is (normally) the cheapest and easiest way to start protecting 
a network. 

Having some security is better than none at all.  Knowing what level of 
security you have is very important. 

Mikey

>
>I tend to agree with Stephen about firewalls.  I have a couple of
>issues with firewalls, and perhaps the most important is that you do
>not need one if the machines behind it are secure.  What firewalls
>can do is give you the sense that your local machines don't need to be
>secure because the firewall gives you total security.  That is quite
>wrong.  What firewalls can do is give you a false sense of security.
>They can make you complacent.  False security is worse than no security.
>Firewalls also do get in the way; that's their purpose.  Many people,
>when the firewall gets in the way, simply poke a hole through it without
>really thinking about the ramifications.  Perhaps they find they can't
>send mail from their favoured email client, so they open up the SMTP port.
>Lookout!  Who knows what buffer overflow is there to be exploited.
>
>I think there is no substitute for locking down each and every machine.
>And if each machine is locked down, a firewall really adds very little.
>It presents, perhaps, an additional obstacle for the hacker to circumvent,
>but as I already said, that comes at the cost of an obstacle to the
>people it is supposed to protect.
>
>One thing that a firewall can do quite well is enforce certain global
>security policies on a network.  I've used them to ensure that all email
>goes through a central email host; which can be used to log incoming and
>outgoing email, and to rewrite addresses so they conform to organisational
>standards.  I've also used them to ensure that intrinsically insecure
>protocols, such as telnet or ftp, are not used; that stops passwords
>being sent in the clear.
>
