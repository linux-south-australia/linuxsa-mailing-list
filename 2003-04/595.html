Re: uptime = 1 day, Grrrrr....

On 2003.04.20 15:21 Alan Kennington wrote:
> For the last 500 days, I've been taking pleasure in the fact that my
> UPS protects me from the vagaries of the Adelaide electricity
> situation, and I've been watching my graph at Netcraft UK
> steadily climbing towards the 500 day mark.
<snip>
> But now I check my uptime expecting to have gone over 500 days, and
> find this:
>   2:38pm  up 1 day, 13:11, 32 users,  load average: 0.06, 0.03, 0.01
> 
> Grrrr and double-Grrrr!
> 
> What is the point of a UPS if linux is just going to reset the uptime
> after 497 days?
> Who's bright idea was it to make it impossible to boast a 500+ day
> uptime?

I think you will find that after 497 days the kernel counter runs out 
of digits so it starts all over again -- just like the odometer of your 
car.

Even worse, when playing around with the 2.5 kernel the counter was 
accelerated by an order of magnitude, so it recycles after 49.7 days. 
This now officially makes the linux 2.5 kernel as reliable as Win NT:-(

I have no doubt the the relevant register will be expanded by the time 
2.6 hits the streets -- then your system can run until Hell freezes 
over.
