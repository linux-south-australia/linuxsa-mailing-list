Re: Honeypots get stickier??

Glen Turner wrote:

> What annoys me is the shoddy performance of the e-mail
> virus scanners.  Almost all computer science courses
> teach state machines.  Anyone can write a program to
> merge the state machines for MIME, dearchiving, decompression
> and virus scanning.

That would be one honking large DFA.  And the virus scanning
state machine would be under a constant state of flux.  What
about a chain of state machines, with the output of, say,
the MIME encoding FSM feeding the dearchiver FSM if required?

I see part of the problem as what actually constitutes a
virus - Microsoft mail readers and web browsers (and other
3rd party programs that use their components) have a great
track record of mis-interpreting MIME types (and therefore
the data it contains), and now it's lead to a state of the
nation where I can't even mention
X5O!P%@AP[4\PZX54(P^)7CC)7}$EICAR-STANDARD-ANTIVIRUS-TEST-FILE!$H+H*
in a plain text message without incurring the wrath of
dozens of antivirus products [1] (although I must admit, it's
hard to work that word into everyday conversation).

> That merge would allow a single-pass
> virus scan of e-mail with no disk I/O (ie: scan the mail
> as you read it from the SMTP session).

It's an interesting idea ...


[1]  Which I fully expect to happen with this.
