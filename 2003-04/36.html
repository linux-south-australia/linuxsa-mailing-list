RE: RealTeks

Personally, I think they are just variable -- sometimes you get a good
one, sometimes it just degrades over time, sometimes it is slow,
sometimes it doesn't work at all, sometimes it breaks rapidly, sometime
it won't work with a specific switch, and so on. That said, I have a few
that have worked fine for years with a variety of hardware. I've also
had a few that have pissed me off no end.

Of course, I've also had problems with Broadcom (as the 4401 doesn't
have a commonly available driver), intel (as they seem to change a fair
bit each iteration of the EE100Pro, and at least under Solaris x86, can
be a PITA to make work), 3Com (mainly due to difficulty finding a driver
once), NetGear (it just died, though I think this is unusual), and
various laptop cars (drivers). I guess RealTek, for me, have a
higher-than-average failure rate... and various untracable errors seem
to just go away when the card is replaced.
