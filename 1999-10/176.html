> I wish to extend my Linux disk capacity by 'joining' another disk to it.

> Bye the way - this is one of the major problems in 'converting' from...

If you want 2+ disks to use the same mountpoint (ie /usr) then
multidevices or raid is what your after.

However, If you just want to say make /usr larger by moving it to a new
disk then:

1> partition the new disk to the size you want (fdisk)
2> make an ex2fs on the new disk (mke2fs)
3> mount the new disk mount /dev/newdisk /mnt/tempmountpointyoumade
4> copy everything across remembering permissions 
   (cd olddir;tar cpf - .)|(cd /mnt/testmountpointyoumade;tar xvpf -)
5> setup linux so it uses the new partition as default (pico /etc/fstab)
6> reboot or by some other method get it to use the new drive now.

Does anyone know of a howto/doc to explain this process of shall I write
one?
