Re: Interesting followup from an earlier question

Daryl Tester wrote:

> I'd say probably bad RedHat behaviour in their attempt to be "DOS migration"
> friendly; a bit like aliasing "rm" to "rm -i" in root's profile.

No, vim works like that under Solaris, so it's a vim bug.  And
a horrible one.  Emacs does slighty better if set up to edit
DOS files: at least the "DOS" mode indicator is always visible.

The use of "rm -i" under root is more good practice, rather than
any DOSism (a DOSism would be "alias del rm -i").  People of
sufficient experieience know how to defeat an alias with a single
character.  Others bloody well better think twice about deleting
files as root.

I mean what would they be deleting?  Source code?  No, becasuse
Makefiles should be run as non-root until the "make install".  Word
processor files?  God forbid!  Surely people don't start Netscape as
root unless they are looking for JavaScript bugs?
