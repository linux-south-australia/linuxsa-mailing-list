newbie books

to the people who were asking for more support for 'linux beginners'

i'd suggest 

1.  Linux in 24 hours  -- to get you started (buy this anywhere)

1.1 of course the APC linux pocketbook is a MUST! (newsagents)

2.  Linux in plain english (or unix in plain english) -- for a good reference
to the os. (uni. adelaide bookshop has it)

3.  read the howtos very carefully.  they do seem silly at first, but they are
worth plowing through. (on your cd-roms and the net (linux.org))

4. o'reily books are *the* source for good linux/unix information, they are
more expensive, but cover detail well enough for pros. ;) (dymocks/linuxsa
meetings:)

like someone pointed out, everyone was a beginner once, and we all learned
through a combination of books/howtos and PRACTICE!  (nb: it is hard to find
good answers to questions if you don't do your homework :)

oi!
