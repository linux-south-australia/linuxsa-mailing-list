Dunno really, but you have to multiply that 133 by the number of cylinders
unless you're going to fire off all the injectors at once. (Don't laugh, I
had a Rover that fired half off at a time <when it actually ran, which was
intermittently - I landed up running away from it>)

In between instructing the injectors how and when to fire, the ECM is
going to calculate the ideal ignition timing based on a whole bunch of
inputs: Engine temp, intake air temp, throttle position, various factors
like which gear it's in, etc, and also take into account fuel economy and
exhaust gas emmissions. Most engine bays have got loads of sensors all
over the place...

No mean feat for any system. And it needs to kick over everytime the user
hits the start switch...
