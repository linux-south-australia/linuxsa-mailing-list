Re: Can't start X

As a stab in the dark, X will chuck huge wobblies if it can't access
your temporary directories. The error you stated is one of the symptoms
that this might be the case. Make sure that /tmp and /var/tmp have read,
write and execute permissions for EVERYONE or at least the user you are
logging in as.

Also, does X start when you log as root?
