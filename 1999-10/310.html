Re: Interesting followup from an earlier question

Mark Newton wrote:

> How do you fix it?  Load the file into vi and type:
> 
>      :set fileformat=unix

As you mentioned later in your post, it's probably preferable to
use binary mode, where the carriage-return sticks out like the
proverbials ...

    :set binary

or stick that in your .exrc (or equivalent).

> Ultimately, this is a vi bug.

The vi that ships with RedHat is some trimmed down version of vim,
as they supply both (my vi and vim are substantially different in
size, but I seem to recall that I did recompile vim at some stage
for some added functionality).

> The CR characters *are* in the file, 
> and vi should bloody-well show them.  To bury a "dos mode" annunciation
> away briefly at the bottom of the screen and then let you carry on 
> editing what is essentially a corrupted file without giving you any
> hint about the bogus data it contains (and will continue to contain,
> even though visual inspection suggests otherwise) is bad behaviour.

I'd say probably bad RedHat behaviour in their attempt to be "DOS migration"
friendly; a bit like aliasing "rm" to "rm -i" in root's profile.
