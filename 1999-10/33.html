Bryan:

> How do I FORMAT the new ext2 partition?

Firstly you need to work out what device the new disk is sitting on.
There are a number of ways to do this, the easiest is to go wander round
the /proc directories if it exist. The other way is the "stab in the
dark method" and doing an fdisk /dev/hd[device names} and looking at the
partition table.

Common names are:

/dev/hda[number] - generally primary ide
/dev/hdb[number]- generally the cdrom but not always 
/dev/hdc[number] - generally secondary ide
/dev/sd[number] - I think these are the scsi numbers

Once you've find it and are absolutely certain that it is it, then do
the following:

mkfs /dev/[the device] and sit back and watch.

(Find out about mkfs and its relatives with man mkfs).

I hope this helps.
