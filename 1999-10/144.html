Re: using sed to cleanup a mac text file

Richard Sharpe wrote:

> Hmmm, technically, I would have said that awk is in the same class as Perl :-)
> That is, it is not a shell, and is thus not a shell scripting language.

OK, my fault for not expressing myself eloquently enough.  Shell script
relies on its use of underlying, specialised applications such as awk,
sed, cut, paste, et al for its power, usually by pipelining constructs
together (hence the dataflow methodology I mentioned peviously).  Perl
scripts tend to be self contained, and not used as a part of the whole
picture, but rather the whole picture itself.  I can't remember the
last time someone said "bugger it, let's use sort(1) to order our
output data from this Perl script".

> I think that this is the influence of Henry Spencer's RE stuff.

Larry has added extensively to Perl's RE that goes beyond Henry's,
although Perl's was originally based on it - the zero width assertions,
locales and the non-greedy specifier spring to mind.
