Re: Daylight Savings Time

Richard,

I'm pretty sure that the last time I heard this question
being discussed, the answer was that in Australia we
do _not_ have Ds in out time standard names.
I.e. In Adelaide we have CST for ever and ever.
Eastern time is EST likewise, no matter what the govt
in each state chooses to adjust by in any particular week.

It's the US where they have the preferable system
of putting in a D when they have daylight saving.

Therefore it is correct, but inconvenient, that we keep CST as
the time name all through summer.

On a related point, my sparc station shows EST5EDT and PST8PDT for
eastern and pacific times in the US.
I.e. because only one timezone file has to do both jobs,
they've incorporated both names in so that there is no confusion.
(Well, nearly no confusion....)

So if any of those legacy OSs out there show up with CDT today in
Adelaide, then they are legally wrong!
