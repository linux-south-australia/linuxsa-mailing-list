Hmmm, this may count as pedantry, but it is important to know the device
names used.

For IDE disks, they are pretty much what David has said.  Some
supplimentary info is:

   /dev/hda[number]  - Primary master and partitions
                       hda is whole disk, hda1 is first partition, etc
   /dev/hdb[number]  - Primary slave and partitions
                       hdb is whole disk, hdb1 is first partition, etc
   /dev/hdc[number]  - Secondary master and partitions
   /dev/hdd[number]  - Secondary slave and partitions

Some of these might not be present.

For SCSI the situation is a little more complex.

   /dev/sda[number]  - First SCSI device on first controller
   /dev/sdb[number]  - next SCSI disk
   /dev/sdc[number]  - next SCSI disk

Where next SCSI disk is the next higher one on the same controller, and if
none, then the first one on the next higher controller.

Eg, if you have SCSI0 and SCSI1, being two SCSI controllers, and you have
SCSI ID 2 and 3, being disks on SCSI0, with SCSI ID 3 and 5 on SCSI1, you
will have:

   sda (SCSI0-2), sdb (SCSI0-3), sdc (SCSI1-3), and sdd (SCSI1-5).

The problem with SCSI is that if you add a new drive to SCSI0, say with
ID4, it becomes sdc, and the following drives become sdd and sde.  

This can be a pain.
