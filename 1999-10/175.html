Re: bootdisk how-to

On Sat, 9 Oct 1999, Kevin Waterson wrote:

> I am following the boot disk how to and am having problems
...
> mke2fs -m 0 -i 2000 /tmp/fsfile

> The last command gives me
> cp: cannot create special file `./dev/xda6': No space left on device

> many hundreds of times,
> According to the howto this should be OK using the -R option

Although you are not running out of space you are running out of inodes
(you specified 2000 to mke2fs) You need at least 1 inode per file.

My dev directory is 2269 files.
Since your making only a boot disk just copy across the files you need,
you won't want to loose space by increasing the inode listB:

kmem
id[a-d]
tty[0-9]
ttyS[0-3]
zero
null
fd[0-2]*
ram               
stdin, stdout, stderr
vcs[0-9]

sg* scd* sd*        (if you have scsi)
dsp* mixer* midi*   (if you want sound)
md*                 (if you have raid)
