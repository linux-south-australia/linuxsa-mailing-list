Re: scsi disk doctor

On Thu, 14 Oct 1999, Alan Kennington wrote:

> Where can I get a SCSI disk doctor for linux, please?
> 
> In the old days, when I had a corrupt floppy disk on
> the Atari ST, I had a choice of several "disk doctors" to
> read any sector on the disk, modify any byte, write it back,
> and so forth and so forth.
<SNIP>

I don't know of any specific scsi tools but you could always use dd on the
raw device. ie:

dd if=/dev/sdx of=tmpfile count=63 seek=0

The difference is you are reading bytes not sectors. Modify the data in
what you read then write it back (swap if & of).
