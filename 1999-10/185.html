Can't format ext2 partition with SuSE

On the weekend I had to rebuild a home system due to a hdd about to crash.
After installing W98 on a 2.4 Gb partition, creating a D: partition of about
1 GB, I then proceeded to re-install SuSE 6.2 on the remaining ~3Gb (plus
swap partition).
The process happily creates a swap and formats it but when it attempts to
format the ext2 partition (~3 Gb), it falls over very quickly and returns
saying their was an error formatting the partition. Maybe SuSE has a limit
on partition size but I doubt it.

I attempted to install Redhat 6 APC version and all goes well during the
format of the same partition. That kinda stumped me. I have successfully
installed SuSE from these CDs before so the install media is OK. 

The hdd is a brand new 6.5 Gb Quantum IDE. The only thing I can think of is
low-level formatting the drive and starting again but that involves a lot of
repeat pain installing all the Win98 stuff again.

Has anyone got any ideas??
