Yeah....All those inputs would need to be converted from voltage levels
to bytes, and you'd probably need dedicated hardware for that and the
output too...
My car has a plug near the glovebox that apparently does 8192 baud
serial and provides a stream of information about the ECM. Doesn't allow
adjustment of parameters though, because as you say, that's stored on
EPROM. It's a 16k EPROM I think, and I was thinking it would be nice to
replace it with a 32k EPROM and just wire the highest bit of the address
line up to a switch on the dash...And then just toggle between 'economy'
and 'performance' banks of data. (Probably requiring engine to be turned
off inbetween of course.)

However that Haltech thing sounds like an easy way to fiddle with the
running parameters - I've heard that they're relatively easy to fit,
just dropping in basically - but are they expensive?
There's always WINE for emulating windows I guess, and their protocol
might be easy to figure out..We've done it before ,)
