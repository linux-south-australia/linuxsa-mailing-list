>Unfortunately syslogd never lies unlike the author of this message. What
>the hell is a DEC LAN Traffic Monitor?

Digital Equipment Corporation built (or had other build, I am not sure) a
box that was about 3 to 5 RU.

It sat on an Ethernet segment and monitored the traffic and could hand it
off to an analysis device.  It could give you info like the most frequent
talkers, total traffic, etc.

Useful for figuring out what was going on on a segment.

They evolved into RMON packet probes ... Latest I have seen is the RMON
pocket probe.
