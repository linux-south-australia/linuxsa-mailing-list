Hi all..... After reading robyn's message I answered him... I then read
the various replies to Robyn's message and not one mentioned mtools.

Am just curious... am I the only person who knows abt mtools???? :)

With mtools you dont need to mount/umount a dos floppy...you just stick
it in the drive & type mdir to see the directory.
You basically use DOS syntax with an "m" in front of the command (ie/
the slashes even go the wrong way if you want :)  )

Just type     mtools   at the prompt & all will be revealed.   man
mtools works too!  

I actually cant remember if it does "vfat" but certainly it will
"squiggle~1" the files so they dont overwrite each other & you can
always specify a destination name anyway...certainly it is simpler than
endlessly mounting/umounting floppies!!

If its not in your linux distro...  
ftp://www.tux.org/pub/knaff/mtools/  is where you can find various
versions of mtools.
