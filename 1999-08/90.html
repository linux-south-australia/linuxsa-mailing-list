Re: "man" function Question ??

Anders Wong wrote:

>>  When I was using redhat 6.0 as root, I had tried to use " man some
>> command" in the terminal. eg. man tar,  etc. I got the No manual Entry for
>> tar. However, it works under user. Does anyone know the problem? Thanks

Rob Wuttke replied:

> The only thing I can think of is your MANPATH environment variable is
> incorrect
> 
> do
> 
> printenv MANPATH (may need $MANPATH)
> 
> Do this foor root and as a user and fix any discrepancies. In bash, look at
> .bash_profile or .profile.

Even better - turf MANPATH, and edit /etc/man.config to reflect the
man paths on your system, then _everyone_ can get at 'em and you don't
have to maintain environment variables across x different shells.

A quick experiment shows:

[dt@orac dt]$ MANPATH=/dev man tar
No manual entry for tar

[dt@orac dt]$ MANPATH= man tar
  [A wealth of knowledge deleted ...]

that if your MANPATH is screwed (as opposed to non-existant, valueless),
you'll get this sort of result.
