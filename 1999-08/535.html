On Fri, Aug 27, 1999 at 05:24:05PM -0600, Michael Wardle wrote:

> I only have one physical disk drive which is an UATA/33 (IDE) with my swap 
> partitions at the "end" of the disk, so that the swap may be as fast as 
> possible.

You may want to check that you're accessing this hard drive with 32-bit access
routines and DMA; from memory, Linux leaves both these settings off by default
since some particular drive and controller combinations have trouble with
them. If you're using fairly standard hardware, it'll be fine. There's a
program called hdparm that comes with most newish distributions that lets you
tune disk access and switch these on (use the -d and -c switches, and the -k
switch to make the setting persistent.) Read the man page first, though. :)
You should see a sizeable speed improvement with your ultradma drive.
