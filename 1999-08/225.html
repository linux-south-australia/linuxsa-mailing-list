Re: Floppy Drives

Robyn Manning wrote:

 > To read a floppy disk I have to type in mount -t vfat /dev/fd0 /mnt/floppy
 > the problem is that I have to unmount and mount again every time I change
 > floppies. A similar problem with the cdrom, I can't open it without umount
 > first.

Yup, that's just the way UNIX works -- No problem there at all.

Incidentally, if you don't do your "umount" command *BEFORE* ejecting
a floppy, you could end up corrupting the floppy that was originally 
inserted AND the one in the drive at the time that you issue the umount
command.
