Something that the linux community doesn't seem to have acknowledged yet is
that the general user of computers, not computer experts, but those who use
them as tools for business (outside of programing, I might add) don't give a
damn about operating systems.  They don't care so long as it is easy to use
and ms know this and probably place the highest  priority on this point.
Lets face it, we don't hear screams of anguish from the general computer
community so ms isn't in trouble yet.  I don't mean this to be ms biased.
What is discouraging to me is the blinked view in the linux community.  It
could be damaging to linux in what I now think is a critical stage in its'
development.  It is good but needs polishing which is the most difficult
stage.  It takes a long time and is the phase where it would receive the most
criticism.
  I would like to see if we can promote creative discussion among this users
group and come up with ideas that would be worth suggesting as a project or
presenting to linux international.  There must be very wide views and ideas
among this group and the least experienced users may even have the best of
them. ;-)
