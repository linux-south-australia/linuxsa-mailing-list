Re: PPP and compression and 56kbps modems

Yes - From my experience I have found this quite true..

Most.. Nay.. All modems with either a Kflex or v.90 chipset (Maybe x2
also) are actually
only 56k recieve, they cannot answer a call at higher then 33.6, so
unless you want to spend
the extra cash on digital modemracks, etc that can do it, grab
yourself some ol' 33.6's for next
to nothing.

*Possibly* this is just a firmware thing, and in the next few months
ITU (or whoever does that v90 thingo) may decide some standard for 56
answer, but I wouldnt hold my breath..

Have the serial port speed set to 115kb - and make sure the init
string is per man (Ive found heaps of modems actually are faster when
you shove in those non standard at commands)
