Maybe winmodem's aren't taking under windows. It would be nice if they
would run under linux. Then at least we would get a true indication of how
cpu hungry they are.

Also what about the fact they have no buffer ram? They use main memory.
Hence if your cpu & ram is very busy (say rendering a huge scene in 3dsmax
whilst you play quake 3) then the modem will be forced to wait for a space
cpu cycle. 

What if I am just on the edge of needing swap space? A few more bytes come
in from the modem & wham, disk swapping aplenty.

You can try and make them look sweet but the truth remains winmodems are
simply annonying.
