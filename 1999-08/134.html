As I understand it, the reason that it is not possible for a 56k modem to send
at 56k from normal (or POTS) telephone line is that the signal is not converted
from analogue to digital (digitised) until it gets to the telephone exchange.
At the exchange it is too noisy (because of all the other telephone lines) for
the signal to be converted at anything above about 33.6k.  So if you want to
send at 56k the signal must be digitised close to the modem.  i.e. you need to
have a digital phone service like Onramp.
