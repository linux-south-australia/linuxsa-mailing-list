Re: K6-2/400 CPU; MSI mainboard; linux failure?

Hi Alan

There is nothing special about the 400mhz frequency. I've got two
servers running AMD K6-2 400s, and another two running AMD K6-3 400's
as well as plenty of K6's at other frequencies. They all work
flawlessly.

The two K6-2 400's are identical server configs:
 Tekram MVP3 S7 100Mhz board (VIA chipset)
 2 64MB SDRAM PC100 (128 total)
 Advansys PCI Ultra SCSI
 S3 AGP 4MB
 2 Fujustsu 8.4GB IDE in removable canisters
 (both masters one on primary, other on secondary)
 2 10/100 Surecom PCI NICS
 ATX powersupply in rackmount case

In my experience it is no easier or harder to build AMD or INTEL
systems. It is always easier to build your second system than your
first & tends to get easier the more you do. Every now and then though
you strike a troublesome combination - such are the joys of system
building.

Your problem could be lots of things, particularly if this is your first
build. Each board is different but some common build problems are:
 the dual voltage settings for the processor
 the wrong clock frequency combinations, external/multiplier
 not checking the purpose & setting of EVERY jumper on the M/B
 not disabling unwanted onboard devices (bios or jumper)
 the memory voltage and frequency
 memory not in the right slots (not just where they fit!)
 memory not supported by M/B (there are lots of types)
 plugging the floppy cable in back to front
 inadequate anti static/handling precautions
 a dud/marginal component (commonly Memory)
 BIOS settings
 dodgy power supply

Email me off list if you want a practical hand

Regards
Adrian
