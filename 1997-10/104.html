Re: A couple of problems

> 	PATH=".:$PATH"

This is NOT a good idea.  If "." is at the front of your path and
someone else has access to your system, they could create a program
called "ls" in /tmp, which would be executed if you typed:
cd /tmp
ls

Not good.

If anyone really wants to add "." to their PATH, put it at the end,
not the beginning.  Ie.:

PATH="$PATH:."

This doesn't prevent against someone anticipating common typos, such
as typing "l s-la" instead of "ls -la", but it is better.
