Re: is there a FAQ on the BEST MODEM

The only thing I have to say in defence of internal modems (and I'll admit 
it is the _only_ thing) is that most modems are let down by the power 
supplies.

In my experience, unreliable modems are often unreliable because the 
plugpacks they plug into are crap.  Not quite enough juice often leads to 
drop outs etc.  The most reliable modems often seemed to have the better 
power supplies (this is not universally true).

The benefit of an internal modem is that it has a connection to a decent 
power supply.

I used an internal no-name brand Rockwell chipset internal modem for my 
permanent BigPond link for several years.  It _averaged_ 2 weeks per call.

Personally I think modems are like printers (and other paper shredding 
devices such as photocopiers and faxes) are evil.   I still have Netcomm 
Prorack nightmares in my sleep.
