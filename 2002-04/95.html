Re: Finding out specs of remote FreeBSD machine

On Thu, 4 Apr 2002, John Edwards wrote:

> Date: Thu, 04 Apr 2002 18:39:08 +0930
> From: John Edwards <isplist@adam.com.au>
> To: Richard Russell <richard@yellowgoanna.com>,
>      'Linux SA' <linuxsa@linuxsa.org.au>
> Subject: Re: Finding out specs of remote FreeBSD machine
> Resent-Date: Thu, 04 Apr 2002 18:42:56 +0930
> Resent-From: linuxsa@linuxsa.org.au
>
> >How would one go about finding out physical characteristics of a machine
> >remotely? For eg, I'd like to know what CPU a machine is running, what
> >HDD Model, what other devices are attached, and so on. In linux, I'd use
> >/proc... What do I use on FreeBSD...
>
> sysctl may provide some of the information you're after, it has the same
> sorts of things in it as /proc usually does.

sysctl will show more of the configurable side of the system whereas dmesg
or the contents of /var/run/dmesg.boot will be the quickest and most
comprehensive list of devices on the system.

> >Also, is there some way of finding out when a machine was first set up?
> >I used a find command to try to find the oldest file, but it appears to
> >be 1996 or so, and I'm surprised it's _that_ old... This was a file in
> >/usr/src somewhere. I suspect it's more likely to be the creation time
> >from the CVS repository... Is there any sensible way to find out this
> >information?
>
> Have a look at the dates on directories in the / directory. Some of those
> like /home and /stand probably havn't been modified, if they're not
> seperately mounted.

John's right, the timestamps on /stand or /proc will probably be the best
bet.
