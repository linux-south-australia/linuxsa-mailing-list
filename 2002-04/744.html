> - on board smarts - some server ones offload some TCP handling etc

The Theory of Everything, ooops, TCP Offloading Engines on these devices 
can be faulty. The BroadCom chipset required several revs before they 
worked properly.

Where I am now we have several Broadcom-based systems and cards, with 
differing revs of chips, from the BCM5700Ax through to the BCM5701xx 
chips.

Some have bugs in them that mean that TCP checksumming does not work in 
hardware. [1]

So, watch out which chipset your cards are based on if you care about 
these things.

[1]It has been a real pain in the posterior to have to develop patches to 
the FreeBSD driver to recognize the specific chip set and switch on the 
appropriate hardware support, only to lose the driver and have to 
redevelop them again. Perforce, one would commit them to source control if 
one had any sence,
