Linux "kernel hacking" course

Hi all,

I'm thinking of trying to organise a Linux "kernel hacking" course - teaching
how the 2.4 kernel works, including device drivers, the file system and
memory management, and giving hands-on experience in modifying the kernel.
It is a 5-day course, and it covers a lot of ground.  This has probably not
run in Australia before, so presents an unusual opportunity.

A course outline can be found at 
http://www.datadeliverance.com/courseware/UXL-107.php3

Pricing per person for the 5 days would be between $1000 and $1650 inclusive
of GST, manuals and lunch, depending on the number interested, and would
likely be at a location in or near the CBD.

I may also try to get a 3-day an Advanced Linux Administration course running -
see http://www.datadeliverance.com/courseware/UXL-105.php3
