Re: pcmcia->pci bridge cards group buy

On Wed, 2002-04-17 at 14:11, Toby Corkindale wrote:
> I don't have one here to test, but a search on google turns up various
> people who seem to be using them.
> 
> >From what I've heard, the intel chipset these cards (and almost all others)
> use, is reasonably well supported.

The intel chipset _specifications_ have become the defact standard. It
is properly supported by linux. My isa-->pcmcia bridge uses a richo
chipset that works in the intel mode, my laptop has a ToPic 95 chipset
which is also intel compatable, and the photo of these nokia bridge
cards I had a look at had a cirrus logic chip. So, theoryetically there
could be a bug in a particular chipset, which could cause compatability
probs in one chip and not the other. However, those that I have seen
work perfectly, and doubt there would be any problems with the nokias
when using current software.
