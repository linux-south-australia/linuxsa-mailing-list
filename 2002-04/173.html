Well... depends... ... ...

<snip>

from the sounds of things, at a minimum, the partition table is corrupt.
Can you do crazy things like read from /dev/hdb? Try making a dd dump of
all the data for a start -- that way, if it all goes wrong, you at least
haven't gone backwards...

Then try parted (esp useful if you know or can work out the original
partition sizes) and the LinuxCare Bootable business card CD... I used a
tool, I think it was from there, that enabled me to retrieve old
partitions... I think it was called gpart. IIRC, when I recovered a disk
that had a windows partition and a solaris partition, gpart told me how
big the partitions were, and parted allowed me to recreate the
partitions without damaging any data...

failing that, if you get really desparate, you could do something
completely crazy like strings /dev/hda, and manually extract some of the
data... this is enormously ugly though.

If, however, you can't actually read the data at all at all... then
there is a company that will do it, but it takes 6 weeks, a minimum
charge of around $900 or so, and then something like $100 per Mb of
data, with no garantees (apart from the size of the bill)... I doubt
it's worth it...
