RE: KDE3

> In my opinion this list is beginning to need some active management.

Strangely enough, this list is a public forum[1], people who say things
that may well require "active management" will, in time, experience
"passive management". They will gain something of a reputation, and this
reputation will extend from the virtual world of the mailing list into
the real world[2]. It will spread to prospective and current clients and
employers, as well as co-workers, and other people associated with them.

This reputation will cause people to actively dissociate themselves from
the person[3]. In this way, people who gain such a reputation will dig
their own graves, and basically make life hard for themselves. It's a
self-destructive pattern of behaviour, and may indicate that "help" of
some kind is needed, but that's definitely Off Topic, and not something
that this community ever set out to acheive.

As many of us know, Adelaide is a small place, and the IT industry is
extremely small. The Unix community is almost inbred. There's no more
than two degrees of separation in the Adelaide Unix community -- news,
and reputations, travel fast and far. While it's hard for many of us not
to treat this list like a small, closed circle of friends, the fact is
that it's more like an amplified conversation in a packed room, with
random people entering and exiting as they please.

Everything on the list is "on the record", and can and will be used
against us in the course of life. Of course, this simply doesn't apply
to people who ask questions that they fear might be "dumb", or people
who offer answers that are actually wrong, as these are simply evidence
of a desire to learn, and a willingness to try to solve problems. In
fact, they are what this list is all about.

This list is a Good Thing. Let's not waste it.
