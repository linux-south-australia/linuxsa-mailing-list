Re: is there a FAQ on the BEST MODEM

Hi Peter,
Internal modems are neat & tidy, but unless you understand IRQs, I/O
addresses etc, you will have problems configuring them.

Another thing to keep in mind is the modem is the weak-link in the
lightning chain.
In the event your telephone line gets hit by lightning, it will
probably take-out your modem. Now if you are using an internal modem,
it will probably take-out your computer too :)

Without exception, (& I know what I'm doing), I've never had a "plug it
in & it goes" experience with internal modems, hell, I've got one in my
SmoothWall firewall now, that I can't seem to get to work, & its a
Supra-Express, which is good one.

I've always been able to get them to work, but only with much fiddling.
Now the above problem is not a lack of good design on the modem.

The Lucent ***VENUS***CHIPSET*** internal modems are a real modems that
have a COM port, albeit with a PCI IRQ & I/O address.
Any other Lucent modem is a WinModem & whilst there is some support for
Linux, get a life & an external modem :)

As Nathan mentioned, internal or external, the real difference between
modems is in their ability to work on poor quality phone lines &
compatability with what they will dial-in to.

You can get a reasonable external modem for about AUS $85, fine if you
have good quality phone lines. If you live in the country or are in an
old suburb where the phone line wiring is old, perhaps keep your hair,
& by the Swann.
