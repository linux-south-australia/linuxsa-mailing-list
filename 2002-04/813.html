Re: sound recording

On Thu, 2002-04-25 at 15:11, Daryl Pilkington wrote:
> 7)
> You need a good A->D device, most sound cards just don't hit it.
> If you can get a quality sound-card with optical in & a DAT machine,
> use the DAT as a dedicated A->D device, the results are worth the
> effort.

When I did this, I found that for good signal/noise ratio I was better
off using my digital camcorder, with firewire link to pc. I modified
dvgrab to write only the audio to wav files, and ran the audio through
the camera. Unfortunatly this meant it was 48khz instead of 44.1khz, but
provided you have good rate conversion software the change in does not
make an audiable difference, and the a/d converter in the video cam is a
million times better than the my crappy on MB line in or any other
recording device in my house. Unfortunatly, the DV API has changed
enough though that my wav hack wont compile on newer linux systems
anymore :(
