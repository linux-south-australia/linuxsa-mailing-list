Re: sound recording

Hi Bruce,
Whilst I haven't done this personally, I've worked as a technician at a
Broadcast radio station, so I know the issues.

Yes many people have done this, using Windoze & Mac.
You would probably be better-off using CoolEdit under Wine & use
Linux's superior file systems to store the large files, before
processing.

Google is your friend, do a search on each of the issues below:
The major ones, in order of importance:

1)
You'll need to clean your vinyl.
2)
You need a good turntable
3)
You need a good tone-arm
4)
You need a good cartridge
5)
You need a stylus that isn't worn
6)
You need a good phono pre-amp
7)
You need a good A->D device, most sound cards just don't hit it.
If you can get a quality sound-card with optical in & a DAT machine,
use the DAT as a dedicated A->D device, the results are worth the
effort.
8)
Once you've got it in the digital domain, CoolEdit is your friend :)
You could create MP3s or RedBook audio CDs.

Finding an Audio Enthusiast would be A Good Thing.
1)->6) are critical.
