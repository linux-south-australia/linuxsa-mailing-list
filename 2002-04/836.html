Re: sound recording [long]

Hi.

Well, sounds like you are persisting with connecting your speaker-outs to 
the soundcard input? Have you tried connecting the tape-out? I think you 
need to. Even if the volume sounds low, you can increase it with sox if you 
need to. The key here is to keep the levels low so you don't overdrive the 
inputs on the soundcard. You will need some noise filtering though, LP's 
are noisy at the best of times, and that noise will be recorded on your 
CD's for posterity!

Don't worry about the file sizes. Remember the cd is 600+mb

To minimise distortion, you want to keep the levels on the amp as low as 
you can. Any circuit on the amp or soundcard that gets overdriven will 
introduce distortion. That said however, I'd run the gain on the amp up in 
preference to the soundcard mixer, and I'd be looking at keeping everything 
low (nothing above 50%) try various combinations until you find the best 
sound with the gear you have.

You can bet that the amp is a better link in the chain than the soundcard, 
so try to set the levels accordingly.

Might be worth having a look at the alt.audio.* newsgroups, I'm sure you 
will find a FAQ on this subject. Google is great for this, just type your 
search and click the 'Groups' tab.

Regards,
