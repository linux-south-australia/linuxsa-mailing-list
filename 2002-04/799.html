Yes, kinda.

I once ripped an old 45 to wav and then to mp3. It sounds quite
reasonable, but as one would expect, not as clear as a CD.

What I did was to connect the record player to my old Technics amp,
then use the headphone output to pipe the sound to the Line In on the
sound card. This way I could manually adjust the volume of the
recording by using the volume controls on the amp.

You then have to setup whatever software you wish to use to sample at
16 bit, 44kHz (or there-abouts), tell it to start capturing and start
the record player.

After you've got your sample, you'll probably need to load it into a
wav editor and cut out the whitespace on the leader and leadout and
pass it through a few filters to cleanup some of the scratches, etc. I
used GoldWave on a Winblows box, but there are quite a few packages
about.

Finally, I converted by resultant wav file to an mp3.

It turned out to be a lot of mucking about to get a single decent
sounding mp3, so I refrained from converting any more, but I'd proven
it was possible ;)
