Re: List Etiquette (Oh Please)

David, 

agree with you.

I could not support that motion. It is too restrictive on the range of 
communications amongst the individuals that make up the members of the 
LinuxSA Mailing List.

I have been involved with online communities since about 92 or 93 and 
for the last 4 years my paid employment has revolved around an online 
community (http://go.compuserve.com/pacific).

Tightly restricting the topics allowed to be expressed is a very good 
way to kill an online community. 

If somebody posts an off-topic story then the rest of the users will 
remind the poster in a clear fashion. 

If users keep to the present advice and add [OT] or [ANNOUNCE] or any 
other appropriate prefix before their topic then the members have ample 
opportunity to delete it unread or follow the discussion. It is up to 
them as it should be.

If others don't agree with it then fine, mailing lists are not 
democratic, it is whoever shouts loudest and longest (and last) that 
generates what is the consensus of a list. Lurkers don't get a vote 
until they speak up.
