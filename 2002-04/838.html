Re: is there a FAQ on the BEST MODEM

> > Hi, can some one point me at a list of the BEST V90 MODEM's for linux
use ?

I missed the first post in this thread.

I found that an external Mitsubishi Diamond Voice external modem works well.
It's K56/V90 compliant so it'll support both types (I don't know who still
uses K56 tho).  Be careful because they have internal versions as well (and
they're also cheap).  Not sure if the internal models are Winmodems or not.

My modem set me back about $100 and it works well with ATZ as the init
string, and ATL1DT to dial (and set the volume lower).  I've found that it's
more stable with that simple init than with a more complex one.  The complex
ones make it croak when it connects - dropping carrier straight away or
various other nasties.

For what it's worth I'm driving it from Smoothwall so I know it's Linux
compatible.
