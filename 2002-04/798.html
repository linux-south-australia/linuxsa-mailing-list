sound recording

Hi list,

I am setting up to record sounds from an old stereo amp to my linux system, to 
see if it is a reasonable proposition to use the system as a medium to create 
and burn CD sound from my vinyl record collection. I would appreciate hearing 
the experiences of anyone who has done this.

I am running a cable - RCA to line-in type - from the REC sockets of the amp 
to the line-in on the sound card.

Sox is recognising the input and creating crude sound files.
sox -t ossdsp /dev/dsp <file>.wav
The resultant files are scratchy sounds at low volume, but at least 
recognisable as a crude version of the input signal. This is a good start!! 
At this stage all wav files are 8 bit mono at 8000 Hz.

I am knee-deep in man pages and learning lots about sample rates, file types 
etc etc. Any advice at this stage would be great. Has anyone on list done 
this sort of thing before me?

Bruce
