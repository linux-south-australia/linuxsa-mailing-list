Serial Port Madness

HELP !

I am trying to setup a modem on a linux box to accept dial-in connections.

Hardware:
P-100 , using modem on COM1 (ttyS1), and KTX modem.

setserial ttyS1 reports 16550A UART, correct IRQ and address, etc.

minicom successfully connects to the modem, and you can type away AT
commands, and all looks well.

When I run 
mgetty /dev/ttyS1, it quits about 1 second later, and leaves a big pile of
messages in /var/log/messages about 'failing to drop soft carrier' and
other bizzare serial IO errors.

After this has happened, minicom will report 'Modem Offline' whenever
keystrokes are trying to be transmitted to the modem.

Reboot OS -> minicom still cannot connect to modem.

Reboot, power down, power back up again - the serial port is now
cleared, and minicom works fine again.

Use mgetty again, and problem repeats.  

Any clues ?
