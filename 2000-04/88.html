If I decide to build a web server, and 

>
>I don't hink you need to register a domain name server.
>You do need to register a domain for which it usually necessary to ahve a 

>configured primary and secondary nameserver.
>
>>From memory typicially you set up a primary  DNS server and secondary as
>if you had the domain, then contact the appropriate registery fromt eh
>domain you want to register.
>
>Typically they have an online form which will allow you to submit your
>details, including those of your now configured primary and secondary DNS
>servers, which the registery normally checks automatically when you submit

>your application or as part of the submission process.
>
>Then if you are nto or cna not pay by credit card you send of your
>payment.
>
>Then you wait a day or two (or months if you want .org.au) and with luck
>you get your domain.
>
>I think the registers for .au are  
>.com.au -www.melbourneit.com.au
>.asn.au  connect west which can be reached via www.west.net.au
>.net.au Connect.com - registry.connect.com.au
>.org.au http://www.aunic.net/org-au.html
>
>I think melbourneit can help you with ointernational registrations as wel
>eg .com .net .org .tu balh blah blah.
>
>You will also need a NIC handle which you can apply for here:
>http://www.aunic.net/
>
>
>The exact process varies from registry to registry, but I think this is
>valid for all of them apart possibly from .org.au(having never
>successfuly registered a .org.au domain  I don't really know. In fact
>havnign never even recieved a reply or even a rejection notice formt he
>registrar - I may have had a bad experience, btu I have on behlaf of two
>organisations applied for .org.au domains and never heard back. I suggest
>.asn if at all possible - typical turn around less than 24 hours often
>a couple of hours).
>
>Typically you will require some proof that you are eligible for a domain.
>Eg for .com.au this is usually a business number or an ACN and the domain
>must amongst other things derive from the letters contianed in your
>businesses name. FOr .asn it might be a certificate of associationor
>articles of incorporation or some such.
>
>The poliices are different form registry to registry so you need ot do a
>bit of reading.
>
>It has been at least 12 months since I last registered any domain. but I
>think this is a reasonably accurate descriptionof the process, and if not,

>I am fairly sure the URLS at least are accurate, frm where you should be
>able to get all the info you need.
