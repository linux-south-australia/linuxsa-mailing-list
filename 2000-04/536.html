RE: Unformating a partition

The formatting wouldn't be the killer - it just erases the table. The idea
that a new filesystem was written over top of existing data (bulldozing) is
the painful one.

Although I agree that the data is likely beyond reasonable means, I'll ask
this as a discussion: Have you tried Norton Disk Doctor (what have you got
to lose?) Just answer "Yes" to the question "Are you unable to access
partitions that you were able to access before" and it should try the backup
FAT (if it exists)

The FAT has a backup kept further down the drive. If the drive wasn't fully
erased (ie zeros written to every byte), then the backup FAT may still be
intact. If so, then the map to retrieve the data would be there too. 

If a piece of a file thread was missing because of overwriting, then a
scandisk or Norton Disk Doctor would declare it truncated and deleted. If,
however, all pieces of the file thread were intact, then it could probably
be retrieved.

Make no mistake about it though... You are sailing into uncharted waters.
But since the data is probably gone, go ahead and try it. I may be totally
off the mark, but if I am I hope another member of the group will correct my
inaccuracies before anyone else risks data loss as a result.
