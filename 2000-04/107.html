Re: fetchmail

Alex Wilkisnon wrote:

> 2. I have edited /etc/sendmail.cw ie
>    [lex@pluto ~]$ more /etc/sendmail.cw
>    # sendmail.cw - include all aliases for your machine here.
> 
>    pluto 10.0.0.1

This doesn't look right - did you mean pluto OR 10.0.0.1, or that
pluto is 10.0.0.1?  If the former, it should be one entry per line.
If the latter, it should be just pluto and the real place for that
entry is /etc/hosts.  Either way, you shouldn't need it, as fetchmail
will deliver to the user@localhost (except you've probably mucked with
that by adding localdomains - you don't need that for a straight
"extract from POP and dump to sendmail in a domainless environment"
setup).

> [lex@pluto ~]$ !f

"SMTP connect to localhost failed".  Is sendmail running?  Accepting
connections?  Try -

1) ps -deaf | grep sendmail
2) telnet localhost smtp
