Rather strange email problem...

Hey all,

I've got a rather strange email problem, which has got me stumped...

I maintain two mail servers, one is for my personal email
(osprey.mtx.net.au), and the other is my work's email server
(boss.syc.asn.au). Somehow, email sent to my personal address is being
delivered to my work's mail server. I'm thinking it could be a problem in
the DNS, but what has me stumped is that works mail server was never set
with an MX record for my personal domain. I did have 'osprey' acting as a
backup mail server for work, in case our link is down, but I have not set it
up the other way around. Mail was being bounced back to the sender with an
error of 'relaying denied' and also 'user unknown'.

Until I can work out what is going on, I have added my personal domain as an
alias in the /etc/sendmail.cw file, and added aliases in the /etc/aliases
file for the accounts in my personal domain on 'boss', so that they do get
delivered ok. A sample header of an email I sent myself after I added these
aliases is as follows:
