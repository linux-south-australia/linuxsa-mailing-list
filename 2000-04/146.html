Re: intranet solution

> I'm not a webmail bigot but I do think that this point has validity. If a
> sysadmin has a choice of letting more or fewer protocols he should go for
> fewer. Webmail allows this elegantly.

This is reason is backwards.  The problem that webmail solves is not how to
provide access to mailboxes for users in remote locations -- pop et al do
that perfectly well.  The problem is how you gain access to your mailbox
from a remote location.  The chances are you'll be using a crippled machine
which only poorly supports multiple users, indeed there may be no choice of
mail client and you might not be allowed to reconfigure the one that is
provided (to access your servers.)  Since most systems now come with a web
browser, webmail permits you to access your email using the tools at hand.
Webmail is not as powerful as a real mail program, for example attachments
are disapointing and they often are a slower than you might hope, but it's
probably better to have poor access to your mail than no access.

webmail helps administrators provide a basic service for users on machines
without the normal basic facilities.
