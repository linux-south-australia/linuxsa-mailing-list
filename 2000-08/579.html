> BIOS error drive 0x82 may be inaccesable x3
> not the first drive

Your BIOS may not actually be able to boot off of this drive.  If
it can (if you have one of those BIOSes where you can specify the
boot order), you can fool LILO into writing on the drive's MBR
by adding something like:

disk=/dev/hdd
  bios=0x80    (or 0x81).

Apparently, this is also an old BIOS limitation where a machine
was only able to boot off 0x80 or 0x81 (first or second hard
drive).  I came across this today on a machine chock full of
IDE and SCSI drives, and were attempting to boot of the SCSI
drive.  I couldn't write the MBR without unplugging the IDE
drives until I found this trick.
