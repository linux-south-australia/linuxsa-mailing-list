And even worse, clause 5.7 in the Acceptable Use policy says
that you cannot use automated programs to continually send
or receive "high volumes of data". Problem is, they don't
actually define what they mean by "high volumes of data" in
quantitative terms.

So, presumably you can't setup your own local news feed (using 
suck, leafnode, etc...), host your own personal web server, use 
rdist to keep your own Redhat mirror uptodate, etc..., for fear 
of crossing their undefined boundary. And if you do cross the 
boundary, they reserve the right to restrict or remove your 
access without warning!

I'm starting to think that I won't bother being an early
adopter of ADSL until the rules become more reasonable. What's
the point of high speed, high volume access if you can't make 
use of it?
