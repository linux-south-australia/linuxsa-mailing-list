Re: Linux support by companies (was Austar Broadband)

Jake Hawkes wrote:

> [3] some ISPs only *count* the incoming, and reason that you will always
> down more than you up.

It also dates from the start of the Internet.  Charging per-octet
allowed a fair distibution of the capital costs between large and
small institutions.

Considering the choice between charging per incoming octet, outgoing
octet, or both, incoming octet was chosen so that people that provided
network content were not penalised (eg: it wouldn't blow out the budget
of libraries that wanted to convert content into digital format).

This model changed as the Internet moved beyond academia.  Consumers want
a known-in-advance cost for their Internet product purchases.  Business
abused the charging model -- look at the number of ISPs using satelite
for incoming data where incoming octet charging is used -- so an attachment
cost plus a fixed amount for a particular virtual "pipe size" (eg: 2Mbps)
is used.
