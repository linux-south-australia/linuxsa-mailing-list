On Tue, 22 Aug 2000, Andrew Hill wrote:
> A while back there was a thread about new cabling rules. I had
> (computer) issues at the time, so didn't get involved (other than
> perhaps sparking it off by talking to friends about it off list - I've
> been doing the Base Cabling Licence course at TAFE. A very worthwhile
> way to spend your time). But I did get around to emailing the ACA to
> find out exactly what was going on. So, for those that do a bit of LAN
> cabling here and there, here is what the ACA has to say. (Thanks to Mark
> for permission to post his reply in the public forum):
>
> ----------------------------------------------------
>
> From: "Burgess, Mark" <Mark.Burgess@aca.gov.au>
> To: "'andrew@fornax.net'" <andrew@fornax.net>
> Subject: Cabling Rules
> Date: Tue, 22 Aug 2000 14:19:16 +1000
>
> Hi Andrew,
>
> Sorry for the delay in answering your e-mail.
>
> Currently, any person who installs customer cabling must hold a current
> ACA/Austel cabling licence. A Restricted licence covers the domestic
> scene
> where no more than two lines terminate on sockets. A General or BCL
> covers
> all areas of cabling along with any endorsements required for
> specialised
> cabling.
>
> A Security installer who is going to instal a mode 3 skt for his alarm
> system must have the appropriate licence. The only exemption is that
> they
> currently do not need a licence from the ACA to instal the cabling from
> the
> alarm panel back to the sensors and control pads.
> This is the same for tech's who instal data cabling for LANs. From the
> modem
> to network, licence required, from modem back to computers, exempt.But
> in
> all cases must instal to the wiring standards and use compliant cable.
>
> Plugging in of equipment to installed sockets does not require a
> licence.
>
> Under the new scheme of CPRs, the current exemption will cease to exist.
> This means that a person who performs any cabling which eventually will
> have
> a connection to a carrier's network must be registered as a cabler.
> ie. if you instal LAN cabling which will connect via a modem, you will
> have
> to be a registered cabler.
>
> If you have further concerns, please give me a ring on (xx) xxxx xxxx
> and I
> will be pleased to clarify.
>
>
> Regards
>
> Mark Burgess
> Customer Compliance
> Adelaide
