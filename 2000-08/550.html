RE: Linux support by companies (was Austar Broadband)

> If I was a business that did not have dodgy brown paper bags 
> under the table being passed or other loyalties to consider, 
> I would leap at a potentially large and rapidly growing 
> market like Linux users.

It's not neccessariliy dodgy brown paper bags -- it's business loyalties and
so on... a company who has a good working relationship with a major
supplier/partner/customer will be careful not the offend or upset that
customer, simply because it may jeopardise that part of their business...
Sometimes, there is a bit of under-handed stuff, but mostly, it's standard
business practice. Also, when you consider what extra effort another OS
costs to support (depending on what it is exactly that you are supporting),
and people's resistance to change, it's just human nature to resist adding
an upstart OS.
