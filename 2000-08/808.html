Re: Cabling Rules

Hiya,

Couple of nit-pick-points:

"Damien Wilson" <wilbs@bigfoot.com> wrote:
> 
> We currently have two of our techs going through AUSTEL training
> at the moment and I quizzed them on this a little while ago.

Austel doesn't exist as an entity any more, it's a concept that
is managed by the Australian Communications Authority (ACA).

> Theoretically, if you are using your LAN for Internet connection/
> phone line sharing then the cabling has to be done by an AUSTEL
> guy/gal because your LAN is being connected to the phone line
> which belongs to AUSTEL

Er, no, the cabling has to be done by an ACA licenced cabler, and
the phone line doesn't belong to AUSTEL, it belongs to whichever
carrier installed it (most likely Telstra).

> If your are going to either staple the cable to the walls or run
> it through the walls

Anyone cought stapling cables to walls should be shot, drawn and
then quartered.  Cripes :)  All the more reason for sending people
on cabling courses :)  That way you learn how to do it right :)

> If you ask me I think that the whole thing (from a SOHO point
> of view) is just ridiculous.....

Indeed and agreed, to a point!
