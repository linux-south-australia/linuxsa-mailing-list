Re: ZIP support

> Would like to mount parallel port ZIP100 drive.  Is this fairly
> straightforward or is it less hassle to stick with CDROM's. Are ZIP100
> drivers available to suit RH6.0 ?

The ZIP100 driver is a available as a kernel module from about 2.0.1...
you just compile it as a module in your current kernel, and select the
options in 'make [x/menu]config for the type of zip100 you have.

After compiling, either reboot if you recompiled the whole kernel, or
run '/sbin/depmod -a' to fix the kernel module dependencies.

The ZIP100 (parallel) drive that I have appears to the kernel as
/dev/sda4.  I have no idea if this can be changed/how to to that.  To
mount a disk with your newly compiled modules, run 'mount -t vfat
/dev/sda4 <mountpoint>' and hope for the best.  You can leave out the
'-t vfat' for auto-detection of FS type.

Hope this helps. If you have any questions about it i'd be happy to
answer.
