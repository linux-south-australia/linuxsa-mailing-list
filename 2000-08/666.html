Increasing swap space?!

Hi gurus,

I followed the advice of Michael Neuling and Mat Tippett and entered the
commands  and also the info in fstab. I have a swap space of 128MB as
/tmp/swapfile. The original one /dev/hdb6 from fstab, is missing though.
In the process of increasing the swap space, I stuffed up and lost the
original swap space /dev/hdb6. Trying to claim it with: "swapon
/dev/hdb6" I get the error message /dev/hdb6 invalid argument. My
intention of swap space was 128MB, but now there is a 64MB of swap space
on /dev/hdb6 shown on fdisk -p, which I cannot use. Could you guys help
me to reclaim those 64MB?
Michael uses /tmp/swapfile as an example, Matt is going further to add
the following line to /etc/fstab:
/dev/sda       swap       swap     pri=0   0  0
/tmp/swap      swap       swap     pri=0   0  0
Now these don't match!  /tmp/swapfile and /tmp/swap are two different
things.
If you have /tmp/swapfile you need to have /tmp/swapfile in fstab!
