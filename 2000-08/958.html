Howdy,
	you can find what colour depth your running by typing (in X)
xdpyinfo |more

Chances are your running in 8 bit by default. This means a maximum on 256
colours (bet you want more). You can default X to 16/24/32 by adding the
line: DefaultColorDepth 16  to the appropriate screen section. You
mentioned that your desktop is bigger than your screen. This implies that
you ahve a virtual desktop. Normally X windows defaults to 1024x768. Your
configuration files seem to indicate that you are running at 640x480.

You can set the resolution you want by modifying the approprate 'Modes'
line under the display depth you want. All this should be under the
Driver "svga",  Screen section (now that's confusing :)

Here is an example of it all wrapped up for 16bit colour in 1024

    Section "Screen"
    Driver "svga"
    DefaultColorDepth 16

    Subsection "Display"
        Depth       16
        Modes       "1024x768" "800x600" "640x480"
        ViewPort    0 0
	Virtual     1024 768
    Endsubsection

    Endsection

If your wondering about the colour depths here's a little table:

+-------+-----------+-------------+
| Depth |  Colours  | Common name |
+-------+-----------+-------------+
|   8	|    256    |    SVGA     |
|  15   |  32,768   |     -       |
|  16   |  65,536   |   Hicolour  |
|  24   | 16Million | True Colour |
|  32   | Too Many:)|     -       |
+---------------------------------+
