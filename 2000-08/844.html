Re: Troubles installing RH6.2

Hi John

As far as Windows 95/98/NT/2000 is concerned the new drive will have 'D' as 
its drive letter, shuffling along all your other drive letters, that's why NT 
wouldn't work with your new drive installed.

When you install Linux your current 'C' drive will be hda1 and your 'D' and 
'E' drives will be hda5 and hda6 (please correct me if I'm wrong). Your new 
drive will be hdb1 etc depending on how you partition it when you install.

Robyn
