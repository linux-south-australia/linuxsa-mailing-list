Re: Can't access Linux after win 98 reinstall.

At 11:30 AM 8/22/00 +0930, Darryl Ross wrote:
>The BIOS does to work out which OS to boot, hence it's not loading LILO :)

Hmmm, strictly speaking, the BIOS really does not, as it should simply
transfer control to the code in the MBR (the first 446 bytes are code, the
next 64 are partition table, last 2 are signature 0xAA55 or something like
that).

However, I have found BIOSes that will not boot if you do not have an
active parition.  They put up a message about OS not found, or something
like that.

You do not even have to have an OS in the active partition (you can mark
swap as active for instance), as the BIOS will transfer control to LILO in
the MBR which does the rest.

DOS and Windows, however, require that they be the active partition.
