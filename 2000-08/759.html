Re: Network Problem

Richard Watkins wrote:

> I then used "ifconfig" to associate "eth0" to
> 192.168.200.1 and 255.255.255.0, which seems
> to have worked fine.

[snip]

> "route add -net 192.168.200.1 netmask 255.255.
> 255.0 eth0"

is superfluous; you have already told the networking
layer (through the netmask on ifconfig) that it
handles the 192.168.200 net (try it; just do the
ifconfig, then a "route -n").

The reason it's probably not happy with you adding
the route (apart from it being a duplicate) is that
the "-net" setting doesn't start on the boundary
(in this case, .0, whereas you've specified .1).

> I currently get the message "Network is unreachable"
