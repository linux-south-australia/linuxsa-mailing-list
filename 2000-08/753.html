Re: Auth firewalls

At 03:51 PM 8/22/00 +0930, Steve Sloan wrote:
>I am having a look at an IPchains script with appear to work quite well
>until I tried to send an email from Outlook express on Win98 and it took
>well over 5 sec to send a short message that should have taken less then 1
>sec.
>
>On checking the logs I found sendmail was trying to send back on port 113
>and was being blocked.
>I let port 113 in and the whole thing works great.
>
>My question is what does Port 113 Auth do and is it safe to leave open.

This is identd. I would prefer to block it, as you never know what buffer
overflows lurk.

The problem is that you are DENYing packets, and should REJECT them. Then
sendmail at the other end will get the hint and ignore the failure to
connect to identd back on your end, and would keep going.
