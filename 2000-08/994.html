OK: Group A is the group that has the files that Group B wants to see...

so you have Group A's homedirs, and you want Group B to see everything, but
not be able to modify any of it, and you don't want Group A to see any of
Group B's stuff...

one solution is to have Group B to be members of Group A (use /etc/groups),
and make Group A's default mask 022 or 026... This will work, but Group A's
members could easily change the permissions on individual files and/or
directories within their home directory, and so on. ie -- this will make it
so that by default, what you want will be true, but if any member of Group A
wants to hide anything from Group B, they can. You could even run a cron job
to change the permissions back to your chosen default if you wnat, but this
wouldn't be ideal.

The other thing to look into is ACLs. I don't have time to go into that, but
I'm sure it would be _possible_ (not neccessarily practical :). look at
setfacl and getfacl ....
