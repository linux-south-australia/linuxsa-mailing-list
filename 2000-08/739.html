Re: Increasing swap space?!

On Tue, Aug 22, 2000 at 05:11:20AM +0000, michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:
> At 12:56 PM 8/22/00 +0930, Nick Stoic wrote:
> >Hi Matt,
> >
> >I realised my computer being very slow. After a pretty long time, I came
> >to the conclusion that the problem must be from having a small swap
> >space. 
> 
> It's unlikely that a small swap space will have a dramatic impact on performance
> unless of course you are using up all your swap, and the system is having to do
> some serious juggling.  At that point you're more likely to see "Out of memory"
> errors reported rather than just slowdowns.
> 
> It's worth remembering that accessing memory off of disk is at least two orders
> of magnitude slower than RAM.  If you want speed improvements it's possibly a
> better bet to try and reduce your memory footprint on your desktop (I'm assuming
> you're running an X desktop).  Try *not* using GNOME/KDE but instead using just
> fvwm.  Don't have some fancy background image.  Get rid of that netscape process
> unless you're actively browsing.  It all helps.  And you'll see great speed
> improvements by freeing up more RAM.  Of course adding more RAM is another
> option :-)

To see the state of your system, gkrellm is a good graphical app that lets
you see your disk activity, CPU load, etc...

Do a search on freshmeat.net to find it.
