On Wed, 2 Aug 2000, Freeman, Peter (ERHS) wrote:

> Hi all
> 
> I have just had an email from a guy who is having all sorts of hassles
> setting up modems.
> He has a Swannsmart 56k internal & is also trying a Netcomm Roadster 28.8.
> Has anyone had problems/success with these.  I have a suspicion that the
> Swann is a winmodem, but I would've thought there would be no hassle with
> the Netcomm.
> Does anyone have an 'out of the box working first go' recommendation that I
> can give him?

My Netcomm Roadster 28.8 worked straight out of the box... Well, I used it
on OS/2 first. It's one of the few modems I haven't had to tweak, and it's
quite fast enough in rural Australia - tho I did get my FIRST 28800
connection today! 21600 was the best till the last few weeks, then they
fixed the line a bit and I get 24000-26400 these days. 

I'm currently using kppp to connect, but before that I was using the
redhat thingy - mostly /sbin/ifup  at the CL. Then I found kppp was
working better than it used to in the 'old' days :-)

So sure, the netcomm gets the thumbs up. Works well with shitty lines. 

An AcerModem 56 surf, on the other hand, is a really crappy modem. best
used as landfill.

Don't know about Swannsmart.
