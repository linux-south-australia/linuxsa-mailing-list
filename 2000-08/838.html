Re: Is this a Bug in ifconfig?

Paul Schulz wrote:
> 
> This might be put down as a 'gotcha'.. (but I'm not sure)
> ifconfig.. (on Redhat 6.2) does the following...
> 
> > ifconfig eth0 10.64.1.2/24
> 
> doesn't set the netmask correctly (set it to 255.0.0.0)
> even though thought it should at lease complain.

Odds are that this is yet another bug caused by the slack
IP address conversion library routine that doesn't return
an error on trailing characters.  Bsaically, everything
from the / is being silently ignored.

The same routines also have the annoying habit of treating
  010.0.0.0
as the octal number 10 not the decimal number 10.

DEC UNIX did the right thing and rewrote the routines to complain
on trash and assume decimal.  GNU continues to be bug-compliant
with BSD.
