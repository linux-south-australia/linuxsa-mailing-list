RE: Footnote in LaTex

I think that the best way to accomplish this is to have two separate
footnotes, with the second one just referring to the earlier one (ie "refer
to footnote 3 above" or something similar)... however, I think that in
general, this wouldn't be correct -- depending on what your footnote refers
to, you should either not have a footnote (eg, if you have "IBM[1]" in the
text and "[1] International Business Machines" as a footnote, the next time
you mention "IBM" in the text, you don't need a footnote at all. Copyrights
and explanatory notes are similar -- you just assume the reader got it the
first time. For citations, there are specific abbreviations that mean
certain things like "from the same source as he last citation" (I think that
is "ibid.") and so on... Basically, I don't think that correct footnote
usage would allow for a repeated footnote number...
