Re: speed problems over ppp link

> Hello, all:

> Hunting through /var/log/messages, I don't see the connect speed (only
> "CONNECT<cr> -- got it". Looking at /proc/tty//driver/serial I see that on
> ttyS1 I have a 16550A uart at a baud of 115200. "stty -a" tells me that
I'm
> running at 9600. Is there a way for me to see what speed I'm connected at?
> Does anyone have any other tips to offer regarding crappy ppp speeds?
>
> Much appreciated,
>

I have always used the chat -r option to setup a log for connection reports.
Just put it in your connect script for instance I use something like

/usr/sbin/pppd lock crtscts /dev/ttyS0 115200 user "username" connect
'/usr/sbin/chat -v -t 120 -r /var/log/ppp0.log
-f /chat.file'

so when I connect up it reports the carrier speed and connect speed to the
log file /var/log/ppp0.log
