Re: Matlab on Linux?

Andrew Pullin wrote:
> 
> Why not try a search for them in your favourite search engine
> and see what turns up.

Indeed.  http://www.google.com/ shows the Matlab manufacturer's
website as the first hit and two clicks from there shows the
products listing by platform.  Linux/ia32 is listed, with
explicit support for the Red Hat and Debian distros.

You might also look at http://www.freshmeat.net/ to see if
there is an acceptable free clone of matlab.

BTW: the Digital UNIX version of matlab is unlikely to run on
     Linux/ia32.  DEC UNIX workstations don't use Intel processors.
     Programs written for Linux/Alpha will generally run on DU/Alpha,
     but not vice-versa.
