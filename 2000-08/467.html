Hi List,

I got a mailer from my pay-TV provider alerting me to the fact that from the end

of Sept. '00 I will be able to access Austar's "Chello"

Broadband internet service with a guaranteed download speed of 256Kbs...via microwave.

What is available in broadband in South Australia at the moment?

A phone call to Austar revealed the following...

This system  uses microwave downlink and a modem uplink.

A PCI board will need to be installed in your computer with associated

 cabling, dishes etc.etc.

When I asked about supported operating systems you all know the answer!!!!

The telephone support person sighed, "Not another Linux question" when I mentioned

that I was using Linux for internet access... must have a few inquiries already I guess...

This will be a win95/98 ("No winNT/2000 at this stage")

Is this service or another like it available in Australia?

Can/has it been "Hacked" for linux?

Is it really as fast as they say it is?

The cost , Austar tells me will be $49.95 per month - unlimited downloads,

5 email address and 10 mb of web page space

Sound good but I guess we'll have to wait and see...
