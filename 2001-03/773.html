it seems that the 2.4 kernel doesn't handle an 'out of memory' situation
correctly or very well as with 2.4.2 the kernel outputs an error
"_alloc_page: 1-order allocation failed" repeatedly and then kills other
active processes such as xfs and bash with "Out of Memory: Killed process
###" for example.

I was told of this problem by a friend who is a kernel developer, but
to further inform those who replied to my earlier messages: kernel
developers have acknowledged the problem - however they are expecting      
admins to make use of the process limiting configurations as john edwards
was describing earlier.

everything would seem ok, but! it would be interesting to see what would
happen if somebody connected say 10,000 times to fingerd (the same problem
would most likely occur as fingerd forks as far as i am aware?)
