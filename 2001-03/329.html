On 08 Mar 2001 22:51:54 +1030, Andrew Galdes wrote:
> hello,
> i am trying to mount a remote windows9x machine accross the net. using 
> smbclient i have tried several methods such as

I'd be inclined not to do such things as it is a <sarcasm>weeny
bit</sarcasm> of a risk to do such things. It could allow other nasty
people with too much time on their hands to try and do stuff to your
files remotely.

> smbclient -L remote_name -I //203.101.93.153 
> 
> smbclient -L //203.101.93.153
> 
> and i get soemthing like this:
> [root@andrew andrew]# smbmount //203.101.93.157/share_name /mnt/mount_point
> session request to 203.101.93.157 failed
> session request to *SMBSERVER failed
> SMB connection failed          

I'd suggest the reason for this would be something to do with the TCP/IP
bindings on the Win95 box you are trying to mount. I think it's under
the bindings for the Dialup Adapter. I can't remmember now... Anyway,
basically it has to be bound to the dialup adapter (read: listening on
the modem).

I'd strongly suggest that you find some other way of  sharing files
across the internet. Maybe even working through a VPN between your
remote Windows box and Linux box.
