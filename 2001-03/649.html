more ADSL ISP queries.

I know this kind of thing has been done to death recently, but ... this is a
question of fact, so shouldn't cause any flame wars :)

I note that ISPs are generally charging more for the faster connections --
eg 512K vs 1500K -- but you get the same MB cap, and I assume everything
else is the same... Is there a cost difference in providing these? Is it in
the modem, the equipment at the exchange, or the link from the exchange to
support the potential extra bandwidth? Personally, I'd love to have 1.5Mbps,
and particuarly the higher uplink speeds, but wouldn't use many more MB in
total if I were to have it... Where is the cost here? Or is it mainly
marketing (which IMHO, is fine, if a little irritating to people like me :)

Personally, what I want in a connection is: permanance (always on), static
IP, no restrictions on what I can do with it (masquerading, serving, etc),
and fast up and downlink. I don't need lots of MB either way, just fast
response. Of course, I want to pay as little as possible for it, but want a
permanant kind of ISP :) -- ie, the extra speed of 1.5Mbps isn't worht much
to me over 512K ...
