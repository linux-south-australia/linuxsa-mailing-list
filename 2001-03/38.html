RE: LinuxSA, Organisers and incorporation

I can see your point about AUUG - but myself as a non-technical person using Linux mostly for political and personal reasons have little more than a passing interest in Unix - I will never likely have a <insert name of kick butt workstation / mainframe here> at home to play with.  AUUG is not relevant to myself and many others in my position.

To us - Linux is freedom - it has little to do with Unix (for us).

The important point is that while yes, Linux is is in the Unix family (and thank the Lord for that - I owe my reliable system to this fact!) - for non techical Linux users this is not so relevant.

Linux is being marketed as a viable desktop and server system - not SCO Unix, FreeBSD, etc (as excellent as these systems may be technically - Linux is the one that is out there).  And considering the intense interest in Linux - LinuxSA should respond to this by presenting a incorporated organisation to the media, other organisations, companies, etc.  In short - Linux needs a public face in South Australia - IMHO
