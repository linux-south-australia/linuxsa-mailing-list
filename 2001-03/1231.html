Re: the business end of DSL

At 08:17 PM 3/29/01 +0930, Stephen Donaldson wrote:
>Ok peeps. DSL is now offered in my area from Telstra.  I have not been
>following all the posts so I may have missed something on this topic,
>however, here goes.
>
>Telstra won't support Linux and use the Alcatel (or close) modem.  Has
>anyone actually managed to get one of these beasts to work in Linux?
>How did you do it, did you have DSL installed on the M$ machine and then
>(after they left) remove it and put it in the Linux box?

Well, what you need is PPPoE, since aDSL proper ends on the line side of
the modem. Your side is Ethernet, and you need a simple Ethernet adapter.

Simply have a dual-boot machine up under Windows when Telstra comes to
install, and use RP PPPoE under Linux. It is real easy ... Get the
parameters out of the Windows side and then put them up under Linux. The RP
install kit comes with a config script that does a good job.
