Re: [OT] Toshiba Laptop Battery pack

MEMORY EFFECT:
Essentially, electric cells are like electroplating during the charge
cycle.

Ask any electroplater how they chromium plate metal:- they superimpose
an AC voltage on the DC applied to the electrodes. This creates a dense,
hard plate of chromium.

The same goes for electric cells, using straight DC, over time, the
crystals of Nickel, Cadmium or whatever become larger & larger, reducing
the storage capacity of the cell. This causes the cell capacity to
diminish over time.

By charging at about 2x the A/hr capacity of the cell & then discharging
at about 0.1 A/hr with a 4/1 duty cycle in the range of 20-50Hz will
create a dense plating on the cell electrodes, restoring the capacity of
the cell & even increasing it!

My exact figures may be wrong but the above has been experimentally
verified with capacity tests & scanning electron photographs of the cell
under test in 1968 with an aircraft NiCd battery for the US Navy.

The above process must be done with each individual cell, so a battery
needs to be configured so each cell is accessible. Full charge is easily
detected on a cell by cell basis & overcharging should be avoided as the
electrolyte is released as gas, permanently reducing the capacity of the
cell.

Just my 2 electrodes worth.
