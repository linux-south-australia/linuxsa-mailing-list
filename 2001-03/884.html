Linux  like  you  mean...  Very  few other Unix like OS's use the eth0
interface   definition.   Most  of  them  use  a derivative of the NIC
driver.
eg:  Most  new  Sun  machines  have  hme0  and  hme1  as  the internal
interfaces.  You  can purchase a quad interface card for them which is
known as qfeX (where X=0 to 3).
HP/UX  is  a notable exception as it's internal interfaces are usually
lan0  and  lan1.  Although  add  on  cards are again named differently
(usually,  but  not  always  the  case),  btlanX  is  a  common add on
interface.
OpenBSD  uses  driver  based  naming  to,  my  firewall  has  an  we1
interface, as it uses an SMC-Ultra (8216) NIC.

You get the point though...I think I've been pedantic enough ;)
