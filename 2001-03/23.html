Re: INCORPORATING LINUXSA - Library

I will push hard for a library.  It just strikes me as a terrible waste of money and paper to purchase books that are obsolete quickly and that I may read a few times and then not use again and not part with because of the money I have spent.

It just seems to be a more effective use of funds to pool our resources for such things.  I think it is important.  How many of us have texts lying around doing nothing?  Would we not prefer to have them put to use?  And of course if we want to lobby for Linux to be taken up by disadvantaged / low income groups and non-profit organisations - then we will have more success if we can avail documentation to these people.

I don't see Microsoft availing software and texts to people unless it will grant them some political or economic advantage.

Jason - that is a great idea to write the software.  A web based inventory and loaning system would be essential.

Forget the Internet - many people prefer books for obvious reasons and there is always the copyright issue.  I would never condone breaking copyright if the restrictions are reasonable.
