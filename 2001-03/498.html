Re: Unlimited Traffic (was Re: iPrimus ADSL)

Sure I understand this argument and I have no problems with its validity, what
I do have problems with, however, is that the TOTAL charges throughout the
industry are way to high.  There is absolutely no need for charges of this
magnetite period.

I am not and have not been complaining how costs are recovered and how profits
are made (on the current model).  My gripe is that greed is the driving force
for our main carriers, nothing more and nothing less.  This has a major impact
on the country, its economic well being, its ability to drive new ventures,
explore new business models and conduct business in a framework that is
competitive compared to overseas rivals.

Even now, the cost of DSL could be a lot cheaper, did you know that Telsta
wanted to offer DSL at lower rates to its direct users than wholesale to
downstream ISP's?  This had more to do with killing competition that
justifying a cost structure.  It took intervention by the ACCC to put a stop
to it and who loses.... we do, we did, we still are, and there is absolutely
no need for it.

And if this is not enough, Telstra even went as far as trying to enforce
downstream ISP's to pay for fibre for high speed connections (BIG $$$) when
they were using copper to provide 2meg speed.

To summarize, yes based on current math we have no option but to pay the
ferry-man our copper coin otherwise we don't cross to E-Valhalla.  Does it
have to be this way, no,  should we demand more because of the overall
benefits to business, community and Australian well being?

yes yes and yes.

Steve
