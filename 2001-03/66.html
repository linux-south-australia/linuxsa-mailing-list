No you should have not have intruded in the first place without the
knowledge to back your comments.  I am also NOT going to enter into a
silly "my reader is better than your reader" conversation.

PLEASE take a single line of (say) Ian's message and place it in
various editors which might be available to you and see how they handle
that paragraph-line.  Then ask yourself WHY they don't all handle that
paragraph-line the same way. 

You are clearly unaware that there are good reasons why mail readers
should NOT wrap incoming mail.  It isn't just the creation of an ugly
mess when your reader wraps a message that is already wrapped.  
Try handling 100 lines x 10 columns of technical results with a reader
that wraps automatically.

I believe that there is an argument that readers should wrap incoming
mail automatically, but so far as I can see it only has merit in the
receipt of M$Word-type paragraphs, which you shouldn't be getting from
a mailing list anyway.

> I do object somewhat to getting .doc

limitations that might result.  That's fine.   Note however, that your
reader will cope with messages I can read easily, but the obverse is
not true.
