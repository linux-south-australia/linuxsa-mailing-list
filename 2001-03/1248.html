Re: [OT] Toshiba Laptop Battery pack

Hi There,
    As I understand it, most rechargeable batteries develop
a "memory" after a while (yes even those ones that aren't
supposed to like Ni hydride). A deep cycle is basically done
by hooking the battery up to a recharging machine that is
very sensitive and it charges and discharges the battery by
reversing the voltages on the terminals so that the battery
is completely charged full and then completely discharged
fully (not just discharged to a point where the
laptop/phone/etc cannot use it any more) several times. This
machine forces the terminals to "flip over", which is
normally a very bad thing, until the battery "forgets" it's
memory.

    The upside of this is that after being deep cycled, a
battery is almost like new and it is a very cost effective
way to renew an old battery. The downside is that the deep
cycling, because it "flips" the terminals, the battery can
physically and/or chemically break down, and the battery is
destroyed. This is not so bad as it seems since the battery
is basically unusable before deep cycling anyhow in most
cases. If you want a more techo explanation, then I suggest
that you contact Pacific Dunlop and ask them, but that was
basically how they explained it to me. Hope this helps.
    Cheers!
        Andrew.
