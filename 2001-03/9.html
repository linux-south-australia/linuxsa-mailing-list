Ramen linux virus

A hackee has just told me that his RH 7 box has
been hacked with a linux virus called Ramen.
A quick check with google turns up this:

http://www.cert.org/incident_notes/IN-2001-01.html

It seems that it uses ports 25 (smtp), 111 (rpc) and 515 (lpd).
In this case, this guy's machine had probed my 515 ports.
And that's what caught my attention.

Now probably all of you securitoids out there think this is
crambe repetita [1], but this one's new on me.

The point I want to make is that this Ramen thing is probably
rampant on the net - because I get probes on 25, 111 and 515
continuously all day and night.
I also get the 27374 port scan that it uses.
And it concerns me that RH 7 has the vulnerability.
This is the latest version, I think.
The very high rate of these probes on my little networks seems
to support the idea that this virus is spreading fast!

So all yous out there who think your RH 7 is safe just need
to make sure you've got the drawbridge firmly up!

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.

[1] Crambe repetita, Latin -> cabbage reboiled. 
I.e. old news or an old joke.
