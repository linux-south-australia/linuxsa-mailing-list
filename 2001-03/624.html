Re: Why do ISPs give away support? (Was: SENET and LINUX)

Phil Hutton wrote:

> Of course the last bit would have to be market share.  If two ISPs
> were equally priced, but one offered support, which one would you go
> to (assuming you were Joe Average)?  And then, once you've signed up a
> user, you may find that you don't make a lot of money out of them if
> they can't connect.  ;-)

Unfortunately or fortunately depending on your point of view, it is a
requirement by law in Australia to provide support for a telephone or
Internet service. Not helping a customer resolve a problem can result in
a fine for an ISP or Telco, even if the customer is clearly in the
wrong, or in some cases, not even a customer. This system is enforced by
the TIO, a private company that has inherited government superpowers due
to poor legislation.
