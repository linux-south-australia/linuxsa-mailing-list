Jeremy Ervine wrote:
 
> Yes, I am aware that ISP's pay for bandwidth, as far as i've learnt, on
> average 8cents per mb, or on high usage, often unlimited.

Most ISP's are using unlimited pipes right now, you can call up UUNET
and they'll happily sell you a 1 Meg pipe for $14000/month (I think the
pricing might even be a bit better now). This will allow just 20 modem
users to download flat out simultaneously. 20 x $21.95 = $439 .. how do
you think they're doing it?

> To begin with, at home, I use an Ihug Ultra Satellite package, paying
> approximately $60 per month. I am allowed to connect for 300 hours per
> month, unlimited downloads. This satellite operates with 33k uplink analogue
> modem uplink, and a 400k sat inbound. This does have the trouble of a little
> bit of latency, but other than that, it's great. I run it on my linux box,
> masquerade it over my network at home and I can get download speeds of near
> 90kilo BYTES per second, of course using a simultaneous downloading utlity
> such as download accellerator. I have been on average downloading up to 20
> gig per month ... no problems ... in which way am I deluding myself my
> wishing to pay more than this for essentially a similar deal, only a little
> faster ?

According to IHUG, there is now a 2 Gig Cap on this service.. hardly
unlimited. There's also several complaints to the ACCC about them since
they're not honouring the original "Unlimited downloads" contract many
people signed up for, yet forcing them to pay out the full duration for
a service they don't want. Also, a "little but of latency" is probably
going to be 400-500ms, at least 4x what a modem connection can get let
alone a digital service. They also have to pay for Satellite time to
deliver this to you, and it's nowhere near $60/400Kbits.
 
> Since ISP's do pay for bandwidth, many ISP owners feel the best way to run

Honest businesses pass the cost onto the customer. Otherwise there are

themselves.

What do you mean by shitloads? Most people we sell lots of bandwidth to

We stopped using IHUG. Not cost effective when you need to buy real
bandwidth instead of 'borrowed' bandwidth. They also do their
wholesaling in US dollars which isn't very attractive right now. If it
