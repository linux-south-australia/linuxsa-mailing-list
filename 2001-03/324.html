Re: UPS "moment of decision"

Alan Kennington wrote:

> Is there anything additional I should know?

If you plan to run during extended outages on a generator, you might
want to look at double-conversion UPS systems. This usually means that
the power is always generated via the battery, instead of just during an
outage. The difference is that normal UPS's often cannot charge properly
from a generator or poor/failing power source because they interpret the
fluctuations as an outage and run solely from their battery. A UPS with
double-conversion will happily run and charge off of a bicycle-powered
generator :)

Double-conversion units cost more, and aren't available from most of the
common brand names. I use a lot of "powerware" units which stack nicely
with up to 3 extra gel-battery packs. 

Also, the only extra quality in APC units is in the quality of their
marketing. Their really good UPS's are apparently made by another
company and rebadged for the US market anyway.
