>
>I would have thought - unless there is some sort of
>status quo that you need to protect - that you would
>have supported us if we came up with ideas that can
>lower costs for ISPs and we were trying to help you as
>well as ourselves.

But that idea doesn't lower costs for ISP's, Ian, it *raises them*. 
That is what you seem to be missing.

ISP's already run caches. Today they use them to lower the average 
cost per MB for all customers, which is a simple, relatively fair, 
and relatively equitable mechanism which leads to few, if any, 
arguments.

What you are proposing is complex, hard to bill, hard to prove to a 
customer in terms of billing correctness, and has zero, nada, zip, 
effect on the total costs for the ISP, other than to raise them due 
to the added complexity of the technical systems that the ISP would 
need to invent in order to satisfy your suggestion.

Please, be clear that the suggestion you've made is a reasonable one, 
entirely reasonable, at face value. But if you think it through, and 
ask the advice of experts, the outcome is that the notion doesn't 
actually help in the long term, and has a total cost of 
implementation that would materially _raise_ the average customer 
bill, not lower it.

Or to put it in terms you might relate to, given your comments above 
- Telstra don't do it, so we shouldn't, right? After all, they're the 
people that nobody has any troubles with, right?

[BTW, if you believe that, you're not reading the papers or talking 
to people who actually rely on Telstra for mission critical needs]
