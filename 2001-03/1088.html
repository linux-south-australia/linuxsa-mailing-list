RE: tar '.*' files

> is there a way to include '.*' files in a tar archive without
> including '..'
> etc as well?

<snip>

> i want tar it all up, including .cshrc, .history etc, but i
> *don't* want to
> include '../*' .
>
> if i do tar 'tar cvf uni.tar .*' it goes to ../* and starts
> adding all that,
> and if i do 'tar cvf uni.tar *' it misses the .* files.

try

`tar cvf uni.tar * .[^.]*`

the "[^.]" should expand to everything starting with ".", with second
character not being "." -- this means you miss . and .. (it must have a
second character)

or try: (careful with backticks here)

tar cvf uni.tar `find .`

(this will include "." though, which should be ok...)

>
> i couldn't find anything of use in either 'man tar' or 'tar --help', so i
> have rtfm ;-) anyone know?

the first solution I offer is a shell wildcard expansion thing, and the
second is the shell executing the command `find .` which expands to a list
of files, so in both cases, it's not a tar thing.
