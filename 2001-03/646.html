RE: Why do ISPs give away support? (Was: SENET and LINUX)

> I'm truly surprised that any ISP bundles support with subscription fees.
> It doesn't matter what platforms you offer support for, because the
> fact is that computers are very complicated, probably infinitely so,
> and I don't understand how any business can afford to say, "ask us and
> we'll give you the answers."

It's a business decision. You are marketing to a demographic who needs
support to be able to use your service. On average, you require (and you
know this through guesswork, rumour, trial-and-error and experience) X
minutes/Week of tech support per subscribed user, distributed through the
day with a certain distribution. Hence, you will require a certain pattern
of support staff, and this will cost you $Y/year, so you divide that by the
number of users, Z, and you add $Y/Z + profit per year to the sale price of
your service. Of course, if your business decision is to target educated
users only, you don't provice this support, and you get a different type of
user. It really depends on who you are targetting.
