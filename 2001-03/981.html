Of course you didn't realise that.  You wouldn't have said it had you
known it was wrong.  I know that that you were trying to post useful
information.  I was not being sarcastic when I said that I was delighted
that you had found the answer to your problem.  You researched the
answer to your problem and came up with a solution, which is wonderful.
I wish everybody would try to emulate that.

I'm surprised that it never occurred to you to read your modem manual,
and if you had done so you would have gleaned the answer a long time ago.
I can imagine that you might have no idea of how your modem and computer
communicate, other than in general terms like "serial" and "bits".  If you
read your manual you will learn about AT commands, and carrier-loss times,
and all sorts of other useful information.  Why wouldn't you read every
manual as a matter of course?  At least skim through it so you have some
feeling for what it is that is being described.

I get so frustrated when people say, "I didn't read the manual; how could
I have known?" or "I haven't got time to read every manual," or worst of
all, "Why should I read the manual when I can get the answer to a simple
question by asking the list."  Not that I'm accusing you of any of these,
far less that terribly selfish last attitude.  I have seen all of these
miserable excuses offered before.  The message I am trying to convey,
and I do realise I'm fighting a losing battle, but it seems to me to be
a noble one, is that reading the manual is not optional.  You MUST do it.
