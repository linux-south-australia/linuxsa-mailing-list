Re: ADSL (residential) - why waste one's time or money?

>>    Total cost per port per
>>    month:                     $103
>
> Of course you can probably run somewhere between 8-12 users on that one
> port.

Quite so.  The point that I was trying is that this is what it cost
for a port for a month with zero bytes of data included in the deal.
Unlike v.90, and ADSL port cannot be shared amongst 8 to 12 users.
If ADSL ports costs are similar to v.90 port costs, and I don't know
if that is the case, but if they are similar, then how could anybody
expect unlimited data to be included in the deal?  Assume that ADSL
is somehow half the cost; so at over $50 per month port cost, with $75
per month income, the operator has only $25 per month to pay for data.
At 8c/MB -- and that's a rock bottom price -- the operator loses money
after delivering 300MB.

Ian, Stephen and anybody else who believes unlimited data is possible:
explain how ISPs can offer that?  Wanting it so doesn't make it so.  Give
us the financial model.  (I did, and it doesn't support your position.)
