On Mon, Mar 19, 2001 at 11:05:42PM +1030, Richard Russell wrote:

 > I note that ISPs are generally charging more for the faster connections --
 > eg 512K vs 1500K -- but you get the same MB cap, and I assume everything
 > else is the same...

Not all ISPs :-)

 > Is there a cost difference in providing these? Is it in
 > the modem, the equipment at the exchange, or the link from the exchange to
 > support the potential extra bandwidth?

It really depends on how much of the connection is "your problem."  If you
go to an ISP which will wear the cost of the router port at their end, 
the link between you and them, and a managed router at your end, that'll
cost more than an ISP which connects you at whatever speed you want for
the same price provided you can get yourself into one of their ethernet
switchports.
