  BA> accompanying brochure, Its specs are: 
  BA> Maximum Resolution and refresh rates: 1280x1024 up to 60Hz

  BA> On this basis I have used this modeline in my XF86Config: #

  BA> used "safe" for the monitor?

Hi Brian,

I'd imagine that those settings would be just fine.  From my own
experience, it would seem that the specified maximum refresh rates may
be rated down a little.  

XFree4 uses the DDC lines to read the specs direct from the monitor.
Here is an extract from my monitor (Matrox "MGA" video card):

   (II) MGA(0): I2C Monitor info: 0x867b890
   (II) MGA(0): Manufacturer: SUN  Model: 4  Serial#: 0
   (II) MGA(0): Year: 1997  Week: 39
   (II) MGA(0): EDID Version: 1.0
   (II) MGA(0): Analog Display Input,  Input Voltage Level: 0.700/0.300 V
   (II) MGA(0): Sync:  Separate  Composite  SyncOnGreen
   (II) MGA(0): Max H-Image Size [cm]: horiz.: 37  vert.: 29
   (II) MGA(0): Gamma: 2.60
   (II) MGA(0): DPMS capabilities: StandBy Suspend Off; RGB/Color Display
   (II) MGA(0): redX: 0.625 redY: 0.340   greenX: 0.280 greenY: 0.595
   (II) MGA(0): blueX: 0.155 blueY: 0.070   whiteX: 0.283 whiteY: 0.298
   (II) MGA(0): Supported VESA Video Modes:
   (II) MGA(0): 720x400@70Hz
   (II) MGA(0): 720x400@88Hz
<snip>
   (II) MGA(0): 1024x768@70Hz
   (II) MGA(0): 1024x768@75Hz
   (II) MGA(0): 1280x1024@75Hz
   (II) MGA(0): Manufacturer's mask: 0
   (II) MGA(0): Supported Future Video Modes:
   (II) MGA(0): #0: hsize: 1280  vsize 1024  refresh: 76  vid: 36993
   (II) MGA(0): #1: hsize: 1152  vsize 921  refresh: 76  vid: 36977
<snip>
   (II) MGA(0): end of I2C Monitor info

Then XFree4 chooses a modeline to maximise your display, while not
exceeding the specs.

I got my Sun monitor to run at 1600x1200, despite the fact that this
resolution does not appear in the manufacturer's specs.  It is still
within the horizontal scan rate limit, which I believe is the most
important consideration.

When I tried a horizontal scan rate which was higher than the
monitor's capabilities, it just wouldn't turn on.  

So the point of my long tale it that both XFree4 and the more modern
monitors have protection to ensure that you can't damage your
hardware.

But hey, if your monitor dies suddenly, don't complain to me ;-)
