Re: Linux RAID

>Hi can anyone tell me the brand name of RAID cards, that will work with
>Linux, ideally with no reuqirement for modules that do not ship with
>standard linux kernels.
>
>IDE preferred(we have tons of disks and hot swap bays for IDE), but SCSI
>will be fine.
>
>I am only interested in cards that people are already using and have
>working.

Duplidisk have a number of different IDE mirroring devices, and I know for a
fact that the OS-independent ones work with linux. Unfortunately, the newer
ATA-66 models are not C-tick approved, so it's illegal to sell them (but not
illegal to use them) in Australia. The older models work just fine as well.

3ware escalade controllers use the same chips in all of their models (up to
8x ATA-66 drives) and the drivers for them are in the 2.2.15 kernel or
better. FreeBSD 4.1 also has support for their cards, but you need 4.2 for
the management software to work.

Beware hotswap bays for ATA-66 drives .. they tend to make the drives not
work at all or behave strangely, even though a good raid controller will
still detect the drives and work with them. I think that the 3ware card did
support hotswap IDE with the right bays.
