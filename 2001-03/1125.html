Re: LinuxSA Etiquette

Nicely done some propsed mods/additions below.


> 1. Is it in HTML. Make it not so -- plain text is preferred by many.
> 2. Do you have line wrap turned off -- turn it on... it irritates some
> readers, and makes the web archive hard to read.
> 3. Is it insulting, or a flame? Don't send it. This includes condescension,
> harsh sarcasm, accusation of stupidity, and personal attacks. LinuxSA
> doesn't need them, so please don't send them.
> 4. Is the subject line relevant to the subject matter, and specific enough
> to be userful? "A Question" is not useful. Nor is "No Subject" or "".
> 5. Is it signal or noise? Signal is good, noise is bad. Noise doesn't add
> anything to the list, and serves only to camoflage the signal. A post is
> generally good if it causes (or enables) you or someone else to learn
> something or fix something. Most Anti-MS jokes have been done before, and
> aren't worth sending to the list, especially if that's the whole content of
> your post. Be aware that some people on the list may be MS weenies...
> 6. Has the subject matter been covered before? Check the archives. Will
> bringing it up again really add anything other than traffic to the list?
> 7. If you're responding to a flame or insult, take it with a pinch of salt,
> and try to let it slide -- flaming trolls doesn't really help anyone.

Proposed addtion
