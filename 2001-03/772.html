Re: ADSL (residential) - why waste one's time or money?

Chris Yeoh wrote:
> 
> Will breaking the duopoly really decrease the prices significantly?
> >From what I've seen of TransACT's prices they're not that different
> from say Telstra's ADSL (for the same amount of bandwidth into the
> home). Traffic is free on the internal network (which will be great if
> there is enough content on it) but you still have to pay to get to the
> 'outside' world at about the same rates.

Premium service is $50 per month for phone, TV and 256Kbps
data, ie: $20 more than a Telstra phone.  The data rate is
currently limited mostly by backbone routing capacity.

As for services, the AARNet mirror will appear on-net for
TransACT users and I know they've been talking with other
large content providers as well.

As TransACT rolls out you'll see off-net prices fall as
TransACT will be able to afford its own off-shore bandwidth
and its sheer size will force free peering with the likes
of Telstra and Optus (else they'll have to provision
extra offshore capacity to send TransACT traffic via
the US).
