RE: LinuxSA, Organisers and incorporation

> To us - Linux is freedom - it has little to do with Unix (for us).

There are two ways you can use the word "Unix".  One has (tm) after it,
and can only be used under licence from X/Open who own it.  If you want
your product to be Unix(tm) you need to have it certified (every year,
I think), which costs a packet.  The other use is what I call "lower
case unix", which covers all unix-like operating systems.  Linux is unix.
AUUG embraces unix.  And  unix is freedom; always was, always will be.
