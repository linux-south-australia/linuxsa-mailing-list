Jason!

Title Case is the case generally used in a locality to denote titles.
When I went to school titles were supposed to follow these rules:

* first letter of all words are capitalised except the definite,
indefinite, conjuctions and prepositions; prepositions of length greater
than 4 characters should also be capitalised

Hence,

"The Man of La Mancha Jumped Over the Moon" is title case whereas "The
man of La Mancha jumped over the moon" is not.

Different localities and cultures define title case differently, though.
Some only capitalise the first letter of the whole title, whilst others
(particular the U.S.A) capitalise all first letters of the title
regardless of what type of word it is.

Oh the joys of internationalisation!
