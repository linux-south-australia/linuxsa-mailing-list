> What signal?

Signal to noise ratio (s/n) is an electrical engineering term.
A sinal is like a bit of data that you are interested in. Noise is
generated whilst transmitting that bit of data, through wires electrical
components, etc. When the amount of noise that gets transmitted gets up
enough it becomes hard to distinguish the signal (the bit that you are
interested in) from the noise (the bullshit crap that no-one is
interested in hearing). Does that make sense?
