Announcement: Linux Course Proposal (4-6 weeks)

Greetings,

I would like some suggestions and feedback on what people would like
to see in a community Linux course.

Endeavour college (Mawson Lakes) is running computing courses in the
evenings, starting in term 2. (I'll have to go and look the start date up :-)
Currently these are for Microsoft Applications, running on their PC's.

I have spoken to the organiser of these, and there is the possibility
of running a Linux or 'Linux' application course.

The other courses are in the order of $60 for 8 hours.
(1 night a week over 4 weeks)

The options are: 

1. Configfest [ May be the most helpful for Linux Community ] 
   - Bring your machine along and let us help you get the most out of it.

2. Starting with Linux
   - Use Sarah B's installing Linux mini-howto to install and configure linux.
 
3. Using Applications under Linux [ Very Basic ]
   - Netscape
   - Star Office

People would have to bring in their own machines, and this would be
reflected in a reduced cost of the course.  This may fit in nicely, if
the same night is used for another computer class.  (There are other
rooms available.)

-- 

What would be really neat though would be to have a bootable CD, which
could setup the machines as 'diskless' clients from a server.  The we
could superimpose a linux network over their windows boxes.
  
I'm resonable confident that this could be done.  Has anyone tried this?
