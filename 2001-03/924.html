Yes, and as I've said already, fixed $ for a fixed pipe size is 
directly equatable to $ per MB, just insert your average utilisation. 
For a domestic customer, in general, whose utilisation won' t be high 
on a 24x7 basis, the utilisation becomes very low.

However, in this situation, another factor kicks in - which is that 
every port at the ISP's side costs them money on a 
fixed-cost-per-line basis (and even the line rentals are more 
expensive than residential lines - Telstra reward high line count 
businesses by charging MORE, not LESS, per line - go figure).

So as people stay on for huge hour counts with very little downloaded 
volume, the dominant cost then tends towards the $26 or so per month 
in line rental fees and the roughly same again in costs of buying, 
maintaining, upgrading and running access server equipment. So if you 
stay on a line 24 hours a day, even downloading nothing, you've cost 
your ISP about $52 already in that month.
