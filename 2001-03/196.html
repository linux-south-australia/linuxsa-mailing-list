Re: etsa-caused ls process loop

We have installed Mandrake 7.2 in Adelaide TAFE classrooms because of the ReiserFS
system.  The institute *powers down* the classes at 10pm regardless, poof, crash and
bang there is always a blue screen win 2000 puter somewhere, just different one each
morning  ;-)  The MD linux has been working fine each morning :-) So they have been
crashed out each day now for about 5 weeks with no probs.

BTW ReiserFS does not support quota settings last I looked, but it sure does rock in
my books.  Suse has it too as an option, Redhat does not.

>
> 1. I would have thought that your firewall was not your server. If you
> have 5 computers there, one should be the firewall, and do virtually
> nuffin else. There are many ways to achieve this, and filesystem damage
> should not be a big risk - you could use one of the router distro's and
> boot it from a floppy. Run no fans or HD's on the box (or monitor) and it
> would run for hours on a small ups.
>
> 2. Keep all the logfiles on your server, using remote syslog to get them
> there from all the 'at risk from etsa' boxen, and the firewall.
>
> 3. Put a larger ups on the server (or 2) - some of the ups can be extended
> with bigger batteries - you could extend the life to a couple of hours
> without a huge amount of trouble.
>
> 4. Go ahead, reiser the whole lot if you are game.
>
> This way, you should lower the risk to your data from security breaches
> and power glitches. The remote syslog will tell you exactly what was
> happening when the power went out because your server will still be up.
