> 2. Posting
>
> When posting, bear in mind that the list is a public forum, with many
> people who don't have much in common with yourself.  We ask that you
> abide by the following guidelines:
>
> 1. Don't post in HTML.
> 2. Wrap lines at about column 70.

1. Don't post in HTML. Plain text only please. Wrap lines at about column
70. Don't quote excessively. See http://www.lemis.com/email.html for a
thorough discussion.

2. Ensure that your message is legible and has a minimum of typos. If people
struggle to read your messages, they won't feel disposed to helping you.

> 3. Use a descriptive subject line.

Add [OT], [AD], [PLUG] where appropriate. Change the subject line if the
subject of the message changes.

> 4. Commercial postings are generally not welcome.  You may promote things
>    that are relevant to our charter, but don't do it too much or often.

I have been thinking. I kinda think that they should be welcomed as long as
they are non-intrusive and clearly marked as commercial. No spam, no
repetition, etc... but IMHO, there's nothing wrong with someone offering a
Linux course, or a conference, or a new driver for their video card, or a
new video card with drivers or some other Linux-related commercial item
(Netcraft's RedHat CDs come to mind...) I'd change this to:

4. As long as you do it sparingly, you may promote commercial activities
that are relevant to our charter. Mark them clearly as commercial postings
(see 3.), and do it gently.

> 5. Don't be unnecessarily rude, sarcastic or offensive.

remove "unnecessarily" -- it's unnecessary :)

> 6. Don't take offense at what people say; they probably just expressed
>    themself poorly, or you misunderstood what they meant.

themselves.

add: Either way, don't take it personally -- just shrug and move on.
