AUUG and LinuxSA (was: LinuxSA, Organisers and incorporation)

*sigh* Why do these things always have to happen when I'm at the other
end of the world?

I'm copying the AUUG executive committee on this blanket reply to up
to 11 messages (I may remove some that I am currently planning to
reply to).  This is the first that the other 6 members of the
committee know about the issue, so this message is obviously my own
opinion, not AUUG policy.

Before I go into specifics, my general take on this is: we already
have too many UNIX-related associations and societies.  There are a
number of reasons to keep down the number:

1.  It's a lot of work running an association.   AUUG has about 600
    members and a nine-head executive committee.  We meet every 3
    months and regularly have to beat our collective chests for not
    having done the work we had promised to do.

2.  It costs more money than you think running an association.  AUUGs
    budget is measured in six-figure terms, and managing it has, in
    the past, proven to be a particular problem.  Except for very
    small (< 20) associations, I suspect that the per capita
    expenditure may be higher for small associations.  That's one of
    the reasons why we're trying to get more members.

3.  There are only so many people who are interested in membership of
    this kind of association, and even now we're seeing that people
    are making choices.  In SA we have LinuxSA (very active) and
    SAGE-AU-SA (spelling?) and AUUG, both trying to regain a foothold.
    We (AUUG) could, of course, just say "OK, the place is
    overpopulated already", and give up, but there are some things
    that we do that LinuxSA doesn't, and I'm still not convinced (see
    1 and 2 above) that they will do effectively, for example opposing
    the latest brain-dead legislation proposals.

    (The reason I'm not convinced is because of the work involved, and
    the difficulty of getting people to listen to you).

4.  An association's influence is strongly dependent on its size.  Two
    groups of 1000 members each have much less clout than one group
    with 2000 members.  The current discussion has centred around
    "big" AUUG and "little" LinuxSA, but at the last AUUG exec meeting
    we realised that we are too small to make much effect by
    ourselves, and we have decided to join the IT council of SA to
    make our opinions more public.  LinuxSA could join as well, but
    then our combined voices within the IT council would be weaker.

I feel that AUUG in its current form has outlived its usefulness.  We
still have people on the committee who would prefer to do things the
way our grandfathers did, who don't like doing business by Email, who
prefer to use Microsoft to UNIX.  For some time we've been wondering
how to catch up with the Real World, and one of the things we've been
talking about is a closer cooperation with the various Linux and BSD
user groups.  I think it would be interesting to explore the
possibility of an amalgamation of AUUG and the Linux groups.

I can appreciate that this doesn't sound nearly as sexy as
incorporating LinuxSA, but consider the points I mentioned about.
Apart from this, people have raised a number of good reasons why
LinuxSA and AUUG should exist side by side.  I'll look at them below.

Here's an opportunity to beat chests.  I think David and I both had
the job of looking at this, and somehow it slipped between the
cracks.  There are two points to make here:

1.  We need to catch up with this as soon as possible.  I'd be very
    interested in constructive suggestions.  One possibility would be
    to lower individual membership fees and increase corporate fees,
    though I don't know what the rest of the Exec will have to say
    about that.  I'd be very open to discussion or suggestions.

2.  The fact that we haven't pursued this is that we don't have enough
    time to do our duties properly.  Believe me, the same will happen
    to LinuxSA after incorporation.
