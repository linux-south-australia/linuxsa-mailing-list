Re: etsa-caused ls process loop

Robert Thomas wrote:

> If you are looking for a linux friendly UPS I would suggest the APC range of
> products. The APC products are a known brand with great reliability and they
> offer linux software to perform an unattended shutdown and monitoring.

I actually have a beef with the process that APC use in their UPS
shutdown logic, and don't know whether it is a limitation of the
software, hardware or both.

The UPS logic kicks in after `x' minutes of being without power,
not as a percentage of remaining battery power.  This can (and
has) caused problems with the following scenario:

1)  Power drops, goes to UPS.  unix daemon kicks in with, say,
    20 minutes to shutdown (UPS lasts for 30 minutes).
2)  Power is restored after 15 minutes, shutdown is cancelled.
3)  Power dips out a minute later.  daemon is reset to shutdown again
    in 20 minutes, but UPS now has only 15 minutes of runtime left.
4)  15 minutes later, computer grinds to a halt.
