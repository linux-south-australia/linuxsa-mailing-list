Re: disable cron mailing

David Lloyd wrote:

> Better to write:
> 
> cmd.sh &> /dev/null
> 
> And then all stderr will goto /dev/null as well. You'd be surprised how
> many utilities waffle to stderr when they're not really telling you
> errors :-(

You'd be surprised how often something vitally important gets
redirected to /dev/null too.  I had a customer last month who hadn't
had a successful backup in months, only because the cron job had
stdout and stderr redirected to /dev/null, and the script didn't
check that the backup utility had terminated successfully.  They
were blissfully unaware.
