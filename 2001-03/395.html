Re: Telstra ADSL problems?

On Mon, Mar 12, 2001 at 12:52:57PM +1100, Daryl Pilkington wrote:

 > I suspect your problem is identical to mine as iPrimus ADSL uses the
 > same infrastructure as Telstra ADSL.

Not entirely true.  iPrimus, Telstra (and pretty much everyone else) 
use the same DSLAM network, but that's about it.  Different ISPs use
their own sources of upstream bandwidth, for example -- If Telstra's
is congested or broken you might find that Telstra ADSL sucks while
their competitors are fine.

For what it's worth, I've seen reports on aussie-isp this morning of
2-second round-trip-times and high levels of packet loss across one of
the Australia - US links from a Telstra ADSL customer who ran tests
over the weekend.

 > David Rowlands wrote:
 > > Anyone else on Telstra ADSL having problems accessing international
 > > sites? I can't get to slashdot.org amonst others, yet via normal dial
 > > up, it works.
 > > Australian sites all work. Telstra's service status at last check says
 > > everything is ok.

Telstra's service status pages *always* say everything is ok.  This is
because fault reporting group employees don't have sufficient privileges
to update the status pages, which means they only get updated by Big
Pond Direct engineering staff *after* faults have been fixed, if at all.
I find they're only ever useful for finding out when scheduled maintenance
will be carried out, and even then they're sometimes lacking...
