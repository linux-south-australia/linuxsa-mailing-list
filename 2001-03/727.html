On Wed, Mar 21, 2001 at 03:24:26AM -0800, Ian McLeod wrote:

 > Why is there not more lobying?  Not more letters to the papers?  I
 > see plenty about the plight of farmers and the occasional one about
 > our right to online privacy - but zilch on subsidies and tax
 > concessions for ISPs and communication infrastructure expenditure. 
 > Especially with our dollar so low.

Might have something to do with the fact that farmers are unprofitable to
the point of bankruptcy, and they don't have the skills which would make
them employable in other sectors.

ISPs are profitable and their employees are "portable".  The social and
economic impact of the death of an ISP is not the same as the impact caused
by any given farmer deciding to leave the land.

Let's be realistic here:  You're demanding cut-price Internet access as
if it's your god-given right, completely ignoring that two thirds of
the world's population has NEVER SEEN A TELEPHONE.  I'm not quite sure
that you're employing the perspective necessary to see the stunning 
difference between what you can run out and get for $80 per month, and
what someone at a different longitude can't buy no matter how rich they
are.
