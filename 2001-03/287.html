I am not a llama, but I believe that for it to be criminal, there would have
to be some wilful negligence in not maximising profits. For eg: assume you
have two competing pizza bars, publicly owned. One decided to go online, and
one decided to stay as it was. Assume the online one made a lot of money
through this venture. Just because the other didn't do that, doesn't make
them criminals. They had considered the costs and benefits, and made the
wrong decision. I'm certain that it could be argued in court that Telstra
reducing QoS is either good or bad for profit, depending on what you are
trying to prove.
