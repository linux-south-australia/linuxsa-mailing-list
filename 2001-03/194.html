I've always held the belief that you use cron's email mechanism
for "exceptional conditions"; ie something that you hadn't
anticipated and trapped for in the script gets mailed to you.
Otherwise, you'd be forever error checking in the shell script
(instead of just the important components).  Of course, you
could also just shift to a language that support exceptions.
