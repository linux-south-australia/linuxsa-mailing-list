In the US most consumer services are unlimited usage.  My cable modem is
$40/month for a 3mbit/128kbit connection.  The business package is
identical, differing only in the name.  It took Adelphia (my provider) 8
months to implement...

ADSL locally (Vermont, USA) is provided only by the phone company at the
moment.  They are loathe to give out actual connection rates, come to
think of it, most broadband providers are.  Instead you can be guaranteed
that your ADSL is "17x faster than a 56k, 3x faster than ISDN" and cruft
like that.  Its actually 640kbit/90kbit.  For $40/month.  As you get to
bigger cities, the options are better -- faster, and cheaper (relatively
speaking)
