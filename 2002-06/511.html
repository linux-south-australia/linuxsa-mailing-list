spam, application/octet-stream

I have been getting spam in my inbox with a Content-Type of
"application/octet-stream", which is sent along with a text/html and
text/plain.. this is the only spam that gets through spamassasin for me
so far (actually, I don't get any other spam anyway.. so I removed
spamassassin) and I have been trying to write a procmail rule to deal
with this spam, but have so far been unsuccessful.

My first instinct was to filter out any mail with content type of
application/octet-stream, but I couldn't end up getting this to work..
after searching a while on google I still couldn't work out how to do
it, but now have doubts whether this is the right way to go about it
anyway.. after all, there may be legitimate use of the content type?

I was wondering if anyone else had thoughts on this matter? Below I have
included an example of the messages I see in mutt:

Content-Type: application/octet-stream;
        name=a[7].htm
		Content-Transfer-Encoding: base64
		Content-ID: <Z2a539922ME0S0i57K>
