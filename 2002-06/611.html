Unless I have missed an email, it seems no-one has mentioned resize2fs(8), 
which allows an ext2fs file system to be "grown" if the partition can be 
extended. 

I have used it and it worked well for me. If you move one partition at a time 
from your old hard drive to the new, you can then use fdisk to delete the 
partition you just moved, re-create the partition at a larger size (see man 
page for appropiate warning about starting partition at exactly the same 
place), the resize2fs to gain useage of the extra space.

I have never tried it with a ext3 partition, but seeing as ext3 is ext2 with 
a journal, I do not see why it would not work. No idea about reiserfs though.
