Debian/Slackware pretty much force a "RAWR, IM YOUR SYSTEM, LOOK AT ME" kind
of approach whereas with Redhat/Mandrake users will generally point and
click everything using linuxconf or the likes and think they have total
control over everything.

In my opinion it is good practice to try a distro like Debian/Slackware,
solely for the completely different approach to things.

I'm far from anti-Redhat; given a decent sysadmin that DOESN'T like doing
--force or --nodeps with rpm's (suicide, I tell ya), a redhat system is
actually pretty nice.  Understand packaging, respect it, and it'll serve you
well.
