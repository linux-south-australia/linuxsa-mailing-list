> "Trademark or servicemark"? Nice subtle one there <G>

Not sure that I quite get it, to be honest... too subtle, perhaps...

the ® character means either a "Registered TradeMark" or a "Registered
Service Mark" -- registered with the USPTO. If it's not registered,
people use "TM" or "SM" instead. The only difference is whether the
mark refers to a good or a service. In Windows' case, I'm supposing
that MS is trying to turn it into a service anyway... It's becoming a
blurred distinction these days anyway.
