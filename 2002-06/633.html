RE: Compiling NKFS into the Kernel

Monday, June 24, 2002, 2:08:52 PM, James wrote:

> I am clueless as to what to do. Do I modify the c files? Do I change the
> sytax of the make command. How the heck do you copy a file to a line 
> that looks like a command?

> "Now copy /boot/vmlinuz.version.h to /lib/modules/`uname 
> -r`/build/include/linux/version.h"

Notice the back ticks `uname -r` around the uname -r portion of the
path? They tell they shell to execute this command and substitute it's
output where the command was specified.

On my system: uname -r returns: 2.4.18
that being my kernel version. So the above copy command would in
effect be:

cp /boot/vmlinuz.version.h /lib/modules/2.4.18/build/include/linux/version.h

Specifiying `uname -r` means you don't have to lookup and then type
out your kernel version.

This sort of construct is usually used to make scripts more portable.

Hope that helps.
