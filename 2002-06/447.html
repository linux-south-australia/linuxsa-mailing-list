Re: Spam Assassin/Prevention

You can make spamassassin (via procmail) pre-process your mail and 
then leave it in your normal in-box for your usual pop3/imap client 
to use.

I do this myself, right now. Works a treat.

* Just set up a procmail recipe like this in your .procmailrc:

PATH=/bin:/usr/bin:/usr/local/bin
MAILDIR=$HOME/mail
LOGFILE=$HOME/procmail.log
VERBOSE=on

# spamassassin
INCLUDERC=$HOME/.procmail/rc.spamassassin

* and an rc.spamassassin file like this:

:0fw
| spamassassin -P -c /usr/share/spamassassin

:0e
{
     EXITCODE=$?
}



* and use a .forward file like this:

"|exec /usr/local/bin/procmail"


... then it all works nicely (assuming you have procmail and 
spamassassin installed first, of course :) )

You can then use the labelling of the spam that spamassassin does, to 
trigger simple filtering rules in your mail client to move the 
probable spam into a separate folder or just delete it or whatever 
you want.

You can also run it systemwide out of sendmail or postfix or whatever 
suits, if you want to 'protect' more than one username. Information 
on doing this is at http://www.spamassassin.org/faq.html

(check the section on filtering mail for a whole network)

Cheers,
Simon

(a recent, and very happy, convert to using spamassassin. its scarily 
effective - at last!)
