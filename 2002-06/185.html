Quoting Martin Sandiford <ms@mcdev.com.au>:
> 
> Maybe 99.99999% (OK, slight exaggeration here) of all TCP/IP stacks are
> based on the BSD implementation.  This is why they all have the same
> bugs :)  Microsoft didn't start shipping an OS with a native TCP/IP
> stack until circa 1993 IIRC.

Umm, isn't the Linux TCP/IP stack a clean room implementation, last rewritten 
by Alan Cox while he was at Swansea University?  

If I recall my Linux history correctly, Linux's TCP/IP originally sucked badly 
_because_ it was a clean room implementation, and it wasn't until Alan put his 
box on the university's backbone that he was able to debug most of the problems.

Why didn't they use BSD's TCP/IP stack?  Again IIRC it was because of AT&T's 
claim over the BSD code base at that point in time.  Fortunately since then it 
has all resolved, and now we have 2 excellent open-source TCP/IP stacks.
