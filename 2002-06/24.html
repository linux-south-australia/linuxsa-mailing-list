> The locale "C" is the default locale, or international area. Funnily
> enough, the locale "C" has things like American spelling and other weird
> stuff. Other locales you might see in Australia are:
> 
> en-uk
> en-us
> en-au
> 
> Something about your locale setup has gone particularly haywire but I
> don't know exactly *what*.
> 
> DSL
> 

In fact it should be en_UK and en_US and en_AU but setting the
environment variable like so.....

export LC_ALL=en_US

in .profile or /etc/profile should fix it. You will need to "source" the
profile first before it will take effect of course.
