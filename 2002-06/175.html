Re: Server VGA cards

Richard Russell wrote:
> What is people's opinion on good, commonly available VGA cards
> appropriate for use in servers

Here's the ones that come to mind (ordered oldest->newest):
- Cirrus Logic cards (often buggy BIOS, but widely supported)
- Matrox Millenium (expensive new, but should be cheap 2nd hand now)
- Tseng Labs ET6000 (quite good 2D, good compatibility)
- S3 series (average, but early models are well supported)
- ATI Rage series (common in servers a few years ago)
- nVidia RIVA TNT2 (good budget card)

Some others that are good and now widely supported, but might not work 
with old OSs:
- Intel i810 series (very common in workstations)
- nVidia GeForce2 MX (excellent performance)
- ATI Radeon (good performance, reportedly has good open source drivers)

If I were looking for a card that is likely to work on most operating 
systems, I'd have a look at the following:
- XFree86 3.x driver list
- Solaris x86 hardware compatibility list

It's not a coincidence that all the cards listed in the first list are 
not easy to find new.  I've noticed TNT2 M64s being sold new, but these 
aren't as good as the original TNT2s.  To get any of these, I think 
you'd need to look second-hand (perhaps the computer trader).

I hope this helps.
