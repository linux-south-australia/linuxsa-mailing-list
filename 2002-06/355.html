>Bascially you need to run the BGP exterior routing protocol to
>BPA, asking them to feed you the BPA routes (and maybe default).
>Set the BGP NO_EXPORT community on your address space announcement
>to BPA.
>
>Then get a default route to Netspace, either through BGP (if
>you want to use BPA as a fallback) or static.
>
>The NAT isn't a problem.
>
>You will need to hold enough address space to not be filtered
>by BPA's BGP filters.  How much address space that is will
>need to be negotitated with BPA.  It's quite possible that
>BPA won't come to the party for a domestic customer, especially
>one who isn't going to send much traffic.  This attitude also
>makes some support and technical sense.
