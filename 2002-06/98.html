Re: Linux Information

Something like Redhat/Mandrake is great for getting into Linux if you want
to learn it;  once you're comfortable with your box, then Debian or
Slackware are great distro's to really get your hands dirty, and they give
you far more control over everything that¹s on your box.  Each distro
appeals to one person more then another, so its more of a case of
try-and-learn, really.

If you just want a linux gateway up and going, checkout a web based linux
distro such as smoothwall.  It'll be very easy to setup, but it won't teach
you much about linux.

I've always found the best way for novices to learn Linux is to have a guru
friend install a distro for them, that¹s reasonably easy to use, and start
teaching them bit by bit until they're confident enough to go prawling
around their system by theirself.
