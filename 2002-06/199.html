Re: Server VGA cards

Good old Tseng Labs ET4000 and ET6000 cards -- I've still got one in my main
Linux server, and i don't intend to change if i can help it.

If I was buying a new card I reckon Matrox make quite consistant quality
cards? I can't say the same for the ultra-budget nVidia-based cards.
A non-budget nVidia could be nice, but perhaps a bit pricey for your needs.
They do provide quite decent picture quality in my opinion -- much better
than the cheapo S3 and SiS cards. Driver support is much better for nVidia
and Matrox cards. Some S3 and SiS cards don't even seem to have any drivers
at all, I swear! (From my attempts to use them in certain linux boxes)

If you're spending a couple of grand on a server, its probably just better
to pay the extra $40 to $70 to get a "Decent" card, which has known working
drivers, and just run with it. By the way, X does support nVidia out of the
stock distro, just in 2D only. For a server thats probably the way to go?

Anyway seeya

Toby
