ldap woes...

Hi All,

  Ok, I've obviously done something stupid, but due to the flu I'm
  still getting over, can't work out what it is.

  I installed and configured OpenLDAP the other day. Added a heap of
  people in three organisations using objectClass=person and
  objectClass=inetOrgPerson.

  Having done that, I could use my email client to query the ldap
  server and retrieve info about the users I'd added.

  All very cool :)

  However, I couldn't get things like squid and ssh to use these
  entries to authenticate against. It seems I needed
  objectClass=posixAccount type entries. So I added another
  organisation (Accounts) and added my users under that with
  objectClass=posixAccount type entries (from the nis.schema).

  Yay, authentication worked :)

  However, the email client no longer retrieved the entries it used to
  - in fact, it doesn't retrieve anything.

  A look in the logs shows that the email account makes the following
  query:

Jun 24 22:38:42 eric slapd[21130]: daemon: conn=10 fd=9 connection from IP=172.16.1.5:4930 (IP=0.0.0.0:389) accepted.
Jun 24 22:38:42 eric slapd[22045]: conn=10 op=0 BIND dn="" method=128
Jun 24 22:38:42 eric slapd[22045]: conn=10 op=0 RESULT tag=97 err=0 text=
Jun 24 22:38:42 eric slapd[21149]: conn=10 op=1 SRCH base="dc=viking,dc=home" scope=2 filter="(cn=*lea*)"
Jun 24 22:38:42 eric slapd[21149]: conn=10 op=1 SEARCH RESULT tag=101 err=0 text=
  
The root of my ldap tree is "dc=viking,dc=home", there's a lea user
at: cn=lea,o=family,dc=viking,dc=home
There's also a lea posixAccount entry at:
uid=lea,o=Accounts,dc=viking,dc=home

It returns neither.

What have I done?
Does the mere inclusion of nis.schema objects break anonymous
lookups?

Thanks for any clue in this matter.

Dave
