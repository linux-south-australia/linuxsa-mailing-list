Re: [OT] SCSI Drives, how hot is too hot?

>Anyway the two SCSI drives run very hot, almost too hot to touch, is this
>normal (Quantam drives BTW).

Seagate 10krpm drives are rated to run at 55 degrees C, according to their
specs.

By disturbing coincidence, the point at which human skin starts to melt is
also around 55 degrees C.

So .. if you touch your drive and your fingerprint seperates from your hand
and remains on it, it's probably too hot.
