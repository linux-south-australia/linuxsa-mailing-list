Hmm. I never use the real names . That way I can mix and match.
Richard is right though. If your after a particular realease and want
the stability that brings then use the "names".  (you will also avoid a
whole heap of side issues-- locales. fontsets etc)

I'm currently using "testing" and have never (after the first build)
upgraded my distribution, just edited /etc/apt/sources.list to show the
packages I'm after. (there are also some gnu packages I have in there as
well. )

If your after the more bleeding edge use "unstable" which works fine for
most people.(I moved away as I was upgrading stuff I didn't want to or
need to --I was doing some development work on CronosII and it was
becoming a library nightmare ith Gnome Gnome2 scrollkeeper etc.)
Generally though "The Big Step" is your Xserver moving from 3.X to 4.X
(I had a few problems, now all as very fine).
