Sorry, you lost me there....what are you referring to??

If you're referring to rolling back Samba from 2.2.4 to 2.2.1a its 
simply a matter of creating a new RPM from the source.  For a technophobe
this may sound difficult but the script is already there for you so if 
you're up to the task of typing "dir" at a DOS prompt then you can 
create your own samba RPMs.... ;P

ie: on Redhat

cp samba-2.x.x.tar.gz /tosomedirectory
cd /tosomedirectory
tar -zxvf samba-2.x.x.tar.gz
cd ./samba-2.x.x/packaging/Redhat
sh makerpms.sh

<go get a coffee depending on your PC spec>

when its finished.......

rpm -e samba    (to rollback the version you have installed)
rpm -ivh /usr/src/redhat/RPMS/i386/samba-2.x.x.i386.rpm

Voila, done.....

(of course you DID backup your /etc/samba directory didn't you??? ;)
