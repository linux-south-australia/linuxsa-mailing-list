Hi Jamie,

Two things to check that I can think of are:
- is it a valid CD-ROM in there?  (okay, dumb question :)
- does ls -l /dev/sbpcd show something like:
brw-r-----   1 root     disk      25,   0 Jul 13  1994 /dev/sbpcd
the important things are the "b" at the start of the line and the
"25, 0" in the middle.

If neither of those is the problem, I would boot the Slackware kernel
that you said worked, and see what happens then.  Perhaps the
Slackware kernel is earlier than 2.0.27 and something broke between
the two releases?

Also, what are the "whole lot of errors" that you said were displayed
when using the module?
