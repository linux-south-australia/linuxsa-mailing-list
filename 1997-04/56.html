Re: Help: .xinitrc

On Tue, 22 Apr 1997, I  wrote:

> > Hi there,
> > I have been editing my .xinitrc to customise my Xwindows, but when I 
> > startx, I get the following error:
> > 
> > blah..line 54.. /home/mael/.xinitrc: syntax error: unexpected end of file
> > 
> > But the last line (line 54) in my .xinitrc says:
> > 
> > 	exec fvwm
> > 
> > All my editing involved adding lines in the middle of the original file, and 
> > before that, it worked OK. 
> > Anybody have any ideas?

Geoffrey <geoffrey@netcraft.com.au> replied:

 Yes, it was something you did in the middle.  The actual last line is
 (mostly) irrelevant.  The error is "unexpected end of file" which
 probably means you placed something in the middle that causes bash to
 slurp lines until it finds something in particular.  Apparently you
 didn't put in there what it was looking for, so it spat the dummy.
 
 My guess is that you have an "if" without a "fi".

Steven Ellison <sellison@pc-connect.com.au> also replied:

Recheck your file to make sure you haven't caused any lines to line
wrap!  If you used pico this could be the cause.
