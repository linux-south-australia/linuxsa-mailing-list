Help: CD200F

Hi all,

I am having great difficulty trying to get my Creative CDROM drive (CD200F)
to work with RedHat 4.1, kernel 2.0.27.

It is connected to my SoundBlaster 16 card with the following specs.

		SoundBlaster interface:	0x220
                SoundBlaster IRQ:	5
                SoundBlaster low DMA:	1
                SoundBlaster high DMA:	5
                SoundBlaster MIDI:	0x330

  		CDROM interface:	0x230

I know these settings are correct because it works under windows 95
and (more importantly) Slackware linux (Slackware 96 and the older 2.3).

I have tried the following in Redhat with the following results:

	1) As a loadable kernel module
	******************************
	When I go to mount the cdrom, the module loads into the kernel
        and autoprobing begins. Autoprobing finds the correct drive (CD200F)
        and the inteface address (0x230).
        After it finds the drive and interface, a whole lot of errors get 
        printed to the screen and finally I get something like:
        "mount failed: /dev/sbpcd is not a valid block device"
        
        2) Compiled directly into the kernel
        ************************************
        I modified my sbpcd.h for the correct interface (0x230), set MAX_DRIVES
        to 1, set type to SoundBlaster (1) and set DISTRIBUTION to 0.
        I recompiled the kernal with the drive support directly in the kernel
        (Not as a module). 
        At boot up, my drive is detected and at the right interface.
        When I actually go to mount it, I get the same error as before 
        "mount failed: /dev/sbpcd is not a valid block device", and the drive
        ejectes and then immediately closes.

Sorry for going on, but ive installed linux before and I cant seem to work this
one out.

Any help appreciated.


Thanks,
 
Jamie
