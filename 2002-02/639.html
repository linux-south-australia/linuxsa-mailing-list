Re: [OT] PSTN specs for Australia & US

At 11:36 AM +1100 19/2/02, Daryl Pilkington wrote:
>Hi,
>PTSN specs may be of use to people looking to connect modems up which
>share the line with other devices, so I feel whilst this request is
>Off-Topic, it is of interest to all computer users.

The ACA can send you a copy of the telecommunications attachment 
standards in this country. Be prepared for a big wad of them. They 
don't publish them all on their web site but you can buy a set (we 
have).

Background information is available here:

http://www.aca.gov.au/labelling/telecom/index.htm

To legally attach the unit in Australia you need to have it certified 
(certification labs are noted at the URL above). Plan on around 
$6,000-$8000 to do the certification process, plus the cost of any 
needed hardware/software mods to bring the unit into compliance, plus 
the cost of subsequent partial re-testing.

We are in the process of doing this for a device we'll be providing 
with one of our future voice offerings to the Australian market. The 
device that does what we need comes from Europe, and we're putting it 
through that certification process right now - the costs I'm 
indicating are not guesses, they're actuals :(

Or you can break the law and take a (low) risk of incurring (huge) fines.

Your choice, but I thought I ought to point that out.

Much simpler to find a product already on the local market that does 
what you want, in your case, IMHO.
