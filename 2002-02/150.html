> A note to anyone doing this:  Make sure you change local
> carriers BEFORE
> you change ADSL providers.
>
> The reason is that when you change phone providers, Telstra's
> fscked-up
> billing system treats this as a disconnection, followed by an
> immediate
> reconnection through your new provider.
>
> Part of the automated stuff which happens during a disconnection is
> that your ADSL service is torn-down.  The cancellation results in an
> order being raised to their provisioning team to have a cable guy
> nip down to the exchange to physically un-jumper your line from the
> ADSL C-Mux.
>
> The reconnection which occurs several seconds later doesn't result in
> your ADSL service being lit-up again.  So the request to have
> your line
> unjumpered doesn't get cancelled.
>
> They don't tell ADSL ISP's that they've done this until their next
> bill arrives at the end of the month (when we see the billing mess
> resulting from the cancellations), so we have absolutely no way
> whatsoever of finding out that your ADSL service has stopped working
> until you've complained to us about it.  And, by the time that has
> happened, your line will have been unjumpered.
>
> If your exchange area has a lot of people waiting for ADSL
> installations,
> it's quite likely that your C-Mux port will have been assigned to
> someone else as well.
>
> The stupidest bit of the whole saga is that if Telstra has asked one
> of their people to unjumper your line, but that person hasn't
> confirmed
> that they've finished the job, their provisioning system still thinks
> you have ADSL enabled on your line.  Which means that if we
> submit another
> order to fix up the mess, they will *REJECT* it because you
> already have
> ADSL.  Even though it isn't working and they're in the process of a
> physical disconnection which they're going to have to reverse
> immediately
> afterwards.
>
> The moral of this story is that regardless of which ADSL
> provider you're
> using, you cannot change local phone providers without losing
> your ADSL
> service.  So, if you decide that you want to switch local
> phone carriers,
> do it *at the same time* as your ADSL provider change (because that's
> going to interrupt your ADSL service anyway, so you might as
> well do both
> interruptions at the same time).
>
> For very much the same reason, we can't switch someone from one of the
> current business plans to one of the new residential plans without
> interrupting the customer's service.  We'd love it if we
> could do that,
> but unfortunately the wholesale service the residential plans are
> implemented over is different from the one the business plans use
> (FlexStream for the business plans, the new ACCC-mandated
> PPTP offering
> for the residential ones), and the only way Telstra's provisioning
> system can cope with the change is to disconnect then
> reconnect migrating
> services.  So people switching from one to the other will
> probably lose
> service for a couple of days during the migration period.
