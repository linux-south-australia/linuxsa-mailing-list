Re: Linux-supported hardware

John Hall wrote:
 > [do these work under Linux?]
> 
> Western Digital 400EB hard disc
> Philips 107T monitor
> LG CED-8080B CD-RW drive
> Logitech S35 mouse

Generic components like this (hard disk, screen, CD drive, and so on) 
are almost always supported without a problem.  It's things like video 
cards, sound cards, modems, printers, and some peripherals that can take 
some tweaking to get them working.

I'm not sure about that mouse (as I've never heard of it), but assuming 
it's a pretty standard PS/2- or USB-compatible mouse, then it should 
work fine.

In truth, I would just get hold of an easy-to-use, up-to-date Linux 
distribution (perhaps Red Hat 7.2), and try installing it.

Hope this helps.
