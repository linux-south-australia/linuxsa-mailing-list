On Sun, 2002-02-03 at 21:30, Michael Kratz wrote:

> Generally speaking the more bandwidth people have available to them the more
> MB they chunk through in a month. Not simply because they download more,but
> also because it loads faster so they look at more, faster. So for 90% of
> people they will find that they end up using more on a DSL connection than
> they would on a modem connection. IMHO

Well, yes and no. I spend most of my days behind an Agile/Internode
microwave link[1], which seems to peak at around 400KBps. I rarely use
(my own machine) more than 500MB/month. That's probably increased a
touch now, seeing as I've setup a local FreeBSD source mirror.

If you spend your time downloading MP3s and DVDs, then yes, you're going
to go a little over the included MBs. However, if you're being
reasonable and rational with your Internet usage, it's easy to keep your
usage down.

   - andrew

[1] Which I'd highly recommend to anyone that wants a real Internet
    connection from an ISP that knows what "service" means.
