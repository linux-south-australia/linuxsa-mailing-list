I mean, seriously, how many residential users require the ability
to run an SMTP server?  On a dynamic IP address?  

I don't deny that there are some;  I also don't deny that the residents
of this mailing list are probably among the most likely to fit that
profile.  But we can't design a mass-market product around an edge-case
(especially when we provide a completely open alternative, at prices
which remain rational and competitive).

 > granted we can change ports etc, but as Mark Mills said, port 25 is a
 > problem
 
But ask yourself:  Is the difficulty involved in running an SMTP server
important enough to make the difference?  At the end of the day, that's
essentially the only thing you can't do as a result of us blocking ports.

We have spent a lot of time to come up with an outcome which is (a)
better, and (b) MUCH cheaper than any of our competitors.  It's
understandable that there are details which some people will decide
they don't like, but I'm pretty confident that we're still better than
anyone else in the marketplace.
