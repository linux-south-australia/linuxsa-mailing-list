OT: perm internet connections

so how do you get a permanent connection at an affordable cost?

The Internode ADSL at $70pm for 3Gb and 256/64k is fine, and
I signed up but it looks like I can't get ADSL (aarggggggghhhhhh!)

So I've been looking at what else I can get instead.  I've got
an ORHH already so I can have ISDN ok.  Or there's satellite.
Or of course stick with 56k modem.

But why does a perm connection using any of those methods cost
more than perm ADSl??  Even a "continuously" connected type
deal with dynamic IP might do - only problem is things like
12hr session limits add $10pm to the total cost just in redialing.

So why is a perm modem $55pm with Internode or Adam for 300Mb
of traffic (or $22pm with telstra BPD for 100Mb) and then somewhere
between 18-22c/Mb for traffic after that?

$55pm + $20pm (for the phone line) = $75pm but that's for 300Mb
not 3Gb!  I can't believe it really costs more for a plain old
modem link than ADSL.  Surely I'm missing something.......
(hopefully!)

The cheapest satellite BTW is roughly:
$22pm telstra BPD perm modem for backchannel
$20pm phone line for above
$40-50pm for 256-400k satellite downlink with around 3Gb data
so total approx $85pm (ignoring setup costs)
