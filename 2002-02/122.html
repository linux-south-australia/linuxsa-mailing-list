Technically it means the PPP session communicated via the PPPoE 
protocol will have an 18 hour session time limit configured into it.

Your connect may not last that long if you log your PPPoE session off 
earlier (for whatever reason), or if Telstra botch something major 
enough (for whatever reason).

If your session does run 18 hours continuous then as currently 
configured that session will then drop, and your trivial-to-write 
to-dialler script will then reconnect immediately.

Whether you get the same IP or a different one is a lottery. But 
planning for a different one would avoid surprise.

doesn't have any issues with MTU size, in the way that PPPoE does 
with some operating systems).
