One danger I could see of that, is that it creates a false sense of
security.

ie. The user goes to the "simulated attack" page, and at the end it says
everything was fine.

User then gets taken out by an exploit that hasn't been incorporated into
the security test page yet. Or else some custom job. or something.

User then complains that Internode said the user's firewall was good, and so
user trusted this expert information, but has now lost money (the secret
plans for their perpetual motion generator machine were stolen) due to the
info being wrong. User sues internode for an infinite amount of money.
(After all, those perpetual motion machine plans could generate an infinite
amount of power)

Only applies to lawyer-happy lusers, but Australia is slowly receiving some
infection in that area from America. You try to help people, and they turn
around and sue you..
