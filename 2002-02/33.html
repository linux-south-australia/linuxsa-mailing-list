Re: Internode Personal ADSL Service

On Fri, 1 Feb 2002, Mark Newton wrote:

> What else would you have us do?  We can't bury our heads in the sand;  
> Quite aside from the exposure that'd result for our customers, it'd also
> make us irresponsible network citizens.
> 

Not everyone needs to be treated like a gumby and be protected by their
ISP from the Big Bad Internet  :)

I know the effects that things like open relays, nimda, codered, customer
boxes getting hacked etc can cause an ISP from experience, but why treat
all customers the same?

It shouldnt be too hard for you to have seperate address ranges on your
system, the 'We will protect you' address range, and the 'Go for it, do
what you want, but dont cry to us if you get screwed over and sent a huge
bill' address range.

By default, give all customers addresses in the 'We will protect you'
range, but have an option for them to request a non-firewalled address.  
You can even charge a setup cost for this, just think about it, charge a
once of $50 fee for the address range transfer, you guys get more cash,
people get their open networks, everyone is happy.  *OR* you could be nice
and do this for free!
