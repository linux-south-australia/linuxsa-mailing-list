Re: Lappy Mem

Doesn't sound that hard to implement.
Allocate the specific memory address (some low level calls requiring
superuser access here) and then mlock() it so it can't be swapped out to
disk and used by something else.

Normally all your memory is "virtual" and doesn't have a linear mapping to
physical memory.. that'd be where the low level calls come in. Can't be too
hard though.. but hey, I'm not volunteering to do the mods ;)

It'd be funny that one of the Pros of Linux is that it has a bad memory
manager.. ;)

Q: "Hey man, how come your PC with faulty ram isn't crashing anymore?"
A: "I installed a bad-memory manager and that fixed it."

TC
