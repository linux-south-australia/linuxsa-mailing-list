Toby: Well that is probably true - but I think you've just given some
student a rude response with that. I also wasn't aware that they would
do that kind of research you mentioned at TAFE.

Troy: This is coming from someone who has had to apply for holiday work
over my time at uni. I mainly applied through the old fashion snail mail
process, but I would imagine these are a few good pointers for online
submissions:

- In your email detail out what you are after and what you can offer.
- Check and double check grammar and spelling please.
- Do some research and limit your queries to suitable organizations and
people.
- If you do speak to someone interested, explain what you've done in the
past and how that can benefit them in a particular job.

I think most employers will not be interested in just any student. I've
seen and heard of situations where a new hire did more harm than good
over their work experience period. Some amount of professionalism has to
be shown. If this is new to you, better learn fast.

Cheers,
