Heh. 11Mbps is the official, maximum-theoretical throughput of the link
that we have. These sort of links sometimes get called 10Mbps links for
the sake of sounding normal.

It's an 802.11b-based link, using Cisco/Aironet equipment.

In reality, it hovers around 3Mbps, depending on an array of different
things, including weather conditions, noise levels and the amount of
pigeons in the antenna :-)

I've seen these links working at around 4.0Mbps (~500KBps) over ~3km
using (IIRC) 24dBi antennas.

Perhaps now that I know that you claim to have a faster link we should
look at provisioning licensed microwave at say, 32Mbps?
