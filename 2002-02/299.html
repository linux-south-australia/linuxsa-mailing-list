Re: spam attack - please help!

One thing nobody has mentioned yet is that most distros (including
rh7.2) are not open relays by default. Did you modify your sendmail
configuration? If you didn't then there are 2 likely scenareos. 

1) You have a webserver with a cgi script with a security hole in it.
Some mail gateway cgis are known to have problems in old versions where
you can send mail to any user from the cgi, and so the mail originates
on your local box, and thus gets sent even without you being an open
relay.

2) You have been hacked and someone has modified your sendmail
configuration to turn it in to an open relay. 

Do you use sendmail? If not then the first thing to do would be to turn
it off. 

/etc/rc.d/init.d/sendmail stop

Next thing to do would be to run ntsysv or tksysv and turn off the
sendmail service so that it doesnt restart next boot.

Then you need to work out what happened. If you were hacked then you
need to find out exactly what has happened as they likely still have
access to your box.

Ant
