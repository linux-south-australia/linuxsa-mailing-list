Re: Internode Personal ADSL Service

Toby Corkindale wrote:

> It just seems a bit silly to pay-per-meg, when the link and the link
> maintenance costs, are basically setup+pay-per-time. I mean, its not like
> the coax or fibre or satellites will wear out slower if there's less data
> going over the cable, right?

It's not like that in Adelaide if you're outside the CBD.

An expensive dedicated link is required to Telstra to connect to their
ADSL network, and to bring sub-$100 ADSL residential pricing requires
oversubscription of such a link.

Using your ADSL connection like a 1.5Mbit Frame-Relay link at full
capacity 24/7 ends up costing the ADSL provider the same as a 1.5Mbit
Frame-Relay link, even if you're only consuming local traffic. Telstra
recommends oversubscription in the order of 200:1, so even if you get a
mammoth data cap, you might not be able to use it if 200 other users on
your DSL service want the same bandwidth.

Non-Telstra-hobbled DSL providers don't have any such requirement for
Telstra dedicated circuits, and don't have the same problem.
Unfortunately all of the wholesale competition
(Optus/XYZed/Worldcomm/AAPT/RequestDSL) at the moment is limited to the
CBD. If you do happen to be in the CBD, don't even bother with ADSL, ask
a local ISP (perhaps even me..) for HDSL.
