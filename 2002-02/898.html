Re: Squid Tuning

Andrew Reid wrote:

> > What sort of content are you caching? Remember, Squid *won't* cache what
> > makes up a large part of the Internet these days.
> 
> That is, dynamic content.

Most dynamic content is meant to impress in some way, and that usually
involves lots of other objects like graphics and stylesheets that should
have good cacheable qualities. These usually make up most of the
bandwidth of a web page request.

Unless someone is browsing dynamic sites with images turned off, a 99%
miss rate doesn't sound right. This could also be caused by one of those
annoying download managers use multiple range requests to retrieve a
single file, statistically dwarfing the hits in squid logs.

If it's running transparently or on port 80, it could also be reporting
Nimda/Codered requests as a MISS.
