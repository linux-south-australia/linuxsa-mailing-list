> I'm thinking that the answer may be in editing /usr/ports/Mk/bsd.port.mk
>
> Has anyone done this before ?

This may be what your trying to do... I have a master repository here at
work called sanctuary that caches all the distfiles we use here on campus.
I then add this into every machines /etc/make.conf file:

MASTER_SITE_BACKUP?=\
	ftp://sanctuary.magill.unisa.edu.au/pub/FreeBSD/ports/distfiles/${DIST_SUBDIR}/ \
        ftp://ftp2.au.freebsd.org/pub/FreeBSD/ports/distfiles/${DIST_SUBDIR}/ \
        ftp://ftp.au.freebsd.org/pub/FreeBSD/ports/distfiles/${DIST_SUBDIR}/ \
        ftp://ftp.freebsd.org/pub/FreeBSD/ports/distfiles/${DIST_SUBDIR}/
MASTER_SITE_OVERRIDE?=${MASTER_SITE_BACKUP}

The client then checks their own /usr/ports/distfiles folder and if it
isnt there, checks sanctuary, ftp2.au, ftp.au and finally ftp before
trying the original master site listed in the Makefile.  I then have it
copy any new distfiles back to sanctuary for future use but that bits
optional :)
