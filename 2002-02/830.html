Re: any downside to USB keyboards?

THey have some upsides..
For instance, you can plug the USB mouse directly into the keyboard, thus
avoiding some excess cabling. Some PCs and monitors have front-mounted USB
plugs too, which in some arrangements can reduce cable mess.

Also, USB keyboards allegedly don't suffer from the inability to work with
some combinations of keys. (This is something gamers care about.. most
normal people wouldn't mind nor notice that once you press, for eg, LCtrl,
LShift, Z, S, W and space simultaneously something gets lost. ;) )

Also, USB keyboards shouldn't suffer from the problem of not working when
you unplug and replug them. PS/2 and AT/DIN style keyboards frequently get
stuffed up if you plug them into a hot system. So, if you have a pile of
headless machines, and just have a single monitor and keyboard (but no KVM
switch), then usb would be better for you.

And lastly, I believe you can do away with the keyboard interrupt! Yay ;)
(Not sure if current motherboards support this though?)
It sucks having only 0xf interrupts, and having so many of them taken up by
legacy devices. (eg. com1, com2, keyboard, ps/2 mouse, printer port)

Next time i buy a new keyboard, I will get a USB version.

hope this helps,

-Toby
