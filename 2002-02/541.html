Re: data transmission - which way does it go?

Quoting Shane Warner <shane_warner@prontomail.com>:

> Can someone tell me, when I am on my LAN, if I have my pop client 
> looking at my server using it's fully qualified domain name, if 
> anything is brought down from that server, is it going to go out to 
> the internet and back into the workstation, or is it going to realise 
> that we are really talking about another computer on the LAN and take 
> the short way?

Depends on your configuration.

If your workstation's default nameserver is configured to a machine on your
LAN, and said machine contains host information for your server and is
correctly configured to recognize local requests, then the query should
never leave the LAN.

If your default nameserver is configured for a machine outside of your LAN,
such as your ISP's nameserver, and if the result is not in your machine's
name cache, the query will go to your ISP.

If you're especially paranoid about lookups going to the internet, you can
add static entries to your /etc/hosts file for each machine on your network.
You should, however, set up a local forwarding nameserver (which can respond
to local request and forward other requests on) and configure your
workstations to use it.
