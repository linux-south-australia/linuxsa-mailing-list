On Thu, 14 Feb 2002, Damian Reed wrote:
> I tried the Ctrl-Alt-Keypadplus and minus and it did not switch between 
> resolutions.
> Does XF86Config have a command to test configs like M64Config on Unix does?

What distribution of Linux are you using? Are you using XFree86 3.x or
4.x?

Did some autoconfiguration tool show you a list of resolutions that were
limited to 640x480?

Sometimes an incorrect detection or choice of settings for the video card
and/or monitor can limit what resolution options you have. Where in fact
you can have a higher resolution. In that case, if 640x480 is the highest
resolution placed in your X configuration file then no ammount of
CTRL+ALT+"+" etc... will help you.

What monitor and video card do you have? What settings does the setup
detect, or what settings are you entering?
