I'm on Telstra BigPond Direct, and the overseas link has been rather poor
for the last two weeks or so. However, even when it's good, the latency is
still 200+ for me. The recent overseas slowdown seems to be at the US end, so
it's probably not Telstra's fault.

When I was working at SE-Net, back when they had a 100 MBit Connect and a 10
MBit UUNet link, the overseas pings were still 200+. Download rates varied
considerably, but were usually above the 50 kilobyte/sec range.

> Do ADSL users find that their latency is much lower?
To local game servers, yes. To overseas ones, sometimes. Download speeds are
universally much quicker, unless there's a bottleneck somewhere at their end.

For instance, KeenSpot (host of many online comics) is always very slow, but
I expect that it's very slow to our US cousins, as well.

PlanetMirror and mirror.aarnet are _much_ quicker than over dialup :)
