Re: [OT] PSTN specs for Australia & US

michael wrote:

> Hmm. I was stripping the wires on a phone line one day when I was still
> young and stupid, using my teeth. The phone must have rung. I can verify
> that the voltage is a lot higher than a 9volt battery to the toungue! I
> would guess at least 30 volts.

On hook, it's -48 VDC (that's negative with respect to ground).  Off hook,
this falls to around -8 VDC or so[1], depending a lot on line characteristics
and load of the off hook telephone.  A ring signal is something like 90VAC
at 25Hz superimposed over the DC bias (which is how the exchange detects
that the phone has gone off hook and stops the ringing signal).  (All this
is bound to be wildly inaccurate due to deterioting brain cells).  Modern
electronic phones are built with so much tolerance these days that they'll
respond to anything that comes vaguely close.

[1]  Hence the problems with T200 touch phones sapping power from
the line when off hook for too long.
