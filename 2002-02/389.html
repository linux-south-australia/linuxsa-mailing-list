Insurmountable problems with PCMCIA and WLAN card on a PC CHIPS mobo and Linux

Hi,

Over the last few days I have been struggling with a PCMCIA problem on a
Linux machine.

This problem exists for 2.4.17 and 2.2.20, so it seem not to be version
specific as I initially thought.

I am trying to get a WebGear WLAN card working on a PCI-only PC CHIPS
mobo (an M810-LR) or something like that.

I have a TI1410-based CardBus bridge card, and the i82365 driver loads
OK for that card, but complains that IRQ10 (or IRQ11, wherever it is)
can't be tested.

It also does not try to test any ISA interrupts.

Then when the WLAN driver (ray_cs) loads, it complains that it could not
get an IRQ (RequestIRQ: Resource temporarily busy) or something like that.

I have fiddled with settings in /etc/sysconfig/pcmcia (this is a
RedHat-based setup), but to no avail.

Can anyone suggest my next approach? Is this remediable?
