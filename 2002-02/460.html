Re: squid

Damien Wilson wrote:

> Is there a way in squid to limit the amount of logging that it does or am I better off just rotating the logs every week or so?

Squid has a built in log rotating system that will keep your cephalopod
happy by not messing with it's file descriptors.

First, add to the config:

logfile_rotate 7

To keep 7 backup logs.

Then add the following to crontab to run during an off-peak time:

/path/to/squid/bin/squid -k rotate

It is pretty safe to turn off store.log, but access.log keeps a lot of
useful information that will help track down abuse, and measure your
squids performance.
