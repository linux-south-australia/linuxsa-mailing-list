The FreeBSD pppd contains its own masquerading code. We're using it at home
on our Telstra DSL link, and it works quite well. "man pppd" for more info
on this neat feature (search the page for the -nat switch), and take
a look at:

http://www.freebsd.org/doc/en_US.ISO8859-1/books/handbook/ppp-and-slip.html

If you're on something other than a PPP link, you'll need to set up an ipfw
ruleset and a NAT daemon, which is slightly more difficult.

There's a fair amount relevant information in the FreeBSD Handbook. See:

http://www.freebsd.org/doc/en_US.ISO8859-1/books/handbook/index.html
