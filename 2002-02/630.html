Re: Linux-supported hardware

John Hall wrote:
> 
> I am looking to buy a new computer - my present one is a couple of years old now and hard drive capacity is getting tight.  Also, I am no longer satisfied with my backup system (LS120 drive - only just able to cope).
> 
> I expect to install Mandrake Linux (as well as, temporarily I hope, Windows 98) and assume I should check on 'www.linux-mandrake.com' for supported hardware.  Could somebody please tell me the bits and pieces I should check for Linux compatibility (eg sound card, video card, motherboard, etc).
> 

Everything!
The best place to start is make a list of what you have in your machine
then check out http://www.redhat.com/hardware to see if it's supported.
I know your installing mandrake but redhat and mandrake support the same
sort of things.

Tick off everything you have in your list and then you'll know you have
a linux compatiable system.
