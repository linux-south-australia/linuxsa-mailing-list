along those lines...

The VIA C3 (www.via.com.tw) processor is specifically designed to run
cool. A rep at a trade show is known to have run a C3 866MHz system with
no heatsink or fan for a few days without sacrificing any speed or
reliability. It consumes something like 10W. The trade-off is FPU
performance, which is about half or a third of an equivalent PIII. VIA
also make the EZRA platform (or is it EDEN? -- the website isn't
responding to me atm), which is a highly integrated motherboard and CPU
(no socket -- it's welded in) designed for low temp etc... If you're
into this kind of thing, and ultimate performance isn't your goal... VIA
are pretty good... (I have a C3 866MHz with a heatsink and no fan, and
I've had it compiling for hours, and the heatsink gets warm to the
touch, but that's all)
