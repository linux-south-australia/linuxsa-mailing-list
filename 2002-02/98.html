Re: Internode Personal ADSL Service

Quoting "nik [tm]" <tmcruisin@sond.com.au>:

> > But ask yourself:  Is the difficulty involved in running an SMTP server
> > important enough to make the difference?  At the end of the day, that's
> > essentially the only thing you can't do as a result of us blocking ports.
> 
> If you know that everyone can sidestep this by changing ports the services
> run on?? why bother?
Because of open relays. If you have a misconfigured mailserver which is 
running on a non-standard port, it is unlikely that a spammer will find it.
However, if you're running an open relay on port 25, your server will be
abused until you notice the increased log entries/traffic or your ISP tells
you about it.

Every time an open relay popped up at SE-Net, it took a couple of hours
of a system administrator's time to block the relay, contact the customer,
explain to them what an open relay is (and sometimes what a mailserver is)
and how one can be fixed. Now, imagine that on a residential customer scale
instead of the limited business customer scale.

> you are basically making a lot of extra work for no-real-reason. with the
> end result of one blocked service.
> isnt it a better option to setup a port scan so you can check and advise
> your clients of possible vunerabilities - (after all you are soo concerned
> about being responsible network citizens)
They're also concerned about responsibility for _your_ benefit: 
an open relay or a compromised system (think Code Red) can rack up a very
large bandwidth bill.
