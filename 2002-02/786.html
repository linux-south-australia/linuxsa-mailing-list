OK, here starts the pedantry :)

There's not necessarily a correlation between being a Linux Newbie and
having a lack of technical aptitude.

In fact, in my limited experience the people who choose to use Linux aren't
usually complete turnips at all - for one thing, they will have usually
already assessed Windoze and decided that it doesn't meet their needs.  This
is based on the pretense that most new retail PC's are sold with Windoze
preinstalled, excepting those which are set up for other people by real
Linux enthusiasts.  However it takes a certain amount of savvy to
successfully make one's way through a Linux install (although decreasingly
so as improvements happen, so prepare for greater numbers of turnips to jump
ship...), so I'd venture a guess that if they're intelligent enough to do
that then they can certainly search a website archive.

The mailing list info page states "HTML posts may be ignored and are usually
not well accepted." (and then contradicts itself further down the page), so
there's no definitive statement that says "Thou shalt not post HTML"...  If
it's that disagreeable, either insist that the list moderator/administrator
filter out HTML mailings, or go join a list which does.
