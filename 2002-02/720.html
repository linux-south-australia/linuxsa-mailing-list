Sanka,

You can open your /etc/mgetty+sendfax/mgetty.config and look for the line that describes your modem init. Mine looks like this:

port ttyS0
  init-chat "" ATZ OK AT&F1M0E1Q0S0=0 OK
  answer-chat "" ATA CONNECT \c \r
  debug 3
  data-only y


using US robotics. Replace the init-chat line with the appropriate initializations of you modem.

BTW, when no one is connected on your ttyS0, it will report something like this:
	init chat failed, exiting...: Interrupted system call
	failed in mg_init_data, dev=ttyS0, pid ... bla bla blaa.
on your /var/log/messages. I guess it's because your mgetty continues to search for connections or some kind (not very sure). ;)

Hope that helps.
