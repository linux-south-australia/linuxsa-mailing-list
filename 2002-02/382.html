Re: Mail Redundancy

On Wed, 2002-02-06 at 12:02, Anthony Symons wrote:

> Have 2 of them, and set up 2 mail servers giving priority to the main
> one in your DNS mail records. Main one goes down, the sending mail
> server will fall over to the second one.

That's no good if you still want to be able to access your mail when the
server goes down. It may guarantee mail delivery, but it certainly
doesn't guarantee that your current e-mail will remain available during
a failure, which is the more ideal situation.

There was a thread on this a little while ago on the FreeBSD-ISP list
which included load-balancing in the equation.

To the end-user, mail is more than an SMTP service. Mail is POP, IMAP,
SMTP and possibly web-mail. -All- of those services should be available
during failure of one of the servers.

DNS is one aspect of a fault-tolerant mail system, but there are other
levels that need to be addressed too. It's going to boil down to how
much you need your mail, and how much not having it will cost you.
