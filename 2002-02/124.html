Re: dsl "modems" what's the diff

Very little difference in practice. Specifically, very very minor 
feature differences (if any), and identical actual performance - the 
latter driven by strong and effective standardisation of chipsets and 
standards.

This is part of why xDSL is so much nicer the modems. xDSL simply 
doesn't have the 'your modem doesn't like my access server' issues. 
They work or they don't. Absolutely bloody wonderful, that.

The DSL-300 is the current popular choice in our customer base for 
the sensible reason that its cheaper, and works just as well, as the 
alcatel ST home.
