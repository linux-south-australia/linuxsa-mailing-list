Re: ext3 vs. reiserfs

On 2002.02.01 14:39 "Wilkinson,Alex" wrote:
> 
> glen.t:>>Reliability: ext2; then reiserfs, ext3; then xfs, jfs.
> 
> 
>   What makes you say that ext2 is more reliable than ext3 ?

The ext3 code is less tested.  These sort of "disk rot"
bugs are hard to find and almost impossible to fix (even
backups don't help unless you detect the rot within a
backup cycle).

You've got to weigh that against a meta-data corruption
on a uncleanly halted ext2, something ext3 will save
you from.

However the ext2 fsck will at least find this corruption
at the next boot, giving you the opportunity to correct
the data whilst backups are still current.

So I might run ext3 on my laptop, which has a good
chance of being improperly shut down, and ext2 on
a server where the data is of more value.

When Red Hat's bugzilla and linux-kernel have seen no
"ext3 data corruption" messages for a while, then I'd
migrate all the servers from ext2 to ext3.  In the
long term, say mid 2003, I'd expect to use xfs on the
servers and probably the laptop too.
