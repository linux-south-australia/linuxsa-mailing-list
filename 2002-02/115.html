For what it's worth, after the initial couple of months, i haven't
downloaded even a gig of "accountable data" per month since.
(My housemates, on the other hand, would love to have more than 1gb each and
are happy to reach into the spare bandwidth if left over)

The thing with having fast links is that it feels like a bit of a waste to
not be able to use it.. I'd like to be able to come home and connect to a
high-quality streaming radio station, and leave that running until evening
-- but i can't, because that chews up way too much of the quota.
Or the other day, I downloaded one CD of the mandrake beta for a friend who
wanted to genuinely test it out (it wasn't just for some sort of
zero-day-leetness) -- however thats almost 3/4 of the monthly quota for me,
gone in 4 hours.

It just seems a bit silly to pay-per-meg, when the link and the link
maintenance costs, are basically setup+pay-per-time. I mean, its not like
the coax or fibre or satellites will wear out slower if there's less data
going over the cable, right?

Anyway, this has strayed far enuf off the original topic.


Good on Internode for providing some competition, anyway! ;)
