Ian,

One way to discover the answer to this sort of question is to search for
the file that starts the process.  You can use ps to find the name of the
process and then use find/grep to search for the file that starts it.
If the process is only started when you login (ie not for all users)
then your likely to find it in the ~/.kde directory; otherwise it's
likely to be in /etc.

I know I'm going to regret saying so, but what I just outlined is the
unix way of doing things.  Asking a mailing list which button to click
is the way the other people do it; they don't want to learn how to use
their computer.
