Re: Socket programming question

Quoting Richard Sharpe <rsharpe@richardsharpe.com>:
> 
> So, it sounds like you are not proposing to use the standard approach of:
> 
>   loop:
>     listen();
>     accept();
>     fork();
>     if (parent)
>      goto loop;
>     else
>      process_child();
> 
> The only clues I have here are that accepting connections, while not on 
> the TCP fast path, is likely to be much less expensive than forking and 
> the attendant copy-on-write behaviour. However, if you have a large number 
> of clients being non-persistent, you might want to consider threads or a 
> process pool.

I already have threads in the server, although not serving requests for 
connecting clients - I was trying to avoid that since that would require me 
guarding the queue of work with semaphores etc which adds some complexity that 
I was hoping to avoid.

If I can determine that the number of clients waiting to be accept()'ed is 
small, then I'll go and optimise something else (that I can identify with a 
profiler).  If the queue is staying full I'll go the threaded receiver 
approach.  I'd just like to be able to tell before committing myself to more 
complexity :-)
