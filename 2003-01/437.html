Re: duplicating a hard drive

David Fitch wrote:
> I'm not sure PC's can boot from secondary devices on the IDE
> controller, you can set IDE0 then IDE1 in modern BIOSes
> but that would be hda and hdc (ie. primary devices on first
> and second controllers).
>

My HP 6425 box is set up to do exactly that. I have two 10G drives on 
my box. Fujitsu and Seagate. Fujitsu has my Win98SE OS, Seagate has my 
Lycoris OS. I don't run a bootloader, I do the switching within BIOS 
and tell it which hard drive to boot from.

My reasoning is that I need two completely separate drives that don't 
rely on the other one being there so I can take my Lycoris drive out at 
any time to do a demo with it etc. I found bootloaders to get stroppy 
if the 'other' drive is not where it expects it to be.

I may be completely mixed up with my reasoning but it works fine for me 
and either drive is completely transportable without talking down my 
box.  It works for me <G>
