Re: [ADSL] The Broadband Battle

On Wed, Jan 08, 2003 at 01:52:38PM +1100, Christopher Yeoh wrote:

> > > I've always wanted to be able to directly host
> > > my own domain, so I can run my low traffic personal website and collect
> > > my own email etc.  But I can't do that efficiently, because I have a
> > > dynamic IP.  
> > 
> > Well I'm on a regular dialup and don't have my own domain, but I just
> > get one free from www.no-ip.org and run my own web server at
> > http://awol.no-ip.org. I also get my MX record setup there too, so I can
> > run my own mail server and mailing lists etc...
> 
> Don't you risk getting your mail delivered to someone else with this
> method? (eg you disconnect, someone gets your old ip address and mail
> is delivered before your MX record is updated)

Perhaps, but the risk of it actually being /delivered/ to someone else
is minimal, considering the number of people on dial-up that run SMTP
servers on their computer.

Moreover, those that do have SMTP servers sitting on the other end of
their dial-up link should, if they're any kind of human being, have
them configured so that they don't relay, meaning that the sending MTA
would be told where to go.

It is unclear to me whether or not the sending MTA will do another DNS
lookup when it next flushes the mail queue (thus picking up the DNS
change), or if it'll just go with what it had previously. I didn't
think that DNS information was cached by the MTA, but I don't know for
sure.
