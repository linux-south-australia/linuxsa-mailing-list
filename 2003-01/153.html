 Oracle has released the Cluster File System for Linux,
 running on the Oracle 9i database. The company is apparently
 committed to making its enterprise software available on a
 variety of hardware platforms running Linux.

 http://cl.com.com/Click?q=11-l8diI2-vPmrVB-3bF0n0hBqVq5IP

 One reason is that "Linux application servers are on a steep
 upward growth trajectory, estimated to be 20 percent a year.
 By 2007, the Linux application server market may reach $7
 billion in annual sales." However, "Lack of skilled Linux IT
 administrators still may be an issue in many regions."

 Who's thinking about getting on board as a Linux IT
 specialist? What's your plan for getting there?
