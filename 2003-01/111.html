building.

> b) won't allow you to communicate to another AP (you need a wireless

> for that).

Look up mesh networks. Buy the same brand of AP to minimise 
compatibility problems, and buy the brand that works.

> If you run links via a telco then $100k is going to get used up pretty
> quick.

Depends if it's a PAPL link or not. It also has the upside of 
continuing to work even when it rains or someone cooks a meal in their 
microwave. For that reason, a Telstra link is still going to be needed 
as a backup (ISDN or broadband) to ensure reliability.

Government grants are funded to a time scale, so a cheaper option for a 
few years is going to win over a more expensive option that lasts for 
10 years. There's no point in spending a fortune on an expensive 
solution that may be completely obsolete in 5 years, especially when 
it's a technology solution.

There's also the further option of finding existing towers and running 
dual-hops. Place an AP on the existing tower, and point the building AP 
at the tower.

> Umm, much of a muchness.  Fibre's not cheap.  Probably more than low 
> loss.
> (termination costs mostly)

Fibre is about $100 for a 10m cable up to about $200 for a 100m cable. 
Laying fibre is expensive unless there's an existing cabling run.

a few things out, please add or replace my costings with your own.
