Re: [NEWS] Linux displacing *NIX?

On Friday, January 24, 2003, at 12:28 PM, David de Groot wrote:
> ~who tries not to think like a manager lest they make me into one.

As long as you're thinking, you're not thinking like a manager.

To be fair (and becoz i r wun now), management is hard, non-productive, 
work. It basically consists of non-stop talking to various people to 
reach consensus, so you look back at months of effort and think "in 3 
months, I've achieved... that person saying 'yes'". Depressing.

Then again, without these people saying yes, the techs in the back cave 
wouldn't have any shiny new toys to work on.

The trouble is that management has to handle so many changes that it's 
easier to decree points of stability ("we shall ignore PCs and buy 
Sun!") than to handle yet another shifting ground of uncertainty. The 
more stable points, the more useful framework. The more framework, the 
more risk of being useless. It's a vicious loop.
