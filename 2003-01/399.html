my gut reaction to the situation like this is deploy a wiki
of some sort. there are many versions about these days.

but, more importantly remember that a twiki or wiki is a 
draft document preparation tool.

it is not the kind of tool to deploy for your final product.

=)

of course the "documents" can be redeployed into another format
at a later stage =)

try to reinforce that word documents and windowswankhelpfiles
do not work well with revision control systems and "quality control"
wth multiple documetn editors

try to lever the aspect of peer review and document standards *and*
revision control ...

windows based document control software costs a fortune!
and licencing is a nightmare...

a wiki costs nothing and does most of the above fairly well
for small groups of people ~5-15 on a project
and you can run several wikis independantly on a given server
for thousands of documents/pages/topics

maybe you could deploy a wiki, then use the ploy,
"but we already have a draft document control system"

there are tools to rip the copntents of wikis and create
flat html... especially for CDs

there are also lots of "web page control" systems but a wiki
is far easier to setup and maintain

there wikis that do full user/pw/group authentication for page
and heirachies of pages... but as always YMMV
