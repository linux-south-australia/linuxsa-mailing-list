In any case, I'm unconvinced that a technical solution is required to
demonstrate due care in this situation.  A strong and (fairly) enforced
acceptable use policy coupled with "privacy screens" (screens on
monitors which restrict the field of view so that those who are not
immediately in front of the screen can't see it) ought to be sufficient.

To be sure, I've never once walked past the Ngapartji public access
terminals and seen anything I'd regard as offensive.  I find that I
need to challenge those who think censorware is required to explain
their priorities, because it seems to me that there are far more
important things to worry about...
