What DSL said then : (if not OK)
1/ Check that your vmlinuz links in / and /boot are
sensible. (they must be very close.)(you didn't rename a
vmlinuz  or something ?) 2/ Check you have made a new initrd
image if your using that.(and you selected ram disk support,
ROM and cramfs (actually that is optional but don't go there
:)	[mkinitrd and or update-initrd]
3/You can also grep the config files for that kernel in
/boot to see what support you put in the thing.

HINT:
In the toplevel Makefile on the third line you can assign
the extra versioning variable:. like this: for example.
VERSION = 2
PATCHLEVEL = 4
SUBLEVEL = 19
EXTRAVERSION =-petez

KERNELRELEASE=$(VERSION).$(PATCHLEVEL).$(SUBLEVEL)$(EXTRAVE
RSION)

It makes it a snack to trace stuff around the system.

Good luck..
