Re: [NEWS] 'Linux is too much like Windows'

On Wednesday, January 1, 2003, at 11:16 AM, David Lloyd wrote:
> Let me end by saying this: Linux is not a panacea. It's not a cure all.
> Its documentation is not bad. People who state a "blanket statement"
> that its documentation is all bad are missing the point.

After 10 years of beating my head against Linux's documentation brick 
wall, I have to disagree. The documentation issue is what drove me into 
buying a Mac.

Go to apple.com/support and start browsing through the help files, 
knowledgebase, support, documentation and detailed instructions to see 
just how pallid and inadequate free documentation is in comparison. 
It's not the quality of the individual documentation that is the 
problem (eg, Apache is fairly well documented), it's the variable 
quality of documentation across the board.

If I bump into a problem, I never know whether it's going to be a "HI! 
HAPPY HAPPY! HELP IS HERE!" answer, or whether I'm going to have to 
wade through the source code from a 1984 variety of Brainfuck. This 
lack of predictability is like walking through a minefield where a 
perfectly rational decision like "let's group those hard drives" could 
be a walk in the park or a trip to hospital.
