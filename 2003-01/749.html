Francesco Peeters wrote:

> Yeah, well... But apparently that's the way the Windoze driver fixes it as

Same end result, but different means of achieving it.  With a ping,
I have to determine a remote IP address to be able to ping (I may
not get assigned a remote gateway, it may have ICMP filtering on
it, etc. - remember, this is on a laptop).  When the card is
associated with a host ap, there WILL be a remote MAC address.
At this stage, I'm not even sure if the messages are generated
via firmware, but there doesn't appear to be a reply, so it's not
the ethernet equivalent of a ping.

But Ryan's right, the ping is a kludge.  And the older I get,
the kludgier these things seem.
