A couple of years ago there was an excellent article in Linux Journal 
regarding a "poor man's high available server" configuration where you 
use two servers that are mirror images. They automagically configure 
themselves into a master-slave relationship using the heatbeat 
application. Each continually monitors the presence of the other -- the 
slave always synching with the master; if the master goes down the 
slave assumes command and when the ex-master comes back online it 
becomes the slave.

IIRC, the article indicated that the "changeover" time was minimal -- 
no longer than a temporarily lost packet on the Net. In other words it 
is very useful for Web servers.

I have no doubt things have become more sophisticated since then, but 
that article might provide a useful starting point.
