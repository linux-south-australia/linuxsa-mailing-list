> yeah I've used them before.
> couple of things to be aware of though:
> - make sure you get one with a CD drive (most don't take
>   the normal sized cd drives (especially earlier models))
> - you can only fit one (IDE) disk inside
> - it's a compaq which makes me nervous about things like
>   the BIOS since compaq tend to do weird and stupid things
>   like make it so you can't get into the BIOS without
>   a "compaq bios" boot cd (not all compaqs though).

The diagnostics discs etc are still available as are "bios"
upgrades etc. from the Compaq US site.
You can usually squeeze another HD in on top of the power
supply or by screwing to the base beneath the tray.(this on
early model 2000 / 4000 ).

They are very nicely put together units other than that.
 Especially for PC s.

There are some Kernel switches for some of the on board
stuff as well.

Oh and if F2 or F10 don't get you into the bios (and it
probably won't if you have deleted the compaq partition???)
Try unplugging something from the IDE's or the FDC, Ribbons.
That sometimes forces the real bios to complain and open an
interface.

No reason to worry though the 2.4 Kernel seems to do most
things just fine. And you can always build a new compaq
partition (dos type) if you want to keep the diagnostics
discs etc on board. (there are Linux tools for everything it
does anyway.. memtest for eg.) Pete.
