Not sure about the asset tracking options, but we distribute the
lifechip for animals (mostly small animal, dog/cat for retrieval of lost
pets)

The company that is doing things in the human area with the technology
is Digital Angel (www.digitalangel.com I think)

Their approach is to have the person carry a chip which is linked into a
vital signs unit (watch type of device) and GPRS cellphone. The users
can set boundaries on the wearer that trigger alarms (anyone ever tried
to keep track of an alzheimer patient?), check if they have fallen over,
or if their heartrate/temperature/blood sugar has gone ape. I think
there is a smorgasboard of 13 or so vital signs available.

Not available in Aus yet, but I believe you can buy now in the US. The
system is reliant on GPRS comms.

I think the cancer issue is not likely to have any legs. The chips
themselves are passive devices that only activate in the presence od
radio frequencies in their band. The whole shebang is quite small and
encased in glass about the size of a large grain of rice.

I heard the other day that Pro Hart is embedding RFID chips into his
paintings in an attempt to foil the forgers!
