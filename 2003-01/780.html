Re: Parental Controls on community systems

On Fri, Jan 17, 2003 at 11:20:04AM +1030, Brian Astill wrote:

 > However, I do not believe that ANY OS can totally prevent access to ALL 
 > "undesirable" sites.  You can only do your best.
 > That must be made clear to the organisation concerned.

Ian needs to be aware that that means the less-than-ideal level of 
protection that technology is capable of delivering is no better than
no protection at all.

If he's worried about the public exposure his project would get if 
a kid downloaded porn through a public access kiosk terminal, then it
makes no difference whether that kid found the porn instantly or after
two minutes of searching (it'll take two minutes, regardless of how 
good your censorware software is).  Either way, the kid has accessed it,
the community will have its little moral panic, and negative press will
be accumulated.

That's what I meant in my earlier post about priorities.  If you treat
this as a high priority item, you can easily use up aeons of time and
effort and money to deliver exactly *zero* benefit.  On the other hand,
you can also invest zero time and effort to achieve zero benefit.  You
can do the cost/benefit analysis from there yourself:  Where should the
project's limited resources be directed?
