Re: Reinstalling Windows while keeping Linux

On Tue, 2003-01-14 at 11:23, Kim Hawtin wrote:
> there is another option however,

there's always plenty of options!
another one is:

assumes your boot device is /dev/hda
and you have an M$ partition mounted under /win

dd if=/dev/hda of=/win/bootsec.img bs=512 count=1

grabs the first 512 bytes of the disk - which I believe is
the boot sector and writes it to a file on your win partition,
or write it to a floppy if you're going to blow away the win
partition completely.

then reinstall M$ or whatever you want to do, when done
boot into M$, stick your linux cd in the drive and find
the rawrite dos program on there - which is the M$
equivalent of 'dd' and run:

rawrite bootsec.img /dev/hda bs=512 count=1
(you'll have to check the command line params for rawrite
hopefully they'd be same as 'dd' but I somehow doubt it)

reboot and you've got your old boot sector back again
(you could do it all from under M$ I guess using rawrite)

Note: this is untested so give it a go first to make sure!
