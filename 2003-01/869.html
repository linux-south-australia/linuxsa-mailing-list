Same sort of thing in the UK: you've got the "choice" of Tories,
Labour, or a bunch of small-timers it's not worth voting for because
they'll never get in anyway. So we had the Tories for several terms
even though everyone hated Thatcher, because Labour looked like a
bunch of headless chickens; then Labour finally got their act
together, people voted them in with relief that they didn't have to
have the Tories anymore... and they carried on doing exactly the same
sort of things that the Tories were doing. In fact they're so Tory
that the Tories aren't really an Opposition, more a
Quibbler-round-the-edges. Some choice.

The good thing about the British regime is not the so-called "choice"
but the results of our centuries-old tradition of not taking shit from
our rulers, so that people can blockade refineries in protest about
petrol prices and not get shot. This tradition seems to have been
exported pretty well to Australia. Not so well to America, where a lot
of the population were refugees from a tradition of being shat on by
their rulers all the time and not getting any effective opposition
together.

People get the sort of rulers they allow themselves to have; the idea
that you can forcibly convert a country to a more liberal system of
government by external military pressure is pretty well bound to fail
in the long run - or not so long.
