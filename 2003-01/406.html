Re: [ADSL] The Broadband Battle

> At present, why are Adam Internet are able to offer a permanent IP
> address on their personal plans for as little as $30 extra per month
> while Internode can't? The plan pricing is relatively similar?  As a
> loyal customer I'd not want to have to switch :-)

With respect to the currently available residential ADSL infrastructure and
PPP connections, the ISP has 3 choices. They can let the IP addresses get
allocated at the concentrator from a pool, allocate ALL IP addresses
themselves, or run their own concentrator (aka L2TP).

All 3 methods have their pros and cons, the latter two have only been
readily available for about 6 months. Letting the addresses get managed at
the concentrator greatly simplifies things, but unfortunately negates the
use of fixed IP addresses (due to policy in the network more than technical
limitations).

Adam opts to allocate *all* IP addresses for customers, giving us greater
control of the allocation process. This makes allocation of static IP
addresses on PPPo[EA] links possible. It also means that a customer can add
a static IP on to their existing service, or take it off as their needs
change without any setup charges.
