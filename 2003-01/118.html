Re: Socket programming question

On Sat, 4 Jan 2003 davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:

> michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:
> > I know there are up to 32 possible clients awaiting a chance to be accept'ed, 
> > is there anyway I can either a) peruse that list of waiting clients to see who 
> > they are, or b) simply know how many are queued up? [1]
> 
> Back in BSD4.1ish sockets worked slightly differently.  I think it was
> listen that you called to block and wait for a connection, although
> that was so long ago that maybe it's now something different which
> we used to call :) Anyway, the point is that you got to inspect the
> incoming connection before accepting; you could call peerinfo to find
> out who they were, and reject if you didn't want to talk to them.
> The reject system call seems now to have gone by the wayside, so it
> seems there's no way to peruse the list, not even the first element,
> without actually accepting.  Most people probably just accept and then
> close if they don't want to talk.

Right. You can do that, but I think, only on the top connection on the 
queue.

FreeBSD also has the concept of an acceptfilter. It is a KLD 
(unfortunately) that can be specified that looks at the incoming 
connections and accepts or rejects them in the kernel based on some 
criteria supplied by the userlevel.
