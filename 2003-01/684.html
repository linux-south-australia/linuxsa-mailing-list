The Joy of Wireless

Y'all,


I've taken the plunge into wireless, and set myself up at home
with a D-Link DWL-520 on my (soon to be new) firewall (FreeBSD
4.6), and a D-Link DWL-650 in the laptop.  All works fine when
the laptop is running 2000 (although to get it to that state
caused the appearance of more grey hairs), but when running
it under Linux *and* the link has been idle for exactly two
minutes, I get a linkstatus=DISCONNECTED followed by a
linkstatus=ASSOCFAIL, and the link drops out (of course,
otherwise I wouldn't be complaining :-).  Other platform
notes are:

Machine:       IBM T20 Thinkpad
Distribution:  RedHat 6.2, with kernel 2.2.22
pcmcia-cs:     3.2.3 (latest)
linux-wlan-ng: 0.1.16-pre8 (latest)

I *think* the firmware on the DWL-650 is up to scratch, but
bear in mind that the same hardware is working under 2000
(although it wouldn't surprise me if 2000 is regularly
transmitting junk that's keeping the link up).  I've been
unable to find any references to timeouts in the linux-wlan
lists, but that may be because the serach engine is being a
bit of a pill.

Does anyone have a working DWL-650 under linux (so that I
know what I'm trying to achieve is achievable), and is
willing to share configurations, wlancfg's or just even
beat me over the head with a clue?  Thanks in advance.
