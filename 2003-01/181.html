Re: getting around port 80 blocks

On Mon, 2003-01-06 at 15:30, Nick Morrison wrote:
> On Wed, Jan 01, 2003 at 10:49:17PM -0700, Lynn P. Tilby said:
> > I wish to set up a low volume 1 pg web server and my isp block incoming port
> > 80 to my ip what can I do?
> 
> Not much, unless you're ok with having people type
> "http://www.blah.com:81" into their browsers.  (ie, you can run the web
> server on a port other than 80)

yeah but then you get a different domain name and "forward" it.
Eg. you get a dyndns.org domain which is your (dynamic) IP then
you have your proper domain hosted somewhere and have the
www.properdomain.com "forwarded" to dynamicip.dyndns.org:1025
("URL Redirecting" it seems to be called) then it all works
transparently.
That's how I used to do it when on a dingoblue dialup (and
I chose 1025 cos it's above 1024 and not likely to be blocked).
