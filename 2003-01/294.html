Re: [ADSL] The Broadband Battle

On Wed, Jan 08, 2003 at 11:46:05AM +1030, Adam Smith wrote:

> At present, why are Adam Internet are able to offer a permanent IP
> address on their personal plans for as little as $30 extra per month
> while Internode can't? The plan pricing is relatively similar?  As a
> loyal customer I'd not want to have to switch :-)

$30 per month is obscene. 12 months of that, and you're paying a
whopping $360. For the so-called priviledge of one, single fixed IP
address.

I pay less than that for a /26 for a year (that said, it's on a
microwave link).

I don't understand where the difference is between the two
mediums. Why does it cost more to provide a single, static IP address
on ADSL than it does to provide 64 of them on a permanent connection?

My guess (based on what I think I heard Simon say once-upon-a-time),
is that Internode want you to use the business ADSL service, citing
the fact that you've got a need for the fixed IP address which *is*
available in the business product. Fair enough from one point-of-view,
but also rather unfair from another.
