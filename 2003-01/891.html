Problem solved.

After a dist-upgrade, reinstalled the 'gnome' metapackage (apt-get install
-u gnome), and this got rid of the old experimental junk and put in the new
unstable stuff (packages had same names, but without the "2" appended to the
end).

I tried this before with no luck, something I did in the meantime must have
caused this to work.  Not sure what, but I certainly did reinstall a lot of
packages with --purge.

The solution's often simpler than what you think :-(
