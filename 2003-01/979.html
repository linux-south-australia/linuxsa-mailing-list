Re: FreeBSD + nvidia on desktop - experiences?

In the immortal words of Ryan Verner
<xfesty@computeraddictions.com.au>...
> Curious to hear experiences from people who run FreeBSD on desktop
> with the nvidia libs.  Considering going back to fbsd on one of my
> desktop boxes, but I still want to be able to run commercial
> codeweaver (Wine) and transgaming(WineX) precompiled linux apps, and
> most my Linux games (UT, UT2003, Q3A) - and at the same speed as I do
> under Linux, preferably.

I'm running a Nvidia TNT2 Pro card in my workstation with the recent
Nvidia driver port and it seems to be working fine.  No OpenGL at this
stage and I haven't had time to fiddle with WineX although I have got
the CVS version downloaded.  I have noticed a half decent speed increase
from using the standard XFree86 drivers but I couldn't tell you if the
3D stuff was working properly at this stage.

Actually, come to think of it, I did get some of the OpenGL screen
savers to work (xscreensaver port) but they didn't work on the second
card (S3 Trio) so I went back to the my standard BSOD saver.   It has
been a while since I tried and I haven't tried with a GForce card at
this stage either.

Cheers

Tim
