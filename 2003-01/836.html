Hmmmm, maybe if vendor backed linux solutions like Sun's LX50 take off, 
but otherwise I'm not too sure. Working for a large multinational I've 
seen that the corporate market are still very shy of adopting open 
source, and in particular, anything that they can't buy a support 
contract for.

In short, corporates are very conservative. If they have an installed 
base of a particular vendor Unix, with an investment in staff trained 
on that vendors equipment, they're unlikely to up and change to 
something else, even if it is vastly cheaper. It's this last point 
which amazes me time and again, especially in the current economic 
climate where companies are cutting back on IT spending and looking for 
cheaper ways to do things.

Anyway, that's my 2 cents worth.
