Hey Adam,

Doc2Help?  Euurgh.  I agree - avoid any .doc transformations as much as you can! 

One way I can think of keeping away from IIS and Doc2Help, but give your boss what he wants is:

Make the 'knowledgebase' site from static, linked web pages (I know, nightmare for maintainability...but you need static web pages with absolute addresses in order to create the compiled help file).
You can then use your usual tools to search these pages.   

For the portable version, the static web pages can be compiled into a M$ HTML help document using the M$ "HTML Workshop" tool.  (There may be others that do the same job...I'm not sure)  This works pretty okay - I've done it here at work.  
(Actually, now I think of it, you may be able to export your php/sql pages to make the 'static' pages...sorry I'm a bit vague with this - php/apache is not my area of expertise.)

At least with this you avoid .doc's and IIS...

Cheers!
