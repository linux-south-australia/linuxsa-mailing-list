Re: Fail-Over web servers

On 2003.01.16 13:36 Matthew Geddes wrote:
> It's also worth keeping in mind other single points of failure that
> will be between your users and your web server. Many people
> seem to have a round-robin (or smarter) reverse transparent
> proxy running on a firewall machine, which passes the request
> to the next available web server.
> The problem is when the firewall goes down. Both web servers
> are fine and happy, but could have caught on fire for all your users
> know. So you make the firewall redundant (not an easy task), but
> then you've still only got one internet link and one ISP, and ....
> 
> Also, placing the two redundant servers in the same geographical
> location may be unacceptable too (in case of fire, flood, nuclear war,
> the cleaners, etc).
> 
> It's also worth noting the difference between failover and load
> balancing. You may find that one is better than the other for the task
> at hand, whereas the other may be completely inappropriate.

Champagne taste but beer income.

One can always go overboard on the whole philosophy of redundancy and 
back-up. I think that the first question to be asked is: "What will be 
the damage on failure?" Your country; your home; your pocketbook; your 
pride?

Sure, I'd like triple redundancy, instantaneous failover, interstate 
and offshore backup ... . I'd also like a Ferrari, but I'll settle for 
a Holden.
