With Wine, at least in my experience, I have found that if a game does 
not work in one version of Wine, it may work in another.  For example, 
the Transgaming and Winehq Wine would not install Harry Potter and the 
Sourcer's stone, but Cross Over Office would. However, the Transgaming 
version of Wine will run AOL 5 (if you create a text file and call it 
wsock32.dll and put it in .transgaming/c_drive/windows/system) on a LAN 
- not dialup AFAIK.

So, if you happened to have Cross Over Office or the Winehq veersion of 
Wine, you may give that a shot as well, as it may work just fine.
