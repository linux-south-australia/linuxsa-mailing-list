SSH, emacs, PuTTY and alternative character sets

One thing that really gets on my nerves is the apparent inability of
PuTTY and (maybe) emacs to deal with character sets other than
English.

This is running from a Windows client, mind. Sadly, it's a work
requirement. Very sadly, actually.

So, say I copy and paste some text from a Russian website; gazeta.ru,
into the PuTTY window, it comes out like so:

........ ..... ......... ............ ........

Which is incredibly helpful. It's meant to be a little snippet of
Vladimir Putin's Christmas message. Dots don't help very much.

And also, if I try and paste in anything that's written in Arabic, it
spews as well. More dots. Lots of lovely dots.

I haven't tried Swedish, but I assume that it won't be too much of a
problem because there are only a limited number of characters that
can't be represented in English.

Does anyone have any clues?
