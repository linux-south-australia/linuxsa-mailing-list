Re: Socket programming question

On Friday, January 3, 2003, at 11:33 AM, michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:
> I don't want to poll them all, just deal with them one at a time (ie 
> they come
> along, request some work, go away and process it and eventually come 
> back with
> the result).  The socket connection doesn't stay open all the time.  
> Clients
> can join and leave any time, may be killed mid-stream, and their 
> number may
> vary etc.

Sounds like a scenario for UDP. You're already coding for unreliable 
clients and unreliable connections, so you only need to add the small 
corner case of a client making a successful report that doesn't get 
received. This won't be an issue if the server is driving the requests 
to the clients.

You can always fire up a negotiated TCP connection if you want to do a 
larger transfer, to avoid writing your own TCP over UDP.
