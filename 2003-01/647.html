Andrew Speer wrote:

>I have been reading the responses to the original posters question, and most 
>seem along the lines of "any reasonable LCD display should work", which is 
>fine and true for those LCD displays with analogue (VGA) connectors.
>
>However I do not know about the rest of you, but if I was going to fork over 
>the money for an LCD display, I would want one with a DVI connector for the 
>best possible picture quality (after all, unless you are desperate to save 
>space, image sharpness is probably your number one goal in buying an LCD 
>display - so why try and re-generate a digital image from an anlogue signal).
>
>At the moment my understanding is that linux support for a full DVI digital 
>signal from the video card is somewhat patchy, and not always supported in 
>the GPL drivers, however if you buy and LCD that supports DVI input, you can 
>always activate it later when you get a new card, or linux support for your 
>particular card comes along.
>
>As an aside, the current king of monitors seems to be made by IBM. Check out 
>the specs (and price - ouch) on this beauty:
>
>http://www.monitoroutlet.com/342898.html
>
>Now that I have got my own answer to the question out, I can use that as a 
>pretext for my own monitor related musings:
>
>1 .. I have always spent far more money on my monitor that the actual CPU, 
>and never regretted it. Whilst I might upgrade my CPU every 2-3 years, the 
>monitor should last a good 5+ years easily if it is a quality item. I am just 
>at the stage of pondering whether to upgrade my current monitor (a little 
>known brand, Cornerstone, but very good specs and quality) to an LCD. However 
>my gut feeling is to leave the LCD display for another 18 months, and since 
>my current monitor is still going great guns, I might as well. 
>
>However when I do buy an LCD display, I will try and select the biggest, best 
>quality one I can afford, as I will be looking at it for a long time. 
>
>As far as Computer hardware goes, I personally have a philosophy that the 
>three most important items are (1) the monitor, (2) the chair !, and (3) the 
>keyboard. 
>
>So long as the CPU is reliable and reasonable speed, it is not really that 
>important to me personally (well, with the exception that it should be as 
>quiet as realistically possible). I understand that other people have other 
>priorities, especially if they are heavily into gaming or number crunching, 
>but if you are at you workstation for any length of time I think that comfort 
>and ergonomics are an important factor.
>
>2 .. If I do go out and buy an LCD display, even one coupled to a fully 
>digital output, there is still the odd concept of "refresh rates" for the 
>display and card. 
>
>To quote Seinfeld, "what is the deal there ?". I would have though having a 
>fully digital LCD display would make video card design (at least the output 
>stage) so much easier. Can't we now directly correlate LCD pixels with video 
>card memory, and only update the display when an area of memory changes ? No 
>RAMDACs, at least.
>
>Why update the LCD display 60/70/85 times a second when mostly (except for 
>movies and games) it never changes ? If I open a new window in whatever 
>window manager I am using, the video RAM changes just where the window is 
>opened - can't the card notice this and say to the LCD "change pixels x-y". 
>If no other pixels change for 30 seconds, then no further comms between the 
>LCD and card should be needed for that time.
>
>It is easy for an armchair engineer to say these sort of things, and I am 
>sure there sound reasons for how things are done currently, it is just 
>puzzling. 
>
>If next week there was a breakthough in LCD design that greatly increases 
>resolution (eg by decreasing pixel pitch, or allows fabbing of enormous 
>displays, or a combination of both) we would currently have no way of driving 
>all those pixels through the current interface technology (although 
>converting all window mangers and applications to fully vector based system 
>to take advantage of the increased resolution would also be a small problem !)
>
>The IBM monitor mentioned above only works at its highest resolution when 
>driven in a tiled or striped mode by *multiple* video cards, and I believe 
>that one of the reasons is that the max bandwith suppored by the DVI spec is 
>not enough to drive that many pixels at a reasonable refresh rate.
>
>So we still have a way to go I think .. but is interesting to see the tech 
>emerge. Just my 2c.
>
