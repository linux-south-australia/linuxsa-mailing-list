Hello again,

I have decided that for the fail-over webserver setup that i am intended 
to construct, either ENBD or DRBD will be used (these allow for the 
exporting of a partition from one machine, which can then be 
incorporated in a RAID 1 by the other machine).

I searched through the archives and noticed that Richard Sharpe has had 
some experience in this area, and ended up favouring DRBD over ENBD for 
the task he was doing. This was in 2000.

I was wondering if anyone has had any more recent experience with either 
of these system (or Richard, if you were happy with your choice of 
DRBD?, has it been stable? any major problems?)..


Any thoughts on these technologies would be cool..

Jon
----------------
The cool thing with ENBD, is that on its webpage it has instructions on 
how to make a fail-over server, that will automatically rebuild, update 
and switch back to the master when it comes back up..

For those wanting to know more:
http://www.complang.tuwien.ac.at/reisner/drbd/
http://www.it.uc3m.es/~ptb/nbd/ <http://www.it.uc3m.es/%7Eptb/nbd/>
