To clarify what I meant by "support":

Basically, management want someone to blame if something bad happens. 
We "support" the OS on our machines whether they be Solaris, HP/UX, 
*BSD, etc, but if a curly one comes up that is OS related and we can't 
work it out, they want someone they can engage that has really in-depth 
knowledge of the system because they wrote it. So yes, we could go and 
hire any number of Linux support companies, and quite a few of them 
could provide exactly what we want, but management aren't convinced 
because there is no one vendor for the OS.
