Thanks Simon,

Like I said - I would more than happily see my dollars
go to a local business.  And I would more than happily
respect that an ISP can not provide unlimited access
to a finite resource.  I would be an even happier
little customer if I could go sick with my new toy
with local content - even if the ISP concerned still
had to charge in some way for it.  I think that is more
than fair.  Massive storage space is not cheap, 
employing people to maintain it is not cheap, etc.

But if it were to be cheaper than a straight through
connection to the Internet - then it is a step in
the right direction.

Even if just maintaining mirrors of Linux ISO images,
software updates, 'legal' mp3s and other music files
or film files, or anything else at all - it would make
me very happy and I would be able to easier justify
the expense of ADSL to myself.

Keep up the good work Simon.  And if you need a hand
with lobying with the ACCC or similar - please let me
know.  I think all of us would like to see more
reasonable bandwidth charges.  Greater margins for you
and more hours of fun for me :-)
