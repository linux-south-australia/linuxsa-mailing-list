Telstra Bigpond ADSL weirdness

Hi!

Greetings to everyone - haven't been here a while, but I have a technical
question that the LinuxSA folks are probably the best-equipped people I know
to answer, if I'm lucky ... I humbly present my Bigpond ADSL Weirdness for
your perusal.

I have a connection to their ADSL network that works beautifully under
Windows, and almost perfectly under Linux as well; I say 'almost', because
there's one small exception. I can reach any server I want to /except/ the
ones that appear to be accessible to Telstra's ADSL network only.

Yes, I know it sounds backwards. :) 

Examples: I can connect to websites, ping www.cdrom.com, etc, etc, but I
can't ping or connect to (http, smtp, pop3, nntp respectively) the following
hosts:

www.vic.bigpond.net.au
mail-hub.vic.bigpond.net.au
pop-server.vic.bigpond.net.au
news-server.vic.bigpond.net.au

I /can/ talk to some of the Telstra servers that are outside that group,
such as www.telstra.com and melbourne.cache.telstra.net.

However, the above DOES work under Windows using their PPPoE client. Go
figure.

The system's a Linux 2.4.3 machine (Debian unstable) talking to the supplied
Alcatel SpeedTouch using the supplied SMC ethernet card. I'm using the
Roaring Penguin pppoe stuff with pppd 2.4.1.

If anyone has a clue for me I'll be unbelievably grateful and very
impressed. :) I'd just like to actually be able to check my @bigpond.net.au
mail.
