Re: Why use DocBook versus Word

David Lloyd wrote:
> 
> Hmmm!
> 
> > The downside of this approach is the OpenOffice won't
> > enforce the syntax rules of DocBook (eg: You can't
> > have <section> <section></section> <para></para> </section>)
> > {X}Emacs does enforce the rules.
> 
> But how would you convince OpenOffice that "Microsoft Windows" should be <application>Microsoft Windows</application>...the thing I like about SGML/XML is that you can separate the presentation from the meaning.

In OpenOffice, have a character style called "application".

The XSLT transformation can the produce DocBook's <application>
from OpenOffice's <character-format style-name="application">
(or whatever the XML elements are, I haven't checked).

What the transform can't do can't do is to make sure that
the DocBook syntax rules are met.  If can at best only fail
if the rules are broken.

Anyway, DocBook and WYSIWYG aren't really a good combo.  But
that's fine, as DocBook is aimed at technical computer
documentation rather than general purpose word processing.
To see what I mean, imaging writing a letter using the
DocBook DTD.

Looked at in this light, DocBook is a huge advance on
LaTeX.  LaTeX/TeX is a pain because the source format
has to change when the ouptut device (web, paper, etc)
changes.
