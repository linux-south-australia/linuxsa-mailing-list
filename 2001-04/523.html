> >
> >> Note also that RAID-1 is a lot more expensive than RAID-5: you can

> > Don't you need at least 3 disks to do raid-5?

> Theoretically you could do it with 2, but then it would just be a slow

Hmm. Yes, but that is no advantage over raid1. It still survives the

drive catches fire. (believe me I know...)

> 
> > More drives also equate to more heat, greater complexity, and more
> > power consumption.
> 
> And more storage space :-)

Sure, but I thought we started this looking at a 2x 12gb mirrored array.
If we want more space, we could throw in a 5x 30gb raid5 and pick up
100+gb, but that might be overkill...

> 
> RAID-5 is an interesting alternative when you have large quantities of
> data which is mainly read, such as web or ftp servers.  You'd really
> want at least 5 drives in a volume, which would give you an overhead
> of only 25% above the data stored, as compared with the 100% you
> always have with RAID-1.

Is raid5 good at rw? I've seen it and raid5e on a couple of database
servers recently..
