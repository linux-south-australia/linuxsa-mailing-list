Not feasible unfortunately, since (with the bandwidth fees included), 
the total cost to us to source the service from Telstra is 
substantially greater than $80 per month. Welcome to the 'free 
market', and yes, we are talking with the ACCC about this issue, as

So I can't fix that one, hence my choice was to design our service to 
be more feature-rich for a comparable price (static IP addresses, no

in the circumstances - i.e. if we can't lower the price, well, we can 
raise the value provided.

You need to step back and consider that prior to ADSL, the only 
options for 1.5 megabits per second to the home cost in excess of 
$20k per annum plus your download fees. Taken in that light, any 
notion that $95 or $135 or even $500 per month for that sort of speed 
is unacceptably expensive is rather, well, inaccurate :)

That's different to whether the price point is low enough for you, in 
particular, to afford. I completely appreciate that it might not be. 
Every drop in price will allow one more tier of people to take up a 
service. Meantime Telstra and Internode are both seeing very strong 
demand at the existing price points, i.e. the price is already so far 
under $20k per annum that it's admitted in a whole heap of people who 
-can- decide that they can afford sub $500 per month for access 
previous impossible for less than four digits per month per line.

Your other option is ISDN data-over-voice. Convert your existing home 
phone line to ISDN "OnRamp Home Highway", it gives you two high 
quality analog ports into a digital box in your wall, a serial cable, 
and an ISDN S/T port.

You plug a Traverse NetJet PCI card (Linux drivers available) into 
the ISDN S/T port, program it to signal the ISDN call as 'voice', and 
call a DoV compatible ISP (guess who...). The OnRamp HH costs circa 
$38 per month including your first $5 worth of local calls (when 
signalled as voice, they are $0.18 plus GST each).

Bingo, on-demand 64k or 128k into your ISP, at $38 per month plus 
whatever your chosen ISP plan costs you.

Total monthly cost easily lower (depending on your ISP's plans). And 
available everywhere, unlike ADSL.

The options are out there.
