RE: Network card

I would tend to think that the reason your cards wouldn't apprear in the
network would be network-protocol related ... eg the wonders of windows
browsing !. I do agree that the 8139 NIC's are shit (that's being nice of
course), but in a windows box they are of reasonable performance, providing
the box is correctly configured, but saying that I wouldn't rule out all $20
cards, we use the Davicom DM9102 in many pc's including our own and we have
found them to be quite reliable and of reasonable perforamnce. The Linux
driver for these cards (DMFE) has proven to us to be a fairly good
performance driver and we normally get fairly good transfer speeds here at
work over it. For those who regularly are faced with the terrors of 8139's
in a Linux box, normally under 2.2 kernels we have found them to perform at
about 2Mbps, however since upgrading to 2.4 kernels we have found significat
performance increase, obviously not fantastic increase, but worthwhile,
especially if you've only got a 10Mbps hub.
