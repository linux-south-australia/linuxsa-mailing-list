>here, but I don't know what to do to fix it. The modem

Generally, if you want to use different (standard) com ports simultaneously,
each needs to be on its own IRQ. By default, ttyS0 and ttyS2 share an IRQ,
as do ttyS1 and ttyS3.

You should be able to change the IRQ's for onboard serial ports in the bios,
if you have enough of them spare!

There are multiport cards out there (equinox, stallion, digi, comtrol to
name a few) that use only 1 IRQ, or none at all. These cards tend to be
faster than standard com ports for multiple modem applications.
