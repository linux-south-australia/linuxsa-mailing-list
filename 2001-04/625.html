Our allocation is as per your latter suggestion, i.e. reserved, 
default route, your ip, and broadcast, all under a 4 host netmask 
(255.255.255.252).

We are also supporting customers who want to have 5 customer hosts 
out of 8 (reserved, default route, customer ip's 1 to 5, broadcast), 
under a netmask of 255.255.255.248, for any situations for which a 
single IP is insufficient and NAT is not desired.

There is a logical and common degenerate case, a /32 netmask (i.e. 
255.255.255.255), i.e. a host route.

In this case, yes, you have one IP and there is no wastage - your 
modem knows that 'default' is just 'up the link' (which is 
effectively un-numbered) to the world.

For static IP assignments, Telstra don't let us allocate a single 
address - minimum burn of the global routing tables is 4 addresses - 
they'll only let us have a single address to the customer if Telstra 
dynamically allocate the IP. Which, for business customers or people 
of any sort who want to run a server at home, is about as much use a 
tits on a bull.  So we could technically be more efficient except 
that Telstra choose to prohibit us from doing so.
