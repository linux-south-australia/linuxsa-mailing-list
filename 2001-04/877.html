Hang on -- Do you think there's actually a cap in the sense that it

Oh, wow - there might be a perception issue here:  "cap" isn't a word 
Internode uses in the materials published for this service, it's a word
which someone else on this list has introduced.  To clarify:  You can 
download as much as you want with no restrictions whatsoever, with the
proviso that megabytes downloaded in excess of the amount included in
your price plan will be charged at $0.18 each (which, I might add, is 
cheaper than Telstra's retail megabyte rates).

If you think you'll use 2 Gbytes/month, then you can get onto the
1.5 Gbyte/month plan and pay an extra $90 per month (500 Mbytes * 0.18c/Mbyte).

Note that other Internode permanent link price plans are available on
these services too:  If you think you'll regularly do very high data
volumes, the sales staff can put you on a non-standard price plan.
