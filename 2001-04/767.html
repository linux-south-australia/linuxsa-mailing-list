At 13:24 23/04/01 +0930, David de Groot wrote:
>
>Monday, April 23, 2001, 12:46:56 PM, Andrew wrote:
>
>> Shrug all you like. The fact of the matter is that these are not
>> reliable cards. Not reliable like 3Com, or maybe even the Intel
>> EtherExpress cards.
>
>I'd  have  to  agree. A while back when I was heavily into LAN gaming,
>one of the crew turned up with a realtek card in his box. While anyone
>else  acted  as  the  server, noone had any real network problems, but
>soon  as  the  realtek  machine was used as the server, we experienced
>severe lag, and sometimes lockups.
>Now,  sure,  this  isn't  a business world example, but if you want to
>seriously  test  your nic, setup a decent sized LAN gaming session and
>act as the server.
>
>I've  found  that  realtek  cards  seem  to  be  quite reliable in one
>direction  only,  but lose packets in the other direction, and seem to
>do  this  only  under  load.  Try  doing a flood ping from one of your
>realtek   workstations  and  vice versa. See how many packets get lost
>compared to doing a standard ping.
>
>Personally,  I  run 3com nics whenever possible, and SMC's as the next
>in line.
