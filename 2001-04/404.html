Whatever happened to ATM over DSL? It always seemed to me that this was the
most practical way towards making money out of plain copper instead of the
razor-thin margins of high-speed net access to end users. Why not plan to
give end users the whole 1.5/2/6 Mbits/s available, but only give them their
Internet access on a virtual circuit with a CIR? Much easier than to
maintain than traffic-shaping access lists etc.

Then with the leftover bandwidth, sell them voice services, gaming services,
unlimited saix access, pay-per-mb faster downloads, security system
monitoring and even profit-sharing with the local video shop for streaming
media, all as cheap incremental upgrades.

 Granted, consumer-priced ATM gear might be hard to source right now, and
there's probably end-user education issues to contend with, but sooner or
later there will be an virtual-circuit-capable ADSL box with PSTN <-> VoIP
phone plugs (oh wait, there's already a low-end cisco with that..) and
RCA/S-Video jacks to plug into the home entertainment system. It's already
established that people will happily pay $40/month for foxtel.. why not plan
for the bigger slice of disposable income?
