RE: [OT] ADSL Modem hacking and network topology

No, that's not it.

Your ADSL device in our world is a dumb bridge and it only has an 
ethernet side IP address for configuration changes (which aren't 
needed anyway), and that ethernet side IP is a *secondary address* on 
the ethernet - it's invisible to normal operation of the unit. 
Pretend it doesn't exist.

The 4 host arrangement is:

reserved: 	a.b.c.d 	(start of the subnet)
default gateway (i.e. the Internode side of the link, through the 
box): 	a.b.c.d+1
your IP host (your linux box :) ): a.b.c.d+2
broadcast:	a.b.c.d+3

Hope that's clearer. You configure a.b.c.d+2 into your linux box with 
a mask of 255.255.255.252, and you configure the address a.b.c.d+1 
into your linux system as your default route.

You completely ignore the ADSL bridge device, and consider it to be 
an ADSL to ethernet media converter. There is no configuration 
needed. You can open the manual and find out how to connect your 
browser to it to have a fiddle (go ahead - I did with mine :) ) but 
it's completely un-necessary for normal usage of the box and there is 
nothing you can set in there that will do anything other than break a 
working link :)
