My understanding of the security is this:

To access the modem from the outside world you need to use an echo port
of a machine on the inside. If you have a linux box or firewall on the
inside blocking access to the rest of your lan and that has no echo port
then I dont think you can tamper with it externally. If you _do_ have an
open echo port then an attacker can send a udp packet to the echo port
from 255.255.255.255 and it it'll bounce of a machine on your inside
network and that packet can rewrite the modems config. If you have a
linux firewall then echo probably isnt open anyway and if it is you can
disable it from inetd.conf easily enough.

It is hackable from the adsl line, but only if you have adsl equipment
and sneak around in the streets at night and aligator clip some your
hacking hardware to the line. Apparently if you had enough electronics
experience and a spare adsl modem you could get the required chips from
it and build such a device, but unless you expect industial espionage
from people with secret labs and lots of money to devote to it, I think
you'd be ok in this regard.
