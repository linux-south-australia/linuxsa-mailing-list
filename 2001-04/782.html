Re: MS Curriculum at schools and TAFEs ...

Hi All,

The key problem is with the teaching staff rather than the IT support 
staff.  In general, they have little or no experience with non-Windows 
products.  Until you can show them an alternative, you won't get anywhere 
in schools.
Unfortunately this creates a chicken and the egg problem, "Give me some 
non-windows machines to show you some products", "ok but show me the 
products first in a class-room environment", "but, but, but ........"

At Trinity College, anything internetty (new word there) is Unix based 
(well Linux actually). We have recently purchased 130 SUNRAY devices and 
are using an E450 as the SUNRAY server.  We also have an Ultra 80 as an 
application server running Netscape, IE, Star Office and The Gimp.  This 
week, while most people are away, we will also trial running applications 
off Linux servers and displaying them on the SUNRAYs.   Very very sexy stuff.

We have not, and probably will not, get rid of all of our Windows 
machines.  There is no denying that there is a place for them somewhere in 
the outside world.

Cheers
Mark Collis
Trinity College
