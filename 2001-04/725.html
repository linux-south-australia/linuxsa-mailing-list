Re: CNET 650/Freesco/486 setup

On 23 Apr 2001 10:19:35 +0900, Evan Bourlotos wrote:

> And how do I cut down RH say to run on this miserly piece of hardware?

Simply choose "Select Individual Packages" during the installation
procedure. You remove all the crud that RedHat likes to install by
default and only install the packages you need. It'll probably take you
30-45 minutes to trim it down to the 'bare bones'.

It's definately possible, though. I managed to get RedHat 5.2 installed
with X, GNOME and Netscape in under 250MB. I'm sure you can fit a
routerr into under 180. Easilly.

Don't forget that there are other distributions out there that are
designed to be 'mini linux' distributions. Have a look on Freshmeat.

> And how what do I configure? (points would be great)

Well, you're probably jumping the gun there. I'd hold that question
until you've got your distribution sorted.

> I need NAT, firewall and on demand dialup with obvious telnet session in
> case. to run in 8mb and 120 mb of disk.

Diald, IPChains & OpenSSH should pretty much cover it. Also you'll want
PPP and the modem stuff, including minicom for when things stuff you
around.

It's really quite a simple procedure. There is plenty of documentation
(http://mirror.aarnet.edu.au/pub/LDP or http://www.linuxdoc.org) out
there that explains exactly what you're trying to do.
