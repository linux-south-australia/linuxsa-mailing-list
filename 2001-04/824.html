RE: MS Curriculum at schools and TAFEs ...

another important difference, with an NT box, any monkey with no skills and
the Administrator password can point and click and mess the whole thing up
(eg, i've just spent the last half an hour sending a message to the IT
"co-ordinator" at one of my corporate sites who just decided to point and
click and delete the managing directors mail box in exchange) ... however
with a *nix box even the monkey with a root password is less likely to screw
things up. *nix has the advantage it can be remote administered, therefore
one administer can look after more than one school ... or business for that
matter. With simply tools like webmin a monkey (teacher [hehe]) can be setup
with the appropriate access to start and internet connection, stop and
internet connection, view the mail/samba logs etc, and add and remove users
from the system. Also with tools like ghost or drive image they can also
re-build any workstation within 5 minutes !
