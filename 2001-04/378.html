On Sat, Apr 14, 2001 at 07:13:16AM +1000, Daryl Pilkington wrote:
> Hi Justin,
> I'm using iPrimus ADSL & had some weird problems too.
> I have a gut feeling it is due to the size of your data packets.
> As you are aware, (because you researched ADSL & PPPoE before installing
> it, <grin>), the maximum theoretical packet size for PPPoE is 1492. The
> supplied EnterNet software forces max MTU to 1454. I would suggest
> strongly not going higher than that as probably iPrimus & Telstra do all
> their testing at that MTU.
> Note this *does* not mean forcing MTU=1454 for the ethernet interface to
> the Alcatel ADSL Speedtouch Home ethernet bridge. (Gee say that after a
> few beers...)
> 
> Look closely at the docs for the Roaring Penguin PPPoE for
> recommendations for:
> 
> MTU discovery being on
> IP fragmentation support being off
> Maximum PPPoE MTU 1454 not being set
> 
> I can't help you with the specifics for Roaring Penguin PPPoE, as I use
> InJoy Firewall on IBM OS/2 Warp.
