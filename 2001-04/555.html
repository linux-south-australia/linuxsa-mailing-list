Re: Ipchains

On 18 Apr 2001 12:36:05 +0930, Mick Collins wrote:
> Yes. I would like everything to go out. But only certain things to come back
> in and or hit my boxes. Eg Dos Attacks No, and other weird shit like this

Why not then just masquerade all the traffic from the Internal LAN
through your (ppp|eth)(0-9) device?

Do get stuff in, you either need to have spare IPs for your boxes, or
use rinetd (or similar) to forward ports on your gateway (firewall) to
the internal server.

Basically, it looks like this:

                        \
+----------------+      / Internal Network
|    Gateway     |      \         +----------+
|                |      /         |  Server  |
|    Port 25 ----------------------> Port 25 |
|                |      \         |          |
|    Port 80 ----------------------> Port 80 |
|                |      /         |          |
+----------------+      \         +----------+
                        /

This is a (small) glimpse of how my DMZ works. Essentially, all your
world-reachable IPs are assigned to the Gateway box. Ports on those IPs
are forwarded as necessary to other servers etc. This means that you
only really have one place to do the firewalling. All you have to worry
about then is what services your running on the receiving end of the
ports.

Forwarding is done with rinetd (this is the way I do it, anyway).
