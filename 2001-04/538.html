Re: Redhat 7.1

"Freeman, Peter (ERHS)" wrote:

> Does anyone know if the infamous gcc issues they had with 7.0 have
> been fixed in 7.1?

http://www.bero.org/gcc296.html

In summary, Redhat are saying that the 2.2 and earlier kernels weren't C
standards compliant, but they happened to work with earlier versions,
since they were all coded with the not-quite-correct version of gcc in
mind.

RH7.1 still has the same/similar compiler, but the kernel it ships with
is a 2.4 series, which is coded correctly for the newer versions of gcc
and won't suffer the same problems. They still include kgcc in case you
want to go back to a 2.2 kernel.
