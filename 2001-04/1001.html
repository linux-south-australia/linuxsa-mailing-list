>The ongoing cost on the static IP is to our forward costs to APNIC; 
>The real issue for us is that the Telstra 'wholesale' model is so 
>puntative that we can't make it any cheaper without loosing money. 
>That's the constraint we face. So instead, I decided to try really 
>hard to deliver higher value for that price point (which, I might 
>add, is certainly circa $10 per month more than the nearest 
>equivalent telstra plan point, though the excess rates are
>lower - and are the place in which we have some room to negotiate).
>
>It's a hard issue. Telstra really are trying hard to screw over the 
>wholesale market. This is the best that we can currently do, and we 
>need to build up a customer base in order to have any leverage to try 
>to argue our internal costs down (and hence be able to move the 
>external ones down).
>
>I'm obviously, however, highly interested in feedback like this, and 
>if we don't hit a high enough sales rate at the current price point, 
>I will have to do the sums on the impact on us of winding it down a 
>tad - but because the economics are so bad for us internally, that 
>would probably push financial break even (in theory) out from 18 
>months to 3-5 years. It's that sensitive a cost model unfortunately.
>
>So I'm asking for support of a local company doing it 'right', and 
>yes, I'm actually asking for more money than the nearest telstra plan 
>- but for a higher delivered value.
>
>In the end, you need to make a judgement call on whether the higher 
>delivered value is worth an extra 43 cents per day.
>
>Again, comments sincerely appreciated and I'm listening.
