Re: Linux and Ethernet Devices

>Can I be assured that, if I leave the cards in the same physical slots
>that they are in now, the addresses and kernel device assigned to each
>card will be the same?

I would say yes. When I've done this before, the cards came up in the same
order as they were physically plugged in. But:

1) Will it always be the same motherboard?
2) Will the PnP Bios be reset?
3) Will the Bios be upgraded?
4) What happens if (n) cards fail, especially the first one?

Perhaps a safer method would be to change the network init scripts to setup
these interfaces based on their hardware address rather than the interface
number, by doing some cunning grepping of the output from ifconfig.

Fortunately some good-quality network cards have the Hardware address on a
little sticker, which may help to avoid trial-and-error when replacing cards
in the event of (4). Also, if the networks themselves are tied to
mac-address of the cards, a marking or color on the back of the card will
help to put the network back together again in the future with a minimum of
confusion. And whenever you have 4 or 5 identical-in-appearance network
interfaces on the back of a PC, you want to minimise confusion as much as
possible :)
