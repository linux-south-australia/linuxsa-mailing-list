Re: Wireless cards

On Tue, Apr 17, 2001 at 08:23:28AM +0930, Andrew Hill wrote:
> 
> However, I have seen that home office type units like the Apple AirPort
> allow you to require that the wireless network name that a client
> presents be correct to allow access.

For DSSS based wireless networks (eg Apple AirPort, Lucent) although
you set a network name you don't need it to connect to the network
(assuming encryption is turned off). At least under linux when in
managed mode it will just find the name for you and connect (I think)
to the network with the highest signal strength. 

Its a bit worrying just how many wireless networks there are around
that allow outside people to directly connect to company or university
intranets simply because people haven't turned on encryption.

For FHSS based networks I think the network name changes the
frequency hop pattern but I imagine it wouldn't be that hard to work
that out.
