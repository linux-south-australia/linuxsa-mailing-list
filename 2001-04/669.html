Getting Slack ... to make a bootdisk ...

Hi,

One of my favourite tricks is to copy /boot/vmlinuz onto a floppy with dd.

This will make a bootdisk for many Linux distros ... 

However, Slackware's vmlinuz is almost as bloated as Tru64 UNIX's is, and
is over 1MB in size, so this trick does not work.

Then I tried to to use the Slackware setup utility to create a LILO bootdisk.

Unbelievable. It does not work. The script creates a lilo.conf that
specifies that the boot disk is /dev/fd0, and then fails to put fd0 in /dev
on the floppy, or alternatively, figures out what the proper device is
(/dev/fd0u1440) and then puts /dev/fd0 as the boot device in lilo.conf ...

Has anyone seen this problem with Slack 7.1 and is there an official fix?
