DOS boot disk or FAT filesystem? If it's like any of the other single
floppy distributions, it will use ramdisks as the root and other
filesystems. These ramdisks are often found on floppys formatted with
the FAT filesystem due to the overhead involved with other filesystems.
The only time the disk is accessed, is to read the kernel and the
ramdisks. It's not a bad way of going about it really.
