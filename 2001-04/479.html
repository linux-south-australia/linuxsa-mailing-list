The key is in what Greg actually said: "RAID-1 is usually faster than
RAID-5, and never slower."

You see, when you write a block to a RAID-5 setup, you have to read the
whole stripe in, insert the block, and recompute the parity. This can be a
whole lot slower than RAID-1 and RAID-1 can initiate reads on all drives
and take the closest block. It can also initiate independent reads on each
drive as well. And since writes do not need to re-compute the parity, they
will be at least as fast as RAID-5 writes, if not faster in most cases.
