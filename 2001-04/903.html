Yes, but the AUP means that if you drive it hard enough, you wind up 
being disconnected for abusing the service.

You can either live that way (on the edge) or pay as you go with 
other ADSL providers. It's your choice.

The AUP is *very very* restrictive. Go and read it. It prohibits, for 
instance, attaching > 1 machine to the service (i.e. that second 
ethernet card in your linux machine is a no-no). And allowing anyone 
other than your immediate family from using the service except under 
your direct personal supervision, and, and and and... they're trying 
to define the social environment in which you won't rip them off. 
Yeah, that'll work.

We don't choose to take the huge economic risk that Telstra is 
choosing with that product offering, it just isn't sensible for us to 
do.

I predict they won't have, and that ultimately Telstra won't have 
either. At least not for a couple of years - at which point the 
incremental cost of bandwidth will be low enough that it might be 
viable to do an 'unlimited' offering (i.e. limited, but limited based 
on peak download capacity).

Most people don't want 'unlimited', they just don't want to see an 
excess bill - which is not the same thing. Enough people interpret 
'unlimited' as meaning 'hello, please abuse your ISP to death' - 
which is the (social) problem that makes 'unlimited' unsafe in 
general (for ISP's).
