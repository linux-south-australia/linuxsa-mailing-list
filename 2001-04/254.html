On Tue, Apr 10, 2001 at 10:02:26PM +0930, Michael Kratz wrote:
> I have been trying for about 4 hours to install a src rpm of squid and it 
> just wont work. I think what I am supposed to do is, rpm -i squid.rpm then 
> cd to /usr/src/redhat/SOURCES or SPECS and the do rpm -ba squid.spec
> 
> But it does not want to work, it just lists the commands of RPM, how do I 
> install the rpm???

If you have Midnight Commander (type mc at command prompt) you will find
this a good tool to unpack rpm (and deb, tgz, etc) into subdirectories so
you can see what you get. It will also install and uninstall them and log
it's actions in /var/log/packages. It's a cool tool, a classic.
