DSL information for comparison to internode et al.

On the topic of DSL plans, CADvision have some interesting features.

(*) Download is unlimited. Uploading is limited
    from http://store.cadvision.com/desc/dedtransferlimit.html
    There are NO charges for the usage of download bandwidth on a
    CADVision DSL account. This means that you can download data
    without the worry of unexpected bandwidth charges. Upload
    bandwidth is limited to 1GB/day/month (2GB for Enhanced DSL).
    Excess upload bandwidth usage will be billed at a rate of
    $5.00/GB/day.

(*) a backup 56k access account. So while you're away from your DSL modem,    
   
    you still have the same, if not slower, access. Also, if the DSL
    network goes down, you can dialin using the 56k modem as a backup. I
    reckon it would be easy to configure a linux router to do this
    automatically.

(*) shell accounts. From what I can gather...

(*) proxies and NAT are allowed for all accounts, with "acceptable limits"

There plans:
(*) standard home use:
    http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store/dialup_dsl.asp
    1500kps down
    640 kps up
    1 dynamic IP
    CA$450 / year

(*) corporate connections:
    http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store/ded_corp.asp
    7680kps down
    1024kps up
    8 dynamic IPs
    CA$850 /year

(*) static ips - basic:
    http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store/ded_host_basic.asp
(*) static ips - enhanced:
    http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store/ded_host_enhanced.asp
Both have:
    up to 7000kps down
    up to 1000kps up
    8 static IPs
    CA$1200/year

Enhanced has:
    Option for more IPs
    the ability to subnet
    and critical response support

I think there Terms and conditions
http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store_terms.asp
and acceptable use policy
http://store.cadvision.com/accounts/store_aup.asp
are reasonable.
