Re: Internode ADSL - here at last :)

Andrew Reid wrote:

> Correct me if I'm wrong, but using the ADSL 'network' is putting strain
> on the link between Internode and Telstra's ADSL network. Another reason
> why even if Internode hosted a local mirror of Linux ISOs this wouldn't
> be feasable.

You're very right. Telstra require you to have a Telstra Frame-Relay or
ATM connection to their POP to trunk the ADSL data across. Even if it's
from one ADSL customer to another. If you don't already have such a
connection to Telstra, the cost is around $20,000 per month for a 2Mb
link.

Hopefully that explains the attitude the list is seeing from ISP-type
folk who don't want to have a single user tie up $15000 per month of
resources downloading VCD's 24 hours a day at 1.5mb/s for $79.95.
There's also a cost per port from Telstra, along with the cost of
internet data.
