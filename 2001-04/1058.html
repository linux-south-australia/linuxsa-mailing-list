This appears to be a string format exploit (%8x) rather than a buffer
overflow, not that it makes much difference to the end result.

2 helpful utilities to use now: lsof and stat. Not a part of most
rootkits, you can use stat to check the last modified date of system
executables popular with crackers (ps, netstat, w, login, su .. etc) to
see if they have been tampered with. 

You can then use lsof to look for TCP connections that aren't showing in
netstat -an. I guess you could also go trawling for connection info in
/proc as well..
