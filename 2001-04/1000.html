At 1:21 PM +0930 24/4/01, Jeremy Ervine wrote:
>I would pay more then $1000 per month for a 1.5mb with no caps and static

Interesting.

Pity that this is economically un-sustainable, but I appreciate the thought
:)

The carrying capacity of a 1.5 mb/s link in one month is 474,000
megabytes. So that works out (ignoring fixed costs of the ADSL
service, which is probably ok for a first approximation) to be $0.002
per megabyte.

Sorry, but I can't do that. Stay with Telstra, rip them off, not me.

You _did_ say no caps - the above is what no caps means!

Of course, you didn't really - mean- 'no caps', just that you wanted
our cap to magically be large enough for your needs. Unfortunately
with the size of these firehoses, that's a huge, huge economic risk
for us.

Worse, as we've found in the past with other services, if we said
'unlimited' and tried to disconnect or excess charge those who we
felt were ripping us off, we'd wind up with people trying to take
legal action to stay connected and to keep leaching from us. The same
people who can't find a few hundred bucks per month to pay what it
really costs. Go figure.

The simple truth is that what you're describing relies on other light
users cross-subsidising your heavy access. That is a risk/benefit
issue. Nobody else in the ADSL market in Australia other than Telstra
is doing it. That's because nobody else has Telstra's almost zero
true internal cost to run the links (since they receive the income
from the analog line rental for voice and mandate that they receive
it in order to supply ADSL).

Primus had a crack at it. Primus, who are much much larger than we
are. They no longer do - even they couldn't stand the bleed rate. We
certainly can't.

>We haven't even had it a month, and we have already exceeded the standard

I can't help you with that. Keep ripping off Telstra. You might as

Sincerely sorry I can't help you.

What we -can- help you with is 64k or 128k links with unlimited
downloads. We'll officially release the price points shortly, but
they're still going to be much higher than you will want to pay based
on your postings so far.

This is one of the problems with 'unlimited' everything - people
develop unreasonable expectations of what is fair to ask for as an
infinite benefit for a finite price. See the archives to see this
topic thrashed to death.

I would love to help you, but there is just no point in you running
us ragged, it doesn't help you or me.
