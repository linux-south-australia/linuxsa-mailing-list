I'll let Grant reply for himself regarding your specific questions.
However, you do raise a couple of interesting points...

Having used Debian GNU/Linux for 4 years, and having never *seen* a
Debian CD, I can sympathise with Grant's question.  Debian systems
have always been biased away from requiring people to down-load whole
CD images.  The CD is only useful during the initial install; after
that the (arguably more-important) *maintenance* is best done using
"apt".  So for most people, downloading the CD is a waste of time and
money. 

Furthermore, I read that an existing Debian installation could switch
to using Progeny Debian just by pointing "apt" in their direction.

Some good reasons why you might like to download the complete CDs:

  - You want to install Debian on many machines (e.g. installfest).
      (A very noble idea!)  (And, no, Grant, not all distributions are
      created equal ;-) )

  - Progeny Debian have no other way of distributing their you-beaut
      graphical installer.  (I don't know; see: http://www.progeny.com/)

(Richard, I know you will already know these things, but other linuxSA
readers may not.)
