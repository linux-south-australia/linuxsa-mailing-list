120MB of disk is cutting things a bit too fine I think. RedHat 7.1
without *any* extra packages selected is still over 150MB (surprisingly
less than some earlier versions). Depending on how long you've been
playing with Linux and how much spare time you have, you might be
interested in building your own Linux From Scratch (can't remember the
URL). You could also modify a tiny linux distribution (there's craploads
of them about). I quite like LEM (found at
http://www.linux-embedded.com/).

Using another CNET card wouldn't be a bad idea. If it works, you know
you're configuration was good, but the card was bad. If it doesn't work,
you're non the wiser. I haven't looked at FreeSCO, but if you do have
problems, it might be worth looking at the Linux Router Project
(http://www.linux-router.org/). It's very easy to configure.
