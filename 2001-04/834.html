Re: MS Curriculum at schools and TAFEs ...

My turn to disagree :-)
I completed my windows2000 pro and server edition training as part of TAFE
skills updating. The instructor was fine and reasonably knowledgeable on MS
systems.  (although getting distributed file systems to work was a nightmare no
matter how many mouse clicks RSI on the finger claim coming ;-))

The first question asked prior to the class was, yup you guessed it 'who here
uses Linux?' Linux was mentioned at least 3-4 times during every session.
Comparisons were made and in most cases Linux could do it much quicker and more
reliably CL made it much quicker. Imagine his shock when he learned we had 148
machines with Mandrake running on them 6 redhat servers for student use and a
Linux cluster in development, and offered 'choice of OS in the drop in centre
for practical work. MS is certainly IMO not in as strong as a bargaining
position as it once was.

We are dropping MS Official Curriculum (MOC) and are seriously reviewing our
need to be an Official MS Training Partner.

And not to labour a point, the driving force is vision, sure MS is still
dominant but we not give up on that side of training, we will just make sure we
cater to all sides.  Asia is a particular interest for us, as an institute we
see opportunities for training middle level techies in management / systems /
networking and e-commerce strategies.
There is more but you get the drift I'm sure

This is not confidential (although we might get a visit if MS peeps read this)

Also I spend time overseas every year and if you think Linux is making headway
albeit slowly here Linux in Europe is simply mind boggling.

Steve
