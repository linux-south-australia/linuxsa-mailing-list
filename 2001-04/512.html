Hi Mark,

>> Huh?...5 x 10Gb drive = 50Gb of total storage space...4 drives hold data
>> and 1 drive holds the parity (conceptually)...therefore 1 x 10Gb is used
>> for parity = 10Gb...therefore 10Gb out of the possible 50Gb is used for
>> parity...doesn't that equal 20% (1/N)...show me how you come up with 25%.

>40 Gbytes of useful storage requiring 10 Gbytes of parity overhead implies
>that the system is working with 25% overhead.

As Greg stated it depends were you stand. If the question is...

I have 5 x 10Gb HDD's and I want to implement RAID 5 how much space will be
used for parity?...the answer is 1/N = 1/5 = 20% of 50Gb = 10Gb.

...however if the question is...

I need 40Gb of data storage in a RAID 5 setup consisting of 5 HDD how much
extra space is required for parity?...the answer is 1(N-1) = 1/4 = 25% extra
space on 40Gb = 10Gb.

The above demonstrates both formulas are correct depending on the question.

>Well, yes.  It's altogether possible that Microsoft's implementations

>I'm left in a quandry:  Should I believe that the MCSE guide is

The third possibility is that the MCSE guide is correct (RAID 5 has better
read performance than RAID 1)...and both Linux and M$ implement RAID in a
similar fashion giving comparable performance...at this stage I am still
not sure which of the these three it is.
