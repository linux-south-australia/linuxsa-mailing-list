Re: Self help

On Tue, Jan 25, 2000 at 07:21:44AM +1018, Alan Kennington wrote:

 > In Unix, everything is possible and difficult.
 > In MS OSs, half the things are easy, and the
 > other half are impossible.

Compare this to the experience of a novice:  "In UNIX, everything is
opaque and complicated until someone shows me what to do, then I can
cope just fine via rote learning.  In MS OSs, everything is opaque and
complicated until someone shows me what to do, then I can cope just
fine by rote learning."

I simply do not believe that UNIX is any less user-friendly than
Windows -- Especially given that I have four years of experience with
UNIX training, where I was able to watch total novices become comfortable
in a UNIX environment (and become confident with some of the more
complicated concepts) in a matter of days.  No doubt other people have
experience watching novices become confident with Windows in a couple
of days too.

My point is not that UNIX is user-friendly and Windows isn't;  my 
point is that the entire concept of "user friendliness" is a marketting
sham, because by and large people will adapt to whatever they're used
to.
