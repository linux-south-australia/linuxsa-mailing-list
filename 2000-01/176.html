Re: Y2K (year=100)

A shorter way to get it would be:
perl -e '$_=(localtime())[5];/(..)$/;print "$1\n";'

This has the bonus of also working for years after 8100 AD. (Your code
assumes a 4 digit year. Although let's face it, it's not too much of a
worry, unless you're programming a very, very long range space probe.)
