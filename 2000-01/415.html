Re: tcp wrappers

Toby Corkindale wrote:

> I'm aware of the acl system, but I had in mind tcpwrappers as being an
> easy way to globally adjust access. 

acl allowed_net src "/etc/squid/access.net"

/etc/squid/access.net:
192.168.0.0/255.255.255.0
127.0.0.0/255.0.0.0

> IE. Changing hosts.allow to control access to telnet/ftp/apache/samba
> etc. without needing to restart any of the daemons.

Then you need an external authenticator (or several), and have it bear the
brunt of reloading its config file everytime it receives a request.  You
certainly can't expect squid to continually reparse a file just because its
contents may have changed; that would be ruinous for its performance.
