Indeed I did quote your post, but please don't misunderstand me.  By no
means was I calling you lazy or stupid, and in fact if you examine those
words of yours which I quoted you will see that my point was in relation to
your claim that unix is difficult to learn.  I apologise if you felt I was
sluring your intellect or work-ethics.

Since it's apparent that you misunderstood the jist of my message I had
better clarify: I reject that unix is hard to learn.  It is as easy to
learn as any operating system that I know.  It has to it's advantage an
easily available, diverse range of quality documentation, and in fact this
makes it easier to learn than many alternatives.

I made a secondary point, namely that the documentation is far too often
left unused.  I described the refusal to even try the documentation as lazy
or stupid.  As an educator, how would you describe a student who expressed
difficulty despite failing to read the texts?  Presumably you would find it
in your heart to use less endearing terms than "gifted" and "dedicated".
