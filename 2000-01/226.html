Re: UART

Yup this a midi (musical instrument digital interface) interface, MPU401 is
standard developed by Roland in the early 80's (think IBM XT 8-bit cards).
When Sound Blaster cards first came out, they had a different standard to
Roland's, which caused huge problems because people would spend $400
on a Sound Blaster, $300 on the midi break out box to connect their $2000
Roland keyboard, and find that none of the Sierra Adventure games actually
used the sound!

It wasn't til the SB16 that MPU401 was adapted as the standard, but even
then it wasn't strictly the same as the Roland standard.

Back to the problem, You should be able to disable the MPU401 so that it
does not conflict at all, because it is only used for the external midi
interface.
