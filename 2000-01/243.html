Re: localhost

At 06:07 AM 1/8/00 +1000, Richard Sharpe wrote:
>At 11:49 AM 1/9/00 +1030, Benjamin Close wrote:

>>Put your hostname in /etc/HOSTNAME and in /etc/sysconfig/network
>>Also make sure that is is assigned to 127.0.0.1 in /etc/hosts (assuming
>>you don't have a network card).
>>
>>Should fix your problems.
>
>Hmmm, a quick scan with find and grep and an eyeball shows that
>/etc/rc.d/rc.sysinit gets the info from /etc/sysconfig/network and creates
>/etc/HOSTNAME if it does not already exist.
>
>It also calls the hostname command to set your hostname based on what is in
>/etc/sysconfig/network.
>
>So, I conclude that setting /etc/HOSTNAME is redundent, and a BAD
>THING(TM), as you will have inconsistent files if you forget to change
>/etc/HOSTNAME in future.

Hmmm, on second thoughts, putting your hostname in /etc/HOSTNAME and in
/etc/sysconfig/network, and then changing it only in
/etc/sysconfig/network, is indistinguishable from putting your hostname in
/etc/sysconfig/network, rebooting the system and then changing the hostname
entry in /etc/sysconfig/network.

Thus, I would have to conclude that Benjamin's answer is the safe way to do
things, if anything depends on /etc/HOSTNAME, and as long as you change it
in both places ... if you change files rather than use programs to change
these things ...
