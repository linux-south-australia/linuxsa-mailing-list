HI again - LINUX rules SUPREME ;)
1. soldered a "bios" battery to the appropriate legs of the RTC/BATTERY
object - no change :(
2. soldered  "as above" to the BIOS chip - hmmm, that let all the smoke
out of it!
3. got another bios chip - stuck it in another pentium & set it up for
what I wanted then took it out
    and stuck it in the m/b in question.....lost some of its memory in
the "delay" - gave me a 1.44Mb floppy and A: C: CDROM but NO HDD's... NO
PROBLEM for LINUX..
4. stuck suse_linux floppy in fd0, stuck a scsi aha152x card & scsi
cdrom in as well & turned it back on
It doesnt matter IF the BIOS cant find HDD's - LINUX DOES find them, so
now I am happy!!

Currently setting X up on it! Hitting F1 when it boots is a small price
to pay for replacing the 486dx40 with a Pentium100 :)
