Paul,


You have a y2k-compliant VT-100 ?!
Lucky you.
That's a good staring point for a museum.

But on a more tedious point, this
"2000 ain't the millennium" stuff, really
no one should be wasting their breath.
It's the wrong issue.

It's the year-2000 rollover that's being celebrated,
not 2000 years of whatever.
I.e. it is not the interval of years that matters.
It is the actual date roll-over that matters.

To argue that only 1999 years of "anni domini" have
elapsed it missing the point entirely, especially since
the clock was back-calculated after it had run for
some 300 years or whatever.

Given the lack of time-points that are
really worth celebrating, it is good that the only
time-point worth celebrating in recent times
has been IT-related, and has brought lots of
focus onto the Internet and IT generally, as
opposed to basket-weaving or wombat-grooming.

An exciting astronomical time-point of
2.45 million Julian days happened a couple of
years ago, but no one took any notice. Only me.

Anyways, NY is coming up for bedlam in 14 minutes
by my clock. Let's hope that something goes wrong there!

Cheerio,
