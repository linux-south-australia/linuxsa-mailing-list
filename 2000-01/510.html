On Tue, 11 Jan 2000, Matthew Tippett wrote:

> 
> One thing I have heard rumblings about with Mozilla is the ability to change

Lynx has always allowed you to do this (see the [O]ptions menu) and for
this very reason too; to allow access to websites that discriminate
against lynx/linux. However a word of warning:  

NOTE that Netscape Communications Corp. has claimed that false
transmissions of "Mozilla" as the User-Agent are a copyright infringement,
which will be prosecuted. DO NOT misrepresent Lynx as Mozilla. The Options
Menu issues a warning about possible copyright infringement whenever the
header is changed to one which does not include "Lynx" or "lynx".
(Refer the lynx user's guide)

I suppose it's a decision to be made upon whether you feel copyright
infringement is the lesser evil compared with internet harassment (:
