Re: Linux Internet nanny software?

>  > We may be looking at providing dial-up access for staff to work, but the will
>  > most likely be able to access the net as well. Does that change anything?
> 
> Probably not; Check the definition of an ISP in the Broadcasting Services
> Act, though, to make sure.


Providing dial-up access to your company's "immediate circle"
(employees and officers) doesn't make your firm a service
provider.

Since you aren't a service provider, the obligations of the
Telco Act (concerning TIO membership) and the BSA (concerning
provision of filtering software, etc) do not apply.

Obviously, providing "guest" accounts would make you
a service provider, so you need a company policy that
forbids this.
