Re: Licensing and registration feedback

On Mon, 17 Jan 2000, Andrew Pullin wrote:
> Postscript is really just a Markup Language just like HTML, and so why
> not do everything Webby or otherwise in it (probably because HTML was
> invented).

PostScript is not an open standard, it's proprietary -- Adobe *own* it.
And it's totally *unlike* HTML.  HTML permits you to mark your text for the
purpose of adding Hypertext links.  It has very basic facilities for text
*layout*, which is why many people find it defficient for good document
preparation.  For example you bloody well do care that the title to your
picture appears centered under the picture, and in a small sans-serif font.
Frankly, you aren't prepared to permit the browser decide where it will
position the picture on the page, nor whether text will wrap around or not.
That's why you went to all of the trouble to exactly specify those things
when you prepared your document.

Postscript is a page layout language.  That's quite different to a markup
language.  In fact Postscript is a whole lot more than a page layout
language, being that it's also a general purpose computing language,
however page layout is it's purpose.
