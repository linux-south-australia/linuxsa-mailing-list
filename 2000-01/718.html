[ORGANISER-COMMENT]Re: Two Samba courses in Adelaide: 17-Feb and 18-Feb

Just thought it would be worthwhile to discuss *why* LinuxSA exists and what
I was trying to achieve when I started LinuxSA.

LinuxSA is a user-group.  It is a usergroup of people who use Linux in *any*
capacity.  

Irrespective of what the cost is, he is making the informing the group (and
yes I have asked him to add a COMMERCIAL or TRAINING tag to the subject which
he has agreed to comply too).

There are all sorts of people out there, and as a consequence there are some
who want some sort of monetary return for their work. Other will do it for the
sake of it.  We can have both, but if someone has gone to the effort then 
they are entitled to make the choice.

By the looks of it there is enough interest on the list for Richard to 
consider running the the course filled possibly with LinuxSA people alone.

Remember that this is a User group.  Which includes <HOME>-User, <OCCASIONAL>-
User and <PROFESSIONAL>-User (and many others who I can't think of at 
the moment).  Some things will be revelent and available to some types of
users, others won't.

Richard has my support for what he has done and offererd to the group, but
if someone wants to do something targetted at the HOME-User, then they
will have my full support aswell.
