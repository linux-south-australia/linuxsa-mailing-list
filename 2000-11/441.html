Re: Virus Guard Warning

On Mon, 13 Nov 2000, Gayan Godagama wrote:
> 
> My Trend ChipAway Virus guard give me a warning that 
my hard disk boot sector has a virus. This happened when I try to boot my PC
with Redhat Linux 6.2. Actually I have two operating systems, the other one is
Windows98, and I am using Bootmagic to select my operating systems once I start
my PC. What shall I do to avoid this problem? >  >                             
                                                                               
I suspect - nothing!  :-)
Try booting from your write-protected Win98 startup disk.  The message should
not then appear.  Whether it does or not, type "Fdisk /mbr" <enter>
This should restore the Master Boot record to plain vanilla Win98.
Remove the floppy and re-boot.  Message gone?  Good.
You can restore the dual boot using Boot Magic as before, and you will likely
again receive that virus warning when you reboot, which you can safely ignore.
The situation is that many virus protection programs (particularly those
attached to the BIOS) 'fail safe' and warn you if you have anything
non-standard, such as an altered boot sector.
Hope that is all you need to bother about.
