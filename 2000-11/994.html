Re: setting sendmail to use an interface

David Newall wrote:
> 
> David Lloyd <lloy0076@rebel.net.au> wrote:
> > I suspect if you had an ipchains rule that said (in English):
> >
> > REJECT ANY OUTGOING TO PORT 25 ON PPP1
> >
> > after
> >
> > ACCEPT ANY OUTGOIN TO PORT 25 ON PPP0
> >
> > you'd achieve the same effect....
> 
> I think not.  This would stop smtp being sent through ppp1, but would not
> cause sendmail to route via ppp0 instead.  Sendmail will attempt to deliver
> mail to the recipient's MX host, or even direct to the recipient, and unless
> that destination happens to be the small ISP on ppp1, the default IP route
> will send the packets out through ppp0.  Denying the packets doesn't change
> the routing decision.
> 
> Perhaps sendmail can be reconfigured to use a "smart relay" in the small
> ISP's network?  They're bound to have an SMTP relay for their clients' use.

actually, the two interfaces are eth0 and ppp0, and I want sendmail to
always use ppp0 (unless of course it is to be delivered locally)
