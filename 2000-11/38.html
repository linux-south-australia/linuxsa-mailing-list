I find it useful to use -geometry with apps in your .xinitrc so
that they startup where you want them. Basic usage is easy:

my_app -geom [width]x[height]+[x-offset]+[y-offset]

So, to run an "xclock" that is 90 pixels wide, 180 pixels high,
and which has its top left hand corner 100 pixels from the left
of the screen, and 300 pixels from the top of the screen, you
would use:

xclock -geometry 90x180+100+300

Note that geometry positioning is relative to the top left 
hand corner of the screen.
