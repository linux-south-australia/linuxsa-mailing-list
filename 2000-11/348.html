Richard Russell wrote:

Another point here is that HTML mail is quite often a risk
to run.  There are quite a few javascript exploits out for
Outlook & Outlook Express (there's a guy in BugTraq who
posts nearly a new one every week), and as Nick Morrison
noted once before, someone on this list sent an HTML message
that opened a http connection (without authorisation) back to
someone's web server.  Now there's a security risk not generally
associated with text messages.
