Why Alpha processes are called EV2, EV3, EV4, etc

----- Forwarded message -----

> Dear all, I thought this might amuse you.
> 
> I tried to find this report some years ago after talking to someone on
> early the Alpha design team but couldn't. By chance I came across a
> reference to it and guess what, it has re-appeared...
> 
> http://www.research.compaq.com/wrl/techreports/abstracts/TN-13.html
>
> In case you are wondering what the hell this is all about, the report
> concludes the effectiveness of the Dill and Kosher pickle in this
> research. One particular pickle was well known to the Alpha design team
> which had a brand name of Vlassic. Keeping the report in mind, when using
> this brand as per the report, it became known as the Electro-Vlassic
> demonstration.
> 
> Electro-Vlassic was a demo that the early Alpha chip design team liked to
> do. The chip designers [you can safely assume that they were a weird group
> to say the least] liked to attach probes to either end of one of these
> pickles, and then plug the other end of the wire into the electrical
> outlet [110VAC type that is]. By turning out the lights (in their
> conference room) it could seen that the pickle glows as the report
> verifies. Apparently it also started to smell VERY BAD after a while.
> 
> Some of you will have heard this before, so I hope I remembered it
> correctly. I'm not 100% sure of my spelling of the pickle's name but it's
> close to the mark.
> 
> You will notice that Electro-Vlassic can be abbreviated to EV.
> Now you know what the EV in Alpha EV4,EV5,EV6 etc. really stands for - no
> joke this is the real meaning!
> 
> BTW the corporate line is that EV "probably" stands for EVAX or Extended
> VAX. It does not. The real origin was not considered commercially
> acceptable (at least not back in the late 80's) but by the time it was
> sussed out, the chip was already somewhere between EV3 and EV4, the
> precedent had been set.
> 
> Regards - Richard
