I was bitten by this only recently.  It's good to be humbled
occasionally (but only occasionally, mind you).


>   for f in `find /home/shared/`
>   do
>     mv $f `echo \'$f\' | tr A-Z a-z`
>   done

Off the top of my caffeine-deficient head -

for f in `find /home/shared -type f -print`
do
  mv "$f" "`echo $f | tr A-Z a-z`"
done

find - search only for files (-print isn't necessary for GNU, but
is for other finds).
mv - quoted arguments.

HOWEVER!  My ol' spidey senses are tinglin' over the use of
find in a for loop; I suspect that files with spaces will be
processed as two seperate tokens (and a quick check confirms
this).  You might want to try something like

find /home/shared -type f -print | while read f
do
  # blah
done

I have no idea how the professionals do it.
