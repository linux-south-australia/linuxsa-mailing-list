Re: MS warns against naked PCs

On Sun, 26 Nov 2000, Alan Kennington wrote:

> But now that during the last couple of years so many really
> user-friendly GUI applications have come out, I'd have to amend that:
> 
>         With linux, half the tasks are easy, the other half are difficult.
>         With MS, half the tasks are easy, the other half are impossible.  

A lot of people find the following statement reflects reality:

    With Microsoft the more you know the harder it gets. With free
    software the more you know the easier it gets.

Draw a couple of sketch graphs on a whiteboard and people soon get the
idea. The entry skill level of Windows is lower than that of free
operating systems (all of them, including FreeDOS.) However there is a
level above "Power User" in one dimension and "Expert Developer" in
another beyond which Microsoft does not wish to encourage people to go. So
it gets harder from that point on.

Embedded, custom applications of operating systems often don't apply in
to the above, of course.
