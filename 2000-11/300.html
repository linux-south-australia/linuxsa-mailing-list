>MySQL or PostgreSQL are more than likely what you're after...

Don't forget Interbase, which is now Open-Sourced, yet was commercial
software up until recently. Not that it's easy to compile the source.. grab
the binaries if you're interested.

For those who are interested, the Telstra shop POS stuff uses an Interbase
backend. Unlike MySQL and Postgres, it seems to be equally capable for
Windows and Unix versions, something to consider if you're trying to ween a
business off of the Microsoft bottle.

I don't yet have an opinion on whether it's any better than other database
systems, but it 'feels' very solid (and slow), and has all of the 'proper'
database stuff like transactions and foreign keys.
