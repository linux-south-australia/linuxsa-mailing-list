there is something wrong there. of inetd is not running, your hosed.

I have not come accross this before, but I suppose if you had installed
used redhat'd installer and selected some sort of "workstation"
configuration, there might not be a need to install inetd. but this is
*highly* unlikely.

did you run your `find' command as root? I dont think pleb users can
`see into' /usr/sbin (which is where inetd is on my redhat system)

The other thing to consider, once you get inetd is that if your kernel
is configured to use unix ptys, then they have to exist before you can
log in, either telnet or at the console. This problem usually appears
after a customised kernel installation...
