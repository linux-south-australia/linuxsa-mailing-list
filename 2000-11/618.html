On Wed, 15 Nov 2000, Mark Newton wrote:

> On Tue, Nov 14, 2000 at 10:39:02PM +1030, michael wrote:
> 
>  > Not knowing how digitally signed emails work exactly, are you saying that
>  > the signature includes some sort of size/content checksum?
> 
> Yes.

Incidentally this is a big barrier to building efficient large mailstores,
even though it shouldn't be. Multipart mail messages have certain general
characteristics inherently and in the way they are used that help in
optimising storage... but not if they are signed. Unfortunately there is
just enough leeway in the MIME spec that you can never be sure whether you
can reassemble a multipart message the same way it was originally built.
Just one trailing space after a BASE64 component will make the checksum
fail, but the spec doesn't say whether the space should be there or not
and clients implement it in different ways.

This is a costly unfeature of Internet email.
