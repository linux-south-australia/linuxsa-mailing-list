Re: LT Winmodem!!!!! EEK!

Glen Turner wrote:

> I'm also a bit confused by all the anti-DSP feeling.  DSP-based modems
> can actually be more efficient than running a serial modem -- the
> interrupt rate is lower as the DSP-based modem can be programmed
> to assemble entire PPP frames.  For example, the Cisco 5300 and
> similar modem-servers are nothing more than boxes with hundreds of
> DSP-based modems.

The cheaper winmodems don't use a DSP, and the ones that do are getting
harder to find than internal 'real' modems (not that I've been looking
for them). I don't think anyone said that DSP's are bad.

Perhaps an open-sourced winmodem could upset a lot of companies making
expensive telephony equipment. What's to stop a winmodem from acting as
a voice processor interface for any number of applications? A winmodem
in a linux box would have much more value as a VoIP node on a phone
system than as a data modem anyway :)

btw, I thought it was the Cisco 5400 that was DSP based, the 5300 having
those Mica modem chips on the boards? Or is that the difference between
programmable DSP's and otherwise?
