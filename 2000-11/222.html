Re: Need help with recompiling of Kernel.

Matthew and Dmitri!

> > Hello. can you tell me if Linux will work faster on 500Mhz if I will recompile kernel?
> > and could you also tell me how to do it?

It almost definitely will if you know a little about what you normally
use, kernel modules and have the time to hack about and test for speed.
I think it would be reasonable to say that there is always scope to
optimise any particular piece of code; easy optimisations start at
changing the options to the compiler (eg changing the -O2 into -O3, read
info gcc), medium ones might be changing the compiler, difficult ones
might include hacking the source, ridiculous ones might be
dissassembling it and changing the assembly.

> If you want to recompile your kernel, there is heaps of documentation on
> the internet. Try www.linuxdoc.org an look in the HOWTOs.

Don't forget to look at /usr/src/linux/README as well. It contains
succinct, easy to follow instructions on how to build the kernel. I
would advise you to get comfortable just compiling a default-ish kernel
before trying out various optimisations - also become familiar with how
to get your system back up if you accidentally "kill" it during
testing...
