> Why not modify the mail list handler to strip/filter html attachments?

or why not let those who get upset about HTML mail delete it. I think the evidence is that HTML mail is here to stay, and even if you were to remove HTML mail from the LinuxSA mailing list, it will still prevail elsewhere. Why can't poeple just deal with it? Many text mail readers handle HTML OK (mutt seems to), and those that don't could probably do with some development so that they can... whether you call HTML mail progress or not, it really should be up to the receiver (at least, on a mailing list) to deal with it. You can't enforce your own preferences on a free-for-all mailing list of 400+ people (what are we up to now?). It's impossible.

PS note that I prefer text mail, even when using OutLook at work, but I can handle HTML mail as well.
