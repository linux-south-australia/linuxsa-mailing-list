Re: converting filenames to lower case

At 01:55 PM 11/13/00 +1030, Freeman, Peter (ERHS) wrote:
>Hi all
>Just setting up a Samba server to replace an Intel storage station (highly
>unrecommended product, had nothing but trouble with it!  Storage station
>that is not Samba ;-)
>(Good news is that Samba is running perfectly as part of our NT
>domain.........but then, whats new!)
>
>Now, most of the filenames on the Storage Station are in all-capitals, why I
>have no idea, but anyway.

Well, I think that early DOS actually represented names in all upper-case.

>Has anyone got a script laying around which will convert caps to lowercase,
>I'd like it to leave the first letter capitalized if possible, but the rest
>lower case?

Samba should be able to do the right thing for you.

However, converting to all lower case will be easier:

  for f in *
  do
    mv $f `echo $f | tr A-Z a-z`
  done

Of course, you will want to build something that walks the tree.
