G'day Lyndon,
		You could try 'tmpwatch' in conjunction with 'cron'.
Tmpwatch seems to be standard issue with redhat releases - mine lives in
/usr/sbin/tmpwatch, and it is run from /etc/cron.daily/tmpwatch. So if
you want to remove the image files and they are located in /tmp/images/,
then it is simply a matter of copying (as root) /etc/cron.daily/tmpwatch
to /etc/cron.hourly/tmpwatch, then editing the new file to:
/usr/sbin/tmpwatch  1 /tmp/images/ 
Now every hour tmpwatch will remove the image files that haven't been
accessed in the last hour.
Refer man tmpwatch and man crontab.
