> I can't understand why it is working now and not other occasions when I don't

It's very possible that you have some odd combination of components that
just don't like each other. When the computer boots they generally spit
messages about what's on which IRQ and such. Check them and if you have
two things on the same IRQ have a go at separating them. Some, if not
most, modern operating systems/mbs or whatever can cope with two devices
on one IRQ but I've seen phantom problems caused by this before...
