> > There are a number of problems with this (compiling your own stuff). Two
> > with Samba are:
> >
> >   - The RPM actually puts things (smbd, nmbd, etc) in different places
> >     than the Samba compile and install process does.
> >
> >     This is partially what you are experiencing. That and the fact that
> >     you do not have an smb script in /etc/rc.d/init.d. There is a sample
> >     script in the source.
> >
> >   - The configure.in and thus configure script supplied with the source
> >     does not compile in PAM, because there are too many Linux distros that
> >     either do not have PAM, or have the PAM libraries but do not use PAM.
> >
> >     I am trying to get a better test into configure.in, but in the meantime
> >     you probably will need to rebuild Samba to include PAM. Do this by
> >     reruning configure:
> >
> >     ./configure --with-pam
> >
> >     You might want to delete config.cache first.
