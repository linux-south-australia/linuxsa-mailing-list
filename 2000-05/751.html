Re: Oracle question...

On Fri, May 26, 2000 at 09:00:12AM +0930, Nick Morrison wrote:

 >  > Even the most die-hard Open Source entheusiast has to admit that 
 >  > Linux and the BSDs aren't even within shouting distance of Solaris
 >  > when it comes to high-end enterprise-wide systems.  Free Operating
 >  > Systems with mysql or postgresql have displaced Sun and SCO at the
 >  > low-to-mid end, but high-end UNIX is still almost exclusively Sun.
 > 
 > Why?
 > (asked purely out of interest :-)

Because of what Sun has which Linux doesn't have:

    - Scalability to 100's of CPUs on 64-bit architectures
    - Rational handling of gigabytes of RAM
    - Filesystems which are perfectly happy in the terabyte range
    - Hardware which isn't a PC :-)
    - A 20 year track record at the high end
    - The aforementioned close relationship between Sun Solaris 
      developers and Oracle developers, meaning that the very best
      platform to run Oracle on is Solaris.

Make no mistake: Linux is catching up, and with each year that passes
it nibbles closer and closer at the heels of vendors like Sun.  But it
isn't there yet.
