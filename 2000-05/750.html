fine. It's actually quite reasonable. Its implementation on various
platforms simply is broken to the extent where I wouldn't trust it in an

Warm and fuzzy...oh my! What's wrong with netcfg by RedHat? It's a
tcl/tk tool that is, essentially, an interface to ifup [whatever you're
sticking up]...kppp seems to create the most amazingly undetectable
problems.

GUI utilities on the Desktop are a Good Thing [tm]. GUI utilities in an
enterprise environment encourage people who have no idea what they're
doing to think they've suddenly become a "Unix/Linux System
Administrator". In my experience, a lot of companies would be better off
contracting system admin support to people who know rather than people
on-site, who only know a little bit, breaking things...

But, again, it's in my interest not to get too heavy handed on this
because I - and no doubt a lot of others - stand to benefit from the
money we are paid to fix the problems that shouldn't have ocurred in the
first place.
