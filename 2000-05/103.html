On Tue, May 02, 2000 at 11:34:46PM +0918, Alan Kennington wrote:

> I think it's odd that although almost no virus (in the sense of
> code infecting applications of boot records) ever seem to
> infect linux machines, they do seem to have a particular
> vulnerability to infection with DDoS zombies.

This is generally due to the fact that people don't bother to write
unix-based viruses; it's not worth it ... you require root access to do
anything "fun" and most sysadmins and unix users are cluey enough to take
precautions when installing software. This'll probably change as Linux
become more popular, althogh viruses seem to be taking a back seat to remote
exploits and elite hax0rs nowadays. :) 

Script kiddies want their ddos stuff running on Unix machines, though,
because Unix machines are usually servers, easy to gain remote access to,
and generally well-connected. Makes for a more efficient media circus.
