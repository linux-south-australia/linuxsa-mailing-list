Why should the fact that MS Office (or any other M$ product) existing
for Linux be a problem. It is a free market place, and it will shape how
popular it is.

Given that part of the success of M$ products derives from its market
domination of OSs it will have its work cut out for it to penetrate the
non-Windows market. It is unlikely to be a fdree product, and its
development costs will be significant, and thus the product won't be any
cheaper than the exisitng ones.

It also doesn't have the secret windows API to take advantage of for
speed, so it will have to write a tight stable application which will work
in a variety of configurations, and window managers, etc.

If it can achieve all this at a reasonable price, then I say this is a
good thing, we have another alternative for our desktop, it means I could
go to an organisation which is Windowes based with my Linux box and
exchange data with my CowOrkers with no problems.
