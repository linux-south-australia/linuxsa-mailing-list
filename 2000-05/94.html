Re: how do you stop site-downloaders?

[Duck for Cover = ON]

If you have a registered domain name and a reasonably cool site AND
someone decides to download ALL your files (for whatever reason), then
some people would consider this a success story. I guess what I'm saying
is that if one puts up a web-site for people to view world-wide AND I
decide to download the whole lot (because I'm running on a fantastically
slow ISP for example)...is this right or wrong or somewhere in between?

Web servers, by very definition, serve pages to web clients (ie browsers
or other programs that can act as clients). It's their role in life. An
analogy is that you place your brochures in a position where absolutely
ANYONE can take a copy. Glossy brochures cost a mint...but if you put
them out for the public to take, the public will take them.

Please note I don't disagree with limiting people downloading a huge
site or any such thing, I'm just interested in the non-technical
explanation. If you had unlimited bandwidth, for example, would you
STILL limit the downloads?
