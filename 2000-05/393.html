Muaaaahahahahahaha!!! (wget defeated!)

Yes folks, I'm delirious...ly happy with my anti-wget Apache module.

Just 4 hours after installing the penultimate stage of my "bwshare"
Apache module on my web server, someone tried to run "wget"
or something very similar on my web site.

They got away with 35 files, and then downloaded about 370
useless 503 status messages (service unavailable due to excessive
server load).

Here's the screen shot:

    http://www.topology.org/src/bwshare/screenshot5.html

After almost a week since 3 "wget" attempts in 2-3 days drove me
to write this protective module, I thought that I wouldn't get
another "attack". But there it was - while looking into the fine points
of presentation, I looked at my screen and saw
robot.pc.mycom.co.jp
trying to download 400 files.
And after 35 files, my module's pattern recognition clicked in and
stopped them. 
So now I can sleep a little more easily at night.
Every download of my complete site costs me about A$3.50 (to Bigpond).
And that's a lower-price permanent connection ISP.
