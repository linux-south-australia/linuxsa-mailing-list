I am one such enthusiast. Like everything Linux, the moment you make definitive
statements, they move the goalposts on you. Xisp for redhat offered the same
functionality, and same problems as kppp. Kppp has subsequently been replaced in
kde 1.1.2 and beyond with Ksaferppp. This new package brings a fresh set of
problems. All in all, if these guis work for you, fine, if they don't you are
better off in my opinion with a simple enuff script in /etc/ppp and a shortcut
link to the desktop to enact it.

In fact, neither script nor gui should be necessary for home desktops. While
a pig to configure, diald is the name of the game.

Just like Gnome enthusiasts, I think most can distinguish between being
enthusiastic about K vs Gnome wars while recognising individual packages on
either platform have their problems.
