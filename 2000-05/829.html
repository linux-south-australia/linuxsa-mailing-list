Netscape rushed JavaScript through the standards process with a neat bit of
forum shopping.  See:

  ECMA-262

downloadable from

  ftp://ftp.ecma.ch/ecma-st/Ecma-262.pdf

ECMA is the European Computer Manufacturers Association.  It is an
ISO contributing body, so EMCA-262 can be referenced in ISO standards
and it can be submitted to ISO to become an international standard.
I think this is unlikely in the short term, given JavaScript's woeful
internationalisation features.
