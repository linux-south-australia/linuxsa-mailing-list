Hi Mike

Just a point or two on winmodems.
Generally a winmodem is an internal (ie: PCI) card which is shipped with Windows
only drivers.
The only one I know of which works under Linux is the Lucent 'winmodem' found
in quite a few laptops (inc my Compaq M700)
They were nice enough to make available a binary only driver with a simple install
script.  Worked first time.
U made the point that USB modems are winmodems.  Fortunately this is definitely
not the case.
The only difference between a USB modem & a standard serial modem is that  the
USB cable transmits both data & power to/from the device.  Unlike the winmodems,
USB is not a Microsoft proprietary thing, its an industry standard, therefore
most USB devices should work under Linux if they conform to industry standards.
 In the case of a modem, these are the CDC & ACM standards.  Check out www.linux-usb.org
for more info + links to the latest USB kernel code backport for 2.2.14 kernels.
