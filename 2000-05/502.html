Re: multi mobo power supplies and fur seal trading

I don't think that this is quite correct. As far as I know, "Power Good"
comes from the PSU to the motherboard, and is supposed to go +5V when *all*
PSU voltages have stabilised. This signal, coupled with some simple logic on
the m/b will stop the CPU beginning to execute boot instructions when before
the PSU is stabilised and all other logic chips have had a chance to
power-up to their initial state.

The most likely cause of the problems is that, as far as I know, almost all
AT PSU power-supplies are "switched-mode" PSU's, and need a minimum load to
function. I think if you put a honking great full height, 5 1/4 inch old
SCSI or ESDI (ah, memories) drive or some such on the PSU, it will have
enough load to function.

With the motherboard unplugged, there is probably not enough load to get the
PSU to start.
