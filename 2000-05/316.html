Re: Linux Laptops.

On Friday, 12 May 2000 at 13:20:39 +0930, Tristan Ball wrote:
> G'day everyone.
>
> I've been asked to recommend a laptop for linux use, something that will
> do at least 1024x768 in X, and support ethernet, and preferably modem
> (ie no winmodem crap).
>
> Looking at something with a PIII, 192M+ 12G+. It's going to be used as a
> traveling engineering workstation, so it needs the grunt.
>
> Does anyone have any suggestions/experiences to share? :-)

I don't know of any laptop with a built-in real modem.  I'm using a
Dell Inspiron 7500, and I'm relatively happy with it, though the main
reason for the purchase was the 1400x1050 screen.  In the USA, it's
available with Linux preinstalled, but not in Australia (I took the
trouble to express my extreme disgust in being forced to buy software
which I wouldn't use to the manager of the ordering department at
Dell).

Before that I had a Dell Latitude CPi, which I also liked.  On the
whole, I find the Dell systems pretty solid.  I've run Linux on the
CPi (briefly), but so far only FreeBSD on the Inspiron.
