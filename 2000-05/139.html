Citrix Metaframe for UNIX

Hello all..

I just received some advertising material talking about Citrix Metaframe,
and how they've now released a version for Solaris.

http://www.citrix.com/products/metaframe/unix/default.htm

For those who don't know what Metaframe is - it was originally released
as a mutation of Windows NT Terminal Server that "enhanced" the already
amazing capabilities of NT TS.  [hopefully unnecessary *cough*]

To its credit though, it does a very good job of remote terminal serving.
I can log into work's Terminal Server over a modem and quite comfortably
use a 1024x768 desktop session to do administrative tasks, and it serves
applications to a couple of sites that dial in via 33.6k modem also.

Using X over a modem .. I've found .. can be painful.  I don't know if
there's something I haven't set up properly - like a compression tool,
or something - but it's very slow over modem.  Netscape grinds to a halt.

So Citrix have made Metaframe for UNIX.  It's said to enhance remote X
sessions in terms of speed and some other buzzy things.

Just thought people might be interested :)

Who has used X over a modem?  Are there ways to decrease its bandwidth
usage?
