Re: Superdisk Drives

On Wed, 24 May 2000, Phil Hutton wrote:
> Yep... I have one.  Works OK with RedHat 6.x.  Not sure if it worked out of the
> box... I've rebuilt my kernel many times since installation.  As long as you've
> got IDE floppy support in your kernel it should work.  Your LS-120 drive will
> appear as /dev/hdaX where X is a, b, c, or d, depending on how you connected
> your drive.  You'll probably need to add your own /etc/fstab entry.

one slight irritation is the difference between preformatted dos diskettes and
linux ones. just like zip, you need to 

mount /dev/hdX4  # for dos
mount /dev/hdX    # for linux

Be aware, that while the technology is very clever the workmanship is shoddy as
hell, these drives are notorious for not reading their own data. The worst
thing you can do is put foriegn standard floppies in them (ones used from
another machine). It is almost garanteed to destroy the
laser. I use LS120 extensively, and am very dissapointed with their poor
quality.
