Re: killing network connections

On Tue, 23 May 2000, Matthew Tippett wrote:

> There may also be a maximum limit ont he number of allocated sockets
> on a system.  You might have to dig around in the kernel to see what
> how things are allocated - it might well be that it is done dynamically
> and your system is limited by physical memory.

The limit does exist and is the same on all machines. 65535 sockets. The
first 1024 are reserved for programs run by root. I don't think you need
to worry either :)

The FIN_WAIT connection should eventually time out. If it doesn't and your
still worried then you can always take down the network card and then put
it back up (ifdown eth0; ifup eth0 - assuming redhat).
