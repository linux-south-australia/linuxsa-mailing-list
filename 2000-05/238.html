> Generation Linux May 2000 has come and gone.  Overall the event was a HUGE
> success!  The response from the children, parents and teachers was very
> positive.  The attendance numbers were excellent, the lab was full for
> most sessions.
>
> More than a few people were surprised at how easily the children took to
> Linux.  The children were enthusiastic and had fun!  I was amazed to see
> one boy go through the first part of the installation in less then 5
> minutes.
>
> I am happy to say that Generation Linux was rewarding and the results well
> and truly exceeded my expectations.
>
> The photos are at: http://www.cis.unisa.edu.au/~sara/gl/images
>
> Lee Anderson (journalist from LinuxWorld) flew out and did an interview,
> he took pictures of the event and also participated by helping the
> children.  He will be doing a full write up about the event.
>
> Conrad Parker (president of the Sydney Linux User Group) gave an excellent
> talk to the children about Free Software in Science.
>
> Thanks to LinuxMall.com we had 1000 Linux CDs to give away and 250
> penguins. The penguins arrived Monday, this was just in time for me to
> give the penguins to the children who did the Monday sessions (actually
> the first session missed out!)  The children loved them!
>
> I spoke to a number of school teachers and they are keen to run more
> sessions in the future.  I will put the documentation and Linux
installation
> instructions (which are specifically designed for children) on the web so
> that other people can run their own events.
>
> Thanks again to LinuxMall.com for the penguins and the CDs!
>
