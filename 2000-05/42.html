On Tue, May 02, 2000 at 02:57:31PM +0930, Jake Hawkes wrote:

 > seroiusly, the machine can take it, but the real question is, how many
 > hits per hour?

Often it is.  But webservers aren't usually that simple:  Hits come
clustered simultaneously whenever a client contacts the server (e.g.:
you might send the output from a CGI script and ten images in 
rapid quick-fire succession, followed by an extended period of "silence").

That means if you count the number of accumulated lines in your logfile
over the course of a day and divide by 24 to get the number of hits per
hour, you're often underestimating the peak load on the server by quite
a bit -- A factor of 5 or ten isn't unusual, because web server load is
bursty.

That being said, I think the P120 will cope :-)
