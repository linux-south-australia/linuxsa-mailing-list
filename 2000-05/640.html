On Tue, May 23, 2000 at 09:09:45PM +0930, Matthew Tippett wrote:

 > Now for those of you who use Linux in a more formal environment, I am 
 > interested in canvassing opinions as to what is a good backup configuration
 > for Linux.

We use Legato across all our servers and desktops;  there is a Linux 
client available.  That's probably beyond your price range for a home
system, though :-)

One open-source alternative which deserves a good look is Amanda
(http://www.amanda.org).  It's a network backup system;  one system
on your network is a backup server which uses the system's native "dump"
utility to archive the disks of the other machines on your network.
It's distributed under a BSD-style license, and has been at a high level
of maturity for years.
