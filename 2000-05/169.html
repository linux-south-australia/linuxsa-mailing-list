Re: Utilising spare HD

> My thought was to repartition the smaller drive (854 meg) into 2 parts,
> then use raid to gain some additional space (raid in linear mode I think
> it was).

You don't need to keep all your /home files in /home.  Disk management is
a standard part of system administration.  As your disks fills you will find
you need to balance their use.  Move subdirectories to your second disk and
install a symbolic link from the old place to the new.  For example, perhaps
you have all personal files in sub-directories of /home.  why not move your
own files to /h2, leaving all your other users as the sole users of /home;
put a symbolic link from /home/markd to /h2/markd.  Consider moving all
the source to locally installed software onto a new disk: symbolic link
from /usr/local/src to /u2/src.  Maybe move /var onto a new filesystem:
move all the files from /var to /mount, then remount the new filesystem
over /var.  (You'll have to shutdown anything writing to a file in /var
before you can complete the last idea.)
