Re: Linux Laptops.

Tristan Ball wrote:
> 
> I've been asked to recommend a laptop for linux use, something that will
> do at least 1024x768 in X, and support ethernet, and preferably modem
> (ie no winmodem crap).
> 
> Looking at something with a PIII, 192M+ 12G+. It's going to be used as a
> traveling engineering workstation, so it needs the grunt.

I'm running a Dell Latitude CPi PPX of expacty that specification with
Red Hat Linux 6.2 and are very happy with it.

The ethernet and modem is done by two PCMCIA cards -- so there are
no free PCMCIA slots.

Being Dell, if you need the free slots you can buy it without the
cards and put in a card of your own (like the recent 100BaseTX/modem
Cardbus card from 3Com: single slot, Hayes modem, no dongles).
