> Hello fellow LinuxSAers...
>
> I'd like to suggest that we should have an InstallFest this year, say in
> late August or September. I'm happy to do co-ordinate things as long as
> there are plenty of people willing and able to commit themselves
> to help in
> various ways. If someone else is interested in co-ordinating
> this, I'm happy
> to let you, I just thought I'd get the ball rolling... I've spoken to both
> Geoffery and Matthew, and they are keen, but don't have the time to commit
> to it...
>
> Also, anyone who has organised anything like this recently (..Sarah
> Bolderoff?), if you are able to offer any help at all, it would be greatly
> appreciated, particularly as I personally have no experience organising
> anything like this at all...
>
> Given the hype that Linux has had recently, we should be able to
> get a fair
> few people along (more than 100, maybe even a few hundred...), so
> there will
> be some serious organising to do. I've made a preliminary list of
> things we
> need to think about, so if anyone has any input on these (or, even better,
> offers of help), don't hesitate to speak up...
>
> * A venue -- must be big enough to hold > 100 computers
> 	with parking for > 100
> 	preferably has a network connection available (the faster the
> better, although a phone line to connect a modem to would suffice)
> 	even better would be a switching infrastructure...
> 	handy to have a place where we can have coffee, pizza, etc.
> 	also a whiteboard or five wouldn't go astray...
> 	preferably has >100 desks and >200 chairs, otherwise we need them
> too.
>
> * Switches/Hubs (10/100BaseT, preferably)
>
> * large numbers of cat5 RJ-45 cables (thin co-ax could be useful
> too, but...
> uughh...)
>
> * Linux CDs -- a variety of distros, but probably mostly RedHat for
> learners... maybe some others for people who like a bit of variety...
>
> * A few working boxes, preferably set up as servers, from which
> to do NFS or
> FTP installs, as well as demos of things...
>
> * desks and chairs.
>
> * Labels for people and items brought in...
>
> * hats or something to identify people who know (methinks we should have 2
> colours, to identify plain old Geeks, who can do most stuff, and god-like
> Gurus, who can (probably/hopefully) fix the things that the Geeks
> get stuck
> on.
>
> * givaways and sponsors... I think we could coax a few IT companies (like
> Compaq, IBM, SGI, maybe a couple of the local stores and wholesalers,
> Camtech(?), a few ISPs, Cisco, Dymocks, O'Reilly, The Bookshelf, RedHat,
> VALinux(?), everythingLinux, LinuxMall.com, NetCraft etc etc etc) into
> giving us stuff to give away (door prizes, promotional Tshirts), stuff to
> use (stuff on this list), and/or money (to et stuff on this list) in
> exchange for a plug... we could even invite them to send people along to
> learn and/or help and/or maybe sell stuff...
>
> * PEOPLE -- we need people to do installs, to answer questions, to present
> mini-presentations (What is Linux? What is the GPL? What's SAMBA? Why do I
> want Linux?), to check people in at the door (make them sign something to
> ensure they don't sue anyone if we lose their data, and make them
> label all
> their stuff and themselves), to set up and clean up, to find people places
> to put their computers, and so on. This is the _MOST_ important
> thing, so if
> people commit to this early, we can go ahead with confidence...
>
> * Pizza, coke and coffee.
>
> Remember, we mainly need people to get this to work. We can talk about it
> for a short while, but then we have to stat doing something about it...
>
> If anyone has any input, or can offer anything, please do...
>
> Oh, I also thought that maybe we could aim to do a smaller event, but make
> it a regular thing... perhaps bi-monthly... that makes it a
> smaller job, and
> gives us an ongoing installfest habit... but it could be too much
> commitment
> to do it 6 times a year... what do people think? Geoffery
> suggested we have
> a variety of Fests -- ConfigFest, SecurityFest, MailFest, etc...
>
> Anyway, any input and discussion would be appreciated, as would ANY offers
> of help, or an offer to organise it better than me :)
