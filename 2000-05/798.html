Re: IRQs in Linux

On Sun, 28 May 2000, Raymond Chin wrote:
> On IRQ 5, it has usb and bttv (which I know is my TV -tuner driver). How then
> do you go about disabling usb so that bttv will work? Note, I do not have any
> currently devices requiring usb support.
> 

simply disable USB in your cmos / bios settings at boot time. Although usb is
_supposed_ to be shareable interrupts, this seems to be a common complaint.

> I'm also interested to know what rtc and cascade are? 

originally the ibm XT had 8 interrupts available, when the AT came along, it
expanded this to an identical chip of another 8 interrupts. There is however
only ne irq line on the cpu, this makes it impossible for two interrupt chips
to both be serviced. Therefore, It is cascaded, because IRQ2 on the original
chip now informs the bios or os that an interrupt is somewhere on 8 to 15

rtc is the real time clock, of interest, it was the primary reason for the
expanded interrupts and uses the first interrupt of the 2nd chip (irq 8). An XT
had no such device.
