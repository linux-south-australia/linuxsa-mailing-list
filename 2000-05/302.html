Re: A Small Perl Question...

At 01:21 AM 5/12/00 +0930, MWP wrote:
>Hi all...
>
>In a perl script i have done the following:
>
>$text="hello";
>$out="text: $text 1234";
>print "$out\n";
>$text="goodbye";
>print "$out\n";
>
>Running this i get the result:
>text: hello 1234
>text: hello 1234

Yes, perl evaluates the value of $text when you assign the value to $out.

>I would like the result to be:
>text: hello 1234
>text: goodbye 1234

Try: 

print "text: ", $text, " 1234\n";
$text = "goodbye";
print "text: ", $text, " 1234\n";

Other solutions suggest themselves, depending on what you are trying to
achieve.
