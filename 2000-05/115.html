On writing presentations under Linux:

 - Applix 4.4.2  --  nice, but crashes repeatedly.  This is
     a bit odd, as the word processor and spreadsheet are solid.

 - StarOffice 5.1a -- less nice, corrupted my slides and
     the 'slide master' concept is broken.  You change the
     format slightly and all your slides go to the
     new default values even if you explicitly set a font
     (such as italics for variables or non-proportional for
     listings).

 - MagicPoint -- the input format sucks, a step back in
     time to the age of FORTRAN punched cards where you
     had to put content in the right column and line.

Unless someone suggests something reasonable, it's down to
SliTeX or MS PowerPoint.

Not just Linux but The World needs a half-way decent presentation
source format for developing presentations that have content rather
than graphics.

PDF is the ideal output format.
