Re: A-BIT BP6 dual-celery mobo experience?

On Thursday,  4 May 2000 at  5:04:46 +0900, Richard Sharpe wrote:

That's an interesting time zone and time you have there.

> I am thinking that an A-Bit dual-celery mobo would be a good thing
> (TM) for checking out Samba performance under Linux and FreeBSD etc.

Funny you should mention that.  I've just got what sounds like exactly
this combination: an ABit BP6 with 2 466 MHz processors.  I wanted to
use it, amongst other things, for checking out Samba performance under
FreeBSD :-)

> Does any have any experience? How is the stability?

I'm quite happy with it so far.  It keeps crashing, but that's because
I'm developing kernel-level code on it :-) It's quite a nice board,
comes with a total of 4 IDE controllers on board (two normal, two UDMA
66).

> Can you overclock say a 500MHz celery?

I suppose you can.  I'm not too interested in the marginal
improvement, so I'm not going to try.

> What is the actual performance increment of the second processor? 

That depends on the operating system and what you're doing with it.
I've found a big increase in performance when building the FreeBSD
world, but I can't give you a % value, and right now it's stress
testing Vinum, so I can't stop.

> What is the cache coherance protocol? Etc.

I don't know the details.  I don't think there's much choice in it.
