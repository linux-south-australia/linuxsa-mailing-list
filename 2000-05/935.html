You need to run 'lilo' (usually in /sbin/ and you need to do it as root)
after modifying /etc/lilo.conf as changes are only reflected in your MBR
once you do that.  

As for why it worked until Corel - all I can assume is that you made
your change using some wizz-bang graphical thingy (I don't know, kids
today) and it ran lilo on your behalf.  If you use linuxconf under
Redhat to modify lilo.conf I think changes are automagically done for
you.
