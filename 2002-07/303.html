> Firstly, to the best of my knowledge, postfix doesn't actually handle
> mail directly to your client programs.  You need to check with your
> POP3/IMAP server documentation as to how you would go about doing this.

no, postfix (as set up on my machine) accepts mail from my client (and
from the internet) and delivers it either to the internet (outgoing)
or to procmail (incoming). Procmail deals with the folder format etc,
and in my case, saves it all to Maildir format. My IMAP server
(courier) then serves IMAP to my clients, and all folder formatting is
irrelevant to my client programs, as they just talk IMAP.

I can't do anything with mail I send with IMAP or procmail, as they
never see my sent mail -- only postfix does... it gets an SMTP
connection from my mail program, does some magic, then makes SMTP
connections to the destination as expected... I want it to
automatically pass a copy across to procmail so I can keep a record of
all mail I send.
