Re: Samba Tuning

On Tue, 2 Jul 2002, Glen Turner wrote:

> Use the ext2 or xfs filesystems. 

Agreed. XFS wins for serving reasonably-sized plain files fast. Reiser and
ext3 are both slower, although each has something you might want instead
(nifty design with plugins for Reiser, and backwards compatibility for
ext3.) I have also found in some recent testing that XFS seems to have
very good for ACL with Samba 2.2.3 and 2.2.4 if you have up-to-date ACL
userspace tools. Posix ACLs are not the same as NT ACLs, but as far as I
know the 2.2.4/XFS 1.1+ combination is as good as you can currently get.

I haven't tried JFS2 with respect to ACLs but its performance was pretty
fair in terms of throughput when I did try it. JFS2 comes from an OS/2
background where the ACLs and EAs are more NT-ish than Posix. Anyone tried
this?
