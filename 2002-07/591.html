> Just out of interest, is /tmp really a sensible place for putting Unix
> sockets?

The question really is why you consider /tmp not at be a good place.  In
between reboots go ahead and nuke there, but on a running system you
need to put temporary data somewhere - and /tmp is for that.

Onto the other post Re: MySQL.  I don't like MySQL's use of
/usr/lib/mysql/mysqld.socket or whatever for it's socket.  When MySQL
goes belly up (a bug in my application when MySQL is in a transaction
means that) it corrupts the database and leaves the socket fd lying
around.  As a user I shouldn't have to manually remove forgotten
sockets, but if I do, it's much easier in /tmp that /usr/lib/mysql.  My
BOFH may chmod 777 /tmp on a server for me, but not /usr/lib/mysql.
