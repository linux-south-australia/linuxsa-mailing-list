Linux as a test OS

Hi,
I've been lucky enough to be employed by an computer wholesaler, that
imports/assembles computers & I am interested in the merits of making a
Linux Bootable CD-ROM to provide a basis for hardware testing before a
computer is shipped to a customer.

We have some DOS utilities at the moment, (AMI BIOS, ISA/PCI post card
etc.)

Currently we dump a Win98 Ghost Image. Now Win9x is a sloppily-written
OS that tarts around & works on almost anything, but a proper 32bit OS,
(Linux, W2k, XP, OS/2, NT...), will really stress hardware.

What are peoples learned thoughts on this?
Would my idea of a CD-ROM with testing utilities on it be A Good Thing?
