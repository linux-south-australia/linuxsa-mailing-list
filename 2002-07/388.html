Re: [Kinda OT] Corporate Backup Systems

Hi.

Having had to migrate from a simple DAT backup on linux to a fancy Windows
backup system, I can offer some comment.

1. The interfaces on the Win backup products all suck. I find BackupExec
better than ArcServ, but getting a reliable unattended backup is not
easily achievable on either. Getting a restore happening is even harder.
Classic case of the designers creating a pretty interface over a
functional one.

2. Try to find software that is smart about managing and tracking tapes,
and also prevents accidental overwriting of previous backups (like on
Monday of a long weekend, or when someone forgets to swap tapes one day)

3. Looks like you are going for an all-in-one solution (One box, files and
backup system) We found that too painful on Windows, but it will probably
work fine on *nix. Our issue was not being able to bounce the backup
system (don't ask) because people were using the data - that is way less
likely to happen on *nix.

4. We're using DLT tape drives for about 170Gb of files and data, which
fits on one tape with compression. Tape drives will be the biggest expense
in your system, so choose carefully. We actually have 2 drives, one for
the nightly complete backup, and one for transaction log backup during the
day. Eventually, we will have to cascade the nightly backup over the two
drives. The DLT's (Quantum) have been trouble free so far (18 months)

5. If you have space (and execution time) available, I'd reccomend keeping
2 copies: 1 on the tape, and one on another system on your network. Others
have mentioned this and the need for offsite tape storage.

6. As far as your existing system is concerned, We went through similar
pain. The answer for us was to upgrade to the latest BE, and hassle
support for answers to the crazy problems as they arose. Eventually, we
had a reliable backup running. Backup Exec is painfully pedantic, and will
classify an entire backup job as failed for the most insignificant
problem.

Good Luck,

Michael
