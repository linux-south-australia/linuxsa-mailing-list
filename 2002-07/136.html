Re: [OT] ISP anti-spam

On Thu, Jul 04, 2002 at 05:11:12PM +0930, Matthew.Cook@tekelek.com.au wrote:

> My personal opinion of SpamAssasin from an ISP perspective is; until a
> person/user asks for their mail to be filtered for SPAM then ISP's should
> by default leave it off.   That being said, they should also advertise to
> their users that this capability exists.   If you allow people/users to
> make informed decision regarding their "choice" of receiving SPAM or
> SpamAsassin filtered email then no one can accuse any ISP of Invading one's
> privacy.

SpamAssassin (after a very slight configuration change) will only
change the headers of the e-mail, leaving the body in-tact. The vast
majorirty of e-mail identified by SpamAssassin /is/ spam.

If you do have a problem with it, I'd suggest that it'd be better to
just complain if and when you start getting propper messages being
tagged as spam.

Since the start of June, one of my mail servers has identified 14739
e-mail messages as spam (and counting). That's nearly 15000 messages
that our users would normally have to go and fiddle around with
themselves.

We don't delete the e-mail automatically, but we do suggest to our
users to filter it into a "spam" folder which they can periodically
check and empty.
