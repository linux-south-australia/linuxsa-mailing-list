Re: Founder of "Enlightenment" thinks Linux on Desktop is dead

I like 'E' its a cool window manager. That said, it does not mean that the 
creator, Aka Rasterman is fortune teller.
Like all people he bases his views on his current perceptions of how things 
are progressing in Linux Land.

Some of us have different experiences and hence different views on how well 
Linux will do/is doing.
What can be said with some certainty, however, is that things change, its 
the natural order of 'things'.

What some people forget is that the world is BIG and POPULATED and 
POOR.  In the grand scheme of things, Microsoft is doing really well, on 
the desktop and, that's if we look in our own backyard.

The Western countries have certainly adopted Microsoft on the Desktop, but 
Microsoft's other war front, the server, is not fairing so well. Halting 
Microsoft on the server front should not be underestimated, particularly as 
'as SUN puts it' the Internet is just a big network. Once the server war is 
won, and, of that not many people would disagree, although it is not 
certain, the desktop becomes easier. The adoption rates in poorer countries 
will accelerate regardless on how much money Microsoft spends buying 
favours from politicians and power brokers.  Ordinary people will look for 
an edge, a fix, a solution, a means of creating a better standard of living 
for themselves and their children. That can only be done if the tools are 
available for them to use, GPL'/OSS anyone?

With China, India and the East in general making up a huge portion of the 
population there is no alternative to FREE.

Also don't forget, that governments are just people, China might want to 
keep people in the dark, America might wake up to the fact that Big 
Business is not the be-all and end all of power.  Worldcom anyone? 
Governments change when new people take the helm.

Europe might want to compete with the USA on the IT front, using and 
supporting US companies is not the best way forward. The list is endless.


Sheesh time to end rant,  the common Linux desktop is inevitable (IMHO)


Steve D
