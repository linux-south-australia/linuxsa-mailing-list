Thoughts on Peanuts

I am interested in hearing from anyone that has experience with the Peanut
Linux distribution http://www.ibiblio.org/peanut/.

It interests me for these main reasons:
* It easy on hard disk resources -as the name suggests (600 Mb)
* .rpm compatibility
* Small download (220 Mb)

I can't help but wonder if it is just a cut down and 're-packaged' version of
Red Hat as releases seem to coincide with RH i.e. it is currently at version
7.3.  Also backing this case is RPM compatibility and the website stating that
it is based on RH.

I don't want to get into a distro debate because I really am looking for a
small operating system.  I have some 486 boxes that I have lying around with
limited hard drive space but would also concider using it on my main desktop.
I am also looking a ZipSlack and DragonLinux as other mini distros.  I am
currently in the process of downloading 7.3, what is the verdict?
