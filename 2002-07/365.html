Re: Squid proxy server

On Fri, Jul 12, 2002 at 03:59:50PM +0930, David Lloyd wrote:

> > cache peer parentcache.foo.com parent 3128 0 no-query default
> > acl all src 0.0.0.0/0.0.0.0
> > never direct allow all
> 
> If you want the local squid to perform some type of authentication then
> that acl line probably should be changed unless sina itself will
> authenticate the users (I'm not sure)...

SINA does the authentication. It is possible to get Squid to
authenticate to the parent cache (rather than passing the
responsibility down to the client) and then do your own accounting on
the Squid box, but thanks to the way SINA does, well, everything, it
makes it a damn pain in the neck to make it all work happily.

Luckily, the SINA contracts for most schools will be coming up for
renewal shortly. I'd expect to see a huge amount of them flocking to
alternatives. Anything that doesn't have either of the Government or
Telstra involved should be a marked improvement.
