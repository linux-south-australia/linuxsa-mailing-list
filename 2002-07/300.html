Re: postfix auto bcc

In the immortal words of richard@yellowgoanna.com (Richard Russell)...
>  I use a number of different email clients at different times, but
>  almost always the same email server (postfix). Each email client
>  maintains its own copy of sent mail... which means I can't look at
>  the mail I sent from one client while using another client. I've
>  cosidered trying to make each client save its own sentmail in a
>  common IMAP folder, but I have a feeling that it may be easier to get
>  postfix to do this for me... anyone already do this? -- I'm thinking
>  that I want an auto-BCC or something... any ideas?

Firstly, to the best of my knowledge, postfix doesn't actually handle
mail directly to your client programs.  You need to check with your
POP3/IMAP server documentation as to how you would go about doing this.

The other thing to worry about is wether or not the different client
programs are on different machines.  I use Sylpheed personally, which
uses the MH mail folder format and so is (I believe) compatible with
mutt/elm/other MUA's.

Again, this all depends on your preference to using IMAP or POP3 (over
SSH of course).

Perhaps some more information is required.
