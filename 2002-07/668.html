> Yeah i've got xfce, dont use it much, but it looks kinda nice, good for an old
> computer. But CDE is ugly, inflexible, hard to switch between windows, etc... 
> Also, the way they've set it up, it's not obvious what desktop apps are

The beauty of CDE is that it's a desktop "standard". CDE on Solaris
works the same as on HP/UX, AIX or Ultrix. Apart from default colour
schemes, it even looks the same on all those Unices.

CDE, is as its name implies, a Common Desktop Environment, and from
that point of view, a "good thing".

However, by todays standards, it's ugly. ;)
