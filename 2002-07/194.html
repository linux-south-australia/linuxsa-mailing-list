Re: Calculating Netmask Values

On Sun, 7 Jul 2002, John Edwards wrote:

> 
> >A host address of all 0's is the network number, while a host address of
> >all 1's is the broadcast address, and you need a router as well in most
> >cases. So, you get two (or three) less addresses to play with. Also, a
> >netmask of 254 in the last byte is useless (why?).
> 
> I wouldn't say useless. Most Cisco equipment with recent software supports
> configuring a /31 netmask on an interface, which helps to save address space
> for point-to-point links. There's no need for broadcast when there's only
> one possible destination anyway.

Thanks. Forgot about that ...

> Where you have IP's aliased to a system for redundancy or political reasons,
> /31 and /32 routes can be handy to glue things together without wasting
