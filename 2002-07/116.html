>> While I find spam slightly annoying, I simply don't find it that
>> difficult a process to drag the obvious emails into my spam folder each
>> morning.
>
>However, you don't get hundreds of e-mails each day and download them

Try 1.5k emails per day, 25% of which are SPAM over a 33k6 dialup.   I
certainly know your fustration !    The longer you have your email address
the worse it gets.   Until SpamAssassin hit the scene there was no hope to
combat spam apart from very long filter lists.

My personal opinion of SpamAssasin from an ISP perspective is; until a
person/user asks for their mail to be filtered for SPAM then ISP's should
by default leave it off.   That being said, they should also advertise to
their users that this capability exists.   If you allow people/users to
make informed decision regarding their "choice" of receiving SPAM or
SpamAsassin filtered email then no one can accuse any ISP of Invading one's
privacy.
