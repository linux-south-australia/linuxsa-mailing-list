Re: whois help

hi wayne,

if your domain is a .com/.net/.org / random TLD the following maybe
useful:

- Record expires on 16-Nov-2002.

this is indeed the expiry date - different registrars act differently but
most require you transfer your domain name to a different
registrar at least 60 days prior to the domain names expiry date... so if
you're looking to move the domain to a different registar then now is the
time to think about it.

most other TLD (top level domains) will keep the domain name registered in
the root servers till at least the expiry date and normally at least 30
days
after. Then, after the domain has expired the domain is up for grabs.

Now, if the below is a .com.au domain name the following is relevant:

my guess is you're querying the now defunct aunic database. the new whois
server (whois.ausregistry.net.au) won't actually provide you with a
renewal/ expiry date.

IMHO and not speaking for AUDA directly:

the lack of renewal date is a part of the new AUDA.org.au regime that is
trying to crack down on
troublesome unsolicited faxes. If you're not aware of the situation, folks
with interesting business
practices address faxes / snail mails to .au domain name holders
asking them to renew domain names as a reseller for a registrar.

these faxes take the guise of a formal looking invoice thus prompting a
"blind" payment by the unwitting customer.

if you're looking for a renewal date for a domain name - contact the
registrar (if the domain was registered prior to jul 1 and is a .com.au
you have a 90% chance it's melbourneit.com.au) and get them to tell you
direct.


hope this helps.
