RE: tr - broken pipe

Ive had a new toy on my desk for while now - a dvd-r. Ive hit many a 2gb
limit. Heres some tips.

Some programs arnt compiled with > 2gb support.

Your shell is probably is OK. Thus shell pipes can get you around some
programs 2gb limits.

Eg  mkisofs -r -J -o test.iso .   will die @ 2gb, 
but mkisofs -r -J . > test.iso    will work.

The simplest way to get huge files copied over the network seems to be:

on reciever:

nc -l -p <port> > bigfile.blah

and on the sender:

cat bigfile.blah | nc dest <port> 

Ive noticed a suprisingly large amount of programs in my distro (mdk
8.2) die at 2gb (mkisofs, proftpd for 2). Still I hope this gets you on
the right train of thought to work out a solution for you...

Of course your file system needs to be able to cope with > 2gb, but im
95% sure you wont have any problems there.
