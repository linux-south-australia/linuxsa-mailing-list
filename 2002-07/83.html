Re: Ex-dynix terminals

Robert Mibus wrote:
> "Dynix" systems used by libraries in schools across Australia are
> being turned off and thrown away. There's a few WYSE terminals available
> from Hallett Cove School. No warranty or anything ;-), email
> grove@hcs.sa.edu.au for more information.

For the information of people on the list, the Wyse terminals
make fine RS-232 serial B&W terminals for Linux.  They were popular
in their time and have a reasonably small form factor (more like
the DEC VT220 than the DEC VT100).

Some early terminals used for Dynix had the terminal key-caps changed
('.' to 'Back', from memory).  You might wish to ask Robert if that
is the case with these.

It would be worthwhile inspecting the screens for burn in (where
the shadow of popular screens remains displayed forever).  You
don't need to attach a computer to do this, simply power on the
terminal and turn the brightness and contrast way up.
