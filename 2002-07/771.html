 --- "H. Raghavan" <hraghav@lsrc.mahyco.com> wrote: > Dear Friends,
> Even though this problem is not exactly related with 
> Linux, I hope somebody will reply.
> Can a LAN say 192.168.30.***   have a gate way 10.15.20.***
> Will it work. I am confused because LAN and gate way or on different
> IP 
> class and different subnet.

I won't go into an extensive discussion of subnetting here, the short answer is
no. You must have a gateway on the same network. The network is defined by the
subnet mask. The most different you could have the gateway is 10.x.x.x with a
net mask of 255.0.0.0. What you'd need is a routher sitting on both networks
acting as the gateway between them.
