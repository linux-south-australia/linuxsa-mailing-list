Re: Founder of "Enlightenment" thinks Linux on Desktop is dead

At risk of top posting,... but the trend has been set with this branch
of the thread...

I spoke to a LAN administrator of a 2000 odd seat enterprise, who last
week had a Microsoft rep call them up to inquire about their plans on
upgrading to XP....

Lan Admin: We are not planning to upgrade... It offers no benefit to us
or our users

M$ Rep: But it has new features.... (insert more waffle here)

LA: Which no-one here will ever use and we will have higher tech support
calls..

Anyway, to cut to the point, the conversion went around in circles a few
times.

Don't forget, Microsoft's biggest competitor for the desktop is their
previous version. In a lot of business large and small, a large
percentage of computers are still running windows 98 (which I think is
the best MS OS to date, small simple and very little for the users to
mess with)....

Just a few thoughts...


George Patterson
