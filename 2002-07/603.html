Re: Founder of "Enlightenment" thinks Linux on Desktop is dead

Not too mention the linux apps are appearing. These days there are
numerous browsers that are pretty good, evolution for mail (with
commercial, but cheaper than M$ exchange server plug in), nice desktops,
the blender3d thread below, star office with official support from sun,
vmware and crossover office if you really need it, etc etc. The apps
will keep filtering in, and the more there are the more of a more viable
alternative it becomes.
