CentreLink / IBM back Linux

Centrelink, Australia's Social Security department,
 has just signed a big deal (said to be AUD$150 million)
 with IBM to develop a new way of handling their
 transactions. Guess what they want to use to build it on?

 "Centrelink hopes to cut costs on the 2.9 billion
 transactions it does each year, and to offer new
 services such as self-service at Centrelink branches
 around the country. 

 As part of the project Centrelink will create a
 laboratory to develop the Linux operating system, a
 universal software language that does not require the
 same licensing fees as other programs such as
 Microsoft Windows. "
