My advice is be brazen; I started my career in Linux by sendind an e-mail
to one of the list members basically saying I have no experience but I'm
willing to learn. It took a while for something to materialise but I haven't
looked back since.

Don't concentrate on the negatives, concentrate on what you have to offer
and make sure you can convincingly state that you'll either learn the stuff
you don't know through your work experience or that you'll consider studies
further.

And don't ignore the "Job Posts" that require experience; sometimes it
takes experience to put an application together. Put it together as best
you can ANYWAY; if you want be really blatant about not having experience
but make sure that you are noticed.

For example, a letter stating that "I notice you have a Linux position offered,
I'm looking for experience blah blah blah blah" to potential employers,
in my opinion, can't hurt.
