Re: Printers which will work with Linux

Hi.

I second the suggestion of a laser. We do quite a lot of printing at home
on an old LaserWriter, and it costs us about $100 every couple of years
for a toner cart. The colour inkjet we have does a few kids projects and
is chewing through over $100 worth of ink a year. I wouldn't be scared of
buying a good secondhand laser from a reputable dealer either. You land up
with a higher spec machine with more robust internals than a cheapie new
machine for the same money.

You also land up spending more time futzing with the inkjet to solve it's
inherent nozzle clogs and paper feed problems.

I think Lexmark now has an inkjet on the market that costs less than it's
replacement ink cartridges. that just confirms my suspicions about the
business plan of the inkjet mastubfacturers.

regards,

Michael
