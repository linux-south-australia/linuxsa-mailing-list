Under RedHat 7.2 and greater, python comes in two flavours.  Vanilla
(aka python1.5.2) and chocolate (aka python2.2).  Python1.5.2 is invoked
as "python", whereas Python2.2 is invoked as "python2".

Pesumably Red Hat do this as they have some serious sysadmin scripts
using python1.5.2 and they don't want to risk serious breakage.  And
Guido allowed a bit of API breakage.

Not sure what the duck does, but one way to find all files related to
your python install(s) is to do a "rpm -qa | grep -i python | xargs rpm
-ql | less".
