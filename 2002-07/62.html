Quoting Matthew Moyle-Croft <mmc@mmc.com.au>:

> Try u/g the kernel from the RPMs -> should give you ext3 as well!

NO!  Bad advice.  Do NOT upgrade your kernel via RPM, but rather install 
another one (ie rpm -ivh ...)

Multiple kernels can be installed (ie they do not supercede each other) and if 
you upgrade, the old one is removed.  You don't want that just in case there's 
a problem.  Leave the old kernel there until you are very sure everything is 
fine and dandy.
