local, even though my live access isn't up to much. I guess this means we
can be less concerned about image sizes and so forth, disk space
permitting!

General Suggestions:

1) More information on what goes on at meetings. The web page doesn't
really say too much. A narrative of a (or every?) meeting would be good,
even better with links to pictures of key speakers, opened computers with
guts spilling out installing Linux etc :)

2) Statistics! Don't know why, but I love statistics. First the obvious
things, like head counts at meetings, computer counts at meetings, mailing
list members, etc. 

Then something else, for instance a bi-weekly survey could ask those on
the mailing list to tell what operating systems they are using. Then we
could graph it and put it on the web pages. Hopefully month by month the
Linux bar would go up, and the Windows one down :) 

I don't see that this could be too difficult to setup. GNUPlot could do
the graphs, I could probably accept the survey data on my box...

Maybe I'm getting crazy here :)

3) Mailing list archives. Indexed and searchable, of course :)
