On 14-Feb-98 mtippett wrote:
> People, as the person who started LinuxSA and being one of the main
> organisers I feel
> that I should make a few comments.
> 
> After being away from mail for the last couple of days and downloading a
> couple of
> hundred messages earlier today I saw a mini-flamewar about questions on
> various parts
> of Linux.
> 
> Take this as the founder's strategic vision of sorts.
> 
> When I started LinuxSA I had a couple of goals in mind.
> 
>       o LinuxSA was intended to be a Linux support group.  In no way did
>               I want LinuxSA to deteriorate into a group of people who
>               play around and hack at the kernel.  In a lot of ways I feel
>               that LinuxSA has acheived that.
> 
>               Although I have may have delusions of granduer (sp?) I feel that
>               LinuxSA is one of the few LUG mailing lists that has a
relatively
>               small amount of either waffle or off-topic talk.  Most of the 
>               questions that people ask are either responded to promptly or
>               people will direct the asker to appropriate references.
> 
>               The fact that Trevor was using LinuxSA as an indication about
the
>               level of support that Linux *really* has is testament to this.
> 
>       o LinuxSA was intended to be for all people at all experience levels.
> 
>               Depending on one's age I am sure most people remember making
>               some pretty stupid assumptions as they started playing with
>               computers.  I would be ashamed to say some of the things I
>               struggled with when I was first starting out.
> 
>               Linux/Unix has a very steep learning curve, but I am sure
>               most people who use Linux would agree that it is well worth it.
>               
>               As a side effect of this there are people who are at the bottom
>               of the incline and some who are in the middle and some who are
>               almost to the top.  It is just par for the course.
> 
>               Some people are eager to get things going straight away (a la
>               QuickStart people) I am one of them.  But I also remember the
>               difficulty that I had coming to grips with some of the more
>               esoteric aspects of Unix.  I am by no means a guru, or even an
>               expert, and I too occasionally ask questions.
> 
>               Probably the most difficult part of Unix is understanding where
>               the resources and references are.  Most Unix references are just
>               that, references.  They are intended to be used to confirm/deny
>               in most cases what a user has assumed.  I feel that it is more
>               important to help people where possible, and point them in the
>               right direction for not only the answer, but also where to
learn 
>               more.
> 
>               Sometimes experience clouds what is sometime less than obvious.
>               Leave your field of experience and you would be suprised how
>               difficult it is to get just a base level of knowledge.
> 
>               I hope that LinuxSA will allow anyone, of any level to 
>               be able to find answers in an appropriate fashion.  If some
>               people find that they get frustrated by 'newbies' asking
>               questions which appear to be blatantly obvious then I personally
>               ask them to let those who don't get frustrated answer those 
>               questions.  It could be worse, having flamewars and off topic 
>               chit-chat on a regular basis.
> 
>       o LinuxSA was intended to be maintain a high level of professionalism.
> 
>               LinuxSA has been operating for about 24 months now (actually I
>               held the first meeting probably 30 months ago but I don't think
>               that 4 people count as a meeting :).  In that time I can't
>               think of any *real* flamewars or even many occasions of
off-topic
>               threads.  To that regard I think LinuxSA has remained focused
>               and has a very high usefulness.
> 
>               I am subscribed to most other Australian LUG's mailing lists, 
>               and have seen major flamewars and just general chit-chat in
>               very high levels elsewhere.  I am sure that some other members
>               would probably agree, (I know who you are).  
> 
>               I feel that LinuxSA has alienated very few people and hopefully
>               has helped more than enough people to make up for the 
>               possible millions of packets that it has sent over the Internet.
> 
>               I applaud and sincerely thank every person on this mailing list
>               for making LinuxSA what it is.  The fact that Trevor used the
>               mailing list to convince a company to set up three Linux sites
>               due to the real support that Linux has in Adelaide stands as
>               testimony to this.  To even drive the point home Trever did
>               not even have to send a 'on your best behaviour' email to
>               effectively 'Package' LinuxSA, we did it on ourselves.
> 
> This has got a bit long so I will cut it short here, but LinuxSA is headed 
> in the direction that I had intended, and it has taken every member to get
> us where we are.  
> 
> I could go on more, but enough bandwidth wasted, ask me next Tuesday if
> you want me to go on, but be prepared to listen :)
> 
> Thank you all for getting LinuxSA to where it is.  I feel it is a useful
> entity in the Linux Community in South Australia, and I hope we can all 
> get LinuxSA even further.
> 
> Have a good weekend, and see some of you next Tuesday (the 17th of Feb).
> 
> Matthew Tippett
> 
> El' Presidente by default 
>       (due to no official office bearers).
