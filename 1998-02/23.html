RE: [Linux-SA] file permisions

Hi Trevor,

The 'chmod' magic numbers actually reflect the permissions you get when
you do a ls -l (long).  So if you have say

-rw-rw-r--   1 mmecjt   mmecjt          6 Feb 12 11:05 foo.bar

this would be equal to 0664

That is, the first dash would = nothing (0)
then three octal numbers (in binary) follow, so rw- = 110 = 6

Thus, 0664.  

As for what they do..

Well the first '-' in this case is for special filenames, ones that
signify that they are links or directories, or other special attributes.
Then you have three sets of permissions each having a possible 'rwx'
value (= 7).  These control the read, write and execute permissions for
the file.  The three sets are for system, group members and world
(everyone in the world :).  Thus, for instance, you can restrict your
file to 'all permissions for the system and members of your group, but
nothing for anyone else'.  That would be -rwxrwx---, or 0770.

I hope this helps,

Chris Trowbridge
