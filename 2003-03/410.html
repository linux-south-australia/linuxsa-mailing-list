> The pic of Tavee Pularmjit pedalling such a generator seems to show

Hard work?? Only from the western world point or view, farming rice
by hand in inches of mud and hot, humid conditions is what I'd call
hard work but are part of the Lao Calendar.

Laos is a very isolated country and mountainous country and a lot of
villages do not have power from the national grid (generators only,
possibly only a few hours a day in the evening) or schools. The other
problem is lack of roads, in the more remote provinces (states).

The article states that a customised laptop is being used. Pedalling for
40 minutes for an hour of internet use isn't bad for those with very few
modern amenities. 

The whole system must be simple enough to be maintained by the village
and portable enough to be carried on foot. Replacing a bearing can be
trouble.
