Benjamin,

>  From my understanding, wep is still a good choice. Tools 
> like the BSD 
> Airtools WepCrack and air snort, still take a large amount of data to 
> crack keys without 21bit weak iv's. I just think all the hype 
> about how 
> insecure wep is getting is out of hand. Rolling wep keys do a 
> great job 
> of solving the problems. Sure there is still problems but lets not 
> instantly discount a security measure. If you want security 
> use IPSec, 
> but don't discount a first line of defence.

I guess you read my last post. I would suggest the same that WEP is a feature and it has a use.  I would think that anyone who has the responsibility for assuring the security of a network would take steps to build on WEP (perhaps VPN, etc) and not assume its going to prevent someone from seeing your network traffic.

In many ways, its the way it's implemented in an environment more so then the technology its self. For example, changing the key by some means on a regular basis (there is no standard way to do this as far as i know) is a good start.
