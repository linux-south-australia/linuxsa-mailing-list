On Mon, Mar 03, 2003 at 10:16:45AM +1030, Richard Russell said:
> 
> /var is commonly separate from /. This is because you do NOT, under any
> circumstances want to fill / up. That would be bad. Filling /var up is
> also bad, but won't kill your server entirely, just most of the daemons.
> /var does need to be mounted as daemons come up, but usually it would
> come up at a similar time to /usr, and most daemons would be run from
> /usr... so it should be fine...

Also from a security point of view, and from personal experience, I once
had a /var directory that was a couple of gig in size.  I did this
specifically because I had plenty of space to use and for me it is normal
practice to create seperate partitions for /var and /tmp and whatever else
I may need.

On this day, however, I'd opened up port 21 to the world and that very
night some sneaky french people logged in anonymously and uploaded 1.5Gb of
Div/X movies.  Had the partition been any larger, and I may have been up
for an even larger data transfer cost.

Food for thought :-)
