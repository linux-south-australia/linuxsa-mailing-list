Re: Innovation

On Friday, March 14, 2003, at 09:41 AM, Matthew Geddes wrote:
> This isn't designed to be flamebait, but we've all seen in the media
> claims that Open Source rarely innovates, but typically just copies
> innovation by others.

I think this is true, and the specific reason is because of a lack of 
documentation. It's hard to co-ordinate a world-wide team of developers 
if none of them have the same ideas in mind.

It's easier to point to an existing product and say "the same as that", 
than it is to write the 500 pages of detailed specs normally required.

On the other hand, OS offers the ability to incrementally improve, 
which is where I think we see the free software eventually surpassing 
commercial software.

So OS embraces then extends, ironically.
