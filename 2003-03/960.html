> Richard Russell <richard@yellowgoanna.com> wrote:

>> OK, but why "run off"?
>
> I don't know.  I assume it was for the same reason that people say,
> "run off a copy on the printer"

I knew the term at the time that runoff (the predecessor of roff,
which ran un Multics) was written.  See
http://athena.fit.qut.edu.au/cgi-bin/man/man2html?7+roff for more
details.  I particularly like this part, misguided as it turned out to
be:

  BUGS

  UNIX[rg] is a registered trademark of the Open Group. But things
  have improved considerably after Caldera had bought SCO UNIX in
  2001.

I was running a small Gestetner offset lithography printer for my
mother, and we regularly referred to printing as "running off" a
number of sheets.

>> OK, ye people who are familiar with both *roff and [La]TeX, why did
>> Knuth write TeX if troff existed?
>
> Knuth was writing the definitive text to computing.  He wrote
> volumes 1 and 2; and despairing of the poor quality of document
> setting applications at the time, took a decade off to write TeX,
> before writing volumes 3, 4 and 5.  I don't know why he didn't use
> troff, but recalling the previous mini-discussion about TeX's
> operating system of origin, particularly that it wasn't unix,
> perhaps he didn't know of it or felt it was not conveniently
> available to him.

I'd guess it wasn't available, or not convenient.  Besides, Knuth
wanted to do his own thing.  Sound like Linux? 

> Seems a bit of a stretch, I'll grant you.  Maybe he just didn't like
> troff's cryptic and arcane language and decided to write his own
> cryptic and arcane typesetting language.  :-)

Maybe troff wasn't cryptic and arcane enough.

>> Are they pretty much equivalent?
>
> Yes, in the same sense that an Alfa and a VW are pretty much
> equivalent.  Actually, the two motor cars are probably more
> equivalent than the two typesetting systems.

Well, they both get the job done.
