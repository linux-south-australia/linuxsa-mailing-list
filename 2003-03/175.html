Ian,

Ian Loxton wrote:
> 
> Duh, gee, and you didn't spot that a full stop at the end of a URL is 
> not a normal thing to find there?

It might not be normal but it is perfectly valid for a URL.

[brings out the big guns]

This is why RFC 2396; "Uniform Resource Identifiers (URI): Generic 
Syntax" <http://www.ietf.org/rfc/rfc2396.txt>; in Appendix E suggests 
several means of escaping URIs in situations like this. To quote:

    In practice, URI are delimited in a variety of ways, but usually
    within double-quotes "http://test.com/", angle brackets
    <http://test.com/>;, or just using whitespace

                              http://test.com/

    These wrappers do not form part of the URI.

I personally prefer angle brackets. If we all employed the above 
methods, the world would be a much easier to understand place. ;)
