Re: RTL8139 cards.

Quoting Ryan Verner <xfesty@computeraddictions.com.au>:

> What do people recommend for cheapish (i.e. < $50ea), reliable cards 
> that are suitable for use in a Linux server that won't have loads of 
> traffic going through it?  I try to use Intel cards where possible, but 
> they're not exactly cheap, especially when I'll need three NIC's.  
> Recommendations on where I could pick one up cheaply/quickly would be 
> great too, as I'll need to grab the cards first thing Monday morning.

You used to be able to get cards with a DEC Tulip chipset on them for around 
$45. By some reports these cards have a better throughput than the Intel. For 
some reason they don't seem to be very popular, I guess the market wants a 
cheap (ie: Realtek) or a 'good' (ie: Intel, 3com) card and there isn't much 
call for something in between.

I think the Alloy brand might still make such cards.
