Re: Innovation

Quoting Matthew Geddes <mgeddes@tellurian.com.au>:
> Hey all,
> 
> This isn't designed to be flamebait, but we've all seen in the media
> claims that Open Source rarely innovates, but typically just copies
> innovation by others. Whether that's true or not is really irrelevant,
> but I was hoping that people could pass on some of the areas where they
> think Open Source software is and has been innovative (preferably stuff
> that's happened over the last couple of years).

I believe your colleague at Tellurian, Dan Shearer, has prepared and delivered 
a presentation on such a topic.  You may like to check with him.

As for innovation, I think the following are good examples:
o Beowulf (cheap supercomputing power)
o i18n and accessibility through atk/pango on GNOME
o kernel performance (Linux and BSD)
o Emulation technologies (Wine, VMWare, CrossOver etc)
o Network Storage Devices (commodity hardware)
o Jabber (service delivery, not just IM)

...there's a lot really.  Just hard to think on the spot :)
