If you saw my previous post about office wireless and what is a good AP
etc etc etc. you would have to know this post was comming :-)
 
Now I have a test AP is the office/school and I can be seen running
around the campus with my laptop testing the signal etc.
 
Also I've been researching the whole wireless 802.11x thing in regards
to security.
 
This had lead me of course to warchalking, for those who don't know
warchalking is wondering around with a wireless PDA or laptop finding
AP's. Then placing a chalk mark on the sidewalk or wall of the building
with the AP, the mark shows how secure the network is. Wardriving same
concept but in a car. Can be done in a light aircraft too they say.
 
This is a funky computer nerd thing to do.
 
Anyways I know Airnet ISP did have a few open hotspots in Adelaide
(Rundle Street East Coffee shop, West Tce. Servo) but I think these are
dead now.
 
Anyone know any current hotspots in Adelaide or some weblinks that
might list this stuff.
 
News on the wirlpool site say Maccas and perhaps some franchise coffee
shops are looking to offer wireless (of course through a particular
ISP).
 
Life on the semi-bleading edge, nerdy but fun.
 
P.S. So I guess the best way to secure wireless network is via specific
MAC address lists in the AP's ???
