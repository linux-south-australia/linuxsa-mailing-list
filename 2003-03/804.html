Re: Innovation

> I've found KDE singularly deficient in such mouse/keyboard
> combinations.  Many functions require finding buttons on the title bar
> and clicking on them.  I'm writing this on a 2048x1536 display, and
> it's really difficult to position exactly on the buttons.  fwwm2
> doesn't require this.  How do I do the following in KDE without first
> positioning on a button?
>
> - iconify a window?
> - lower a window?
> - raise a window?
> - maximize a window?
>

Click the KDE Control Centre Icon, navigate to Look'n'feel->Shortcuts, there 
you can bind any key combination you like to just about anything, including 
all of the above. By default you could press alt-f3 and use the cursors to 
navigate down to minimize (for example) and press enter. You could also right 
click on the taskbar icon of the app and click minimize, etc.. if you want to 
lower a window you can middle click on its title bar.

I agree that the window widgets should be able to be scaled to an appropriate 
size just incase you are using an insane resolution, or you have poor 
eyesight. AFAIK GNOME will support SVG for its widgets soon, which will allow 
this. I hope that KDE goes the same way as it will allow the system to be 
made much more accessible to everybody.

IMHO KDE is the best looking, most functional desktop environment on the 
planet for Unix and Unix-like systems (and better than windows as well!).
