On Mon, Mar 10, 2003 at 11:10:32AM +1030, Brian Astill wrote:
> On Mon, 10 Mar 2003 04:19 am, Pigeon wrote:
> 
> > Would it not be much better to unhook the chain from the rear
> > sprocket and hook it over a sprocket attached directly to the
> > generator?
> 
> Of course it would.  Important not to destroy the use of the bicycle as 
> a bicycle, though.  IE the switch from normal bicycle-to generator-to 
> bicycle would have to be simple.

Well, the picture in the article shows a super-duper mountain bike. If
this is typical, the derailleur gear system should allow plenty of
slack to simply unhook the chain from the rear wheel sprocket and hook
it over a sprocket on a shaft which sticks out of the generator in the
right place.

Alternatively, such bikes are usually designed to make removing the
wheel entirely a ten-second job; attaching it to a generator designed
around this feature would be an operation of comparable complexity to
parking such a bike in a Western city centre and taking the wheels off
to stop it getting nicked (as people do).
