Re: linux on 16mb of ram

A couple of years ago I installed Redhat 5.? or SuSE ?.? on the oldest,
most obscure crap I could find in the junk box, ie, 386sx25, 8 or 16mb
ram 200-500mb hdd, cdrom via panasonic or other early proprietry sound
card based cd interface. It was just an exercise in time wasting as it
took ~7 hours but I just wanted to see if it would work.
It installed ok and picked everything up. I had to use the text install
as there was insufficient ram for the x-install.
Starting x took ~3-5 minutes or something silly but it ran a lot quicker
on Icewm than Kde.
I posted my finding to the list at the time.
The bottom line is that yes it will all work but most/all the current
distros will want more ram.
Even some of the text installers are getting gready these days.

Hope it helps.
