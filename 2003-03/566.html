Yep, sounds like your domain doesnt resolve. I assume this is mail from
your server to elsewhere, and the remote site says this?

> BUT I CAN SEND TO YAHOO OR HOTMAIL BOX BY MY OOTLOOK
> ADRESS TO SERVER.

So you can send outgoing mail to other sites, that arnt picky about it?
 
> TEST WITH SERVER :
> WHEN USED 
> #NSLOOKUP X.X.X.X

So, you are looking up the IP, but have you tryed to look up the
hostname?
 
> Nom :    ns1.ISP-NAME.com
> Address:  X.X.X.X

You need to either a) get your domain registered either properly and
hosted by yourself, or with some dyndns type service or b) cheat, and
change your hostname to localhost which should resolve on most peoples
servers already (what I did on my home box for testing). 

To accept unresolvable domains for incoming mail look in the file
/etc/mail/sendmail.cw find the line that contains:

FEATURE(`accept_unresolvable_domains')dnl

And if it has a dnl at the _start_ of the line remove it. Then Follow
the instructions at the top of the file for regenerating the sendmail.cf
file and restart sendmail. (m4 /etc/mail/sendmail.mc >
/etc/mail/sendmail.cf) That should solve this error for incoming mail
from domains that dont resolve. 

Theres also MX (Mail eXchange) dns records that you should understand if
your playing with this, but I dont think thats your immediate problem.
