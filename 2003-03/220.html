Re: sec:unclassified NTP Question

Wilkinson,Alex wrote:

> On a device that has two Ethernet interfaces, is it possible to bind one interface
> to act as an NTP Client and to bind the other interface to be an NTP stratum 1 server ?

No.  The machine's system clock is always updated
by the best available time source (in your case
the stratum 1).

Why do you want to do this?

If it is for some sort of fallback purpose, then
you might want to consider the difference between
NTP's "peer" and "server" commands.  With those
you can use a public clock to police and act as
fallback to a local higher-stratum clock, without
updating the public clock.

If you are puting some NTP infrastructure into
place behind a firewall, you might choose to
run four machines -- the stratum 1, and three
stratum 2s clocked from the stratum 1.  The
stratum 2s will police the stratum 1 -- clients
will use the average time of the three stratum 2s
should the stratum 1 go weird.
