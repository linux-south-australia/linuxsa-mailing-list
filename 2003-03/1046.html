I've been asked to setup a MySQL based application on a fancy new 
dual-xeon platform with hyperthreading etc. While FreeBSD is usually my 
first choice for a production server, various articles on the Internet 
suggest that the threads in FreeBSD are far from optimal and that 
options like the linuxthreads port or FreeBSD 5.0 aren't suitable for 
production systems.

Redhat looks like the next best choice (For ease of support and 
management), and there's some suggestion that Redhat 8.0 is better 
equipped for this kind of hardware, although I've been burned by point-O 
releases before.

Does anyone have any anecdotes they would care to share on running this 
sort of system? Is it worth optimising the system so that the database 
can get the most out of SMP when there will be other applications 
(httpd, multiple forking perl processes) running on the same box?
