Re: Innovation

Steve Pryde wrote:

> So you think that:
...
> are not innovation?   They certainly weren't copied from windows!!

Of course they're not innovation.

Someone that could drive a 1973 Xerox PARC Alto could
drive a 2003 KDE desktop.  That's evolution.

Someone that could drive a IBM 3270 or DEC VT100 terminal
would see the Alto as something quite different, requiring
a deep change in the way they think about computing.  That's
innovation.

There are recent innovations in computing -- MP3 music,
all-digital cameras and the like.

I don't view Linux as innovative, but as making the past
30 years of computing innovation available to everyone.

eg: clustering doesn't just happen on DEC machines anymore,
RAID can be done on a home PC, hypertext just isn't for Macs,
Internet isn't just on BSD.
