I'm sorry you've had some bad experiences Alex.

The age old question rises again, how can a person express humour in a
print medium where people can't see facial expressions etc?

Someone and I had a run in a few years ago,someone sent me a private
message partly in answer to a question I'd posted that I thought was
rude. I posted the message and my answer to LinuxSA asking who this
person was. Richard Sharpe answered me saying he thought he knew who it
was and he'd handle it. So David was it you? Do you remember? I know
David better now and I'm not so touchy, not that I'd let anyone get away
with bad behaviour.

If you don't know a person well their writing style etc can be off
putting. Not that that excuses rudeness etc.

David your bluntness and sticking literally to what you meant by what
you typed can be taken as insulting, rude and a put down. Words in
sentences and sentences in paragraphs, there can often be a second or
third meaning assumed by different readers. Accept this David and look
at what you're writing from more than 1 point of view (put yourself in
someone elses shoes) and think "Can my meaning/intention here be
misconstrued?" You might think why should I bother with what they think
about what I wrote. 

We are in a service industry, others are looking to us at work, at home
and on this list for help with their Linux. Cop it sweet and behave
yourselves. If we want some humour and it's good to have (yeah I made a
doosie of a blunder yesterday see messages re Sun) we need first to be
able to laugh at ourselves. As Aussies we're the best in the world at
that and if we can laugh at ourselves we're less likely to take others
poking at us as seriously.

Alex, more than a couple of years ago someone was kicked off this list
for being rude, obstrepolous and nasty, he hasn't come back. There are
those of us who tell David to pull his head in occasionally.

There is more than 1 side to all disagreements. Look for it!! It's
called Interpersonal Skills.
