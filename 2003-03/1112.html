Re: Fibre Channel

stephen white wrote:
> Has anyone had experience with Fibre Channel cards? I'm currently 
> pondering a project where I grab some cheap PCs, load then up with 4 
> hard drives each, then export them as devices via FC.

Linux will act as a fiber channel initiator (client).

I haven't heard of any work making Linux a fiber
channel target (disk).

You might want to look at iSCSI, especially if you're
trying to keep prices down.

 > Then the NFS server can mount these devices and RAID
 > across them, serving them out via NFS over FC and Ethernet.

IP over FC sucks, don't go there.  Just use FC as a link
for the backend SCSI I/O.

It's not clear why you'd want to do what you suggest,
unless it's to overcome "number of disk in chassis"
limits.
