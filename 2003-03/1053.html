Re: Best OS for Dual Xeon

> Does anyone have any anecdotes they would care to share on running this 
> sort of system? Is it worth optimising the system so that the database 
> can get the most out of SMP when there will be other applications 
> (httpd, multiple forking perl processes) running on the same box?

This is an interested read to get your head around the issues.
http://kt.zork.net/kernel-traffic/kt20020902_182.html#21

Linux based but is a good read on symmetric multi-threading (SMT) [1]
issues anyway.  Explains why 2 HT processors don't look exactly like 4
processors.

2.5 kernels supports good HT scheduling.  2.4 kernels will think they are 4
identical processors and will therefore do dumb (ie. slower) things.  In
fact with a non HT aware scheduler it can sometimes be better to turn HT
off (which IIRC is an option in the BIOS).

[1] Hyper-Threading (HT) is Intel's implementation of SMT.
