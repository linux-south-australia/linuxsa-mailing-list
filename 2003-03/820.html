KDE window manager functions (was: Innovation)

On Friday, 14 March 2003 at 23:33:17 +1030, Damien Uern wrote:
>> I've found KDE singularly deficient in such mouse/keyboard
>> combinations.  Many functions require finding buttons on the title bar
>> and clicking on them.  I'm writing this on a 2048x1536 display, and
>> it's really difficult to position exactly on the buttons.  fwwm2
>> doesn't require this.  How do I do the following in KDE without first
>> positioning on a button?
>>
>> - iconify a window?
>> - lower a window?
>> - raise a window?
>> - maximize a window?
>>
>
> Click the KDE Control Centre Icon, navigate to Look'n'feel->Shortcuts, there
> you can bind any key combination you like to just about anything, including
> all of the above. By default you could press alt-f3 and use the cursors to
> navigate down to minimize (for example) and press enter. You could also right
> click on the taskbar icon of the app and click minimize, etc.. if you want to
> lower a window you can middle click on its title bar.

Thanks for the hint.  I've tried this, and after about 5 minutes
managed to understand what it wanted (the help button doesn't work).
As far as I can tell, I can only use a key combination: it doesn't
understand mouse buttons in combination.  This makes it useless, since
Emacs already uses all key combinations.

> I agree that the window widgets should be able to be scaled to an
> appropriate size just incase you are using an insane resolution, or
> you have poor eyesight.

No, that's not agreeing with me :-)  I think that window widgets are a
waste of space.  I don't want to have to position on them.  Sure,
making them bigger would make it easier to position on them, but
that's not the issue.
