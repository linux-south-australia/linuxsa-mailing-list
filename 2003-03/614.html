> Forums (such as IRC) where the censorship of humour happens every so

That depends entirely on which irc channels you hang out in. On EFNET
(one of the busiest groups of irc servers) there are many linux help
channels which have large numbers of people coming or going or simply
lurking, and humour plays an important role in the health of the channel
conversation. Humour to the detriment of channel participants however,
is strongly discouraged. As is racist, sexist, and other socially
inappropriate comments that are just as unacceptable in real life as
they are on irc. See www.irchelp.org for some docs on irc ettiquette.

The #linuxsa channel where some find it amusing to denigrate others by
abusing a priviledge is an exception. In the 7 years I've been on EFNET
such behaviour would automatically result in a kickban from the channel.
Repeat offenders would recieve a permban. In #linuxsa however no such
sanctions are ever enforced, and so the behaviour could be construed as
having tacit approval.

Similarly flame wars on this mailing list are not moderated at all, but
if they continue for too long, over too trivial a matter, they
eventually get complaints from other list members.
