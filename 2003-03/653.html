Sorry, forgot this one. I'll leave you all alone soon, I promise.

I don't know that Active Directory can be classed as a standard. I guess
it depends on your definition. Active Directory uses other (real)
standards, like DNS and LDAP to get it's job done.

I think that the following things I've mentioned are standards or have
standards describing them:

	* PAM
	* LDAP
	* SSL
	* RADIUS
	* NIS+ (perhaps?)
	* NT GINA

I don't think that SMB/CIFS/RPC (the NT domain stuff) is a documented
standard either.

It's always handy to point out to management types that the stuff you're
using is based heavily on standards that even The Other Stuff(tm)
borrows heavily from. You can also use big long buzzwords, such as
interoperability and heterogeneous. :-)
