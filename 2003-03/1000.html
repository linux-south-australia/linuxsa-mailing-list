Re: PC hardware interface for push buttons

On Thu, Mar 20, 2003 at 11:15:48PM +1030, Florin Dumitru wrote:

 > > I'm faced with the situation that I need a doorbell, but why just buy 
 > > one when I can do something cool, like rig up a SMS notification as 
 > > well as getting a boring old chime? :)
 > 
 > Try a PCI card (one of the lowest available has 24 static I/O) from NI, 
 > or similar..

Too expensive.  A DB9 connector, a length of wire, a resistor and a
pushbutton switch will suffice.

Wire the switch between +12V and DCD on the RS232 connector.  Wire the
resistor between DCD and GND.

RS232 data pins are active-low, signal pins are active high.  Presenting
+12V on DCD will assert carrier on the serial port.

UNIX serial ports block on open until DCD is presented.

The code to monitor the doorbell will look something like this:

    forever {
	open /dev/ttyS0	  # this will block until the button is pressed
	close /dev/ttyS0  # don't need it anymore :-)
	make doorbell noise
	send SMS message
    }

Simple - A few lines of perl, and less than $10 expenditure.
