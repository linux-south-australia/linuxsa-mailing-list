Re: DNS delay or mail server

> A friend sent me this and said that the TTL for the mail records was 24 
> hours. Does this mean that I can't expect mail to be received for 24 hours?

Depends on what is was in your old dns records (I obviously can't see
them!).  If someone's never emailed you before then they'll do a lookup and
get the new details.  If someone's recently emailed you then they'll have
the old details in their dns cache which will be valid for whatever the TTL 
was for your domain.

Normally when moving DNS records around it's a good idea to drop the TTL for 
your domain down to something quite low for a few days to ensure that any 
change will propogate quickly.

You may just have to deal with email taking a bit to come in.
