Re: sec:unclassified NTP Question

Do you have an atomic clock to be a real stratum 1 server? If not then
just set up ntp on that box, configure it to broadcast to you lan if you
want (you can allow your clients to poll, or both), and use the access
restrictions in the conf file to stop evil people updating it remotely,
and allow connects from your lan. If you connect to a stratum 2 server
on the net, you'll be stratum 3, which is fine. Its a pretty accurate
protocol, an you'll probably be within 100 milliseconds or closer to the
correct time (thats a total guess, but ntp tests and takes lag in to
account. How close you get would depend on how bursty any lag is on your
connection - but probably be insignificant.).

Ant
