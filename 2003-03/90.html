CPP is the C PreProcessor as you might have guessed. Its job is to
expand macros and other special things in C files before the C compiler
itself sees the file. Unfortunately they are not standard over various
Unixes. The configure script is trying to work out how standard or what
type of C Preprocessor you have.

It has, however, failed.

It looks like you have one so my guess is that you may have downloaded a
package which doesn't exactly meet your operating system. The name of
the package (a URL to its README) and what OS version(s) you are trying
to compile it on would help us diagnose this problem.
