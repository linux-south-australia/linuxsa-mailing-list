I'd suggest that there are three broad categories kinds of blogs:

  - Aggregators. This could be for news (/.), opinions (kuro5hin), links 
or whatever else. Good reads if you're into whatever they aggregate.

- Personal journals. Online diaries. Usually as boring as hell unless 
you know the person and/or they don't just use it as an outlet for 
bitching about life.

- Public journals. These tend to be related to a specific person, 
project, or some other subject. For example, Dave Hyatt (of Mozilla and 
Safari fame) has a blog which is an interesting read if you're into 
those projects; he posts updates and asksk questions which directly 
relate to the projects themselves. The difference between this and a 
personal journal is that posts to it usually contain content that is of 
interest to a larger audience than just, say, yourself.
