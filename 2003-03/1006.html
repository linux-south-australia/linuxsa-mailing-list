RE: rsync and symlinks

> I need to split an rsync over two disks and I can't work out 
> how...my problem is that:
> 
>  1) I don't have access to LVM or "vinum"
>  2) The directory seems to be about 40Gb in size
> 
> ..but I have a total of some 100Gb but none of them in 40Gb chunks...
> 
> Any suggestions?

Huh?

hmmm... after thinking about this for Quite Some Time, I think I know
what you are trying to say here...

you have 40Gb of data in one directory, and have 100Gb of empty disk
space in small sized chunks. You want to make a copy of said directory
somewhere. Not sure if it's just me, but I found your explaination
remarkably confusing.

Anyway...

try rsyncing the subdirectories to appropriate empty space, and create a
single directory full of the relevant symlinks... You could re-rsync at
any time without losing your symlink structure.

eg:

/directory/subdir1 --> /disk1/subdir1
/directory/subdir2 --> /disk1/subdir2
/directory/subdir3 --> /disk2/subdir3
/directory/subdir4 --> /disk2/subdir4
/directory/subdir5 --> /disk3/subdir5

Or...

Shift stuff on your existing disks to make 40Gb of space on one
filesystem. Use symlinks to hide this...

eg:

/var --> /disk1/var
/usr --> /disk1/usr

Or...

buy a new disk (100Gb, for a wholesaler, would surely be cheap...) ...
if that $200 outlay saves you 10 (or whatever) hours, it's probably
worth it...

Or...

rsync to /dev/null -- it's really fast, and has unlimited size!
