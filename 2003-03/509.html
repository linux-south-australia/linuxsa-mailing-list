Re: flash --> taboo?

In the immortal words of Justin Hawkins <justin@internode.com.au>...
> Worse, if you are using flash for a menu, and don't provide an
> alternative for flash-less people to navigate with, then you are
> limiting your audience. And you are alienating those of your existing
> audience who don't like flash.

I am rather partial to this line of thinking myself.  I personally
dislike flash but that's my personal opinion, it also means that if a
site "requires" flash I don't "require" that site.  I'll go somewhere
else, and I know that quite a few others on this list think along the
same lines.

At the very least a flash enabled site should have a plain HTML
alternative, if only for navigation.

Admittedly it's (sometimes) smaller and faster to download flash content
than Java content, however I still have trouble justifying either.

Again, that's just my not so humble opinion and I'm sticking to it.
