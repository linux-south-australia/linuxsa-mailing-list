OK, I may have missed something here (it's a while since I set up my
DHCP stuff), but why are you leasing a static address? If I want an
address range to be DHCP-managed, but I want certain machines to have
static addresses, I usually make the DHCP server statically assign an
address to a MAC.

Why put eth1 under DHCP at all? Choose to make it static, in which case,
