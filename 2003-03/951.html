RE: Kernel upgrades

>Apart from the 
>installation process of
>the source itself (rpm vs. tarball I guess, which is fine) is 
>there much of
>a difference between redhat kernel source and vanilla source, in  the
>configuration and compiling stages?

There is very little difference apart from the extra config options
sometimes found in distribution kernels as opposed to vanilla kernels.

You'll probably find it easier to work with the vanilla kernels as most
of the documentation out there refers to vanilla kernels.  Also most 
patches you find out there aren't made for customised distribution kernels,
so you'll have all sorts of trouble compiling anything other than the 
defaults supplied with the distro kernel.
