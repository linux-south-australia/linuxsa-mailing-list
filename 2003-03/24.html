Use 6to4 tunnelling (unless your ISP supports IPv6 - mine doesn't).

The absolutely easiest way to setup a tunnel is to go to
www.freenet6.net and follow the instructions.
>

> This seems to suggest that to get IPv6 going, there are many tasks

> 1	Get IPv6 to go over my Internet connection.
> 2	Get IPv6 going in my DNS.
> 3	Get an IPv6 gateway to connect to with IPv6-inIPv4 encapsulation.
>
> I guess 1 and 3 are alternatives.
> But I can't even get IPv6 going from one end of my home office to the
> other.

You may also want to look at radvd (the Route ADVertisement Daemon)
which once configured advertises routes and prefixes to other machines
on the wire, who, due to the coolness of IPv6, automatically assign
themselves EUI addresses from that prefix.
