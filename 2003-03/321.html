Off site backup (was: Re: FW: Re: I can't do it)

Matthew Western wrote:

>may i ask how ppl back up a TB of data?  sure you can build a box that holds
>a TB of data, but back it up every night like i would have thought is
>expected??  that's the problem we have, not about disc space, but the cost
>of buying new tape drives all the time.
>
>yeah i've heard all the wind about mirroring it to another machine etc etc,
>but a fire doesn't care about a mirrored server.  :)  just a thought.
>  
>
Have a look at rsync to mirror to a second box. If you're just comparing 
regular files and documents etc, not that much is going to change in a 
day, and you can probably back up that much data over whatever links you 
have [1] available to a remote location if you do the initial 
synchronisation over ethernet [2] first.

John Edwards

[1] If you have enough staff to create 1TB worth of regular files, 
presumably you have plenty of bandwidth somewhere for them to do other 
stuff during the day.
[2] At 100Mbits/s, 1TB will take approximately 1 day to copy, you might 
want to consider that in your disaster recovery plans!
