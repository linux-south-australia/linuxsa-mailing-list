Ryan,

> Is there going to be a GPL copy of your distrib (I'm assuming you guys
> have finished it now?)... would be interesting to look at.

I'm having discussions about that particular aspect now. Parts of the
distribution are under a non-free licence, however I believe the value
of what we're offering is:

 1) the hardware is inexpensive
 2) the distribution is rock solid and stable
 3) it works out of the box
 4) end users don't have to choose which of the 11000 packages in the
    Debian packages list to use

That said it may not be the most appropriate distribution for a power
user who wants the very latest, bleeding edge software; it's more
appropriate for an end user who wants to use their computer for office
type work, maybe play a few games [tux racer does work], do some work
with the Gimp and so on.

If that user chooses to look under the hood they'll find a real Linux
that's capable of running Squid, Apache and whatever else they choose to
"apt-get" onto it.
