Re: PC hardware interface for push buttons

On Fri, Mar 21, 2003 at 05:00:46PM +1030, James Day wrote:

 > Mark> Too expensive.  A DB9 connector, a length of wire, a resistor and a
 > Mark> pushbutton switch will suffice.
 > 
 > This approach seems good for a single input, but for a similar project
 > I require 8-12 hardware inputs. Does anyone have any ideas for
 > multiple inputs ?
 
8 - 12 serial ports? <grin>

 > How many inputs can serial and parallel ports take without the need of
 > some kind of multiplexer ?

Probably two inputs and two outputs.  

DCD and CTS can be counted as inputs.  DTR and RTS can be used as outputs.

You can get analogue inputs by putting a resistor across the joystick 
port - four inputs per port (X1, X2, Y1, Y2).  You also get four
digital inputs for the buttons.  Use pull-down resistors between buttons
and GND to stop them from floating when they're open.

(I've used the Joystick port as a temperature sensor before:  A $3 
thermistor (temperature-sensitive resistor) across the appropriate
pins;  the joystick device driver in the kernel gives you a number 
between 0 and 255 for the "position" of the axis you're reading, 
depending on its resistivity.  Pick resistors which will give you
a 0 - 100 kohm range for best results.

The pinout of the DB-15 connector is as follows:

       1  XY1 (+5v)
       2  Switch 1
       3  X1 (potentiometer #1)
       4  Switch 1 (GND)
       5  Switch 2 (GND)
       6  Y1 (potentiometer #2)
       7  Switch 2
       8  N.C.
       9  XY2 (+5v)
      10  Switch 4
      11  X2 (potentiometer #3)
      12  Switch 3&4 (GND)
      13  Y2 (potentiometer #4)
      14  Switch 3
      15  N.C.

The average PC with USB, a joystick port and two serial ports gives you
four analogue inputs, eight digital inputs and four digital outputs and
a 100 mA 5V power supply (from the USB port) without any necessity to
buy additional IO cards.  Pretty good if you're on a budget, handy
with a soldering iron, and not too concerned about blowing things up :-)

The parallel port is handy too - modern parallel ports have 16 digital
inputs and outputs (selectable on a per-pin basis).
