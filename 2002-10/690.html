Re: Bash Colours

Hi Wayne ,
I'm on Debian but RH should behave itself :
Do this:

[wayne@waynes] env
all those things may be set from your bashrc file. (If you don't have a
local one it falls back to the stuff in /etc)

now this 
[wayne@waynes] alias

now this
[wayne@waynes] echo $LS_OPTIONS

edit your .bashrc file  (or the RH equivalent) to export the settings
you like.

/etc/X11/rgb.txt and or /usr/X11R6/lib/X11/rgb.txt
have a list of human friendly standard colours.
(I keep a copy in my home directory for reference) or better (just tried
this)[wayne@waynes] dircolors --print-database |less
gives some usefull info.



[wayne@wayne] /usr/bin/dircolors 
may be usefull as well. You can set your own (I think) using the
LS_COLORS env variable. man dircolors

finally the bash howtos are worth reading (about a thousand times) :)

Pete.
