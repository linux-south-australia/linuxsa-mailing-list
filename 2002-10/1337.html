RE: Portable server-side Java rare-to-impossible [LONG]

On Wed, 23 Oct 2002, Michael Rubin wrote:

> The JVM's are written in c++ and assembly.  In an ideal world they have to
> at the very least be compiled on every os/hardware.

That doesn't quite address what I meant to ask, which is whether the
JVM/Hotspot combo has to be _ported_ to different toolchain versions. Ie,
does it grub around in calling conventions or method table handling or
other areas that apps shouldn't ever touch? One motivation I can imagine
would be to enable other language bindings, even if it isn't a good
motivation.

This is one of the unfeatures of OpenOffice (for its own very specific
reasons) and it make porting very hard. From what Michael Gratton was
saying I wondered if that too was the case here. An i386 Linux application
that takes significant porting effort to make compile on i386 FreeBSD
sounds like it is doing something of this kind.
