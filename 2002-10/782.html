The Cyrix 6x86 got a bad reputation from gamers, many expected Pentium
performance for much less money but 3D games started to appear at that
time. In fact there are many people today who stick to Intel because of
their experience with the Cyrix 6x86 and it's PR rating. They speculate
that AMD's Athlon XP are not worth buying, when in fact today's Athlon
XP != Cyrix 6x86 in any analogous manner.

But... we're not talking about games here, for the application of a
network appliance I wonder how the VIA C3 stacks up to a modern Celeron
or Duron?
