Ronke,

> in response to your feeling that i'm running BSD, i
> wish to let you know that the error is actually from
> my REDHAT Linux 7.2 installation on a compaq proliant
> server.

Linux device names by themselves aren't always obvious, and it honestly
looked more like a FreeBSD device (which has things like ad0s1e and so
forth)...

> the error normally just shows and does not actually
> cause a system halt.

It's a soft error.
 
> do i have to change the scsi or what?,

No, but I'd investigate the device that is causing the errors. Check
/var/log/messages and dmesg. If you get really excited you can try to
find where the device appears in the /proc filesystem and you may find
an answer there.

Furthermore, if you get even MORE excited you could take a look at the
kernel module or source and set the debugging up a little higher.
