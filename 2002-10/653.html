Re: when do hard drives go to heaven?

Mcleod, Ian wrote:
> So to summarise - it really is quite subjective these days - although SCSI
> drives *may* be better mechanically speaking because it is convenient for
> the manufacturer to do so.

Other way around.  SCSI drives in the past have had better mechanicals
and magnetics.  The race for size on IDE has increasingly lead to
a single design aiming for SCSI drive relaibility and IDE drive
manufacturabily.

The manufacturer whacks on the appropiate electronics at the end of
the production line.


> Which brands are cheap and nasty?

None.  It's particular *models* that are problematic.  It's
not at all obvious to the consumer that the Big Grunt 40GB
drive might actually be two or three different models across
its manufacturing life, or that the Big Grunt 20, Big Grunt
40 and Big Grunt 80 might be variants of the same model.
