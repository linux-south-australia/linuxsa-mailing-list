RE: status of transmeta?

Think of it not so much as native as optimized for the translator .. some 
x86 instructions are ugly (complex, many clock cycles - intel recommend 
avoiding certain ones).  You can essentially "drive" an x86 using it's more 
simple instructions like a "kind of" RISC chip.  This gives better 
performance in today's deep pipe lines etc.

Again Ian, I'd focus on the VIA C3 chips - they're reasonable speed (they're 
starting to supply 1Ghz versions now), easily available, work in Socket370 
MB's (or at least a number of them).

Crusoe's are still a more embedded solution and aren't widely available - 
and might never be.

MMC
