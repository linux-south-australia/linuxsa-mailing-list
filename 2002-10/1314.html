> Sounds like you need a laptop :) (or a customised Knoppix CD)

Most people have laptops already and those that don't can grab one
from the pool, but really, increasing the number of desktops that need
management is not the way that I want to go.

Supporting desktops is hard enough without people galavanting across
the countryside with their own personal laptop, prone to their own
special problems.

Thin-client lets the desktop management sit in the one spot -- on the
server. That way, I can do all the application management and desktop
support on the one box, and only do things like updates once.

I also get other benefits, like being able to hijack a user's session
when they get stuck, rather than trundle off to their desk (which is
not always possible, particularly when there are people scattered on
either the other side of the country, or the other side of the
world).
