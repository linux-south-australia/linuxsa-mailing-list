vmware and redhat 8

If anyone gets the following kernel panic when installing Red Hat 8 (I think
this will occur on 7.x also) on vmware on a Duron or Athlon >= 1ghz, with
the host OS being windows:

CPU: AMD Athlon(tm) Processor stepping 02
Enabling fast FPU save and restore... done.
Enabling unmasked SIMD FPU exception support... general protection 
fault: 0000
CPU:    0
EIP:    0010:[<c02180b7>]    Not tainted
EFLAGS: 00000202
eax: 000006d0   ebx: 00005000   ecx: 00000001   edx: 00000001
esi: 0009fe00   edi: c0105000   ebp: 0008e000   esp: c0217fd0
ds: 0018   es: 0018   ss: 0018
Process swapper (pid: 0, stackpage=c0217000)
Stack: c021817c c0208980 c01d98d6 c0208980 c02187cc 00005000
        00005000 00005000 00005000 00000000 c024a060 c0100197
Call Trace:

Code: 0f 22 e0 68 ea 97 1d c0 e8 6c 9c ef ff 83 c4 04 db e3 dd 05
  <0>Kernel panic: Attempted to kill the idle task!
In idle task - not syncing


The solution is to upgrade the bios of your pc. The reason being is that the
linux guest has detected the SIMD capability (The new AMD chips support SSE)
but the bios has not turned it on and Windows doesn't do it either. The
linux guest OS tries to turn it on but can't, hence the general protection
fault. An upgraded bios should recognise the chip capabilities and enable
SSE.
