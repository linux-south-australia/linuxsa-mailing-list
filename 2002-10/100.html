[ANNOUNCEMENT]: AUUG-SA October Monthly Meeting

AUUG SA Chapter, October Meeting

The South Australian chapter of the Australian Unix User Group (AUUG)
invites you to attend their monthly meeting.

DETAILS
-------

DATE  : Wednesday 9th October 2002
TIME  : 7pm Onwards
VENUE : Internode

	Level 3/132 Grenfell St
	ADELAIDE

     aka 'the old AAMI building'

It is very close to Hindmarsh Square, which is your best bet for
parking. However the square is often very busy so plan to look around
for a park.

WHO CAN COME
------------

Anyone is welcome to attend the meeting.  We will pester you to
join up if you come too often :-)

Please feel free to invite anyone who is interested.


AGENDA
------

* Glen Turner of AARnet will be talking about the new
   protocol IPV6.

   Glen has had vast experiance setting up the
   infrustructure to support the new generation of internet
   protocol; IPV6. He'll talk about how the change will affect
   you, why it was needed and how finally there should never
   be a lack of non routable IP addresses again.

* At the conclusion of the meeting many members of the group
   ajourn for a drink and a meal at a restaurant or cafe nearby
   (Rundle St is just around the corner).


COMING UP
---------

* October:  Richard Sharpe from Panasas will be talking about
      "An Open Source Participant's Perspective on working
       for Capitalism" (Thursday 17th October 7pm)

* November: Allan Packer from Sun Microsystems talks about database
             performance on Unix platforms.

* December: AUUG-SA Looking back at 2002 Dinner


Look forward to seeing you there.
	
Cheers,
	Benjamin Close
         AUUG-SA Secretary
