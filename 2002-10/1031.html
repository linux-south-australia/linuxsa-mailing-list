RE: [OT] Watchdog eyes broadband limits

there is also the convenience of being able to select items to view rather
than just switching on the telly and hoping to catch that segment you like.

When I was in the USA - this convenience is what finally pushed me to decide
to get ADSL - it was just such a wonderful time saver.  Not to mention the
ABC broadband site.

Radio Nation also archive many of their shows online.  They always seem to
have great shows at really weird times like Sunday 9am (so people can listen
before going to Church??  Obviously the programmers are from another era).

I am going to work out how to set my Linux box to just go out and fetch ABC
and BBC items of interest and queue them locally on my server.  I won't have
to worry about missing a show or setting the video ever again.

Pay-TV will *never* have that convenience - unless it just becomes another
digital technology - but why pay per view?  I would rather pay $135 / month
and be done with it.

Digital streaming media is the way of the future (for news and information
junkies like me).

Looking at this it is not hard to see who even 8GB will need to be strictly
rationed - but it is certainly sufficient for my needs (for now).
