RE: when do hard drives go to heaven?

Mcleod, Ian said:
> Why are SCSI drives more reliable?  Are they better mechanically?
>

Hard drive manufacturers could build IDE and SCSI drives with the same
mechanical quality if they wanted to, but they tend not to. SCSI vs IDE is
a convenient way for them to partition (pun intended) the hard drive
market into two groups: SCSI hard drive products being better mechanical
quality and more expensive, with IDE being lower mechanical quality, less
expensive.

So generally speaking SCSI is better mechanically than IDE. Not because of
the technical characteristics of the SCSI vs IDE interface, but more so
because it is a convenient way for the hard drive manufacturers to market
low quality vs high quality hard drives.
