RE: Internode ADSL mirror - way to go!

1) Actually, Business Internode ADSL customers on Internet routable 
IP addresses that we allocated as IP's intrinsic to the service (1 or 
5 fixed IP's) do have un-metered access today.

So to clarify what Alex is referring to here - he is referring to the 
routing of *additional* IP ranges that the customer has requested on 
top of the 'built in' static IP's that we route with the service.

I wanted to point this out in case anyone thought that Alex was 
implying that un-metering didn't work on BADSL at all - when in fact 
it does, with this specific limitation (which is explained in the web 
pages that explain the un-metering facilities offered in Internode 
xDSL).

2) Yes, we'll be offering another access method for BADSL customers 
in the future which will avoid this 'edge condition' entirely, Alex.

Probably a few months away as yet, though, because we're completing 
some other necessary engineering work first, in order to make this 
possible.

Its taken until just a week ago for Telstra to finish offering us 
enough of their promised improvements in their xDSL network to let us 
move in this direction properly at last.

Its a long story. Suffice it so say that this wasn't an intended 
situation, but its currently unavoidable until we do re-engineer the 
BADSL access method, and meantime you can access those resources via 
the 'built in' IP's we routed to you underneath your tunnel if you 
want to - and I'm sure that Netcraft are more than technically 
capable of figuring out a way to do that, at the same time as using 
the additional ranges that you have requested that we route for you.

NB this is a separate, but related, project to the re-engineering I 
was referring to for direct ethernet-attached 'iPoP' customers in 
Agile-fibre-access buildings, in order to offer them the same 
outcomes in the future.
