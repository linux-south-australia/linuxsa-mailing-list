Luke,

> I also used it to connect, until recently it started playing up.
> Now, I'm just using wvdial at the command prompt, which is fine.

Unfortunately things in Linux generally don't just play up for no reason
(unlike other operating systems that I can think of). Can you think of
anything at all that might have changed? This could be:

* you changed a modem
* you switched a modem from one slot to another
* you changed phone lines/cords etc
* you did something in /etc/sysconfig/network-scripts
* you rebuilt your kernel
* you upgraded/downgraded your system

...or anything else no matter how obscure? Try running the network
monitor from the command line in an xterm. Often these types of programs
send debugging messages which can are easier to see running them this
way.
