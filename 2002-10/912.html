> If you drop the packets the IRC server will sit at the other end until
> it's TCP connection times out.  With TCP it expects a reply 
> if it can't
> connect to an IP/port.  if you reject with tcp-reset the 
> server gets its
> reply immediately and carries on.
> 
> As far as you're concerned DROP and REJECT are the same thing; packets
> never make it past (or even into) your firewall.  The 
> exception is that
> port 113 looks like it exists, rather than your machine just not
> replying (looking like it is not there).
> 
> One thing I was going to ask the list:  is it possible to set iptables

> machine to the machine the packet originates from?  Otherwise 
> I want to
> drop the packet.  It's a little more stateful and to random 
> scanners it
> looks like ident isn't there.  To things like IRC (and some 
> web) servers
> it just appears closed so that they don't take forever to respond.
