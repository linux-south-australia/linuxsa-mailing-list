> "Typically", spinning up and down the hard drive increases the failure rate.

I believe so.  I think temperature CHANGE is also a problem.  If a hard
drive is constantly getting hot, going cold, getting hot, etc. then it's
more likely to fail.  That said, even on my 24/7 machines, I mount my
hard drives onto the side of 5 1/4" bays, and stick an ex-power supply
fan in front of them.  Even 7200RPM drives stay just slightly warm to
the touch.

BoldWeb used to have a Seagate SCSI egg-cooker fail every year or so. 
Now that they're kept cool they never fail.
