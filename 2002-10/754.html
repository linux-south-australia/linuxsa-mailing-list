RE: diskless firewall?

Take a look a the VIA EPIA 5000 ITX form factor machines, all in one
motherboard with fanless 533MHz processor for around $200AUD.   Replace the
HDD with a Compact Flash card (32-128Mb), with suitable adaptor for the IDE
bus.   Load the machine with 512Mb of RAM and get Ramdisk support built
into the kernel.   Allocate a 256Mb ramdisk for squid proxy, 128Mb ramdisk
for logs and the remaining 128Mb for system.   Note the CF cards are only
good for 1000 writes, so anything that writes continously to the card eg
logging, opertunistic file locking etc will result in rapid death of the CF
card.   You wouldn't believe how fast a linux kernel loads when there is
0ms seektime.

The power requirements of such a machine are modest enough that a 300W PSU
*should* survive without its fan, you can only derive this empirically.
You will need to be *very* careful if you decide to remove the outer
housing to improve cooling, there are *very* nasty voltages (~400Vdc)
floating around in there waiting to bite !   You will need to load the 5V
supply with at least 10W otherwise the PSU will not operate correctly due
to the regulation technique.

I have been working on a diskless IPCop box on and off for a few months,
still having teething problems with my Sparklan 2.4GHz WAN card :(
