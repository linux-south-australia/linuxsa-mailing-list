RE: Text of virus infected email - Bugbear

The Bugbear worm is a pain even for Linux admins. It forges the senders
address by combining names and domain names from the infected persons PC.

For example an infected user has the name jblow@company.com and another
address of wally@microsoft.com

The infected email it send out might have the "senders address" of
jblow@microsoft.com
We have been quarantining infected emails the last 3 days in ever increasing
numbers. Every time we get one a message is trying to be sent to the sender
to inform them their PC has a virus/worm. These are ALL bouncing back as no
such user
( undeliverable ).
Whilst the worm doesnt directly cause any damage to Linux machines, it must
be causing some disruption to email servers.

The Subject and body of email is very random.

Attachment names are all double extensions such as these ( real examples )

error.log.scr
mpie4ful.exe.exe
My Money.mny.exe
TO.doc.exe
Biz1Logo.jsp.scr
RECORDS.XLS.scr
resume2000.doc.scr

Subject name is Random and Body of the infected email is also randomly
picked from the infected PC ( I saw one yesterday that would have been
confidential ).

Regards,

Michael
