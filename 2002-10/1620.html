Re: Why ECC RAM?

Ian,

Just an observation that I learnt the hard way. 

While ECC RAM is a luxury for most desktop machines, if you are running
dual CPU Athlon MP system based on the AMD 760MP/MPX (that is really the
only SMP Athlon chipset that I know of), then you are REQUIRED to
populate the banks with Registered DDR RAM. Since all of the registered
DDR modules that I've seen include ECC, this means getting registered
ECC DDR RAM.

Some motherboards like the Asus A7M266 say they don't require Registered
modules on a limited number of slots, but I've experienced many lock ups
and related problems before I replaced them with the Registered ECC
stuff.

Since Dual Athlon machines are popular (I use them at work and at home)
thought some people might take this onboard.

Cheers,
Joseph Tan
