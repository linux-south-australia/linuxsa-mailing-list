RE: diskless firewall?

Do a google search on mini-itx

It's in integrated motherboard/cpu with onboard video, sound, LAN, USB,
TVOut, etc.  It uses standard PC133 SDRAM and has two on-board IDE
controllers.  Apparently the chipset has the capacity to support a
floppy drive, but there is no connector on the board.

I suspect even the 500MHz (fanless) version would be perfectly suitable
for a network appliance such as this.  There are even fanless power
supplies available for these beauties.

There are very few cases available directly from australia for mini-itx
as far as I know (again, try google) but this will only get better.  I
think the form factor may be compatible with Mini-ATX and another spec
that alludes me for the moment

Jordan.
