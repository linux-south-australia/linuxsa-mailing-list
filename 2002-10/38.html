I can't believe we as a global community, following thousands of years of
successful evolution (no flames please) - need to expend as much resources
on network spam as we don security.

some spammer tried to sue a anti-spam crusader in Perth, failed, went in to
spamming again, tried to sue the anti-spam crusader again and is still

> Over the weekend I tested an installation of SpamAssassin at message
> accept time. This means that SA is run against each line of 
> the message as
> it is accepted from the remote system, and the connection is 
> immediately
> closed with an error.
> 
> Advantages:
> 
>    - saves paying for an entire message to be downloaded and processed
>      later
>    - helps get people off spammers lists because it will be marked as 
>      a failed delivery
>    - uses less system resources
> 
> I did this with Exim 4 with integration work from Marc 
> Merlin. Seems happy
> so far.
