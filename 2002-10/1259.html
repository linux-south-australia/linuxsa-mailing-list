> The corporate dialup is on 172 private addresses, which have a gateway to 10.
> private addresses which also have a gateway to 192.168 private addresses. The
> corporate network does not have a gateway to the outside world.
> 
> I wish to be connected to the Internet and the corporate network at once, but
> currently when I have both connections open, I lose the routes to the network I
> opened first.

Welcome to the wonderful world of Micro$oft routing :(

What you are seeing is default behavior. Microsnot believe that _any_
dialup connection must be your new _preferred_ default gateway. Ugh!

> 
> I suspect I should put in some static routes to tell the network how to reach
> each side. Am I correct here ?
> 
> Given the affirmative to the previous, then what kind of route commands do I
> have to type ?
> 
> Route add <Internet route>
> Route add <172 route>
> Route add <10. route>
> Route add <192.168 route>
> 
> does this seem right ?

I can't give you specifics as you have not indicated addresses in your
diagram but you need something along these lines:

route delete 0.0.0.0 mask 0.0.0.0 <last dailup>
route add 0.0.0.0 mask 0.0.0.0 <Internet route>
Route add <172 route>
Route add <10. route>
Route add <192.168 route>

Not sure why you need all of these, isn't your gateway set?
Bear in mind that the order in which you dialup is important.
Or you could just install a firewall that will do it all for you!
