Open IT - Govt to rewrite source code in Linux

NEW DELHI: If the Chinese have IT, get it. The Indian government
seems
to be taking a leaf out of China's operating system, and is
planning a
countrywide drive to promote the open source operating system,
Linux, as
the 'platform of choice' instead of 'proprietary' solutions.

For proprietory, read Microsoft, which controls over 90% of the
desktop
software market.

The Department of Information Technology has already devised a
strategy
to introduce Linux and open source software as a de-facto standard
in
academic institutions, especially in engineering colleges through
course
work that encourages use of such systems.

Research establishments would be advised to use and develop
re-distributable toolboxes just as Central government departments
and
state governments would be asked to use Linux-based offerings.

Read more about it here ...
http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/cms.dll/articleshow?artid=24598339
