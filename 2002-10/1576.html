Why ECC RAM?

Why use ECC RAM?  I quote it up by standard for clients - and it is
*significantly* more expensive (or maybe that is also because it is
"registered" and somehow supported).  I can see why it is important for
mission critical applications - obviously - but are desktop users at a loss
because RAM is not ECC?  I am not sure if errors occur at random and this
can lead to data corruption over time?  I've never had a problem that I can
detect with errors in RAM - it's always seemed quite reliable to me (unless
some problems I have had are due to RAM errors but it did not appear that
way).
