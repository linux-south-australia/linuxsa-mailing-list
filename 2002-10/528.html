At 1:08 PM +1000 9/10/02, Mcleod, Ian wrote:
>Page 8 - Telstra 'interconnect' process - can it really cost an ISP up to
>half a million dollars to establish an 'interconnection'?  Sorry - I was so
>flabbergasted that I must have forgotten what this means too.  What is
>'interconnection' mean?  Just establishing a link?  Just the few pages I
>have skimmed through on this report are quite enlightening.

Frankly, half a million dollars is probably way way under the full 
costs. I just keep finding more costs in that process every time I 
look at it.

By 'interconnection' this means SS7 telco interconnection of voice 
networks, BTW. On our side, VoIP based Cisco switches, but they have 
to speak the crufty old protocols to talk to the crufty old Telstra 
switches.

Telstra are excellent at making an economic barrier out of a 
technical problem. For various reasons, this particular barrier is a 
classic case of 'they might be legally required to offer it to other 
carriers, but they don't need to be nice about it'.

Hardware costs, software costs, Telstra certification testing costs, 
Telstra exchange 'conditioning' (the voice version of static route 
configuration) costs... each of them is far, far, far, far, far more 
than you'd rationally expect in a world with $2000 kick-ass Linux 
servers in it. These people do not live in that world.
