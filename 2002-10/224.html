> Hi Richard,
> 
> We have been customers of AirNet for some time and our Project has 5 
> accounts with them. I have no other relationship with them and the 
> following information is readily available from reports that AirNet 
> provided.
> 
> From March to around August/September AirNet were shafted by Hellstra's 
> incompetence and belligerent attitude, well they didn't fix the problem 
> that Hellstra caused. The fault was with equipment owned and operated 
> by Hellstra and it kept kicking AirNet's clients offline, or else 
> refusing to allow them to connect. Hellstra kept telling the public to 
> take it up with AirNet as they are the ISP. TIO was just as useless for 
> also insisting that AirNet were the problem.
> 
> Finally ACCC were brought into the picture and the TIO and Hellstra got 
> the message to fix the problem as it wasn't AirNet's equipment and it 
> wasn't under AirNet's control. Thank goodness for some sort of sanity 
> in the telcom industry.
> 
> The problem cost clients hundreds of dollars in connection charges and 
> it cost AirNet hundreds of clients who got so fed up with not being 
> able to connect. It cost AirNet buckets of dough and time trying to 
> resolve a problem that wasn't of their own making.
> 
> Eventually AirNet dropped Hellstra and went over to iPrimus and things 
> have been much better since that change.
> 
> Any ISP in a similar situation would probably have gone through similar 
> trouble trying to get such a problem resolved and I hold no grudges 
> against the company, I think Hellstra needs a fair boot in the backside 
> for their attitude and poor problem solving structure. Likewise the TIO 
> deserves a bucketing for insisting that the ISP was at fault when it 
> was clearly nothing of the ISP's doing.
> 
> Your problems may or may not be connected to this fault but there are 
> many out there who were affected by the problem but may not have 
> understood the background.
> 
> As for the community wireless thing. I am looking very seriously at it 
> as one means of bringing Internet access to a disadvantaged community 
> and helping to break down the digital divide. I have been talking to 
> Ross Chiswell and folks in the US over the last couple of years about 
> delivery of Internet by wireless to the community and I still think it 
> is achievable.
