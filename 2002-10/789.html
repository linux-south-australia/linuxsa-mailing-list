Re: booting Linux

Yeah, I had a similar problem when I installed SuSE at the place I 
worked at once, I didn't know you still needed LILO even if you were 
using a different boot manager (in my example, we were using the win NT 
boot manager). So what I did was I installed the LILO package, then I 
created an /etc/lilo.conf file from scratch (had to read the man pages a 
bit for that), I then ran lilo but told it to write itself to the root 
partition, not the MBR. You can type "rootdev" to find out what device 
is the root partition.

My advice about writing lilo.conf files, is to get somebody elses and 
modify it for your needs, actually I think I copied the one out of the 
man page and modified that when I did it. I'm not at a linux box at the 
moment so I can't help much more than that.

Good Luck!
