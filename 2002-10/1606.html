Re: 32 bit colour rather pointless?

No good Punk wrote:

>>So is 32 bit colour pointless for desktop users and
>>really only for people
>>who use X or Linux for professional work 
>>
>
>24 bit is a fine enough sample rate of the visible
>spectrum to appear seamless to the human eye, so it's
>"good enough" for displaying colour on almost all
>monitors. 
>
While the above is mostly true, there is now talk of using 64 bit colour 
in graphics cards. Even though you can't see the difference, and your 
monitor can't display the difference, the extra precision will ensure 
that rounding error and speed compromises in video card hardware when 
rendering don't dim or brighten colours unrealisticly when they undergo 
some 3d matrix operation.

Also, the human eye isn't consistent about it's perception of colours 
throughout the spectrum. There could well be parts of a 24 bit colour 
evenly-distributed pallette that aren't up to the resolution of some eyes.
