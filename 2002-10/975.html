If I may be so bold to say, I think one of your main problems is you try
to do too much in too little a time. There's an old adage that one
shouldn't run before one can walk; I fear if you start attempting to
understand:

* IP networking
* VPN's
* IPTABLES
* Routing
* IP Accounting

...all at once you may very well get unnecessarily confused.

I would suggest steps in this order:

1) Get a good understanding of networking (TCP, IP, UDP, ICMP and so
forth)
2) Get a good understanding of how to relate (1) to a packet filtering
tool such as IP tables
3) Get a good understanding of routing
4) Then get into IP packet accounting

If you're having difficulties understanding the concepts required to
setup a packet filtering firewall with one of the scripts, not to
mention by hand, then I would suggest you forget about learning IP
accounting until you do. It sounds harsh, but you need to know the
basics before getting into the more advanced stuff.
