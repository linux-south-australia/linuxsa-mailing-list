Re: 32 bit colour rather pointless?

> So is 32 bit colour pointless for desktop users and
> really only for people
> who use X or Linux for professional work - and then
> only with a decent
> monitor?

24 bit is a fine enough sample rate of the visible
spectrum to appear seamless to the human eye, so it's
"good enough" for displaying colour on almost all
monitors. 

Some features of 3d video cards only work at certain
resolutions and display depths. The last 8 bits allow
256 levels of transparency for colour blending, and
some features, eg often the stencil buffer like their
memory to be word aligned.

24 and 32 bit colour are not noticably different from
a users point of view if you do not require 3d accelaration.
