> anonymous and virtually untraceable" ads "straight to the

"... not to be confused with MSN Messenger ..."

which is a network already subject to enough spam, much like web
browsing, ICQ, IRC, Email.  I've heard of plans to use Bluetooth to make
your mobile phone display an advert when you're in close proximity to a
McDonalds, offering a discount cheeseburger if you go in and display the
advert to the service staff.

I suppose television and radio commercials are legitimate advertising,
as these are paid for by the advertiser, and not by the consumer, where
internet spam is paid for by the end user (in terms of $ per byte.)  But
TV isn't so innocent -- who's seen those things pop up half way through
a TV show you're watching which pop up on the bottom corner advertising
another TV show which will be out in three weeks, or something.

People ring me at work frequently spamming me with products on my
telephone.

Suppliers frequently me faxes that I constantly throw away instantly,
much like closing a pop-up window.

What's next?  Will my bathroom mirror pop up an advertisement
advertising a "face wash for men" product when I'm trying to shave?
