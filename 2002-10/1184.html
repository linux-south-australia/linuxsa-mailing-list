> Now AirNet are not stupid and I really doubt that anything is wrong 
> with their DNS and the way the host is set up  but what can you make 
> out of the message displayed on the front page?
>
> "Please note that we tried various methods in order to accurately 
> determine what would be your preferred language, but failed. One of the 
> reasons is that your IP address (211.26.59.55) could not be resolved to 
> a valid hostname.

First: I think all active, exposed IP addresses should have a reverse-name
mapping.  Your address doesn't.  To that extent there is something wrong
with AirNet's DNS (assuming the address is theirs.)

More generally, some sites try to guess your location based on your IP
address, or more specifically, the name of your IP address.  Should your
address be something.au they would assume you are in Australia.  This is
not a foolproof technique, but it's probably mostly correct.
