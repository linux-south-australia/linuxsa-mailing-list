Re: when do hard drives go to heaven?

Anthony Symons wrote:
> Generally - you get what you pay for.

With some notorious exceptions with particular IDE models from
... and ....... .

That's the real difference between IDE and SCSI now that the
drive mechanics are often the same.  Because they're aimed at
different markets firms will keep pumping out drives with a
lower MTBF into the IDE consumer market but will withdraw the
SCSI drive from sale.

Apart from that with SCSI you're basically paying for the lesser
economies of scale and the SCSI protocol, which gets you better
performance in multiprocessing scenarios.
