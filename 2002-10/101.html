No, you're missing two points here:

1.  There should be no spaces round the dash:

    "0x2d0x2dI should get over it0x2d0x2d".

2.  That's the hex representation.  If that's what you want, you
    should write:

    "0x2d0x2d49200x730x680x6f0x750x6c0x640x200x670x650x740x200x6f0x760x650x720x200x690x740x2d0x2d".

3.  This is unnecessarily clumsy, though.  In such a case, you don't
    need the 0x:

    "2d2d492073686f756c6420676574206f7665722069742d2d".

4.  This is still unnecessarily verbose, since all the characters are
    printable.  Instead, I'd be inclined to include them directly,
    rather than their hex representations:

    "--I should get over it--".
