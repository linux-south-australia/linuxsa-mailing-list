Re: [OT] Watchdog eyes broadband limits

Brian Astill wrote:

> How could one monitor the download value of an hour's websurfing?  I guess you
> could empty your browser's cache before commencing to browse and see what you
> had collected after you had finished?

The PPP dialer under Windows has as long as I can remember shown bytes
uploaded/downloaded statistics.  Unless you're running RAS, I don't think
you even have to go into properties to see it.  Surely it wouldn't take
much eduma-cating the end users that everytime the one marked "bytes
downloaded" increments, they're paying for that?  Under pppd (now we're
talking Unices), it records some lovely statistics such as:

Oct 16 00:03:28 zen pppd[23678]: Modem hangup
Oct 16 00:03:28 zen pppd[23678]: Connection terminated.
Oct 16 00:03:28 zen pppd[23678]: Connect time 298.9 minutes.
Oct 16 00:03:28 zen pppd[23678]: Sent 1643613 bytes, received 7796250 bytes.
Oct 16 00:03:29 zen pppd[23678]: Exit.

And you can query the interface while it's up and connected online
dynamically.  Outrageous!
