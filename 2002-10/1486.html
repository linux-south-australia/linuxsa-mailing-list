Re: ADSL providers - debian mirror?

We are perfectly happy to keep augmenting the things that we mirror. 
As has already been said, the facility is very new and we're still 
shaking it down - but we are also adding additional distributions to 
it on a continuing basis as well.

Please don't construe what Ian has said below as policy or as 
anything negative.

I want to make sure that this is clear - just because Ian didn't get 
an instant prediction of the nanosecond at which his hearts' desire 
will be fulfilled, that isn't a statement that we aren't likely to do 
exactly what he's requested - just that like all things in the world, 
sometimes just a tiny bit of patience is worth including in your 
expectations and requests :)

We are still feeling our way in terms of what is most useful for our 
customers in mirroring terms.

Helpful suggestions are always welcome (most usefully directly to us, 
not surprisingly - via our feedback email address, 
feedback@internode.on.net, or via a standard support request through 
our support web interface, or whatever).

But... the below reads ( to me ) like an accusation that we won't do 
it or we don't care - and neither could be farther from the truth. 
We're at the forefront of listening to what our customers need, and 
we always have been. That is why the mirrors exist (and in each 
state, so that every customer, in any state we provide ADSL, get the 
same high performance access to the mirrors as well).

In terms of other things to mirror - again we're looking into this at 
present. While its easy to ask for something, please bear in mind 
that sometimes its harder to do than it might seem from the outside 
to do these things at the snap of the fingers.

(and again the debian primary cd distributions are already on the 
Internode mirrors now)

Cheers,
   Simon Hackett
   Internode
