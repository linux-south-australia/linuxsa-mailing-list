Re: linux distro on small HD

Hi.

Actually, if I read your message correctly:

1. You want to build and run a linux box headless and communicate with it
using telnet from your OS9 Mac via cross over cable.

2. You want to develop DB and PHP based webpages on the linux box for
testing before uploading to the net.

It will work well if you have your head around the networking issues and
the clients on the Mac. You could even edit the html and php on your mac
and submit them to your linux box over the crossover cable with little
effort. I'd suggest Transmit (for ftp) and niftytelnet for ssh/telnet.

I'd definitely be looking for some extra memory for the PC, but otherwise
it will run fine. I have a P150 doing exactly what you are thinking of and
more and it is sweet. 

In fact with a bit more memory, you could give the dialup role to the
linux box and get firewalling and ip-masquerade and even perhaps a mail
server as a bonus.

Regards,

Michael
