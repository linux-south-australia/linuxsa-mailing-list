RE: ADSL providers - debian mirror?

cool - so *is* it possible to mirror open and close source software
archives?  Is it storage and server infrastructure that's the issue?  I
think it is quite reasonable to be concerned over blowing a quota sky high
just through updating one's OS if this can be avoided.  Like I said - it has
nothing to do with Internode - more like the Southern Cross Cable company or
something - I don't know..

ISP's can't share or link up mirrors can they?  I agree that it does seem
like an awful amount of effort that is just duplicated across various ISPs
to set up their own individual mirrors - so perhaps this can be shared?  It
does seem like a terrible waste to have each ISP suck down 10's of gigabytes
per day just to maintain mirrors if other ISPs are doing the same thing.

Speaking of which - can ISP's also share news server to perhaps one day
allow unmetered or increased quota for these?  Once again - I see how this
can not be provided at present if each ISP sucks down many gigabytes per day
just to maintain the news servers.

I also think that now that I have signed up for ADSL - I have some right to
voice my opinion.  It is not as though I am criticising anything without
actually signing up :-)  Need I say I signed up for the mega 8GB account too
(I am not THAT stingy a customer) so I don't appreciate being labelled
greedy or impatient.  And like I said I never pretend to be an authority on
the technical side of it - I just want to see more services like a customer
of any other service would.  I'm only human - and a big fan of Internode and
the quality services Internode provides..  I spend more time promoting
Internode to my friends than criticising it Simon :-)
