Re: diskless firewall?

> Is it possible to have an old Pentium 133 with no hard drive boot off
> another machine to be used a firewall?  Then a small partition could be set
> aside - say a GB or two for smoothwall (or whatever) on the host machine.
> Unless someone has some old hard drives lying around.

Why not just have it boot off a floppy or cdrom?  It'll dump everything into 
ram and then not access anything anymore.  

Diskless fw's are bad news -> if your other machine is compromised then you 
lose it.

> But this would save space and noise if it is diskless - I could probably
> cram it in somewhere unobtrusive..
> 
> Is it possible to 'flatten' a motherboard so cards are not at right angles
> so one can fit it in a small box?  I don't know if PCI cables exist?

YOu can get little risers to put them at right angles but they can get 
cranky.
