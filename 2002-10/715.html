> It is hard to imagine a new PC coming out with a 133Mhz processor. What makes 
> something like this keep up with current computing standards.

PC 104 stuff is not meant to compete with your desktop PC. It's very
small form factor, and designed to be packaged in embedded products.
You don't have one on your desk. Given the market, CPU speed isn't
really a priority, at least not when compared to size, heat
generation, packaging, and reliability. Even if you think about your
desktop, how often are you really limited by CPU speed? If you're
making a router, then you just need enough speed to keep up with the
network, and 133MHz is likely to be more than plenty for that.

That said, I don't actually understand your question/statement (I
assume it's a question...)
