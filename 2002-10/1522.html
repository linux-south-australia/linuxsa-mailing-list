What David is trying to say is that we're completely unprejudiced against
BSD systems, but that many of us have little or no experience with them.

In general, a non-fatal error is the same as a recovered or soft error,
which means that an operation was attempted, the hardware reported an
error, the operation was retried, and it then succeeded.  Soft errors
can be an indication of deterioration of a device.  There's no need to be
overly worried about an isolated incident, but if it recurrs you should
think about replacing the device.
