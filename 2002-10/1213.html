Monday, October 21, 2002, 9:35:05 AM, Andrew wrote:

> On Wed, Oct 16, 2002 at 12:20:38PM +0930, Richard Russell wrote:

>> What are LAT and PAT? I assume PAT is Port Address Translating(?) -- I
>> haven't heard of it before. Why is it useful?

> Seems that PAT is how Cisco say what the rest of the world calls NAT.

PAT is as stated earlier Port Address Transaltion, which is also known
as IP Masquerading or Hide NAT.  Used primarily where you want to
translate several internal IP's to a single external IP (or in the
case of Cisco gear, a pool of external IPs).

Alternatively, you could use static one to one NAT (where you have an
external IP for every internal IP).

Then you can mix and match with source and destination NAT or source
PAT and destination NAT, etc, etc...
