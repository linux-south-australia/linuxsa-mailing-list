RE: [OT] Watchdog eyes broadband limits

Whichever way you look at it - when a over 200MB/hr can be downloaded over a
512K link - with the average limit about 1 - 3 GB - it is still a matter of
concern.  That's about 1GB / 4 hours or so - a not-so-aware average Jo could
quite easily blow their limit sky high in a week and wind up paying over $30
/ hr.  A $500 excess bill is not unlikely - in fact - seems quite
reasonable.

Telstra did advertise their 'broadband' connection with a 'whopping' 300 MB
limit in the IT section of The Australian earlier this year or late last
year - I really wish I kept that add - it so pathetic it should be in a
museum.  They do give people the impression that one can watch news,
download trailers for films, etc without a worry in the world - which is
complete rubbish.

Everyone I meet from the LinuxSA group say the same thing in person but
another on the list (damn ignorant newbies) but they themselves say they
won't touch ADSL with a 50 foot barge pole when I inquire if they intend to
get it in person.

Germany, Denmark, etc have no limits with connections up to 8Mbit (I can
forward emails from a friend there if you don't believe me) - the USA is
another complex story.

Australia - the land of the lucky subsidised farmers.
