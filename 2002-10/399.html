Re: how to start remote X session?

On Tue, Oct 08, 2002 at 10:27:32AM +1000, Mcleod, Ian wrote:
> If I have a machine at IP (X) say 192.168.0.1 - how can I start a remote X
> session on that from another machine?  It may make administration easier
> than Webmin or give more flexibility..

Have you had a look at the HOWTOs yet? There's at least one HOWTO that talks
about this exact thing (XDCMP HOWTO). Other HOWTOs (like the Thin Client
HOWTO) mention this a little more briefly.

Depends on how you want the session to work:

single applications:
	* use ssh to log into the other machine
	* run application by typing command into ssh session

Entire desktop
	* Turn on XDCMP support in the display manager of your choice (gdm is
probably one of the easiest - have a look at /etc/X11/gdm/gdm.conf)
	* Run something like X :1 -query 192.168.0.1 on the machine you want
the display to appear on.


You may also want to look at Xnest, which I think allows the nesting of X
servers within other X servers.

The man pages aren't too bad either.

Hope it helps,
