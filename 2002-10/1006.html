Re: IP-masquerading / firewalling with IRC

Daniel Mundy wrote:

> If you have no other services already being let through, then using DROP
> instead of REJECT will make your box effectively invisible to scans.

To SYN scans.  There are other scans that can still provoke responses.

Quite often, I find that firewalls that drop packets (in either direction)
make diagnosing network problems harder.  I had a problem with fetchmail
out at a client, where someone else had set the firewall to drop what was
once a working connection, and fetchmail would hang for a couple of minutes
before timing out[1].  It would have been nice for fetchmail to receive an
immediate reject (people who are concerned about reject packets causing
a DOS attack should bear in mind that ICMP messages are (or can be) rate
limited under Linux).
