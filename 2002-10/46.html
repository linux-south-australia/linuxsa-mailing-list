Blocking Spam - blacklisting IPs?

I have heard that IPs can be blacklisted - which is why that idiot in Perth
tried to sue our courageous anti-spammer - as he placed the idiot in
question on a block list - thereby blocking the idiot's consitutional right
to inundate people with his rubbish.

How can one blacklist IPs?

I go through my ISP (ihug) for email - of course they never do anything when
I report spam requesting the IP be put on a block list for their email
server.  I am not sure if this is because they can't or because they
couldn't care.  They just respond advising me to follow other avenues.

Is it possible to 'reject' traffic and have one's ISP bill the spammer for
wasted bandwidth?  Or legally advise the spammer that they will be billed on
a T&M basis for filtering through their rubbish - at say $10 an email (plus
GST)?  I am seriously considering this path if anyone has any legal advice?

> Another possible alternative is shunning. 
> 
> You block the spammers ip address and notify the net block 
> owner. I mean
> block the address from sending email to you, redirect all web request
> from that address to an explaination page with instruction on how to
> contact you. It is necessary to block all ports requests to and from
> that address because some spam contains web addresses for futher
> information promotional material, confirmation of a valid 
> email address.
> 
> If you dont get any response from the spammer or the net block owner,
> you may consider blocking the entire netblock. It is a RFC requirement
> for postmaster address to be read by humans or something to 
> that effect.
> 
> I have read about this method before somewhere on the web about 18
> months ago. I assume that there is a catch. ISPs may not be able to
> follow this path but there is nothing that says that you must 
> carry any
> traffic on your private network as the end user.
