That's a pretty mellow average for us,
we get anywhere from one every 3 days to 3 in the same day ;-)
(mainly on our ISP mailserver and nameserver)

They're always the same thing too, "portscan" or "mscan" 
(mscan is a portscan derivitave with a phf-attack for your
webserver included). We have pretty much any port blocked that 
isn't essential to the server now, so it rarely causes a drama.
(There are the odd 'netbus' attempts too, hehehe)

"mscan" seems to be the prefered tool these days. It has a
default list of ports to scan/DoS-attack:

domain, telnet, finger, 143(imap), pop3, sunrpc, 
and of course three hits to "/cgi-bin/phf*" on your port 80 
(which any _reasonably_ recent release of Apache has a contingency for
anyway, just check the bottom of access.conf or srm.conf to enable it)

*But it can be customised to run any list of ports supplied*

>The second was reported to me by someone in the US who said
>they found someone doing attacks on a hit list which
>included me.

Yes, there are such hit-lists around (and it seems you always end up 
on more of them whenever you catch/suspend a warezdude/cracker user ;-P)
I think they can also do really stupid wildcard IPs too (eg: 203.*.*.*).
