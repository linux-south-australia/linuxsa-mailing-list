I'm tempted to buy now... 
by Anonymous Coward on Tuesday November 17, @11:24AM 

AMD has supported SMP since K6 i believe, but there are no chipsets
available because no manufacturers think people want SMP K6 or K6-2  
systems. Intel processors use APIC to do SMP, and AMD uses OpenPIC (an
open standard created by IBM, HP, AMD etc...). But there are no x86
mother-boards that support it. Blame it on Intel ;P 

Multiproc AMD's (Score:1)
by RISCy on Tuesday November 17, @01:58PM 

AMD's have been Multi processor for a while, the catch is that they use
OpenPIC(I belive) Multi processor standard, not SMP. This means that
their are neither motherboards or software to suport multiprocessor 
AMDS. Another fact is that the AMDS are Super7 not Slot 1 since Intel
has refused to licenes this to AMD(can we say monopoly?). This drasticly
limits your choices, infact I have yet to find a Multiprocessor Super7
board. Currently AMD has recomended only two motherboards for use with
the AMD K6-2 400, and they are both FIC, which I have never used before. 

But I do have good news. AMD will be implimenting the SMP standard into
the new K7's which should be out 1st half 99! I'm really excited about
that, but I a waiting to see how many companies build multiprocessor 
motherboards with Super7 sockets.

I hope it won't be a long wait...
