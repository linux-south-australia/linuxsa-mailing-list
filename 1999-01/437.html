Well, if it's any consolation, I run a server that's online for 90% of
the time, and if I check through the logs I usually see a few people
running a portscanner over it, or else trying various nukes out on it.
My server doesn't actually provide any public services, but I guess
there are people out there who have tools that accept a range of IPs,
and then scan through all ports on all of them, looking for security
holes, and the like. wingate lusers are often a nice target for this
sort of stuff, since most of them have no idea what's going on, I guess.
Whenever I see this going on on my PC, I send an email to the admin at
the originating ISP, stating that I have detected one of their users
doing whatever they were doing, and I provide the IPs and time, and my
offset from GMT time. Some things can have IPs spoofed, but port
scanning usually doesnt.
ISP's don't like kinder-hackers, since they're usually the same type
that will be downloading warez 24 hrs a day or flooding people, and thus
using a lot of the ISP's bandwidth. I know some ISPs have a strict 'no
port scanning/BO-scanning/flooding/spamming/etc' policy, so if you see
someone doing it to you, then send their ISP an email and let them deal
with it ;)

On another note - you can use linux to monitor the various common ports
that are targets of winnukes, BO, netbus, etc. and then log the incoming
IP, and automatically email them stuff back...It's quite neat, and
totally automated. Although i guess it leaves you vulnerable to flooding
and thus sending millions of emails out, so I tend to just log it
locally.
