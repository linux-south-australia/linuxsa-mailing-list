XFree86 support for Intel740 AGP video card

Question:  Is there a new version of XFree86 for an Intel 740 AGP video card?

I've just tried to help someone install redhat 5.2 on a nice
shiny new computer.
There were some substantial problems:

1.	The LS120 drive (on an IDE bus, secondary master)
	could not be written to by the boot disk kernel,
	which seemed to only want to use the FDC interface,
	although the BIOS is configured to use the LS120 for
	boot disk in preference to drive C: (primary IDE master).

2.	The AGP card (Intel 740) seemed not to have any support
	in XFree86, and the SVGA support in XFree86 only seemed to
	want to look at the PCI bus, apparently not wanting to
	look at the AGP port at all.

Problem 1 meant that it was difficult to read the
supplementary and rescue disks from the boot-disk kernel
(in fact, it was impossible). "rawrite.exe" worked fine though.

Problem 2 means that it looks like the PC cannot be used
with any linux graphics until XFree86 supports the AGP card.

I've looked at the site www.xfree86.org or whatever, and
found no encouragement. 

Question:
Is it really true that this card will not run in XFree86?
Or is there support somewhere that I don't know about?

Cheers, and thanks in advance for any info,
Alan Kennington.
