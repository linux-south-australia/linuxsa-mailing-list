Re: Linux:MBR & bootloader

To what others have already said in answer to this, I would like to add:

You will get a considerable performance boost if you put your swap
partition on a different physical drive to everything else (unless you
have an obscene amount of ram).  On my system, I have 2 drives: one with a
large partition for linux and a small partition for 'doze 95's swapfile,
and the other with a large partition for 'doze 95 and a small one for
linux to swap on. 

In your case, I would (after deciding how to partition your new 4.3G
drive) use FIPS to cut off a ~100Mb partition from the other drive, and
use that for the linux swap.
