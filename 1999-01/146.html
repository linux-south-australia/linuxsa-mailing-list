Be aware that this only shows interrupts that have devices that have
*registered* them and attached to them.  Note also that some PCI devices
grab some interrupts that may not be in /proc/pci.  

Although there are no Linux drivers that hook into an interrupt there
may be some IRQs that have devices listening...

Eg: on my system

/proc/interrupts

 0:  223866432   timer
 1:     722356   keyboard
 2:          0   cascade
 4:    2604341 + serial
 8:          1 + rtc
11:     390894   3c509
13:          1   math error
14:    2075841 + ide0
15:         48 + ide1

/proc/pci

PCI devices found:
  Bus  0, device  14, function  0:
    VGA compatible controller: S3 Inc. ViRGE/DX or /GX (rev 1).
      Medium devsel.  IRQ 7.  Master Capable.  Latency=64.  Min Gnt=4.Max 
  Bus  0, device   7, function  2:
    USB Controller: Intel 82371AB PIIX4 USB (rev 1).
      Medium devsel.  Fast back-to-back capable.  IRQ 9.  Master Capable.  

So merge the two and get a better picture.  But still that leaves ISA 
and on board devices that may be listening but not have drivers installed.

If I did use USB support on my system under Linux then the driver would
hook into IRQ 9, including it in /proc/interrupts.
