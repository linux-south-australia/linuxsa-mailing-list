RE: system lockup

On Mon, 4 Jan 1999, Stock, Nick wrote:

> I have experienced the same problem several times.
> Usually you have a few seconds between noticing that
> the machine is swapping everything to HDD and it
> locking up to key strokes.
> In this time you need to hit Ctrl-Alt-Backspace to
> kill the X server. In the 4 times it has happened
> to me in the last 6 months, three times it was due
> to Netscape 4.0x and once due to the KDE control
> panel. For some reason the process just starts eating
> resources.

You can set limits so processes can't consume too many resources. In
particular I limit the number of processes a user can run and how big a
processes data segment can get. You can do this under bash with "ulimit".
Once you have worked out what sort of limits you want to set put them in
/etc/profile.
