>                       The British government is quietly forming a crack task 
> force of police officers to handle an expected
>                       crime wave that could result from the Year 2000-induced 
> failure of alarm systems and computerized
>                       security systems. 
> 
>                       According to various media reports and Newsbytes' 
> sources, at least 1,400 police officers across the
>                       UK have been recruited into the specialist teams, and 
> will undergo special training in the months
>                       ahead. 
> 
>                       The Times, meanwhile, has reported that police on the 
> National Crime Squad (NCS) are being refused
>                       leave across the New Year period at the end of the year, 
> because of worries about Y2K problems
>                       with company security systems. The daily paper also 
> notes that 35 of the 43 regional police regions
>                       have canceled leave for the New Year period. 
> 
>                       Because of overtime and other issues, including the 
> rental of army helicopters to fly in police officers
>                       to specific areas in the event of an emergency, such as 
> rioting and looting, the times says that the
>                       Y2K problem will add 58 million pounds ($95 million) to 
> the cost of policing the UK.
> 
>                       The British Home Office has refused to comment on the 
> reports.
