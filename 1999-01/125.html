Sorry about the large snips.

GNAT is indeed the Gnu Ada compiler, and it is integrated with the GCC
compiler family.  However due to some update cycles and other random,
silly reasons, it isn't included in the base GCC distribution unlike
just about every other language that is part of the GCC family, hence
the 'other' rpm.  

<waffle>

You may need to play around with your LD_LIBRARY_PATH, other paths,
environment variables and dead chickens etc to have both the latest GCC
C compiler and a GCC GNAT (Ada) compiler on the same machine and both
working successfully.  Of course GNAT _includes_ an earlier GCC than the
latest, but most people seem to want the latest and greatest of both.

</waffle>

It does produce reasonably large exe's, but they can be stripped,
optimised etc and at least Ada is strongly typed! :-)
