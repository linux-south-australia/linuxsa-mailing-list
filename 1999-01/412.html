David Newall wrote:

 > > Does the retailer have no comeback -at all-, considering the end user
 > > agreed to the price of the computer -including- the software?
 > 
 > What stops the retailer refunding you a pittance?  After all, neither
 > of you have agreed on the value of the software which was bundled despite
 > that you didn't want it.  If the retail offers you $10 for it, what can
 > you do?

Complain to the ACCC:  Selling a product for less than you bought it
wholesale so that you can gain market share is known as "dumping."
If a retailer is going to claim that the Windows license you've
bought is worth $10, when there are contracts between the retailer
and Microsoft which state, in writing, that it's worth (say) $110,
then the retailer is not going to find themselves in a nice legal
situation:  It'd look like they were wearing a $100 loss on the sale
to gain a competitive advantage.

 > I'll tell you what you can do: You can claim it's not a genuine refund

 > what an OEM edition of Win9x costs the retailer, probably it's about
