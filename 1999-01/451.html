Re: Bogomips

>  Is 699.6 bogomips a lot?

699.6 is exactly what I have, and I'm running a k6-2 350. From my
experience, bogomips go as follows:

486 = ~1/2x clock speed.
Vanilla Pentium (non MMX) = ~1/2x clock speed.
Cx686 = ~1x clock speed.
K6 and K6-2 = ~2x clock speed.
I can't really vouch for anything else, though.

Bogomips really aren't a good benchmark. That's why they're called
bogomips... bogus millions of instructions per second. AFAIK, they're used
for calculating hardware delay loops and suchlike.

Toshiba/Bennett thing?
