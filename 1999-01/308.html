Re: PCI Discovery Modem and Linux

Simon Lindsay wrote:

> I think I may have outsmarted myself by getting the latest PCI v.90
> discovery modem in my PC at home, and now trying to put linux on, it wont
> recognise it.
>
> The crazy thing is using IRQ 10 or something, and has no port as such.
>
> Has anybody had any luck getting these beasties going, or do I have to
> revive my old external?

It's known as a 'winmodem' it uses the cpu to do the real work - hence the
reason it's pci; it needs the bus bandwidth. You will not get this beast
working under linux as the creators refuse to release the specs to anyone
but microsoft. Go back to your external.

NOTE:
To all linux users - Beware! Be very careful when getting a pci modem. Most
are winmodems. Cheek before buying it by asking if it works in dos WITHOUT
any drivers.
