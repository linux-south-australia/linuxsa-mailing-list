Re: Portscan Attacks

Daniel Callan wrote:

 > You could even be really sadistic and write some 
 > retaliatory "go away"/"nuke-em-back" programs to respond instead :-P

Andrew Rutherford did that when he was at Adelaide Uni:  He modified
tcpdump to look for TCP sessions with known MUDs;  when it found them
it'd automatically forge a TCP RST packet with the MUD's source address
and the mud-user's destination address, effectively closing their
connection.

Bastard :-)

 > But your right, it would be a lot easier (not to mention less stressful)
 > if it were all layed out in HOWTOs

The problem with a security how-to is similar to the problem get get
if someone telnets to your SMTP port and finds out what version of sendmail
you're running:  They know what steps you've taken *and* they know 
which vulnerabilities *haven't* been closed.  If you publish a network-
security HOWTO then the black-hats will immediately step-up attacks
on anything that isn't mentioned in the HOWTO.

[ and you're fooling yourself if you think the HOWTO can ever catch
  up with that ]
