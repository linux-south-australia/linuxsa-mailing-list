BSD --> Bloody Semantics Discussion ?

>What you are excluded from doing is taking a GPL work, enhancing it,
>and then redistributing it on different conditions than the base
>work.  GPL forces you to do nothing at all.  It *permits* you to
>do some things, if you so choose.

Heya...  well I'm no hard core Linux/BSD nerd, but I can spot a
self-contradicting statement when I see one. (i.e. I'm with Mark on this
one)

How can you claim that a license imposes an exclusion on someone, and then
claim that it doesn't force them into a course of action ?

Your *choice* is whether or not to want to distribute your work so that
other people can use it.

Once you've decided that, it sounds like the GPL *forces* you to give away
the source as well, whereas the BSD license allows you to just give it out
and let people use it as they like (including selling it), providing they
acknowledge where they got it in the first place...

Well that's what I made of it, anyway :)
