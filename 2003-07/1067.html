Actually, I don't think that this last claim is true any more, since SCO 
has started making stupid noises like it can sue people for using software 
that has been supplied by a developer who breached copyright law.

At least here in the US, as a result, more and more companies are asking 
for indemnities and are asking about the provenance of the software they 
are being sold. Up on /. is an article about one software development 
house being asked to certify that their product contains no "Free 
Software." I wonder if they asked MS that also :-)

Now, here is my personal experience with both the GPL and BSD licences. We 
use both in our product. While there is a lot of crap running around about 
the GPL being viral, it is true that the GPL places an extra burden on 
developers that the BSD licence does not.

To ensure that we can avoid making life easy for competitors (which is a 
real imperitive in business and VC look increasingly nervious when they 
see business plans that are totally based on GPL'd software) we have to:

1. Avoid using any of our libraries in GPL'd code. That means re-writing 
any functions that we want to use and adding them to the GPL'd code.

2, Spend effort in getting the source ready for release so that when we 
release the product, the source is available to our customers as well.

3. Invest brain power in coming up with ways to keep stuff out of the 
reach of the GPL, and look at re-writing the GPL'd stuff, possibly in a 
clean-room environment.
