James,

> matter, tell me if there's a way to find out what options were used to
> compile the kernel, so that one can make one the same as, say, a Redhat
> pre-packaged kernel...

Look at the ".config" file in:

 /usr/src/linux/.config

[or /usr/src/linux-2.4/.config]

You can "make menuconfig" and it will pick the settings up.

RedHat tends to store their kernel configurations in:

 /usr/src/linux-2.4/arch/i386

[or some such place]

If you don't have access to the .config file, I'm not sure how you'd go
about finding out what was built into the kernel.
