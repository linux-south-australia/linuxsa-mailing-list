Well, the original code is often a small part of any product. There is a 
huge amount of work that goes into a product over and above writing the 
original code. Testing, making sure that it is usable, figuring out what 
UI customers want and so on.

For example, at Panasas we use FreeBSD as the basis of our NAS. It runs on 
the metadata servers, and it runs on the object based storage devices, and 
we use other open source software including Samba, Kerberos, and etc. 

However, to turn it into a product that can deliver a number of gigabytes 
a second (larger than three) of read and write bandwidth to a Linux 
cluster has taken an enormous amount of work, and is arguably more 
valuable than the other pieces of the puzzle. Of course, that has involved 
writing a lot of software ourselves, and extremely tricky parallel IO 
software at that. [1]

> which the GPL avoids.

recover your investment and make a profit.
