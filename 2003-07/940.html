Re: [OT] Creation Mythology

Graham Smith <linuxsalist@sonicresolutions.com> wrote:
> The point I'm trying to make here is that accepting the "big bang"
> theory is as much a belief as Christianity is, to the extent that it
> relies on the unexplained.

No, there's a fundamental difference between belief in Chistianity and
belief in big bang.  Douglas Adams said it neatly, when he wrote, the
proof of God's non-existance:

  ``"I refuse to prove I exist", said God, "for proof denies faith
 and without faith I am nothing."''
     -- Douglas Adams, The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Universe

When you start proving God it ceases to be religion and becomes science;
and religion is the antithesis of science.  That is no argument against
Christianity, and it is not my intent to argue along those lines.
My point, which I hope is now clear, is that Christendom's belief is
intrinsic (you are not allowed to question the dogma), whereas science
has no belief, merely unproven theories.  And science expects you to
question everything.
