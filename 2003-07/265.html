This was posted in the Lindows forum a few days ago.  I will say this, 
Revolution OS was the only DVD movie I have ever played in Linux. I have 
tried it in Mandrake, SUSE, Red Hat and Lindows without the DVD 
decryption codec.

So  I recently decided to use BeOS to watch DVD movies because it just 
isn't worth mucking around with Linux to get it to work. Watching a DVD 
in BeOS is drop dead easy and dependible, and that's the way it should be.

All you have to do is go to bebits and find the open source application 
called vic. Just click on the application download, unzip the 
application, and viola! you are done.  Vic was easy to install, and that 
is very nice for a change. I don't know why Linux can't be that easy, 
but maybe the decrypted codec is the answer.
