Re: comparative worth of Qt toolkit and GTK+ ?

On Sun, Jul 27, 2003 at 11:23:54PM +0930, Alan Kennington wrote:

 > I'm finding that GTK+ is very _grey_ indeed - like dusk in Moscow on
 > an overcast day. (Now I know why the GIMP and ethereal have such a
 > grey look and feel.) 
 > But when I look at the Qt toolkit documentation, I get the impression
 > that it might not be that much better.

They're both themeable.  What that means is that the choice of color is,
generally speaking, supposed to come from the end-user's preferences 
rather than the programmer.  If the end-user hasn't expressed a preference
by selecting a theme, they both look grey.

Gnome and KDE both come with preferences dialogs which end-users can use
to select the theme they want.  It's pretty straightforward:  When you 
select a theme, all GTK apps suddenly adopt the look-and-feel that the
theme expresse.
