Re: Aunt Nelly (was Re: Re: Re: bsd- easier than you think)

Regarding costing too much money to chase individual users, I think MS can
take RIAA's lead and go for it.  Make an example of ~1000 people which will
scare the rest into line (ie. paying the required licenses).  They can sue
every license abuser for $150,000 per violated copyright, extract maybe $10k
in the end, and reap the massive profits from all the newly generated sales.

Is a boycott of MS products going to be successful?  Yes, about as
successful as file sharers boycotting the current-flavour-of-the-month
boyband (ie. no).  By sheer coincidence (sarcasm) those doing the boycotting
weren't interested in buying the product anyway.

There is no doubt that piracy has helped MS products become ubiquitous, but
I don't think an individual user crackdown will see a large number of new
users flocking to Linux.  Still, I'd like to be proven wrong ;-)
