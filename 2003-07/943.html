Re: Re: bsd- easier than you think

Mark Newton <newton@atdot.dotat.org> wrote:
> On Wed, Jul 23, 2003 at 03:37:49PM +0930, davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:
>> Mark Newton <newton@atdot.dotat.org> wrote:
>> > What do you see as the deficiency in the BSD license which doesn't 
>> > allow "... others to benefit from [your] gift"?
>> 
>> Someone can build on my gift and then deny me the benefits.
>
> But then what they've built on it is THEIR gift. 

Well, no, the argument is that they don't want to make it a gift.

> I know there are emotional arguments describing why it should be ok
> to force them to provide you with access to the fruits of -their-
> labours

Sure there are, just as there are well-reasoned, non-emotive arguments.
I do believe, though, that GPL doesn't *force* them to do anything
with the fruits of their labours.  What it does, is makes them decide,
before they expend their labour, what their long-term intention is.
If they intend to make a proprietary product then they may not start
with GPLed work.  If they choose to take my gift as a starting point
then it follows, ipso facto, that they already chose to make their
work GPLed.
