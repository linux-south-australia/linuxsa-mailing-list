Re: The censorship debate...

A film is a method of telling a story. If sexually explicit scenes are 
required to tell the story, to make a point, or to get that point across to 
the audience, then it is quite acceptable. 

Considering that most films portray moments in time, the most logical thing 
to do is to make the films as realistic as possible. While many may argue 
that it is a stupid comparison - Talking and sexual intercourse are both 
natural expressions of humankind. In films, we are allowed to express 
ourselves by talking, but not by engaging in sexual intercourse, unless it 
is simulated. Should we simulate talking as well?
