This claim is interesting for a couple of reasons:

  1. The scientific theory of evolution does not deal with how the
     universe began or how the solar system got here or how the
     very first thing that could be called life arose. Those are
     the subjects of other areas of study, including abiogenesis.

  2. A number of popular explanations for how things began posit that
     one or more gods were involved. These explanations (sometimes
     called myths) leave out all the interesting details, which, to 
     any sentient entity, is just totally unsatisfactory.

> The thing I find most ironic about the theory of evolution is that
> ultimately evolution relies on creation anyway.  Even the "big bang"
> theory relies on the existence of material and energy, in order for it
> to take place.  Where, might I ask, did all this "stuff" come from.  To
> date I haven't heard a single theory on the matter because ultimately it
> involves a question that can only be answered with something being
> created.

So, where did this 'creator' come from. Please explain why we must posit a 
creator, who was not itself created?

Also, please explain how we are to recognize the results of 'creation' and 
deal with the expistemological issues.
