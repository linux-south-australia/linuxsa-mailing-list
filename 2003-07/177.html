You may wish to try recording it on a daily basis for a week or two, and
estimate from that what your expected usage should be, given no
abnormalities. Also check for signs of 0wnage, because people often crack
machines, make an anonymous FTP server there, and store warez -- see if you
have any unexpected files lying around (MP3s, AVIs, ISOs, etc)...

You may also wish to consider traffic that might have been sent to you but
you have been blocking... It's possible that traffic is being charged by
your ISP, because it gets sent to you, but gets filtered out before your
machine sees it... I have a feeling that the number you list above would
include packets rejected by iptables & friends, but if you have a separate
device that is filtering packets, perhaps you are being charged for these
packets but not actually seeing them on this interface.

Also, as an aside, I'm just wondering what derivative of Linux you would be
talking about? I didn't know there were any... Perhaps uLinux or something,
but... Hmm... the BSDs and Unices are not Linux derivatives...
