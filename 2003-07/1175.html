Re: Re: bsd- easier than you think

Mark,

I understand your points about BSD vs GPL license.  As I posted with my 
beermanifesto earlier I see there's room for both (and more licenses) and 
it's great that you have a strong opinion on the various licenses, but that 
doesn't mean that other licenses are wrong just because they don't meet 
your particular set of views or needs. 

> If I develop code, with my knowledge, my skill, my art, and my time
> and effort, I cannot see why I should not be able to license it under
> any terms I see fit.

And therefore why if I developed the piece of code that you want to modify 
shouldn't I be able to license it under any terms I see fit, including ones 
that restrict how you can distribute modifications?  Why do YOUR rights 
override MY rights to control MY piece of work and how YOU may modify it?

You may not like it, but tough, it's my decision.  I could have chosen not 
to release code in the first place, so the fact that you can modify it in 
way (even if you feel it's restrictive) is an added possibility that you may 
not have had in the first place if I hadn't chosen to release code under the 
GPL (or equivalent).
