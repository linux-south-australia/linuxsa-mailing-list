Tom, not sure about turning it off, but you will sve a lot of space if you
turn the compress option of logrotate on. See those .1 .2 etc files? With
compress on, logrotate gzips those files, which will reduce them by at least
half.

You might find an option in logrotate that suits your situation better than
the default, seeing as the files are large, you might want to rotate them at
shorter intervals or something.

There will be a logrotate.conf somewhere, probably under /etc. Some distros
have an /etc/logrotate.d/ directory with individual per-logfile configs.

Another question is, are you really expecting to get that much activity? Is
all the access legit?
