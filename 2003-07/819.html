I understand this cynical view of politics, I am also a cynic, but the 
fact remains, if you make an argument for doing something, it should 
actually achieve your aims - or somebody will rightfully point this out 
and use it against you. If you are in the minority, ie. you don't have 
much political sway - you will need to convince others to support you. 
If your argument is full of holes, I would suggest you would have less 
chance than if it wasn't.
Just because others make illogical arguments, doesn't mean it a good 
thing to do. The things you claim matter before logic help illogical 
things get through _despite_ the fact they are illogical not because 
they are illogical. Applying logic as well should only make those other 
reasons _easier_ to meet, not harder.
Play the game to your advantage - yes. Give your opponents unecessary 
ammunition to shoot you down with - no.
On top of all this, going by your examples, how is asking for open 
source anymore likely to succeed than asking for open-standards? At 
least by asking for open standards you aren't taking on those big money 
companies head on - they won't be forced to open their code just to be 
considered.
