> > Has anyone used the apple ipod with Linux?
> 
> I had a look at it when I first bought my iPod, but back then (18 months
> or so ago), the support for HFS+ (or whatever derivation of HFS the iPod
> is using) wasn't too flash.
> 
> That said, you can get Windows support for the iPod now, so actually
> accessing the thing wouldn't be such a task. The major issue that I
> think you'd encounter is talking to the "database" which lists all the
> music on the device.
> 
> From what I can recall, the database is proprietary, but I some guys
> have reverse engineered it and written a spec[1] for it.
> 
> GNUpod[2] is a project which has come up with a bunch of Perl that'll
> let you access the iPod from Linux, and, I suppose, any other OS that'll
> let you mount the iPod filesystem.
> 
> Possibly the most interesting project (to my mind, anyway) is the
> GTKpod[3] project. It's a full-blown GTK interface to managing songs on
> your iPod. It's quite crude when compared to iTunes, but then, most
> music management software is :-) I haven't looked, but I believe it'll
> run on FreeBSD, too. I'm not sure if that implies that FreeBSD has
> sufficient Firewire and HFS+ support (I've been out of the FreeBSD loop
> for a few months, now, unfortunately), but it's certainly interesting.
> 
> Finally, for something that has *enormous* possibilities, there's the
> Linux on iPod project[4], which is, as the name implies, a port of Linux
> to the iPod. The possibilities are endless.
>
