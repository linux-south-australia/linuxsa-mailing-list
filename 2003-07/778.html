Re: packet radio

On Mon, Jul 21, 2003 at 01:47:08PM +0930, Glen Turner wrote:
> 
> Go to an amateur radio meeting.  See the links page at
> http://www.wia.org.au/ for Adelaide groups.
> 
> This is better than asking in the mailing list as you'll need to drum
> up helpers as well as information.  The mailing list you need is
> linux-hams.
> 
> It's also a good time to talk with the ACA.  They have "band plans"
> that allocate spectrum and licensing conditions for various uses of
> radio.  You need to know what bands and licensing apply to your
> project.
> 
> These are likely to be stricter than you expect.  A 5W HF transmitter
> can cause world-wide interference, so use of the long-haul HF
> frequencies is very tightly controlled.
> 
> Your project also partly replicates the work of School of the Air and
> Royal Flying Doctor Service, so you should also chat with those
> organisations.  People won't regard as cheap an option that involves
> the purchase of a second HF transceiver.
> 
> Codan and Barrett make most of the HF transceivers used for SOTA and
> RFDS.  Their top-end kit offers a e-mail option.  So an alternative
> strategy might be to work with manufacturers to get e-mail as
> inexpensive option across their HF product range.
> 
> In the US, Volunteers in Technical Assistance have already got a
> field-deployable HF Internet solution, see "Low-Cost Digital and
> Packet Radio Techniques: Operation and Applications" which is a USTTI
> training course and a PACT book.  Try and find the person that wrote
> these -- you can't write either without learning a lot and making a
> lot of contacts.
> 
> It's not clear what fixed installation HF Internet is useful for in
> the long run if satelite costs keep dropping. You'll need an answer to
> that before you get too much further.

Thanks Glen

For a rough idea of what I am thinking about, please see:

people could bring this down to reasonable levels.
