RE: [OT] WD Hard Drive Failures?

Hi Ryan,

Like Michael, I'd reccommend looking at your power supply. Just buy a new
high quality one (Enermax tend to be around $100), and be done with it.
Also, check the temperature. You could monitor it with lmsensors, or buy a
digital thermometer and have the users occasionally check it to see if it
ever goes above (say) 50 degrees. Even without doing that, it may be worth
getting them to feel the case to see if it feels warm... The only other
things I can think of are water or even insect/rodent caused damage, or a
faulty cable. They sound unlikely though :)

Hmm... actually, re-reading your post, I can't quite tell if you're saying
that you've replaced the nth drive in a single machine in one month, or if
you've had n separate machines with similar WD drive failures. If they are
separate machines, then that's looking remarkably odd. Are they
geographically close? Likely to be using the same mains power circuit? Any
other machines nearby that haven't failed? Are they all the same model and
age of drive? Any other common components? Weird...
