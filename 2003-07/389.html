Japan govt considers Linux
Correspondents in Tokyo
JULY 10, 2003 
THE Japanese government is considering using Linux program when it
upgrades its computer data files for public servants in 2005.

Japan has chosen a proposal submitted by a group made up of Fujitsu, IBM
Japan and Oki Electric Industry. The companies suggest using Linux to
manage salary and other personnel data for the nation's 800,000 central
government employees, government official Masanobu Arao said.
