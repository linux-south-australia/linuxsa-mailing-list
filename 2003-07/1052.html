Re: Re: bsd- easier than you think

On Thu, Jul 24, 2003 at 06:38:13PM +0930, davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:

 > >> It's guarranteed "free-speech" permanently, even for derived works,
 > >
 > > The "even for derived works" bit is what grates.  A developer needs to
 > > make a choice between "compelled-speech" (which isn't free!) or no speech
 > > at all.
 > 
 > Rubbish!  You aren't compelled to derive your work from a GPLed origin.

You are if your aim is to enhance a GPL'ed software package. 

If you want to continue the "free speech" analogy:  Should a Government
be able to put you in jail if you criticize their policies, on the grounds
that nobody was compelling you to say bad things about The Party?

 > What you are excluded from doing is taking a GPL work,
 > enhancing it, and then redistributing it on different conditions than
 > the base work. 

Ok, let's look at that statement.

I can express exactly the same idea like this:

"If you take a GPL work and enhance it and intend to distribute it 
for the benefit of others, you are forced to license your own hard
work, blood, sweat and tears under identical license conditions to
the base work."

How is that not compelled speech?

Why am I not able to license my enhancements to a GPL'ed work under
any terms I like?

 > GPL forces you to do nothing at all. 

Yup, everyone would be in perfect compliance with the GPL if they 
never wrote any software at all.
