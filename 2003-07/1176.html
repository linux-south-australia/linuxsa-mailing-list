Re: Re: bsd- easier than you think

On Sun, Jul 27, 2003 at 11:21:34AM +0930, Matthew Moyle-Croft wrote:

 > > If I develop code, with my knowledge, my skill, my art, and my time
 > > and effort, I cannot see why I should not be able to license it under
 > > any terms I see fit.
 > 
 > And therefore why if I developed the piece of code that you want to modify 
 > shouldn't I be able to license it under any terms I see fit, including ones 
 > that restrict how you can distribute modifications?  Why do YOUR rights 
 > override MY rights to control MY piece of work and how YOU may modify it?
 
That's easy to answer:  We're suppose to be developing FREE software here.

The FSF's aim is to develop software which can be used by anyone and fixed
by anyone and extended by anyone;  It's pretty plain from this discussion
that they've failed in that aim, because there are types of applications
which will never be served by FSF software ("Pantone GIMP") and there are
groups of people who, for one reason or another, find that the GPL is just
as restrictive as a commercial software license when it comes to fixing
and extending software.

I don't mind if you license software in a way which insists that my
rights to do certain things with it are subordinated.  Just don't call
it "Free Software" when you do, because it obviously -isn't-, and
trying to convince the world that you believe strongly in free software
when you're pulling that kind of trick is hypocritical.
