Ugh.

I'd suggest to keep very bloody clear of wireless cards with non-standard'ish
chipsets in Linux.

Especially if the driver is binary only; but even if the source is available
(seems a lot of companies 'Linux drivers' are written poorly as hell, and
break in different ways with every new kernel release).

There's a crapload of wireless cards out there that have very, very poor
drivers, that dropout continually, have various networking issues, all sorts.
 I generally stick with either Orinico/Cisco/Prism cards, or nothing - other
chipsets tend to be worse than Telstra'a TinCanWan(tm).
