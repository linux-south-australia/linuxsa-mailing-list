Andrew,

> When changing my password - how does linux know if my new password is too
> similar to my old password? Surely it doesn't store it in clear text...

It doesn't store it in clear text. It most likely takes the password you've
entered, uses crypt or md5sum to encrypt it and then compares it against the
current password. It's most likely a horrid, mathematical probability algorithm.

You'd do well to read the source of the relevant pam modules that do this type
of thing.
