Acer to sell desktop PCs with Linux

By Online Staff
July 8 2003

Acer Computer Australia has started offering customers the option of having
Linux installed on the Veriton 3600, 5600 and 7600 series of commercial
desktops, a media release from the company says.

Acer marketing director Raymond Vardanega said products could be configured
online and bought from Acer's channel partners nationwide.

Acer is installing the latest version of RedHat Linux on these desktop
models. Vardanega said the PCs could be set up and delivered in five working
days.

Research firm Gartner has predicted that use of Linux on the desktop will
rise from seven percent to 14 percent in the space of two years.
