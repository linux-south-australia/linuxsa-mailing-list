Richard Russell <richard@yellowgoanna.com> wrote:
> DB9 doesn't automatically mean it's serial, just like DB25 doesn't
> automatically mean it's parallel.

See, that's interesting, because I would have said that DB25 doesn't
automatically mean it's serial.  I probably would be showing my age.

> All you need is at least 4 wires (I think -- I could easily be wrong there :)

All you need is two wires to send data in one direction.  A third wire
lets you send in both directions.  Handshaking (telling the other side to
stop transmission) requires another wire per direction.  You really should
wire up the frame ground.  If you want to be sure that the other end is
turned on (and ready), another wire per direction will give you that.
