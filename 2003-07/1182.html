Re: Is it really User Friendly?

Greg 'groggy' Lehey wrote:
> A lot of people who say "it's not user friendly" mean "it's not enough
> like Microsoft".
>
A wise statement that we (me included) should take to heart and 
memorise.

Many things in the recent developments of KDE etc we regard as User 
Friendly (UF) are in fact things that work like Windows does therefore 
they are familiar.  Familiarity does not preclude user friendliness but 
ascribing UF to items and processes just because we have met them before 
in a Windoze application does not mean they deserve a place in open 
source software.
