Looks like MS and the ISC have the government bluffed:

---------------------------------------------------------
Rann's man slams open source
John Sterlicchi
July 15, 2003

MIKE Rann's South Australian Government has moved to demonstrate there 
is little chance of it mandating open source software in state departments.

Governments around the world are being asked to vote on open source 
preference laws, which are viewed by opponents as anti-Windows and 
anti-Microsoft.

Microsoft lobbyists are labelling such laws as protectionist and anti-US 
and the timing of the latest brouhaha comes just before US and 
Australian negotiators are due to meet to discuss a free trade agreement.

A SA politician, Democrat MLC Ian Gilfillan, has flagged the State 
Supply (Procurement of Software) Amendment Bill, which calls on public 
authorities to use open source software in preference to proprietary 
products "where practicable".

But to make sure the upcoming discussions are not disrupted by claims of 
protectionism, Rann's Government wrote to US lobbyist, the Initiative 
for Software Choice, to say it did not support Gilfillan's Bill.
