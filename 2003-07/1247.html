Don't know how much of this will help, but this is my post from about a 
month ago:

> Important tips of the day:
>
> If you have a Promise TX2 ide controller, and you want to install SuSE
> 8.1 on a drive attached to it, I found the following steps useful:
>
> 1. Download the driver disk from the Promise web site (www.promise.com)
>
> 2. Configure your BIOS to use PnP for the IRQ assignments, DO NOT use
> manual IRQ assignments.
>
> 3. Use Grub, lilo had a cow.
>
> 4. On an SMP system, you must specify "noapic" as a kernel argument or
> you will get "ide_dma_lostirq" messages and the system won't boot
> properly.
>
> Hope this helps others not spend an entire day trying to get this to
> work.
