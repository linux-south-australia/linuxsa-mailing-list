Re: packet radio

On Mon, 2003-07-21 at 20:56, OfficeFix-iT wrote:

> VHF-UHF is a different story, there are many people ham and non ham using 
> packet radio over
> both UHF CB and amateur allocations. 

Just FYI, packet radio on UHF CB is illegal under the current class
license.  There is no provision within the current license to utilise
this mode on any of the UHF or HF CB frequencies.

There has been some discussion regarding the possibility of lobbying for
packet to be legalised on UHF channels 22 and 23, which, in late 2000,
were allocated by law to "telemetry and telecommand".  However the ACA
is very specific about what constitutes "telemetry and telecommand" and
packet radio does not fall within these categories.  


I quote from:

http://www.aca.gov.au/aca_home/publications/reports/info/cbrcl.htm

        "Uses not permitted
        
        Other types of data operation, such as Packet Radio, are not
        permitted on CB bands.
        
        Voice communications is not permitted on UHF CB channels 22 and
        23.
        
        Telemetry and telecommand is not permitted on any channel other
        than UHF CB channels 22 and 23."

Our website contains more information on the subject:

http://acbro.org/packetoncb.htm
