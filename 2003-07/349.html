It would be if you didn't log in as root.

When you mess up, or a program you are using messes up, and fills the disk
somehow (it's not that hard), then if the program filling the disk is not
running as root, it won't *really* fill the disk, and you can still log in
and do stuff as root to fix it all. Not to mention the advantages of not
running as root: eg if you accidentally type "rm -rf ~/.*", you won't end up
deleting everyone's home directories (which may include ftp and http, among
others)... There's a myriad of reasons why you should be using a normal
user. But it's your call.
