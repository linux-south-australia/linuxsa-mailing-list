I'll just say this:
The precautions you take in a bank, national mint, missile silo 
or ICBM command centre are of a different order
to what is suited to a suburban home office in Prospect.
I don't have the time to think every last scenario through and study every
text on security. I am paid 0 cents/hour for my security improvements,
compared with a big heap per hour for productive work.
I already spend more time on security than is economically justified.
A top computer security person at MI6 or the Pentagon will have
different attitudes to security to someone living in Prospect, SA
with an old 486 and a 33k modem.

All that said, I think I agree that .rhosts is much worse than I
had thought.
But I don't know if I would recommend to everyone to use triple-DES
crypto from one side of their living room to the other.
