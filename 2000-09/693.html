Re: ADSL review

Glen Turner wrote:

> Linux supports some ADSL native cards (ie: plug phone cable in to a
> PCI card).  The native cards are often better than using a modem --
> for one you can tell when link loss occurs.  Does Telstra offer a
> bare-wire ADSL service with the modem being customer-provided
> equipment?

When using the Telstra wholesale ISP ADSL service, it is up to the ISP
to provide CPE as specified in
http://www.telstra.com.au/adsl/docs/inter_op_cpe2.pdf.

AFAIK, the Alcatel unit was the only approved modem a month ago. There
are always lots of ADSL boxes including the Cisco ones for good prices
(a quick search shows nothing over $152US) on Ebay, but they won't yet
be approved for use on Telstra, assuming they're even legal for .au and
using the same standard.

A point of interest with the Telstra specification is that the modem
must be capable of 6Mbps, even though the service has a speed limit of
1.5M.
