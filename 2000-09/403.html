Re: rat was cracked!

There will always be security flaws in any distribution and/or package.
The main thing that allows these flaws to become serious problems are
either:

* ignorance
* lack of diligence
* simply not being aware (as opposed to ignorant, which I define as
being deliberately non-aware)
* human error

Those four things account for most security breaches...they are rather
constant in anything that human beings do or undertake. I'd say a
well-secured RH 5.2 system is much better than a sloppily put-together
OpenBSD system....

We have to realise that we are all system administrators on some scale.
The responsibility for our systems lies with us. If someone cracks into
our system it is OUR responsibility and OUR fault. I am certainly not
condoning crackers' actions but I don't believe we should abdicate our
responsibilities and obligations to ourselves either.
