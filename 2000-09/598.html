Suspicious phone number

Hi,

I use a chat script to connect to my ISP (part of the "pon" system
under debian), and for a while now, sometimes it fails, giving the
curious output:

Sep 13 13:11:30 localhost chat[20278]: expect (OK)
Sep 13 13:11:30 localhost chat[20278]: A^H ^HT^H ^HZ^H ^H^M^M
Sep 13 13:11:30 localhost chat[20278]: 28800^M
Sep 13 13:11:31 localhost chat[20278]: ^M
Sep 13 13:11:31 localhost chat[20278]: 000516413300****^M
Sep 13 13:11:42 localhost chat[20278]: ^M
Sep 13 13:11:42 localhost chat[20278]: BUSY
Sep 13 13:11:42 localhost chat[20278]:  -- failed

(Except that I've deleted the last four digits in the number above and
replaced them by asterisks.)

The number 000516413300.... looks very much like a phone number, but I
have no idea how it gets there.  It looks like perhaps this is stuff
_received_from_ the modem, rather than sent to it --- am I wrong?

I don't recognise the number, and don't particularly wish to dial it
to find out.  Could it be that someone has hacked my system and has a
botched attempt to dial out?  It doesn't make sense to me, and if the
number is stored somewhere on my system, I don't know where.

Any ideas?

Mark.
