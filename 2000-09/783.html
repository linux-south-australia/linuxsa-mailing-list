Re: slack admins in the UK

Glen Turner wrote:

> The other thing I want is for distros to boot into single user
> mode if the latest RPM/deb is over six months old.

Just threw this together as an exercise in self-masochism.  It's
a python script that interrogates the RPM database (so it's
limited to certain distros) for packages with build dates of more
than 'x' days ago (where x defaults to 182 days, or just under 1/2
a year).  I've only tested it under 6.1 (6.2 looks like it needs
rpmmodule.so installed, but I'm not sure from where as I don't have
a 6.2 CD handy).  The options are -q to reduce verbogosity, and
-o "days" for pakages older than "days" days.

    http://www.picknowl.com.au/homepages/dt/Python/outofdaterpm.py

To use it as Glen desires, you'd attack /etc/rc.d/rc.sysinit
and add something like:

======
/usr/sbin/outofdaterpm.py -q
if [ $? -eq 1 ]; then
    echo
    echo "Ack!  Your age is showing ... better do something about it."
    echo
    sulogin
    reboot
fi
======


Warning!  On my 6.1 system, 285 of 470 packages (over 60%) are
older than 365 days, let alone Glen's recommendation of 180
days.  If you're going to attack anyone's system, don't make
it mine.  :-)
