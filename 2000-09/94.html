Re: Echoing commands to modem

mark dickeson wrote:

> I must have brain-fade. I'm trying to echo a command to the modem by
> using:
> echo "atdt#33999#" > /dev/modem
> but it just hangs. Anone with any ideas?

Yes - sometimes you can't write (or open) a port if its control
signals aren't up (usually a modem with DCD set to follow
carrier, etc).  Time was when there use to be two devices per
serial port, one ttySX and the other cuaX, which would let you
open the port with/without honouring control signals.  These
days, I no longer know what they do to get around this (possibly
open the file in non-blocking mode, or something flamboyant with
termios; dunno, just a guess).

<Goes and has a quick 'net-surf, as my curiosity is piqued>.  Ah,
termios has an element in its data structure for setting control
flags, one of which is CLOCAL - ignore modem lines.  This won't
help you unless you write a script/program to take advantage of
this.
