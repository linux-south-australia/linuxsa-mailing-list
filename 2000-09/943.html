G'day Sarah,

Sarah Bolderoff wrote:

> I was after your personal opinion and the reasons for it 
> (if your so inclined)!

I like Xemacs, but I will agree that it took some time to set up 
to my liking.  

My personal opinion - (vi vs emacs) is that neither one of these 
editors are very easy for people to learn.  

For whatever reason, I found vi more unintuitive than emacs, but I
have a feeling that that's because I didn't INVEST the same amount
of time in learning to use it that I did with Emacs.  I definitely 
invested a fair amount of time in educating myself on it's use and 
feel I've only manged to learn enough to get me comfortable with 
the files I work with everyday. 

I have yet to find a truly amazingly easy to use (from the keyboard,
not with a mouse) feature rich (meaning it has typing macros) text
editor, but I'd certainly be willing to hear comments and opinions 
from others - maybe there's something else out there.  :-)
