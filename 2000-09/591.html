Re: failsafe bootloaders

On 13 Sep 2000, Paul Schulz wrote:
> One question is where to put the BOOT_SUCCESS flag:
> - In the lilo MBR
>   Is writing to the MBR a bad idea?
> - (I read somewhere that) there is some some space that can be written
>   to in the BIOS. 
> - Do watchdog cards come with any non-volatile RAM?

Perhaps a dedicated partition would be useful. The OS/2 Boot Manager and
other boot managers such as XOSL require this.
Given that you could then allow yourself more than the tiny MBR limit,
then you could create quite a potentially fault tollerant system for the
purposes of recording and even backing up information.
