Re: Linking 2 companies (was: Wireless LANS, and the Samsung MagicLan)

On Mon, Sep 25, 2000 at 02:44:34PM +1000, Michael Lambert wrote:

 > > This means you can link two offices of the same company together
 > > if they're
 > > less than 500 m apart;  you can link an employee to an office if they're
 > > less than 500 m apart too;  But you can't link offices from two separate
 > > companies together over any distance without a carrier licence, nor can
 > > you link an employee to their office if the distance is greater than 500m.
 > 
 > So does this mean (technically speaking) that if two companies were in the
 > same premises, and one company got a high-speed Internet connection they
 > could not on-sell bandwidth to the 2nd company without a licence?
 
No.  Internal fixed wiring is different.

However, the company onselling the high-speed connection would then be
classed as a carriage service provider under the Telecommunications Act,
which would mean they'd need to join the TIO, look after obligations under
the Broadcasting Services Amendment Act 1999, etc.

 > And, if this is true, what would happen if Company A got an Internet
 > connection, there was a join venture which required Internet access, but
 > which was operated under the banner of Company B? Would Company A need a
 > licence?

The "Immediate circle" exemption would probably come into play here for
as long as the joint venture continued.

 > And if a licence is needed in the first case, but not the second, could
 > Company B get bandwidth from the joint venture, which would ultimately
 > result in Company A receiving monetary compensation for the bandwidth
 > Company B used?

It'd probably be ok as long as the joint venture continued - consult a 
lawyer.
