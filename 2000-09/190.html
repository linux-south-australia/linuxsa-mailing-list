On Wednesday,  6 September 2000 at 11:06:33 +1030, Justin Hawkins wrote:
> On Wed, Sep 06, 2000 at 09:55:14AM +0930, Greg Lehey wrote:
>>
>> You can always make mistakes.  But it would be incorrect to blame dd
>> without some really hard evidence.  dd is so reliable because it's
>> low-level: unless the CD-ROM driver has problems reading the CD-ROM,
>> it will work.
>
> Some clarification: The CD was of BeOS Release 4.5 - perhaps it
> has something weird that confuses the driver? I do recall that
> I got kernel error messages at the end of the dd (end of media??)
> - I wasn't sure if this was normal or not when reading from the

It's possible that you ended up with a partial block at the end of the
medium.  But you can get past that with "conv=sync".  But it would
surprise me if a missing partial block at the end of the CD would
result in a bad copy.

> The CD-ROM drive in question was also quite old (2x?) so that may
> have been a factor as well.

That seems more likely.

> As I said originally I don't take this to mean this method does not

> manner if the opportunity presented itself again :-)

I'd certainly wait until I had an image which dd didn't complain
about.
