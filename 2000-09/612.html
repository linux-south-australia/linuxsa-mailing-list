Re: Suspicious phone number

Russell, Richard (DEH) [rrussell@deh.sa.gov.au] wrote:

> you could always do a `find / -type f -exec grep 00516413300xxxx {}` to see
> if it's anywhere else ... takes a while, but you'd fine every ocurence of it
> unless it's encoded somewhere...

I think I tried searching for it a while ago without success, but I've
just had another go, and again, the only place the number comes up is
a few times in log files (eg ppp.log).

As you say, it could be encoded.

The curious thing is that it appears while waiting for an "OK" from
the modem.  It's as if it's hoping for an okay, and instead the modem
spews out stuff like:

A^H ^HT^H ^HZ^H ^H^M^M
28800^M
^M

and then the phone number:

000516413300****^M

Could it be that there is some buffer within the modem which hasn't
been flushed properly on disconnect, and contains this stuff?

I notice the following "tcflush" error appearing in the ppp.log file:

Sep  8 11:12:52 localhost pppd[32181]: Connection terminated.
Sep  8 11:12:52 localhost pppd[32181]: Connect time 19.6 minutes.
Sep  8 11:12:52 localhost pppd[32181]: Sent 10752 bytes, received 37166 bytes.
Sep  8 11:12:52 localhost pppd[32181]: tcflush failed: Invalid argument
Sep  8 11:12:53 localhost pppd[32181]: Exit.

It occurs at the end of every ppp connection.  I suspect this doesn't
have anything to do with the mystery number, as the error occurs after
every connection, and the phone number only appears occasionally.  But
I don't have much to go on right now, so I thought it was worth a
mention.

My other thought is that the number may somehow be stored on the modem
itself.  Don't modems have some form of persistent memory?  That is,
you can store various settings and things.  Can you store numbers too?

Cheers,

Mark.
