>I would suggest it depends on circumstance... vi is great for making lots of
>small changes to many different files, as a sysadmin may do, emacs is great

>has a generally nimbler "feel", emacs is large, slower, and some would say
>clunky (emacs stands for "escape meta alt control shift"), but it has more
>features than Linux (some people do almost everything in Emacs -- read mail,
>program, compile, debug, etc etc etc...) Really, it ends up being a
