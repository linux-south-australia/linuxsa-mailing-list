Re: 56k dial in server

>If your looking at setting up less than 20 lines your best bet is say
"Sorry
>guys, 33.6 is all you get", otherwise it might pay to go for a bigger
>solution.

It doesn't have to be that way any more, there are a number of systems now
that work on BRI-ISDN, which is almost affordable ($39/month/2-channels)
with the advent of Home/Business Highway.

At the low end, there are modems (ie: courier i-modem) that can plug into
ISDN and be used for dialling up at 56K, then stuff like the Digi Datafire
RAS4 BST PCI card (for 8 56K dialups), and the Ascend Max 3000 that's
recently appeared to bridge the gap between BRI and PRI access servers.

If you do see a need for 20-30 lines or more, then you might need to
consider a more proprietary alternative to linux.

You're still going to need reasonably deep pockets though, you might find a
VISP system (renting ports off of an existing ISP) a better solution to
start out with.
