Re: sshd unreliable

> SSH not 100% reliable!!

	Sigh.

> Once again, I find that "sshd" can just lock up and
> refuse to accept connections. Here are the symptoms on
> the client side when I try to use "scp":
> 
> ===========================================
> Could not create pipes: Too many open files
> lost connection
> ===========================================

	There are a set number of file descriptors _per-system_.

	If some other process is opening a lot of files, then SSH may fail
	after opening only a handful of files.

	(Ran a test: 8 processes opening 256 fds each on a redhat 6.1 box
	is enough to fill the file descriptor table. Binaries won't even run 
	because the dynamic linker uses the open(2) syscall at runtime. 
	Complete resource exhaustion).
	
> I tried killing off child processes of the "sshd" process.
> But that didn't help.

	What other processes are you running on the box? What is the operating
	system, and what is the kernel optimised to do?

> If you rely on "ssh" as your only way to log in to a machine,
> then you risk losing all contact.

	Ditto for telnet. Ditto for rsh, rlogin, deslogin, whatever.

> The OpenSSH software is clearly not yet at the level of reliability
> that you would want to place too much trust in it.

	Tsk, tsk. You just haven't installed the "infinite system resources"
	patch on your kernel, now, have you? 	:)

> While checking this all out, the main sshd process crashed but left
> a few child processes (one of which I am using to write this note).
> But none of these child processes reponds to new login attempts.

	No, the child processes would be servicing other connections, I 
	suspect.

> So much for removing all services!!!

	Sigh.
