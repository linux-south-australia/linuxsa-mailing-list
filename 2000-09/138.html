On Tue, 05 Sep 2000, Mark Newton wrote:
> On Tue, Sep 05, 2000 at 12:14:04AM +0930, Alan Kennington wrote:
>  > For access between machines _within_ your network,
>  > use the .rhosts file to specify which hosts/users
>  > are trusted.
>  > The reason for this is to make sure that your
>  > passwords don't appear on the local network even.
>  > Then use "rcp" and "rlogin" (or "rsh") without
>  > passwords instead of ftp and telnet.
>
> That's an ASTOUNDINGLY bad idea.  The security problems with rsh,
> rlogin and rcp have been well documented for about a decade now;  If
> you're making extensive use of .rhosts and/or hosts.equiv files, it's
> reasonably certain that a script kiddy will be able to dig up a perl
> script which will break root on your system within a few seconds.
>
> The rsh/rlogin protocol is so badly designed that it's wide-open
> to an address spoofing attack.  Don't go there, just turn the
> fucker off.
>
> Use ssh.  Don't use telnet, comment the rshd and rlogind crap out of
> your inetd.conf, accept no substitute, make no compromise:  Strong
> crypto on the wire is the only way to go.
