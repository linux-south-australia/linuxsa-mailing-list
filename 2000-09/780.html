Re: Freesco Firewall

Stephen Donaldson wrote:

> Ahhh, to clarify, the FAQ's recommend NetBEUI or IPX for internal networks
> because win98 will use TCP out to the net.

There are two ways to stop this.

1)  Don't use masquerading, but instead use application proxies to get
access to the net (ie, squid for http/https/ftp, sendmail+imap for email,
etc).

2)  Block the ports you think are going to cause specific problems on
the internal network.  I always block port 137-139, both TCP and UDP,
from seeing the light of day outside of the internal network (there's
another port too, but I can never remember that one without looking
it up).
