Hi Daryl,

Save yourself the pain and set it to your location
rather than using the POSIX UTC offset form of the
variable.

eg: In your equivalent of ~/.bash_profile say
  TZ='Australia/Sydney'
  export TZ

You can also do this to see what the time is anywhere in the
world:

  TZ=`tzselect` date

The tzselect utility is an interactive utility that queries
the timezone database for locations and prints the location
you select.
