> Like I said, yes, winmodems _do_ work, but they add extra overhead to
> your CPU that normal modems do not.  With a winmodem the digital signal
> processing (DSP) part is done by your CPU instead of a dedicated CPU
> with specific add-multiply machine-instructions.  It just means your
> expensive Intel/AMD processor is doing the work that a $50 DSP chip
> could be doing instead.  If if your CPU is modem stuff, it has less time
> to do everything else.  Granted, your 800Mhz PIII in your laptop is
> probably twiddling it's thumbs most of the time right now, but in 3
> years time when Windows3000 or Linux2.8 comes out you'll be wanting
> every once of CPU to stall off another upgrade/purchase.
> 
> Yes you can get them to work (if they're Lucent-based, which fortunately
> most are) but you have a performance hit.  Not saying you shouldn't buy
> one, but that it should be considered when you weigh up all the features
> of your laptop prior to purchase.
>
