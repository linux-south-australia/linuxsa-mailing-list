Re: Boot from floppy

Evan!

> 1. man indicates supply of kernel version number, how do i find this?

Take a look in /lib/modules. It's one of the "directory names"
there....for example....

[lloy0076] ls /lib/modules
2.2.14-5

I do mkbootdisk 2.2.14-5

If you have multiple versions there, try correlating the names there
with the version you may find under /usr/src/ or by doing rpm -qa | grep
kernel (if you're on an RPM based system).

> 2. will this mount all my paritions as the normal boot process?

It *should*. You could make your bootdisk, then reboot to see what it
does. Make sure, of course that your computer looks at its floppy for
boot first. I'd advise you to do this whatever method you decide to make
your bootdisk.
