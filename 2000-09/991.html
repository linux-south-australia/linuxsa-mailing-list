Re: Vi or Emacs?

nickm@bhwb.nsw.gov.au wrote:
> 
> I've always been a vim person.  But, I've never really tried Emacs.

I've used both.  I'd say that emacs has the edge for the people
that program for a living.  Most professional programmers usually
end up knowning half a dozen editors, a dozen languages, and far
too much about the effects of shoddy management.

> Does anyone have a good learn-the-basics-of-emacs-fast link?

There's a fine tutorial within emacs.  In XEmacs, select Help,
then Tutorials, then English.  On a text terminal press Ctrl+h i,
cursor down to "Emacs" and press Enter, cursor down to "Fundemental
Editing Commands -- Basic" and press Enter.
