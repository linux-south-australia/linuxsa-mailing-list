RE: laptops and RedHat

Hi Michael,

The winmodem in the Toshiba 4300 series is based on the Lucent Technologies
chipset. Fortunately they have distributed a binary only kernel module which
can be located at http://www.linmodems.org/ . I had no problems installing
from the distribution. It was compiled for RH 6.1 and reports unresolved
symbol errors on loading, but it still works very nicely.

Being a command-line person from way back, I use wvdial rather than
something like kppp or similar. A collegue of mine does use the RedHat
X-client dialer, also with great success.

> I've bought a DELL Inspiron 5000 recently, with Win98 preinstalled and now
> running RedHat 6.2 (dual-boot).  I even used FIPS to shrink my
> FAT partition
> successfully, first time.  I do not believe there is any issue
> having NT instead
> and having that work with Linux/RH6.2 (perhaps disk partition
> resizing though?)
>
> You should check out the Linux Laptops page at
> http://www.cs.utexas.edu/users/kharker/linux-laptop/ which gives
> step-by-step
> guides for many, many laptops.
>
> The two areas of biggest concern is modems and X windows.
>
> Make sure that if you purchase any laptop with an internal modem
> that it isn't a
> winmodem.  Yes, it's more than likely you can get it working, but
> at sub-optimal
> performance (since your CPU is doing the DSP stuff).  I bought a
> Xircom 56k
> modem / 10-100mbit combo PCMCIA (Type-III) card which works well
> for me with the
> supplied open-source driver that comes with RH6.2 (actually pcmcia 3.18.20
> (??)).
>
> X is another area of concern - make sure that XF86 3.3.6 supports
> the chipset in
> your laptop.  XF4.0 probably won't (yet), but I was able to use
> the standard
> XF86 that came with Red Hat 6.2 (3.3.6) albeit with the XF86Config file
> specified in one of the howtos listed on the web site I mentioned.
>
> Summary - only buy a laptop which someone else has installed
> Linux on and you
> have the step by step instructions, or an offer from them to help
> you out.  Much
> easier that way.  You could go and get a Dell I7.5K, which is RH
> certified, but
> at a huge premium - it isn't a cheap laptop.
>
> Having said that, I love my Dell I5K.  I now sleep less now that
> I've got it.
>
> All IMHO, your experiences may differ, proceed with caution...
