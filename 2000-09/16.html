Re: ssl an ftp

Adam Hawes wrote:
> 
> > Why on earth would someone SSL ftp is beyond me, surely user/pwd is better
> > security than protecting someone sniffing the packets as they are transmitted.
> 
> Well, if the FTP server contains files of a sensitive nature (company
> secrets) that MUST be available over the internet (for company employees
> who travel a lot) then SSL is probably a good idea.  Username and
> password keeps the bad guys out of the server, and SSL stops the bad
> guys snooping the data when a good guy is getting it.

Another approach is to PGP encrypt the files and place them on a
standard anonymous FTP server.  We do this for distributing Cisco
router experimental images.  You are welcome to download them, but
I'm afraid that I won't give you a key :-)
