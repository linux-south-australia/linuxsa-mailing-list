Re: postfix to listen on a different port to 25

One assumes this is not actually a product to make your system more
secure per se, but rather to intercept viruses spread by email to protect 
the unwary from nasty email transmitted viruses such as "I love you",
"Melissa" etc.

In which case whatever it is that listens on port 25 is in effect a proxy 
MTA, which presumably scans incoming email for viruses,( hence the term
"virus scanner" :-) ) and then passes any email it thinks is unaffected on
to the real MTA.

However one assumes that you could also reject any packets on port
whatever (eg 5000) that don't come from a local address or local host.

Then all email connection would ahve to pass through the virus scanner.

Then your port scan would not do much at all - at least not in regards to 
finding any SMTP exploits which are not in the proxy listening on 25.
