Re: Multiple NICs ???

At 04:22 PM 9/23/00 +0930, Richard Watkins wrote:
>Hi All,
>
>I have an old 486DX-33/32Mb RAM/850Mb HDD box that I would
>like to set up as a packet filtering firewall (maybe proxy
>later).
>
>It has RH6.2 installed following the suggested method written
>in "Securing and Optimizing Linux", and all lines in the file
>"/etc/inetd.conf" have been commented out.
>
>The firewall is intended to stop naughty people accessing the
>local LAN from the Internet (dial-up access).
>
>Many articles keep talking about having two network cards in
>a firewall, but I think they are assuming that we all have
>permanent Internet access with cable modems (I can only wish).

Well, more generically, they mean two interfaces. One can be Ethernet, the
other ppp.

>So my question is do I need two NICs?

No, just two interfaces.

>If "yes" then can someone explain what I need the second one
>for, otherwise do I set up "ppp0" somehow with ifconfig?

Use netcfg or the dialup too to set up ppp to automatically dial when your
machine comes up.

Then set up rules that control what can come in on the ppp interface (ppp0).
