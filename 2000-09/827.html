Richard,

What are you basing that 1 mS number on?
Ping output? Or are you looking at /proc/net/tcp or something?
The RTT is, of course, the total round trip time, including
processing time from TCP layer back to TCP layer.

I.e. Does the TCP layer think the RTT is 1 mS?
I don't see any RTT in /proc/net/tcp.
So how do you know that the TCP layer's internal RTT
estimate is 1 mS?

Ping goes a darn sight faster than most other protocols
obivously. So it's not a good measure of RTT - only the
physical propagation time + lowest level IP processing.

That's why I was suggesting that you use some native TCP.
E.g. 

http://www.topology.org/src/ipp/tcptx1.c
http://www.topology.org/src/ipp/tcprx1.c

But I'm sure you've got zillions of your own
such minimal TCP programs.


Cheers,
Alan Kennington.

no windowing problem?
