Perhaps a mention of OpenSSH might be valuable as well? It's licencing is
probably more suitable to linux users (normal ssh is not free for commercial
use). It may also require more attention as it can be slightly more
difficult to install on linux than normal SSH is. Some will be comforted
know that it's been coded with the same paranoia as openBSD, and remains
compatable with all ssh clients and servers.

In fact, the only thing you'll miss from the standard ssh is speed
approximations in scp.
