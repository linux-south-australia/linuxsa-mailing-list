scp over ethernet with compression - make a cup of tea!

SSH users,

Just to confirm that "ssh" with compression _is_ slow,
here are some results for "scp" (from OpenSSH) over
ethernet from a 66 MHz AMD and a 233 MHz Toshiba laptop.

Summary: bit rate = 563 kbits/sec = 5.63% of full bandwidth.
(Yes I know that you need a factor of about 0.88 here
to take into account ftp/tcp/ip overheads.)

======================================================

Sep 21 20:10:11 tiger sshd[13198]: log: Connection from 203.38.148.51 port 13500
Sep 21 20:10:11 tiger sshd[13198]: debug: Client protocol version 1.5; client software version OpenSSH-1.2
Sep 21 20:10:11 tiger sshd[13198]: debug: Sent 768 bit public key and 1024 bit host key.

That's 14.8 MB in about 3.5 minutes, or about 563 kbits/sec.
But really only half of that was sent, due to compression.
So real speed is about 281 kbits/sec on an idle 10 Mbps ethernet.
