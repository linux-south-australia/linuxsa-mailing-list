> Reasoning/explanation can be part of checklist can't it?

Hell yeah, but the purpose of the two documents is different, hence the
reasoning/explanation in a checklist should be simple, like:

33: turn off telnetd -- it's insecure, so use ssh instead
(http://some.reference.if.really.neccessary.com/)

Perhaps it would be a good idea to refer to the relevant section of the
security howto (maybe enven make a checklist an addition to the howto)...
the point of a checklist is so that inexperienced people can do enough to
make their box mostly secure, and check that a box is pretty much secured,
without having to wade through explanations of why it is so -- these people
_trust_ the writers of the checklist to give them useful, mostly correct
advice, so they don't have to analyse every decision themselves...
