David Lloyd wrote:

> Hello!
>
> I've just reviewed Robyn Manning's firewall, which she has built from
> the Freesco Firewall (http://www.freesco.org/). I concur with its web
> pages and can say that:
>
> * it's easy to setup
> * it runs with a very small foot print
> * it appears to be secure
>
> Whilst I did not, nor will I have the resources to do a full security
> audit of Robyn's system, it does appear to be relatively secure and
> block services that you do not want to advertise to the world. Here's
> how it works:
>
> * it defaults, essentially, to a "DENY EVERYTHING" policy
> * through a series of screens. you answer a number of questions about
> what you want to ALLOW...commentary is included with some of the
> questions
> * after you have answered the questions you are presented with an
> overview, "advanced" screen where you can "fine tune" what your question
> and answer session answered
>
> This is all done in text. You can administer it by telnet (icck), web
> browser (icck) or at the actual console. It understands DHCP, but it
> does appear to be based on an older kernel that uses 2.0.38 - I say
> "appear" because ipfwadm looks like it's on Robyn's disk.
>
> I would recomment this particular product for someone who is concerned
> with security, who is on a dialup account but who doesn't particularly
> want to delve into the intricacies of the ipchains syntax immediately.
> Whilst a business or larger home network may choose to use a full
> distribution for this important service - simply because you then have
> the utmost control of your firewall and router - you will buy yourself a
> certain amount of protection using this product.
>
> I suspect that a home user on a relatively non-permanent connection
> would be able to deter crackers, who would more likely turn to either
> easier or more interesting targets...
