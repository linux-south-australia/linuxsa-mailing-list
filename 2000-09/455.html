1 msec timer in linux kernel

Question about 1 msec resolution timers.

--------------------------------------------------
My apologies for continually asking so many
questions, but....

I really need a 1 msec or better resolution timer
in the linux kernel.
Is there such a thing?

I know there's a not-very-real-time 10 msec timer,
but that just isn't good enough.
In my application, that would correspond to
about 350 kBytes of data.

I'm aware that the Intel/AMD chips have a sort of
1 usec clock which can be read in about 170 nsec
to know what the time is, but I really need to
have a wake-up (preferably a hardware interrupt)
every 1 msec to service buffers etc. etc.

So far, I haven't found anything suitable in documentation
or source files.

Thanks in advance for any help on this.

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.
