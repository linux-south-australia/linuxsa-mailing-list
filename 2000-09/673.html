Re: samba client for backing up to an NT box

Andrew,

It is an excellent question you ask, and very relevant to helping free
software move to the centre of computer rooms. Heterogeneous backup onto
Linux servers is one of the few largely-unexplored areas.

On Thu, 14 Sep 2000, Andrew Hill wrote:

> David Lloyd wrote:
> > 2)
> > Ask an NT list about third party Windows backup software.
> 
> But I want software that will backup Windows and Linux, and I want it to
> run on Linux. Surely Linux is the overriding factor here?

An example of a product that claims to do what you want is Arkeia,
http://www.arkeia.com/platforms.html. One of the harder things is making
sure that the registry is backed up correctly for NT machines. There are
alternatives (do a google search) but as far as I know not many yet.

I don't know of any free software of release quality that does what you
are looking for.

Don't be fooled into thinking Legato Networker will do the job, they only
do a Linux client. Legato has a pretty iffy reputation for restorability
too (corrupted indicies etc).
