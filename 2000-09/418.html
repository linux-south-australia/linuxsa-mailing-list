Evan, I think you make a very good point here- (yeah I kno not enuf but...).


Not everyone is a 'security guru' but alas these concerns are slowly being
forced on us due to the 'dark side' that awaits at the other end of the
dialup/cable/adsl/isdn connection.
While I am certainly not a guru on the subject, my job requires me to take a
lot more than a passing interest in the subject. As you could imagine, we do
get the odd scan in this business <very major understatment>.
Even if the average user on this list only has one or two boxes that are
connected at_any_time to the net then how far should you go with security?

ANYTHING IS BETTER THAN NOTHING....and for those who didn't hear it the
first time......ANYTHING IS BETTER THAN NOTHING

Most attacks are from pain-in-the-port script kiddies who just want to see
if they can hack/crash someone's box. Its the net's answer to vandals with
backward baseball caps and spraycans. They generally aren't interested in
your mp3/porn/recipes-from-mum/nuclear-bomb-plans/emails/research/study/etc.
They also don't care that it has taken you many hours to get all that stuff
together.

Go and buy a 486SX25 (monitor and HDD optional). This amazingly cheap
scenario will allow you to run something like www.freesco.org . What will
this do? Firewalling, NAT, web/print server, diald etc all from a floppy if
necessary.
Although there are a lot of single disk router projects out there, THIS ONE
IS EASY to set up for a novice.
OK, so we wouldn't hide our internet casino behind it but it will mean that
you aren't the easiest target in the pond anymore.

Maybe one of the people that currently use freesco or something similar
could give a demo at a meeting. It will open the eyes of the
'its-all-too-hard-I-don't-understand-about-ports-and-ipchains' people and
that includes almost any newbie and a lot of the older hands. I'd offer to
do it but someone'll have to pay for the airfare down from Alice <grin>.

Now that we all have some kind of condom on our connections, did anyone
remember to backup that data we just mentioned?

>If you start from a deny everything perspective, then allow back in the
