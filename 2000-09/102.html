RE: Web Client Port Allocation

At 10:38 PM 9/4/00 +0930, David de Groot wrote:
>
>Monday, September 04, 2000, 7:05:52 PM, David wrote:
>
>> Personally I think that IANA's allocation of ports above 1024 is rather
>> stupid.I might not want socks (1080) or X (default to 6000 plus) or
>> other stuff. But, they might be the ports http decides it wants to talk
>> to....which I've blocked....which I don't want to....
>
>And why are you blocking those ports?
>If you haven't got any listeners on those ports, then nothing is going
>to respond to an attack on them.

Not quite true. Paranoia rules :-)

The IP datagrams have to make their way up the stack to the TCP layer, and
be defragmented, etc. They may trip a bug in the TCP stack and DOS you. The
Land attack and many others did that.

It is far better to block all packets on ports you don't accept with a
filter. They are thus dropped at a low layer of the TCP stack where they
are less likely to do you much damage.

>And as Daryl mentioned, you're blocking source ports rather than
>destination ports...

Yes, this speaks of un-knowledge.
