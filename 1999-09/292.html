Re: Linux Console Security

On Mon, Sep 13, 1999 at 05:31:13PM +0930, Phil Pittard wrote:

> OK..... I have contemplated the various comments made & one comment in
> particular has swayed me. (That comment being along the lines that IF
> SysAdmin's are aware that an insecure linux box can easily be hacked by
> someone at the keyboard  , then perhaps they will view the "physical
> security" of their linux boxes as being VERY important.
> 
> Step 1:
> Reboot PC

Firstly there is very little you can do to stop this, BUT disabling the reset switch (when 
was the last time you really used it on a production box?) and requiring a logged in user 
for Cntl-Alt-Del (see man shutdown) to work MAY be a start.

> Step3:
> type this
> linux init=/bin/sh

If you look at man lilo you will see a very simple way to defeat this. Parameter passwords,
 you can require a password when ever a kernel parameter is entered. This stops the init
 trick you described. 

At this point you have secured the OS to the point that someone has to remove the Hard drive
 to get access to your system and we get back to physical security.

Hope this dispels a least part of the myth that console access (which isn't always the same thing as physical access) is inherently insecure.
