Re: Internal modem 'HOW DO I FIND IT'

Hi .... a quick mod to /etc/rc.d/serial  (or rc.serial or whatever
depending on the distro) should fix your problem.

Just go to the manual section of /etc/rc.d/serial or /etc/rc.d/rc.serial
(cd to /etc/rc.d and then look for a file that has the word serial in it
;)) and edit the first four lines (of the manual configuration section)
to reflect the ports/addresses/irq's of your serial ports, including
your modem. If your modem is on COM4 & IRQ3 then set /dev/ttyS3 with
irq3

Save the file then run /etc/rc.d/serial start  (or  /etc/rc.d/rc.serial)
and it will show the comports, including, if you got it right, your
modem!

If you want you can "link" /dev/modem to /dev/ttyS3  OR just use the
various options in kppp or wvdial etc etc , to set the port (/dev/ttyS3
for example) that your modem is on.

All the above is totally irrelevant if you have a winmodem. When using
linux, winmodems are mainly used to stub out cigarettes or rest coffee
cups on! You can however always sell them to a poor unfortunate windoze
user!
