Re: scsi vs ide (off-topic) and recompiling/redhat's problems

I have a Sony 24xR/4xW/2xRW IDE with 1MB buffer. I use it exclusively under
Win98, as I haven't had time (or the need) to set it up under Linux. It
works fine. I only have trouble if I try doing anything else while it's
burning. This is partly an OS issue, but partly an IDE issue. IDE uses the
CPU a lot. SCSI doesn't. When you are only using one device at a time, and
not doing much else while a burn is being done, then this is not much of an
issue. However, if you have multiple devices (CD reader, CD writer, 2 HDs
etc) and you are likely to be doing a few things at once, then this is where
SCSI excels. You can maintain the high transfer rates on multiple devices,
without putting much load on your CPU. IDE can get very high burst transfer
rates, but they can't sustain this, and suffer when mulitple devices are in
use.

Having said all that, the most important thing when it comes to CD-Rs is the
buffer size. A large buffer can compensate for an inconsistent transfer
rate. Make sure you get at least 1MB, and remember that the effective size
of the buffer is inversely proportional to the recording speed. ie a 1MB
buffer will last for ~7 seconds at 1x, ~3 seconds at 2x, and <2seconds at
4x.

HTH
