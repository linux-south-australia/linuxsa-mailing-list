Re: Nat32 (Linux Version)

I had a bit of a look at their web site, and there doesn't appear to be a 
windows client, let alone a linux one.
Which makes sense, you don't need one :-)

Network Address Translation (NAT) works on the server, changing the source 
address of the IP packets from the client PC's on the way out, and the 
destination address on the way back in. It should be totally transparent to 
the client. It does need to do a little trickery for protocols like FTP, 
but any good NAT implimentation (including the linux MASQ one) will do it.
