Re: Internal Modems

Mark Williams wrote:

 > > 2) If an internal modem isn't working it's normally the internal modem
 > > at fault.
 > 
 > Why???
 > An internal ISA modem even though they are cheaper SHOULD be easier to get
 > working... because there is one less link in the chain you have to worry
 > about (the motherboards com port). If there is no physical problem with the
 > internal modem (which 98% of the time there isnt) its obviously a software
 > problem or a problem with the the set up of resources (ports and irqs).

Look, I don't intend to debate this, I'm just going to tell you that
you're pushing it uphill if you intend to make a case that internal
modems are "better" than external modems by almost any definition of
"better" that doesn't involve real-estate.

Whether they *SHOULD* be easier to get going or not, take it from me
and from the others on this list who have been making their career out
of computers and communications for many years:  Internal modems suck
donkeys' balls.

You may have a modem which works correctly, but the presence of one
example does not prove a general rule:  You're just lucky.

 > Dont give poeple the wrong idea... internal ISA modems are as good as any
 > external modem.

Palpably, horrifyingly, disastrously wrong.
