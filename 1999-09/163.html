> On Tue, Sep 07, 1999 at 08:01:03PM +0930, Andreja Zivkovic wrote:
> 
> > Firstly, from this list I beleive I read that
> > SCSI is faster and more reliable than IDE, and IDE burners should be
> > avoided. I recently asked a huy at a computer shop, and he said that the IDE
> > burner's technology has improved, and is now better than the SCSI burners.
> > Can someone tell me (or tell me where to read) about the difference between
> > SCSI and IDE, and which type of burner I should get? I know of the price
> > difference, but to me speed and reliability (of the drive and the media
> > produced) is more important than initial cost.
> 
> Most hardware people say SCSI is better. Period. Often you can't actually get
> more than that out of them. <grin> It's more expensive, but it's generally
> faster and places less load on the system (with a good SCSI card),
> and you don't run into slowdowns with having more than one disk on each
> channel like you do with IDE, as far as I understand it. IDE's only
> advantantages are that it's cheap and doesn't need an extra controller
> nowadays that it's on the motherboard.
> 
> As a hard drive related aside, I've seen claims that the newer UDMA based IDE
> drives are getting close to equaling SCSI for raw speed, but having just
> installed a new LVD drive and Adaptec controller on a server at work, I'm very
> very very impressed at its speed. It feels a hell of a lot faster than the
> older SCSI drives we have, although I haven't done any tests. 
> 
> Bottom line is that if your system is unloaded and reasonably fast, it
> shouldn't make a difference which you pick. If, on the other hand, you do load
> it down a bit while you want to do burns, I'd say it's safer to go SCSI; it'll
> be more likely to survive.
