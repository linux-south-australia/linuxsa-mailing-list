Re: Warm boot from Linux to Windows

> It seems to me that a warm boot into WinBlows is not possible (at the
> present) as  Linux must be shut down properly, and Linux does not know how
> to chain to another program when it halts.

This answer is appropriate for SCO Unix, or for Solaris, or NetWare, or any
other operating system which provides neither the facility to do warm boots
nor source code.  This answer is quite inappropriate for Linux.

Some unixes do warm boot, for example BSD/OS, so I gather it is possible.
There may be difficulties, and in particular a warm boot may not reset
peripherals quite as well as a cold boot (and in turn there's nothing as
good as a power cycle for resetting equipment.)

Obviously it is left as an exercise to the reader to decide how to modify
Linux to warm boot instead of cold boot.  Elsewhere a suggestion was made
(INT 19?) and I suggest that is one place.  I note, by the way, that BSD/OS
achieves a reboot by configuring the keyboard controller to send a reset
ctrl-alt-del.  Mysteries of PC architecture?
