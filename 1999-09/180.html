It is worth pointing out that this can also be done during an installation
of at least RH, TurboLinux and Linux Mandrake.  I will find out about SuSE
and Caldera soon.

During the stage where you partition your disk, you can select what gets
mounted where.  Simply decide what structure you want, and go for it.

However, it is worth thinking about what you are trying to do. Having had
many years of experience with partitioning on other versions of UNIX, and
recently having been bitten[1], I can think of a few situations where you
might want to separate some parts of the file system out to separate
partitions, but in general do not think it is worth it.

[1] Recently was working on a system which had separate /, /usr, /tmp,
/var, /home, on an 8GB disk. The disk was also storing backups from other
systems. There was plenty of space on the disk, but it was scattered over
all those damn partitions, and our backups failed.  The machine now has two
8.4GB disks, with only swap, /boot and / on the first, and the second is
all one partition.

As a bonus point, why did I use /boot?
