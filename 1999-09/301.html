know the password :-)

In any case, linux init=/bin/ash or something similar is pretty effective,
as all you want to do is bypass the SysVInit stuff that asks you for a
password.

This is also an effective way of getting back into your system under some
circumstances.

The only way to secure any machine is to keep it under lock and key. Anyone
who can get to the console with a screwdriver can remove the disk and get
into the system that way!
