On Wed, 15 Sep 1999, Net Media Solutions wrote:
> 128-bit encryption (block encryption, mind you, we're not talking
> public/private key encryption here) will take longer to crack than the sun
> will take to finish super-nova'ing , even given
>
>> the DES cracker built by EFF, "Deep Crack," ...  can crack a 56 bit DES
>> key in a few hours.
>
> ...And 128-bits will "only" therefore take 4700000000000000000000 hours
> (give or take) :)

Very succinctly argued: "a few hours" * 2 ^ (128 - 56).  And you allowed
that "a few hours" equals only one hour.  A mere 5 billion billion years.

For those impressed by PGP's multi-kilobit keys, you must understand that
the public key is only used to encrypt a session key, which is randomly
chosen, much shorter (sorry, don't know or care how long) than the public
key, encrypted by the public key and then transmitted with your cypher.
Your cleartext is encrypted with the session key, not with the public key.
