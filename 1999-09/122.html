Re: sendmail.cw and sendmail.cR

>>>>> "SaiGKrish" == SaiGKrish  <s0g6620@cs.tamu.edu> writes:

    SaiGKrish> What is the difference between sendmail.cw and
    SaiGKrish> sendmail.cR ?

sendmail.cw defines alternate local mail addresses for the host.
sendmail.cR defines hosts that are allowed to relay through the current
host (ie non-local addresses that the host will accept mail from).

I am not sure why putting a host in sendmail.cw allows you to relay from
this host, but I am pretty sure that things will break if you have mail
comping back the other way.  All hosts in sendmail.cw will be treated as
local addresses, and sendmail will likely attempt to deliver them
locally.
 
[...]

    SaiGKrish> Am using sendmail 8.8.8

I believe that $=R (sendmail.cR) may have been introduced in sendmail
8.9.x (not completely sure)?  You may wish to upgrade to sendmail 8.9.x
anyway, and use the access database, as this is more flexible (in my
opinion).
