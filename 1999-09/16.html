I gotta admit, there are benefits to both, i like to have linux on a
seperate machine because i can play around with networking etc, my linux box
is also my dial in machine, runs the cdburner and will soon be running
samba, so i can share pronters with the mac here.....if linux is on a
seperate machine you can play to your heart content in the knowledge if you
really mess things up, you still have a working machine that you can get on
the net with and fix it!!

Be radial, do both, network cards are cheap and easy to set up :)
