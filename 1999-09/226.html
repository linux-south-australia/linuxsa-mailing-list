>	Some of our dialup users when staying in Motels have to use pulse
>dialing to dial up the server.. They report poor performance and low access
>speeds. Does anyone know the technical detail behind this if so I would love

I am often dialing to an ISP from a hotel, and have stayed in Hotels around
Australia (Sydney, Brisbane, Canberra, Perth, Melbourne).  I have never had
to use pulse dialing, and have typically got 28.8 or 33.6 speeds.

However, since you mention Motels, and as I assume you are referring to
Motels in country regions around SA, I assume the problem is old style
PABXs in these motels and poor couontry exchanges.  The pulse dialing, I
assume, is simply because of the age of the motel's PABX.  And the quality
of those country lines and exchanges is, I assume, not as good as what we
have here in Adelaide or other capitals, so the modems cannot achieve high
speed.
