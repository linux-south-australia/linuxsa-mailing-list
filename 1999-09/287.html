Basically any box that is ultimately as physically secure as it's security.
A box that is out in the open can be assumed to be insecure for most 
purposes.

To find out what the 'professional' security people tend to do, have a look
at the bugtraq archives.  Visit http://www.securityfocus.com/ and follow the
links to Forum/bugtraq/archives.  

The reason that bugtraq is full disclosure is that most security issues, 
once the vendor has had reasonable time to fix a security hole, to have the
problem and solutions out in the open allow similar problems to be fixed.

Security through obscurity (or letting latent security problems go unfixed)
is not generally good practice.  

But then again, it is a personal choice, and in the paranoid world of computer
security some things are better left undisclosed :).
