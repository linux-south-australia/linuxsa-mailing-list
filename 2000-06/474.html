> Hi.
>
> Reading freshmeat the other day, I fell over buildkernel and I have to
say, it
> was (last night) the most painless kernel upgrade that I have done.
>
> buildkernel has a couple of command-line switches, but the simplest for
most
> would be:
>
>    buildkernel NEWESTSTABLE
>
> Which goes off and checks (what is the latest 'stable' kernel?), downoads
the
> source from kernel.org if it isn't already on the system, configures,
builds
> and installs the kernel with pauses at the appropriate places for you to
> modify/check the settings.
>
> On my system, there were 2 minor problems with the build:
>
> 1. the directory name for the modules directory was correct, but the
system was
> looking for a directory with a space at the end of the name:
>
> depmod: depmod: Can't open /lib/modules/2.2.16bk1 /modules.dep for writing
>
> Now that might have been my doing during the configuration (it WAS late).
I
> solved it with a symlink in the /lib/modules directory.
>
> 2. the lilo.conf part of the process did not transport my
append="mem=256M"
> onto the entry for the new kernel. This was not a show stopper.
>
> So, with buildkernel, and a local repository for the 2.2.16 kernel source
> accessible over the ethernet, all your new systems could have the latest
kernel
> applied, and the new users experience a relatively painless kernel build,
> repeatable at home with the addition of a ~14mb kernel source download.
>
> fwiw
>
> Michael
