>     Sometime ago there was talk here about 'problems' with ATX power
> supplies and their usage with servers.
> Specifically, after a power outage they need a kick in the butt to get them
> started again.
> ...
> Anyway, if someone here can tell me exactly what is required for this I'd
> greatly appreciate it.

All ATX systems I have worked with have had a BIOS entry that allows you
to configure this. So that when power is returned to a system, it will
boot up immediately, rather than wait for 'secondary' power switch to be
pressed. This will depend on the BIOS of a given machine. It is normally
found under a BIOS menu called something like "Power Management".
You can access a BIOS menu just after you power on a machine, by pressing
a key such as DEL, F10 or F1. This key is normally displayed on the
screen.
