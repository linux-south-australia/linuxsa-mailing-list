Re: linux for mips chips

Having just posted that it is dead, someone points out this site for
pulling apart the N64: http://www.cs.earlham.edu/~odo/N64/n64.html.

If this strikes anyone as a strange thing to do then read Alan Cox's
"Space Aliens Ate My Mouse" paper on porting Linux to the 68k Macintosh.

If you think it is a pointless thing for grown-ups to do then reflect a
little on the history of Russell King, who was busy porting Linux to a
hopelessly outdated little RISC machine called the Archimedes that just
happened to have design features well ahead of its time - to find that his
port to the ARM chip suddenly took on great significance when the core ARM
technology started being licensed to major European and US companies and
manufactured in volume. Now he sits in the UK hacking on the kernel all
day.
