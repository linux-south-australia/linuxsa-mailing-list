Russell, Richard (DEH) [rrussell@deh.sa.gov.au] wrote:

> When will people learn that image is by definition superficial and
> irrelevant to the real substance? Whether we are talking religion or
> operating systems, it doesn't matter...

I agree that substance is more important than image, but that does not
render image unimportant.  Indeed often image is important because it
can influence people, thus leading to substance.

Image is a form of communication and transference of ideas.  I can
understand the Christian school's stance against the devil mascot.  It
conveys the idea that the concept of the devil is a lighthearted idea
not to be taken seriously.  Christians take the devil seriously and so
do not wish to trivialize him, as the mascot does.

Of course this poses a dilemma for Christians wanting to use something
like BSD, and Christians will handle it in different ways.  But I can
understand a Christian school, wanting to portray a clear message,
wishing to steer clear of this mascot.  Especially when there is a
linux alternative with the non-offensive penguin mascot.

Cheers,

Mark.
