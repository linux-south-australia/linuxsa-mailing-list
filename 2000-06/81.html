On Sun, Jun 04, 2000 at 02:34:16PM +0930, Stephen M Doherty wrote:

 > While were on the topic of crossover cables here
 > can someone tell me the proper wiring for these cables? (or point me
 > to a website)

Ok, start with the industry-standard color coding (yes, you'll need to
remember it):

   pin  conductor

    1.  White/Green
    2.  Green
    3.  White/Orange
    4.  Blue
    5.  White/Blue
    6.  Orange
    7.  White/Brown
    8.  Brown

For a start, remembering that sequence will mean that all the cables you
make are industry-standard, so someone else coming along and looking at
one isolated end of one of your cables will be reasonably confident that
he knows that the pairs are correct.

Even better, though, is wiring for crossovers if you remember the sequence:
Wherever you see "Green" above, substitute "Orange," and vice-versa.  So
a crossover is:

        A-end                      B-end
   pin    conductor              conductor
    1.  White/Green              White/Orange
    2.  Green                    Orange
    3.  White/Orange             White/Green
    4.  Blue                     Blue
    5.  White/Blue               White/Blue
    6.  Orange                   Green
    7.  White/Brown              White/Brown
    8.  Brown                    Brown
