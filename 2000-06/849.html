I'm fairly sure that the Athlon does have support for multiprocessors at 
the CPU level, however there are no motherboard chipsets that support 
multiple Athlons. This is a great pity, especially since AMD would have the 
majority of the x86 scientific computing market if it just had dual 
processor support, since its floating point performance is so much better 
than the Intel alternative.
