Re: Treeware magazines: what do people recommend for linux interests?

Matthew Geddes wrote:
> Ian Hogben wrote:
> > I'm interested in treeware magazines that have a useful focus on linux. 
[snip]
> 
> Linux Journal (essential)
> Linux Magazine (quite good to read)
> Linux Format (UK - PC Format for Linux type thing)
> 
> Sys Admin (Unix admin stuff. Good for a linux admin, as many articles
> are specific to Linux).
> 
> They are all around $12-$13 in newsagencies.

I'd agree with these.  I regularly buy both Linux Journal and Linux Magazine
(the latter has regular articles by our local ex-SouthAussie Paul "Rusty"
Russell on security/networking).  

The point I wanted to make is that Linux Journal is well worth subscribing to -
the price I paid when the Aussie dollar was reasonable was like a third of the
newsstand price.  (Even now, it'd be less than half).  And you receive it 8
weeks earlier than the newsstand!  The content is excellent of both Linux
Journal and (slightly less so) Linux Magazine.

To me Linux Format seems a bit too much like a newbie magazine.  Another in this
category is Maximum Linux from Canada.  I bought their first issue last month
and it wasn't too bad, and it included Manduck 7 and Storm Linux 'rain' on two
CDs.
