Alan Kennington wrote:
> 
> You'd think there would be some International Standard
> Cabling Institute which has a web site for explaining all

Crossover cables are documented in Figures 14-20 and 14-21
of IEEE 802.3-1998.  An international standard, no less.

It points out that you may need to terminate the unused
pairs into an isolation transformer.  The implication being
that unterminated crossover cables have nowhere near the
reliable drive length of standard 100BaseTX.  They should
only be used for short hauls, such as interconnecting
switches or computers that are in the same room.
