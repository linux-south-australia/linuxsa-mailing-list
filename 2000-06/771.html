Re: Sendmail filtering

Matthew Geddes wrote:
> 
> Hi,
> 
> Whilst we have few Outlook clients ;-), I am still looking at filtering
> messages based on the file extension of any attachments within the
> message. Does anyone know of a relatively simple way of achieving this?
> It should be possible when ActiveState's perl module for sendmail comes
> out. I also think procmail would be capable, but the man pages didn't
> give enough information.
> 

Have a look at inflex, it's just a shell script based on metamail and
the scanner of your choice. It's not the most effiecient system ever,
but for low to medium message loads its not too bad.

Essentially you run a modified sendmail.cf which delivers all mail to a
local inflex delivery agent. The inflex script then checks the message,
by name, virus scanner if you have one, and "file" magic file
identification, tho I have this turned off. If the message passes
muster, its reforwarded through sendmail using your normal sendmail.cf.
If it doesn't notification is sent to the sender, recipient, and
sysadmin.

If you use a scanner and recieve several (5+) messages in very quick
succession it can slow your mailserver down quite a bit :-).
