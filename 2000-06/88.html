Re: TV Card

Hi Robyn,

I have a 'Spirit Viper' car, based on the Brooktree Chip..
The best bet is to compile the module that came with you kernel.
We can only get 3 channels due to reception problems (2,7,9) but
it works well.  Is you video card PCI?

[I don't have any experience with having the tuner card in a PCI slot
and
the Video card in an AGP slot... the video is usually displayed using
DMA,
and I thought that the PCI and AGP (a form of PCI) slots were put on
two separate buses.  Can anyone enlighten me on this?]

My system has the following....
> cat /proc/pci
 Bus  0, device  11, function  0:
    Multimedia video controller: Brooktree Bt848 (rev 18).
      Medium devsel.  Fast back-to-back capable.  IRQ 11.  Master
Capable.  Latency=64.  Min Gnt=16.Max Lat=40.
      Prefetchable 32 bit memory at 0xfb9ff000 [0xfb9ff008].
  Bus  0, device  20, function  0:
    VGA compatible controller: Silicon Integrated Systems 5597/5598 VGA
(rev 101).
      Medium devsel.
      Prefetchable 32 bit memory at 0xffc00000 [0xffc00008].
      Non-prefetchable 32 bit memory at 0xffff0000 [0xffff0000].
      I/O at 0xffffff80 [0xffffff81].

with the following modules..
> cat  /proc/modules
bttv                   33688   1 (autoclean)
tuner                   1996   1
i2c                     3264   2 (autoclean) [bttv tuner]
videodev                2400   2 (autoclean) [bttv]

I needed to add the following line to /etc/conf.modules
(or /etc/modules.conf depending on your distribution)

> cat /etc/modules.conf

pre-install bttv /sbin/insmod /lib/modules/2.2.12-20/misc/tuner.o
alias char-major-83 tuner
alias char-major-81 bttv
