Re: PPP, Dynamic IP, and an IPCHAINS script

  Jake> also, you might like to look at the scripts that bring the
  Jake> interfaces up and down. that is, the scripts is
  Jake> /etc/sysconfig/network-scripts/

  Jake> ifup calls ifup-ppp, ifup-ppp execute pppd, then ifup calls
  Jake> ifup-post.

also.. (from man pppd)
pppd calls /etc/ppp/ip-up with the following parameters..
  tty-device
  speed
  local-IP-address
  remote-IP-address
  ipparam

Redhat does an interesting job of forcing ppp into it network
interface scripts (as above).  It's much more straight forward in the
Debian (and Linux Router Project) distribution for those who want to
get down and dirty with pppd.
