On Tuesday 06 August 2002 14:04, James Leone wrote:
> A copy of what I posted on the Lindows.com Board:
>
> First, I want to say that I am not "for" or "against" AOL being in
> Linux. This is just information.
>
> I give all of the credit to Tina Gasperson, who posted her article at
> Newsforge on April 19, 2002.
>
> AOL 5 Works fairly well, it will "just die" once in a while, and I
> haven't checked out all of the features within it yet. I just did this
> about an hour ago.
>
> Anyway, I found this article at NewsForge:
>
> http://newsforge.com/newsforge/02/04/19/0053224.shtml?tid=5
>
> I then found a blue AOL CD labeled "America Online's X-tra 7.0 1000
> Hours Free! For 45 Days. BTW - I have no problem making a copy for
> Lindows.com, or maybe a few people to "spread the burden."
>
> When I mounted the drive, I noticed it had a variety of subdirectories
> labeled AOL30, AOL40, AOL50, AOL50B, AOL50NT, AOL60, AOL70 and AOLTECH.
>
> Seeing that I had AOL 5 in the AOL50 directory, the next thing I did was
> subscribe to Wine X ($5 a month), and I got their RPM file and installed
> Winex.
>
> After that, I went to a Windows 98 Machine and obtained a copy of
> WSOCK32.DLL, and copied it into the /home/james/.transgaming/c_drive
> directory.
>
> I then insterted the blue AOL CDROM drive and went into the shell.
>
> I typed mount /media/cdrom (This is SuSE, others may use /mnt/cdrom)
>
> I then typed the following at the shell:
>
> cd /media/cdrom
>
> cd aol50
>
> winex SETUP32.EXE
>
> After a momentary pause, but some hard drive action, a dialogue box
> comes up and asks you if you are a current member or not.
>
> EVEN THOUGH I WAS NOT A MEMBER, I CHOSE CURRENT MEMBER, AND CLICKED ON
> NEXT - SAVES A LOT OF TIME!!
>
> Next I said CREATE AN ADDITIONAL ACCOUNT FOR THIS COMPUTER.
>
> The install went fairly fast, and AOL was even nice enough to give me an
> icon that starts AOL. It also put a small AOL symbol on the bottom right
> of my KDE desktop.
>
> The rest is a normal AOL install, so I won't go into too much detail.
>
> James Leone
