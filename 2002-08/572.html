davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:
> 
> John Edwards <isplist@adam.com.au> wrote:
> > An interesting problem, but you'll notice that all current domains use
> > hostnames for NS records, so the IP address isn't directly related to
> > delegation.
> 
> What about domains that contain their own authorative name server?

A dynamic DNS system should have some sort of 'glue' records that point
to the nameserver (based again on a dynamic update). This is already
what happens in today's static DNS system, which allows domains to host
their own DNS servers.

Robust is again the keyword, and there might be scalability problems
with having TLD servers contacted by thousands of servers daily for
updates. It might become more practical to let your ISP's DNS servers be
the delegated placeholder for your domain, and they act as slaves to
your Master DNS.
