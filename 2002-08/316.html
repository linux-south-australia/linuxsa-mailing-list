It's probably time to ask yourself why you got into an rpm battle in the
first place. I suggest it is because you haven't installed an 'rpm battle
manager', ie apt. Most users simply don't want to know about these
details, and I find that programs are much better at managing package
dependencies than most people. Not all people, but most.

And in case anyone wants to propose urpmi, here's an example of why I
don't think it is mature enough yet to compete with apt: how do I roll
back a distribution upgrade? Answer: you can't. urpmi has made great
strides recently though.

And once again: no matter how good the tools, if the dependencies in each
package haven't been carefully constructed it will all fall in a heap.
