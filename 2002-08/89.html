Re: 2.4.19 question

On Mon, 5 Aug 2002, Brian Marr wrote:

> In the changelog of the new 2.4.19 kernel I came across the following and 
   :
> "The HP merge is now down to 3937 lines pending"

Almost certainly to do with HP's 64-bit PA-RISC, which is now mostly
integrated into standard kernels. I hope so, because I've got one and my
employer has another. The HPPA architecture is now in Linus 2.5 and
Marcello 2.4 kernels, and was released with Woody, so it can be called
mainstream now I guess. HP-UX is fast and stable and 64-bit too, but not
very nice to drive compared to a modern fast-moving free Unix. There is a
lot of it around though. Back on to PA-RISC Linux, see
http://www.parisc-linux.org for more info.
