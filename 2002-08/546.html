Re: serial laser printer

You may be lucky enough to find a bank of DIP switches on the printer
somewhere with a legend telling you how they affect the serial port
settings. Or if your printer has an LCD display and set of buttons there
may be an option hidden in it for displaying/changing the serial port
settings.

Otherwise, a lot of serial devices seem to want no parity, 8 bit data, 1
stop bit: try these settings and ring the changes on the baud rate till
something works!

Also try google searching for lists of connectors, pinouts etc. in case
you need to make up a cable! There are lots of them and they mostly tell
you a fair bit about RS232 in the process.

Pigeon
