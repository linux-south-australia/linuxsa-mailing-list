Re: Slow boot up with Debian Linux

Quoting alcaron@senet.com.au:

> The machine won't even touch the network until its initialised its network 
> interfaces, which won't happen until the rc scripts are read, which won't 
> happen until the filesystems have been checked. A different scenario applies
> for network boot machines, of course.

Sorry, a correction:

The boot process will usually go:

o Mount the root filesystem read-only
o Run init
o Read single-user rc scripts (runlevel 1)
o fsck the drives
o Remount root as read/write, mount other filesystems
o Read multi-user/network rc scripts (runlevel 2 & 3, sometimes also 4 & 5)

However, this doesn't change the fact that the system probably isn't going to 
touch the network until after the filesystems have been checked.
