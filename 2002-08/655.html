Re: device files

Searching google for "linux sr0 dev minor major mknod" turns up in the
first link: (despite being non-english, the example still shows you how
and can be understood from the examples)

cer@nimrodel:~> l /dev/sr?
brw-r-----    1 root     disk      11,   0 Sep 24  2001 /dev/sr0
brw-r-----    1 root     disk      11,   1 Sep 24  2001 /dev/sr1
brw-r-----    1 root     disk      11,   2 Sep 24  2001 /dev/sr2
brw-r-----    1 root     disk      11,   3 Sep 24  2001 /dev/sr3
brw-r-----    1 root     disk      11,   4 Sep 24  2001 /dev/sr4

Se crean con mknod (detalles en el man), pero puedes ver que se trata de
dispositivos de bloque (la b de los atributos), major 11, minor 0 y 1.
O sea:

mknod /dev/sr0 b 11 0
mknod /dev/sr1 b 11 1

Solo te queda apuntar los enlaces. En mi caso, son:
lrwxrwxrwx    1 root     root            8 Dec  5  2001 /dev/cdrecorder
-> /dev/sr1
lrwxrwxrwx    1 root     root            9 Dec  5  2001 /dev/cdrom ->
/dev/scd0
lrwxrwxrwx    1 root     root            9 Mar  9 13:55 /dev/dvd ->
/dev/scd0
