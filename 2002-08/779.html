tty snooping programms are great from a sysadmin/helpdesk point of view.

I used to administer a SCO system that ran the stock management/accounting
software for a company. All the users ineratected with the sytem via tty
sessions. That made diagnosing and fixing their problems so much easier
since I could bring up their tty and watch what they were doing while they
explained their problem to me and if they got totally lost or stuck, I
could can take over their tty and get their session back on track for
them.
It sure is more usefull to fix a problem having access to their tty than
the usual user response over the telephone of: "It doesn't work for some
reason. Fix it NOW!" :)
