Don't forget the 'First Allocation Fee' of $2500 :-)

My uneducated opinion is that, unless you're really anal about this
stuff, take your IP addresses where you can.

Right now it's cool to have a class C. It's a finite chunk of a finite
resource. It has some 'name' type value. IPv4 addresses can, with
effort, be memorized.

But when IPv6 comes around, this name value drops to near 0:

	2A20:0000:0000:0000:C13D:2120:0000:A180

              or even

	2A20::C13D:2120:0:A180

Hardly 'off the top of your head' material is it?

So even more reliance falls to DNS. Which is good, since that's what
it's for.

With so many addresses available, I'm sure that it won't be hard to get
yourself a subnet for low or no cost. It might not be as permanent as
buying a 'real' chunk, but there it is.

You can do this right now: http://www.freenet6.net/
