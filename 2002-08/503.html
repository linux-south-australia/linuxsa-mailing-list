AOL & Linux Caveats

Here is a message I posted in the Linuxforum in Compuserve.

http://go.compuserve.com/linuxforum

It appears, and I may be wrong, that customers with more expensive a
Compuserve "classic" account will be able to use compuserve in Linux.
However, compuserve does not advertise this AFAIK. In fact, it appears
that compuserve is tyring to get people to switch from the classic
accounts to the Compuserve 2000 accounts. It also seems that Compuserve
is being closed down in the UK, leaving some Linux users there out to
dry. I don't know about other plavces.

This concerns me, especially since I spend a significant amount of my
free time doing various acrobatics to help Linux desktop users have the
same options as Windblowz users do.

So, it angers me to see that the home page for compuserve appears to be
another dead end for Linux users, when in fact, a mechanism exists for
Linux to use Compuserve as a way to get Internet Access.

Why not offer these classic accounts to linux users?
