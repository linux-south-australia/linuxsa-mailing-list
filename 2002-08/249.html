[ANNOUNCE] LinuxSA August 2002 Meeting

Hi all,

Time for the LinuxSA August 2002 Meeting...

Here are the usual details:

  When:   7:00pm (doors open 6:45pm) on
          Tuesday, 20th August, 2002
  Where:  2nd Floor
          Compaq Computer
          139 Frome Street
          Adelaide SA
  Cost:   FREE
  Who:    Anyone and everyone.
          No pre-registration necessary.

Presentation:

  Sean Burford will be giving an introductory talk on Reverse
  Engineering Linux Binaries:

  Reverse Engineering of Linux Binaries is considered somewhat of a
  black art.  This talk aims to demystify the process and tools that
  are used in reverse engineering.  Focussing on the recent HoneyNet
  Reverse Challenge ( http://project.honeynet.org/reverse/ ),
  attendees will be introduced to the process of reverse engineering
  and documenting Linux binaries for which source code is not
  available.

  Outline:
    What is reverse engineering?
    About the Honeynet Reverse challenge.
    Overview of a reverse engineering methodology.
    Introduction to the ELF file structure.
    Methodology
      Determining your objective.
      Taking notes.
      Basic information gathering.
      Importance of program structure.
      Importance of data structure.
      Disassembly.
      Decompilation.
      Tracing and live debugging.
      Network protocol interpretation.
    Reverse Engineering Tools.
    The Future.
      Advances in reverse engineering.
      Advances in binary protection.
    Questions.

Extras:

  As per usual, if you are having a problem with your Linux
  installation, feel free to bring your machine, but please warn
  organisers@linuxsa.org.au beforehand.
