No, it is probably because Microsoft provides "ipconfig /all" with those
Operating Systems.

You may be able to check your speed and duplex by right clicking network
neighbourhood and going into the properties, but it is probably set to
Auto.  Intel make a tool available for download for their NICs that
tells you the actual speed and duplex the card is running at.

I am unsure of how to check this under Linux.

Under Solaris, you can use ndd to check and set the speed and duplex of
network interfaces and you can store your settings in /etc/system.
