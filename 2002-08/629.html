Re: serial laser printer

michael wrote:

> Have you got a serial break-out box? What may be happening is that your
> cable is set up as DTE and the printer is actually DCE. I've forgotten
> what the standard cable is, but if the interface is ok, and you cannot
> talk with it then this is probably the problem.

Along related lines, quite often these older printers need their control
lines raised high before they will send/receive data correctly (especially
if they use hardware flow control).  For starters, if it is a DTE device
(as they typically are), look at tying CTS (pin 5), DSR (6) and DCD (8)
to DTR (pin 20).
