Re: Can the LGPL have extra conditions "tacked on"?

On Thu, 29 Aug 2002, Martin Sandiford wrote:

> From reading the web site, my understanding of this is that the clean
> folks are offering to:
> 
> 1) License clean to you under the LGPL if you are not a commercial
>    entity.
> 
> 2) License clean to you under another undisclosed license if you are a
>    commercial entity.
> 
> I don't believe that there is anything wrong with this - they are free
> to license their source code under whatever conditions they desire to
> whomever they choose.

If you are correct, then I think they are in the wrong according to
clause 8 of the LGPL ("incompatible sublicensing"). Alternatively, even if
somehow they can get away with such a clause, they cannot impose it on
anyone else, which means that some other website certainly offer Clean
under the LGPL with no other restrictions or addenda.

See http://www.fsf.org/licenses/lgpl.html and look at

It might also help to think of it this way: anything published under the
LGPL may be converted to the GPL in a one-time conversion (clause 3 of the
LGPL.) The GPL is well-enough understood that most people can probably see
that limiting users to non-commercial entities is simply not possible. So
I cannot see any way to make this condition stick even if it is allowed in
the first place, which I doubt.
