[ANNOUNCEMENT]: AUUG-SA August Chapter Meeting

  The South Australian chapter of the Australian Unix User Group (AUUG)
formally invites you to attend thier monthly meeting. Details are as
follows:

DATE : 14th August 2002
TIME : 7pm Onwards
VENUE : IBM Corporate Division Boardroom
180 Greenhill Road
PARKSIDE
AGENDA:

* 1. Geoffrey D. Bennett will be talking about hacking the Tivo

Geoffrey is a prominent computing consultant with Netcraft Australia. 
One of his many projects involves working on the Tivo: A video recorder 
based on a computer running Linux. Geoffrey will be talking about what 
exactly the Tivo is and how the Tivo has been 'hacked' to support 
additional functionality such as Australian PAL support, video over LAN 
and much much more.

* 2. The BIG PGP Key swapping session

Following the presentation there will be a PGP key swapping session. In 
this session you can provide your pgp finger print to the group who can 
then verify your identity and possibly sign your key. Thereby creating a 
web of trust. If you wish to get your pgp key signed, simply email your 
pgp key id to cisbjc@cs.unisa.edu.au and be sure to bring identification 
and a copy of your public key that you have made.
If you haven't already created a PGP key you can use the following 
process to create one and upload it to a key server:

1> Install gnu privacy guard ( http://www.gnupg.org )
2> Create a public/private key pair: gpg --gen-key
3> Create a revoation key in case you ever need to revoke your key: gpg 
--gen-revoke yourid > revoke.key
4> Export your secret key for backup: gpg --export-secret-keys yourid > 
secret.key
5> Export your public key for backup: gpg --export yourid > pub.key
6> Now backup: revoke.key secret.key pub.key (don't encrypt these with pgp!)
7> Send your public key to a keyserver: gpg --send-keys yourid 
--keyserver pgp.mit.edu (You can use any key server).
8> Send yourid to me so I can grab your key off a keyserver and list it 
for printing.
You can list all the id's using gpg --list-keys
