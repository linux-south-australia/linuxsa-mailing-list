[NEWS] Linux is in the Aussie News again

Linux is in the news in Australia.

"Telstra will explore using the open source Linux OS as part of a new 
standard operating platform being scoped by the telecommunications 
giant as it looks to reduce its $1.5 billion annual IT budget. Telstra 
Retail CIO John Pittard said that while Telstra was not using Linux at 
present, it was considering the OS in its wide ranging IT review that 
will dictate the future direction of operating platforms supporting 
Telstra’s 45,000 desktops."

http://www.itnews.com.au/story.cfm?ID=10596

"Air New Zealand, Centrelink and the Department of Veteran Affairs are 
among a number of high-profile customers that have recently adopted the 
Unix derivative in areas where Microsoft products would traditionally 
have been used. "

"Air New Zealand is to replace 150 Compaq servers with a single IBM 
mainframe and replace 4000 Microsoft Exchange email and file and print 
clients with Linux and open-source email application Bynari. "
