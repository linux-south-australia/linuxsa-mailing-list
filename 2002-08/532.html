I too have been hosting several domains on a Linux box via 512/128K Business
Class DSL with Internode for about 2 months now and it's been very reliable.

I don't work for Internode either :)  But I do highly recommend their
business class ADSL product.  It represents good value for money ($100
p/month) and without the PPPoE hassles it seems very stable.  There is no
usage charge for upstream traffic which is ideal for hosting!

If you need a backup DNS server for your ADSL hosted server and don't have
any "mates" willing to do backup DNS, you can always use:

http://soa.granitecanyon.com/

For an indication of the "real world" speed of stuff hosted behind the
512/128K connection, have a look at:

www.sonicresolutions.com

It's not a big site but it will give you a rough idea I guess (and hopefully
I won't be sending a 2MB e-mail at the time you visit) :)
