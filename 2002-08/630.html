> michael wrote:

> >Hi.
> >
> >I wasted a bit of time on this, so am posting a solution in the hope that
> >others may benefit.
> >
> >The setup is:
> >
> >hda: RedHat
> >hdb: WinXP (don't ask)
> >
> >WinXP won't boot on the slave disk by default, it needs a little help with
> >some smoke and mirrors in the drive mapping within grub:
> >
> >/boot/grub/grub.conf:
> >
> ># grub.conf generated by anaconda
> >#
> ># Note that you do not have to rerun grub after making changes to this
> >file
> ># NOTICE:  You have a /boot partition.  This means that
> >#          all kernel and initrd paths are relative to /boot/, eg.
> >#          root (hd0,0)
> >#          kernel /vmlinuz-version ro root=/dev/hda3
> >#          initrd /initrd-version.img
> >#boot=/dev/hda
> >default=0
> >timeout=10
> >splashimage=(hd0,0)/grub/splash.xpm.gz
> >title Red Hat Linux (2.4.18-5)
> >        root (hd0,0)
> >        kernel /vmlinuz-2.4.18-5 ro root=/dev/hda3
> >        initrd /initrd-2.4.18-5.img
> >title WinXP
> >        map (hd0) (hd1)
> >        map (hd1) (hd0)
> >        rootnoverify (hd0,0)
> >        makeactive
> >        chainloader (hd1)+1
> >
> >WinXP changed the mbr on hda during install (and offerred to install on
> >the second disk, even though it was incapable of continuing past the first
> >reboot -there were 11 in all, some were because of this issue) I landed up
> >installing hdb as primary master temporarily for the install to continue.
> >
> >After XP was up, I had to switch the disks and boot from the RedHat
> >install CD, re-install grub to mbr and configure it to boot both os's
> >
