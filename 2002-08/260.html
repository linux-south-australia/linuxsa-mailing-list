But only from your point of view; besides everyone knows that I have a
slightly different point of view than everyone else around here.

I use LaTeX a lot and if you understand (La)TeX it's quite usual to
stack boxes into boxes to achieve certain results especially to ensure
that what is in the inner box is a certain length or height. For
example, if you want a 15 cm line you make a box that is 15 cms, put a
\vline into it and place the box into the document where you want it.

It's not much of a leap to correlate that if one wants to get a 640x480
screen stretched to 1024x768, simply place it in a suitably sized box.
Hence, the solution displays the following properties:

1) The hardware sees 1024x768
 - and displays full screen

2) Counterstrike sees 640x480
 - and is in the inner box

3) You stretch the inner box to the size of the outer box

...and done.
