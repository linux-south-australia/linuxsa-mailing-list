Re: more spooky stuff on the net!

Alan Kennington wrote:

 > It's clearly this wierd socket which is caching
 > IP packets.

I'm always amused when mathematicians use the word "clearly" when 
talking about things they're still learning about. :-)

Sockets don't cache anything.  The socket concerned isn't wierd.  The
socket never carries IP packets, so it's hard to see how it could be
caching them even if it could cache data, which, as we've already
established, it can't.  nscd should be documented on your system.  The
operation of the name service switch, the resolver, the name server,
nscd, and the communication mechanisms they use between each other all
have publically available source code which will answer your questions
as exhaustively as you care to probe.

At the end of the day I'll lay dollars to doughnuts that we're looking
at a stale cache entry in your personal nameserver cache, which would
go a good way towards explaining why nobody else who has spoken to you
about this is able to duplicate what you're seeing.  Restart your 
nameservers (which will clear their caches) and enjoy life.
