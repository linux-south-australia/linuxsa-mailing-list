Re: Running the CDRW as SCSI

On Fri, 12 Nov 1999, lloy0076 wrote:

<SNIP>
> 1) I compiled SCSI support and IDE as SCSI support into my kernel
> 2) I added to conf.modules:
> 	alias scd0 sr_mod
> 	alias scsi_hostadaptor ide-scsi
> 	options ignore=hdc

<SNIP>

> I've kludged things a little by adding the
> line:
> 
> * insmod /lib/modules/2.2.5-15/scsi/ide-scsi.o
> 
> 1) What am I doing wrong in conf.modules which would stop kerneld from
> loading my SCSI emulation dynamically?

First if your using kernel 2.2 you should kill kerneld asap. The 2.2
kernels use a kernel process called kmod. If you have both running things
won't unload correctly

second your options line is wrong, it should be:

options ide-scsi ignore=hdc

scd0 doesn't look correct either but I'm not sure what should go there.
Your using the generic devices to record sga. I can't help you out with
this though - I have SCSI not emulation


> 2) How come when I manually insmod the ide-scsi emulation it hijacks my
> whole secondary IDE interface?

Because your options were wrong :)
