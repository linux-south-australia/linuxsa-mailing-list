Jo\\ao Campos wrote:

> Where can I find rmnod? It isn't on my system, and couln't find it on
> the web . . .

There isn't such a thing - mknod defines a "special" inode and
symbolically maps that inode into Unix file sytem space.  Once
there, rm (or technically, unlink(2)) can disassociate that
link, and if the number of links drops to zero, removes that
inode.
