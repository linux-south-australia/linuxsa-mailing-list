DNS can be tricky stuff,
NT dns manager doesnt add a default A record for domain.somthing @ ip,
It doesnt matter though if domain.somthing doesnt resolve, the record
your most probaly (guessing form the little information provided), is a MX
record so the outside world can find out which server is your email server
to send to for your domain.


eg	

	MX	10 mail-server-here

I suggest you go read up at,

http://www.dns.net/dnsrd/

which contains various resoures on dns stuff.

If ya provide more information might be able to give a better answer =].
(May also have to add the domain you want to recieve mail on to your mail
smtp program configuration prorgam which in sendmail is sendmail.cw
nomrally located in /etc or /etc/mail).

Just remember not to get confused between your smtp mail program (the one
that send and recives email on your mail server(eg sendmail) to the
outside world, and pop3 for collecting your email from the mail server to
your mail reading program.
