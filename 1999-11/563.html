Zebee Johnstone wrote :

> "years of experience" is, in my opinion, an overrated metric.
> It can be a wide range so the person has seen many things,
> or it can be one year many times over.

What could we reasonably use instead?
Self analysis holds little weight (ie. novice, intermediate, advanced, expert)

WHEN the skills where gained and last used helps. For example, I have 2-3
years
in HPUX 10.x admin but I haven't touched a HP box for 2 years.
I would say that someone who has only 1-2 years experience, gained over the
last 1-2 years, would be better suited if those skills were needed.

What else could we use to gauge a person's skills in a résumé?

I am really interested in what the group has to offer on this.  I am nearly
finished building an agency-free IT jobs web site that uses the
'experience and last used' scenario to gauge your skills.

I would be interested in other suggestions on ways to make this better.

Zebee Johnstone also wrote :
> Interviews are a problem though - it's really hard to get a grip  on
> what someone is like and how much their resume lies in that short
> time...

We've all seen the buzzword kings/queens at work in an interview.  I
always try to give them a problem to solve with as little information
as possible.
(ie. you have a unix box that seems to be running slow, what's wrong?).

A GOOD admin/tech will immediately ask you 10 questions.
An AVERAGE admin/tech will take you through the process of elimination.
A POOR admin/tech will tell you what the problem is, with heaps of buzzwords.

It generally works OK for me, but one so-and-so always slips through ....;)

I would also be interested in interview tricks other people use to show up the
exaggerators.
