Re: UDP port 752 does what?

Alan Kennington wrote:

 > Yet another sysadmin tool for me to learn!
 > So I tried "rpcinfo" on a few machines, and was
 > amazed to find that NFS is always the same
 > (port 2049), but mountd is all over the place.

Since the portmapper is able to perform program-number-to-port-number
translations, most servers register any ol' port number, so they'll
show up randomly.  NFS is a special case, but it doesn't have to be:
An NFS server can run on a port other than 2049, and the portmapper
should still perform the correct translation and permit it to work.

 > Do you happen to know if these ports are
 > dynamically allocated?

Yes - The sockaddr_in given to bind() is allowed to say, "Give me any
port number."

 > If so, then it's pretty difficult to
 > firewall/filter them out.

Yup.  RPC sucks around firewalls.  It's the prime reason why most
firewalls block all UDP altogether.
