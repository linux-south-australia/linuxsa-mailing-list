<snip>

> Monitors are also a problem.  In small installations it's probably
> best to cable them to *not* use the UPS, as their power consumption
> is quite large.  Some desktop UPSs have `filtered only' sockets
> that are ideal for monitors, printers and modems.

A handy idea - if you're in need of a working monitor for your servers
during a power failure - invest in a monitor/keyboard/mouse switch.  That
way you can plug it and a single monitor into the UPS for all x computers
and have only one monitor sucking on the power.

*country mode* In Broken Hill you get power failures all the time, ay.
*normal mode* so yeah - having a monitor on the machines as they're being
shut down is a handy luxury.

Long live monitor/keyboard/mouse switches.
