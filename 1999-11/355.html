RE: moving /usr/local & /home

> A Linux novice question, I have a RH5.2 dual boot system where I have a
> 2G DOS HD partitioned as C&D drives and have Linux on a second 800M HD.
> I am running out of space on the linux HD and thought that if I
> symbolically linked the /usr/local & /home to d:\linux\usr\local &
> d:\linux\home I could get by without upgrading.  Noting that, from
> memory, I couldn't partition the DOS drive because I had used up all my
> primary partitions ? or some thing like that.  Any suggestions ?

Not a good idea to use FAT or FAT32 filesystems to store UNIX files.  FATx
systems don't support the same things that UNIX filesystems (eg ext2)
support such as the extended file permissions, the owner/group of the files,
etc.  It's also not possible to create links on a FATx drive.

But - if you're just wanting insecure data storage, go for it.  I use a FATx
parition to store things like mp3's on my personal machine..  so both
Windows and Linux can get at them with ease.

So there are pro's and con's :)

Mostly con's tho, IMO.

/usr/local is NOT a good one to put on your FATx disk, as incorrect
permissions/ownerships will screw with programs' minds.

Use Partition Magic, or FIPS, to resize your DOS disk and use linux's fdisk
to create your partitions - DOS's fdisk follows some silly rules that Linux
need not.
