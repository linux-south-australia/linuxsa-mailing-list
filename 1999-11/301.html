Re: Red Hat and ATA/66 Drives

On Mon, 15 Nov 1999, Andrew Hill wrote:
> Making my first move to Linux on my home computer, and having a bit of
> trouble with the hard drives. Red Hat 6.0 is quite happily set up on my
> EIDE drive, as /dev/hda, but when I try and have a look at /dev/hdb, a new
> ATA/66 drive, with fdisk /dev/hdb, I get the following:

<SNIP>

> Finally, I tried recompiling the kernel without IDE floppy support, and
> this time, fdisk /dev/hdb give me a kernel panic!

Andrew,
	Your problem is that the 2.2 series of the kernels do not support
UDMA 66 at present. This support is in the development series of kernels.

Since you are only new to linux I suggest you make one little change to
solve your problems. In Bios, force the drive to use UDMA/33 rather than
66. As you mentioned, all the drives are backward compatable so it will
have no affect on the drive. It may slow down access times a bit but it
won't be that noticable. Once you get more experiance you can upgrade the
kernel and use udma/66 again.

Finally, Ide-floppy is for drives such as the LS-120 drives (super disk)
and does not apply to hard disks - It is interesting you got a kernel
panic though!
