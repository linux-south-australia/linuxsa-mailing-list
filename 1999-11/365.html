Re: cpio problem

Hello Predrag,

While I haven't used cpio on tape devices for quite some time, I
understand your confusion because I once had to change from cpio to
tar and discovered that they take their input by slightly different
means.

With cpio, the following style works best:

/dev/tape > cpio -i+options
or
find ./stuff -print | cpio -o+options > /dev/tape

Whereas, tar likes to control everything thus:

tar -cf /dev/tape filespecs....

(replace /dev/tape above with whatever works on your system)

If this doesn't put you on the right track, please let us
know what cpio options you used after the mt command.
Also, you say that you are doing entire partitions, would
dump(8) and restore(8) be better suited to the task?
