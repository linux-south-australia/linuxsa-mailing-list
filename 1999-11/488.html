Re: Ultra3 SCSI on linux?

Hi!

On Tue, Nov 23, 1999 at 03:59:27PM +1030, Mark Brown wrote:

> The only cards I have found are from Qlogic and LSIlogic(symbios) as far as I
> can see there are currently no drivers for the Qlogic card but LSIlogic claim
> to support Linux via SDMS which I know nothing about except that it stands
> for 'System Device Management Software'.
> 
> Just wondered if anyone had tried/looked at Ultra3 on Linux and had any info
> on available drivers or adapters?

I haven't used Ultra3 stuff personally, but I seem to remember that Alan Cox
was tinkering with drivers for a symbios card with the I2C bus, which is also
some sort of system device management bus, from memory. This is staggering
quickly into wild conjecture, actually, now that I think about it. ;) Have a
look at Alan's diary page and see what you can dig up, somewhere on
http://www.linux.org.uk/.

I _do_ use an ultra2 LVD drive, with Linux and an Adaptec AHA2940 card which is
solid as a rock; this drive produces very warm and fuzzy bootup messages:

olympus kernel: Detected scsi disk sda at scsi0, channel 0, id 0, lun 0
olympus kernel: (scsi0:0:4:0) Synchronous at 80.0 Mbyte/sec, offset 15.

It's a hell of a lot faster than the older SCSI drives it shares the card with, anyway. :)
