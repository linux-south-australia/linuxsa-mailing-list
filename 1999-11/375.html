Check your hosts file.  This problem occurs when the IP address, or
addresses, associated with most interfaces on your machine (not lo, I
think) do not appear in /etc/hosts or the other sources of name to address
translation, like bind.

It also occurs if your hostname (from the hostname command, and thus from
/etc/sysconfig/network if you are running RedHat, Mandrake or TurboLinux)
does not have an entry in /etc/hosts or another source of name to address
translation.

So, check /etc/hosts.  Make sure that dad is correct. It appears to be .dad
above, so it looks like you might have made a small type with the hostname.
