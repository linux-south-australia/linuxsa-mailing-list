Re: Warning?

Brian Astill <bastill@adam.com.au> asks:
> Is this genuine or just spam FUD?

It's hard to say without knowing the provenance of the warning, however
most of these things are untrue.  The normal form of hoax warning is,
"If you get an email xxxxxxxxx don't open it because it will yyyyyyy.
Tell all of your friends or they will be infected twice over.  Microsoft
know about it, AOL have warned all of their customers, and the Whitehouse
is actively developing a vaccine."

Were the warning genuine you would find details on the webs of all of
the organisations cited, e.g. Microsoft, AOL and the Whitehouse.  If
you are in any doubt as to the veracity of a particular warning, check
out those web sites.  If a simple search doesn't bring up an immediate
hit then it's a hoax.

You should under no circumstance forward a warning to anyone without
having first made sure it's true.
