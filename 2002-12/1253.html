Re: [NEWS] 'Linux is too much like Windows'

On Tuesday, Dec 31, 2002, at 21:50 Australia/Adelaide, James Leone 
wrote:
> It does make me wonder weather what seems to be a conclusion will 
> actually materialize. I honestly do not think so, unless there are 
> significant improvements in people's experience with Linux.
>
> I will argue that this is because Linux is not percieved as a user 
> friendly operating system, and in some ways that perception has merit. 
> I will also argue that steps need to be taken to change this 
> perception, otherwise, a great number of computer related items will 
> remain unsupported due to lack of demand, all the more reason to be as 
> user friendly as possible.
>

Well actually the uptake problem is not a matter of user friendliness 
or not, but rather that PC manufacturers don't ship linux as the 
default OS. Joe Homeuser and the other 98% of the general public won't 
install a different OS if the one they already have does what they want.

If it was a userfriendly thing the PC wouldn't have taken off in the 
home market at all, everyone would have bought Mac's or Amiga's instead 
of those nasty command line driven PC's running DOS.

A home user will invariably learn to use the computer he has purchased 
regardless of what it's running, especially if he's not familiar with 
anything else.
