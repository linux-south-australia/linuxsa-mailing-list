Point still being, do you get $2350 worth of functionality with the

there are many more value added 'features' that you can plug in down the
track for the cost of your time and not the cost of the software.

This is probably the main reason I would favour a Unix based solution more
than
a Windows based one, as Glen touched on in his post.  If you asked me 2
years
ago then probably I'd have said Windows wins that argument, however 2+ years
of using *nix in our environment for web/proxy/file serving (and a whole
heap
of other minor tasks here and there) has proven many of my original
assumptions 
to be wrong.

Absolutely right there.

That is why I'd be sitting down and ignoring the Windows/Unix argument,
and writing down all the things you must have in your new server
environment,
all the things you'd like to have, and then all the things you haven't yet
thought of that you'll need in 3-5 years time.

After that, go out and find the OS that will do all that for you.  Windows
will probably look after the first two ok, but looking after you in 3-5
years
time?  I'd back *nix in anyday for future proofing..... just my opinion.....
