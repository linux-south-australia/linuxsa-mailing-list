I'd have to say that Debian is not hard to install if you have a vague
idea of the hardware underlying you system - I've installed Debian on 3
(difficult systems - an old iMac, just after Debian released the PPC port;
a new Gateway laptop; and a new Dell GX260) the only problems I
encountered were a result of absence (total absence, not just from Debian)
of necessary drivers (eg winmodem, IntelExtreme video etc.).

Debian also has the advantage that it is rarely gimick driven (unlike many
of the more popular distros). Solid and reliable.
