Here is another interesting reply I got from Ralph Grabowski, after his last
issue of Upfront where he said he was considering to HTML.

<snip>

People hate HTML!

Within a few hours, I received dozens of emails requesting that I _not_
switch upFront.eZine to HTML -- and just one lone proponent.

Reasons for going against HTML include:

- larger size (a mockup I did doubled the size of the newsletter in KB).
- hard to read (some email clients render HTML pooly).
- security (HTML can contain malicious code, whereas ASCII cannot).
- and a couple of readers recognized it would be more work for me.

</snip>

Pretty much sums it up.  I am glad he is staying with ASCII, nothing wrong
with it the way it is.

Ralph is another one dabbling with Linux and CAD, but also admits the is
only slim pickings available at the moment. (<-- linux reference just to
make sure this is on topic =)
