I've just bought four genuine Intel rackmount servers. These are only
IDE (in RAID), but they're pretty quick. Depending on what you're
looking for in the footprint department, there are quite a number of
nice options available.

We purchased:

1 x 1U Chassis (SR1300), 2.0GHz PIII Xeon, SE7500WV2 Server Board,
    2 120GB 7200RPM cold-swap HDDs, onboard IDE RAID, 1GB RAM

3 x 5U Chassis (SC5200), 2.0GHz PIII Xeon, SE7500WV2 Server Board,
    2 120GB 7200RPM HDDs, onboard IDE RAID, 1GB RAM

All three are dual-processor capable and will take another 11GB of
RAM. We bought the IDE versions, but much sexier SCSI versions are
available, including hot-swap and RAID capabilities.

The build quality is quite outstanding. I'm really happy with them so
far, particularly the 1U box; I wish I'd bought more of them instead
of more of the 5U's.

I'd say a box based on a similar platform would be more than adequate
for your SQL Server, particularly if it is currently surviving on
450MHz PIIs.

Also, I'd say you'd want to go for something with a SCSI backplane,
rather than IDE. You get all the groovy hot-swap features, as well as
a faster I/O platform.
