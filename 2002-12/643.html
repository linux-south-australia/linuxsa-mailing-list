I remember that Apple were pretty parsimonious in handing out
information to programmers, as well as users. The company where I came
into contact with Macs were doing mainly word-processing type stuff.
So we had enough programming manuals to write things like word
processors, but we couldn't, for instance, have written any networking
software.

I think it's something of a shame that Apple have always had such a
small share of the market; I would find it nicer if the machines which
sold in telephone numbers and were correspondingly cheap were based on
the 68000 rather than the 8086. It's a much nicer chip to program, and
it doesn't have this demented segmented memory architecture which
screwed up the PC for so long. But I'd have been absolutely gasping
for Linux to come along and give me my command line back and some
decent docs.
