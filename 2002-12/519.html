Re: Help with rsync and ISO updates

rsync is better suited to where you have a bunch of files that some or
parts of some are changing often. In this case rsync will work out which
bits of which files have changed and only download them. If your getting
an iso, and the iso is not going to change but rather be completely
replaced by the next release then I dont think rsync is an advantage
over anything that can just resume a file transfer like ftp (or even
http). I used to use rsync on the mandrake cooker, to just keep the tree
of RPMs up to date, which it was perfectly suited too.

Ant
