On Thu, 12 Dec 2002, Greg 'groggy' Lehey wrote:

> > i686 is AMDs Athlon and all Pentiums after the PentiumPro including II,
> > III, IV and Celeron. 
> 
> And the PPro, I believe.

Yes, I meant this but didn't say so.

> > In my experience having an i386 distribution on i586-or-higher hardware is
> > noticeably slower.
> 
> That would be surprising.  Yes, there would be a little difference,
> but I wouldn't expect it to be noticeable.  Can you quantify the
> difference?

Resource-hungry GUIs seem to be a really good way of illustrating the
difference. The screen action is really noticeably slower. This is going
to be dependent on lots of other factors too of course (like how the
particular XFree driver is written, etc) but I've seen this often enough
to be sure it is true.

On the server side, there seens to be a noticeable difference (10-20%-ish,
from memory) if you are trying to maximise performance for a protocol that
requires lots of processing, like SMB.
