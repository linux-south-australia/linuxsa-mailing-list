> I'd be interested to know some background on this. Do they really
> work, or is the guy thinking that Adelaide is far enough away for

They work wonderfully; I can only ever remember one accident in the
Adelaide O-Bahn when some delightful person had left a large object on
the actual tracks. Their greatest advantage is that buses can drive on
roads; hence they can get places where trains can't.

Adelaide's O-Bahn, I think, is about 11 KM's long, runs along our Linear
Park, has excellent views and is very well used. It is, apparently, the
longest busway of its type in the world. The two main terminuses
(spelling) are in Paradise and Modbury.

These two terminuses are drop off points for other bus services, are
well designed, have lots of parking and safe.

I suggest you contact the metropolitan transport board:

* http://www.adelaidemetro.com.au/

..who can give you much more information about the O-Bahn.
