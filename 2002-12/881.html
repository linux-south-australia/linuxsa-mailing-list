Jake Hawkes wrote:

> canada has more guns per captia than america, but less than 1% of the gun deaths.

Sounds like a Hollywood Statistic.  Let's work the numbers, using
UN statistics from 1998.  [The UN doesn't collect these stats, it
only collates them from national agencies.  Errors would probably
understate US firearms, understate US firearm deaths.]

                    Canada     USA
  Population         30m      270m
  Firearms            7m      222m
  GUNS PER CAPITA    .25       .82

So "canada has more guns per captia than america" is false.

                                           Canada    USA
  Firearms-related deaths, per 100,000       4.3    11.4

And "less than 1% of the gun deaths" is false.

There's a good case for a simple correlation: "Canada has one-third
of the guns than the USA and Canada has one-third of the gun-related
deaths than the USA".
