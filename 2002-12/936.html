Lindows heads for store shelves

By  David Becker
Staff Writer, CNET News.com
December 17, 2002, 12:02 PM PT


Lindows, a start-up dedicated to creating an edition of Linux for average 
PC owners, released the first retail version of its software Monday.

The company has enlisted retail partners to sell a spiffed-up version of 
its operating system, Lindows 3.0, which comes with e-mail and telephone 
tech support and a one-year subscription to the company's Click-N-Run 
Warehouse, a central resource for downloading open-source applications.
