Re: Debian DPKG and APT Musings

On Tue, 10 Dec 2002, David Lloyd wrote:

> It strikes me that managing Debian packages _without_ apt seems to be
> just as difficult as managing RPMs.

I think that is the case, yes. rpm and dpkg are meant to solve the same
problem, although they do it in different ways. Tools like kpackage handle
both.

apt is a higher-level approach. urpmi approaches at a similar sort of
level.

I think that rpmfind.net gives an insight to the rpm way of things:
perfectly acceptable with a narrow focus, completely uncontrollable with a
wide focus.
