[NEWS] UniSA and Shared Access Points

Shared access points using leading edge transmission technologies
could make broadband satellite communications services for people
in rural and remote Australia up to ten times cheaper than existing
commercial services, a University of South Australia study shows.

While broadband services are increasing rapidly, a significant number
of people in rural areas don't have access to satellite technology
because it remains too expensive, according to Dr Sylvie Perreau,
Senior Research Fellow at UniSA's Institute of Telecommunications
Research (ITR).

Read the whole story at
http://www.unisa.edu.au/news/media2002/251102.htm
which includes details of a Network Performance (NP) device
developed at Foursticks Pty Ltd to monitor, intelligently control and
manage network traffic.
