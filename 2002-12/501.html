Personally, I don't find the Debian installer /that/ bad.  Given, it isn't
all that user friendly (which yes, they do need to work on), but it does
pretty much everything you want an installer to do, and the most important
thing: its compatible with pretty much any system you throw it into.

I've seen so, so many failed Redhat/Mandrake installs where the installer
will just die in the ass for no apparent reason, both text & X11 installs. 
the problem can usually be tracked down to some piece of hardware/bios
setting that can be overriden with a kernel parameter, but Debian usually
just works.

Installers aren't anywhere as near as easy as most people will have you
believe... I've tried and failed many times ;-(
