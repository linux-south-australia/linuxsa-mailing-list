>         a) Know what packages you need to accomplish your goal. Linux 
> application names are strange to newbies.

That's what apt-cache is for (part of apt-rpm, at least, the same project
produced it.) 'apt-cache search postscript', for example, returns a
screenful of things, with Ghostscript listed first and all sorts of other
things that manipulate or emit postscript.

>         b) Spell the package the exact way it is in the repository

apt-cache doesn't exactly do a fuzzy match, but it lets you guess any word
in the description. That gives you the package name.

> Hope this is interesting or helpful.

Both. (despite what I say above, apt-get/apt-cache are not meant to be a
substitute for a GUI tool for beginners though.)
