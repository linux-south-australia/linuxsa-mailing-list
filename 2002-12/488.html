If one can work out how to get aptitude setup on one's system then it's
really quite easy to get KDE running:

  1) Wander about in uninstalled packages
  2) Find kdebase or any thing in the X11 section that looks like it
might be from KDE, press +
  3) Find ANYTHING with xfree86 in the name, press + on one or more of
them
  4) Press g then g

..and it's there. If you get lucky it will configure itself.
