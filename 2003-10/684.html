Vinum on BSD outperforms Linux! True or false?

I tried to send this message out earlier this week, but it doesn't
seem to have made it.

Now that I have your attention, some more level-headed background:

At the Linux.Conf.Au I'll be presenting a paper about Vinum, a volume
manager for BSD.  One thing that has never been adequately explained
is the performance.  There's plenty of "<MyOS> is better than
<YourOS>" and "<MyVM> is better than <YourVM>" out there, but I
honestly don't know myself how well they compare.

For the paper, I'd like to attempt some sort of comparison, both of
software (and maybe hardware) RAID systems on the same OS platform,
and also across platforms.  In particular, I'd like to be able to give
the delegates an incentive to port Vinum to Linux.  On the other hand,

1.  I want to be scrupulously honest.
2.  I don't have much time.

Both of these goals would be helped if somebody else, preferably with
a strong Linux background, were to help me with the testing.  If I can
also get my hands on some state-of-the-art disk hardware for testing,
that would also be excellent.

Any takers?
