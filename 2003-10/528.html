Re: export in bash scripts

On Sat, 18 Oct 2003, David Lloyd wrote:

> John,
> 
> > from what I know from bash scripting, your missing a $ to tell it that 
> > it is actually a variable.
> 
> I've tried both:
> 
>  X="x"; export $X
> 
> ...and
> 
>  Z="z"; export Z
> 
> ...and both work. I think it's an environment inheritance issue as someone
> else pointed out.

Hmmm, what do you mean by they both work? I would expect different 
results.

	X="x"; export $X

should export the variable 'x' into your environment variables, I would 
think. Hmmm, I can try that to see. ...

>[root@ns root]# X="x"; export $X
>[root@ns root]# echo $x
> 
>[root@ns root]# Z=z; export Z
>[root@ns root]# echo $Z
>z

So there is a difference. $X is evaluated first, as I expected.
