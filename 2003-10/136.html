apt-get install ipmasq dhcp bind9 (if you want to do dns)
dpkg will take you through config of ipmasq, its a bit painless that
way.

your client boxes should be set to dhclient or whatever win protocol
they want.
my debian settings for a client:
(/etc/network/interfaces)
auto eth0
iface eth0 inet dhcp

http://www.debian.org/doc/manuals/network-administrator/index.html#contents
absolutely essential stuff imho:)

as for dhcp, well, i really can only heartily recommend you do a bit of
reading on it, tis very very very simple stuff.

try stuff like this for dns and dhcp etc (besides the obvious rtfm and
tldp http://www.tldp.org/  ):
http://www.linux.org/docs/ldp/howto/DNS-HOWTO.html
