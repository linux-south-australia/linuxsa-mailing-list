Hi.

On Thu, 02 Oct 2003 19:53:32 -0400, Curtis wrote
> At the end of my ~/.mailfilterrc file, I have three rules:
> DENY<>^(To|Cc):.*naota@tampabay\.rr\.com
> DENY<>^(To|Cc):.*anithen@tampabay\.rr\.com
> DENY<>^(To|Cc):.*cnixon3@tampabay\.rr\.com.
> 
> The problem is that all mail sent to anithen@tampabay.rr.com 
> and cnixon3@tampabay.rr.com is being deleted, because it isn't 
> being sent  to naota@tampabay.rr.com.  As you all can see, 
> this is a huge issue.
> 
> Is there a way to put all three addresses in the same rule?

For what it is worth, I would be saving the junk mails to a trash
mailbox. What you have set up there is a system with no margin for
error, as you are now aware. At the point of these filters being
applied, you have already accepted the mail onto the system, you might
as well keep it long enough to verify that it IS junk, at least while
you get your filter rules sorted out (but I'd suggest it's a good
routine practice to do so anyway)
