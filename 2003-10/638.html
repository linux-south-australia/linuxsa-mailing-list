Well, it's still at late beta right now.  The formal public release is
November 3 or something.

It make a mistake configuring XF86 (only allowing 800x600 on my laptop
where RH9 got it right with 1024x768), but besides that everything appears
to work nicely.  Oh yeah, redhat-config-display allows me to easily setup
my laptop's monitor and an external monitor as either one large desktop,
or in a mirror setup (for a projector).  Nice.

GNOME 2.4, OOo 1.1, up to date Perl and Pythons.  Nice eye candy.  up2date
can now suck from rhn, apt and yum repositories - meaning no need to go
through the personal rhn subscription thing anymore.  yum is also
available, but not apt yet (officially).  Graphical boot screen, appears
to be faster than RH9, and uses less memory anecdotally from last night's
usage.  Nice so far.  I'll report more later.
