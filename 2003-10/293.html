Re: [OT] 24 Internet Cafe's in Adelaide

On Sat, Oct 11, 2003 at 10:47:31PM +0930, Ryan Verner wrote:

 > Anybody know of any Internet Cafe's in Adelaide operating 24 hours a day?  
 > Failing that, any that are open for a reasonable amount of time out of hours?
 
If you have an account with a participating ISP (Internode, Airnet, 
Escape Net, probably others by now) and an 802.11b card in your laptop
or PDA, you can get Internet access from most high-traffic parts of the
CBD without needing to visit an Internet cafe at all.

Coverage currently consists of most of Hindley St, all of Rundle Mall,
most of Rundle St, King William St North of Victoria Square, bits of
Pirie St, bits of Pulteney St, most of North Terrace, the North Adelaide
Aquatic Centre, the Festival Centre, and bits of Melbourne St and O'Connell
St in North Adelaide.  http://www.citilan.com.au/images/citilan-locations.pdf

http://www.citilan.com.au has coverage maps and information about where
to sign up.

(There's a lot to be said for downloading at 500 kbytes/sec (yes, that's
kiloBYTES, not kiloBITS) while sitting in the sun under a tree
in Hindmarsh Square.  I've even walked down the mall and seen a guy sitting
on the benches in front of Woolworths carrying out two simultaneous
videoconferencing sessions on his Vaio. 

Participating ISPs can set their own rates for access, so ask yours if
you want to know how much it'll cost (Internode is offering free access
for a while to promote the launch of the network;  Not sure about the 
others)

The network is a joint venture between the State Government, the Adelaide
City Council, Cisco, Agile Communications, AirNet and the m.net corporation.
