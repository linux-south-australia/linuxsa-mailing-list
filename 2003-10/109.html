The web site probably explains it better than anyone here can. But it
will basically mean that now redhat is evolving into a more debian-like
community, where many developers collaborate to improve the overall
distro with a team of Quality Assurance people to decide what goes into
the final releases. The aim is to have more regular fedora releases
(about 4 to 6 months), while redhat concentrate on their Enterprise
product which only incorporates what proves to be the best and most
stable packages from the fedora development "branch".

IMHO it's a nice step in the right direction, and frees redhat from
having to support the huge linux home user market and empowers users to
help themselves. The new up2date tool will also support both apt and yum
repositories.
