Hi David,

> Is it worthwhile (not having been before)?
>
FWIW I took a laptop to Perth and I found it valuable. You will find 
the majority of the audience using their laptop to record elements of 
the speech or other things. There is a classic shot from last year's 
edu conf where a photo taken from the back of the room showed one 
delegate playing solitaire!

Having you laptop with you is a boon, just drop in the network room or 
log into the WiFi and get your email, check on software, grab info from 
other delegates or any number of other things that can be done.

If you have one then I would take it. 

Security. 
Now that is a different ballgame. I made very sure I knew where my 
laptop was at all times. It was always very close to me or locked in my 
dorm.
