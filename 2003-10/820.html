> I have apache executing bash scripts on a machine and echoing the
> results back to the browser in html.  Works great.  Is there 
> an easy way
> to pass information from a html form to a bash script as a 
> variable?  Am
> I missing something obvious?  I've tried a few things and 
> googled around
> and can't seem to come up with anything.  Am I going about this the
> wrong way - should I learn perl/something else?

Heya Jordan,

     I suppose you could always find out what and where the variables are by
runnig something as simple as this.
-------
#!/your/path/2/bash

echo "Content-Type: text/html"
echo

echo "<html><head> </head> <body>"
echo "<pre>set stuff:"
set
echo "</pre>"
echo "<pre>env stuff:"
env
echo "</pre>"
echo "</body> </html>"
-------

and by passing get (http://URL/bash.script?stuff=nothing) stuff to it, it
should tell you what variables the info is stored in.  I would take a guess
that everything past a "?" would all be in the $1 variable respectively, so
you would have to `awk` it or something.
