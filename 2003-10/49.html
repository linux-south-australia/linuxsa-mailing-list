> Brian,

> > Has anyone had success with inexpensive cordless keyboards or cordless
> > mouse under linux ? I am wondering what alternatives we have to MS
> > products. Naturally cost comes into it. If you have found something that
> > will do a similar job while working under linux, at a reduced cost, tell
> > us about it.

> They just work.

Indeed; checkout the Logitech wireless keyboard/mouse combo, they're quite
nice, and decently priced.

The multimedia keys even work under Linux (which is why I was confused at the
question a week ago about needing drivers; extra keys just generate different
scancodes, you can use a tool like xev to find what they are).  If you're
using Gnome 2.2, things are even easier - open up the Multimedia Preferences,
click something to assign, and hit the key; I've got things like volume, mail,
terminal assigned on mine, and they work fine.
