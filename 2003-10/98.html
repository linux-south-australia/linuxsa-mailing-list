Microsoft moves beyond patches

  "Conceding that its strategy of patching Windows holes as they emerge
  has not worked, Microsoft plans next week to outline a new security
  effort focused on what the company calls "securing the perimeter,"
  a company executive told CNET News.com."
     -- http://news.com.com/2100-1002_3-5085251.html?tag=st_pop

I predict this new strategy will be even less effective than they think
their current one, of patching Windows, has proven to be.  One of the
weakest attributes of firewalls (and virus checkers) is that many people,
particularly those who lack technically savy, think, "Oh, I'm secure
because I've got a firewall; I don't have to worry about attacks."
This is wrong.  A firewall does NOT make you safe.  It might give you
added protection, but only if your machines are already secure.  If you
think like the person I described then I earnestly and strongly advise
you to remove your firewall.  Either way you'll be insecure, but without
a firewall you will at least know it.  Ditto to virus checkers.
