Re: ssh with public and private key

At 2003/10/27 13:08+1030  John Edwards writes:
> Chris Donovan wrote:
> 
> > I guess I have never really understood why you shouldn't allow root 
> > logins.  I mean really what is the difference between logging into 
> > your user account and then su'ing to root?  What security does this 
> > provide? 
> >
> 
> For systems with multiple administrators, forced non-root logins can 
> provide a better audit trail and a more fine-grained control of access. 
> It's by no means foolproof, but like most security measures it's a good 
> way of keeping honest people honest.

Another technique I've seen is to allow root logins *only* using ssh
keys (eg no su and no sudo) and generally not allowing password
logins. The reason for this is that a lot of people have the bad habit
of sharing passwords among machines. When one machine gets broken into
and the user account passwords for that machine sniffed, the intruder
gets access to a whole lot of other machines (and potentially root
accounts through sudo).
