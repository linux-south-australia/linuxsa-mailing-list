Re: Discovering an IP address

On Fri, 28 Nov 1997, Don Collins wrote:

> If I know someone is getting on to the 'net via a dial-up link, how do I
> find out the IP address for that connection.
> I wish to be able to ssh into a machine that dials in to an ISP at least
> once a day, but will need to know its IP address, which of course is
> different each time it dials up.  I'm sure it's a simple command/procedure
> but right now I'm suffering a severe case of mental blockage.  Any helpers?
> regards....Don

I think you might need to read the Dynamic-IP-Hacks mini-HOWTO. You see,
generally you can't figure out other peoples' IP addresses if they haven't
got a domain name, except if you know the subnet (then you can
ping'n'tell).

Some of the hacks in the howto show you how to 'publish' your IP address
on a webpage, or send it to somebody every time it changes. Perhaps this
is way too clumsy for you, and you just want to be able to ssh to a
domain.. Don't despair ;-

There is a service on the 'net called Monolith, and they offer a service
called dyndns.. It's a DNS service that changes when /you/ change your IP.
Whats' more, its' completely free for any sort of use, and they offer some
other great services as well (home pages, free normal DNS).. For more
information, visit http://www.ml.org/
