> Geoffrey and all.
> The problem turned out to be a faulty CPU.  My 486DX2-66 is once again a
> DX-33.  :-((
> I now have a different problem though.  Having recompiled the kernel I'm
> getting the following error messages at boot.
> 
> modprobe: Can't locate module net-pf-5.
> This is repeated several times.
> The other is as follows:
> cat: /proc/scsi/scsi: No such file or directory.
> This is surprising as I removed SCSI support from the kernel config.
> 
> Why am I getting these and what is the solution????  How will this effect
> my machine's running?

Hi Don,

Both messages are harmless.  You can fix the net-pf-5 messages by
adding this line to /etc/conf.modules:

alias net-pf-5 off

Just ignore the /proc/scsi/scsi message.  It's because the Red Hat
initscripts presume that you have SCSI support.
