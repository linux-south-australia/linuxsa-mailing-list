I disagree, it should be theoretically possible to have virii on 
any platform.  Multi-user or not.

Fortunately under most multi-user operating systems it becomes 
difficult to do things like memory resident virii due to the 
paradigm that these operate under.

When you say system independent, I assume you mean one that is not
tied to any particular binary/cpu format.  It is trivial to get
an application to compile a piece of code.  With consistent formats
such as 86open and ELF, it should be possible to make a binary
that could be portable across many platforms.
