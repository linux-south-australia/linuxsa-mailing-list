Re: Tertiary Qualifications (was Re: [Job Vacancy] IT SYSTEMS ADMINISTRATION)

Hmm...

I studied computer and information science for a year. I have since left to
pursue medicine and keep my computers as a hobby, so i don't get overworked
out of enjoying them - which is what i was afraid was happening. I did
Computer and Internet Technology (which was a repeat of year 12 Info tech
essentially), Technical Communication (i don't like to be pessemistic, but
what a waste of my time - i already know how to write a letter and a resume
several times over), Programming in Java, Java Programming Lab, User
Interfaces and Mathematics for Computing.

I loved learning Java (not because it was Java - Java really sucks in my
opinion) but because of the principles of it. And maths was my favorite
subject. I pretty much did every exercise in the text and every assignment
twice, as well as seeking out other books with practice work in them. The
principles are what counts in this case - to just judge the content on the
surface would be unfair, as that way, it appears pretty elementary and
pointless.

I found also that some procedures are not in line with what you should be
doing. If you are learning programming, you are learning it to program a
computer. Why then should you be required to handwrite the code for 10
situations in a test, without the assistance of a reference book (which you
would have reasonable access to in real life situations) or a compiler or
interpreter (which would pick out your mistakes before you pass in your
final code. No wonder half of the students failed Java, handwritten code
would have more bugs than the pile of dirty washing behind my door!

I love doing university study - I still have 10 years ahead of me including
postgraduate psychiatry and my specialisation in forensics, and i would
study for the rest of my life if i could. But in the case of computing, I am
still dubious as to whether it provides you with the best method of
education for a job. There must be better ways to ensure that the candidates
that you are after for a job have the skills and knowledge that you require.
Maybe holding a Cisco certification or similar (or equivalent). A practical
test before you are given or offered the position? A theoretical quiz? If
you were placed in whatever situation and something-or-other happened, how
would you deal about it? I could get this job had I finished that course.
But I wouldn't have the slightest clue how to use a router. I constantly am
looking up how to do stuff - learning how to do it myself under my own
steam. Uni didn't give me any skills in that area, and I ain't gonna do any
specific certifications at this point.

Anyhow, I've had a bad day, so I don't feel like spilling my mind out in
binary any more tonight. I'm gonna go and play GTA now.
