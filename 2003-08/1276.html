57 ICMP/hour/IP-address - ideas on cause?

During the last 15.5 hours or so (i.e. today local time), I've received
about 42000 ICMP packets. 
They mostly look like this:

Aug 24 15:30:23 dog kernel: IN=ppp0 OUT=ppp0 SRC=203.48.59.33 DST=203.48.NNN.157
LEN=92 TOS=0x00 PREC=0x00 TTL=121 ID=25512 PROTO=ICMP TYPE=8 CODE=0 ID=512
SEQ=51941

Aug 24 15:30:44 dog kernel: IN=ppp0 OUT=ppp0 SRC=209.174.88.201
DST=203.48.NNN.129 LEN=92 TOS=0x00 PREC=0x00 TTL=106 ID=6660 PROTO=ICMP TYPE=8
CODE=0 ID=512 SEQ=20659

where NNN is some integer.

This amounts to about 57 ICMP packets per hour per IP address.
The total is about 2700 ICMP packets/hour for the whole IP space.
Sometimes they are a scan across my IP spaces, other times they're
apparently random.

My question is:
Is this somehow related to the Sobig.F thing?

I haven't heard of it sending lots of ICMP packets.
