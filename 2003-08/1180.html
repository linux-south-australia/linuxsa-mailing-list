> root@ipcop:/var/log/snort # grep CyberKit alert|wc
>   12314  221652 1945538
>
> That's just this week.
>
> Is there any reason why ISP's don't just block traffic of known worm
> activity like this at their border routers? I know it's a MS user's
> problem, but it seems a bit pointless to carry it at times of high worm
> activity.

ISPs probably don't want to step into a legal minefield.  If they block this
traffic then politicians might get ideas that it's also easy to block
porn/nasty websites.  Additionally users might rely on the ISP taking action
and thus not protecting their PCs.  The next time the ISP doesn't do that
users may be inclined to sue.  Civil libetarians might be upset as well (ISP
actively monitoring packets).

None of the above are terribly good reason to not block, but an ISP probably
has better things to worry about.
