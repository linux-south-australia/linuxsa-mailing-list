You're close, but I never said free (in fact I said I'm happy to pay for
it), and I never said "easy to use" or "you beaut" - if I wanted that
I'd stick with Windows and Exchange, cos it is pretty easy to use and if
you like nice graphical point and click stuff, it's you beaut too (I do
say this with tongue firmly in cheek...).  And I'm after more than just
a calendar server.

I'm after a Linux "equivalent" of Exchange (or close to it), which
Outlook clients can connect to, which provides much of the functionality
of Exchange, such as shared scheduling, free/busy data, public folders,
folder permissions, delegates, global address lists, etc et al.  I also
was interested to hear of experiences that anyone had had with doing
such a thing or trying out Bynari, OpenExchange, etc et al.

I'm not really interested in Evolution, because as you know it's
difficult/impossible to convert average Windows/Outlook/Office users to
Linux.

Thanks anyway for the info, I have learned a bit more about Evolution
out of it, which is always a good thing  :)
