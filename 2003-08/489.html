Re: BSD Licence vs GPL - AGAIN

On Friday,  8 August 2003 at 10:01:34 +0930, Angus Wallace wrote:
> Hmmm. Is there a licence that has the 'good' aspects of both BSD and
> GPL? i.e.
>
> 1) Requires source code inclusion with derived products

BSD doesn't require that.  That's one of the biggest differences from
the GPL.

> 2) Ensures buyers/users of derived works have the same rights

BSD doesn't ensure that.  I'm not sure that any license does.

> 3) but allows the 'original' [1] author to negotiate a closed source
> sale (to a particular vendor for their improvements only - i.e. sell a
> BSD style freedom to a party)...

I believe there are precedents for this in GPL licensed code.  Wasn't
there something with ReiserFS?  Correct me if I'm wrong.
