On Thu, Aug 07, 2003 at 02:16:01PM +0900, Michael Davies wrote:

 > It's just a software licence that specifies the conditions under

That's bullshit, Michael - We went through this already last week!

It specifies conditions under which derived works of the software can
be -distributed-.  That's why the GPL is titled "TERMS AND CONDITIONS
FOR COPYING, DISTRIBUTION AND MODIFICATION" (read it;  it's right there
immediately after the preamble).

One of those conditions is that the distribution can only occur if
the derived work is also covered by the terms of the GPL.

That makes it viral (As far greater contributors to freely distributable
software than you or I seemed to agree decades ago).

To say it's "no more, no less" than any other software license is
pretty tenuous at best.

(Does -anyone- on this mailing list actually understand what the GPL
is about, or do they just buy-in to the 15-word soundbites from RMS
on slashdot?  Hands up everyone who has actually sat down and read it?)
