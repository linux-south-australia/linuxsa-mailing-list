emacsclient is easy.  In an emacs window, do c-x server-start.  In
your .muttrc, add:

set editor=emacsclient

That's it!  When you hit r (reply), etc., mutt will look for the
client, and it will come up with the text.  To end, hit c-x # instead
of c-x c-c.  I continually mess this one up, which is why I have this
line in my tidy-up-reply macro:

  (local-set-key "" (quote server-edit))

This remaps c-x c-c to the exit function (confusingly called
server-edit).
