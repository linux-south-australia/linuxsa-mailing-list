RE: satellite

>
> Be aware that 'Best Current Practice 38' (aka RFC 2287) encourages ISP's
> to deny ip packets with a source address that doesn't match the routes
> for an interface. Plain IP satellte services will not work with ingress
> filtered networks unless there are special exceptions made. Even if your
> ISP doesn't do it, their upstream might.
>
> BCP38 is a good thing, as it makes denial of service attacks easier to
> deal with and adds a little more integrity to stateless network
> communications. Some satellite providers work around it by providing a
> tunnel for backchannel data, so that the user's ISP sees only packets
> with the correct source address for the dialup interface.
>

Yes correct and it's also why ISP's that implement it get the, "Your
network/service is no good because it works when I dial Bigpond!", because
Telstra/Bigpond do not appear to beleive in RFC's like 2287 and may others.
