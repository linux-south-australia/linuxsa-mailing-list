Steve,

A default Redhat install leaves a lot of services running, some Distro's may
be better or worse in this area, which of course means that out of the box
one distribution might be better than another... However the first two
things you should do to any system (Linux, Windows, whatever) before its
connected to the internet for the first time is shut down the services you
don't need and apply latest security patches to the services you do need.
I'd imagine that once you do that to any linux distribution they'd all be
pretty much alike in terms of security.

I've never had too many problems with Redhat security, my firewall _is_ a
RedHat box!
