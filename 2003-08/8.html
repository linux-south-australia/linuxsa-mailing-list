Brian,

If I'm not wrong, the author may be talking in particular about those annoying
kernel customisations which SuSE and RedHat make, with the result that
when you download the standard kernel and use it, some subset of applications
ceases to work correctly. Is that what he's talking about?
Or is he talking about the configuration or installation tools?

Linux users _are_ very dependent on the distributions because no one
who has a real life has the time to bundle together and compile/configure
2000 applications or even only 200 applications in their latest versions,
not even once a year. So the distributions are necessary for people who
have other things to do. But then they do these annoying customisations.

Question:
Does anyone happen to know which distributions are minimally or not at all
affected by this vendor customisation practice? I know from experience that
SuSE definitely has harmful kernel customisations in the above sense,
and I've heard reports that RedHat has them too.
