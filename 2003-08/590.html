The vim equivilant of m-q is gq, then a movement command.

gqap will wrap a paragraph, for example.  Vim's smart enough to understand
'>' markers too.

In fact, most of the 'g' commands are kinda funky - type ":help g" for
more.

Here's a handy mail editing command - if you want to cut out all the text
between your cursor and the signature (to cut out spam, for example), like
this:

 | > Quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable
 | > quotable quotable.
 | > _
 | > Unquotable unquotable unquotable unquotable
 | > 
 | > Free!  Live!  Cheap!  Half price!  Rant Spam Penises!
 | > Free!  Live!  Cheap!  Half price!  Rant Spam Penises!
 | >
 | > -- 
 | > Their signature
 | 
 | -- 
 | Your signature

(where _ represents the cursor :-)

You can type a groovy delete command to remove the guff:

d/--[space][enter]

Which will turn the above into this:

 | > Quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable quotable
 | > quotable quotable.
 | 
 | 
 | -- 
 | Your signature

Nice huh.

Bind that to an F-key in your ~/.vimrc and you'll have a handy key you'll
use forever more.

Remember u will undo (multiple levels, too) if you make a mistake.

And remember, most importantly, that vim is a million times better than
emacs.
