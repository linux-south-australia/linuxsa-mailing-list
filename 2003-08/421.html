I'm a high school dropout who drove taxis for nearly 20 years and has
been using linux at home for the past 8 years. Nowadays I spend 12 to 18
hours a day on irc helping all sorts of people with their linux
problems. A lot of them are college graduates, some are highly paid
SysAdmins. Many of them don't even know how to use the su command
properly and wonder why they keep getting "Command Not Found!" errors. A
lot of them have no idea how to resolve dependancy errors or why
compiling from source on an rpm based distro is a bad idea.

My point is, it's not always about knowing all the answers. Sometimes
knowing where to look for the answers is a more desirable skill. Having
a degree doesn't necessarily mean they have such skills. Sometimes the
hardest part is actually knowing what the real question is, and
understanding the nature of the problem at hand, instead of just looking
for a quick fix.

I'm broke and unemployed and sure would love a job like the one
advertised here. However when I see the words "Tertiary qualified" in
the job requirements I don't feel encouraged to apply just to make the
other applicants look good. Potential employers merely see "taxi driver"
on my resume and move on to the next one. I don't even get to the
interview stage. The best I can usually hope for is a "Thank you for
your application" letter.

Having a degree doesn't mean you're better than anyone else, but it sure
can help to open doors that are closed for those without one.
