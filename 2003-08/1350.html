Assuming you use the same version of exchange for 3 years (not uncommon)  
then as long as you get at least an extra $200 per person of productivity
per annum (pretty easy) then the business case looks pretty good.  Let's 
face it that's in the order of 7 work hours per year so it's not too hard to 
make that up (8mins per week!) and we're talking a company of 5 here.

And that's how business looks at.  The cost of each employee is such that if 
you can increase the productivity (or various other measures) of each 
employee then it's worthwhile.  This is even more the case when dealing with 
companies with thousands of employees.
