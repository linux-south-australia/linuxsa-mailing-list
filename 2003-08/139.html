Ah, funny stuff.  The old registration and licensing site for
transport.sa.gov.au (back when they used Camtech E-Commerce) was the
same.  It went through a complicated browser check to make sure you
were using a recent version of IE on a PC or Mac (!) and gave you an
error if you weren't, but there was nothing to stop you from pointing
your browser directly at the Java payment applet and running with it
anyway.  Worked fine under Netscape on lots of UNIXen.  Using the
appletviewer that comes with the JDK worked too.

I sent them email on multiple occasions asking them why they bothered
insisting on IE when it plainly wasn't a requirement;  I never did
get a satisfactory answer.  Then Camtech tanked, RLS switched to
something else, and now it's quite correctly browser-agnostic.

What is it about the State Government that makes it perform utterly
useless browser checks for otherwise open web sites?
