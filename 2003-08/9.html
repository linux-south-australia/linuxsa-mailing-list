Kernel customisation (was Re: Linux & Lock in)

Alan Kennington wrote:
> Question:
> Does anyone happen to know which distributions are minimally or not
> at all affected by this vendor customisation practice? I know from
> experience that SuSE definitely has harmful kernel customisations
> in the above sense, and I've heard reports that RedHat has them too.

I'm not sure vendors patching kernels is a "harmful" practice.

Often these are back-ports of things that appear in the latest unstable
kernel.  For example, things like O(1), NPTL, bigmem etc get included. 
This is a *good* thing IMHO.  I want these improvements today, not in 18
months time when the next stable kernel series arrives.
I guess it depends upon whether you are prepared to maintain a subset of
the 2000 or so packages (like you said) yourself.  If you really just want
to get on with your work, use the vendor kernels, and let them worry about
the updates needed to the user-space utils.  That's their value-add
remember - if you do it yourself, you might as well "roll your own" and
add topologyLinux to the list of 200+ distros available.
My guess is that the "community" distros are probably the least "tainted",
i.e. Debian, Slackware, etc and maybe Red Hat in the future[1].
[1] See http://rhl.redhat.com/ once they fix their site.
