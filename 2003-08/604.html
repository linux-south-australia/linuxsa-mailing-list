Re: one LF/para... Re: [SCO] What the SCO guys are doing with shares

On Sat, Aug 09, 2003 at 01:23:45PM +0930, Alan Kennington said:
> Recently I noticed that the "one word line-wrap" problem (where an indent
> with "> " causes 80-char lines to split into two lines) was not happening
> in Mark Newton's responses using Mutt/1.2.5.1i. Somehow his editor
> seems to recognize paragraphs, narrow them, and then indent them, even when
> the original has line-splits.
> It would be interesting to know which editor does that.

vim does that.  Tell it how wide you want your text with, for example,

":set tw=75"

then position the cursor on the offending paragraph, and type "gqap".  It
understands >'s and wraps the text to whatever width you specify.

Example!

:set tw=70, then gqap:

> Recently I noticed that the "one word line-wrap" problem (where an
> indent with "> " causes 80-char lines to split into two lines) was
> not happening in Mark Newton's responses using Mutt/1.2.5.1i.
> 
> Here's an extra paragraph to illustrate things.
> 
> Somehow his editor seems to recognize paragraphs, narrow them, and
> then indent them, even when the original has line-splits.

:set tw=40, then gqap:

> Recently I noticed that the "one word
> line-wrap" problem (where an indent

> split into two lines) was not
