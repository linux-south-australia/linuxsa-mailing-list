James Leone wrote:

>Out of curiosity, and not about brain sizes I just want to know, what do 
>you find that Windows 2000/2003 server does that Samba deoes not ?

James - although the discussion wasn't about Win2000/2003 - for some clients
we are using Samba (some with E-Smith as it's a great "out of the box"
product for smaller networks).  However if someone wants to apply security
policies to groups of users (OU's) then the tight integration between
Windows 2000/2003 and Windows XP clients is really nice - this is something
as far as I'm aware there is no integrated Linux solution for, but please
bear in mind I haven't looked too hard on this!

>Just curious as to what features do most people use Exchange for, I have 
>never used it.

Collaboration.  Our medium and larger clients love collaboration.  Most users
in our experience however just use emailing, rules, calendar, tasks and contacts.
More advanced users tend to use grouping, document management with revisions and
quite a bit of scheduling.

>What does "true collaboration" mean in general ?

True collaboration as opposed to "if you send him/her a copy or keep a
copy of this information here then they can look at it and send you their
input and you include it.  Or they amend their copy, they send it to you
and you add it to your copy".
Ie We have access to others information, can actively participate and
have that participation tracked, handled, controlled and initiated.
I hope this gives you some idea.

>What features do you find most lacking ?

I've seen a few that, from what it looks like, should be good once they are
stable and have been in use in substantial numbers for a while.  It would
honestly take far too long to detail all the things that Exchange can do
for an organisation.  But some of the features that should be included
that spring to mind (in my view):
- Sharing information between users or defined groups of users who have
varying rights dependent on their role.
- Sharing mailboxes, calendars, group appointments, task scheduling all
 *within* the client connection.
- Document management including revision and comment tracking.
