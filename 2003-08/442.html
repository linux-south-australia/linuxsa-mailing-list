NEVER EVER use --force or --nodeps options unless you're truly aware of
the consequences!!!! One can easily trash their rpmdb that way!

Shame on you David for even suggesting it to someone who obviously is
still learning to use rpm!

The short answer is put both rpm's in the same empty directory and do
rpm -Uvh *.rpm and rpm itself will sort out and resolve the dependancy
problem.

A better answer would be to do man up2date and learn how to use the
tools redhat ships with to install applications along with all their
dependancies painlessly. Or get apt-get for redhat from
http://apt.freshrpms.net and go along to the next AUUG meeting where
David will explain how to use it.  ;-P
