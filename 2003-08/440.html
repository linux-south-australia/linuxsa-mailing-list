No, dependencies are there for a reason. Only use nodeps if the
dependencies are not satisfied by another RPM package. For example if
you've manually compiled mysql and stuffed it into /usr/local/mysql, and
you know rpm doesn't know about
/usr/local/mysql/lib/libmysqlclient.so.10

The correct way to solve your problem is to do the following:

rpm -Uvh perl-DBD-MySQL-2.1021-3.i386.rpm mysql-3.23.54a-11.i386.rpm

No forcing. No 'nodepping'. Just happy rpms playing together in the sun.
