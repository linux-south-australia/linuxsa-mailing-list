[NEWS] Sun pragmatic about open-source software

Sun pragmatic about open-source software 
By Stephen Shankland 
Staff Writer, CNET News.com
August 5, 2003, 5:49 PM PT

 SAN FRANCISCO--Linux and open-source software have swept
 across much of the computing industry, but on Tuesday, Sun
 Microsystems' top software executive called for a dose of
 realism about the movement. 

 Open-source software--which demands that anyone may see,
 change or redistribute the source code that underlies a
 product--is good for attracting programmers and spurring new
 ideas, Jonathan Schwartz, Sun's executive vice president for
 software, said in a keynote speech at the LinuxWorld
 Conference and Expo here. But open-source software is not
 the answer to all problems, he said. 

 "The thing I worry about most with the open-source community
 is the sentiment that open source is somehow different. It
 isn't," Schwartz said. What matters to customers is quality,
 he said. It was the quality of Linux on Intel servers, not
 the open-source nature of the software, that attracted so
 many customers and eventually forced Sun to offer such a
 product, he said.
