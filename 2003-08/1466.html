Luke,

> I know very little about ADSL deals and connection problems.
> How problematic is it?

It works out of the box.

> If you do this deal, what are the problems getting it to work under Redhat
> 9, if any?

Absolutely none. Because the modem is an ADSL/ROUTER it can do all the NAT
and Firewalling for you. You can also make it a "MODEM only" device and do
all the authentication, pppoe and stuff yourself.

> How long do you have to wait for it to be activated (including having
> your normal phone working)?

That depends on Telstra. I expected to wait for two weeks but it connected
in two days.

> Are there any hidden costs, such as extra h/w (ethernet cards etc.)?

Not really - you do need a credit card though. The modem is both a USB and
Ethernet modem. If you're on dialup it could actually _save_ you money -
it's the main reason why I got ADSL.
