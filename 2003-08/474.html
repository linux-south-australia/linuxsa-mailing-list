RE: BSD Licence vs GPL - AGAIN

> Hmmm. Is there a licence that has the 'good' aspects of both BSD and 
> GPL? i.e.
> 
> 1) Requires source code inclusion with derived products
> 2) Ensures buyers/users of derived works have the same rights
> 3) but allows the 'original' [1] author to negotiate a closed source 
> sale (to a particular vendor for their improvements only - 
> i.e. sell a 
> BSD style freedom to a party)...

I don't understand 2, really, but I think the GPL will do that. Reason being
that the 'original' author (presumably, the copyright holder) can license
software in whatever way he or she wants. You can dual license your code,
you can sell proprietry versions and give away a GPL version, you can do
whatever you want. What you can't do is take someone else's contribution to
the GPL software, which would be under their copyright, and relicense
that...

> 
> Perhaps this would be a good compromise...
> 
> -Angus

> inventor of decimal numbers, or positional notation... ;-) Hmmm. It 
> seems like we're drawing a line in the sand...

Copyright holder would be sensible. I don't see why the writer of the x86
instruction set would have any claim over software written to run on x86,
even if it's written in assembly...
