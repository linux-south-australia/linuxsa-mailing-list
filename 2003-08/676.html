Re: [Monday] Good news for all IT workers who know a lot about hu man genes

On Mon, Aug 11, 2003 at 03:36:27PM +0930, Martin Lewis wrote:
> We can end up with bits of virus in our genomes after viral
> infections.

Note that this doesn't mean you end up with bits of cold virus in your
genome every time you catch a cold. Viruses like the cold virus are
"userland" viruses that run "make virus &; $0 &; $0 &" until the cell
bursts, but leave the "kernel" alone. Viruses like AIDS try to patch
the kernel, which eventually (millions of years?) responds by
blacklisting certain code sequences. Or possibly by pinching bits of
them and turning them to good use.

This also happens with entire organisms. Mitochondria, the bits of
cells which provide energy for other cellular processes, contain DNA
of their own which is unrelated to the nuclear DNA, and are thought to
have originally been independent organisms which have become
cellular-level symbionts. (Dunno what cells did for energy before this
happened...)
