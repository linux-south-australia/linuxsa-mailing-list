To be honest, I think the biggest problem here is the issue of support. M$
support their products for years whereas Red Hat, by way of example,
supports their server and professional products for a year I believe. And of
course, M$ techs are EVERYWHERE.

If one person in a small organisation is trained as a sysadmin for Linux
and, say, SuSE was available to use, it could work. There will be some
teething troubles (particularly since the M$ juggernaut will want to snare
the market before anyone else could give them any other choice) but it could
potentially work because everything from productivity apps (OO.org) to
accounting software (GnuCash) to browsers (Moz, Konquerer, Opera, etc)is
covered by most Linux versions. The Linux community has in fact bent over
backwards to make their products work with M$ products and have got nothing
of the sort in return. 

Training up some coders or hiring some to write some customised software (or
modify existing software) would probably help too. It's not impossible, just
difficult.

let people know. :-) I'm well aware of Linux geeks' affinity for a decent
coffee drop. Hey, LinuxFormat had a section for best coffee in their yearly
