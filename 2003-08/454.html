Good Karma:-
1. All vanilla source code from the developers, no horrid distro patching.
2. As small or as big as you want.
3. Very stable.
4. Easy to update for security fixes, lastest source, compile, install.
5. Pick your own applications.
6. You learn heaps.
7. No forever updating if you don't want to, you're in the driving seat
   not the passenger seat.

Bad Karma:-
1. Takes time and patience to do.
2. You can't join in distro flame wars.
3. You have to think up your own name :)
