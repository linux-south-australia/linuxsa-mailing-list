>  > Does anyone know the difference between CAT5 types, and other info
>  > i need to know?
>  
> There are two types of cable.  "Patch" cable is used for, well,
> patchleads. It's very flexible because the wires in the cable are
> stranded.
> 
> The other kind is used for internal building work, is less flexible,
> and has solid-core conductors.  That makes it nice and strong, so you
> can pull it through ducts, gaps between walls, etc without causing
> internal fractures.  When you bend it it wants to stay bent too.
> 
> If you ask a vendor for patch cable you'll end up with the stuff that
> isn't usually used for internal building work.
> 
> This may not seem like a problem on the surface, but it is:  the two
> types of cable have two types of connector.  Most wallplates and
> patchpanels have Krone terminations on them which expect solid-core
> cable.  And most crimp-on RJ45 plugs are intended to go on the end of
> patchleads, and they therefore expect stranded cable.  You might end
> up with dodgy terminations if you mix the cable and connector types.
> 
> So for building work specify that you want a 100m roll of solid-core
> CAT5E cable.
> 
>  > Anyway, does anyone know where to get some cheap CAT5 in Adelaide?
>  > JayCar has it for 75c/m, but i've heard you can get good quality
>  > cable for 40c/m.
> 
> Try Page Data.
