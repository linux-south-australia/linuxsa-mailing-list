Re: Is SCO MS/Sun backed?

On Sunday, 24 August 2003 at 12:24:03 +0930, Richard Russell wrote:
> Hi all,
>
> Recently I've read that the SCO lawsuit is funded or backed not only by MS,
> but also by Sun (http://www.theinquirer.net/?article=11168, among others)...
> Now both MS and Sun are favourite targets for Linuxistas, being Evil
> Megacorporations Plotting Against Linux, but is this accusation
> true?

It's the first I've heard of a Sun involvement.  It also takes more
than unsubstantiated accusations like this to convince me:

   The SCO Group's Microsoft (and Sun) funded campaign of continuing
   attacks in the press on Linux and the Open Source community has
   known no bounds.

> It sounds to me a lot like the counter-accusation, that IBM is
> funding the ESRs and RedHats and whoever else of the world.

Haven't heard that one, but ditto.

> As far as I know, MS owns SCO stock (I don't know how Sun is
> linked), but that's the extent of it.

Certainly Microsoft stands to gain from damage to Linux.  It's not so
clear that Sun would.  And I can't imagine Microsoft and Sun
collaborating on *anything*.

> Certainly sounds like less of an influence than IBM's billion dollar
> Linux investment (not just the $, but the credibility). What
> confuses me further is why Sun would be implicated in this -- aren't
> they trying to get in on the Linux market these days?

Exactly.

The Inquirer has never appeared very trustworthy to me.
