Ian,

It might be a little early for triumphalism. I think that IBM will be
panicking about loose cannons and such -- shooting from the hip, etc.
It's also covered here:

Eric Raymond gets very mad at SCO
http://www.theinquirer.net/?article=11168

If I had someone talking like that at my side, I'd move out of the
firing line a little. Linus has spoken a little too loosely too.
They should follow IBM's example and "speak quietly and carry a big stick".
IBM have a very big heap of lawyers and experience, and they are not shouting.
If SCO win, they will not forgive permature triumphalism. 

The Inquirer (UK) also has this:

How to muzzle SCO
http://www.theinquirer.net/?article=11175

Unfortunately this is another bit of amateur legal advice which doesn't
really suggest anything with any definiteness. The whole point is not
to muzzle SCO but to get them to spill as many beans as possible.

Personally, I think the whole affair is going very nicely. Linux has never had
so much good publicity as this.
