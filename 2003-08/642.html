The non-coding DNA is probably recognised by the coded proteins which in turn
has an influence on regulation of proteins.  

Every time a cell divides a bit of DNA is deleted off the end of chromosomes to
keep count so the cell knows when to suicide, making room for the newer less
cancer prone cells to take its place.  This is where animal cloning has had
trouble, old cells with the clock already running, used to grow apparently
young animals.
