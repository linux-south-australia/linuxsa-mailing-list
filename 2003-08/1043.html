John,

Not only that, but as I understand it, the majority of linux
kernel lines are just kernel modules. The core is a rather small
fraction of the total, much less than half.
They can't be claiming that the modules are all theirs too, can they?

I think that if they really said "millions", then they were either drunk,
or they lost their script for that day, or else they thought like this:

-	We said hundreds of lines. Our shares went up.
-	We said thousands of lines. Our shares went up again.
-	We said hundreds of thousands of lines. Our shares went up yet again.
-	So today it's "millions".

When they say _tens_ of millions of lines next week, it might be time
for the guys in white coats to move in. The inflationary bubble must burst
some time.

Here's my little listing for linux 2.4.20:

-----------------------------------------------
akenning@shark> pwd
/usr/src/linux-2.4.20
akenning@shark> find . -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; |wc -l
4303246
akenning@shark> find drivers -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; | wc -l
2493469
-----------------------------------------------

That leaves 1809777 lines outside the drivers (which are about 60% of lines).

Then I get this:

-----------------------------------------------
akenning@shark> find arch -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; | wc -l
 683550
akenning@shark> find ipc -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; | wc -l
   3273
akenning@shark> find kernel -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; | wc -l
  14230
akenning@shark> find include -type f -name '*.[ch]' -exec cat {} \; | wc -l
 536381
-----------------------------------------------

So it looks like you get a little over 1 million lines outside the drivers.
That must be where they got the word "millions". Or else they read it in
and industry magazine or something.
