[News] Hong Kong urged to cut IT waste

HK urged to stop exporting e-waste 
Correspondents in Hong Kong
August 20, 2003
 

 THE environmental group Greenpeace called on Hong Kong to
 stop exporting electronic waste to China and other countries
 to prevent environmental pollution and health problems.

 "If the waste is not treated properly, large quantities of
 toxins such as mercury and lead are released into the
 environment and are hazardous to health," Greenpeace said. 

 The group said in a report last week that Hong Kong
 residents disposed of more than two million items of
 electronic waste per year, with used computers accounting
 for almost a quarter of the total.
