Re: Exchange replacement for Linux

On Thursday, 21 August 2003 at 16:50:47 +0930, Brad Wilson wrote:
> There has been some discussion regarding OpenGroupWare recently on this
> list.  I would like to open the discussion to the wider issue of
> Exchange replacement under Linux, a discussion that has been touched on
> only a few times on this list in the last few years.
>
> This is something that is of huge interest to me as I am looking for a
> way to migrate all of the services running on our Windows 2000 office
> server, of which there are many, across to Linux.  So far, I can
> implement all current services in an equally or far more effective way
> under Linux, with the exception of Exchange.  The reason for requiring
> an Exchange or "emulated" Exchange server is that most users in our
> office are heavy Outlook users and rely on the shared scheduling and
> public folders, etc, that Outlook/Exchange provides.  As those who check
> out email headers can see, even I have to use outlook so that I can be
> involved in office scheduling, etc.  So the one thing holding us back
> from giving our W2K server the boot is Exchange.
>
> I am aware of a few possible solutions, including:
> 	* Bynari InsightConnector/InsightServer (www.bynari.com)
> 	* SuSE Openexchange
> (http://www.suse.com/us/business/products/suse_business/openexchange)
> 	* Samsung Contact (www.samsungcontact.com)
