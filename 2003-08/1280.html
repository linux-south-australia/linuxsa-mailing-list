Hi Dan,
Computerbank exists to stop good hardware becoming landfill by making
usable systems out of it, installing the best operating system and
applications we can find. We then GIVE these systems away using
volunteer labour for the whole process. We offer training, we offer
hardware and software support all for free for as long as we can.

Feel free to talk to me personally about sorting out any problems your
friend has. We give out the best hardware and software we have
available.

If you feel that we are doing a really bad job, please come along and
help us build better systems. I am happy to personally work on the
system to fix this problem.

Now about why we usually use KDE, simply because most of our volunteers
are familiar with it and it is easy for Windows users to navigate.
Personally, I use fvwm on FreeBSD, but this is not about what I am
comfortable with, this is about doing the best we can for as many people
as possible.
