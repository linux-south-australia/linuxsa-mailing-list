> I wanted to write to thank Greg and Mark for helping me out with Mutt and emacs. Mutt is really fast. Will this be my first good Mutt email?

With a bit of work, maybe.  You need to set auto-fill-mode.  I've
already posted some macros IIRC.  If you end up with something like
the line above, you can save the day with:

1.  Separate it from the other lines with at least one empty line:

> spam spam spam
> spam spam spam
> spam spam spam

> I wanted to write to thank Greg and Mark for helping me out with Mutt and emacs. Mutt is really fast. Will this be my first good Mutt email?

> spam spam spam
> spam spam spam
> spam spam spam

2.  If it's a *single* line, and only then, add a second line '> '
    (i.e. a > at the beginning of the line, followed by a space):

> I wanted to write to thank Greg and Mark for helping me out with Mutt and emacs. Mutt is really fast. Will this be my first good Mutt email?
> 

    Don't do this if there is more than one line in the paragraph, or
    you'll end up with a > at the end of the last line.

3.  Position somewhere in this line and press m-q (alt is usually
    meta):

> I wanted to write to thank Greg and Mark for helping me out with
> Mutt and emacs. Mutt is really fast. Will this be my first good Mutt
> email?

Wonderful, isn't it?
