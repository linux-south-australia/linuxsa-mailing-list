RE: $1399USD per CPU for SCO's linux 'indemnity' license

This is getting silly!

I can't imagine a situation where SCO would win. However, in a sense, I hope
they do, and millions of Linux users around the world are legally forced to
pay this bizzare fee. Why? I think the people will revolt. I think we would
see businesses revolting. We'd see students, hobbyists, consultants,
businesses, and governments all agreeing that this is stupid, and promptly
ignoring the decision. Because SCO is drawing parallels with the RIAA, we
may even start to see those people come on board against SCO, and Linux
users against the RIAA (which we already are, I think). Maybe this could be
a precipitating event to demonstrate the irrelevance and wrongness of
current IP laws.

Can we build a campaign against this, incorporating things like:
  * SCO Linux claim
  * MS EULA
  * RIAA
  * Amazon one-click patent
  * US encryption export laws
  * DeCSS
  * "Unix" as a generic word...
  * Censorship
  * PS2/XBox etc modding

?

And could we win? Are these issues related enough? I think they are -- they
are all about personal freedoms with software and digital media. Could we
perhaps have a practical victory, even if not a legal one, and render these
laws irrelevant and unenforcable?
