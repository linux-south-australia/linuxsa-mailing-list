Re: squid ntlm/basic authentication

David de Groot wrote:

> On Tuesday, Aug 19, 2003, at 16:02 Australia/Brisbane, jonathan soong
> wrote:

>> I was wondering, in general, do proxies always support Basic, and
>> sometimes support NTLM?

[...]

> NTLM isn't really a particularly nice way to do authentication. Far
> better would be to use LDAP or RADIUS, modules for each exist for Squid
> and most commercial proxies also support this form of external
> authentication.

This depends on which side of the authentication you're doing - Basic
authentication *typically* occurs between proxy and browser, not proxy
and authentication service.  Likewise, RADIUS and LDAP occur between
proxy and authentication service, not proxy and browser.  NTLM can
occur on either leg of the link, but given Jonathan also uttered the
word "Basic", I'm presuming he meant the browser side of authentication.

The answer, Jonathan, is "it depends" - with Squid, this is all
configurable.  Given some other http proxy, probably less so.
