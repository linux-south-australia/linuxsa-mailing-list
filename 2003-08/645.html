Dogma stated that when we diverged from older life forms the fish genes we no
longer expressed for example were just turned off & became junk.  Many of these
genes are still turned on during foetal development even if briefly.  

Diversity allows evolution through natural selection, & gene duplication within
our genome allows individual genes to take on different functions.  We
therefore have numerous copies of very similar genes, often where only one of
the copies ever works (ie codes for the protein / enzyme etc).  These
pseudogenes often with corrupt code could be thought of as obsolete.  Below is
an example of a publication.

The human genome has 49 cytochrome c pseudogenes, including a relic of a
promordial gene that still functions in mouse. 
Z Zhang and M Gerstein. Gene (2003) 312:61-72.
