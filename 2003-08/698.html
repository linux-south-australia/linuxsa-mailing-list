Geoff,

     This sounds like your version of dump does not understand anything 
other than ext2.  There are some versions of dump that have that 
"feature".  I don't think they call it a bug either (:  What 
version(<1.0 ?) is your dump and more importantly what does your man of 
dump say it supports?  If it doesn't say it explicitly supports ext3 
then most likely it doesn't.  The easiest thing to do is to just use the 
-B functionality of dump.

dump 0jMf /path-to-backups/file-name  fs

This command creates a full multi-volume backup to files 
"/path-to-backups/file-name001 ... file-name002 ... file-name003 ...etc" 
and "j" in the options says to use bzip2 as it's compression.  You can 
do this with incremental backups too.
