Two very different uses for the same word.

AOL copied the concept of Forums from CompuServe at the time that 
CompuServe was the leader of online services. 80's to mid 90's.

Classic CompuServe (pre 96) had a 6 letter name ID (or longer but only 
the first 6 letters were significant) to get into a Forum, eg Pacific 
Forum used the GO word of PACFOR. To get into Pacific Forum using the 
proprietary software you would click on the GO button and enter PACFOR 
and this would be written GO PACFOR in adverts and communications. 

So AOL did a similar thing and called their section ID's Keywords. 
CompuServe now call their navigation words Keywords (since AOL bought 
them out a few years back) so their access words are now full name used 
like this, http://go.compuserve.com/pacific or 
http://go.compuserve.com/linuxforum

The Keyword isn't for Searching, it is to get into that forum/section 
on AOL.
