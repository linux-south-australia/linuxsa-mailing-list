Simple economics really. Do remember that Telstra are trying ever so 
hard to get their share price up.

Approximately 1-2% of Australians have broadband connections.

http://www.dcmtbarker.sa.gov.au/internet/services/demographics_growth.htm 
shows that Hahndorf is home to roughly 2000 people, and the exchange 
doesn't have the benefit of servicing multiple suburbs.

This means that the total broadband marketplace for Hahndorf at the 
moment is 20-40 people. Telstra probably have even better statistics 
based on profiling the phone bills of people in the area vs those with 
high broadband uptake. The same council stats suggest that Nairne would 
probably be a better candidate for getting DSL if you're going to enable 
marginal areas.
