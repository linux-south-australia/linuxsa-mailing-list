Re: BSD Licence vs GPL - AGAIN

Hmmm. Is there a licence that has the 'good' aspects of both BSD and 
GPL? i.e.

1) Requires source code inclusion with derived products
2) Ensures buyers/users of derived works have the same rights
3) but allows the 'original' [1] author to negotiate a closed source 
sale (to a particular vendor for their improvements only - i.e. sell a 
BSD style freedom to a party)...

Perhaps this would be a good compromise...

-Angus

[1] I guess this is a can of worms... ;-), based upon what we've been 
saying - how does one define 'original'? (the author of the x86 
instruction set, of course! Or ENIAC? I wonder where that was derived 
from - perhaps all source code is really owned by Babbage... or the 
inventor of decimal numbers, or positional notation... ;-) Hmmm. It 
seems like we're drawing a line in the sand...

> In theory, yes.  But in practice GPL advocates tend to defend the

> Which is really just another way of saying, "The Community has no stake

> The other thing it ignores totally is that -I- own the copyright in 
> modifications I make to GPL software... yet I am expressly prohibited

> the copyright in the modifications used to make those derived works,
> and I may not want to license that copyright under the GPL).  The GPL
> is the only license I can think of which exhibits that trait.
> What part of the GNU Manifesto or the ideals of the free software movement
> says that the property rights of an original licensor are more important
