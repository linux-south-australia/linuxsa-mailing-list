Re: Mystery of the dead keyboard -- did the mouse do it?

On Tue, Aug 05, 2003 at 10:42:24AM +0930, Dr Mark H Phillips wrote:
> 
> Do people concur with my conclusions?  Any ideas about whether it
> is the mouse or the motherboard circuitry?  I have access to another
> Microsoft "intelligent" mouse, so I could plug that in, but is it
> possible that this could damage the mouse (if the motherboard
> circuitry is dodgy)? 

Not possible to give a definitive answer. It's fairly unlikely but not
totally impossible. I'd try it, but then I've got loads of PS/2 mice :-)

> Are there any other tests, suggestions,
> fixes, etc people could suggest?

Real stabs in the dark:
- do you get raw mouse output in console mode? Can you boot to a
console, type 'cat /dev/psaux' and get a string of rubbish when you
move the mouse? If not, does echo -ne '\377' > /dev/psaux make any
difference?
- Have you got any screwed files in /etc, /etc/X11, /boot, /dev,
/lib/modules...
- Does wiggling the plugs make any difference? It's not impossible
that there's a bad connection whose intermittent faultiness has
coincided with your experiments in such a way as to suggest an
underlying but obscure logical pattern.
- You may be able to check if the mouse/keyboard themselves are
working by wrapping the cable around a small radio tuned in between
stations on medium or long wave, moving the mouse and seeing if it
makes funny noises on the radio. The false negative rate is undefined;
calibrate with a known-good setup first.
