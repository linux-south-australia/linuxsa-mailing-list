Re: Cable questions (RJ11, RJ12) and ADSL

On Sun, 2003-08-24 at 11:06, Brendan wrote:
> G'day,
> 
> Just have a few questions about cable types. I did some Googling and didn't
> come up with much. I found that RJ12 connectors allow 6 wires, and RJ11 4.
> And RJ45 8 i think?

Sounds about right.
 
> Anyway, i'm wondering, does it matter if the telephone cable i'm using has 4
> or 6 (or even 8 or some other number) wires for the ADSL-phone outlet link?
> How many wires does ADSL use?

It doesnt matter. ADSL uses your standard phone single pair, which are
the center two pins that line up which ever size socket you plug your
(modular connector equipped) phone line in to. Be careful though, a
ringing phone line can do nasty things to ethernet cards, and it will
fit in the socket.

> And on the JayCar website they have "3 Pair (6-wire) Telephone Cable -
> 100mt" for 90c. Surely the "100mt" doesn't mean 100 metres? If it does then
> that's a pretty good bargain, considering 10 metres at Coles with RJ12
> sockets costs like $20. And also, does anyone know what "3 Pair" means in
> regards to that?

100mt meants that it comes on 100 meter rolls. 90c is the per meter
price when your buying less than 100 meters. 3 pair means that there is
3 pairs of individual wires in the cable - 6 wire cable. If your making
your own cable for phone or adsl you only need the standard 2 pair (4
wire) cable. It may work out cheaper to just buy an extension cord
though.
