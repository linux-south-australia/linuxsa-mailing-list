Re: group scheduling followup

Paul Vinton wrote:

> Whilst I am not entirely happy with ISA server I also am not happy with
> Squid at the moment as they currently have interoperability issues with
> dynamic content, in particular .asp and .cfm sites (to name a couple
> that I have found).

Can you elaborate on the "interoperability issues"?  I've often found
problems with dynamic content and caching are due to the web server
not issuing the correct headers (e.g. pragma no-cache), and Microsoft's
proxy server reading a little too much implicit behaviour into certain
content _extensions_, instead of obeying the headers according to the
HTTP standard.  Certainly, you can instruct Squid to not cache .asp
and .cfm extensions, but that's not the correct way to do it.
