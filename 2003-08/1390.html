I went to microsoft.com using Mozilla, and couldn't see "Microsoft.com
guide" anywhere.  Then I searched the page for the string "guide".  Then I
searched the website code.  Neither of the two revealed any matches.

THEN I thought, what the hell, and loaded up Terminal Services and used
Internet Exploder 6.  Well, well.  Low and behold I was now presented with
a page that looked almost identical, except for one thing up in the top
right hand corner...

"microsoft.com Home" in Mozilla had been replaced with "Microsoft.com
Guide" in Internet Exploder.  In Mozilla, the link took me back to the
front page; in Internet Exploder, the link dropped down a menu of
additional choices unavailable to me on Mozilla.

In my case, Mozilla was unable to display these JavaScript menus correctly,
so I had no idea this hidden section of the Microsoft website existed.

Maybe it's Microsoft -- maybe they're not standards compliant -- maybe
they didn't bother to test their website on any platforms besides
Internet Exploder -- maybe they designed a seperate page which looks
similar for all non IE browsers (hence the "Guide/Home" swap) -- maybe they
just don't care.
