RE: Xwindows on an NT client

Running X on NT ...

At my client's site at the moment, we use XVision (From SCO) to do
Xwindows under NT.

In summary :
 - Slow as a wet week
 - Horribly complicated setup (big tabbed dialog with lots of buttons -
yuk)
 - $700  (corporate special pricing), is , uh, cheap !
 - Prone to crashing or loosing connections

I believe that there are some free X servers for NT that are even worse.

A good alternative is VNC - freely available from Olivetti/Oracle UK.
(check the web - there is plenty there)

It is a cross platform PC-Anywhere type thing, where you have a VNC
server on 
linux / solaris / win95 / NT, that accepts connections from other
machines. There
are clients available for all platforms - linux/solaris/AIX/Win*, even
PalmPilot !

I have a linux box & a 95 box at home, and I can run a remote win95
session in a 
big window on the linux box, or a linux session in a big window on the
95 box.

The Unix servers are much quicker, as they do more intelligent parsing
of the screen.

It is basically like those old DOS-TSR programs that poll the screen
contents rapidly
and zap a differences report to watching clients.

try it out anyway ... it's free.
