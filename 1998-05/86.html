I have an AWE64 which I am attempting to configure for sound. Perhaps we 
can share our experiences. I found out that running sndconfig is not enough.
I had to run isapnp to get a new isapnp.conf. Even then, the wavetable 
part is still not probed. I had to manually edit isapnp.conf and put in 
the 3 i/o port addresses as specified in the AWE manual; and set 
VERIFYLD N in isapnp.conf.
I am referring you to the Sound Blaster AWE32/64 HOWTO and the 
AWEDRV faq (http://bahamut.mm.t.u-tokyo.ac.jp/~iwai/awedrv/awedrv-faq.html)
I am currently preparing to recompile my kernel for SB AWE and the 
low-level awedrv (free time permitting....)

I can understand your frustration.....about the only sound I have heard 
from my AWE is the test speech (Alan Cox's voice?) at the end of 
sndconfig's run.

I too would be interested to know how to sort out the AWE from a Linux 
Sound guru out there.
Config is: i586-200MHz, 64MBSDRAM, RH5-2.0.31-7
