Re: [OT] IT Religions -- the ugly side...

Aristole,

Unless your experiences have been different to mine, I've generally
found the LinuxSA and most other Linux groups are very helpful.

You occasionally get people who like to tear down others, but
fortunately these are fairly uncommon.

Linux for desktop use (general computing for the masses) is only 3-4
years old. Microsoft and Apple have had 10 years or more to work on
their desktop usability. Since the desktop market is continually
evolving, competitors have to watch out for new applications opening,
and capture new territory. 

Today's savvy home users are editing DV video, creating high-quality
digital photo albums, playing high end 3D games, and creating DVD-R
movies of their kid's birthdays. 3 years ago I would have never have
thought this possible on a home computer. Linux should be able to catch
up reasonably well giving 2-3 years. For most other mundane stuff like
word processing, spreadsheets, and general productivity - well I think
it's getting pretty close especially with the expected delivery of
StarOffice 6

The fact that I'm using Linux as a top quality desktop OS, proves to me
that it's making real progress there already. Then again I could be
wrong.

Cheers,

Joseph Tan
