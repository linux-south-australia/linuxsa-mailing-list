Re: [OT] HDD Cont....

On Sat, 12 Jan 2002, JaSOn wrote:

> I am about to purchase a new HDD for my little computer. My hardware
> supports Ultra/66 and the HDD I am looking at is an Ultra/100.  I wanted
> to confirm the Ultra/100 is backward compatible to the Ultra/66 that my
> motherboard provides?

Yep, should be fine.

Note however, that I have an ata/100 capable motherboard (via kt266a) and
two drives. One is ata/66, and one is ata/100. I had to lock both to ata/66
in the bios before I could boot. But, they are both on the same chain. I'm
sure it'd be fine if i seperated them, except the other chain has two drives
too.

So, you might not want to put your new ata/100 or ata/66 drive on the same
chain as something LESS THAN ata/66. (ie. ata/33). It'll work, but dependant
onmotherboard it might run at the lower speed.

>
> Some of the more common brands that have appeared to me are Maxtor,
> Samsung, Fujitsu, West. Dig, Seagate etc I have had good use of the
> smaller Maxtor and Seagate HDDs and have preferred the Seagate because
> as the HDD have aged, the Maxtor's have become quite noisy in
> comparison. I have also been warned by some about the decrease in
> Western Digital's quality recently.

Seagate, Fujitsu and IBM have all provided me with long life and few
problems. Maxtor and WD have given me many more problems, so I avoid them
now. Samsung, well, i dunno. I haven't tried buying one to find out.
