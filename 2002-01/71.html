Re: upgrading redhat 6.2 to 7.2

Michael Kratz wrote:

> Hi all, I have several 'live' servers that I am running redhat 6.2 on at the
> moment, I am thinking about upgrading them to redhat 7.2... so I pose this
> question, what is the best way to do this, do an over the top upgrade, ie.
> let the install upgrade everything on the system, or format and redo
> everything, which I can do.


I recently upgraded a Red Hat server from 6.2 -> 7.1 -> 7.2.  Many 
components (including samba, apache, printing, and X) required 
configuration changes, and inn (a news server) was broken beyond repair. 
  Unfortunately Red Hat upgrades do not always go smoothly!  You'll also 
notice that some things still look like 6.2 unless you apply Red Hat's 
new configuration files or nuke the old ones (such as GNOME).

On a desktop system I would suggest a reinstallation, but you'll have to 
decide what's best for your servers.  (I would *strongly* recommend you 
perform a backup before attempting an upgrade!)

Something else I've learned from previous upgrades is: it's important to 
check that any third-party applications work on the new system. 
Anything you've installed from anything other than an RPM (source, 
tarballs, and so on) may also need to be upgraded separately.

I hope this helps. :-)
