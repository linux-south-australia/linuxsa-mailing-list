> any idea on the advantage of using ext3 over reiserfs or vice versa?
>

Basically ext3 offers the ability to gracefully, in theory and in practice
(at least so far), recover from a failure.  This includes, if necessary,
accessing the FS per ext2 when ext3 fails.  As far as I know Reiser can't do
this and if it becomes corrupt then you are screwed.  However, reiser is a
faster FS than ext3 (but not by very much).
