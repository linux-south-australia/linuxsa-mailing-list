Re: PC Authority: Linux as a Desktop is dead

On Fri, 2002-01-18 at 14:49, David Fitch wrote:

> Another alternative may be OpenOffice - the successor to StarOffice.

Er, no. OpenOffice is the open-source development wing for StarOffice.
Once Sun are happy where OpenOffice is, they haul in the code, stick a
bunch of Sun logos everywhere and call it StarOffice. Sun have no plans
(that I'm aware of) to kill StarOffice and replace it with OpenOffice.

> Another list I'm on there's a bloke who's a developer for openoffice
> and he reckons it's much much better than staroffice in all respects
> but especially stability, simplicity and compatibility with the M$
> office stuff.

But it's a bit of an unfair comparison. OpenOffice builds are
practically snapshots from a semi-stable CVS tree. You're likely to get
all the latest features (as you'd expect from a CVS version) in
OpenOffice. Expect to see at least some of the improvements and
enhancements merged back into StarOffice 6.0. Moreover, as a developer,
it's likely that your acquaintance is running an even more bleeding-edge
version.
