Bryce,

I've been looking at both ,and currently have both RedHat 7.2 and Mandrake 
8.1 on my box. 

I would have to say that Mandrake is the way to go for new users and even 
users who just want a Linux system to 'do desktop work'. I believe these 
reasons make it stand out:

- - Software Manager makes installing and managing your applications RPMs a 
breeze.

- - The Mandrake community forum website (www.mandrakeforum.com) and Mandrake's 
free online resources (www.mandrakecampus.com). Both of these are really good 
sources of information in case you haven't got close friends using Linux.

Overall Mandrake feels much more polished as a desktop OS. The distribution 
does have it's downside though, the big one is some of it's buggy 
applications.

If Linux still feels too hard right now, hang on for another 6 months when 
the next round of linux distribution arrives. I think it's getting much 
better every 6 months.
