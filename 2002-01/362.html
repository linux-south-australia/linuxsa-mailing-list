If you speak of the 'Advanced filesystem implementor's guide' at
DeveloperWorks, then it is quote a useful series of nine articles about    
Linux filesystems.  The URLs are below:
  
* The benefits of journalling and ReiserFS
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/library/l-fs.html
* Setting up a ReiserFS system
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/library/l-fs2.html
* Using the tmpfs virtual memory filesystem and bind mounts
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/library/l-fs3.html
* The benefits of devfs, the device management filesystem
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/library/l-fs4.html
* Beginning the conversion to devfs
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/library/l-fs5.html
* Completing the conversion to devfs using an init wrapper
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/linux/library/l-fs6/
* The benefits of the ext3 filesystem
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/linux/library/l-fs7/
* Ext3 and the latest kernel updates
  http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/linux/library/l-fs8.html

The latest article 
(http://www-106.ibm.com/developerworks/linux/library/l-fs9.html) is all 
about XFS.  It also contains a comparison of XFS, ReiserFS, and ext3, 
which may be useful.
