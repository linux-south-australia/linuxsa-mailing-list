Re: Super Block

Close..
the ext2/3 system is a tree structure, and the superblock is the block at
the beginning.. Contains info like the volume name, last mounted position,
uuid, magic number, filesystem flags, state, inode, block, etc. counts,
block, fragment, group, etc. sizes, last mount/written times, number of
times mounted since last check, check intervals in times and mounts, and so
on and so forth.


It's not like the FAT table though -- the FAT table contained chains of
allocated clusters, all in one big table.
The ext2/3 filesystem will store that sort of info in the directory entry
inodes instead.

Features like that, and distributed replicated superblocks, and later on,
journalling, are some of the reasons why ext2 and now ext3 has been much
more resilient to harddisk corruption and crashes.

FAT16 and FAT32 were an "all your eggs in one basket" kind of deal.
There was a second backup FAT table, but it was placed immediately after the
first version, so anything that corrupted the first table was likely to
damage the second as well. Also, there was absolutely no checksumming done,
so even if you could read both copies of the FAT, you didn't know which one
was right, and which one was wrong!
