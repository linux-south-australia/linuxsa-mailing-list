Re: Laptops, Linux and Compact Flash

I used a PCMCIA CF card reader in my compaq laptop for years.  For me, it worked like a charm.  

Things you will want to do:
 - use the independant pcmcia tar ball from sourceforge
 - make sure your CF card is already formatted (put it in your camera first)
 - dont eject the card without first unmountint it.  Very important.  The kernel does not handle
it well when a mounted filesystem *in use* goes away.

Watch /var/log/messages too.  It may be that the card is inserted OK, but the module is not loaded
right.  In this case, you can load it yourself "insmod ide_cs" or ide-cs.  This mixup of names
came about because the pcmcia install scripts (from RH I think) did not match what the pcmcia
tarball was generating.

Once the module is loaded (check with insmod) you can then mount it by hand:
mount /dev/hdc1 /mnt/cf -t vfat

hdc1 (or at least hdc) will appear in /var/log/messages after insmoding hte module or inserting
the card if *that* part is working.

I would guess (hope) that the latest versions of the RH pcmcia scriptshave fixed this bug.
