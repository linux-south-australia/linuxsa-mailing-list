Re: Blocking Spammers.

Here's what I have in place:

1. Select an ISP that uses the spam "Blacklist", that way you don't
even have to download the rubbish.  
2. For posting to usenet (the internet news system), I have my reader
set to a bogus reply address. VERY IMPORTANT - a major source of spam.
3. For surfing the net, I have given netscape a bogus username plus I
use an account on my machine that does not match anything at my ISP.
4. Subscribe only to trustable mailing lists.
5. Where I subscribe to a less than trustworthy mailing list, I'm
using one of the free e-mail aliases provided by my ISP, if that were
to fall into the hands of the spammers, I would just blow it away and
subscribe again with a new one.

... and some observations
I get very few spams, perhaps one per month. Its not clear which of
the above is working but I suspect that it is the first item. If so, I
will never know to what extent the remaining strategies contribute.
So far, I haven't had to use item number 5.

Thanks Toby, I like the sound of ricochet (no pun intended). As my
strategy appears to work due to the blacklist system, I feel that I
should contribute to keeping the list up to date.  Would ricochet do
that?

>There are various solutions.. One is to always give out your email as
>realusername+fnork@your.isp.com.au
>
>This way, once you start receiving spam on that email address, you can
>filter it into a seperate folder, and start posting as
>realusername+spamMeNot@your.isp.com.au, then
>realusername+spamboozled@your.isp.com.au, etc.
>
>Another method is to customise your email server, so that whenever you
>receive email, it checks to see if any of the servers in the headers are
>open relays, and if so it dumps the email and sends an automated reply to
>the admins to fix it.
>
>Personally though, I just give out addresses at mydomain usually, based on
>the source company.
>
>Eg. ebaycomau@mydomain.net, redhatcom@mydomain.net, msnspam@mydomain.net,
>etc.
>
>This way if i start getting spam sent to one of those addresses, I know
>which company was responsible for selling my address on, and can also block
>it totally.
>
>Also, I have ricochet installed, which is an automated anti spam tool. You
>just hit | in pine to pipe the email to ricochet, and it figures out the
>sources, the open relay, etc., looks up the admins from whois info and
>another database i forget, and then emails all the people who need to know.
>
