Re: Laptops, Linux and Compact Flash

On Tue, Jan 15, 2002 at 04:13:15AM +1030, Jay wrote:
> Hi there,
> 
> Has anyone had any experience with linux, laptops and compact flash cards? I
> have PCMCIA support and my ethernet card works fine. When I try 'cardctl
> status 0' it reports the CF card as a "3.3v 16bit PC Card", sounds like it
> noticed the card is in the slot, but the next line says "function 0: [busy],
> [bat dead], [bat low]" Any atempt to identify the card using 'cardctl ident
> 0' causes the laptop to hang. Ejecting and then reinserting the card causes
> "cs: socket 0 timed out on reset." Any suggestions would be great.
> 
> Jay

If you are using PCMCIA support in the kernel it can still be a bit flaky.
David Hinds modules available from http://pcmcia-cs.sourceforge.net/
are a better bet. There have been a couple of bug fix releases recently,
so it's worth a look.
