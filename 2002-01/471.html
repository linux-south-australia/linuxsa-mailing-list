Re: evolution 1.0.x probs/webmail

On Tue, 2002-01-22 at 17:50, ant wrote:

> Ive been running evolution since v0.13 and have found it pretty reliable
> (in later times anyway). However I think I've damaged the data files with
> all my mail in them as the mail component dies after evolution has been
> running about 10-15 seconds. Ive rmed everything out of ~/evolution/config
> and ~/evolution/local/Inbox/*/*.xml (all the metadata and summary files
> which I thought  could be recreated), but it still dies not long after
> coming up. Fortunatly I got this webmail going today so I've been using
> that as a backup :) Any ideas about where to look for whats going wrong,
> or any software that'll do a check over my mail files (mbox) and fix
> anything too wierd about them?

Try deleting the mbox.ibex files in each of your folders. Restart
Evolution, and it should rebuild the summary files. That *may* fix your
problem -- it's helped me in the past.

Note that rebuilding your indexes may take a while. It took a PIII-500
about 3 minutes to rebuild my indexes, but that was when my ~/evolution/
folder was about ~180MB.

> These mail problem first started occuring under evolution 1.0.0 and an
> upgrade to 1.0.1 didnt fix them :(

Did it just start happening all-of-a-sudden, or was there a precursory
event (such as a dirty shutdown to Evolution when it was writing to the
mail store)? 

> Also, in regards to my request for wemail reccomendations the other day -
> squirrelmail was simple to set up and exactly what I wanted :)

Yeah, I found SquirrelMail OK for a while, but its interface sucks, and
it didn't seem to cope with quoting particularly well. That said, it was
