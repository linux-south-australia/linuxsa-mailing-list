RE: Big Pond Direct (was: [OT] Telstra ADSL Price Increase)

I'm scratching my head here. There is very little that depends on 
static IP addresses in the modern world. The Internet runs on the 
DNS, not on static IP associations. People don't find your web site 
by IP address in the modern world.

One just plans the move via a careful sequence of DNS entry changes 
and its all seamless (lower the DNS TTL the day before; Bring up the 
new IP's; Change the DNS entries; Raise the DNS TTL again once the 
move is verified to be clean and ok).

All decent ISP's can do this for their customers. The only 
organisations that consistently seem to botch such moves are banks 
(they must think they know better). Not one, but multiple banks have 
botched a change of ISP's (and then tried to blame ISP's for their 
lack of appreciation of how the DNS works, and their choice not to 
hire someone with nous to manage the move).

Other than that, ISP moves/adds/changes are coordinated and executed 
daily all over the world with nobody being adversely affected, when 
they're done right - whether that's for one IP address range or lots. 
Even lots of globally routed IP's can be ported cleanly by using a 
good NAT gateway (which allows a customer to do an ISP change and 
then gradually move their IP's over later if that works better for 
them than a snap change).

Anyway, Michael, you seem to be saying below that on the one hand 
moving is easy, but on the other hand for you personally its hard, 
for some unspecified reason. You've asked my company to make you an 
offer. No sweat, drop me a line privately and explain what sort of 
offer you need. Cos I just can't work it out from your public post 
(maybe that's just because its late and I'm tired... :) )

Regards,
Simon
