>transfer quantities. I'm also willing to accept a dynamic IP, and
>pay-per-incident for tech support. (For non-isp related problems.. ie. i
>don't want ot have to pay $5/min just to tell them their router at
>xx.xx.xx.xx is dropping packets or misconfigured or whatever..)

Pay-per-incident tech support isn't practical in Australia. If an ISP
refuses to fix a user's problem on the grounds that the user won't pay the
support cost, then the user can complain to the TIO.

This in turn costs the ISP something like $60 to have the compaint resolved,
even if the user is completely in the wrong.

In other words, it's not worth the hassle to do pay-per-incident support.
