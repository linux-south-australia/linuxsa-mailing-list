Re: Request for tips on keeping distros up-to-date

I try to stick to the RPMs if "they" (any author) make it easy enough. I
try and install them, and if they get the dependancy checks wrong in the
RPM and I know better I give them a --nodeps. If the RPM versions are
behind the source (as often the case with apps where the author brings
out the source tar.gz, and rely on volunteers to create the RPMS, or
sometimes something else about them puts me off I get the source and just
compile and install it over the top, and dont tell the RPM system :).
Most good rpms seem to check for library files on disk rather than the
librarys rpm itself so I havnt had any problems with this mixture. I still
use the evolution RPMS though and have replaced several mandrake RPMS with
their ximian equivalents with no adverse effect.
