Re: Requirements for NAT?

Tim,

I have freesco running on an old 486 (with the optional hd) and was using las 
(lite account system) to collect stat about my traffic and show it.  Won't 
actively stop exceeding you limit but at least you can look at your usage.

try: http://www.freescosoft.com/home/v0.2.7/3rdparty/g-l.htm

There was/is a Windoze add-on that interrogates las and provides progressive 
daily and month totals/graphs - I'm pretty sure there is a linux version.

Btw - I'm looking at moving off freesco to either e-smith, smoothwall or 
mandrake's firewall offering (all need at least a pentium and a hd)as these 
seem to provide better reporting of probes plus some other useful features.

Having said that, freesco has been v reliable and allowed an old 486 to be 
gainfully employed in its 'retirement'.  Also, there is a fairly active 3rd 
party s/w development group for freesco add-ons as you can see from the 
supplied url.  As to security - try probing it, once installed, via 
http://www.grc.com - it seems pretty good considering its default installation.

Cheers,

TonyR
