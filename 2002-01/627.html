Re: Sawfish not running

This may sound a little drastic Sue, but if you don't really have
anything on the screen, it may be worth moving the .gnome and .sawfish
directories in your home directory to something completely different
(like .sawfish.old and .gnome.old or simial) and then starting again.

This should leave you with a default sawfish setup.

To do this (I'm assuming you don't have a terminal to play with), press
Ctrl+Alt+F1 to get you to a console.  Login with your username and
password and ls -la.  Now, if you don't have a .sawfish directory, then
this is a kinda pointless exercise.

Let me know how you go....
