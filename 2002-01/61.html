RE: Multi List Address Book

Tim,

I grabbed Sylpheed Claws last night and I'm test driving it over the next
few days.

One thing that could be a big set back is that the address book in Slypheed
right now can't import data. I looked at the XML file, and I guess someone
may have already written an import script.

I've been using KMail over the past weeks and it's quite good, particularly
with it's integration with GNUPG and it's data import function.

Another issue is that on the desktop aesthetic side I'd like everything to
be as standardised as possible. Being a KDE user, I'm also trying to look
for QT-based apps. This is important for cut 'n paste (with something else
already highlighted), and drag 'n drop support inbetween apps.

Cheers,

Joseph Tan

In the immortal words of John Hall <jmh@senet.com.au>...

> Joseph Tan wrote:
> I am trying Sylpheed which is better than Kmail - it handles multiple
> lists and sub-lists in its address book, but you still have to select
> addresses manually (although Shift-click on the first and last name of
> a sub-list works).

Sylpheed works rather well, although I'm using the "bleeding edge" version
sylpheed-claws.  Lots of very nifty stuff and definately better than the
other mail clients I've tried (Balsa, Pine, Mutt, Evolution, Netscape,
Mozilla, etc) for my purposes.  Especially the recent LDAP, GPG and Pspell
additions.

One note, you can make your own mailing lists by defining an entry in the
addressbook, and adding multiple email addresses to it.
