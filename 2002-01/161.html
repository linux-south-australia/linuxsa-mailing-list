Solaris x86 as an advertisement for Open Source

Here's a few interesting comments from a Solaris mailing list that may
be interesting to some people.

> > Sadly, those days are long gone.  I don't think I've filed 
> a software 
> > bug report with Sun in a decade because I grew tired of performing 
> > exhaustive tests, sometimes *giving them* the fix to the 
> source (which 
> > I could do because we had a source license) plus the fix to 
> the manual 
> > page plus all the test results before/after....and having it all 
> > apparently disappear into a black hole.
> > 
> 
> If this is the way it is, why does anybody wonder why Linux 
> is more popular?  At least with Linux, you get ALL the 
> source, and you are guaranteed future improvements by the 
> license.  This seems like a classic example of how the 
> proprietary source model has truly failed.... no matter how 
> good the OS is or was, everyone who recommended it now has 
> egg on their faces because Sun has betrayed them.  I do not 
> think "betrayed" is too strong a word.  This has definitely 
> lowered my estimation of Sun as a company.  What good is 
> support like you mentioned above?  It deserved to die.  How 
> much is such support worth?  $0. in my estimation. 
> 
> As someone who formerly worked for a proprietary software 
> company, I have seen bugfixes get swept under the rug, and 
> it's not pretty. Upper management can kill a product by such 
> foolish decisions. Maybe Sun's reputation is merely based on 
> nostalgia.  I certainly couldn't recommend any of their 
> servers, knowing that they could 
> kill a product at any time on the basis of market conditions.
