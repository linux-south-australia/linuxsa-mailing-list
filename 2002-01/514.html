AMD bug affects some Linux users

For those of you who have not yet read The Register or Slashdot news or 
have not been listening, there is a bug in some AMD chips that could 
affect Windows and Linux users.

Here are some extracts from what they said on the Register;

http://www.theregister.co.uk/content/3/23749.html

"Affected users will need to be running a 2.4 kernel, an AGP and an 
Athlon or Duron CPU. At cause is a compatibility issue with Intel’s 4MB 
page size. (x86 processors have used 4k pages, ever since paging was 
introduced in the 80386). 
"

"For Linux users, it's a one line workaround, too. Pass the 
line “append mem=nopentium” at boot time (via your boot loader of 
choice), to disable all Pentium specific instructions. "

Hope this helps.  Ian
