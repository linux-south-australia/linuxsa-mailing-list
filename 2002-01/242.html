Re: Super Block

I may be wrong in the technicalitys but I belive it is the filesystem
info. Eg file names, filename to inode lookups and inode locations on
disk. Like your FAT table (File Allocation Table table?) in a FAT file
system. The good thing about multiple copys all over your disk are, say
the first half of your partition gets blown away. You can use an
alternate superblock to recover the last half of your files, or vice
versa etc. How many you have and exactly whats in them depends on your
filesystem.

Ant
