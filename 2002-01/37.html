Re: mail relaying

mark dickeson wrote:

> Ok, I had a look in /var/logs/maillog and found:
> 
> Dec 31 07:39:39 marvin sendmail[468]: fBUL9Yu00468: ruleset=check_rcpt,arg1=<jack@4-vendor.com>, relay=[65.164.43.91], reject=450
> 4.7.1 <jack@4-vendor.com>... Relaying temporarily denied. Cannot resolve PTR record for 65.164.43.91
> Dec 31 07:39:40 marvin sendmail[468]:  from=<shmaggle22@4vendor.net>, size=0, class=0, nrcpts=0, proto=ESMTP,
> daemon=MTA, relay=[65.164.43.91]
> 
> So does this mean that jack@4-vendor.com tried to relay, but was
> denied; and that shmaggle22@4vendor.net tried and got through?

No - the sendmail processing id "fBUL9Yu00468" ties these two entries
together, and shows that it "temporarily" failed (450) due to name
server problems.  You have two addresses because one is the recipient
and the other is the sender.  I'd say from the size=0 in the second
line that sendmail didn't even get past the session setup (ie, actually
send the data portion of the email) before the other end put the kibosh
on the whole transmission (due to the failure).  

Interesting that the IP addresses for mail.4-vendor.com, mail.4vendor.net
and your above relay IP all fall in the same (class C) subnet.  It
appears to be just another spammer testing for an open relay before
using it, because the recipient appears suspiciously close to the sending
IP address.

> I had a look in /etc/sendmail.cf, /etc/sysconfig/sendmail,
> /etc/rc.d/init.d/sendmail and /etc/mail/sendmail.mc for mention of the
> other log, 

Sorry, what other log?  The syslog log is usually the only one that
keeps track of these things.

> so I'm not sure what happened on the outbound side of things.

Outbound is definitely kept in the same log.  If it's not there,
it didn't happen.
