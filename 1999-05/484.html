questions - thorns amongst roses

	As a newbie-newbie I have to make a point.  I have been put off
Linux for some of the things that I've read on this list and, seriously, the
lack of support to anyone new to Linux. I mean there is no one to talk to
about it at the beginning, as a newbie in the sense similar to what a MS
newbie is, only stuff you can read and for real boffins (no offence intended
here) this may be OK but not for a lot of up and coming newbies. Linux is
getting a real plug at the "am new to computer OS's altogether" end of the
market (that not being me though). Sorry but Linux is not easy to get used
to in comparison to other OSs.  The beauty of Linux is that once you master
it, it is very, very good and efficient and fast and that is why so many
newbies get attracted to it.  I don't have a problem with categorising as
long as I can come to grips with Linux.  But if someone asks a question, I
don't think the answer should be posted on the list but directly to the
person who asked.  If anyone else wanted to know they would have asked also.
This will minimise the incredible amount of stuff floating on this list
which I suspect is mostly what's getting up everyone's noses - seeing the
same answer over and over again and not necessarily seeing the same question
over and over again.
