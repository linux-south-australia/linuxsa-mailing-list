FYI: Coldfire and PPPTP ports @ http://www.mortonbay.com

Hi folks.

For those of you that didn't see this last week and are interested in Linux/GNU ports and MS ringbarking. Here is a little item I pulled out of  Computer World of 14/5/99 http://computerworld.idg.com.au/ regarding the web appliances that MS wanted to control via NT servers.  Seems that  a Bob Waldie of http://www.mortonbay.com/ and a few other "clever" Ausies have managed to remove the necessity for MS to be in the loop.

They have ported a uClinux microkernel (http://ryeham.ee.ryerson.ca/uClinux/) onto the Motorola Coldfire processor (http://www.mot.com/SPS/HPESD/index.html) . The Coldfire is a derivative (cutdown) of the 68K chip, that implements a "variable length RISC architecture". The project results (so far) can be reviewed at http://www.mortonbay.com/coldfire/coldfire-linux.html.

This same group have also ported to Linux (of course) the "server code for PPPT"(P) (http://www.mortonbay.com /?? probably PPPTP/ - lost connection) which is MS's entry into the VPN "security space". Chip. Chip. Chip. These 2 ports remove the necessity for NT servers in the communications between web appliances and browsers.

Cheers Terry
