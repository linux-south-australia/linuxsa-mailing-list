Re: Redhat i686 or i585 cds

I think what he was asking about was not just the kernel, but all of RH6.0
(apps included).

Tim, myself, i doubt if RedHat would even consider distributing 5 or more
different versions of RH6.0 for different iX86 platforms.... it would cost
them too much.

The best thing I can sugest is to go download the source for egcs (about
12Mb) and then the PentiumGCC patch for egcs. Then after about 1.5hrs of
compiling egcs you will be able to compile all prog sources optimised for
your CPU. This, of course means you will have to get the source tars for new
apps rather than just getting the RPMS.

So far, im not sure how much speed is gained by using PGCC, but it would be
at least 25% in most apps (pure speculation) as the orig gcc only optimised
for the i386 or i486.

Mark Williams
