Re: questions - thorns amongst roses

The only problem with only replying off-list is that nobody else knows who
answered the question, so if another non-pro asked the same questions later
on, it does happen, they would have to ask again and hopefully get an
answer.

Okay so they would still have to ask, but they could be told, "Well that was
answered approx <enter date>, have a look at http://<;some url> and see if
that helps, if not, let us know."

Which is more helpful than "Well this was answered, we think, not sure, umm
lookup a similar question from <enter date>, by looking at http://<;some
url>, email the person that asked the question, see if they got an answer
and ask them to give it to you, along with any other info they have
gathered. See if they can help you resolve the problem, as they probably
should/would have, and if not then come back and tell us all of this info
and we shall see if we can help".

Which seems easier. Personally, I love seeing all the answers to all
questions posed, whether pro questions or non-pro question. The answers
usually have small gems, and sometime massive diamonds of information.

The short version: Answers off list, help 1 person, on list, help everyone
(whether they need it or not!).

As I have said before, pro's are non-pro's in areas where they are not pro's
(should I be a politician or what with this answer???)

Natalia, I hope you learn all the information you need from this list, there
are many wonderful people on it with the answers you need, but just like
anywhere, there are people who have a question but will not ask it for fear
of looking stupid.

Should they be penalised for not asking the question in the first place ????

If you are new to Linux, welcome.

Rob
