Re: hard drive control

Conrad Gittins wrote:

 > Hi,  a perfectly good hard drive sounds like it may be crashing the
 > heads when run under linux.  It isn't loud like serious head crashing is
 > but does sound suspicious.  Normal head action is always audible from
 > drives but a pronounced click comes from this one. 

If you had a head crash the drive would be totally destroyed (friction
has a nasty tendency to vaporize the tip of the read/write heads).

 > Does anyone have
 > particular experience with hard drives and does linux control drives is
 > a different way to windows?

It drives the disk a lot harder than Windows, which is why it sounds
different.  You're getting more efficient use of the disk from Linux's
filesystem, so there is less "idle time", and the disk can be busier.
