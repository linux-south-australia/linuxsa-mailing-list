Naked PC's... only two options?!? (not)

Someone sent this to a friend of mine... apparently it comes from MS
(the non-MS comments are in [brackets] below):


"6. Naked PCs?

Can we talk for a moment about Naked PCs?
That is, PCs sold without operating systems preinstalled.

  [Sounds like PC as component to me;
  leaving me free to choose other components
  that best meet the client's requirements]

It's how some short-sighted system builders are
selling PCs these days.

  [Sounds like the <ad homiom> fallacy;
   indicating the debater's arguments
   have run out...]

The problem is that selling "naked" means
selling your customers a problem, not a solution.

  [An informed customers gets more freedom;
   freedom to make intelligent system choices]

It leaves them only two choices:

 - acquire a more expensive operating system elsewhere or,

    [Did somebody forget FreeBSD, Linux, et al. here?]

 - unfortunately, obtain one illegally.

   [Fallacy of hiding important information]

Don't expose your valuable reputation.

  [What then?  Insult the intelligence of even well-informed clients?]

Sell your PCs fully equipped-with legally licensed operating systems
preinstalled.

  [Plural? What if just one - e.g. FreeBSD or Linux - would suffice?]

Otherwise, who knows what you're leaving your customers open to?"

  [Open-minded... Free to Choose...]
