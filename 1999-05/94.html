The Ultimate Solution to the Newbie problem

> While I am as guilty as the next person as far as asking a quick question
> because I am too lazy to check the HOWTOS, it does raise an interesting
> point ...
> 
> At which level does a question pass from one layer of complexity to
> another,
> and who should be the judge of which layer a question belongs to ?
> 
> May I suggest that we define 7 layers of experience (just like the 7
> layers
> of the OSI model, the 7 days of the week, the 7 members of the G7, the 7
> horsemen of the apocalypse, etc).
> 
> Questions may only be posted & read from the layer that the postee belongs
> to 'And they were alllowed to post their questions, neither above nor
> below
> their place', with very top layer reserved for only a select few - Linus,
> Alan Cox, etc.
> 
> In order to pass from one layer to another, we could impose a strict set
> of
> guidelines, maybe requiring a weekend of exhaustive mental and physical
> testing out in some secluded bush retreat, culminating in a midnight
> graduation ceremony at torchlight for the few survivors. Before the rising
> statue of the penguin, they could then be applied with the secret mark of
> the new level, and be sworn into their new fraternity.
> 
> Whilst this would be extremely cool, it creates a problem in that a
> question
> at one level may in fact have the answer somewhere else. Unfortunately,
> experience is not a linear scale ... there may be some total assembly
> language kernel hacker guru who has no idea how to setup KDE, or there
> could
> be someone that knows every possible thing about Samba and PPP but who
> could
> not write a perl script to save their pet cat.
> 
> Maybe a better idea would be to have 1 list for 'Linuxsa_questions' and
> the
> other for 'linuxsa_news', if you must split the list.
> 
> SteveOC
