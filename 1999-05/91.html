I've been swung - RE: Newbie vs Pro list

I think that the teacher from south pak put it best:-  "There are no stupid
questions, only stupid people."

I'm joking.  :)

Reading all these posts has been mightily interesting.

This discussion is perhaps brought about by some confusion as to what the
list is actually designed for..  I believe Mr Tippett has summed it up for
us nicely.

Also, on thinking about it, how does a newbie know what a newbie question
is?  They might consider themselves newbies, but know a hell of a lot more
answers than someone who professes to be a pro.  Or the question may be one
that the pros have never considered.

And, as someone else said, the pros can always simply ignore the question.
Not hard.

So yeah, perhaps we should be a little more understanding of the people
behind more straightforward questions.

But.. gad.. HTML emails from overseas addresses that say nothing more than
"I can't get X to work, has anyone had any experience with this?" get on my
nerves.  But I get over it and delete them.  :)

PS  I've been tossing around the words "newbie" and "pro" a lot..  please
append each mention of them with " for want of a better word"  :)

*sigh*  Back to work :(
