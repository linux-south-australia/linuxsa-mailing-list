Re: PRO: (c-code)writing and reading from pipes

On Tue, 18 May 1999, Jake Hawkes wrote:
> ok, two progs (proc A and procB) want to communicate with each other via
> a pipe, or pipes. The communications is to be 2-way. 

I believe FIFOs are unidirectional, so you will need two of them.

  ProcA
    pipe1 = open("/tmp/rip_pipe1", O_RDONLY);
    pipe2 = open("/tmp/rip_pipe2", O_WRONLY);

Further, I believe you cannot open a FIFO for writing unless a process
currently already has it open for reading.  And when the process which
opened it for writing closes it, the process which has it open for reading
gets an EOF from further reads.

I recommend that you use a named socket instead of a FIFO.
