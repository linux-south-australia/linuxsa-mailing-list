> What Toby is saying (albeit a little bit bluntly) is that
> Computers are NOT Cars & LinuxSA is NOT Ultra Tune.
> LinuxSA IS here to help solve problems but saying "it's making
> a bang bang clunk noise", is not going to help.
>
> If you ask a question you must provide information to help
> people solve it.
>
> If it is a low memory issue then include info from utils
> such as 'free' and 'top'. OS and Distribution details.
> When the problem started, any affecting factors etc...
>
> Also, when ever you have a problem the two key things to do
> before coming to a support group like LinuxSA is RTFM and
> upgrade to the latest stable release (if possible).
>
> If you don't know where the "M" in RTFM is, then ask. No one
> would have a problem in pointing you in the right direction.
>
> In your particular case John, it would be useful to have info
> from 'free' and 'top'. Also...
> What is the machine doing?
> What services are running on the machine?
> How much RAM & SWAP?
> Why are you still using V4.2?
> etc...
>
> If you can provide this info it would help us help you.
