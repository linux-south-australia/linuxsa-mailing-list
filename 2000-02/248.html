Re: Your Daily Dose of Microsoft Reality #3

One thing to consider when reading such articles is to realize that despite 
criticism, IT WORKS!!!!...

The average person who surfs the net has very VERY little technical 
knowledge..   Therefore, what you and i know for sure is a dose of 
propaganda, the public public will precieve as a dose of reality and get 
drunk with it.
I have to admit that I almost fell for their argument the first moment I 
read it since I didn't have my morning coffee, yet.

But for those of you who may be in that situation, consider an example from 
the Microsoft progaganda:

>Sun claims to be a leader in system reliability and more reliable >than 
>Windows. The Reality: Major customers, such as Quote.com, are switching 
>from Sun to the >Microsoft Windows platform because it offers better 
>reliability.

The above states MS realization of Sun's claims about reliability and could 
not provide a solid sientific proof.  To say that Company1 runs windows so 
does company2 is not a proof of windows reliability over Sun's systems..  I 
have not heard ONE computer science professor complement windows in ANY 
aspect.  I personally will have to admit that Windows have come ways with 
their system to handle networking, but that system IS an incremental model 
from their original MS-DOS operating system.  The same problems they had in 
the past are still a daily challenge to winNT Lan admins.  A reboot on the 
fly has become a sickening philosophy despite the user frienly interface 
strategies for MS.
If you are not sure what to think of such doses of reality, please, show it 
to someone who is a little more achademic than the writer of the article 
itself.
Nour
