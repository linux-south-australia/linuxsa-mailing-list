David Drury wrote:

> In general, if you put an inadequetly powered SUN server under a heavy
> load it will fail (example Adelaide Uni Student Email server). If you put
> a Win NT station under a light load it will run well for a long time (many
> departmental secretaries reboot their Win NT work stations less than once
> a month).
> 
> I think I'd still prefer to have any form of non Win NT server for mission
> critical applications.

I used to run a few NT servers with heavy loads and varied internet
applications, and I could always get better than 3 month uptimes out of
them. The problem was having to always tiptoe around any configuration,
and needing a reboot whenever something was changed. To expand the
tiptoe metaphor, I can throw a party in an equivalent linux box, and
nothing bad will happen, and there's never much need to reboot.
