Actually I have been using Glade to build a GUI with GTK, and it is quite
nice.

Of course my basis for comparison is about an hour of Visual C++ which I
did not "get"(did not spend the tiem to find out either) and vi.

I have found Glade quite a time saver and reasonably intuitive to
use...which is lucky as there is not that much that I have been able to
find in the way of docs.

In the last two days I have built a gui that judgung on past experience
with GTK and vi would ahve taken me about a week or so to get to this
point.

All in all a surprisingly pleasant experience, especially given someof the
reviews some of my freinds gave me about earlier versions.

There are also a variety of language bindings for GTK, including but not
limited to C,C++,Perl, Python, Ada.

I have had a look at C, C++ and perl.
The C++ and perl kits are soemwhat lackingin docs, but it is possible to
get something going.
