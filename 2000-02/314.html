No, that's 100% not correct.  Basic Rate ISDN provides 2 B-channels 
plus 1 D-channel (16kbps signalling) in the one box.  Dual-channel TA's
can address the two channels independently.

Anyway, I've already replied to the original poster with the same
advice I gave out about a year ago when he first explained that getting
128k ISDN working was "urgent", i.e.: go to a search engine and look
for multilink PPP on Linux.  Just for the hell of it, I followed my
own advice and typed "+multilink +linux" into Altavista and found
a step-by-step guide to do precisely what he wants with the very
same make/model TA he's talking about within the first four or five
returned links, so I really can't understand why it's taken him a
year to work it out.
