here, you have "<mail.chariot.net.au", whats that "<" doing there? 
 .
> .
> .
> Reporting-MTA: dns; localhost.localdomain
> Action: failed Status: 5.1.2
> Remote-MTA: DNS; <mail.chariot.net.au

there's that "<" again. Check for typos in your orig message, if its not
a typo, then you may have an error in you sendmail.cf file. If this is
true (and you haven;t made too many changes directly to this file) you
can recreate the sendmail.cf file by using the m4 macro file sendmail.mc
by doing 

m4 sendmail.mc > sendmail.cf

correct me if I'm wrong, but I dont reckon you should be relaying
anything. You are simply sending mail right?

Relaying is where your box is "passing the (message) baton" to another
server. 

> #who I masquerade as (null for no masquerading) (see also $=M)
> DMandroc@box.net.au

masquerading is where you are hosting more that one mail domain. Again,
not what your trying to do methinks.


I would do as Mat suggested and check your DNS setup. like this

$> nslookup mail.chariot.net.au
