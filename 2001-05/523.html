I have seen this problem before. The BIOS refuses to boot because none of
your partitions are marked bootable. Linux and LILO do not need the
bootable flag set, but some BIOSes do.

Fire up fdisk or diskdrake or whatever from the installation CD, and mark
at least one partition as bootable. Could even mark your swap partition
bootable.

Of course, there might be other problems as well, but this I have seen before.

Seems only some BIOSes have this problem.
