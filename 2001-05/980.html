Except that lots of dial-in routers off a simulated UNIX
"login:" prompt.  If they get a PPP packet in response
then they start PPP, else they say "Password:" and drop
the user onto a simulated command line or start PPP.

Best to configure your PPP without much of a chat script
and just start PPP when you see "CONNECT*\n"
