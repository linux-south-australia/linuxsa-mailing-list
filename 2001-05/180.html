Companies amoral (Was: Re: Latest MS response to free software)

At 12:15 PM 5/7/01 +0930, Glen Turner wrote:
>On 05 May 2001 00:22:46 +0930, Richard How wrote:
>> 
>
>I could live with Windows being the dominant operating system if that
>dominance came from overwhelming technical strength, rather than amoral 
>business practices.

This seems to be a welcome opportunity to point out something that I have
pondered on before.

Companies are not moral entities, so, why should we expect them to engage
in moral behaviour?  Indeed, their very structure, and companies law in
most western countries, force amoral behaviour on all companies, and to
succeed, they must act amorally.  For example, it is immoral, and illegal
to kill someone. It is expected and required behaviour of a company to
destroy (kill) their competition.

We, as a group of societies, must decide whether or not this is acceptible,
or not, and act appropriately.  It seems to me that the majority of people
would be unhappy if they realized that companies not only are not moral
entities, they are actually required to act amorally.
