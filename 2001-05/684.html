Well, no, all is not as it should be, or so I suspect.  Frankly I
don't know what super is, but I gather that its supposed to let you
run programs under different userids or groupids than your own.
To do that it would have to be suid root.  It's not.  It has broken
permissions.  Try:

  chmod u+s /bin/super
