Re: Has anywone looked at OpenOffice's sxw file format?

On Tue, May 08, 2001 at 01:42:13PM +0930, David Lloyd wrote:

 > > I am looking at the potential for 'office' staff to maintain a complex
 > > tecnical document with something like Lyx or OpenOffice ...
 > 
 > The only problem is that things like StarOffice/OpenOffice and Microsoft
 > Word rely on the format, and not the content of the document. Hence you
 > can specify to the hundredth of a degree what heading one's will look
 > like, but they have no "document object model" and can be difficult to
 > work with programatically.
 
That's not entirely true.  They have content-based structuring, 
but nobody bothers to use it.

Look at your StarOffice top-left corner:  There's a drop-down box 
there which is labelled with your current style.  You're supposed to
use templates to define styles for headings, quotations, tables, and
so on, but hardly anyone does that.  Most people just use the 
menus to define their styles as they go ("That line needs to be bold",
rather than, "That line needs to be a heading").
