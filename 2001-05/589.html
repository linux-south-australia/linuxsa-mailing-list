Which UNIX systems still allow SUID scripts ...

Hi,

I just noticed an article on one of the on-line sites about the dangers of
SUID scripts, and was intrigued.

Had I dropped the ball; had I failed to notice that bash honoured the SUID
or SGID bits on shell scripts now?

After a quick check, I determined that no, SUID shell scripts are a
non-event under current versions of Linux, and have been so since at least
Linux 2.0.x.

So, I wonder, which Linux systems out there allow SUID scripts.
