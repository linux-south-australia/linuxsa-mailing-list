Re: Companies amoral (Was: Re: Latest MS response to free software)

Richard Sharpe wrote:
> 
> ... companies law in most western countries, force amoral
> behaviour on all companies...

Common misunderstanding.  Companies are to maximise returns to
shareholders consistent with the constitution of the company.
Nothing prevents the company from having non-monetary objectives
listed in its constitution.

Furthermore, companies have non-tangible assets such as goodwill,
trust and branding.  Directors' responsibilities towards tangible
and non-tangible assets are the same.

Finally, I expect companies to act morally because I expect people
to behave morally.  There's some justification for this in the
companies law -- for example, the ability of a court to overturn
or modify unconscionable contracts and the requirement that contracts
be entered into in good faith.
