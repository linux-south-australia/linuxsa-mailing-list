Hi,

> I have a network of Linux PC's running Suse 6.4 and have set a cron job to
> run weekly and reboot certain workstations. 4 machines share one monitor
(on
> a switch box) and there seems to be a Bios setting on X86 machines which
> prevents a successful reboot if there is no monitor.
> 
> Does anyone know how to overcome this problem?

I can't tell you specifically as I don't know the DIOS, but most BIOSes
have a setting to either ignore, or continue on most errors, including
absence of keyboard, mouse and monitor.

The problem is that if that happens, then console and X apops may not
initialise properly as the computer might not thing that the devices are
present.

Why do you need a weekly re-boot. Usually a re-boot is necessary only if
you have updated the kernel.
