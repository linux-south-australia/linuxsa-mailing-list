Re: hda - irq timeout

On 22 May 2001 20:55:01 +0930, David Newall wrote:
> > Can someone please tell me what the following message relates to?
> > 
> > May 19 03:40:55 marvin kernel: hda: irq timeout: status=0xd0 { Busy }
> > May 19 03:40:55 marvin kernel: ide0: reset: success
> 
> I would say that the ide driver is expecting an I/O operation to complete
> within a certain time and it isn't.  The drive remains busy.  Linux then
> attempts to reset the the bus, which suceeds.  It's not a good thing, but
> it also might just be a harmless quirk.  (I wouldn't count on that, though.)

I wouldn't count on it either.  My 686 server was doing this for a while
when serving up some directories over NFS under load.  Saw some really
ugly disk corruption.  Like needing a "e2fck -b 8193" to recover.  I
decided to stop NFS on that box after that and put them on another :-)

Morale of the story?  I think your box can't keep up with the disk
activity you're requesting it to do and it's causing corrption.  Do I
have any qualitative data to back up my theory?  Nope.  But anecdotal is
better than nothing :)
