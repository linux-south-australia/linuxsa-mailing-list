Re: NTLM and Web Browsers

On Thu, 17 May 2001, Richard Sharpe wrote:

> OK, I am not familiar with how Internet Exploder and the NT proxy use NTLM
> authentication, but I do know a bit about NTLM authentication and how SQUID
> uses NTLM, and they are an entirely different kettle of fish :-)

I suspect this is more the scheme where IIS (and presumably NT proxy)
exchanges auth info in the header of HTML pages which all browsers but IE
ignore. The times I have observed this happening I was replacing the web
server with Apache (and Roxen in one case) at which point authentication
magically reverted to W3C/RFC mechanisms.

However this behaviour does exist and I vaguely recall that it is just a
BASE64 verson of an NTML hash.
