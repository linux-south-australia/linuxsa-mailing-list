I suppose, Chuck, that my little hint to you about the permissions of
super was what prompted you to tell us the permissions of cadduser.
But that does just miss the point.  I still think you have broken the
permissions of super.


> 'super cadduser username password domain'  is the call that I want to use.
> But using 'super cadduser /?' will not function either.
> I keep getting 'super: setgid(gid=0) failed: Operation not permitted'

This tells you a lot.  First, "super:", tells you that the message is
coming from super, as opposed to some other random program.  Second,
"setgid(gid=0)" tells you that super is trying to set its group, and is
getting an error, "Operation not permitted."  This is documented (man
setgid): "The user is not the super-user, and gid does not match the
effective or saved group ID of the calling process."  Probably "gid=0"
indicates that super is trying to set its gid to 0.

Now if you could kindly tell us the permissions of super.  By all means
show the output of "ls -l super"
