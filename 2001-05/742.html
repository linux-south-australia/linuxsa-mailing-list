Re: serial loopback device MDK8.0?

If you connect pins 2 & 3, being transmit and received data, you have
a serial loopback cable.  Whatever you transmit is sent back to you.
If you have a modem that echoes whatever you write to it, which many
do by default when they are in command mode (loosely: not connected),
and if pppd gets to talk to that modem, it would probably conclude that
you had a serial loopback device connected.

I'd guess that you are starting pppd before the modem connects to the
remote end; or else that between dialing and starting pppd you put the
modem back into command mode.

Some modems take instruction from DTR: if you lower it (e.g. by closing
the serial port) the modem will hang up, or go to command mode, or both.
It's a configuration option, and those of you who notice what I write
will be unsurprised when I tell you that the particular command, and
the particular effect, are specific to your brand and/or model of modem.
(AT&Dn on some of Netcomm's modems.)

Probably you have an incomplete dial script.  Make sure that you correctly
identify a connect versus a failed connect.
