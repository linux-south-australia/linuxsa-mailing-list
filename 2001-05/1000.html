Re: WoL and ADSL

>My network card is connected through a switch to my Alcatel ADSL/Ethernet
>modem. My modem and switch were powered on during the night, and the switch
>showed a link to both the modem and the computer, so evidently my LAN card
>was powered up even though my machine wasn't. My modem was connected to my
>ISP (it connects when it's powered up -- the magic of ADSL), and I have a
>static IP. Is it possible that some dork sent a WoL packet to my IP
address,
>and hence, powered up my PC remotely?
>
>If so, it's true that the only safe machine is an unplugged one... :)

It doesn't sound like very likely, as WoL has nothing to do with IP as far
as I know. This is because IP is rarely implemented in hardware for PC's,
the OS handles it instead.

The WoL setups I've seen in the past used a wire connecting the motherboard
to the NIC, so you could probably disable it easily enough physically, if
not in the motherboard (or NIC) bios.

Maybe it's some ATX function that wakes on any data to your ethernet card?
