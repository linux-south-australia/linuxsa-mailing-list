Re: [COMMERCIAL] Red Hat Linux 7.1

On 15 May 2001 12:05:25 +0930, Michael Kratz wrote:

> And while I am writing I might aswell ask, how is Redhat 7.1 on the playing 
> field in terms of 'net' servers, I heard some pretty bad stuff about 7.0 and 
> thus stuck with 6.2, I am most likely going to be building serveral 
> webservers over the next few months, should I move to 7.1 or stick with 6.2 
> and all the RPM updates???

There's something that you need to note about RedHat 7.0 on boxes
(servers in particular).

Those who had problems with it probably didn't apply the updates. Apply
the updates and you should be fine.

Note that some things wouldn't compile correctly (see John Edwards' post
http://www.linuxsa.org.au/mailing-list/2001-04/538.html) under RedHat
7.0

7.1 seems alright, though I'm not 100% sure about a 2.4 kernel on a
firewall. 7.0 updated regularly seems a decent way to go. Don't trust
the default install unless you're installing OpenBSD :-)
