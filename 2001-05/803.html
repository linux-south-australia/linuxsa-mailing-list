> How do I clean out the ^M (carriage return, I think) ?
> 2) Same file, I have a line similar to:
> Q EXP  QTY;RIV 201 547;RIV 201 557   ANO RS   ANO
> within the script, I want to convert all semi-colons to newlines.

"tr" is the answer to both.  Use tr -d '\015' to delete newlines, and
tr ';' '\015' to translate semicolons to newlines.
