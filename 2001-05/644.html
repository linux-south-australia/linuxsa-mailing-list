RE: [Sort of OT] Username Conventions

Nick burbled:
> When I log on, I like to use as few characters in my username as
> possible.. so I always try for "nickm" :)  (plus nickm sounds kinda
> cool)

Why not go one step further and go for 'nm'?
It follows the unix convention of shortening everything to the minimum
possible number of characters, and you could almost get away with saying
"my nm is nm". 

Of course, readability takes a bit of a hit.

Remember the Stonecutters episode of the Simpsons?
I can't quote verbatim, but a line went something like this..

"Marge, finally I've found a society that treats me like a real
person. They looked deep into my heart, and assigned me a number based on
the order in which I joined."
 -- Homer Jay Simpson


Anyway, why can't you just give them a >8 character username?
[tjcorkin@ruiner tjcorkin]$ telnet wiggum

Red Hat Linux release 6.2 (Zoot)

login: this_is_a_test_long_username
Password:

OK, so you're going to want to change your PS1 to something compact, say
PS1="[\w]$ "
but otherwise it seems fine to me?

8 character limits just remind me of DOS...
