It may interest you to know that nearly all providers directly deny
access to port 80 outbound - guess what even the 'big' so-called ISPs !
Telstra are presently testing Cisco Cache engines that transparently proxy
a web request - that is they rewrite a request for data to a proxy cache
that fetches the pages.

I know of a lot of ISPs who use cisco policy routing and squid configured
in accel/cache mode with linux transparent proxy to do the same thing.
It is quite reliable and (if done right) will lead to better QoS to the
user.

>  conserve bandwidth (which often they have too little of ;-) Apparently

When you consider that 40% of what a large group of users look at can be
stored on 20 gig of disk caches are a cost effective way to povide
internet access in a country where WAN costs and per byte charging make it
hard to do the crazy deals people see in the US though for some stupid
reason expect here !

Mind you plently of ISPs have tried to do the crazy deals - 9 have now
gone broke in 20 months in SA alone !
