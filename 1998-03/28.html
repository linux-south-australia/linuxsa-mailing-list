Re: RedHat 4-5, Metro-X and Software

> First and foremost, I tended to prefer the Slackware release, mainly for the
> Ncurses setup and pkgtool utilities, I wasn't very keen on using X for
> everything as RedHat seems to prefer.
> I would be interested to know if RedHat have remedied this situation in
> release 5.

Red Hat have a few more text-based utilities in Red Hat Linux 5.0 than
in 4.2, but there will be more & better ones in 5.1 (they are looking
at linuxconf).

Current text-mode utilities:
cabaret for editing /etc/fstab
ntsysv for starting services
Xconfigurator, mouseconfig, timeconfig, kbdconfig (obvious I hope :)

There's also glitter, available from Red Hat's FTP site but not yet in
the distribution that lets you manage RPM packages.

Of course Red Hat don't force you to use their tools.  You can always
use vi (my favourite sysadmin tool still) or whatever editor you like
:).
