Redhat 5 upgrade.

Hewwow!

I have just installed (not upgraded) a new flavour of redhat (5.0) on my
dialin server (from redhat 4.2). I am using a stallion 8/64 intelligent
serial board and I have successfully compiled some drivers into the kernel
and it's happy.

I am using mgetty to answer the lines, I have two versions - the one that
came with rh 5 and a compiled one for tidier logging and locking. I have
tried the new setup with both.

I also have a compiled copy of pppd which I rolled a while ago because
AutoPPP isn't supported 'out of the box' with redhat
"version_whatever_it_was_at_the_time_I_set_this_machine_up." I am lead to
beleive that pppd now supports this even if you don't recompile. but to be
safe I have tried with both versions of pppd.

The problem I am getting is PAP login failures for any user who logs in
with AutoPPP (win95 and the like). A regular manual or scripted login works
fine even with NT *gulp*, it's just the AutoPPP's. Tonight I will download
and compile the latest version of pppd I can find just for superstitions
sake, but I figure something else somewhere is broken, and I don't know what.

If anyone can help I'd appreciate it. If you need any more info then ask away.

Oh yes I just remebered. Here is the relevant part of the login.config file
for mgetty.

/AutoPPP/ -     -       /usr/sbin/pppd +pap
