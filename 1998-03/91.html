Re: SCO get's a corporate agreement.

> Expect things to fail, it's part of life.

Backups are just fine, if you have an appropriate backup
strategy and check the backups and do offsite, all should
be well and good.

Software fails.  You maybe backing up lovely, useless,
mindless rubbish.  If you've spent all the time and money
on offsite backups, firesafes, etc, but you've just been
backing up rubbish because your database has been corrupting
everything, where does that leave you?  

Have you checked your backups recently?  Do you know your
tape drive hasn't been writing out garbage?  Can you read
them back on a different tape drive (of similar type) if
yours dies or burns up?

Also, just backing up stuff isn't good enough.  If you need
a 24x7 operation, then what happens if the software doesn't
cope with some situation?  Who do you call?  [I've been in
situations like this, and it's comforting to know that there
is a team in the USA being paid to solve your problem].
