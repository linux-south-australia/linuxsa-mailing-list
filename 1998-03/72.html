Re: Merced + Linux

> Which of intel's chips is merced?

Merced is a new hardware platform, being jointly developed by Intel and HP.
It is to be a 64-bit RISC system, and will be positioned to replace the
current 80x86 line of chips (so called, "IA").  Rumour had it that Merced
would be capable of running all IA applications, and I suppose that means
a section of silicon will be devoted to a Pentium.  Based on Intel's prior
art, I expect there will be a Merced "real mode", which will make the chip
act like a "Real Pentium".  Seems uncomfortably familiar to me; but that
must be the price of, well I was going to say success; but I suppose it's
really the price of failure.  :-)
