I was going to stay out of this, but I can't help it :).  The main
reasons I prefer Red Hat are:

- it is well maintained
- there is a standard method of upgrading between releases that does
  not involve backing up, reinstalling, and restoring
- it includes a great package manager

The same points apply to Debian (which is also an excellent
distribution), but not to Slackware.

I find the fat comment "interesting"... Red Hat ships with one kernel
446K in size.  Slackware ships with 57 different kernels totalling
37MB, none below 563K, and averaging 661K.  This is thinner, not
fatter, but you didn't say what you actually meant by the fat comment
so this might not have been your point.
