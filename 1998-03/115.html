Intel Expands I960 Processor Family (both in range and geography)

Over the years, Intel chips have been getting bigger and bigger, due to
an increased number of transisters.

This increase has resulted in larger and larger socket sizes.

Due to the advanced technology Intel have developed, their latest I960
risc processor, used in a number of embedded applications, has had a
dramatic increase in the number of transistors. This has again resulted
in a increase of the socket size required to hold the chip on the
motherboard.

Details are on this web page :

http://www.techweb.com/wire/story/TWB19980319S0016
