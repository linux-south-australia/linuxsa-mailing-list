There's nothing wrong with the statement.  Linux is not *the* standard
Unix OS for Intel based hardare; in fact it's not even *a* Unix.  Only
when Linux passes the SVVS (or whatever it's called now), will it have
any genuine stake in the Unix market.  For now it has as much stake as
Apple should expect with their new (yet to be released) Mach based OS.

On the other hand, do we care about Unix(tm)?  For me it's enough that
Linux is a Unix-like OS.
