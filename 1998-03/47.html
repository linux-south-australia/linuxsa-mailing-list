RedHat 5.0's runlevel management.

I have just encountered a small gotcha moving from RedHat 4 to RedHat
5.0 that concerns the management of runlevels.

In earlier RedHats the easiest way to enable/disable a daemon was to
install/uninstall its package.  However, there is now an additional
tool called `/sbin/chkconfig' which will enable/disable an _installed_
daemon.  This is great except that, at some point in upgrading to the
portmapper in the RedHat 5.0 errata, the package's post install script
was not run and the new portmapper was never turned on.  Needless to
say I spent several embarassing hours scratching my head trying to
decipher a very unfamilar error from `mountd'.

Hope this saves someone a little time.
