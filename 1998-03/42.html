Re: Who sed what?

On Mon, 9 Mar 1998, Don Collins wrote:

> My problem is how do I use sed to delete a string of text from several
> places in all files in a directory.
> ie.  I wish to delete all occurrences of the text '/dir//' (no quotes but
> yes, a double '/') in several places of all files in a directory.
> I am having trouble getting sed to process the '/' characters, and all
> references I have give no clue as to what to do.
> I know there are some sed freaks out there who love this sort of (basic)
> scripting opportunity.

I'm no sed freak (far from it) but the following should do what you want
(bash+sed):

for I in *; do sed -e 's/\/dir\/\///g' < $I > TEMPFILE;mv TMPFILE $I;done

Btw a while ago I came across a prime number generator written in sed by
Olaf Kirch (of NAG/NFS fame). Freaky... :) It should still be on his ftp
site if you want to have a look.
