RE: SCO get's a corporate agreement.

I have found that one of the major difficulties in implementing linux in
a corporate environment is not specifically a technical issue, rather a
political and financial one... the problem is this... Since Linux is not
a commercial operating system, with a defined 'owner', Insurance
comapnies _will not_ insure it... This is something of a problem if u r
an out-sourcing IT company wanting to implement systems for clients...
basically, u cannot get insurance unless u either use a commercial
flavour of un*x or use windows nt (which despite the technical
difficulties - of which there are many) nonetheless has a defined owner,
and is therefore acceptable to an insurance company....
