Ok,gutsy question here....how many computers can be set up in an
installfest? Could 1 school be covered in a weekend by all of 
AUUG/LinuxSA/volunteers-for-the-day?

Some schools are just desperate for IT help, and IT help that comes with 
software -- if well-coordinated -- could be a boon. And if you think of 
schools in the less well off areas, would they qualify for Computer Bank 
to help them? If so, if enough well-organized volunteers descended at 
one time, computers could be fixed/upgraded, software installed and 
tested in a military-style operation. Not forgetting documentation, 
which schools often never see!

Just imagine, a group of working bees carrying 300 hard disks with Linux 
preinstalled on them, swapping all 300 in a weekend, taking the Windows 
disks home, formating them, putting Linux on them.

But, to leave and never come back would be irresponsible. It would take 
a significant amount of support from a committed group of liason 
volunteers to help the school with issues from classwork to network 
administration. Otherwise, it would be just another Red Escobar.
