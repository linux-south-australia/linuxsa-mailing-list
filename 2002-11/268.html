The company I was working for in 1989 was using TOPS networking
software to stick a couple of PCs onto an AppleTalk network. To cut a
long story short, we were using TOPS in a sort of weird way which
confused the licence-enforcement code so things sometimes didn't work.
(We weren't breaking the licence, just confusing the code.)

It was me who hacked TOPS so it did work. I would not be surprised if
there aren't really that many people out there who want to hack Macs,
in the same way as not many people want to stick their hand into a
meat grinder.
