On Tue, 26 Nov 2002 16:16:32 +1030
richard@yellowgoanna.com (Richard Russell) wrote:
> And while we're on the topic, what's with the power connectors on
> UPS's?  They all seem to have the male computer power connectors
> (don't know the real name for that style of three-prong), which means
> you can't plug just any old device into them, you have to have one
> that takes the right type of cable. Why don't they just have the
> normal Australian-style connectors?

That would mean that they would have to manafacture a differnt UPS for each country! The plugs are a world standard, where as outlets are not! In the current situation all they need to do is change the power cord they supply.

> Can you get adapters, so I can
> plug (say) a Modem PSU into it?

Buy a power board.
Cut of plug.
Go to Jaycar, buy plug thingo.
Put plug thingo on power board.
Instant adapter with many outputs!


> Is there any reason why you shouldn't?

Nup ! We do it so that if the power goes down we can still remotely get to the machine :)

> I mean, seriously, who has oodles of male-female computer three-prong
