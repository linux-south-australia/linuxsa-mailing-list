Re: Html email gets a reply

Daniel Mundy <djmunds@gmx.net> wrote:
> Anyone notice how in this whole thread not one person actually posted
> in HTML?

I think we've already discussed this issue, and decided that the reason
is courtesy.

> My 2c: I use mutt and pipe HTML messages through lynx, so they are
> readable and easily replied to, but none of the formatting advantages
> eg. bold/italic do not actually show, and so are just a waste of
> bandwidth.

Just a waste of bandwidth *for you*, perhaps, because you choose to
use a text-only browser.  Even then you must have your system poorly
configured because Lynx does highlight bold and underscore italics.
Further, the tables probably render nicely, and you just can't do that
with plain text when you don't know whether the reader will be using a
26 or 80 character screen.

Since the majority of us use graphical tools, not text-only, the
expressiveness isn't lost.  For example, you might not like 24pt pink
text, but as the author if I used that it would be for a purpose.
Communicating is hard enough without being hampered by placing what is
arguably a reasonable improvement out of bounds.
