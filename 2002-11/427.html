Re: Html Emails

Brian Astill <bastill@sa.apana.org.au> wrote:
> The fact that the increased bandwidth has to be paid for
> by every recipient member can be a telling argument :-)

The same reasoning can be used to argue against RFC822 email.  All that
bandwidth wasted in the headers.  And you know, the headers probably
waste more bandwidth than the HTML tags do.

I suspect that what you mean is that people who send HTML email sometimes
like to include multi-media attachments as part of their message.  I don't
think such attachments have any place in the normal course of business,
but blaming the format for the message is surely the same as blaming
the messenger.  Take a look at the advantages of HTML.  Understand what
the disadvantages really are.  I feel that the current state of 'debate'
is a parrot like call to flog people for using HTML without personal
understanding of the issues.
