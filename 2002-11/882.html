Re: This distro goes with that CPU?

On Tue, Nov 19, 2002 at 03:19:08PM -0800, Simon Lahiff wrote:
> > Mandrake.  The whole distrobution has been compiled
> > for i586 for years now.
> 
> What does that mean anyway? Isn't the whole i586 thing
> just a marketing tactic used by Mandrake. It is really
> just i386, but branded differently.

no when you compile the kernel it's an architecture type
option (ie. enables certain optimisations and uses extra CPU
operations/instructions that have been added to later/newer
CPUs).  i386 is compatible with newer ones (586, 686, p3 etc)
but the newer ones aren't backwards compatible, therefore
a kernel compiled for (eg.) 686 won't work on a 486.
