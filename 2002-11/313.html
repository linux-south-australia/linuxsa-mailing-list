Well (to get the ball rolling) and in 25 words or less....

I would use Linux (in any case) because:

1/ I "own" the tools I am using
2/ Huge range of applications
3/ Better performance
4/ Choice of mix and match file systems
5/ Better application (and OS) install processes
6/ Way better security
7/ Better networking
8/ It's an important alternative to the "corporate norm"
(so it adds some bio-diversity to the computing world)
9/ Updates to everything are really easy
(well on Debian at least)
10/ I can get under the hood
11/ I have the opportunity of contributing / putting back something to a
community.(so it is more human in approach) 12/ I can request (or
implement) features or even new applications. 13/ Stability !
14/ I can jump onto the "train" at any level form absolute newbie 
through <shudder> "poweruser" to guru/ wizard.(and/ Who uses office
suites anyway :>) 15/ The choice of interfaces is stunning I can find or
configure (even make) something that suites me and the way _I_ work not
the way some MS / Marketing wanker thinks I should.(E17 to TTY/consol).
16/ In the end it is a more creative environment and that is a "Good
Thing" for humans.
