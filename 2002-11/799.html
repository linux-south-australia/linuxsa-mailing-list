> These are good thoughts Greg - thank you for sharing them.
>
> I don't disagree with what you say - indeed I'm all for the
> combination of effort to minimise wasteage, but I hear rumblings
> that another incorporation may occur in the "SA geek space"[1]
> anyways so perhaps LinuxSA should be that body.

That's an interesting statement.  Firstly, if another body (who?) is
about to incorporate, why should they stop if LinuxSA incorporates?
Why should this be a reason for LinuxSA to incorporate?

> I guess you can always throw SAGE-AU and Airnet into the "SA geek
> crowd" as well.  More broadly you could include AJUG, ACS, and SEA.
> Why are there so many groups?!? :)

Well, taking that list, I'd say:

SAGE-AU: Not specifically UNIX oriented.  We've had a long
         relationship with them in the past, some of it good
         (especially lately), and we've come to the conclusion that
         they have a sufficiently different focus that we both need to
         be distinct entities.

Airnet: Not really sure.  Again, I suspect they don't fit completely
	into the UNIX space.

AJUG: Very specialized group.  It could theoretically be a Special
      Interest Group within AUUG, but we haven't really defined what
      that is yet.

ACS:  Different focus.  Not only UNIX, not overly technical.

> [1] the reason why is another thing.  Perhaps existing groups aren't
> meeting new needs.

It would seem to be a rather strong reaction to that problem.  One of
AUUG's problems has been that we've been around for ever, so to speak
(1975, to be more exact), and the world changed while we had our heads
down.  We're catching up now, but if there are needs which we can
fulfil and which aren't being addressed, we'd certainly like to know
about them.
