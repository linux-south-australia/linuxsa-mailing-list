> I think you answered your own question.  To join AUUG (and thereafter form a

I believe that we need to form a community based, volunteer organisation who is
supported by volunteers for the good of the whole. People who are willing to
part with $110, I believe, also expect to receive a return on their investment.
I seek an organisation that gives no return, but for the satisfaction of helping
the community and promoting all commhnity software as a whole.

> I think we need some kind of 
> associate membership relating to the SIG(s) only. eg becoming a member of the 
> LinuxSA SIG would involve a membership fee of $25 pa, but provide all the

But community software is so much more than "Linux" or "FreeBSD" or Unix
variants. Closely aligned with open source software, it is something that I
think empowers people to become part of the IT community; with the rise in the
use of IT those not part of that community are becoming illterate.

And it can empower people on any platform, whether that be Mac OS X, Solaris 9
or Windows 2000 Professional.

> We do need to work together and be - and be seen (heard?) to be - speaking 
> with one voice which is big enough and loud enough to be influential.
