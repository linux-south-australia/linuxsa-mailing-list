Re: UPS's

Lead-acid batteries (the type found in cars and trucks etc) go to
custard if they get too flat. They can give a LOT of current over a
short amount of time, and are cheap and efficient at that, but don't
do deep-cycle well. The demands on a car battery are very different to
that for a UPS.
