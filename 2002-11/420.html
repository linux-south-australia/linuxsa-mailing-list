I have a hearty scepticism too.  But things aren't quite the way
Senator Lundy portrays them.  As you're aware, AUUG is currently
involved in discussions with the NOIE (http://www.noie.gov.au/) about
the use of Open Source in the Government.  Some of us were in Canberra
on Thursday, where we had quite a long discussion with some of their
people.  I can honestly say that I'm very impressed by the people they
have there, and I think they're following the correct path for the
correct reasons (which, however, are not the reasons that I would
have).  This is the incumbent government, of course, and they're
addressing exactly the issues that Senator Lundy is talking about.
Given that, and Senator Lundy's stand (running in an open door), I
suspect that it wouldn't change under a Labor government.

AUUG has a web page up at http://www.auug.org.au/ocg/.  It's pretty
bare-bones at the moment, at least partially because we still don't
understand all the issues involved.  I've found it easy to get up on
my hind legs and say "The Government should be doing *this*", but
after looking at it more closely, I realize that it was easy at least
partially because I didn't understand the problem.
