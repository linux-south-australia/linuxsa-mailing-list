Mouse trouble

I recently installed RedHat 8.0 on what was previously a windows machine.  Due 
to antiquated ports (it is an old AMD K-6 box), I am running my Logitech 
optical wheel mouse(USB) into the ps/2 port via an adapter.  While installing 
redhat, the installer detected the mouse just fine and it worked for the 
graphical install.  however, during my first boot after install, Kudzu (the 
hardware detection program) reports that my mouse has been removed.  Obviously, 
this is very troubling.  I have tried several things, including tinkering with 
(/etc/sysconfig/hwconf), (/etc/fstab), and disabling gpm.  The mouse does not 
work in console mode using gpm, either.  I have read other posts that said that 
X doesn't play nice with gpm if you're using a ps/2, but disabling gpm doesn't 
have any affect.  I guess what the real problem is that my mouse was somehow 
removed, and I'm not sure how to get it back.  Thanks for any help.
