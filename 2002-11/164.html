Re: status of transmeta?

Hmm... (old thread, I know, but hey)

IMHO, Transmeta don't really have a serious advantage over anyone
regarding heat and power (not since Intel made Speedstep, and started
caring about the portable market, and VIA came out with the C3). If
they ever get their performance together, the others will have caught
up with heat and power.

However, where TM have a great advantage is in being able to
effectively emulate anything (well, theoretically). I'd like to see a
Notebook that I can boot as a x86, a SPARC, and a PPC (Mac). Even
better, I want a special key that will enable me to switch through
multiple running virtual machines. Of course, this will never happen,
because I'm not that big a market. :)
