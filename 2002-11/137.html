Re: 32 bit colour rather pointless?

BUT you need _less_ blue than you do red or green to make white look
white on a monitor.
(Does that make sense? Yes, I think so)
This is why full-blast white, red or green text is readable but
full-blast blue is really hard to see.

Maximum sensitivity of the human eye is around the sodium lines, which
helps street lighting authorities not to worry about the amount of light
they radiate upwards.

This is not relevant, but possibly interesting: Cats appear to have much
shorter persistence of vision than humans. Look at how they react to a
TV picture. They appear not to notice the picture content - they don't
react to pictures of cats, or mice or whatever - but they will have a go
at catching the current scan line as it moves down the screen.
