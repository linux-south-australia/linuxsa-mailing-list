On Sun, 17 Nov 2002 13:23:19 +1030, Mike Gratton <mike@vee.net> wrote:

>Richard Russell wrote:
>
>> Is anyone planning on going to see the total solar eclipse on December
>> 4th?

There was one in August 1999, visible from the southern extremities of
the UK and the north coast of France, and most of central Europe.
Don't miss it. And don't forget that ANYWHERE within the zone of
totality will do, preferably somewhere isolated, without ice cream.

DIY eyeshade test: if you can only just see a 100W incandescent bulb
through it at a few inches range it's probably OK. Arc welding shades
are good. Sunglasses are useless. Multiple layers of thoroughly
exposed black-and-white film (not colour) are OK (eg. black bits of
X-ray photos). Smoked glass is awkward, messy, uneven, hard to get
dark enough and generally an option of last resort.

Photography: you need a tracking mount to photograph totality. You
also stand quite a good chance of melting holes in your shutter when
setting it up if you're not careful.
