Re: Html Emails

davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:

> The same reasoning can be used to argue against RFC822 email.  All that
> bandwidth wasted in the headers.

Which provide a useful diagnostic tool, for example, to trace what path
the email has taken to get to your MUA.  Which may tell your MUA the
content type of the body of the email.  Which tells you who the intended
receipient is (although not necessarily the same as the SMTP recipient)
and who the alleged sender was.  The date on which the email was sent.
A mine of information in those attributes, most of which are required
by necessity.

If you rip the HTML out of a multipart message, it's still likely to
remain readable.  The same can't be said of the headers.
