It is just too much because I do other things - currently I have
exams starting next week and I'd like to put in a submission on GE
Food before 15/11. Apart from that I am trying to get my head
around PODI* and PPML** and to research the market for on demand
book printing and capital requirements, and supporting my ageing
mother and visiting my daughter in Victoria, and maintain a house
and garden, and .. oh, yes and to earn a crust. 

For me my computer is a tool, to send and get e-mail, to get
information, to write on.
Why am I considering open source?
I have a 486 and it is getting slower and slower, and hangs a lot
more, and although I could replace it there are other things I'd
prefer to do with my money - e.g. go see my daughter. Apart from
which as a greenie I like to make things last, and don't like to
buy things unnecessarily.
Why haven't I done it yet?
I'm nervous about doing it. 
+   I'll have to partition my hard disc, what if I lose things?
+   I've got some favourite programs 
one End Note is perfectly legal, but it was provided to me under
an education licence and can't reload it - which means I'll have
to buy it $700 odd, 
and the other a nice little game of jigsaw puzzles which I bought
online and I'll have to go through a lot of hassles 
+    I've been saving a lot of my emails to save the hassle of
going to archive files when I want info from 5 years back. One day
I'll find a good search engine so that I can burn them to disc and
take them off the hard drive.
+    I'll have to keep on hunting on line for things such as
drivers and fixes and stuff. I loathe internet searching and avoid
it as much as I can.
+    All the open source material  I've seen wants you to have a
pentium processor. Oh yes, there are "light" versions on the net.
After searching through an arcana of technobabble, and after
downloading for an hour and half, the system has a hernia, I get
an error message and the download is incomplete. Try again? you've
got to be kidding see previous point.

Quite frankly it is too hard, and the fault doesn't lie with me. I
am the customer. The customer is always right. And I don't

Now consider - I'm what is called an early purchaser and I still
