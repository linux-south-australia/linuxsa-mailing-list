I think this was covered in the response to Glen, but that's a long one,
so: apparently under Australian law Wassenaar only applies to tangible
goods and Internet downloads of free software aren't regarded as tangible.
This is how people like Eric Young (SSLeay) and some DSTC Queensland
people, and others, got away with such exports despite the enforcing
authority (Signals Directorate IIRC) wanting to prevent it. Everything has
changed now and probably there is no going back, but who knows.

Worth reading Peter Gutman's experiences with NZ's spooks. Google will
find it for you. Peter is a noted crypto expert, software author and
collector of a massive database of crypto links.
