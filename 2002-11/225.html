FYI,
Don't refer to them as "press releases".  Refer to them as "media releases" 
otherwise media other than newspapers can get stroppy and ignore them.

However, I'd agree that a well worded, short, media release is useful.  
Remember that they should be:

- short
- factual - no airyfairy waffly stuff.  It's got to grab their attention in 
5 sec.
- seem compelling or cover an issue that an editor would see as compelling 
and newsworthy. 
- cover of the basics of who/what/when/why in no more than a paragraph each
- easy to understand (no deep technical stuff) 
- contain GOOD quality contact information

I'd suggest using the "geeks helping charity" kind of angle.  It's a good 
human interest thing - don't push the open source thing too hard - or at 
least do it in the context of how it helps charities - not too much 
political stuff.

(My Fiancee (wife in 9 days) used to work for a PR firm and occasionally I
picked up useful tips).

If anyone writes one, send me a copy and I'll run it past her.
