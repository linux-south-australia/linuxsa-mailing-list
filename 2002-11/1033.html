Re: removing the framebuffer penguin logo

On Sun, Nov 24, 2002 at 10:12:37PM +1030, Richard Russell wrote:
> Hi all,
> 
> I have a question that has probably never been asked -- how do I
> disable the penguin logo on boot on my debian woody system without
> recompiling? Surely I can just disable it simply?

Don't use framebuffer mode and all will be well. It's also possible to change
the logo to something else (as long as it's 80x80 pixels), but that will
require a recompile. We're using a similar patch to the one used by Mandrake
systems on a few machines here - We've actually put a larger, centred copy of
the company logo in the kernel to replace the penguin.

I couldn't find a
'disable-the-boot-logo-thing-but-still-use-framebuffer-mode' option in the
kernel-parameters.txt kernel docs.
