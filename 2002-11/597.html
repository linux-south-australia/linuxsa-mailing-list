You could always try using a protocol other than SMB/CIFS. Windows NT has
*very* basic support for LPR/LPD through the 'Microsoft TCP/IP Printing'
service that you can install through the network control panel. That will do
what you want (by the sounds of things) and will be tied to the particular
machine, as opposed to SMB/CIFS printers, which aren't seen as local printers
and tend to follow people around the network. Bad Printer! Stay Boy! Play
Dead!

If you find you do want to use SMB/CIFS, you could always try allowing the
guest user to print and by mapping the guest user to a generic unix userid
(such as nobody). Have a look at the smb.conf man page and the Samba
documentation. In particular any smb.conf options that refer to either
printing or the guest user.
