Re: Html email gets a reply

On Friday,  8 November 2002 at 13:27:04 +1100, Ian Mcleod wrote:
> yeah I agree - time to get over it - this whole 10 year long
> neo-luddite anti-change protest against all but the most ultra-basic
> forms of communication is a bit thin.

I think you're missing the point here.  I too would like a better form
of communication than plain text.  But it has to be better.

> I have never known HTML to be platform specific - so what's the
> deal?

HTML is worse than platform specific.  What you see is dependent not
only on your browser, but how you set it up.  There is as good as no
possibility that what you see on the screen is the same as what I see
on the screen.  With properly formatted plain text, or with PDF, the
discrepancies are minimal.

> Surely even the most basic of basic text email readers can still
> render HTML - unless it has been deliberately turned off as a silent
> protest against change.

In fact, most can't.  I don't know of any which can, though at least
mutt can grab a passing Netscape and get it to display for him.  But
answering such a message is difficult.

> I'm sure the security concerns of HTML are dead easy to work around
> too - so give the web site maintainers a break eh?

OK, how would you stop dereferencing an embedded a tag?  I don't want
to have to think about it.

On  Friday, November 08, 2002 12:33 PM, Daniel Moore wrote:
>
> Hmmmm... what exactly is the problem with HTML format? Isn't that
> meant to be *the* platform independent document formatting language?

No.

> It's much more light weight than pdf, which is a proprietary
> encoding (it's just not MS).

Agreed, that's why people generally don't advocate PDF.  PDF has the
advantage of showing pretty much the intended text at the other end,
but it's also as good as impossible to reply to using UNIX tools.
