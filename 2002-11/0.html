You get what you pay for. A site like www.no-ip.com (I should've said .com 
not .org since you will be redirected there anyway) has several plans to 
suit your budget and needs. A basic plans has limitations but you can 
redirect one domain for free including MX records. There are other dyndns
hosting companies around though. Google is your friend.

For example to see my web server point your browser to 
http://awol.no-ip.org/ and you can always email 
me at awol@awol.no-ip.org too. My irc bots both have their own mailing lists 
too. For example on #linuxsa on irc.freenode.net my wombat bot has a web page 
at http://awol.no-ip.org/~wombat where you can subscribe to his mailing list.

The hosting company provides a small program you compile and then include in
your boot scripts or run manually. It only needs to be run once and perhaps
cronned if you're paranoid. This updates your IP on the hosting DNS servers 
every five minutes so if your connection drops out (I get kicked every 6 hrs)
it gets renewed automatically.
This way you can host as many domains as are publically resolvable on the one
machine. I'm not entirely sure how you'd go about hosting other domains
on an internal LAN machine as I've never tried. Perhaps another list member 
could shed some light on this. It would require some clever firewall
manipulation no doubt. 

It all depends how elaborate you want/need to get. The beauty of redhat is the
mail app rpm's like mailman and squirrelmail come with everything all setup for 
you. So all you have to do is tweak a few configs to get webmail and mailing
lists all happening and get on with business.
I dont know how easy debian is to setup though. I shall have to give it a try
sometime.   :)

Hope this helps!
