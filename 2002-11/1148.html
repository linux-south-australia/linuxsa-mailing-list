Re: UPS's

Matthew.Cook@tekelek.com.au wrote:

>A word of warning here...
>
>The rapid/float charger within your UPS may not be capable of charging your
>car battery, if it was designed for a SLA battery.
>
Another warning from experience .. if your double-conversion UPS is 
designed for a certain load X, when charging from flat it's going to 
consume X + Y load to ensure it can charge and deliver power at the same 
time. We recently commissioned such a UPS rated for about 40A which 
threw a 60A breaker when turned on *unloaded* for the first time.

Charge time can be important. A few years ago, some businesses in 
Adelaide were caught out when they survived an hour-long power outage, 
only to have everything fail the following day when the 
not-yet-recharged UPS's couldn't carry them through a 2nd identical outage.
