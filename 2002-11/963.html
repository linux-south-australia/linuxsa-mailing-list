Re: Configure a cron job not to email me?

On Fri, 22 Nov 2002, Adam Smith wrote:

> Hello all,
>
> I have a cron job that executes extremely frequently on FreeBSD, and I'd
> prefer it not to email its stdout/stderr to me.  How do I go about
> formatting the cron job correctly so that its output diverts to
> /dev/null?
>
> The cron job:
>
> 	*/2 * * * * /usr/local/rrdtool/scripts/do-all

Some might be tempted to say:

*/2 * * * * /usr/local/rrdtool/scripts/do-all > /dev/null 2> /dev/null

Bzzt - wrong answer.

There is nothing wrong with your crontab entry. The problem is with your
script. If it works, it should be quiet and print nothing. Then you'll
get no mail. Otherwise, it should tell you what the problem is. Problem
solved.

Get into the habit of redirecting noisy scripts to dev null and you will
regret it one day.
