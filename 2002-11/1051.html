> michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:

>  >
> > I'm looking at organising a Linux conference here in SA in the centre of 
> > Adelaide, in January 2004, and I'm looking for venue cost and
> availability.
> 
> Hi Michael,
> 
> I took ten ten minutes out of my day and made a few calls.
> 
> I've no idea how many people will go to lca 2004 so I took
> a stab at 400 -- this effects costs a lot, bigger conferences
> being more expensive per person.
> 
> A good budgetary figure is $750 per day per facility.  LCA2003
> has three streams and goes for five days, so that's about
> $12,000.
> 
> Now the way to proceed from here *isn't* to ring Jim Denny on
> 8303 6105 and book Union Hall at Adelaide Uni.  Ring the
> organisers of LCA2003, get an idea of eventual numbers,
> and then ring Jim.  Don't agree to anything, just get
> pricing, availability and T&Cs (get him to fax these to you).
> You'll need the T&Cs to answer questions later (does the
> Hall come with audio, if so do you need to pay an audio
> tech if you use it).
> 
> Then start to build a team of interested people.
> 
> With a good team you'll have someone from inside Adelaide Uni
> working for you.  That will make things a lot simpler. It may
> not on the surface cost less, but items like data projectors
> will then be located by Means Nefarious rather than cost
> upwards of $500 per day.  And any little emergency items like
> powerboards can be found.
> 
> I suggest what you do to build that team is to put out a call for
> a preliminary organising committee.  Noting that you'll want to
> influence the membership a tad (the people you want most have
> been burnt by conference organisation before and will need to be
> personally begged/bribed/threatened).
> 
> You'll need to buddy up to the LCA2003 organisers and make
> sure that there's no one unofficially lined up for 2004.  A
> 2005 date would be better in many ways -- Jan 2004 is very
> close, most conferences look at 18 months as barely enough
> time.
> 
> Then you should schmooze LinuxSA, AUUG-SA, ACS-SA and groups
> you don't even know about yet.  Things like accomodation,
> net access, etc will fall into your lap.  But I've found that
> building a core of effective volunteer organisers is by
> far the hardest part of organising a grassroots conference
> and you need to cast far and wide for these.
> 
> Best wishes,
> Glen
