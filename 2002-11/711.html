> > G'day all,

> > I've been attempting to get uudecode to decode a base64 encoded
> > attachment that came with an e-mail to me, but so far without success.
>
> That's "base64 encoded", not "uuencoded" - you should be using "base64 -d".
> If you don't have base64 (mine is part of the openssl suite), then
> "uudecode -m" may work, according to uudecode's man page.

The -m option is used with uuencode to switch it to base64 encoding.  Tried 
uudecode -m anyway and no results to show for it.

Man also reckons that uudecode ignores any leading & trailing lines and that  
it automatically determines which of the two supported encoding schemes are 
used. So I'm a bit stumpped as to why it won't work on this file.

But ! :-) :-) :-) I tried your base64 thing with openssl (never used that 
package before either at command line) and it was a goer!  For anyone 
interested, the format to use for such decoding is, in shell;

openssl base64 -d -in inputfile -out outputfile

Works a treat!
