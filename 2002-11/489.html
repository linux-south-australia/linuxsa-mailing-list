Re: Ext2 disk gone bad...

James et al,

> Does anyone think it is safe to mount the disk as a second disk in 
> another Linux box with compatable hardware, mount it and then copy it 
> over to the other disk?

Don't mount it at all yet.

1) Insert a similarly sized or larger disk into the system
2) Boot it into Linux (or FreeBSD)
3) Then do something like:

# dd if=/dev/BADDISK of=IMAGE_OF_BAD_DISK

,,,where /dev/BADDISK is whatever the BADDISK is (eg /dev/hdb).

I have a suspicion that Francesco may need to go hex editing and
superblock or partition table rewriting by hand ;-P
