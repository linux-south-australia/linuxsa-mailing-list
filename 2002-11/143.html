Re: [NEWS] Linux - an industrial success?

Linux in CARS?
Does that mean that the code for the engine management system will be
open source, and we will all be able to create our own
performance-enhancing ROMs?
Does it mean that the code driving all the horrid, horrid, unnecessary,
potentially unreliable and hideously expensive <3Mb of flame removed>
microcontroller-based gadgets that are infesting modern cars will be
open source, so we can figure out what the thing really does and can
build a $5 replacement for a $500 lump of resin with a PIC somewhere in
the middle of it?
Could this possibly be the best thing for car electrical systems that we
can hope for short of all manufacturers being forced to produce cars
which are no more complex electrically than a Morris Minor?
I think I like this!

Pigeon
