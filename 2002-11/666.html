bptimm@uwm.edu wrote:

>Compatriots,
>  I have re-installed redhat 8.0 so many times that i could successfully 
>install it blindfolded.  My mouse (an optical logitech wheel mouse through a 
>ps/2 adapter) still refuses to work.  
>

More info:

From:

>
> http://koala.ilog.fr/anyboard/MouseWheel/posts/1212.html


Solution as posted:

>
>
> Here's a general dump of everything I know RE gpm and this mouse.
>
> You can't use the same mouse for gpm and XFFree86 at the same time. 
> Try disabling gpm.
>
> Here is my XF86Config-4, for USB. It works with PS/2 also:
>
> Section "InputDevice"
> Identifier "Configured Mouse"
> Driver "mouse"
> Option "CorePointer" "true"
> Option "Device" "/dev/input/mice"
> Option "Protocol" "ImPS/2"
> Option "Buttons" "5"
> Option "Resolution" "800"
> Option "ZAxisMapping" "4 5"
> EndSection
>
> That works very well. My system is Debian Sid, 2.4.17 and XF86 4.1.0 
> (I think).
>
> I don't think you can use the fourth mouse button, although I am 
> working on this. 
>
> It worked! Thanks for your posting! Much appreciated!
