Thanks. I have wanted to know that for ages. Now I have another
question:

My motherboard will take 3 x SDRAM DIMMs up to 128M each "ordinary type"
or 256M each "registered". Until I read this article I had never seen
another reference to "registered" memory.

for this reason I have only half the memory I theoretically could have.

I don't, of course, expect anyone in Australia to give me a UK source of
133MHz registered 256M SDRAMs. What I would like to know is:
- Is being registered implied by some other feature which will be

but nobody thinks it worth mentioning that feature specifically? If so,
what are they? (?Some ECC but not necessarily all?)
- Assume the shop simply shifts boxes without being knowledgeable about
their contents, but are willing to let me open the box and examine the

registered memory?
