g = (local) acceleration due to gravity, approx. 9.8m/s^2 at the
Earth's surface, so acceleration of 2 Gravities (say) means 2 x
9.8m/s^2, or 2g. (Confusion with "2 grammes" is avoided because it is
the kilogram which is the standard unit these days.) Hollywood etc.
incorrectly write "2G". G is the gravitational constant, which is a
different thing, basically a constant to make the equation for "force
between two bodies due to gravity" (F = G.m1.m2 / r^2) come out right.
G is a universal constant; g varies according to the strength of the
local gravitational field.
