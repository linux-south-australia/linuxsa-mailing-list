But ultimately it's still six of one, half a dozen of the other - at the

Not much.  We're arguing over what colour the wrapping is.

What we _really_ need is DSLAM level competition.  Then things will be 
shaken up.  

The big issue is that one player (Tel$tra) controls everything.  They also 
charge the ISPs for:

- a port on a DLSAM
- AND a connection to the Tel$tra ATM network to connect to the Telstra ADSL

"sometimeswork".
- AND make them connect in multiple areas around Oz (even treating ACT as a 
different region! Such an "old style" telco thing).

We need DSLAM level competition to remove this artifical playing field, 
especially making the ISPs connect via expensive ATM connections (which adds

to the costs).
