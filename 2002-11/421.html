Re: Html email gets a reply

On Sat, 9 Nov 2002, David Lloyd wrote:

> People can, indeed, use HTML to add to the meaning of e-mails but they
> generally do not.

Ahh, the crux of the matter.

People don't actually generally use HTML to add meaning. They just click
the little B, U and I buttons, play with the font and so on. They don't
know (or care) that they are using HTML.

HTML is bad for email. It's too easy for webbugs to be embedded in
message, too easy for you to be maliciously tricked into visiting
somewhere you didn't want to go (goatse.cx anyone?) and I think worst of
all, most HTML mail readers use the local web browser rendering engine.
The bugs in those are bad enough for general surfing, but in your
inbox they can bring a whole new world of pain.....

Perhaps the days of 7-bit plain ascii unformatted 72 column word-wrapped
black on white text in emails are over, but if you want to replace it,
find something that isn't HTML.
