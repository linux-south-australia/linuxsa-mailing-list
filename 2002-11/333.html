RE: Tape drive for FreeBSD

also as far as hardware, i wouldn't touch the quantum rebadged as ibm DLT
drives.  we've had a lot of issue which IBM palms off as 'driver' or
'firmware' problems.  the HP slim ones seem ok so far...




>I am looking into getting a tape drive to do backups on a freebsd
>system, what systems are the most easy to live with? At the moment I
>have Sony tape drives but they seem a little unfriendly to
>anything not MS...

We have both a Sony DAT and a Sony AIT in different boxes, both running
Linux and Arcserve, never had a problem with either drive.  We have had
hassles with other Sony DAT drives in a number of NT servers (hardware
fault, not OS) so I wouldn't recommend their DAT drives.

Depending on what your requirements are, I'd recommend AIT, its the first
one I've had
the pleasure of installing, but it kicks serious butt over DAT, the only
downside
is the cost of the media in comparison (work on price per MB and the sums
are
a bit easier to sell).

(I'm unsure how compatibility with FreeBSD goes, but I guess it wouldn't
be too far off that of Linux as far as tape hardware goes)
