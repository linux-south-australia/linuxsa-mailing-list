Debian Woody 3 and fuser -k on autofs...

I am currently working on a Debian Woody 3.0 2.4.18-bf24 as packaged
with the installation disks. I have a flash usb device, emulated as a
scsi disk setup by automount as:

flash -fstype=vfat,intr,sync,nodev,nosuid,user,uid=student,gid=student \
:/dev/sda1

..and it's mounting under /var/autofs/misc/flash quite nicely.

However, if I run (as root):

# fuser -k /var/autofs/misc/flash

..it doesn't unmount it and says use fuser -v to find it out what is
using it.

Now the glitch is that sometimes people will have konqueror (or
something) open and I want the fuser to unceremoniously terminate
konqueror | nautilus | the shell but it's *not*.

I've looked at an strace and it's going through the process list in
/proc nicely...

Interestingly this works under Libranet 2.7...
