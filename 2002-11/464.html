Re: Html Emails

Brian Astill <bastill@sa.apana.org.au> wrote:
> In the context of Art Galleries exchanging photographs of items for
> distribution from their collections, HTML might be good (though attached
> jpegs would surely accomplish the same objective).  In the context of
> the LinuxSA or FreeBSD lists HTML is just a costly nuisance.

I dispute that it's costly.  Granted people <u>can</u> embed stupid and
unnecessary objects, but that's a misuse of the tool and not a fault
of HTML per se.  I know it's not often that we need the expressiveness
of HTML, but it does solve some problems (for example overlong lines)
and on occassions you really do need something much better than HTML.
A comparison of ADSL offerings, for example, is easy in an HTML table, but
difficult within the confines of plain text because the 7x character line
limit is just not enough.  It's galling to have to trim and abbreviate
to fit that limit when you know that everyone can simply maximise their
mail window to fit more detail, and even those who can't will still get
a fair rendering because that's what HTML does.
