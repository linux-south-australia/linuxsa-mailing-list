> Here's an idea thou - on your linux box, where you have root access, setup

> 
> Now, use ssh, scp, sftp, or alternatively use its port-forwarding abilities
> to allow whatever the hell you like! ;)

This is where packet filtering will always fail -- if you leave
one single thing open, and there is someone who has access to
both sides of hte firewall, they can tunnel any IP (or even
non-IP) traffic through that link. Basically, if you have a
dertermined person on the inside, packet filtering won't do
anything but hinder them and give you a false sense of security.
It really becomes a management problem then... Though host
security will help (but you have to be pretty thorough -- if
someone can just plug their laptop ethernet into a port, that
won't help either... even if you can stop that, a really
determined person could tunnel ssh optically, encoding bits as
colour changes on a monitor... you can never counter a
determined insider with technology alone...)
