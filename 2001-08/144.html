The last line (quoted above) is really what it all comes down to. Where
is /dev/hdc9 usually mounted? Try booting up using the floppy and typing
'mount' and sending the output.

You see, init(8) lives in the /sbin directory. From what I can see of
your partitioning schema, you've split up the various major directories
and stuck them on their own partitions. That's not a bad thing. Possibly
not necessary on a machine that's fairly static (or a
development/test/muckaround machine).

/dev/hdc9 seems a little far down on the queue for my liking. We'll know
for sure after we receive the output from 'mount'.

Have you made any changes to the system post-install? Have you changed
/etc/lilo.conf (specifically the section that deals with Linux)?
