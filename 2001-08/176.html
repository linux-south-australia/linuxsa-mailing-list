Re: Security question

Brian Marr wrote:

> Aug  5 04:49:26 gringo ipppd[1515]: sent [0][LCP EchoRep id=0x7c f3 8e 0f a6
> 00 06 c0 0e]
> Aug  5 04:54:26 gringo ipppd[1515]: rcvd [0][LCP EchoReq id=0x7d da 6a da 55
> 00 bd f6 26]
> Aug  5 04:54:26 gringo ipppd[1515]: sent [0][LCP EchoRep id=0x7d f3 8e 0f a6
> 00 bd f6 26]

These are nothing to worry about - they're "PPP pings" (for want of a
more complex term), and are used by your ISP to check that the link
is still active.  As they occur at the LCP layer, they can only be
generated by the remote end of the PPP connection.

> Aug  5 04:55:19 gringo in.ftpd[3209]: warning: /etc/hosts.allow, line 5:
> missing ":" separator
> Aug  5 04:55:20 gringo in.ftpd[3209]: connect from 213.44.213.24
> (213.44.213.24)

This is the one you have to worry about; the reverse name entry maps
back to 213-44-213-24.rsv.club-internet.fr, so unless you've authorised
some French users access to your system, someone's been having a look
(and unless you have some firewalling in place, I'd be worrying about
what they've accessed that _isn't_ logged, but that's just me).  You'd
best check to see if your variant of ftp daemon (I don't know SuSe)
does any form of logging.  "man ftpd" and work your way from there.
