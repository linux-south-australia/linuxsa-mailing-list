RE: [OT] Virus Signatures

I had a quick read of one of the disassemblies of the Code Red 2 worm, and
it seems that the AAAA  or whatever is significant, in that you need a lot
of data to overflow a  buffer (i.e.. lots of X's or N's) used for an ASCII
to Unicode (double byte character) conversion.  Two of the characters from
the converted buffer then get used to form the jump address into the actual
worm code.  In the case of CodeRed2, that code ends up at an address
something like 0x004E004E (which when written in ASCII is NN).

I'm no expert in assembler, or buffer overflows by any means, but that's how
I read the analysis.  So its not really a case of the Black Hat's signing
their work, as a case of being the easiest way to achieve the desired effect
when overflowing the buffer.

Jim.
