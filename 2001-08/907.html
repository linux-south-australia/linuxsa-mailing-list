Re: Gigabit Routing?

On 24 Aug 2001 11:10:41 +0930, John Edwards wrote:
> > Switches usually have the option of setting broadcast ranges on each port.
>
> They do? I'd say that depends largely on how much you spend on your
> switch. Half decent switches have broadcast storm control, but it's only
> at Layer 2. To handle broadcasts for a specific set of IP addresses is a
> Layer3 activity, and if your switch does that it's practically a router
> anyway.

Switches already handle destination MAC addresses. Broadcast packets go
to broadcast MAC addresses.

ASICs handle the routine switching and forward the special cases to be
handled by the (usually quite weak) onboard CPU. That's how it counts
the types of packets for implementing storm control, SNMP statistics,
cached ARP queries and other goodies.

Perhaps I've only seen good switches, but I haven't heard bad things
about 3Com so I thought it was worth checking for that option,
especially since it was no cost.

> Didn't UniSA have an extremely large switched-only network at one stage?

So did I.
