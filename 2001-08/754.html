Re: virus infects on boot sector

On 21 Aug 2001 01:20:38 +0800, thanhnhan... wrote:

> when you have boot disk sector infected that means that
> you cannot boot from hard drive , cannnot see "C:" drive
> or dos FDISK program. The boot sector of hard drive had
> been altered by the infected virus. Ofcourse you cannot
> access C ; therefore cannot scan from virus software.

No, that's not quite right. Boot sector viruses don't stop you using
your hard disk. I'm yet to see or hear of a virus that actually renders
the hard disk useless.

It's booting off of that hard disk that will generally nuke your data or
do some other similarly evil thing.

Boot off of a floppy disk and do a low-level format. Some BIOSes have an
inbuilt function to do this.
