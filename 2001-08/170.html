Well you're right & you're wrong ;-)

There definately is a new CodeRed. You can identify it by a string located
at offset 0x240 in the packets.

NEW CodeRedII
230 : 00 00 00 43 6F 64 65 52 65 64 49 49 00 8B 1C 24   ...CodeRedII...$

Old CodeRed
230 : FF FF FF FF 8B 85 68 FE FF FF 83 E8 07 89 85 F4   ......h.........

It also has a shorter Content-Length string.
Content-Lenght: 3379 against Content-Lenght: 3569

But You are wrong about the addresses it is attacking.
I have web servers in 207.168.0.0/16 that are getting attacked by it.
