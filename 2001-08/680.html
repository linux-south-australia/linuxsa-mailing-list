telnet vulnerability thingy...

Nothing to be alarmed about.
Most people know about this already.
But just in case...

In the last 24 hours, I've been noticing systematic
probes of my telnet ports. 
Of course, when I realised a year ago that leaving the
telnet service active is the Wrong Thing, I immediately
disabled it on all machines. And I continue to remove
it within an hour of installing any new distribution.

According to this:
http://www.linuxsecurity.com/articles/forums_article-3519.html#telnet
linux telnet is now exploitable.
And what's worse, there are people out there doing this sort of
thing:

Aug 18 03:46:58 dog kernel: IN=ppp0 OUT=eth0 SRC=209.94.221.63 DST=203.38.148.56 LEN=48 TOS=0x00 PREC=0x00 TTL=105 ID=23012 DF PROTO=TCP SPT=4724 DPT=23 WINDOW=8192 RES=0x00 SYN URGP=0

This is coming from cuscon4557.tstt.net.tt
in Trinidad and Tobago. Probably a hacked machine.

So anyone with Debian, Caldera or Mandrake should check
that their telnet is off or fixed.
Does anyone know if the other distributions are affected?
I can't find anything on the SuSE security page. But RH has this:
http://www.redhat.com/support/errata/RHSA-2001-099.html

Hmmm. The RH site says that it's a local vulnerability only.
So why would a machine in Trinidad and Tobago be
scanning the world's telnet ports?
