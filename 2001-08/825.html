Re: What to put in /etc/sources.list for Debian ...

On Tue, Aug 21, 2001 at 05:24:31PM +0930, Richard Sharpe wrote:
> Hi,
> 
> I am running Debian Potato and want to use apt-get to upgrade from 
> ftp://ftp.au.debian.org.
> 
> What do I put in /etc/sources.list so I can upgrade to, say Woody?

haven't seen any replies to this, so:

I have (in my /etc/apt/sources.list):

deb http://security.debian.org/debian-security potato/updates main contrib non-free
deb http://security.debian.org/debian-non-US potato/non-US main contrib non-free
deb http://security.debian.org/ potato/updates main contrib non-free
deb http://ftp.planetmirror.com/pub/debian potato main contrib non-free
deb http://ftp.planetmirror.com/pub/debian-non-US potato/non-US main contrib non-free

Just substitute "potato" for "woody" and that's it.
(note: potato == stable, woody == testing, sid == unstable)

http or ftp, probably doesn't matter, http you don't have to login though.

(don't forget to "apt-get update" after changing your sources.list)
