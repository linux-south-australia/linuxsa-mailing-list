That's a really vague request for advice, with insufficient
background info, but....
The only thing I'd recommend to anyone buidling or buying
a PC for linux is to just make sure you have plenty of RAM.
At least 128 MBytes - if you want to have a good "experience".
Virtually anything else, as long as it isn't too wierd,
should be fine.
Just check here first:
http://hardware.redhat.com/

The question about the x-window manager doesn't make sense.
If you mean "variants" rather than "versions", then there are
maybe a dozen or so X window managers to choose from.

As to the partitions, you only need to ensure that either:
1.	You have < 1024 cylinders on your boot disk, or
2.	You create a /boot partition, or
3.	You don't mind risking wierd things happening
	when you boot under certain circumstances.
