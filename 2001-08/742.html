These cards sound suspiciously like the cards I have. They've always
worked under Linux, but they originally didn't work under Windows 98 1st
Edition. Then I reinstalled sound sound drivers and video drivers and
they worked.

Try the following steps:

1) Forget about DHCP for a while
2) Give them an address you know works on your system
3) Try "ping" to the DHCP server

I suspect that Windows isn't running the cards right.

Make sure you have the latest updates.

ALSO, try to think of how your computer differs from the other two
computers; if there really is a network happening then that may provide
a clude.

Finally, not that this is much help, I've seen Windows machines not
"see" DHCP for no reason that I can fathom at all before :-(
