> There is another interesting board out there, which is the Axis embedded 
> developer board: http://developer.axis.com/hardware/devboard/. It has a 
> RISC processor, an ethernet port, 2MB of flash and 8MB DRAM.
> 
> The 2MB flash makes it a bit limiting, though, since you could not get 
> enough of a file system on it.

I guess it would depend on what you needed and how you went about it. I
think that the RAM would be the biggest limiting factor. If you had twice
as much RAM, the hardware could be used for a great deal of things. You
could have the custom kernel and tarballs of the root file system in flash
(tar has less space overhead than most filesystems) and mount a 4MB
ramdisk as the root FS. I believe that this is how the Linux Router
project and other little-bitty floppy distributions work with 1.44, 1.6
and 1.7 MB floppy disks.
