Re: Dell pulls the plug on desktop Linux

Mark Newton wrote:
> 
>  > For all we know, Dell might have not the slightest interest
>  > in the linux customer. We may be mere bargaining chips to them.
> 
> Like, duh.

Actually, I think Dell is in a tough spot there.  About
25-30% of their server sales specify Linux, and Linux
server sales have a higher margin than Windows server
sales (as the servers tend to be at the higher end of
the market -- say aimed at Internet web serving than
at workgroup file/print).

So Linux is no mere bargaining chip, which must make it
hard when talking Windows pricing across the entire Dell
range with Microsoft.  If Dell drop Linux on servers,
then they are effectively leaving the bit of the business
that has any appreciable profit.  But if they annoy
Microsoft too much (say by MS simply drawing out
negotiations whilst other vendors get cheaper access to
Windows XP) then Dell also lose.

It is possible that Dell not selling Linux to punters
is due to Micrsoft pressure, but there's no way of
telling from the outside and their 'lack of demand'
explanation does seem reasonable.
