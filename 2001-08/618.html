On Thu, Aug 16, 2001 at 09:46:05AM +0930, Anthony Symons wrote:

 > > eg: I have a /30 subnet here at home... (ie netmask 255.255.255.252)
 > 
 > Yeah, you can do what you like, but technically, it is correct and
 > people keep saying "thats not correct".

"That's not correct". 

There is no such thing as a Class-A, Class-B, Class-C address anymore.
There are merely /8, /16 and /24 networks, and they basically never get
allocated (e.g., the smallest allocation you can get from APNIC is a /20.
What would you call that?  A "Class-B-and-a-half"?).

Hand in hand with that is the idea that "default" netmasks are obselete.
The netmask used for a particular network is derived from the subnetting
scheme of the organization that's using it, not from some historical 
default based on the first few bits of the network's address.

We've only been using CIDR addressing now for, like, 7 or 8 years.
Get over it!  If you want to carry on like grandpa does when he's moaning
about how different things were in his day that's absolutely fine, but
try to remember that we don't live in those days anymore, the world has
moved on.
