Re: RH 7.1 Processes

On Mon, Aug 13, 2001 at 08:54:21AM +0930, Jim O'Halloran wrote:

> Hi...  I've got a Red Hat 7.1 box running here as primary DNS and mail
> server for our domains.  Looking at the process tree (ps axf) for this
> machine there seem to be a lot of things running on the box.  Problem is,
> I'm not 100% sure exactly what I need, and what I don't.  Obviously, the
> named and sendmail processes are essential, as is xinetd (qpopper runs under
> that).  I need crond for a few scheduled jobs.  

[ ... ]

> Output from a 'ps axf' taken at 3am this morning by a cron job.
> 
>   PID TTY      STAT   TIME COMMAND
>   437 ?        S      0:00 portmap

Portmap is used for the RPC stuff which is required by NFS. Probably not
needed on an NS/MX server.

>   536 ?        S      0:00 /usr/sbin/apmd -p 10 -w 5 -W -P
> /etc/sysconfig/apm-sc

APM? Unless it's a laptop, I can't see any reason for it, especially on
a server.

>   585 ?        S      0:04 /usr/sbin/automount --timeout 60 /misc file
> /etc/auto

I've not played with automount much, but I think it's the thing that
automatically mounts (hence the name) removable media such as CDs when
you 'cd' to the directory they've been set to be mounted in. Again,
probably not needed for a server.

>  2477 ?        S      0:00 /bin/sh /usr/lib/sa/sa1 600 6
>  2479 ?        S      0:00  \_ /usr/lib/sa/sadc 600 6 /var/log/sa/sa13

I'm not sure what this does. Perhaps you'd be able to enlighten us?
