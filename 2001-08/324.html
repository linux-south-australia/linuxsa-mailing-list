Andrew Catford wrote:
> Consider:
> [example showing traditional buffer overflow]

There's nothing particularly illuminating about that example, other than
the claim that MS compiler can't be exploited.  Yet quite clearly two
craps were allocated in immediate succession, so an overflow of the first
crap's buffer can overwrite the pointer to the second crap's buffer_output
function.  The example is just as exploitable, using the same general
technique, with either an MS or a GNU compiler.  I find it interesting
that someone would claim that the MS compiler is not vulnerable, yet
fail to notice that it really is.  That is the real moral of the story:
Weak code is weak code, and if you rely on the compiler to protect you,
you will (deservedly) get bitten.
