Re: Gigabit Routing?

>Here is the Linux question. Can Linux boxes be used to provide 1Gb routing
>in this situation?

I havn't actually tried Gigabit routing with Linux, but I imagine it's a
pretty big ask to get the full bandwidth given that the PCI bus isn't that
fast.

Do any of your applications actually require a Gigabit? Chances are that
they don't and multiple 100mb virtual links in your Gigabit connection will
suffice.

In that case you could use VLANs on your switch (I think the better 3com
stuff does dotQ vlans) to get either 100Mb lan extensions between similar
school areas, or break a gigabit up into more managable chunks for routing
at 100mb/s.
