On 16 Aug 2001 11:18:02 +0930, Phil Hutton wrote:

> Actually, I thought PHP was a recursive acronym standing for "PHP:
> Hypertext Preprocessor".

It is now, but has not always had this meaning.

    "...some time in 1994 when Rasmus Lerdof put together a bunch of
    Perl scripts to track down who was looking at his resume. Little by
    little, people started to get interested in the scripts, and they
    were later released as a package "Personal Home Page" tools (the
    first meaning of PHP). In view of the interest, he wrote a scripting
    engine and incorporated  another tool to parse input from HTML
    forms: FI, Form Interpreter, thus creating what was called variously
    PHP/FI or PHP2. This was done around mid-1995."

    	-- p9, "Professional PHP Programming", Wrox Press.

A bit of useles information. At least I can now claim more than one use
for this book![1]

   - andrew

[1] Seriously, it's a fairly useful book. If you're interested in PHP,
it's an excellent start. Covers everything from variables to LDAP. I
