Opening just the ports you require can be very useful to track
internally misconfigured machines or viruses.  I've picked up a number
of viruses where an internal machine get infected and then tries to
contact an external host (to alert it to a new invected host).  If I
had that port open (or all ports) then that virus or Trojan could
cause additional damage which I may or may not have noticed.

So even if you trust your internal users to "do the right thing" I
still wouldn't allow access to every port.  If they don't know how to
look after their machines (as most users don't) then you can save
yourself and them from additional damage if you close of unnessesary
ports.   

I've mentioned this before but it's relavent here so I'll say it
again.  There are 3 things you should consider when designing a
firewall.  These are:   
   o Security - well der
   o Control - This is what you guys seem to be discussing
   o Watchfulness - This is what I'm talking about
