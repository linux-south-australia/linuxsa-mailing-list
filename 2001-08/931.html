Re: budget shaper case study

At 9:33 PM +0930 23/8/01, Alan Kennington wrote:
>By the way, on the subject of webhosting being "usually run out
>of an ISP's premises", this has always struck me as curious.
>ISPs worldwide do make this sort of assumption, although there
>are no totally compelling reasons to not have web sites collocated
>with a business premises. I guess it's a cultural thing.

No, it's an economic thing.

Does your home have :-

- Large three phase Uninterruptable Power Supply
- Generator Set
- 24x7 monitored security
- Physical security around the server units
- Rack mounting systems for boxes and cables
- Multiple high bandwidth upstream feeds with automatic failover
- No additional costs for high bandwidth tail to your office
- Automated backup systems driving high density robot tape stacker units
- Bandwidth and server capacity upgrades on a dynamic, no-added-cost-basis
- Separation of the security domain of your public web server from 
your private network

?

So you're right, I guess it's not totally compelling, Alan.

It's only compelling if the above matters to you.

If don't care about the performance or availability of your web site, 
put it in your office, that's perfectly fine. Most people who do that 
simply haven't (yet) had their link or their server or their 
power/security environment fail on them. Yet. So the only cultural 
issue here is a cultural question in terms of whether you actually 
give a darn about quality of service of your offerings to your own 
customers.
