webserver collocation [was: RE: budget shaper case study]

> By the way, on the subject of webhosting being "usually run out
> of an ISP's premises", this has always struck me as curious.
> ISPs worldwide do make this sort of assumption, although there
> are no totally compelling reasons to not have web sites collocated
> with a business premises. I guess it's a cultural thing.

Well, as an eg, if you have a site that requires 24*7 support, and high
bandwidth, then it may not be economical to provide that at your business
premises... At the other end, if you have a small, low bandwidth site, but
you want it available most of the time, and run your business from a site
with a non-permanent dialup connection, or with dynamic IP... etc...

There are a number of legitimate, non-cultural reasons why collocation is
chosen by many (perhaps most)businesses...
