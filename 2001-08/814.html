> what dist are you running? potato is the stable one -- if you are running

To paraphrase the Linux PocketBook: "The Debian Project considers man
pages and other documentation not in the right place, or non existent as
a bug. Report these things as such". And, from this list: "Debian
Unstable [it may be the Pocketbook actually] tends to be more stable
than other [nameless] distributions".

Whilst I think Debian is a good distribution and that apt-get has got
the right idea, I'd hazard a guess that apt-get is probably getting used
by the wrong people; that is to say, Debian appears to be a hacker's
Linux and apt-get is a really good way to keep up with all the hackers'
work.

Of course, RedHat and others tend to bomb their beta distributions and
this is to be expected. But generally, things like RPM still work...
