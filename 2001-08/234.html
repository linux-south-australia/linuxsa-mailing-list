Re: CodeRedII - New non-variant codered worm - Analysis.

On Mon, Aug 06, 2001 at 02:57:19PM +0930, Jim O'Halloran wrote:

 > Patching an NT4 Box is a simple matter of installing Service Pack 6, plus a
 > single hotfix...  Took me two hours to download the hotfix and install on
 > our 5 NT4 servers here (two reboots each, so about 5-10 minutes per machine
 > of downtime is required).  

Only if you ignore the fact that you'll need to do it again in a few
weeks, and again after that, and again after that... ad infinitum.  And
if you ignore the fact that installing SP6 breaks a substantial number
of NT boxen so they need to be reinstalled anyway (so, you got lucky,
doesn't mean everyone else did).  And if you ignore the fact that a 
significant number of patches from MS over the last 6 months have either
not fixed the problem they claimed to fix, or have introduced new problems
which even the most rudimentary testing should have uncovered.

At some point you will plan to upgrade your NT4 servers to NT2000, which
will either involve a box swap or an extended downtime period.  If the
boxen are going to be down anyway, you might as well replace them with
something that's maintainable (because NT clearly *isn't* maintainable).
