Michael,

Although no one cares about Code Red any more, it looks like this
no-default.ida version of CR2 has been analysed and interpreted
at incidents.org:

http://www.incidents.org/diary/diary.php

Part way down the page, they say:

=========================================================
Notice that the "GET /default.ida?" part of the request is missing. 
This request is not dangerous in terms of propagating the infection 
since the request would never reach the faulty ISAPI extension. 
However, it is unclear what is corrupting the data. Some people 
have theorized that intermediary routers or repeaters are to 
blame, others speculate that corrupted memory on infected hosts 
may be the source of the problem.
=========================================================
