Re: Robust date parsers

dan@tellurian.com.au wrote:

> This is particularly important when converting to Maildir or Maildir++
> formats, where the date is part of the filename. My particular source
> is Cyrus (prior to version 2.0, ie the message-per-file one) but most of
> the problems are generic to MH and others.

How is this a problem? My experience with maildir is that you can put
any old file in there and it will be treated as a message, it then
becomes the MUA's problem to sort out dates and interpret the message
format. For both POP and IMAP (with qmail and courier-imap), I can't see
where the filename *requires* the date to be a part of it, since the
order is pulled from the filesystem dates anyway. 

The filename is used by the Imap software for some basic message
management (message read, marked as deleted, etc).

The filename nature leads to another neat thing you can do with maildir
message, you can place a symbolic link to a file. Great for bulletins
that you might want to recall or modify at a later date.
