I think the reason you have not had any replies is that the question is
very open, as in you are not being specific about what you are trying to
configure/setup

I will try and give you a few pointers although I am pretty new to modem
config in linux and have never used debian.

basically if you are looking at creating a dial-up to the net you would
need to configure the ppp daemon
try typing 'man pppd' in a console window (without the quotes) and it
should tell you a little about how it works

with regards to the modem, you basically need to point whatever is using
it, like pppd, at the device
now the device is just like a com port, and you should know you have
your main com ports com1, com2, com3 & com4

on RH7.1  /dev/ttys0 is com1       /dev/ttys1 is com2 and so on

so if your modem is com3, the pppd need to be told to use /dev/ttys2
you then need to configure chap or pap scripts to authenticate logins
etc

now if you want to let people dial into your machine then you should
look at mgetty

try reading from http://groups.google.com it is the best source of
information but requires you do the groundwork
http://groups.google.com/groups?q=pppd+debian+modem+internal+config&meta
=site%3Dgroups  - pppd
http://groups.google.com/groups?q=mgetty+debian+modem+internal+config&me
ta=site%3Dgroups - mgetty

I would also imagine there is heaps of mails about pppd, mgetty and
modem config in the archives for linuxsa
goto their website
http://www.linuxsa.org.au/mailing-list/archive-index.html
and have a browse through

hope this sets you off in the right direction
