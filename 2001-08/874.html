Re: budget shaper case study

Alan Kennington wrote:

> Just in case anyone's still interested in the
> issue of download limits for broadband ISPs,
> and so forth, there's an exciting link on this
> subject:
> 
> http://gisol.org/gisol/11/a.html

Except he's talking about webhosting, which is usually run out
of an ISP's premises, and is unrelated to broadband.  In addition,
while you may think it is possible to bandwidth throttle that
sort of traffic to a web server, bear in mind the fact that a
lot of domains are virtually hosted these days, including this
gisol[1] one.  Simple enough to check; go to http://gisol.org/,
take a note of the page.  Then do a "dig a gisol.org", and go
to the IP address of that (216.117.169.162).  Not the same page
(the reverse name lookup gives nameservices.net).  How will your
traffic shaper distinguish that sort of traffic?

[1]  At least, where it's currently residing.
