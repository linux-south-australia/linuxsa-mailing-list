Re: Mail only accounts

* Corey Gilmore (cfg@dln.uvm.edu) wrote:
> How would I go about creating some email only accounts?
> 
> Say I have domain1.com and domain2.com.  What I want is for
> foo@domain1.com and foo@domain2.com be able to receive their own mail, but
> not necessairily have shell access.  /etc/mail/virtusertable seems to be
> close to what I want, but I want to be able to create multiple POP/IMAP
> only accounts for domain1 and domain2.

Hi,

Create two normal user accounts "foo1" and "foo2". Set their login shells to
"/bin/false"[1]. This will stop them from both logging into the system and
logging into your ftp daemon, assuming that your /etc/shells doesn't have
/bin/false in it.

Add two entries to /etc/mail/virtusertable:
foo@domain1.com		foo1
foo@domain2.com		foo2

Of course, it might make more sense to use usernames which contain a small
part of the domain, eg d1_foo and d2_foo. But it's up to you.
