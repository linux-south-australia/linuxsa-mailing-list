Re: Arrrgh, another RedHat screwup in 7.1: /usr/include/linux?

Hi.

Sorry, I missed the point? Redhat has had init files in /etc/rc.d and
/etc/rc.d/init.d for as long as I can remember. That's at least 4.0.

RH 6.1:

[michael@sandwich michael]$ ls /etc/rc.d
init.d/  rc.firewall*  rc.sysinit*  rc1.d/  rc3.d/  rc5.d/
rc*      rc.local*     rc0.d/       rc2.d/  rc4.d/  rc6.d/

Items in those directories are links into the init.d directory.

Not sure what you are getting at with the /usr/include/linux statement,
but should this be called a 'screw-up' when it's crime is to be different
from some other distro? I'm not aware of this _breaking_ anything? It's
not like the problems caused by the inclusion of the wrong gcc or whatever
a while back.

Michael
