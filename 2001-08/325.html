I agree.  It's different horses for different courses; I like to use
printf() because it makes me think more about the problem, which some
people can do with a debugger.  Stuck inside a triple-nested loop with
a printf at the inner most wouldn't be fun, however.  Whenever I fire
up gdb, I usually have to spend more time acquainting myself with its
interface than solving the problem at hand, which is a shortcoming on
my part (or maybe not - I like to think spending less time on the
debugger is a good thing[1]).  I think this all stems from teaching
first and second year students C (a while ago), who write and debug
in a complete different fashion to me (ie, gobs of code, then long
hours randomly jumping about with the debugger).
