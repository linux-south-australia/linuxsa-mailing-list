Robust date parsers

I am looking for a very robust RFC822 date parser. I have a large
collection of dates pulled from RFC822 messages that the Python and Zope
parsers have trouble with, and I am just moving on to Perl now. RFC822
dates are really messy because the implementations are all over the place.
All of these come from real messages.

Skipping the obvious bad dates (such as "---------------" and "[]") there
are others like:

Fri, 10 Jul 1998 17:49:29 +0930
Friday, July 10, 1998 4:05 PM
Sat, 11 Jul 1998 18:35:09 +0930 (CST)
Tue, 4 Aug 1998 07:42:21 +1000 (east)
   5/5/99 4:48 PM  
21 Jul 99 05:15:25 +0930
Mon, 26 Jul 1999 05:53:48 +0000 () 
14 Aug 99 03:11:08 
den 7 februari 2000 01:50  
Monday 21 February 
 3/2/00
den 30 mars, 2000
Thursday - 31 August 2000 9:15.

... and so on. I just sorted through a few by hand.

Has anyone found, for example, that some database has a particularly
robust set of date normalisation routines?

This is particularly important when converting to Maildir or Maildir++
formats, where the date is part of the filename. My particular source 
is Cyrus (prior to version 2.0, ie the message-per-file one) but most of
the problems are generic to MH and others.
