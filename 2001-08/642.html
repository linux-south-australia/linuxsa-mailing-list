Re: Damn you dhcpd

On Thu, Aug 16, 2001 at 02:18:16PM +0930, David Newall wrote:

 > > There is no such thing as a Class-A, Class-B, Class-C address anymore.
 > 
 > I notice that network 191.253 is not assigned (or has no in-addr.arpa
 > map.)  If what you say is true, then I could theoretically anticipate
 > being given 20 bits from that.  Do you believe that will ever occur?

The allocation policies of the various regional NICs, and descriptions
of the address space they have control over, are on their websites.

Address space has either been approportioned out to regional NICs
or reserved for future regional NICs.  If 191.253 is in the former
group then its ultimate fate will be decided by that NIC's allocation
policy.  If it's in the latter group, then its future use is undefined.

 > I suspect that, no, all the class B addresses are reserved for allocations
 > of 16 (host) bits or more.  I could be wrong.  What do you think?

By that logic 63.0.0.0/8 would have been reserved for allocations of
24 (host) bits or more.  But it isn't, it has been broken up and
distributed among the various NICs for the purpose of providing address
space for broadband users.

There's no reason to believe that 191.253.0.0/16 won't be treated 
similarly.
