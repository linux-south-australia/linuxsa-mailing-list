LDAP (or indeed, most other) backends being added to Samba will be a
huge step to making Samba 2.2.x as close to an NT PDC as possible.
Things like user account expiry and workstation restrictions, just start
working. I believe that they are going to try to make it as similar as
possible to the Windows 2000 Active Directory schema, to make the next
step easier.

As far as I know, that stuff isn't working at the moment. I think that
Jerry Carter is one of the people working on it. There's been a little
more activity on samba-technical, regarding LDAP, recently and I know
that they already have their Enterprise Number as assigned by the IANA.

Having said that, Samba TNG supported LDAP quite well when I last
checked (http://www.samba-tng.org/). Samba TNG has other problems,
though.

If you are still interested in Samba/LDAP, please read the HOWTOs that
were written on the topic. I can't remember who it's by or where it
lives, but you can probably still find a link from
http://www.kneschke.de/projekte/samba_tng/faq/index.php3. There used to
be a 'More Information' link. If it's not there, try somewhere on the
Samba TNG site (http://www.samba-tng.org/).

Hope it helps,
