Linux still needs good administration too though.. and although it is better
and needs less maintenance when implemented well, it is harder to reach that
state. The out-of-the-box functionality and security keeps improving, and
starts getting easier to use for people brought up with Windoze.

Personally, I like to keep things where I can vi them, but for more complex
issues, GUIs are a bit nicer; as long as Linux distros continue to provide
both remote-control-by-shell and gui wrappers for the ppl who want them,
I'll be happy. All it takes is a recompile and 5 minutes of 'rm -rf'ing to
remove the bloat.. you can't say that about windows..
