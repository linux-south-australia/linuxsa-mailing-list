> Also is there a way of running a Dynamic Mail server.

yes... you just run it, and set up the MX record in your DNS...

However, you will run into problems, as your mail server's address will most
likely end up cached somewhere, and someone will be sending mail to the
wrong box, thinking it's you, and will be unable to connect, or worse, will
connect to someone else's mail server.

the problem with dynamic dns for servers is that there is usually the
assumption that a server is:

a) always on
b) static ip

and you can't quite tell what some other piece of software is going to do
when you aren't both of these. If it's non-essential, and you won't hurt
anyone, you could try it, but bear in mind that when you change your ip,
whoever gets your old one will also get some of your old traffic.
