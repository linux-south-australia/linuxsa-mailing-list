> Being reasonably new to Linux any advice before I start would be much 

Dont expect it to be like redhat or mandrake, Debian assumes you know what you 
want to do, and how to do it. No point+and+click install, but it is fairly 
smart about what to do, so is actually quite easy, Partitioning is a bit hairy 
if you have never used anything other than disk-drake or another newbie program.

Make sure you are comfortable with using cfdisk, configuring X from scratch, 
stuff like that.

Debian is harder to install, harder to use, but is Much, Much more rewarding 
than RedHat or Mandrake. Take the plunge and install it, dont let what i have 
said scare you off.

Peter Bock said it well: "Bite off more than you can chew, then chew like hell!"
