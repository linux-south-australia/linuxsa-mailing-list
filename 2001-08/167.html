Dell pulls the plug on desktop Linux

Dell pulls the plug on desktop Linux 

By Ashlee Vance 
August 2, 2001 1:20 am PT

http://iwsun4.infoworld.com/articles/hn/xml/01/08/02/010802hnnolin.xml?
0802thpm
  
 DELL HAS QUIETLY stopped offering the Linux operating system as an 
option on its desktop and laptop PCs, saying low demand forced the 
Linux-advocate to pull the software from its online stores.

Dell has championed the open-source operating system through 
investments in companies such as Red Hat and Linux desktop software 
maker Eazel, which has since gone out of business. Founder and Chief 
Executive Officer Michael Dell gave a speech at the LinuxWorld 
conference last year in San Jose, Calif., where he remarked that "the 
only thing growing faster than Linux is Linux on Dell."

While the company has seen strong sales of Linux on workstations and 
servers, it has sold few desktops and laptops this year loaded with 
Linux, Dell spokeswoman Sarah Lavender said. The Austin, Texas-based 
vendor dropped Linux from its PCs and laptops about six weeks ago but 
did not announce the move publicly, she said.
