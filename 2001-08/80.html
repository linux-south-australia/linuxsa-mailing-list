The rate seems to be more or less stable over the time from
midnight (Adelaide time) to the present.
If the IP address for probes are determined at random out of
the 4 billion possible, then the rate of probes should
be proportional to the number of numerical IPs you've got.
I'd hate to be routing a big block right now.

Code Red seems to be emitting about 100 probes per hour to
each IP address in the world, for a total of possibly
400 Billion per hour worldwide.
That's about 100 million probes per second being generated
by about 150,000 hosts or something (CNN's number), 
which sounds a bit high
to me in terms of probes per infected host.
I don't see how they can be emitting 650 probes per second 
each. Maybe the infected host count is higher now already.

Anyway, here's a sensible article from the Register:
http://www.theregister.co.uk/content/55/20779.html

As they say, you can't call a worm financially damaging if
all it does is to force sysadmins to fix their dud software,
which is supposedly part of their job.
