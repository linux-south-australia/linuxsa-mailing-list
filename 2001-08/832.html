budget shaper case study

Just in case anyone's still interested in the
issue of download limits for broadband ISPs,
and so forth, there's an exciting link on this
subject:

http://gisol.org/gisol/11/a.html

I had been saying that download limits with penalty pricing for
excessive use could lead to very high bills, and therefore
one should insert a linux box in the path to
"shape the budget" - a "budget shaper".
This link puts on the record just such a case.
This guy was sent a bill for about A$30,000 for
traffic - although there were various convolutions in
the story.

I'm sure that anyone who's interesting in bandwidth
limiting for broadband will be interested in this!

Cheers,
