> busy
often this happens with the driver not finding the right io address &
irq for the device.

if you use Windows(R)(C)(TM) on the machine as well, try to find the io
address & irq.
(Control Panel->System i think)

then use a command as such:

modprobe sym53c416 io=0x100 irq=10 (or something, not 0x100 & 10 :)

just to make sure the driver isnt already loaded, run "lsmod". 
(in case it's being loaded at boot)

other than that, i don't know - my only 'experience' with modules is
with my sound card.
