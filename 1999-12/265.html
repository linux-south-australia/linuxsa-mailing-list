Re: Mail Filtering

On Mon, 13 Dec 1999, Adam Hawes wrote:
> I currently fetch all my mail from my ISP using fetchmail.  My local
> mailer is Sendmail.  My ISP was nice enough to give me a multi-post
> mailbox, so all email to my subdomain ends up in one mailbox.

I assume the problem here is that by the time the email gets to you the
envelope address(*) has been lost.  Your ISP might already be providing
this information (and if not you could ask if it could be done.)  Perhaps
your ISP might add (or replace) a header like "X-Original-Recipient:".
Mine(+) puts the envelope address in a "Received:" header (see for <...> in
the last line):

> Received: from koala.aussie.net (koala.aussie.net [203.56.129.2])
>          by rebel.net.au (8.8.5/8.8.4) with ESMTP
>          id OAA22086 for <davidn@rebel.net.au>; Mon, 13 Dec 1999 14:20:07 +1030



(*) Mail messages come with various headers, most of which could be a pure
fiction.  There's no requirement for the sender's address to appear
anywhere in the mail headers, nor even any recipient.  Your actual
recipient address is carried in an "envelope", which is used to route and
deliver the message by each mail daemon along the way.  The envelope is
often discarded at final delivery.

(+) I am my own ISP.
