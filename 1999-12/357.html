I'm biased, because I work for Internode, SA's largest locally-owned ISP.  

We have more external connectivity than any other ISP in the state
(and most of them in the country), with three 100 Mbit/sec links to
different sources of bandwidth (Telstra, connect.com.au and SAIX),
Satellite bytes direct from the US, very fast internal infrastucture
based exclusively around Cisco 100 Mbps switches, backup power, backup
airconditioning (very important :-), backups of all of our servers, 
a professional controlled environment with pin-number and swipe-card
security, 24 hour monitoring, 24 hour support, a "No busy signals, EVER"
modem pool provisioning policy, etc.  We're the State Government's ISP,
so reliability, speed and professionalism are the centerpoints of the
business.

http://www.on.net/

If you want to inspect our facilities, we're at 31 York Street, Adelaide
(in the laneway behind Cafe Buongiorno, near the Ngapartji Internet
Cafe).  We are justifiably proud of the setup we have, and we welcome
visitors who want to see it and have their questions about it answered
(it's good geek-value too :-).  See http://www.on.net/why.html for a 
more spirited description of why we're better than the competition.

Prices for dialup access are at http://www.on.net/s_modem.html -- You
can see that we're not a budget-price ISP, but we're no more expensive
than other ISPs which are offering a similar (but, in our opinion,
