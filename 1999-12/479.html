Re: Network cards

Hi,

> Your advice, please.
> I want to set up a small network & thought it would be a GOOD idea to have
> cards with jumpers so I could be CERTAIN of the IRQ and address each card
> was using.

I haven't seen a PCI Jumpered card ever, haven't seen an ISA one for a
while now.

> 
> However, Messrs Westan advise:
> "None of the PCI ethernet cards have jumpers on them. I think it's the same
> for all brands of PCI ethernet cards. On most ISA ethernet cards, you can
> run the SETUP driver disk to configure it to Plug 'n' Play or Jumperless
> (legacy)."
> 
> What should I do?

Try an SN3200 card. Comes with Linux drivers, and is PCI, and is
configurable through software. Load DOS boot disk. Insert network driver
disk, run setup program, take out of Plug and Pray mode, and assign IRQ,
and other stuff if necessary. Has successfuly been assigned for me in the
past.
