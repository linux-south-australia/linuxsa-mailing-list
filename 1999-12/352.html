Re: ISP Recommendations

I know people from SENET to, that do tech support and without being too
disparaging, the ones I know(who are the ones you are likely to get if you
call up know zip about linux). 

This is however true for nearly all ISPs, and understandably so given the
majority of their clients are Win and too a lesser extent Mac users.

Many ISPs have a sort of(unofficial) philosophy, if you are smart enough
to use linux you are smart enough to set it up for dial in.

Which can of course be very easy with stuff like kppp.

And if it is other services you want support for(ie other than pop/imap
mail client set up,browser set up and dial in config), then you probably
are not looking for "regular" dial in access, and then you will in all
probablity have acces to different support staff(in many cases at
appropriate consultation rates), and maybe should be
be looking at soemone like connect or telstra direct.

Of course the network operations/sys admin staff of SENET and most
other ISPs know tons about linux, but they are not likely the people who
will be solving your day to day support issues.
