Re: LaTeX - section numbering problems

Nick Morrison wrote:

> 
> But - if I use \chapter instead of \part, I /almost/ get what I want. I
> say /almost/ because it numbers everything /perfectly/, exactly as I want,
> but it puts "CHAPTER x" at the top of each chapter, which doesn't look
> good in a report!  So - I'm guessing I can tell the \chapter command to
> not write "CHAPTER x", right?  I'm going to have a look, but tell me if
> it's not possible or totally complicated *grin*

try using \documentclass{article} instead of \documentclass{report}. If
you want to change a few things there, you can say (for eg)
\documentclass[a4paper,twoside,notitlepage]{article}, or any other
options you want... Should make it look more like a report...

There's plenty of packages our there to make Chapter headings look
nicer, but they are REALLY hard to comprehend unless you know what
you're doing (I don't...)

> 
> > If you really need Parts, then initialise the Part counter
> > manually.
> 
> I /may/ look into this..  unless I can think of another way to format and
> section this document!  I need one more level of numbered sectioning than
> section/subsection/subsubsection will give me.  I think.

\paragraph{}, \subparagraph{}, \subsubparagraph{}

also, use \setcounter{secnumdepth}{5} and \setcounter{tocdepth}{3}
immediately after \begin{document} to change the depth of numbering and
toc respectively...
