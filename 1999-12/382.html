Rod,

This makes it sound so complex.
The rule is, I believe:

Bill in each month = maximum of

1.	$20
2.	19c per received MB.
3.	19c/2.75 per transmitted MB (i.e. served to the Internet by user).

Note that 19/2.75 is very close to 6.9.

Usually one of the above 3 items will be larger than the other two.
This will create the illusion that the lower items are "free".
They're what are called "slack constraints" in optimization theory.

I'll just re-write this more neatly:

Monthly bigpond bill = MAX (A, B, C)
A = $20
B = 19 cents * (Number of received MB)
C = 6.9 cents * (Number of sent MB).
