>
> I had a similar problem which I emailed the list about before. As it turns
> out, ATA/66 drives require drivers other than your normal IDE drivers to
> work. Of course, these are going to be dependent on the kind of ATA/66
> BIOS that the motherboard has.
>
> I had an Abit motherboard with the HighPoint Tech HPT366 BIOS - Linux drivers
> are available from the hipoint-tech.com web site, as well as links to Linux
> drivers for other ATA/66 BIOSes.
>
> Also, bear in mind that you should not be running a normal IDE drive from
> the ATA/66 controllers - these are for ATA/66 drives only.
>
> (You can run an ATA/66 drive from a normal IDE controller, but you may need
> to download a drive utility to turn off the ATA/66 function of the drive -
> my Western Digital ATA/66 drive came with the ATA/66 function enabled, even
> though the documentation says that it ships disabled by default.)
>
> For those that are interested: I contacted VMware about raw disk partition
> booting on ATA/66 drives (as this is not supported currently with SCSI drives)
> and they think that is should work, even though it's not tested. I opted in
> the end to run my drive in normal IDE mode, although I had to change
> motherboards in the end - has anyone else ever seen the HDD parameters
> change in the BIOS after rebooting as a result of partitions on the drive?
>
> > Windows boots and works from that drive
>
> Windows 95 and 98 seem to have ATA/66 support built in - Windows NT will
> not install without suppling the ATA/66 drivers during install.
>
> Cheers,
>
