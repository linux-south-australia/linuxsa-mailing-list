Re: ISP Recommendations

Now for my $500 worth...
One of my clients is using a v90 Big Stink Direct connection & it works
well:
You are assigned an account manager which deals with any hassles
You have on-line configuration / DNS delegation etc
It is a high-quality, *reliable* connection straight into the Telstra
backbone.

In short, you get what you pay for, I'm very happy with it.
The product is squarely targeted to the professional user, or the
business user who is employing a skilled IT person to set-it up, (gee,
skilled IT person, that's a bit of a oxymoron these days...).

I think the $500 setup fee is a great idea to get rid of time-wasting
home-users that tie-up quality help desk resources, increasing the
end-cost to users.

My client uses OS/2 which, like Linux, isn't supported ;)

I'm sure anyone who is interested in purchasing the above service could
find a keen, knowledgeable person on this ListServ to help them set it
up in return for hosting one of their low-bandwidth servers.

I'm seriously considering getting the above connection for *mobile* use
with my laptop, since Telstra have points of presence right across
Australia...

I'm sick-&-tired of crappy, unreliable, over-subscribed ISPs.
