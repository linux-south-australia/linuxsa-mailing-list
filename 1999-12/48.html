Re: re; chmod

ok this isn't a flame war, or even kindling.

but

if someone who hasn't yet fully come to grips on how the UNIX security
system works, needs a solution *right*now* then chmod 777 will work. 

My advice would then be however to immediately fix the problem, and lock
the security down as soon as possible.

Saying that chmod 7777 is "terrible" is saying "its an ugly fix" which
is true, but it "fixed" the problem. I say "fixed" becuase it might
(will) cause more problems later.

I think here we delve into the "quality" of UNIX admin - and for those
that have read Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance will understand
this.

Both fixes, the chmod-777-everything and the
read-man-page-and-do-it-properlly are both solutions, just one has more
quality.
