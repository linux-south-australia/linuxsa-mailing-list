David Newall wrote:

> Should be interesting.

yes, but potentially counter productive...

It's a distribution vs distribution debate. Interestingly, RedHat won't
participate. Sounds like a good option to me...

All this seems likely to do is encourage "differentiation" between the
distros, and that's potentially a Bad Thing. I know competition in
general is good, and so is competition between Linux Distros, but this
kind of debate is not competition, it's rhetoric. SuSe will criticise
Slackware for being hard to install, Slackware will criticise TurboLinux
for not being community minded enough, TurboLinux will point to Caldera,
Caldera will criticise VA-Linux for not being a distributor per se,
VA-Linux will criticise RedHat for it's unrealistically high stock price
with no IP, they'll all criticise FreeBSD for not being Linux, and
FreeBSD will criticise SuSe for being German, and the rest of the world
will add all these criticisms together and accuse Linux of all of them,
plus infighting...

I just think the excercise is a pointless waste of Linux Exec's time...
