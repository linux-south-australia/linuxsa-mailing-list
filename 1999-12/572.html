> AFAIK, this is just not true! At least, the *must* part is not.

> I *never* set adapters into Plug and Pray mode.  I have lots of old

Read the StepByStep before pronouncing it's not
necessary. The cmos (here's that word again) *must* be set up for isa on almost
all modern pc's. (ok so that's not strictly an isapnp issue) Some cards cannot
be removed from pnp. You can also jump  through hoops loading dos, running
ethernic Jumperless mode floppy disks, you can get lucky and let a dual boot
windoze system do all the hard work so that linux just happens to work ok.

And, until the advent of the 2.2 kernels isapnp.conf was something you added to
your system, now, it is the very first binary called out of
/etc/rc.d/rc.modules The fact that you don't use pnp mode is fine, the fact
that many newcomers to linux are hit with this problem is not fine. They have
the choice of using your method (which works) or taking advantage of the new
kernels ability to do so.
