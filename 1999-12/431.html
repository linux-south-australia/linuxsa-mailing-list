Re: Linux Internet nanny software?

Alan Kennington wrote:

> Are there any Internet censorship filters available for Linux?

The Internet censorship lists are going off half cocked.

An ISP has to provide filtering software from an approved
list.  Full stop.

The customers don't have to install it.

It doesn't have to run on any of their customers computers:
you could offer just a product for a PDP-11, if such a
product was on the list.

The take down provisions are much more a day-to-day concern,
especially in corporations with a complex corporate structure
(eg: the notice for content housed in SA could be sent to the
head office in Sydney and the take down period expire whilst
the notice is in transit to SA in the company's internal mail).

I'd ask people to actually read the legislation and Code
before posting their views on it.
