At 10:21  16/12/99 +1030, Paul Schulz wrote:
>Does SENet support Linux?  (If you say yes, what does this mean?

While there are always people around who _can_ support linux, they are 
usually the busiest people at the ISP, and thus the hardesst to get hold 
of. The newer versions of redhat/caldera etc all have nice dialers now 
anyway, and for most isps now, there is no scripts, just "use pap auth", 
"dynamically assigned ip" and dial "xxxx xxxx"

>If I ring up and say 'I use Linux' what responce an I likely
>to get?) Do you have a squid cache that I can
>point my squid cache to?

In general, pointing your local squid cache at an ISP's cache will just 
slow it down, cause then you'll query our 5 squids before you actually go 
and request things, better to just have your squid request stuff directly, 
and we'll query all of them across 100mb ethernet instead of a 56k modem.

you could use:

cache_peer proxy.iweb.net.au 3128 3130 no-query default

which will use the parent specifically.
