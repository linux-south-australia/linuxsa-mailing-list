Re: ISP Recommendations

On Sat, Dec 18, 1999 at 12:25:29AM +1018, Alan Kennington wrote:

 > > The ADSL roll-out will happen slowly,
 > > because each local telephone exchange needs to be fitted out with
 > > a DSLAM and ATM connectivity back to whatever ISP owns it, but in
 > > the end it'll at least *be there*, which is more than you'll ever be
 > > able to say for Cable.
 > 
 > ATM??

Yup.  ADSL architecture looks like this:


              Subscriber
ethernet  ----	ADSL  ---- DSLAM ----- Aggregator ------- ISP
                modem
	     |<-------------- ATM PVC ------------------>|

It essentially extends an ATM PVC from the ISP's backbone back to the
subscriber's ADSL modem, which provides ethernet connevitity to the 
subscriber's equipment.

 > Does that mean that all that work going into linux ATM drivers
 > is going to pay off?

Probably not :-)  The end-user just sees ethernet.  There may be 
a place for it back on the ISP's backbone, but hey! there's already
a place for Linux back on the ISP's backbone anyway :-)

 > And does that mean that we can write native ATM applications
 > in linux to go over the net?

Doubtful.

 > People had been saying for a while that ATM would stay in
 > the backbone.

Yup!

 > If ADSL is going to bring ATM to the user's home,
 > ubiquitously, then this is reason for me to take an interest in
 > linux ATM again.

Blurgh.  It's only going one-hop closer to the subscriber than it does
at the moment:  In some cases ATM is in the telco's network between the
local switch and the rest of their core, and a copper loop hooks up the
subscriber;  In ADSL's case, the "copper loop" bit will be an ethernet
cable, and the ATM side will extend to the end of their local subscriber
line.

 > Can you give any info on low-cost ATM cards that would be
 > suitable for ADSL?

None.  But there are standards-based ADSL modems available from just
about everywhere in the US;  It's quite mature technology now.
