"Tulip" is the 21140/21142/21143 chipset from DEC.  It is the single most
widely used 10/100 chipset, used in 10/100 cards from dozens of vendors;
and performs pretty much the same as all other good 10/100 chipsets.  The
Linux driver is quite mature and well-tested, though some tweaking may be
needed on odd brands and very new cards (especially from DEC, they are the
first to try out new variations on their chipset).  I personally like
Kingston and Netgear for Tulip vendors. 

The other 10/100 cards that work well with Linux are the Intel EtherExpress
Pro 100B/100Plus (the original had bugs, but that was a while ago) and the
TLAN chipset, which I haven't had a chance to test at 100Mbps.  The HP
10/100 chipset may be decent too but I know nothing about it.

The Epic100 chipset (SMC EtherPower II) is fine except for disfunctional
multicast filters.  There is a new RealTek 10/100 chipset, which should
make for cheap but slow 10/100 cards; and there is a Micronix chipset I
know nothing about used in the Addtron AEF 330 ($30 after rebate!).

There is also a PCNET/FAST chipset from AMD which is hard to find but
should perform well (I don't recall whether there is a Linux driver, but
I'm sure it would be easy once the cards are more available).

These are just the PCI 10/100 chipsets.  If you want cheap and don't need
100TX, there are many NE2000 PCI clones that work much better than any ISA
card at 10Mb for $20-$30.  There are a probably a few other oddball PCI
chipsets out there, but Tulip + 3Com + Intel + NE2000 clones is the vast
majority so far.
