It's not an innovation (altho - to Internet types - it may seem like it),
since radio clubs do it (e.g. North East Radio Club - NERC) all the time!

But it *is* a good idea...  Speaking of Radio Club's, by the way... I'd
like to suggest multichanneling... i.e. keeping a two-way radio on *beside*
the computer, e.g. while hacking and/or surfing... like those independent
car parts places... a "conference call" channel... best if Voice-Operated,
but even push-to-talk would work... a former TAFE (or was it UofSA instruc-
tor used to have a simplex channel for such purposes (on the Amateur "two-
meter band" - somewhere between 144 & 148 MHz nFM for those with scanners)

He would typically be in contact with any number of people, throughout
much of he day... as he made satellite contacts (fetching or forwarding
e-mail via packet radio)... or experimented with Slow-Scan TV (also over
radio)... etc.

Why not a similar on-air LINUX voice-conference channel...?  If not enough
are licensed amateurs, yet... ;)  UHF CB *might* work... in some areas...

Speaking of radio... has anybody else followed up the Tue Australian's call
for participation in Ericsson's Bluetooth technology...?  (Leave it to The
Australian to get it right, BTW, not... they described the technology as
"shortwave" (2.45 GHz... right... Very Shortwave...)

It would be good to get some pro-LINUX people in on the ground floor, I'd
say, just in case this thing flies sooner than expected...
