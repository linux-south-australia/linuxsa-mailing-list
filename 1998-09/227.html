> 
> > How do you use PINE to check if you have mail waiting? When I go to the
> > in-folder nothing happens (no activity on the modem) however, I can send
> > mail okay. Thanks in advance.
>
> the simple answer is uless you have imap4 mail boxes setup you don't :) if
> you are popping your mail from a server at you ISP you are going to want to
> use fetchmail(my pop3 client of choice) or qpopper(recent security concerns
> there) or another pop3 client to get the mail into your local inbox. I
> suggest a combination of fetchmail and pine, both work well and are easy to
> configure. Pine you see is not a mail transport agent(MTA) or mail delivery
> agent(MDA) or a pop3 client, it is simply a mail and news reader. You it to
> read mail that you get from an MTA, like sendmail, or pop3 client, like
> fetchmail, which may even have gone through an MDA, like procmail, you do
> not use it to dowload or deliver your mail for you :)

Irrespective of transfer method, go to the last message in the
INBOX index and hit the down cursor key a couple of times.
