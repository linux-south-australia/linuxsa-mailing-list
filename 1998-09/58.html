Re: Where's are the best How To(s) for Setting-Up RH LINUX 5.0 to...

On Mon, 7 Sep 1998, SBS expt wrote:
firstly just install all the HOWTO's there is a .deb and if I remember
back to the bad old days ;) an rpm .

>  1) Dial-up my ISP & Setup my browser & E-mail
>     client to make use of the connection..?
the PPP-HOWTO and the ISP-hookup-HOWTO will cover all you need, however
there are other procceses for debian and RH that you can use. best to
check out all the stuff about your dist.

>  2) Gateway Internet packets between my LINUX
>     box as a LAN gateway (so that two Win NT 4
>     boxes can share the same dialup-link, sup-
>     porting more than one User concurrently)..?
the Firewall-HOWTO and Networking-Overview-HOWTO are a good starting
point and the ipfwadm man page. Note if you are running a devel kernel
then you'll need to get the IP-CHAINS (an adelaide based project) info and
use that, you can get that at
	http://www.adelaide.net.au/~rustcorp/ipfwchains/ipfwchains.html

>  What would it take to have LINUX let me set up
>  Internet e-mail addresses..?
You would need an MX record and an MTA(Mail Transport Agent) like sendmail
or qmail or exim or smail. The MAIL-HOWTO is a good place to look for
info.

>  Does this take coordinating with (= paying $)
>  with my ISP..?
not necesarily, look at my email address, nikolai@ukraine.humbug.org.au
I get my domain name and MX entries via HUMBUG. We have our own DNS as I
think LinuxSA does and it is club poilcy that people with static dialup
IP's can get a DNS entry and MX records. my records are:

Address:  130.102.2.15

ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 100, mail exchanger =
mail.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 10, mail exchanger =
ukraine.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 20, mail exchanger =
kiev.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 50, mail exchanger =
uqmail.humbug.org.au

for my comercial dialup through University of Queensland. And:

nikolai@Ukraine ~ $ nslookup -q=mx kiev.humbug.org.au
Server:  krefti.cc.uq.edu.au
Address:  130.102.2.15

kiev.humbug.org.au      preference = 20, mail exchanger =
ukraine.humbug.org.au
kiev.humbug.org.au      preference = 10, mail exchanger =
kiev.humbug.org.au

for my student dialup (completly firewalled, uqmail.humbug.org.au is
			inside that firewall though).

If you do not have a static IP then there are other options (HUMBUG
doesn't offer them) including UUCP or multidrop mailboxes with a
virtual domain on a machine and pop.

hope this helps.
