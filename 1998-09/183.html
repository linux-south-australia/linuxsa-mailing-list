As for date, you are looking in the wrong place.  The version included
in zoneinfo is not used.  Check the sh-utils SRPM and RPM, where

> chroot.1, hostname.1 and ansi2knr.1 from sh-utils:
[snip proof that chroot.[1c], hostname.[1c], and ansi2knr.[1c] are in
the sh-utils source RPM]
> bash$ rpm -qpl /mnt/cdrom/RedHat/RPMS/sh-utils-1.16-4.i386.rpm | egrep 'chroot|hostname|ansi2knr'
> 
> No output!  My, my, my.  Case proved.

No, it's *not* proved.  You have ignored my previous responses which
state that the binaries and man pages are in different RPMs to the one
you are currently looking at, and you still continue to argue that the
chroot/hostname/ansi2knr man pages from sh-utils should for some
reason make it into the sh-utils RPM (even though the programs
themselves don't!).

Let's make it really clear what the situation is:

There are (at least) two copies of chroot.c and chroot.1 in the source
RPMs.  One is in sh-utils, and another is in util-linux.  You just
proved that the version of chroot in sh-utils is not used (so of
course the man page won't be in the RPM).  If you look in util-linux,
you'll see both the chroot program and its man page present.

There are (at least) three copies of hostname.c and hostname.1 in the
source RPMs.  One is in sh-utils, another is in util-linux, and yet
another is in net-tools.  You just proved that the version of hostname
in sh-utils is not used (so of course the man page won't be in the
RPM).  If you look in net-tools, you'll see both the hostname program
and its man pages present.

The ansi2knr.c and ansi2knr.1 that you find in the sh-utils source RPM
are just part of the build process for sh-utils, and are not part of
the sh-utils utilities themselves.  The versions of ansi2knr.c and
ansi2knr.1 that you are meant to use can be found in the automake RPM.

I'm puzzled why you have gone to all the trouble of comparing SRPMs
and RPMs to locate "missing" man pages, but you haven't tried typing
(eg.) 'man date', which easily demonstrates that Red Hat have in fact
not forgotten to include the date man page.

ansi2knr: the program and its man page are both in the automake RPM.
