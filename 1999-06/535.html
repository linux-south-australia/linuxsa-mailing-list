On Sat, Jun 26, 1999 at 01:53:44PM +0930, david wrote:

> I was woundering, what do you call the electronic devise that ISP's put on
> the phone lines to answer incomming calls from there customers.
> And where can i get one.
> I dont want a stallon board and lots of external modems, just one device
> that hanndles it all.

You're probably thinking of a terminal server - plugs into your network at one
end and ISDN PRIs (note: expensive ;) ) at the other, and handles lots of
analogue lines, as well as digital (56k/ISDN). Livingston, Ascend, Cisco all
sell them. Not sure who you'd talk to in Adelaide about them, doubtless
someone here who's currently using them can tell you... 

Also note that this is required if you want to handle above 33.6k, as 56K needs
a digital link to the exchange, but costs start seriously increasing once you
start shelling out for these things. 's why people still use Stallion cards et
al for some things.

Oh, BTW - if anyone wants a Stallion 64-port card and two modules (32 ports
each), drop me an email ... we have one for sale that we don't have a use for.
