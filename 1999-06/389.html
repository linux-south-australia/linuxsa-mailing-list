> > > if u added something like "ttyp0" to securetty in /etc

> > > in redhat 6, the tty ports seem to be called "pts/0" "pts/1" etc 

the /dev/ttyp?? and /dev/pts/?? are psuedo-tty devices that are allocated
to incoming sessions.  So even if you do allow root logins, it will only 
allow conenctions for the telnet session that gets allocated that tty,
if you are not expecting any other connections, then this may work.  But 
if you are, then you may have problems logging in.

BTW the /dev/pts/?? is a 'standard unix' thing, I think it is covered in
the Unix98 spec or something similar.  They are dynamically created under
the 2.2+ series kernels.

Although technically it doesn't really answer yor question, it gives you
a bit more of a background :).  Can't guarantee that /dev/pts/0 will 
work but there are alternatives such as ssh.
