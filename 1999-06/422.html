Re: How to tell when someone logs on

At 11:40 21/06/99 +0930, Benjamin Close wrote:
>Hi All,
>	I have a dialup machine. I provide a guest accound and a few user
>accounts for my friends. They can access these when I'm online.
>
>My problem is that they can logon and I am none the wiser. Hence you can
>imagine thier frustration when I log off unknowingly.
>
>Is there anyway I can get a beep/sound/visual effect to indicate that
>someone has logged on so I don't hangup inadvertantly?


This may or may not work in Linux -- haven't used it yet. But it works with
tcsh on Digital Unix ('cause I use it):

In .cshrc, add something like this:

	set watch = (20 any any)

and you'll be notified when someone logs on or off.

Unfortunately, I've forgotten the details -- the 20 is some kind of timer,
the first any is the login id, the second the tty -- with 'any' meaning ...
any.


Mine says set watch = (20 any console) -- 'cause I'm paranoid and the
console's in another room. This is probably unnecessary for most of you.  ;-)


If someone has a man page for tcsh, they could probably make this a lot
clearer. Sorry.  ;-(
