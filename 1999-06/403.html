Re: How to tell when someone logs on

At risk of pissing people off by saying that the answer is obvious,
but for crying out loud, the answer IS obvious.  When you log in
an entry is written to /var/log/wtmp.  This is clearly documented
in the last command, and since people have been bandying around
ideas such as w and who, I don't think I've introduced any great
leap of imagination.  To be notified when somebody logs in (or out)
you can monitor the last modification date of that file.  When it
changes you can use who (or w) to find out who is logged on.  The
same idea works for the utmp file, too.
