Re: FreeBSD vs Linux

Tim Ansell wrote:

 > Hi, i'm just wondering is there anyplace that compares FreeBSD against
 > Linux...

A "FreeBSD for Linux users" FAQ/tutorial is currently under development
(there has been various amounts of discussion about it on the 
freebsd-doc mailing list).  It'll be published on the web site in 
due course.

 > I have heard that FreeBSD was better as a firewall and was wondering if
 > i could have a look at some comparisons.

They are very similar, generally speaking.  In the past FreeBSD's
networking has been a very strong point (in terms of performance
and scalability), but that's probably no longer the case, especially
in the period since the Mindcraft fiasco, when Linux has done a lot
of catching up.  On 4-way multiprocessor systems, Linux may even
have more scalability at the moment, but the situation is in such
a state of day-to-day flux that it's impossible to make any definitive
judgement.

 > I know that KDE and gnome both work on Linux do they work on FreeBSD,

Yup;  they're included on the distribution CDs too.

 > also what about package management?

See http://slash.dotat.org/cgi-bin/man?name=pkg_add,
http://slash.dotat.org/cgi-bin/man?name=pkg_delete, and
http://slash.dotat.org/cgi-bin/man?name=pkg_info

There's also the ports collection, which is hooked fairly closely
into package management too (but I'll be talking more about that
at an upcoming LinuxSA meeting).
