> > Most modern DOS attacks require an exceedingly low volume
> > of carefully crafted packets.
> 
> And these typically exercise IP stack bugs.  Given 1-2 day 
> turn-around of Linux kernel releases addressing DoS problems
> I don't see how "carefully crafted packet" attacks are
> relevant to a Linux server.

	DoS covers more than just taking out a machine's 
	networking. Take down their web server continually,
	and they are likely to lose business. Ditto for
	sendmail.

	Just pointing out that the size of one's link has 
	nothing to do with your ability to be DoS'ed or not.
	
	(Also, it doesn't really matter whether you crash the
	 remote machine or not: if the link IS saturated (by pinging
	 a multicast network, for example), then it doesn't
	 matter whether the machine dies or not. People can't get 
	 _their_ traffic through to your machine, so the end result is
	 the same: Denial of a server's Service.)
