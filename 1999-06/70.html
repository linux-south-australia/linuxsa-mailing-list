Re: Exchange Server

Darryl Ross wrote:

> Which brings me back to my original question. Is it possible to do this
> under Linux?

It depends where the mail delivery occurs.

If it occurs to your local machine, then yes.  Basically
you set the sendmail local delivery agent to also
notify the user.

If delivery occurs to a remote POP or IMAP server, then the
client has to poll the server.  There are also programs
like biff and xbiff that will do this for you and
change an icon.

The reason for the client polls architecture is simple:
it allows more users on the server.  Here we have mail
servers with 21,000 or so accounts and about 7,000 PCs.
Holding open 7,000 TCP sessions for occassional traffic
is stupid.


Now I'd like to know why you want to do this?

It seems counter-productive.  The benefit of e-mail is
that you read the mail when it is convenient to you.
This differs from, say, the telephone or ICQ.

I'm continually amazed by the people that read e-mail
as it arrives.  I read mine twice a day.  The Inbox
in the morning amd my mailing list folders over my
afternoon break coffee.  That way I avoid distractions
during the morning when I want to get things done.
