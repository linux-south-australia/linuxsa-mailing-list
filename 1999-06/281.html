> Mike Earnshaw wrote:

>  > In Linux when you have your periodic filesystem checks, 20 boots or 6 months
>  > (from a book, no idea what the intervals are), how does this fit in if your
>  > system has been up for 12 months?, because it has not shutdown etc, can you
>  > assume that the filesystem integratity is sound?
> 
> Yup!

Our experience is no: not because of bugs, but because at
some point over a long time scale the physical factors
start to predominate (`bit-rot').

So for a long-uptime machine you simply break the mirror,
fsck the off-line volume, and reattach it.   RAID5 systems
come with similar tools.

Mirroring also provides a very neat backup system.  Attach
a disk to the mirror, wait for it to come online, then
take it offline and dd/tar it to tape.  You can buy
hard disks with multiple SCSI ports that are designed
for this job (as the backup then happens away from the
SCSI chain you are using for your running system).

All these are well-known techniques in the mainframe
community and it is fascinating to watch them move
into smaller systems.
