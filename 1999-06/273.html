Re: Periodic filesystem checks

Mike Earnshaw wrote:

> In Linux when you have your periodic filesystem checks, 20 boots or 6 months
> (from a book, no idea what the intervals are), how does this fit in if your
> system has been up for 12 months?, because it has not shutdown etc, can you
> assume that the filesystem integratity is sound? or should you do a
> maintenance reboot every 'x' months?

Note, however, that hard disks fail, and accidents (including 
finger trouble) happen, so you should back up irreplaceable data 
quite often.  A snapshot of your entire filesystem every month or so
(plus incremental backups at lest weekly and preferably daily) 
would be very handy, but you have to invest in a serious mass 
storage device (tape drive, or possibly CD-R) to do this.  As with 
any risk management, you have to balance the cost of guarding against 
problems with the cost x risk of losing things.  

Incidentally, when I perform an entire-disk snapshot, I shut down the 
system and reboot in single-user mode.  This helps provide integrity 
as files aren't being changed while the backup is running.  So the 
filesystem does get a check occasionally.  

Any comments from the real experts on the do's and don'ts of 
backups?
