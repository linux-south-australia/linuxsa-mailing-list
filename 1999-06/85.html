This is not as easy as you might think. There are a lot of basis things to do
- N.B. M$ does these as well but hides them - I suggest the easiest way to do
what you suggest is to get Slackware and install ONLY the "A" series. this
will give you little more than an installed kernel. It will take care of all
the nasties like a bootable floppy, directing you to partition the disk,
loading basic utilities and libraries, making necessary device nodes and
directories, and installing a few "essential" utilities. Studying it is also a
good place to start if you want to learn what to do.
