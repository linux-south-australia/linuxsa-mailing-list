David,

The 2 and the 7 represent the number of hard links to the file/folder in
question.  In directories, there are two hard links, one to itself (.) and
one to the parent directory (..) which is why directories appear to always
have more than 1 link.  If you can visualise this concept, the My
Documents folder may have 5 subfolders as one of the links is the parent
folder of My Doco linking to My Doco, another is the (.) link inside My
Docuements and the other 5 are probably the .. links in the subfolders
underneath it.

Quick test:
sanctuary> ls -al
total 6
drwxr-xr-x   2 sayersjm  staff   512 Oct 24 14:55 .
drwx--x--x  82 sayersjm  staff  5120 Oct 24 14:56 ..
sanctuary> touch test
sanctuary> ls -al
total 6
drwxr-xr-x   2 sayersjm  staff   512 Oct 24 14:56 .
drwx--x--x  82 sayersjm  staff  5120 Oct 24 14:56 ..
-rw-r--r--   1 sayersjm  staff     0 Oct 24 14:56 test
sanctuary> ln test fred
sanctuary> ls -al
total 6
drwxr-xr-x   2 sayersjm  staff   512 Oct 24 14:56 .
drwx--x--x  82 sayersjm  staff  5120 Oct 24 14:56 ..
-rw-r--r--   2 sayersjm  staff     0 Oct 24 14:56 fred
-rw-r--r--   2 sayersjm  staff     0 Oct 24 14:56 test
sanctuary>

See the two files now have a 2...

Hope it explains it :)
