Re: Hot swap IDE

On Sun, 2001-09-02 at 17:54, michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:

> I'm no expert, but I've seen an IT guy experiment with this and he didn't get 
> far.  From what I remember he was using those removeable hard drive 
> chassis/cartridges.  He got some data corruption during testing, and so decided 
> that going SCSI was the way to go.  And this was rolling out to quite a few 10's 
> of people so the cost advantage of IDE wasn't ignored.

Whilst I've not dealt with hot-swapping IDE drives either, surely if you
had one of those removable hard disk caddys, pulling it out would just
be like a drive failure to the RAID array?

Once the disk is replaced, I'd have thought that the array would start
working to resync it. That, or the Linux IDE drivers would just get
upset and spit at you. I don't know for sure, I'd expect them not to
behave in that way, but I'd be disappointed in them if they did.
