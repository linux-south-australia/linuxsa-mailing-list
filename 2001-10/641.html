Re: Mobile phone plans for remote monitoring...

On Thu, Oct 18, 2001 at 10:59:03PM +0930, Richard Russell wrote:

> So, the core of the question: What's the cheapest way to get access to
> a mobile network for SMS only? This would require a cheap phone
> (perhaps second hand, but preferably new, to get warantees etc), or
> some other (?) device, as well as a plan of some description (or a "no
> plan"...) ...

I'd consider one of two things. Firstly, there's always Internode's SMS
offering, which requires some connection (direct or indirect) to their
network.

Secondly, buy a Nokia 5110 and the serial cable (I've got one spare if
you want it) and use gnokii (http://www.gnokii.org/) to send the SMS
message. gnokii could be triggered from NetSaint if required.

The latter provides a more likely chance of the message being sent. The
phone has its own battery, so assuming it has a full battery, it can
last several days without problems.

You might be able to find one of those cheap $10/month plans for the
phone, or even a Virgin prepaid plan for network access.
