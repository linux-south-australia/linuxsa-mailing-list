Re: Red Hat 7.2 file system

On Wed, 24 Oct 2001 michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:

> 7.2 emphasised stability in their press release, so I don't think there's much 
> chance of ext3 having any problems as shipped by RH.

Yes, press releases do tend to be a reliable guide as to how buggy the
software they describe is.

> On your other email, I think ReiserFS does look exciting.  It will be 
> interesting to see where all this ext3/ReiserFS/XFS stuff goes, i.e. is there 
> room for multiple players in the journalling filesystem market?

ext3 and XFS (and don't forget JFS from IBM) are traditional journaling
filesystems. ReiserFS is something brand new based on research that you
can read about at www.namesys.com. Journaling is just one thing that
ReiserFS can do. So if your question is about picking from functional
equivalents I think that you have to put ReiserFS in its own category.
