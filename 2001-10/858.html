RE: DSL Card for Linux

Depends on the ADSL provider. We offer one fixed IP or five in our 
offering (and the ethernet port is just an ethernet port - this means 
you can put five active devices on your local ethernet each with a 
different IP address, and they are all bridged to our ADSL system 
which runs another IP in the same range as your default route).

In PPPoE land, typically, there is just one IP routed to the PPPoE 
client (running in your linux box), and you either route more address 
ranges or NAT to get more addresses up if you need them (again 
somewhat DSL provider dependent)

Simon
