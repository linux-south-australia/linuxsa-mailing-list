Like yourself the prospect of "home computing" on a real operating system
has motivated us to give linux a go. There is also an element of nostaga -
Unix line commands and vi takes me back to uni 15 years ago !!

So far it has lived up to expectations. In particular Sun does not offer an
SDK for Java 1.2 on the iMac (OS8.6) and yes Sjaak, it works a treat on
linux.
For us, to make a real "home computer" however there are other elements:
(score out of ten in brackets)

1.	An office suite that really works (Star Office 9/10)
2.	Works with our existing Epson 740 (10/10)
3.	Some games (tuxracer and Civilisation 7/10)
4.	A compiler and downloader for the lego mindstorms brick. (NQC 10/10)
5.	A variety of development systems, Java, C, prolog, lisp .... (20/10!!)
6.	Music notation, Midi sequencer and a synthesiser program (jury is
out pending technical difficulties)

It has not been without it's cost however. It has taken us 3 months to get
to this stage, and a huge amount of learning.
