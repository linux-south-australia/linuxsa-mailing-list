Ah, you haven't read CFBSD.  That says "test with ping *first*".

> This is what I find on my network:

> PING Unit_2.gihon.org.au (192.168.1.2) from 192.168.1.1 : 56(84) bytes
> of data.
> <snip>
> 5 packets transmitted, 5 packets received, 0% packet loss
> round-trip min/avg/max/mdev = 0.229/0.285/0.491/0.103 ms
> [ba@Unit_1 ba]$ su -
> Password:
> [root@Unit_1 /root]# mount Unit2:/home/ba /mnt/remote
> mount: RPC: Unable to receive; errno = Connection refused

Well, that's an NFS problem.

> I get a similar result if I try finger, telnet or ftp.
> <sigh>

And they're finger, telnet or ftp problems.  In each case, you're
assuming that there's a daemon on Unit_2 which is interested in the
protocols.

> My hosts.allow file on Unit2 says:
> telnetd, ftpd: LOCAL, Unit_1.gihon.org.au
> fingerd: ALL
> ALL: LOCAL @gihon
> ALL: .gihon.org.au
>
> OK, so there is something I have overlooked - what is it, PLEASE!!

Are the services enabled in /etc/inetd.conf?  If not, enable them and
don't forget to SIGHUP the inetd.
