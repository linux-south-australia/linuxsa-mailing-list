> Hmmm, I do not think this is true. The 386 and greater, apart from 
> introducing some extra commands, differed in having 4G per segment, 
> rather than the 16M or whatever it was for the 286.

The other differense between the 286 and 386 was the introduction of 
protected mode, and full multitasking instead of task swapping. There were 
two models the 386 SX was internally 32bit and externally 16 bit to allow 
it to be placed in existing 286 machines and the 386DX was fully 32 bit, 
which is why it is the minimum spec for alot of stuff now. The 386 was the 
last major advance in chip design in the x86 series, everything else has 
been speed related, or switching to emulation in RISC, or adding extra 
instruction sets.
