Benchmarking maildir vs mbox

Someone asked me if I know of any objective measures of performance. The
best one I know of is "benchmark it yourself in your
environment". Fortunately someone has already done a worked example, which
can easily be replicated: http://www.courier-mta.org/mbox-vs-maildir/.

The results for his set of environments talk about performance.

Questions of safety are much clearer-cut. Have a look at the design of
Maildir (or Maildir++ if you like, it is the same thing in principle) at
http://cr.yp.to/proto/maildir.html. There is no question that Maildir is a
much more careful way of handling mail. There are other formats which are
just as safe (Maildir isn't a particularly clever piece of engineering!),
however they aren't supported by a wide range of software.

For a small and/or lightly loaded site, mbox should work without any
problem (but it is less safe.) For a large or busy site, Maildir should
give performance advantages on average, plus security advantages.

For anyone storing mail folders on NFS volumes, there is no choice; mbox
cannot be used.
