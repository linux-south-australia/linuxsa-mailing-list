>  > RH 7.1 needs :
>  > 286 processor
>  > 32Mb ram
>  > 650Mb HDD Space
>  > CDrom and/or Floppy
>  > 15inch moniter
>  >
>  > RH 7.1 can't run on that ..... can it?
>
> No.  It'll need a 386.  It doesn't need as much hard disk space as that.
> It also doesn't really need any monitor at all (depending on what you want
> to do with it).  I reckon you could get by with a lot less RAM than that
> too:  If you're looking at a low spec processor you obviously aren't
> the least bit interested in performance, and lowering the amount of memory
> will simply make the thing slower, so if you have cheap and nasty hardware
> you don't really need to beef it up to 32 Mbytes.
>
> As an application example for such a low-spec machine: 386 or 486 with no
> monitor, 8 - 10 Mbytes of RAM, and potentially no hard disk at all would
> make a relatively decent router for a dialup permanent Internet link (peak
> throughput = ~5kbytes/sec:  You could pass that much data on a Vic-20, let
> alone a 386 :-)
>
> Note that if you're building a kernel for a 386 or 486 you might need to
> disable various Pentium-specific options.  Sooner or later a decision will
> no doubt be made to stop supporting those architectures altogether (I mean,
> how much effort should be invested in maintaining compatibility with a
> chip that isn't even manufactured anymore?  Eventually ISA will be in
> the same boat too, and good riddance to it).
