Re: Hot swap IDE

Quoting Michael Neuling <Michael.Neuling@keyworks.com.au>:
> Does anyone know about the requirements/capabilities of hot swappable
> IDE drives under Linux (or even FreeBSD)?
> 
> I've heard that the IDE standard does support it but not all hardware
> IDE controllers do.  
> 
> I've done some searching on the web but have been unable to find a
> conclusive answer.  I believe it can be done with SCSI but there is an
> obvious cost advantage with IDE.

I'm no expert, but I've seen an IT guy experiment with this and he didn't get 
far.  From what I remember he was using those removeable hard drive 
chassis/cartridges.  He got some data corruption during testing, and so decided 
that going SCSI was the way to go.  And this was rolling out to quite a few 10's 
of people so the cost advantage of IDE wasn't ignored.

Oh yeah, this was under WindowsNT3.51 or 4.  Not sure if the OS was an issue 
though - may have been hardware connector stuff where SCSI had the advantage.

Just my vague recollections, YMMV.
