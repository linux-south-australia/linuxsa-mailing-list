> On Wednesday, 17 October 2001 at 15:17:32 +0930, David Lloyd wrote:
> > 1) Make sure that there's at least 128Mb or swap
> 
> Why?

Two reasons.

Everyone seems to agree that it's the right amount [side note: I think
the FreeBSD VM is optimised for at least 2 times physical memory]. And
if you've got 64Mb of physical memory, these days it's not much.

> > Try rebuilding the kernel and throwing as much as you can out of it.
> 
> I'd be interested to see if that makes a significant difference.  You
> might save a few 100 kB like that; how much difference will that make?

You might actually lose memory, however if you fiddle with the
optimisation options (eg, send them to -O3) and the architecture options
(-march and -mcpu) and the alignment options (-malign-loops et al) and
other optimisation options it is possible to get your kernel to go
significantly faster.

In my opinion, unless you're dealing with embedded Linux, it's best to
optimise the kernel for speed rather than size.
