Hi Ant,
Good question. The cable runs between floors and through a roof cavity,
but doesn't go particularly close to anything remarkable. It's about a
15M cable run - crossovered from one device to the other. The alternate
cable I tried went a different route past some air conditioners.

The time it takes for the problem to surface is random and it's
difficult to reproduce - the only other thing I know that I didn't
mention is that on our router the collisions on the ethernet interface
are about 2.5% of the packets transmitted, whereas on the other router
it's 0.9%. The link is half duplex, 10Mbit. I thought about changing it
to full duplex, but the module parameter to change this requests a value
between 1 and 8 :) Whether or not this is related I'm not sure. I ran a
duplicate setup in a different location with all the same equipment and
it functioned flawlessly for a few days, with much fewer collisions.
With the other cable collisions seemed to happen at the same rate, so I
switched back to the original one as it presented less of a trip-over
risk to everyone.
