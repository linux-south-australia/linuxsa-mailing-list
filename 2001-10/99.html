Agreed.
I normally just look for the cheapest external (serial) with a Rockwell 
chipset.
External name-brand modems can _sometimes_ get niggly without their drivers
(they still work but can misbehave).

I know this is rare but I've seen it happen on Xircom and US Robotics and never
had a single such problem with simple AT-string driven Rockwell-chip cheapies
such as KTX, WebExcell, etc (crack most of 'em open and they look near 
identical).

The most important reason to go external is because you do occassionally 
need to
quickly power-cycle the modem if it (or Win DUN) is playing silly-buggers. 
If you have
sit through a full shutdown->startup routine just to reset a bloody modem, 
things can
get a little frustrating.
