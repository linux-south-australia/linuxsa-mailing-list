Re: bsd v gpl licensing

On Tue, Oct 30, 2001 at 12:00:50PM +1030, Anthony Symons wrote:

 > I really dont like seeing microsoft ripping BSD code, and adding a
 > little pissy footnote somewhere that most people wont see with bsd
 > credits.
 > Then, they charge a fortune for it, the non-bsd bits are still
 > dodgy and people make money out of it who, in my mind, dont deserve it.

But the developers who wrote that BSD code don't mind.  Isn't that 
what's important?  They're the ones who chose the license, after all.

Important question:  Do you value the GPL because it delivers software
freedom, or do you value it because it means free software devlopers
can write code without being "ripped off"?

The reason I ask is because the latter reason suggests that you're in
favour of free software in the "free beer" sense and don't care much 
about the "free speech" sense, and the former reason suggests the opposite.
It strikes me that a lot of GPL advocates aren't quiet sure about which
of those alternatives they really support.
