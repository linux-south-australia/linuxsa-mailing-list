Re: Red Hat 7.1 (modem choice)

On Tue, Oct 02, 2001 at 07:17:21PM +1000, Daniel Callan wrote:
> The most important reason to go external is because you do occassionally 
> need to
> quickly power-cycle the modem if it (or Win DUN) is playing silly-buggers. 
> If you have
> sit through a full shutdown->startup routine just to reset a bloody modem, 
> things can
> get a little frustrating.

(but surely no one connects a modem to a M$ PC, especially not
someone reading a linux list!  hopefully you're not connecting a M$ 
PC directly to the internet!)

I've heard people say that before (about external is better for power
cycling the modem) but I've never had that problem, maybe I've
always just happened to pick good quality modems, or that I've never
used M$ stuff with a modem, dunno.

I'd say the best reasons for external are:
1) you avoid the whole "win modem" thing
2) you keep slots inside your PC free for other things (these days
   where PCs have fewer and fewer slots)
3) hopefully a lightening strike on the phone lines will only take
   out your modem rather than the whole PC
4) you can power cycle the modem separate from the PC (if you so desire)
5) you can easily move it to other machines
