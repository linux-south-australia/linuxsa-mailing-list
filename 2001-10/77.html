On Tue, Oct 02, 2001 at 11:49:42AM +0930, Simon Healy wrote:
> recommend an internal or external modem that will definitely work with Red
> Hat and the updated kernel , is readily available in Adelaide, and won't
> cost a fortune? Even a second-hand one would do.

any external modem will work (talking normal serial modems here,
I've never tried a USB modem).
IMO don't bother with internal (most PCI ones will be "win modems"
ISA ones should be ok if you're keen on that).

There's a D-Link external V.90 one that seems to work quite well.  
I just had a look on www.ht.com.au and they've got them for $109 
(D-Link DFM-560E) - so you should be able to find a local place cheaper 
than that (HT are in Sydney and don't have a reputation for being cheapest!).
I normally recommend www.everythinglinux.com.au but they don't appear to
stock them.  I know 2 people with them who've had no problems.
http://www.dlink.com.au/products/modems/dfm560e/
I've got a Banksia Wave that works well but is a bit pricey at around
$200 (I wanted one fairly quickly at the time!).
