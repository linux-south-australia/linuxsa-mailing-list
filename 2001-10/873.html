CD-Rom mounting

Hi All,

I recently installed Red Hat 7.2, and I have a problem with mounting the 
CDROM - it won't mount!

I recieve the error 'mount: /dev/cdrom is not a valid block device' when 
trying to mount it. /dev/cdrom is a symlink to hdb:
brw-------    1 tr       disk       3,  64 Aug 31 06:00 /dev/hdb
Hdb is detected by the kernal as an ATAPI CDROM drive (according to 
dmesg).  Its line in /etc/fstab is as below:
/dev/cdrom    /mnt/cdrom              iso9660 noauto,user,kudzu,ro 0 0

The cdrom was working perfectly well under Slackware, and it was used to 
install Red Hat also, so I do not think it is a hardware problem.  Would 
anyone have any suggestions as to how this problem can be fixed?  Finally, 
I am not very familiar with RH, and would like to know whether my user 
(tr) should be the owner of many of the entries in /dev (they seem to be 
associated with the fd, cdrom, framebuffer, sound, and video devices).

Thanks for your assistance,
