He's specifically talking about RedHat 7.1, so whilst it's still possible
to run Linux on a machine with tiny amounts of RAM, You couldn't run the
RedHat installer (even in text mode). Most modern installers use a ramdisk
of at least 4MB as their root filesystem (in fact, I think Mandrake's
might be as big as 16MB). For those of you out there looking at using
machines with low resources, I'd look at slackware....
