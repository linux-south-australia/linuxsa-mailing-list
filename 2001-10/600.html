 > 
 > but don't you really want the so called "high speed" serial ports
 > for such an application?  (16550 or whatever?)  
 > didn't they not come until the first pentiums?

No - I have an 8-port AST ISA shared-interrupt serial card with 16550
UARTs on it which I used when I ran cleese.apana.org.au back in the 
386 days.

16550s have been around for aeons.  

The thing that changed when the Pentiums came out was that the better
chips were placed on the motherboard by default:  Previously motherboard
and IO card manufacturers had supplied the $6 per chip parts instead of
the $30 per chip parts, so most cheap-n-nasty motherboards and IO cards
had the lower-spec serial ports on them.  But it was still possible to
specify 16550s if you really wanted 'em;  it was also possible to 
desolder the 16450's and insert 16550's in their place (because the
chips had identical pinouts).
