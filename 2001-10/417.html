Re: Editors, Component Software and 3D engines

Hi There,

First, what do you mean by "syntax highlighting editor ". If
you mean one that points out you have forgotten brackets etc
(without wishing to start a "this editor is better than that
war") try emacs. Emacs has been around for a LONG time and
is not just an editor, but also an environment (in some ways
like a shell, but not exactly). I do know that there are
heaps of extensions for individual programming projects
including c, c++, java, lisp etc and it would be just a
matter of digging them out of the net. Emacs is also fully
customisable, so if you are not happy with it, them modify
it yourself (you will learn lisp in a hurry if you do).

Secondly, I haven't done much GUI programming, but Gtk seems
popular or TkTcl. If you are running Gnome or KDE, why not
just use whatever the default is on your system.

I am not interested in a project at the moment, but
certainly read some of the HOWTOs on the net, and there are
some very good Linux Programming books out there at the
moment for relatively not much money. Why not pick one up
and away you go. Hope this helps.
    Cheers!
        Andrew.
