Linux vs. Microsoft requirements (was: [OT] Should I be worried...)

On Thursday, 18 October 2001 at 10:53:51 +0930, Mark Goddard wrote:
> A guy in my TAFE class is doing an assignment... NP there! According to his
> 'information'...
>
> windozes 98 needs :
> 486DX, 66 MHX
> 16Mb RAM
> 300Mb HDD Space
> 2x CD ROM
> 14 inch moniter
>
> RH 7.1 needs :
> 286 processor
> 32Mb ram
> 650Mb HDD Space
> CDrom and/or Floppy
> 15inch moniter
>
> RH 7.1 can't run on that ..... can it?

If you look at everything except the processor, your TAFE bloke is (a)
trying to make Linux look more resource hungry than Microsoft (b)
doesn't know what he's talking about.  I'm pretty sure that the 286
doesn't address more than 16 MB of memory, so as others say, he's
probably confusing an 80286 processor (maximum 16 MHz, IIRC) with a
266 MHz Pentium-class processor.  I'd be interested in why he thinks
the monitor should be larger.

Apart from that, of course, he's just plain wrong.  I believe it's
still possible to get Linux to run in 8 MB of memory, for some
definition of run.  I don't think that you'd get Microsoft to perform
anywhere near as well on the same minimal hardware.
