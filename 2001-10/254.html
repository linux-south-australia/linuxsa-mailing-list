I start to get worried when a distribution becomes "unstable". It's a
bit of an ambiguous term really. It could mean that the kernel they've
used is crap, or perhaps it's the software they've included.

It's astonishingly bad (IMO) when a distribution is producing
instability. Perhaps it's just your experience.

Then again, it'd not surprise me. Linux distributions these days (bar a
few) are driven so much by the commercial marketing world that they're
forgetting about producing decent software. RedHat's an example.
