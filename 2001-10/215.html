tcpdump and ethereal are pretty good tools for seeing what's happening on
your network. Perhaps they will give you some insight into the problem.

> But is there a way to easily scan all SMB clients in a LAN? I've read
> its not scalable, a few other reasons againest its use, etc.. but really
> for the kind of small LANs that most people run - its an invaluable
> tool.

It depends on what you mean by "scan all SMB clients in a LAN". Windows'
Network Browsing stuff does not guarantee that you will get a list of all
SMB-abled machines on the network. Are you running a WINS server? If not,
it might be something to consider. Both Samba and Windows NT WINS servers
provide a way of getting a list of all registered NetBIOS names (which
doesn't just include PC computer names). Once each machine is configured
to use a WINS server, it will usually register all of it's NetBIOS name
info with the WINS server each time it boots.
