Re: bsd v gpl licensing

On Tue, Oct 30, 2001 at 11:37:52AM +1030, jonathan soong wrote:

 > I was wondering if anyone felt strongly
 > regarding gpl vs bsd licensing?

Don't have any strong feelings one way or the other.  I feel slight
disquiet whenever someone describes GPL software as "free" software 
(as in speech, not beer), but other than that I can't see any reason
to care.

I think people who know, understand and use the BSD license think about
the issues in a fundamentally different way to the people who know,
understand and use the GPL, which is why it's so difficult for each
camp to see the other camp's point of view.  I also think that a lot of
people who advocate the GPL are confused about where they stand on the 
issues -- I often hear them saying the BSD license is bad because it
allows commercial software developers to profit from the work of free 
software developers, which kinda tells me that they're more interested
in the free beer aspect than the free speech aspect.  Which is ironic,
because the idealogy behind GPL is definitely free speech, even though
it doesn't achieve it in practice.
