Re: Hot swap IDE

I have seen an IDE hot swap device here on a linux system, but I dont
know what it was called. It worked by having a special case with 2 IDE
drives in it, and then it mirrored them. It had its own IDE chip. If one
drive died, the system was unawear and due to the mirror the data stayed
accessable. Then you could remove the faulty drive and add anther
transperent to the system. I think you had to reboot before It rebuilt
though. Unfortunatly I only had a quick look as I went past and dont
have any more details. The kernel didnt need to be awear of IDE devices
coming or going (as long as one of 2 was ok).
