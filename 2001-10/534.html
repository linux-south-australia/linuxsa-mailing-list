Re: Tweaking Linux for performance

Ian!

>  The computers are generally running Intel 200 MMX CPU
>  with 64 M RAM and a 4 G drive.
> 
>  At present with a default workstation install the
>  machines run "slower than a wet week". It seems to take
>  ages to bring up StarOffice 5.2 and then do any real
>  work.

1) Make sure that there's at least 128Mb or swap
2) Make sure that DMA is turned on for the hard-drives if you are able
to get it to work

(I was working with an 866MHZ PIII, IBM 70G drive without DMA [1] and it
was fantastically slow]

>  Even with StarOffice 6 beta and OpenOffice 638C the
>  machine seems to be slow.

If you are able to, dump KDE and/or GNOME.

The desktops hog way too much memory; use something like fvwm. I know
this runs counter to "easy linux", however even with Linux's efficiency
being "user friendly" takes a huge amount of resources.

>  Has anybody got some ideas on tweaking the system to
>  come up with significant speed improvements without
>  resorting to RAM upgrades and other paths.

Try rebuilding the kernel and throwing as much as you can out of it.
Also, if you're able, take it to Kernel 2.4.10-linus kernel; the VM
(virtual memory management) in <= 2.4.5 is particularly bad and although
it's unlikely to be the sole cause of your problem, given you'd be
running X, Gnome/KDE and Office on 64Mb I wouldn't be surprised if the
VM is being used...
