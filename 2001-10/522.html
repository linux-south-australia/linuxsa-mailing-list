Re: 802.11b PCI cards?

ian_hogben@computercorp.com.au wrote:
> 
> Two parts = Two things that can go wrong. Besides, PCMCIA cards are usually
> much more expensive than PCI cards due to the miniturisation that they
> feature. Just looking at network cards, you can pick up a 10/100 PCI for
> under $50, but to get a PCMCIA card without any external dongles, you're
> looking at over $300.

Hi Ian,

RF devices need a lot more design and certification work than
non-RF devices.  Modular systems are common in RF electronics
as a result.  A product family that had two tranmitter housings
and antenna designs would cost the manufacturer twice the
design work.

At 2.4GHz miniturisation is an advantage -- it prevents PCB
traces from becoming displaying too many characteristics of
antennas.

I think you'll find that the Magiclan card is also simply
a PCMCIA card mounted on a PCI card with some glue electronics.
