Well, so far that hasn't magially resurected the mail server, so I've
decided to give a more proactive outlook a go.

I found this (apparently I missed it when I sent the original message)
from 'dmesg':

Bad md_map in ll_rw_block
EXT2-fs: unable to read superblock

<weep, weep, weep, weep> That doesn't sound good. To quote the Linux
Software-RAID HOWTO:

"In short: quite often you get a temporary failure of several disks at
once; afterwards the RAID superblocks are out of sync and you can no
longer init your RAID array. 

One thing left: rewrite the RAID superblocks by mkraid --force"

Ahhh! 'man mkraid' reveals something even more disturbing:

"Note that initializing RAID devices destroys all of the data on the
consituent devices."

<weep, weep, weep> That's not good, Jimmy! It's just not good!

If anyone feels inclined to offer some advice, I'd be happy to hear from
you.
