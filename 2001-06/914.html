Re: RAID Array Problems

Um, what's the problem?

One of the two mirror disks is injured.  The other should 
be fine.  So mark the disk with the oldest superblock
as a "failed-disk" in /etc/raidtab, restart RAID, mount
the RAID device as read-only.  Then run e2fsck to ensure
that you selected the correct disk of the pair.

BTW, your power fail strategy is wrong.  As I've pointed
out many times on this list, a UPS is to give your machines
time to power down properly and then to ride out the
on-again off-again nature of power restoration.  That
means that a UPS without a wire to your serial port to
enable an automated controlled shutdown is useless.
