RE: Hard Disk Duplicating

> At home I have a small dedicated linux server with two 20gb hard
> drives. The
> first hard drive is divided up into about 4 maybe 5 partitions. The second
> is not doing anything, it's just connected but always left unmounted.
>
> Rather than not use the drive I would like to use it to completely
> duplicate/backup the first drive. I assume that this is not possible while
> the first drive is mounted and files are in use. Other than using software
> raid, what can be done ?, and what should I do ?

it's ok, as long as you can be sure that there is no activity on the disk
(at least, this is the case in the world of Sun)...

I would suggest going to single user, doing a dd, then returning to your
usual runlevel.

Better, I'd suggest using software RAID, with mirroring. You will (I think)
require a reboot to remount the / partition as a mirror... I'm not familiar
with doing this on linux...

Really, it depends what it is you want to acheive. If you want availability
(so a disk failing won't cause the computer to crash) then software RAID
would be your thang. If you want to ensure that if your disk fails, you have
a backup from a week ago, then dd is your thnag...
