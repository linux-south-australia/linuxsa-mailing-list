>I'd agree wholeheartedly with the nonsense comment - because the inital
remark
>is nonsense.
>
>If I was a CIO and you came to me with a proposal stating those kind of
costs -
>I would definitely go the MS admin. For example, the on-costs (super,
workcover,
>payroll tax, etc, etc) associated with each would be around 15-20% of the
base
>salary, so now we are talking a total "employment cost" for the *nix admin is
>pushing $135k per annum whilst the MS admin is 'round the $75k per annum
mark. I
>could see the CIO going "Hmm, now lets see, on one hand I need to spend
$20k up
>front and the other nothing, but the nothing option is gonna cost me an extra
>$40k per annum and I am gonna get a bunch of skills that will be hard to
replace
>(not impossible but its gonna cost).....". You see its the ongoing costs that
>are the killer - not whether the box you are playing with is costing a
bundle or
>not.
>
>TCO is a hell of lot more than just looking at what it costs to get a fully
>kitted box onto someone's desk (ever tried calculating what it costs your
>company in your time and resources - like net time - in setting up a *nix
box?).
>I think if you checked your budgets year-to-date, the cost of the O/S and the
>requisite hardware/apps is nothing compared to the ongoing costs of
maintaining
>the bits and bobs (labour, utilities, space, training, et al).
>
>Personally, I think as sysadmins we are beholden to our employer/s to keep an
>eye on our costs, and not just what we happen to spend out of the pot for
>hardware & software. TCO issues affect Sagers as much as they do the
>professional bean-counters, and its something we should all heed - especially
>with tough times just over the horizon.
>
>Happily throwing my $0.02 into the ring.
>
>Stu.
