Re: Emergency Recovery ... How TO?

On Wed, 6 Jun 2001, Richard Sharpe wrote:
> I did something extremely stupid a little while ago. I ignored some error
> messages when I made some software. I copied the .so that resulted into
> /lib and created a file called /etc/ld.so.preload which had one entry: /lib.
...
> Question is, what other ways are there.

Are you looking for a way that does not involve booting from another
OS/Linux, such as using a mini distribution (tomsrtbt, ramf etc...), the
supplied CD, a recovery CD such as the Linuxcare one, or a 'recovery'
partition with a similar or mini-distribution on it? There is the option
of a nightly clone (dump or rsync) of root to a similar sized partition,
where the clone program would modify the fstab, lilo and other boot
details. This would give you something to boot from, and a place you could
copy important files from, such as the times libc.so is overwritten and
can't boot. For added redundancy, you would have this on another drive.

I am assuming here that root includes /boot.
