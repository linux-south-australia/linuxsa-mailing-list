I think what Apple is doing is great - afterall, as Jordan's announcement says, 
the power of unix with "real" applications on top.  But the problem (as far as 
I see it) is that these wonderful things on top of the FreeBSD core aren't 
available to me unless I purchase Apple hardware.  I'm not even talking about 
cost, I'm talking about running all this stuff on the (Intel) laptop I own 
now - there is no Intel port of the Mac OS X graphical shell.

BSD licences are good, but (IMHO) GPL is even better, because I know I can get 
the source to the "latest and greatest" enhancements (ie GPL discourages 
forking or proprietry versions) and port it myself if I have to.  But of course 
that's only my opinion, and others are equally valid.

But for my next laptop, an Apple might just make a sale if I can overcome the 
philosophical problems :-)
