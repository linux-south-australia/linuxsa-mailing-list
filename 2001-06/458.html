That's fine for you - but Red Hat is a general purpose distribution, probably 
most suited to people who just want to throw in a CD and see everything 
working.  If you want the latest experimental XYZ that requires a beta version 
of the ABC library, sure, go on and use something which requires a little more 
effort.  Red Hat is huge, because for most people it provides most things, with 
the least amount of thinking required.

I use Red Hat becuase I don't care for fiddling with ISP dialers, firewall 
settings, or spending hours configuring the latest windowmaker - I just want 
something that works.  I'd rather spend my time _developing_ rather than 
_configuring_ software - Red Hat allows me to do what I want to do, without 
making me spend time fixing other things that may be fun, but get in the way 
of what I'm trying to achieve.

That doesn't mean _your_ choice is any less valid.  If I had lots of spare time 
I would "./configure; make; make install" everything myself, but I don't, so I 
don't.
