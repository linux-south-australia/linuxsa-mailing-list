I often do things like this, and there is a good reason for it -- my time is
worth more than an extra pipe...

I explain:

I want to see the contents of /var/log/messages, so I usually type:

`cat /var/log/messages`

Then I realise (either after running the command then editing the command
line, or immediately) that I want to grep for "sendmail", so I add:

`| grep sendmail`

...

I often do this with cat and more:

`cat /etc/passwd | more`

Yes, it's less efficient. No I don't care, as it's even less efficient for
me to retype the command...
