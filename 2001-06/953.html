Re: tcsh does not work as root shell in SuSE 7.1??

> As it happens, the problem turned out to be suprisingly simple:  The
> permissions on / were drw-r--r--.  Someone had been a bit over-aggressive
> with a recursive chmod at some stage in the past, and not noticed (because
> they were root, so it didn't affect them)!
> 
> Not that I think this has anything to do with Alan's problem;  It's just
> a funny story from way-back-when... :-)

Had a similar problem at one stage with sendmail and .forwards. Forwarding
was refusing to work, claiming that the directory wasn't readable. The
directories were quite clearly readable, but it turened out that the root
of the home directory structure wasn't readable to everyone. Change the
permissions and voila, problem solved.
