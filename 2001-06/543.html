Netsaint is mature. The functionality Tellurian added (the embedded perl
interpreter) got in to version 0.0.7b1, and was in production at several
quite large sites by version 0.0.7b3. Sorry if you don't like the number
zero but that's how it is :-)

If you really want a bad day, look at the output of this:

  rpm -qai | grep ^Version | cut -d : -f 2 | sort  

or

  grep -h Version: /var/lib/apt/lists/*Packages | cut -d: -f2 | sort

My systems give pages of packages at 0.0.xx :-) rpm -q is better than
dpkg -l for this sort of thing.

Question: what is the best way to do this with BSD?
