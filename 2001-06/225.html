> people will do so on such a plan, others won't. If it is
> properly priced, then the ISP won't go broke, and the
> punters will stop complaining because you have given them

Pretend you're a business.

You have customers who cost you more than they give you. You don't want
them. You change a policy and either get rid of them or turn them into
profitable customers (at the least, you limit what they cost you). Why would
it matter if these customers complained? They are unprofitable anyway! Even
more so, if they weren't your customers, you'd go out of your way to keep it
that way -- why attract loss making customers?
