Re: Hard Disk Duplicating

On Wed, 20 Jun 2001, Wayne Simes wrote:

>
> Hi All,
>
> At home I have a small dedicated linux server with two 20gb hard drives. The
> first hard drive is divided up into about 4 maybe 5 partitions. The second
> is not doing anything, it's just connected but always left unmounted.
>
> Rather than not use the drive I would like to use it to completely
> duplicate/backup the first drive. I assume that this is not possible while
> the first drive is mounted and files are in use. Other than using software
> raid, what can be done ?, and what should I do ?
>
> Nothing to complicated please!

I can't beleive no one has mentioned rsync!

Use rsync to copy the files to the other drive. Several advantages:

o Don't necessarily have to have the same disk size/partitioning structure
o After the first rsync, subsequent rsyncs are very quick
o Files are totally live and available just in case you do something
  'foolish' and need a backup
o No unmounting/single-user mode stuff required

Disadvantages:

o No automatic failover
o A little bit of work needed to make it a bootable disk in the
  event of the first falling over

If you can't deduce an appropriate set of command-line options from the
man page, let me know and I will dig something up.
