RE: Learning how to programme in Linux

On Wed, 6 Jun 2001, Richard Sharpe wrote:

> However, when it comes to big projects, then you need to learn more. Most
> big projects are done in C. There is not a lot of C++ out there in big
> projects, but there are notable exceptions: Jade and HylaFAX being two that
> I am aware of.

There is a very large amount of free C++.

$ cd /var/lib/apt/lists
$ grep Depends: *_Packages | grep libstdc++ | wc  
    740    9413  133168  

There are over 30 Gnome packages. Only 7 KDE packages are listed because
usually the only libstdc++ dependency is further upstream. This needs some
weeding out:

$ grep -h -B 8 Depends:.*libstdc++ *_Packages | grep Package: | sort \
| uniq | wc
    395     790    6977

Large packages that this yields include: jikes, abiword, various bits of
apt, a dozen libraries, asla and mp3 programs, umsdos.

Many user interface projects are done in C++. The object-oriented model is
a good conceptual match for graphical interfaces.  KDE is based on Qt, a
GPL'd C++ toolkit. There are hundreds of KDE programs, and besides kde
itself contains, what, a million lines?

There are other projects not in my apt lists, such as yodl.
