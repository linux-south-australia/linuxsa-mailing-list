Re: Socket Queries

"Davis, Harvey" <Harvey.Davis@dsto.defence.gov.au> wrote:

> 1. Do the elements of the sockaddr_in structure need to be in network byte
> order?

Yes - use htonl when assigning to the structure.

> 2. What the are the reasons for getting a connection refused (appart from
> the obvious one that no one is listening) ;) ?

No one is listening, or the connection is being rejected by a IP filter rule.  I'll
go out on a limb and presume that you haven't converted the port to network
order either (htons()), and therefore you're connecting to the wrong port.

> 3. Is there a limit to the number of sockets a process can create?

Yes.

> Is this limit low (I need about 6 sockets).

No.  Check your hard/soft limit, but it's most likely near the 1024 mark.

> 4. Is there any program that can tell you what sockets are currently being
> listened for connections on?

netstat -nap
