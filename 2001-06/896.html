Re: RAID Array Problems

Andrew Reid wrote:

> three hours. The UPS lasted for 2 of them, but it died. We couldn't even
> see in the server room to plug a monitor in and type 'halt'.

Probably just as well .. unless you have big UPS's, the startup power
required by even a 14" monitor tends to wipe them out. Better to leave a
monitor on than have it cause an instant outage.

> <weep, weep> Please tell me that my RAID Array is OK and that everything
> will be happy happy. Please! Anyone?

I'd say your data is safe in there somewhere.

I saw something similar a long time ago, but I can't remember the
specifics. I vaguely remember re-creating the superblock, and running it
as a degraded array (single disk) so that I at least had one disk left
of data in case anything was trashed by me in the fixup process.

It might be worth getting a spare matching disk to dd the contents to,
so you can have a 'practice' fix first.

This is the hidden benefit of hardware raid - the recovery procedures
are documented in the book that came in the box, with open source
software raid it's up to the original installer to document the process
:)
