You're viewing this whole thing bass-ackward.

If you want FreeBSD on Intel, run FreeBSD on Intel.

If you want near-as-dammit FreeBSD (plus other nice stuff) on Mac 
hardware, buy Mac hardware.

We both win. Its only Microsoft users that loose. Remember which side 
of the fence Apple is on, whether you feel like buying their hardware 
or not

[personally, I -like- their hardware - unlike the Intel clone soup 
[*] the hardware benefits from consistently designed architecture and 
a corresponding stunningly high degree of compatibility with 
additional peripherals of almost any persuasion. For people who want 
to actually -use- a computer, not fiddle with its innards as a 
profession, Apple have created 'Unix for the rest of us'. They 
absolutely should be applauded for leading the consumer market in 
client desktops - again. And now I get a highly useable system that 
also has gcc, perl, real unix, apache etc etc pre-installed out of 
the box. It's heaven on a stick]
