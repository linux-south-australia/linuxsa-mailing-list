Re: Samba with OS/2

At 01:35 PM 6/13/01 +1000, Daryl Pilkington wrote:
>Hi,
>OS/2 file sharing uses SMB technology & supports TCPBEUI compliant to
>RFCs 1001/1002.

Hmmm, by this, I assume that you mean NetBIOS over TCP/IP as defined by
RFC1001  and RFC1002.

>Based on this information, do people think an OS/2 requester or server
>will talk to a Linux Samba server?

I believe so. There is a chapter on OS/2 in Special Edition, Using Samba.

>Can Samba replace a PDC yet?

Yes. The only thing currently missing in 2.2.0/2.2.1 is BDC functionality,
and it is being worked on.

>What testing have PDC replacements had with other than Windoze clients?

Quite a lot, really.

>FYI
>OS/2 can & does run TCPBEUI protocol without NETBEUI.

Hmmm, by that, do you mean NetBIOS-less SMB, over port 445, or simply that
you can choose to run NetBEUI and NetBIOS over TCP/IP side by side, or not,
as you choose?

Samba only supports SMB over NetBIOS over TCP/IP and NetBIOS-less SMB at
this stage. You loose virtual servers if you choose the latter.

>An OS/2 workstation or server configured as above will talk to a Windoze
>workstation or server.
