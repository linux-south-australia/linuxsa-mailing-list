Well, I did say in point (b) "with loads of parameters tunable by me". In
other words, take the leaky bucket as you described it in pserldo code and
put in as much of the benfit of your research as you can, and stick that
behind a cgi that talks to the billing database. I think the only
innovation (if it is that) which I'm proposing is that both the ISP and
the customer have a known worst-case.

If I was faced with a web form that offered me "TB (token bucket) arrays,
TB trees, TB array-trees, sustained bit-rate profiles (max bit-rate as a
continuous function of time-scale), probabilistic sustained bit-rate
profiles (total traffic distribution as a function of time-scale), trees
of PBRSs" and more, I would

   i)   read the help pages until I roughly understood the parameters
   ii)  satisfy myself that I wasn't going to go broke by fiddling
   iii) make sure there was a way to measure the effect of any changes
        without me having to construct my own graphs etc
   iv)  start fiddling

But no way would I understand what was going to happen before I did it.
Over time, I would start to get a users-side feel of how your 12 years of
research bears on my desired and essential usage patterns.

I also think the data from this exercise (ie what charging regime I
eventually feel happy with) should be valuable feedback for the ISP in
creating packages for users who are scared by the concept of user-fiddles
charging schemes.

Maybe some local ISP should employ you to write a test rig to run over
historical data to see how these algorithms would have impacted customers
and end-users in the past.
