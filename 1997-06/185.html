Ahhh... The old mount cdrom question... A personal favorite of mine.

I thing Geoffrey Bennett has writen a html page or two on this subject.
http://www.linuxsa.org.au/

But just in case...

You need to put a line like this in you /etc/fstab file

#<device>    <mountpoint>   <filesystemtype> <options>     <dump> <fsckorder>
/dev/hdb     /cdrom         iso9660           noauto,ro,user,exec 0 0

(The bottom line of course, not the top which is just a description of what 
each field is.)
You will need to adjust the <device> and <mountpoint> to suit your own system.

Then just type
mount /cdrom
and
umount /cdrom
as required.

Of course you must have cdrom support in your kernel but if it is an IDE or 
SCSI drive then you should already have it.
