Re: The Home and End Keys

On Thu, 12 Jun 1997, Alex Garner wrote:

> Hey ppl,
> 
> How can I make the home and end keys do a ctrl-a (beggining of line) and a
> ctrl-e (end of line) respecivley? I'm using bash from a console, and in a
> vt220 telnet window.

Don't know about the vt220, but this works from the console:

Put the following line in a file called .inputrc in your home directory.

"\e[1~"= beginning-of-line
"\e[4~"= end-of-line
"\e[3~"= delete-char

This does not work for me in an xterm. Doh. Sure I could fix it with
xmodmap or similar, but lack the motivation :)

Oh yeah, this also makes the del key do something useful.

This was all from RTFM BTW, try man bash. It's in there somewhere :)
