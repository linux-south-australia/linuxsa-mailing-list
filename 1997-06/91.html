On Fri, 13 Jun 1997, Geoffrey D. Bennett wrote:

: > i currently have a 1.1 gig hdd, formatted with a 100meg swap file, and
: > 500 meg each for redhat linux and win95
: > due to necessities in work and study, i need another 200 meg for
: > win95..is there any easy way to accomplish this, without having to
: > reinstall anything?
: 
: Unless you buy a 200MB drive like Richard suggested, the answer is
: 'no'.
: 
: The only other option that I know of (apart from deleting both
: partitions and redoing it all) is to delete the Linux partition, use
: Partition Magic (a commercial program) to expand the size of the win95
: partition, then recreate your Linux partition and reinstall it.
: 
: You cannot use fips in this case.  fips is only for _shrinking_ dos
: partitions (and not FAT32 ones either).  You cannot resize a Linux
: filesystem.
