<rave mode = on>
SLUG (Sydney Linux User Group is a well organised but 
non-incorporated group. Maybe a contact to that group
would be useful at this decision making time.

My personal thoughts... well, Linux is attractive
partly because it is free (in more senses than
just monetary) and it seems appropriate to keep
that in mind. Having had experience over a long
time with committees I fear it is too easy to
have a group make decisions which may cost us
not just our money (eg the recent ad-hoc statement
that we should hit everyone for say thirty bux
so we can buy books and stuff) but also the loose
camaraderie that typifies the Linux community.

Having said that, I won't try to impose that view
if it turns out to be a minority view. The group
should do what the group, as a group, wants to do.
Check out all the alternatives. Then go for it
and make sure you all have fun doing it.

Bruce
