I don't think Pine supports POP3 at all.  It does however appear to
support IMAP.  If you start up Pine, press "S" for "Setup", "C" for
"Config", scroll down to "inbox-path", and press "?", it says:
----------
[...]
A typical remote inbox-path entry would be:
{monet.art.nowhere.edu}INBOX where "monet.art.nowhere.edu" is replaced
by the name of your IMAP mail server.
[...]
FOR MORE INFORMATION: From the Main Menu, select "L FOLDER LIST" and
press "?" for Help... Look for the section "Valid Folder Names".
[...]
----------

This should get you started on the right track if your ISP supports
IMAP.  If it doesn't, have a look at fetchmail, which will download
email from a POP or IMAP server and feed it to the local delivery
system on your computer.  Fetchmail will be on your Red Hat Linux CD
if it is not already installed.

There are plans to have a talk on setting up fetchmail, etc for dialup
users.
