Re: [Musings out Loud] Squid, Netscape and Mozilla

>(2) In a fit of brilliance, I decided to repartion /var and make
>/var/spool/squid reiserfs because XFS is really crappy at deleting lots
>of little files. And you can fit more little files in reiserfs than XFS
>because of the packing algorithm

Actually, if you apply a little lateral thought, a busy squid works better
if you don't cache lots of little files.

If you set minimum_object_size to something like 5-10K, then squid won't
waste ram making an object reference for it once it's cached, and your
filesystem won't have so many entries to deal with, so it too will be more
efficient. Being tiny files, they'll download so quick anyway you're not
likely to notice the difference in speed.

Somewhat counter-intuitive, but great for performance.
