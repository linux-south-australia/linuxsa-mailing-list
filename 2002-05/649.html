Re: W2k->RH7.2 codpage problem?

On Wed, 22 May 2002, Darryl Ross wrote:

> If it is the usual \n <-> \n\r problem, you can fix it from within vi:

You mean within vim.

vim != vi

If you use a vi clone that is closer to being fully vi compatible (read
'faithful' perhaps :-), you will find that the extra characters on the
ends of lines are rather noticable. vim tries to hide this 'complexity'
from you. This can result in tears before bedtime if you aren't aware it's
happening.

Anyway, in a real vi (!), it's a simple case of:

:%s/^M$//

Where the Ctrl-M character (represented above by ^M) is produced by
pressing Ctrl-V then Ctrl-M.

Anyway, this is a job for sed, not an editor.
