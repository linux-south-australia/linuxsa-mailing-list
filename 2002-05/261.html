> Hmmmmmmm, based on my current experiences at work, I'd avoid 3Com NIC's
> at all costs and go for Intel NICs.  For some reason, 3Com and Cisco
> don't agree on what autonegotiation is and it makes for some really,
> really slow backups (which I just happen to manage *sigh*).

> Chuck an Intel in the computer and bingo, things are all sweet.....

Of course, any half decent Cisco person knows never to rely on Cisco's
autonegotiation and to lock the ports to specific speed and duplex
settings.

We had a case recently whereby we'd locked our Sun qfe interface to
100/full duplex but the switch (a 2950), was set to auto. The stupid
switch decided to then change between 10 and 100 very frequently,
resulting in delayed packets and slow connections for the users. Once
the switch was also locked to 100/full, everything worked a treat.
