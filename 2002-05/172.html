Re: Getting linux onto a PC with a fried KB controller

Nathan...

I've done quite a few "serial" installs of BSD onto systems without
video cards / faulty keyboard controllers.  Basically all that's
required is a standard laplink style serial cable, throw one end into a
serial port on the installing system, and the other into a working PC
running your favourite serial communications app (I use minicom under
Linux or SecureCRT/Hyperterminal under Windows).  You then control
everything from there - no keyboard or monitor needed.

I've got no idea if the Redhat install kernel has support for serial
console though - I don't see why it wouldn't.  Give it a go.

- Ryan
