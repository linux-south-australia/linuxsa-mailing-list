Re: postgresql under load

Slashdot's kinda like mainstream australian newspapers:
I'll look at one while i'm waiting in a take-away-food joint, and there's
stuff there thats interesting to read, but its definately not where I look
for accurate, unbiased, and professional information.

As I said, and you've agreed, MySQL definately was the inferior product for
serious databases, several years ago. And we also agree it's been improving
since.

However, Postgres started out ahead, and has been developing continually
too.

MySQL is good for lightweight apps, personal DBs, etc... it has less
overhead, smaller footprint, etc. and is simpler to use.

Postgres, on the other hand, is comparable to professional databases in
terms of high availability, live backup, replication, high user numbers,
etc.

If those terms don't mean anything to you, go for MySQL, otherwise... ;)
