With all respect, I'm seeing about 10,000 networks, mainly class Bs, from
our Internet2 peering.  This includes every large US university and
research institution.

You might consider that the dorms of some of these unis use more bandwidth
than the southern hemisphere (Australia included). The University of
California is in itself the world's most heavily used network (if you
consider the Internet an "inter-network").  For many years UUnet was
nothing more than the outsourced UC network engineering department.

Of course, the I2 media release is a PR stunt, but that's not to say that
I2 is the ivory tower network that you imply.  There is a story behind the
I2 long-haul performance work highlighted by the media release -- see
http://www.web100.org/ for that.
