On Thu, May 02, 2002 at 09:40:09AM +0930, dmdeflui@subcorp.com.au wrote:
> Also, you can clear out the mail files, eg you could go to your mail file
> folder
> cd /var/spool/mail
> and then clear out each mail file causing you problems, eg
> cat /dev/null > mailfilename
> 
> Good idea to do this from time to time as some users just fill up their mail
> file with junk and don't bother clearing it.  You can tell them they are
> going to lose all their mail completely if they don't clear up their own
> mail files.

that's why I like to have the mailfile (ie. "inbox") in the users
home dir rather than /var/spool/mail (or /var/mail).  You then
stick a quota on user home dirs and if they let their mailfile
grow too big their incoming email starts bouncing.  The result
is they look after themselves and you have zero maintenance.

(and as an added bonus it's easy to backup the "inbox" with the home
dir rather than separately trying to backup /var/mail - if you
bother trying to backup mailfiles - which I do at home but with
lots of users may well be a waste of effort)
