For the hardware, you may wish to consider hardware IDE RAID to SCSI
devices... They look like a normal SCSI drive to the computer, but
actually consist of a collection of IDE drives set up transparently as a
RAID -- there is no driver or special software needed at all, just a
SCSI interface.

eg, this one http://www.positiveesolutions.com/Araid/12000/index.html
seems to have dual Ultra3/160 interfaces, and will take up to 12
UDMA/100 drives... If you were to use drives over the 100G mark, you
could easily reach 1Tb... and it would be fast random access to boot. I
don't think these are that expensive -- I beleive this model is around
$A10K...

Of course, a file system that size may be fun to fsck, but ... :)
