Re: DAT tape (DDS-3) support in linux ?

On Wed, 10 Dec 1997, Matthew Tippett wrote:

> Mark Brown wrote:
> > 
> > Can anyone tell me if linux supports DAT tapes?
> > Specifically a 12GB DDS-3 drive?
> > 
> > Hoping for the answer 'of course it does' :-)
> 
> It should.  There is a SCSI tape interface which *ALL*
> drives should match too.  Similar to the SCSI Disk 
> interface.

FWIW I've used DDS-1, DDS-2, QIC (24, 150 and 1.3G) and
Exabyte without any problems. Some vendors have configurable
drives, e.g. HP, and you may have to set DIP switches before
you get full functionality. Check your manual, or in the
case of HP, find someone with the technical manuals on
the drive.
