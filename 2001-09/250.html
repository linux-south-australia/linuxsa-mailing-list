> Hi all,
> 
> On a plain install of RH 7.1, I get the following error.
> 
> Warning: unable to open intial console
> Kernel panic: No init found. Try passing init= option to kernel.

That's bad.

> What is an initial console?

init (the mother/father/legal guardian of all unix processes) needs to
open some kind of device to write it's boot messages to and accept input
from. This is usally /dev/console or /dev/tty0 or something similar.

> Where to I put init= if I can't get into the machine?

At the lilo boot prompt. You may need to hit Ctrl-X at the boot menu. You
can then boot your machine with something like:

 linux init=/bin/sh

This may not work, given that /bin/sh is going to need a console as well.

You may need to boot your machine off the CD / magic disk and mount your
root filesystem, change to the dev directory of your root filesystem and
use MAKEDEV to create the devices that are missing (something like
./MAKEDEV console may do the trick).

Hope it helps,
