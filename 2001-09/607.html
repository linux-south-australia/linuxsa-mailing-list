It also spreads through any visible/hidden shares on your network, so 
as soon as some twit runs the readme.exe/*.eml/*.nws attachment you 
can kiss any freespace on your fileserver goodbye.

We're running a Samba box here with roughly 60 clients, in about 4
hours we had 20,000+ .eml files & about the same number of .nws files
scattered thru it.  At 70-150k each it chews up the space fairly rapidly.
3 cheers for scripts & cron jobs :o), had a script running every 10 mins
to clean out the above files so no users got any bright ideas about opening
any of them.  (err, do that on NT! esp. when you're 420km offsite & 
connected via RAS, on a Linux box of course ;o)
