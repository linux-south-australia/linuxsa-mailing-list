Re: Gateway Solo laptop problems

On 05 Sep 2001 15:38:28 +0930, Richard Russell wrote:

> WinModem -- probably not... I would think that if you spent some time on
> google.com and/or gateway.com, you could find the chip it uses, and whether
> it is supported... PCCard (PCMCIA) Modem/Net cards aren't that expensive
> nowadays anyway, so that may be your best option...

If it's got the more popular Lucent LT Chipset, there's quite a strong
possibility of compatibility. I tried it with a PCMCIA LT Winmodem the
other day. You just need to download and compile some drivers which take
care of the wacky serial and modulation stuff Winmodems like to do.

> Ah, there's your modem chip -- is it on the RH HCL? Any documentation for
> it visible with google? your DVD probably won't work as a DVD player... at
> least not without some serious messing about...

Unfortunately, the RH HCL probably won't list individual modems (there
must be hundreds of them, surely?). It's (at best) going to say "Hayes
Compatible Hardware Modem" or "XYZ Known Brand" but probably not "ABC
Backyard Modems, Inc. 56kbps modem". The HCLs may list chipsets,
possibly like "LT Winmodem chipset", but that requires you to know the
type of card you've got.

Run (as root) '/sbin/cardctl ident', which should give you some
information on the cards currently inserted. That usually sheds some
light on the chipset.

Have a look at http://www.linmodems.org/ as well as
http://walbran.org/sean/linux/linmodem-howto-all.html
