Robyn,
I suggest you may have accidentally trashed something from /dev/.. If you
are missing things like /dev/console then you're really not going to get
anything to work at all, no matter what init=... you append to lilo.

I really think that the bootdisk option someone suggested is the best thing
to do now. Its pretty simple..

boot the rescue cdrom or floppy
(you could use the redhat/mandrake/etc installer CD here if you wanted, and
just Alt-F2 to the spare console after a little while, if you don't have a
rescue cd handy)

mount your normal root partition, and cd to its dev direcotry,  eg
cd /mnt/rootdrive/dev
now run MAKEDEV with the std option
./MAKEDEV std


This should recreate the basic essentials.

You should probably question how they disappeared though..
What were you doing to the machine prior to its refusal to boot up?

Often the answer to "what did i change?" answers the more important question
of "how do i fix it?"
