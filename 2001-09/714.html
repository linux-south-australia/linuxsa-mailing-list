Re: Auto start squid

> i have squid running and operating on redhat 7.1 but cannot find or at least
> understand how to automate it at bootup, any help much appreciated

to get anything to run at boot time, you want to look at the init
scripts... see /etc/init.d/* (or /etc/rc.d/init.d/*) for examples...

you need to know a little about runlevels (see /etc/inittab), and the
rc symlinks (/etc/rc?.d/*). Note the naming convention -- S means
start in this runlevel, K means stop in this runlevel, and the number
is the order in which they are started/stopped (for runlevel n, the
symlinks in /etc/rc<n>.d are run as `<name> start` and `<name> stop`
depending on the name...)

Most pieces of software come with a pre-cooked init script -- it may
already be in the init.d directory, or it could still be in the source
tree...

Some software, specifically that which you use rarely that has a quick
startup time (eg ftp servers) may want to be run from inetd... This
starts them whenever someone connects, rather than running them so
they wait for a connection...

HTH, or at least gives you some places to look ;)
