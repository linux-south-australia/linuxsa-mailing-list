Re: Slackware 8 / Sound

Chris!

> I have been told it is fairly simple to
> recompile existing kernel as it has built in drivers to support this style
> card. Do I make_menuconfig and config the desired drivers with a Y option or
> M- Module and modprobe? 

Yes. In /usr/src/linux (or wherever your kernel lives) there should be a
README. Also you can checkout the Documentation directory which might
mention your soundcard too.

> If anyone could set me in the right direction it
> would be greatly appreciated.

I would build it first as a module and then try to modprobe it. Look in
/lib/modules (after having made the modules and installed them) and try:

* modprobe [sound_card_module]

NOTE: don't include the ".o" when using modprobe. modprobe automagically
adds that.

Then you can try "playing" a wav file. If I recall correctly, the Sound
HOWTO says you can do something like:

* /dev/dsp < sound.wav

Naturally, "sound.wav" would be some sound file.

You could also just try "play". Then just add it to your modules.conf.
Mine has:

...
alias sound-slot-0 es1370
...

The es1370 is what I modprobe manually.
