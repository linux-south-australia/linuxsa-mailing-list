Re: [OT] CodeRed Attack during O/S install

Just a comment on some aspects..

1.
  1.5 Gb of outbound traffic over the course of a few hours??
  I remember seeing an analysis of CR and CR2, and it should not generate
  that much traffic during the target acquisition stage.

2.
  It destroyed his motherboard and harddrives??
  I *really* doubt this. CR[12] is a software worm; it cannot physically
  damage your HARDware.

3.
  "he didn't have email installed, so it didn't spread to any of his
   contacts."
  It isn't an email-based worm! Email has nothing to do with it. If this
  guy had a local network of other PCs, he should obviously be scanning them
  too.. (if running IIS)


Notes.
  I have seen posts on Bugtraq relating to Windoze installing IIS even when
  it was turned off.. Its considered a prerequisite for other services, so
  if any of them are turned on, IIS gets installed anyway, and turned on.
  Pretty silly if you ask me, but thats MS for ya.

  Congrats on your friend swapping to Linux.. I hope it goes well for him.
  Note, however, that Linux isn't magically secure.. In the past Linux has
  had its share of remote root exploits too. Mind you, its a ****load better
  than Microsoft's history ;)
  Still, I would advise your friend to read up on some security HOWTOs,
  and to get familiar with a good iptables setup.

  If he really does need MS stuff, the trick is to put it behind the Linux
  or {Open|Free}BSD firewall, and make sure you only let things in and out
  that you want.


-Toby
