Re: Digital camera suggestions

Hi.

I have a similar story with an Olympus Camedia C1400L (sold as D600
overseas, go figure) It has a 1.4 megapixel ccd, which was groundbreaking
at the time, but commonplace now. Cost a couple of grand.

I managed to get transfer via the serial port, but the files are a bit
big, and I am a bit impatient, so I bought a pcmcia adapter and a pcmcia
drive for my desktop linux box. A bit of a fiddle and I had an automatic
script that copied files off the pcmcia card and put them on a web page.

The pcmcia adapter also works out of the box on a mac or win2k laptop,
cannot comment about other os's. Low end mac laptops no longer have
pcmcia, but you can get a flash/smartmedia to usb adapter that also works
well for them, don't know if linux works with the usb adapter though.

With the image sizes increasing as they are, I'd be looking (as you are)
for a direct connection between camera and linux. I think the only way for
this to happen is via usb or firewire, but you will probably have a bit of
a task to get things working. The pcmcia route is one that works.

The pcmcia drive I bought came from Microgram computers. They advertise in
most Aussie PC mags. 

Regards,

Michael
