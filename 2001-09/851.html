On Sat, Sep 29, 2001 at 08:02:38PM +0930, Richard Sharpe wrote:
> Hi,
> 
> In an effort to liven up proceedings in this mailing list, I would like 
> to enquire whether Debian has any pretty-boy config utilities like 
> netcfg and netconf under RedHat, or do you have to be a Macho Man and 
> config it with the files?

Richard,

You might like to try the Ximian Setup Tools - while not being Debian 
specific, their Perl backends work with Debian systems (as well as many 
other distributions).  I've taken a brief look at them, and they offer a 
reasonable array of simple conifguration options, dealing with user 
administration, network setup, timezone configuration, and such things.  A 
couple of their newer tools (in CVS only until the next release) include a 
display setup tool (for XFree4) and an internet sharing tool.  The tools 
in general show a lot of promise, especially in that they are designed to 
deal with setup rollbacks, and do not mangle hand-written config files.

For more information, check out 
http://ximian.com/devzone/projects/xst-devel.html.

HTH,

Tim
