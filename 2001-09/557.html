On Thu, 20 Sep 2001, Simon Hackett wrote:

> Lets compare run costs, shall we. For permanent 64K links on ISDN vs 
> the permanent 512K link on SDSL:
> 
> 1) Permanent Link using Internode SDSL, 512k symmetric, including 500 
> Mb of downloaded traffic:
> 
> $165 per month. All-inclusive total.
> 
> 2) ISDN 64k Permanent Link from Telstra (using an Onramp 2)
> 
> $ 55.00 per month, ISDN socket rental (ISDN OnRamp2)
> $103.95 per month, 1 x Xpress in NDD1 plan fee
> $165.00 per month, 'Limited Cap' for Xpress service
> $ 90.00 for 500 Mb of downloads @ $0.18/Mb
> -------
> $413.95 per month total (for 1/8th of the peak speed), plus whatever 
> minimum monthly cost your ISP requires for his ISDN port.
> 
> 3) ISDN DoV based permanent link (using the voice call hacks to 
> sidestep the Xpress plans, on an ISDN OnRamp Home Highway service):
> 
> $42.90 per month (ISDN Onramp Home Highway)
> 	(includes 5.50 worth of local calls, i.e. 30 x $0.18 roughly 
> - enough to
> 		keep the ISDN dialled up permanently over the month)
> $90.00 for 500 Mb of downloads @ $0.18/Mb
> ------
> $132.90 total, providing OnRamp Home Highway works in your situation (i.e.
> 			not supposed to be provided to business customers).
> (Plus whatever your ISP charges as a minimum for use of an ISDN 64k port)
> 
> 
> ---
> 
> The above ignores startup costs in all cases for simplicity.
> 
> So compared to a traditional business ISDN 64k permanent link, SDSL 
> at 512k with Internode works out to save $248.95 PER MONTH,  for 
> eight times the speed, and no ISDN router needed.
> 
> Even compared to OnRamp Home Highway plus the use of the 
> Data-Over-Voice hack, its only $32.10 per month more expensive to use 
> SDSL @ 512k, and again this is 8 times faster than ISDN 64k, and 
> probably is no worse than break even when ISP fixed monthly costs for 
> the ISDN channel into their access server is added in.
> 
> I was comparing #1 with #2 when I wrote that text. Even using the 
> hacks and hence option 3, its pretty darn clear which one provides 
> better -value-, isn't it :)
