Source files are checksum'd and kept in the ports tree.  Patches in a port
are not though...

The MD5 checksums are kept by the system when a port or package is
installed.  If you suspect that the binary has been replaced or change,
then you can check the actuall checksum to the stored one.

bin/pine
@comment MD5:064782df23283c406f9b9b10506703b5

MD5 (/usr/local/bin/pine) = 064782df23283c406f9b9b10506703b5

Though if the attacker gains root access and knows his/her way around a
FreeBSD box, then I guess they could remove /var/db/pkg/??? thus removing
all traces of the known safe MD5 checksum.
