Re: Digital camera suggestions

On Tue, 11 Sep 2001 14:51, Fraser Farrell wrote:
> Any recommendations for a digital camera that:
>
>  - works with Linux,
>
>  - is available in Adelaide (or Australia at least),
>
>  - doesn't cost more than my car,
>
>  - doesn't have any proprietary gotchas such as unique memory
> packs or weird batteries.

I have the opposite.  I recently tried to configure a Kodak EZ-200 USB 
digital camera (retails for around 200 $) under my newly USB-enabled 
system, and couldn't get it to work.  Admittedly I didn't try very 
hard, but in all the mailing lists and postings I saw lots of requests 
for help, but no responses, so I guess nobody's got one working yet.

As Toby has just suggested, gPhoto has a list 
<URL:http://www.gphoto.org/cameras.html>; of many digital cameras that 
it claims to support.  Most of the cameras on that list seem to be 
moderate to highly priced (no 200 $ cameras there).  The 
most-frequently listed brands seem to be Canon, Kodak, and Olympus, but 
of course you should check the list.


Hope this helps.
