Re: PCI USB cards in old box

On Thu,  6 Sep 2001 14:18, Matt Brennan wrote:
> Anyone have experience whacking a PCI card with a bunch of USB ports
> into a Linux box?
>
> I have an old mobo (from the dawn of USB in PCs) which has USB
> support on the board/bios but no USB header/connectors installed. I
> would like to use USB on this box and wonder if it is easier to
> attempt to find and install connectors or buy a PCI card and whack it
> in.

I bought a box "with USB support" a few years ago, but found that there 
was no backing plate / connector, so went without USB for a while.

Last week I saw a USB 2-port backing plate at Dick Smith Electronics 
for about 15 $, which I was very pleased to find (when I bought the 
computer, a backing plate cost over 50 $).  My Linux box now has USB 
support.  :-)

(I'm still having problems configuring my Kodak EZ-200 digital camera, 
tho.)

I think buying a backing plate is likely to be less expensive than 
purchasing a PCI USB card.
