Re: Locating ethernet card I/O address

Don Collins wrote:
> Hi Nathan,
> 	Richard's suggestion is good except that you should try
> `modprobe ne` in place of `modprobe 3c509`.  You could also try
> `cat /proc/ioports`.  Failing this, download the drivers for the
> RTL8019 chipset from Realtek and run the setup utility
> (setup.exe or rset8019.exe) from a DOS prompt!

Oops, did not notice that Nathan wanted to use an NE2000 look alike.

'cat /proc/ioports' will tell you what is in use by drivers that are 
already loaded. It will not tell you what your SureCom is configured to 
use. However, knowing what is already in use will cut down on the 
addresses you need to probe.

Does the 3C509 still work? If so, your SureCom is not using the same 
addresses.
