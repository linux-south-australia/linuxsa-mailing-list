Re: FreeBSD

Richard Russell wrote:

> > Think of this, what if someone broke into the debian servers - however
> > unlikely - and replaced for example the bash package with a new bash package,
> > trojan and all.
> > Ports gets past this.
> 
> how does ports get past that? Unless you investigate the source yourself,
> you are no better off...

ports comes with MD5 sums of the original source code tar.gz's to
packages as part of its install.

In this way it will not install packages that don't have a matching
MD5sum. Because it does the sum on the original source and then applies
patches, you don't have to rely on pre-defined mirrors to get the
tarball from.

The drawback is that it's not very friendly to upgrading to newer
versions that have been released since you installed ports, but there's
probably a way around that :)
