Well there are certainly some fanless (or low cycle time fan usage) 
examples out there, for instance:

Apple iMac; Apple G3 Cube; Apple Powerbook Ti G4/500 (if you run it 
at low speed - else eventually a tiny little cooling fan cycles in 
now and again); Laptops based on the TransMeta low power, high speed 
chipset; Almost all 'organisers' (all the way from my Nokia 9110 
through palm pilots to other such things, all of which have more 
grunt than the Amstrad would have)

The most direct examples would certainly be the iMac and the G3 Cube.

Returning to the original requestors question - answers to the 
obvious subsidiary questions (e.g.: What size, what budget, what 
speed, what purpose, does it have to be Intel based or just Linux 
based) might help a lot.

One can run Linux on a single US$25 chip (http://www.netsilicon.com) 
if you want to , and that's utterly silent. But it depends on what 
you -really want-, eh?
