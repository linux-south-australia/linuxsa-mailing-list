  IPA Bulletin: New DSL option - 512k symmetric DSL - now available from =
Internode

  In this IPA Bulletin:

  * New 512/512 DSL speed available
  * Telstra 'Competition Notice' related to ADSL

  ------------------

  * New 512/512 DSL speed available

  Internode is pleased to announce the immediate availability of a new =
Digital Subscriber Line speed option, as part of our broadband Internet =
service offerings powered by our sister company, Agile Communications.

  The new speed is 512 kilobits per second -full duplex symmetric-, =
which offers an important alternative to the existing three 'asymmetric' =
speeds in the market.

  The new speed choice, which we call SDSL (Symmetric Digital Subscriber =
Line) offers high speed (512 kilobits per second - eight times faster =
than ISDN) in both directions at the same time - at less than the cost =
of a Telstra 64K ISDN line!

  This new speed option is available now throughout our South Australian =
coverage areas.

  It uses standard 'ADSL' access devices for access in your premises - =
so existing ADSL routers and bridges will work perfectly with this new =
speed. Existing customers are welcome to change over to this new speed =
if they wish.

  The prices for this new service (and for speed changes), an order =
form, and other information about our DSL offerings are on our web site =
at http://adsl.internode.on.net

  We hope the new SDSL offering will be of benefit especially to =
business customers looking to use DSL as an alternative to ISDN for the =
provision of a permanent Internet link.

  * Telstra 'Competition Notice' related to ADSL

  Those of you that follow the telecommunications press will be aware =
that Telstra has recently been issued with a 'Competition Notice' by the =
Australian Competition and Consumer Commission (ACCC).

  Details on this action are provided on the ACCC web site at:

  http://203.6.251.7/accc.internet/digest/view_media.cfm?RecordID=3D449

  ... and we invite you to read about it there.

  Internode believes that the issuing of this Competition Notice by the =
ACCC will lead to rapid and critical improvements in the viability of =
the further deployment of ADSL in Australia.

  The technical demands placed upon Telstra through this notice should =
assist innovative organisations like Internode to develop new and more =
interesting services around the core DSL technology offering in the near =
term.

  Internode applauds the ACCC's necessary decision to take this action =
to help drive positive change in the Australian broadband marketplace.
