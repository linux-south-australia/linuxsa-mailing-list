RE: AMD vs Intel

Kudos to Intel for their clock throttling capability. However, If one reads
the fine print, "AMD did not bless the Thunderbird core with ANY thermal
protection whatsoever. If the heat sink should come off, the owner is facing
a significant financial loss.", one understands that the writer of the
article is obviously talking about the possible loss of data and not the
hardware that got cooked being that AMD is so much less expensive  @$170(US)
than Intel @$550(US). I'd like to know the MTBF of  "socket A notches" never
having seen one fail, personally. And who, other than Tom's,
removes/replaces heat sinks on a running system?  8 ) Really!

 Having both Intel and AMD systems,  I enjoy the performance of the AMD
enough to recommend touching it with a barge pole. In fact, a twig. Do know
the idiosyncrasies of both platforms before your purchase and its targeted
use.

I'll let you know when the eggs are ready.
