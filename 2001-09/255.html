Re: Recording video

If you have a firewire setup, then dvgrab and associated utils work
brilliantly!

In the past i tried using streamer (might have been part of xawtv, i
think?), but the performance was generally pretty average. I don't know if
the BT848 cards have ever offered that good capture performance thou - i
think they were designed to do video overlay, and the capturing was more of
a hack or secondary feature, rather than the main focus.

With firewire and dvgrab you get 720x480 video at 25fps and a very high
bitrate, without any problems.. with bt848 and streamer i could never manage
full framerate at any except tiny res, and 352x288 (hi) res was unuseably
slow. This was on older hardware though, so discount my advice as neccessary
;)

firewire isn't lossy, whereas bt848 capture and playback is. But firewire
VCRs and camcorders cost more than standard ones.

Toby
