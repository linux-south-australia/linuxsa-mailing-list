Noel Cordell wrote:

> my feeling is that there is no way 40% of the internet servers in the US

WTC had the best backup power in New York, along with excellent air
conditioning that used the Hudson river instead of cooling towers. All
of the national carriers had capacity to there and Verizon had an
exchange in the building.

It would have technically been a good place to have a datacentre, but
was probably cost-prohibitive due to the high profile of the building
(which was rented to capacity). I havn't noticed any major web sites
down as a result of its destruction.
