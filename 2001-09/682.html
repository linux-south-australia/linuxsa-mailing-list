Hi.

I went through this saga a while ago. It's the second time RedHat has done
it, and it annoys the hell out of me. It's no good having rpm if it's
creator is going to break it without a clear method of upgrading an older
system. 

Anyway, if I remember correctly, I went to rpm.org and got hold of the
3.0.6 packages, you are correct when you say that the 3.0.5 packages are
missing from RH's 6.2 up2date packages. Why do they do that? It's like
they intentionally break the older release by not keeping the archive
current!

popt-1.5.1-6x.i386.rpm
rpm-3.0.6-6x.i386.rpm
rpm-build-3.0.6-6x.i386.rpm
rpm-devel-3.0.6-6x.i386.rpm

You do have to install them in one long rpm -Uvh command or they die with
locking dependencies. If you can't find them, I still have the originals
here, it's a couple of megs all up.

The system I was upgrading was RH6.1

Michael
