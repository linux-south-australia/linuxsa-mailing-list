Yes.

In addition I find that "qmail generally requires patching" is a fair
statement. Look at qmail.org for evidence. That doesn't mean qmail is bad,
but it does mean you make a commitment to learning qmail internals if you
want to have a really flexible toolkit of qmail solutions.

Unless someone is willing to make that commitment, I tend to suggest they
don't use qmail. It is a very powerful tool in the hands of someone who
knows the code and the patches (but probably no more powerful than postfix
or exim.)
