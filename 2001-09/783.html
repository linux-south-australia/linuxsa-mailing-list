Agreed. I think there is no excuse for not using linux/FreeBSD on the
backend. Samba can replace most of the point and click filesharing
capability that the end users are used to. There are enough point and
click/hope-for-the-best gui admin tools around these days, to shield M$
centric IT staff from the supposed difficulty of a text config file.
Although for the more adventurous will see that by going direct to the
config file it is often easier to see the big picture with finer grained
control. And all at the right price. However, I think that linux on the
desktop has a little way to go. Star Office is great, I use it with
success 90% of the time. But it would be that 10% where someone gets a
word document where the author has spend more time on presentation than on
the content, and Star Office screws the formatting completely, that the
helpdesk calls would become a nightmare. Most people can get around Office
ok (or at least they like to think so ;-), but there would certainly be an
ugly transition period from a training point of view.
