Is that a bad thing? Microsoft themselves admit that it's best to run one
service per server, to avoid stability problems. It may not be a bad thing
to let your Win2K boxen do the Windows specific stuff and let your unix
box do the rest.

One thing I forgot to mention in my previous e-mail:

 If you are planning on using Samba as a PDC for Win2K clients, it is best
to use Samba 2.2.1a (or soon-to-be, Samba 2.2.2). Win2K SP2 added a nice
little "feature" that caused a large compatibility problem (ie, it stopped
working completely). These late versions of Samba overcame this.
