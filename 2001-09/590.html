Re: OT: Best licence for a new Open Source package ...

On Fri, 21 Sep 2001 12:41, Richard Sharpe wrote:
> I am starting a new SourceForge project that I intend to provde code
> to be made available to a wide range of people and projects ...
>
> I am unclear on what licence to use. It seems to me that there are
> two licences I can use, the GPL or the BSD licence.

Thru your involvement with Samba have you discovered any reasons why 
the GPL is inappropriate?

> My intent is that anyone be able to use the project that I am
> starting, without subjecting their project (even if propietary) to
> the dangers of the GPL.

Dangers?  I suspect you mean you want others to be able to take the 
code and do whatever *they* want with it, rather than whatever *you* 
want them to do with it.  Perhaps you could elaborate some more on what 
your aims are, and what you perceive to be problems with the GNU GPL.

Certainly, if you aim to have the code freely available to all, 
including those who would not give their modifications in return, the 
BSD license would seem appropriate.
