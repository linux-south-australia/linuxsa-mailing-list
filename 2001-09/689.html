Re: Restricting outbound email addresses

On Tue, 25 Sep 2001, Freeman, Peter (ERHS) wrote:

> One of our clients has set up a general use email account.  
> What they want to do is restrict the users ability to send 
> to addresses other than those specified.
> 
> ie: they can only send to "fred@largepuddle.com" and "betty@largepuddle.com"
> but not "barney@largepuddle.com"
> 
> They are set up at the moment with a Linux 56k dial-up
> gateway which is running their internet connection w/ proxy etc.
> 
> Are there any OSS options for putting some sort of mail filtering at the 
> firewall level,

This is quite misleading. The answer to this statement would be 'no'.

> in other words, re-directing all mail through a filter
> on the firewall before it is sent on the outside mail server (largepuddle in
> this instance)

However, this can be done. I would suggest looking at the documentation
for your MTA (sendmail, exim, postfix, etc). In this case I would
recommend exim, because it has very powerful filtering capabilities, it's
easier to configure than sendmail and has some very powerful mail routing
capabilities. Have a look at http://www.exim.org. There is a very large
manual that comes with it also.

Hope it helps,
Matt
