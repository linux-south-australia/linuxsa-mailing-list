Re: Outlook alternative for Unix?

On Thu, 13 Sep 2001 13:52, Benjamin Close wrote:
> Hi All,
> 	Whilst most of us know the problems with Outlook, I've yet to find
> anything for unix which integrates everything
> (Mail/AddressBook/Scheduler,...) into one nice client.
> What are others experiances in solving this problem. In particular
> I'm interested in solutions which also provide out of office access
> from a remote machine without any clients installed on it.

Ximian Evolution <URL:http://www.ximian.com/products/ximian_evolution/>; 
aims to be an integrated "workgroup information management solution", 
which essentially is a mail client and a PIM with group calendar 
functions (I think).

For out-of-office access, perhaps a web-based system would be suitable. 
 This sort of collaboration software is often called "groupware", so 
perhaps search Freshmeat and other places for this term.

Also, KDE <URL:http://www.kde.org/announcements/announce-2.2.html>; is 
coming far, and you may be able to get this functionality by combining 
its various components such as KMail, KNode, KOrganizer and KAB.

I haven't been able to find anything that rivals Outlook yet, let me 
know if you do!
