Now that you have a working machine, the first thing I
would do is run (as root)

/sbin/ntsysv --level 2345

and turn off sendmail, netfs, portmap, ssh, isdn, ppoe
and a bunch of other things I cant remember (but dont
do them unless you are sure it is OK!)  that will make
the machine boot faster.

Some of them will disappear anyway if you later
uninstall the rpms for some of the network things.

sendmail is definitely one you can remove, as I think
I remember you saying there will be no internet on
these machines; and anyway even if there is, it is
easier to let people just use yahoo or something
through a web browser.

To list all RPMs installed in the PC: 

rpm -qa

To get rid of packages (e.g. sendmail)
* Assumes you wont be using the local mail client
(mutt) or fetchmail

rpm -e fetchmail
rpm -e mutt
rpm -e sendmail
rpm -e sendmail-cf

This will free up at least > 3MB of disk space... now
you just need to repeat for other things you dont
need.

Net clients especially, if you are not hooking this up
to anything - and for simple internet browsing for
lots of unknown people, you dont want too many tools
other than a web browser...
