Re: long delay when trying to telnet/ssh/ftp into Debian 2.2r 2

On Mon, Sep 03, 2001 at 11:12:18AM +0930, Schenk, Jarrad wrote:

 > Has anyone else come across this problem before. Is this a debian problem or
 > is it just me. 

Probably just you :-)  You either have a botched nameserver/resolver
configuration, or you're on address space with no reverse mappings (e.g.,
you're using 192.168.0.0/16 behind a NAT firewall), so attempts by your
system to reverse-map the incoming telnet/ssh connections so their
hostnames can be logged in utmp are timing out.

If you are running behind a NAT firewall, put a fake reverse-mapping 
zone for your address space onto your nameserver.  You'll probably also
find that the delay goes away if you delete /etc/resolv.conf completely,
but that'll make your system somewhat less useful on the Internet :-)
