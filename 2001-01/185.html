RE: Question Re Compilers

one could try a practical experiment one somethign large and complex (ie not
helloworld.c -- try the kernel) simply by setting it up in a script to
compile it ten times with exactly the same flags and then doing a diff on
each pair of kernel binaries to check if they are identical. I would assume
they would be identical. If they are not, then there are a few things to
check, which could best (IMHO) be checked through the use of od and grep (ie
od the binary contents to an ASCII representation, then check where this
differs). Most likely, one may find time stamps, versioning information, or
somehting like that. Gcc itself actually appears to have a flag that will
include timestamps and so on whether or not they are in the source code
somewhere ... look at -midentify-revision in the gcc manpage...

As for optimisations, most of those mentioned in the gcc manpage appear
(after a cursory inspection -- one who cared may like to look further) like
they would have no reason to be non-deterministic.
