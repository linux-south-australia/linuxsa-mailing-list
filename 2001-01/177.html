> At 06:17 PM 1/7/01 +1030, Michael Kratz wrote:
> >Hi all,
> >
> >i am trying to configure a DX4-100 with 24M of RAM as a webserver,
> >I am having a couple of problems however:
> >
> >1. it can't find the network card. all I know is that the network card is
a
> >10M isa card, it has a BNC and a RJ45 connector on it. how do I get Red
Hat
> >6.2 to find it??
>
> Well, you will have to know the type of card and the IO address it is
> located at and the IRQ possibly also, although it is possible to probe the
> card.
>
> Assume it is an NE2000, then try the following:
>
>   modprobe ne io=0x300
>
> if that fails, try
>
>   modprobe ne io=0x340
>
> and walk through a few of the usual addresses for NE2000 cards on the ISA
bus.
>
> Once you have found the values, add the following lines to
/etc/conf.modules:
>
>    alias eth0 ne
>    options ne io=<what you found above>
>
> eg
>
>    alias eth0 ne
>    options ne io=0x340
>
> >2. whenever I boot the system the date goes back to current day and
month,
> >1994, ie today it tells me is the 7/1/1994
> >I can manually set the date to 2001 and that works however as soon as I
> >reboot it goes back to '94 how can I correct this. I am not sure whether
the
> >bios is flash upgradeable!!

I had the same problem with an old m/b. One fix is to run Rdate to a time
server on the net ie. Rdate -s -p 138.194.21.154
Put it in a cron job and run it once a day or what ever you like.
worked of me.
