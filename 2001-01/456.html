I agree with what Jason said, but I think he might have misunderstood
what was meant by "hire for attitude."  Attitude is the attribute which
I prize most highly in my employees.  If somebody has not the technical
skills that I want, but knows that he or she can easily pick them up;
if he or she needs no motivating and is very willing to put in effort
to learn and understand a project requirement; these are the attitudes
I desire.  What I don't need is somebody who works to eat, not eats
to work.  A person who hasn't really got the skills, so please give the
project to someone else, also has the wrong attitude.
