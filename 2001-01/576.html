Re: UNIX &&|| Linux training

It always amazes me how many people generalise how they learn and expect the 
same rules apply to others.  Fortunately nature created diversity and as such 
different people learn better under diferent conditions.  Without getting too 
theoretical on learning styles it suffices to say that most people prefer 
being in an environment where they have access to software, hardware and peer 
groups that in turn allows them to form a cultural link with the target 
employment market and develop the language needed to operate in such 
employment places.

To be honest, those who claim structured training is crap, rubbish of no 
value etc etc maybe telling the truth as they see it, but it is certainly not 
the truth for lots of other people. As linux people you above all should know 
the value of choice, freedom and individual preferences.

There is no absolute right and wrong here, there is just choice and 
preferential learning style.
