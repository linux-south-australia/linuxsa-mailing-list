Re: Via soundcard problem

On Monday 15 January 2001 00:37, Fraser Farrell wrote:
> Just run into a problem with a fresh Mandrake 7.2 install on
> one of my workstations. The soundcard doesn't work :(
>
> Mandrake is correctly detecting it (an onboard VIA VT82C686)
> but audio CDs and other multimedia only produce silence. It
> did work okay when I had Win98 on this workstation.
>
> Online references are inconsistent. Some postings reckon I
> will need a 2.3.x kernel and tons of compiler tweaks, others
> claim Mandrake 7.x included builtin support.
>
> Anyone know of a solution for this problem? Apart from "go
> and buy a SoundBlaster..."?

I'll check my old config and send the modules.conf line if I find it. I did 
have the via686a working okay on this machine. It would play mp3s and that 
sort of thing. Didn't check it with CD's

mdk wouldn't detect it auto, I had to edit modules.conf myself.

The lines in modules conf were...

alias sound-slot-0 snd-card-via686a
post-install snd-card-via686a modprobe snd-pcm-oss      

You have to have the alsa drivers included od course - on the mdk CD   

BUT the card wouldn't work right with SVGA stuff - so I couldn't play Quake 
and Quake II with sound - tho sdl-quake worked fine with sound. 

I dumped it and put an old ISA SB16 in and now I have sound for EVERYTHING :-)
