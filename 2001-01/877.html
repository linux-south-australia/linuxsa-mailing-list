Re: AOL? or best ISP

On Fri, Jan 19, 2001 at 11:50:34AM +1030, Mike Lindner wrote:

 > > one of the common attributes of that mindset is that people who want
 > > something for nothing don't pay bills very well.
 > 
 > so you're saying that all of us that are on one of these plans are
 > freeloaders???  that's quite a generalisation! 

It certainly is.  It's not the kind of generalization I'd ever make, either
(hint:  Any sentence which starts with the phrase, "So you're saying..." is
probably wrong).

There's a HUGE difference between saying, "unprofitably cheap access
tends to attract people who don't want to pay any money," and "Everyone
who is using an unprofitably cheap access plan is a freeloader."  The
first phrase is talking about a tendency, a bias;  The second is talking
about an overgeneralization.

 > i can tell you never did psych
 > or even economics for that matter :)  for a  psych there are probably seven
 > (just a guess) or so different motivations here, the economist would say
 > that not wanting something for nothing is totally unnatural and untrue.

Right -- And that's why ISPs which don't run on unprofitably cheap access
plans have so many customers:  People actually *LIKE* customer service.
They *LIKE* being able to talk to a helpdesk person who knows how to tie
shoelaces.  They *LIKE* ISPs which never, ever, ever produce busy signals.
They *LIKE* being able to have their modem call answered by modern equipment
which won't drop-out.  They *LIKE* ISPs who don't channel thousands of
users through congested upstreams.

If you want those kinds of features, someone needs to pay for them.  For
the EISAs and OGNs and AusNets of the world, the people who actually paid
for them were the shareholders.  The result now?  The shareholders got
sick of it, and those ISPs are now dead.  Can you give me any hints about
how the customers got a good deal out of that arrangement?

 > are you a commie? (only joking)

Sigh.

 > my point was that multi-national telcos, that own satellites etc. have

Yes, you're right -- And, in case you haven't noticed, multi-national

How much will you pay to use UUNet?  BigPond?  AAPT (aka connect.com.au)?
Lots!  

Optus' DingoBlue is an aberration.  In fact, *OPTUS* is an aberration.
C&W want to bail out for all kinds of reasons.
