They will probably appear on next reboot if you do so...

Ports > 1024 controlled by different daemons so you need to find out which
one working there and remove it from your /etc/rc.d/init.d

As I wrote few days before:

# netstat -napt | grep LISTEN | grep ":1359 " | awk {' print $7 '}

will tell you process PID/name which listens on this port (for tcp ports
only).

Seems that you're security paranoid - believe me, most problems with
security arise when you have outdated packages because alot of people
informed about some security holes in old packages. Someone use this info to
patch his system and someone will try to break into another systems.

This VERY RARE case when someone will hit all of your ports expecting that
something will be wrong with your system and he gains (may be root) access
or simply make some DoS. Such kinds of attacks detected quickly by watching
logs and you simply block attackers IP (subnet, domain) on your router.
