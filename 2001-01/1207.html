Re: Experience with DAT or DLT vs Travan?

Hi.

I switched from Tanberg 1/4" SCSI 1.2Gb tape to Sony DAT a year or so ago.
The drives are used for database backup in linux boxes every working day,
and I have yet to have a failure. I did have one drive that went awol
until we rebooted the box (it was confused, wouldn't release the tape),
and I've had 1 tape cartridge die on me. I think I have about 7 drives in
circulation. 

Certainly price comes into the DLT vs DAT equation. Not only are the
drives a lot more expensive, but you can buy a bootload of DAT tapes for
the cost of a set of DLT tapes. I would expect the DLT drives to be
superior for the price.

Greg, how did you get DAT drives for $70 ? The best price I see around is
just under $1000 for a namebrand DDS2...

Michael

> I've found DDS hardware to be very short-lived.  I bought a job lot of
> 3 DDS-2 drives in America about a year ago (USD $70 each), and one is
> already dead (you can have it and play around if you want; maybe some

> DDS(-1) drives I had years ago lasted less than 6 months.  I also have

> 
> I also have a refurbished Quantum DLT-4000 (20 GB native).  It hasn't
> died either, but I haven't used it as much.

> I've heard, DLT has the best reputation for reliability.
