Hi Leigh,

Um, it's a control protocol for the network, transport and
session layers.  I would be learing of arguing that ICMP
is a transport layer.  Perhaps the best argument is
to observe that an ICMP packet can't generate another ICMP
packet.  Thus ICMP can't be used to provide any transport
function as it has no mechanism for reporting errors
when providing that transport function.
