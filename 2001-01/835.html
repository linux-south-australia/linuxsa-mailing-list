It depends who you mean when you say "other providers". Dingo blue has
always had an unsustainable pricing structure for Internet, especially for
their more remote areas. Fortunately for Optus who provided the network for
them, this massive loss paid off when they sold Dingo Blue to AGL.

Optus cannot indefinately give Dingo Blue customers a free ride now that
they don't own it anymore, and sooner or later, AGL shareholders are going
to notice that the Internet business is losing money for no good reason.
They will either have to raise prices, reduce service levels or even
introduce download limits .. the horror. I suppose it's also possible that
AGL paid millions so they could discover the secret of remaining
cashflow-postitive whilst delivering 5Gb for $27 each month..

Internode, on the other hand, actually makes a profit and employs people in
Adelaide without using anyone else's money.

If you don't care where the service comes from, and price is the only
important thing you might want to ask someone who pre-paid for 12 months on
EISA about how clever it is to use an obviously-losing-money ISP.
