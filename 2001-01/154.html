As I said, fsck is called by rc.sysinit.  In particular the line

  fsck -R -A -V -a 

is called on all other filesystems.  The -R avoids / (since it is usally
booted RW by that stage (so that logs and such can be written)).  -A
walks through /etc/fstab to find out what are important filesystems that
will be mounted.  -V says verbose output.  and -a repairs as much as
possible automagically.

With the -A fsck *does* apply its parallelized algorithm (as described in
the man pages).

> > So all in all your situation can't be improved much using existing

Yeah.  Some investigation might be needed.  LVM might also cut down on the

disks).

> Oh but there are some who INSIST on making HUGE / partitions....

more or less proportional manner.
