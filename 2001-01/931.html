 > 
 > This also depends on advertising and communication with the customer.
 > If an ISP advertises "All you can get for $25" and uses that to lure
 > people in knowing that they either do not read or may not understand the
 > fine print then they should provide what they advertise.

As Optus@Home found out recently, if you advertise a service as "$x per 
month with no limits", the ACCC will use the full force of law to make 
sure you provide a service at $x per month with no limits, even if that's
not quite what you had in mind when you wrote the ad.

ISP advertisements using the word "unlimited" are a hot-button issue for
the ACCC.  ISPs who use that word without really meaning it, and without
being clear about exactly what they *do* mean, are tempting fate in a 
very major way.
