my understanding is that ICMP is on the same level as TCP and UDP. Ports are
defined _within_ TCP and UDP (separately, which is why tcp port 80 and udp
port 80 are completely different...). In fact, there is a facility within
ICMP that according to the RFC can be used _like_ ports in TCP or UDP:

http://www.landfield.com/rfcs/rfc792.html

      The identifier and sequence number may be used by the echo sender
      to aid in matching the replies with the echo requests.  For
      example, the identifier might be used like a port in TCP or UDP to
      identify a session, and the sequence number might be incremented
      on each echo request sent.  The echoer returns these same values
      in the echo reply.

Basically, TCP, UDP and ICMP are protocols that sit under IP. FTP, HTTP, and
so on, sit under either UDP or TCP.

No doubt someone will correct me if I am wrong :)
