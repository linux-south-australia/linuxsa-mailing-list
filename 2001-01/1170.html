Re: colocation swap

Leigh Hart wrote:
> 

> Lastly, there is absolutely NO (general) need for a permanently
> connected host (ISP or otherwise) to have a secondary MX - mail
> servers are smart enough to store and retry at the source these
> days, why make someone elses day go bad when your mail server is
> down?  :-)

Because if you have the secondary MX, then you control the
amount of disk that waiting messages can queue in.  This
then allows statements to be made with certainty about the
robustness of the mail system.  Without a secondary MX, that
robustness is under the contol of the sender (and their small
spool disk).
