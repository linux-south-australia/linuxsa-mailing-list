[Much deleted]

>I think Sun mis-moulded a lot of people's views of the
>applications that are suitable for UDP when they foolishly
>tried to run NFS over it.

Ummmm, this is unfair. NFS was designed as a LAN File sharing protocol with
Ethernet as the target. In such an environment, packet loss is extremely
low, and you can justifiably use higher layer timeouts to handle the few
problems. By doing that, they considerably simplified the implementation.

It was not until people started trying to use NFS over the Internet that
the problems with using NFS over UDP became obvious, and support for NFS
over TCP was needed.

>My point to David was that you can't use ICMP as a transport

>becomes unreachable.

Hmmm, I see lots of ICMP Dest Unreachables in response to ICMP Echos (which
might be carrying IP over ICMP ... :-)
