>

>
>> All up, I have 6 IDE channels, although only three have drives on them, so
>> Linux has only reported 3.
>
> Isn't it true that with some combinations of drives, IDE controllers and
> such having a master/slave pair on a single IDE channel can slow
> performance?

Most IDE controllers can only handle a single request.  That means
that you effectively single-thread transfers.  When you consider that
a typical transfer takes 8 ms to position the head over the data, and
then about 500 µs to transfer the data, you can imagine what an impact
that is: it effectively halves the throughput.

Nowadays, you can get round this problem if you have:

1.  A controller that supports tagged queueing.
2.  A disk that supports tagged queueing.
3.  A driver that supports tagged queueing.

I have (1) and (3) (BTW, the same hardware that Richard has), but not
(2).  The only disks supported by the FreeBSD ad driver are from IBM.
