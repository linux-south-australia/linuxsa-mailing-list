Hmm.  I'm not sure you've addressed enough of the issues.  I try to at
http://www.vinumvm.org/vinum/intro.html, but it's a complicated
business.  To compress it into a couple of lines, though, RAID tends
to issue multiple requests in parallel.  Put one ATA (IDE) drive on
each controller, and it works fine.  Put two ATA drives on each
controller, and it works at half the speed.  And yes, I have a
prototype NAS box here, built by a company which thinks it knows what
it's doing, and they've chosen a motherboard with 2 ATA66 controllers
over one with 4 ATA33 controllers.  No question which is faster for
RAID.
