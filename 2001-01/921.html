Re: AOL? or best ISP

Andrew Gosling wrote:
> 
> The bottom line is that if you charge $24.95 unlimited, you will lose money,
> or you will provide an unacceptably bad service or more likely both.

The opposite side of this coin is that a fixed, known, budgetable
and controllable price for their basic Internet service is exactly
what customers want.

Analysts suggest that this desire is the major reason for the
success of the fixed-price, limited call, rechargable mobile phone.

The problem with most "fixed price plus charge per GB over a
reasonable limit" is that the "reasonable limit" hasn't
been reasonable at all and ISPs weren't notifying people
when they crossed the charging point (despite this being
simple to do with an automated e-mail message).

Similarly the "fixed price plus reasonable Acceptable Use
Policy" plans is that the "reasonable AUP" often doesn't
allow the download of one CD, let alone RedHat7 + Powertools,
or the running of a moderately popular hobbyist-style
web site.

The other problem is simply the outrageous cost of bandwidth
in this country.  If it cost less, then people wouldn't
be living in fear of having a denial of service attack
eat up real money.
