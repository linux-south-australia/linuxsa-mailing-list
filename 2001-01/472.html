Surely Glen was bring up an academic point.

UDP is by definition an Unreliable data protocol (or something)

But what Glen is saying (if I may be so bold) is to disagree with the
comment that "[it] cannot transport anything with certainty[1]" and say
that you can "transport data *where data loss is acceptable* with
certainty[2]", and even employ a checking process (feedback) to make
sure that some parts actaully do make it (key video frames maybe?)

I think from a statistics POV, there is a close to zero chance that no
data will get through, therefore making [2] believable.

The difference between [1] and [2] being the quality of the data being
transmitted, not the quality of the transmission itself.
