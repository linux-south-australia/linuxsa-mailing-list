RE: OK Sysadmins

Quote from the office wall:

Always try to look at a problem as if for the first time.
Forget the ways it has been handled in the past.
Take nothing for granted - except the assumption that a solution can be
found.

Other things not already mentioned AFAIK:
- have an unbiased view on solutions (ie. not 100% MS, 100% Unix just for
the sake of it), find the best/most cost-effective solution, period.
- learn from your peers (and those at a higher level than your peers, gurus
i think they're called ;-)
- think 'outside the box'
- research new stuff when you have the time, you never know when you might
find something useful (i 'stumbled' across Linux doing this about 18 months
ago.......)
- if we're talking technical knowledge, you must know how to hit the reset
button on an NT server & boot from a Linux cd

<ignore> borderline contradiction between last statement & first statement
</ignore>

as already mentioned:
- be able to RTFM (unless its MS software, in which case TFM wont help you
at all!)
- have sense of humour (I'd almost rate this near the top of the list)
