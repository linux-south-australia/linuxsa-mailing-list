>> my understanding is that ICMP is on the same level as TCP and UDP.
> 
> As far as I was aware, ICMP was considered network layer (layer 3) and
> UDP and TCP are transport layer (layer 4)?

IP doesn't fit the ISO 7 layer model.  ICMP is certainly at the same level
as TCP and UDP (it's a protocol layered on IP), however it's use is for
managing IP networks, and not for transporting (user) data, so in that
sense it's lower than TCP and UDP.
