I agree with most of this.  The current attitude to computer education
in SA seems to be to make good users of computers rather than people
who understand computers.  My daughter is 15, about to start year 11,
and based on the curriculum we decided that there was no point in
doing *any* of the computer subjects, since they were so clearly
geared towards "how to use Microsoft".  There's more than just
Microsoft that's wrong in this picture: "how to use Linux" would be
only marginally better.  The thrust should be "understanding (oh, and
using) computers".

> AUUG exists but - let's face it and no offence to AUUG - LinuxSA
> does seem to be the strongest, most vibrant "unix like" users group
> in SA.  There is no friction between AUUG nationally and LinuxSA
> locally.

Of course, being one of the local AUUG people, I'm going to have to
take issue on that one :-) And yes, there's no friction.  Still, it's
obvious that LinuxSA is there to promote Linux.  In that respect, it's
similar to Microsoft.  AUUG is a little broader in approach, but even
then, we still have UNIX in our name.  Still, we can point to our
support for open systems in general.

One of the things I've been considering is that AUUG join the IT
Council for South Australia (http://www.itcouncil.asn.au/).  I suppose
it's time to follow up on that one.  I've spoken to Denis Wall about
that, and he thinks it would be a good idea.  There's no reason why
LinuxSA shouldn't join too.
