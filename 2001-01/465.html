Re: ping

David Newall wrote:
> > As I pointed out earlier, this argument doesn't fly --
> > because there can be no ICMP error in reply to an ICMP
> > packet, then ICMP can't be used to transport anything
> > with certainty.
> 
> Neither can UDP be used to transport anything with certainty.

Yes it can -- you can transport data where congestion and
corruption loss is acceptable to the application.  For
example, realtime voice, video and cricket scores (there's
a multicast channel for this).

Even in UDP you still get errors if there is no application
listening on the socket, or if your traffic is administratively
denied, etc.

So you can transmit knowing that data will reach the other end.
What you don't know is the quality of the received data.

The standard real-time protocols built on top of UDP have 
provision for a feedback loop so that the sending application
can determine the quality of the received data and downsample
or use forward error correction as appropiate.
