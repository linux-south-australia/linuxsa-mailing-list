/dev/hda1	2GB	Solaris 7		(active)
/dev/hda2	1GB	Windows 2000
/dev/hda3	1GB	FreeBSD 4.2 RC1
/dev/hda4	[extended]
/dev/hda5	128MB	Linux swap
/dev/hda6	6GB	Linux Native

/dev/hdb1	1GB	OpenBSD
/dev/hdb2	1GB	NetBSD

Installed in that order. If you are having problems with installing
W2K/NT on your second partition it is almost certainly because Windows
will believe everything the BIOS tells it about the hard disks. On some
BIOSes, I have seen the 'Invalid Partition table' error when it can't
find an active partition. This could be because your BIOS can't 'see'
the Linux partition. If you put LILO in the MBR and make the W2K
partition active, you may have some success
