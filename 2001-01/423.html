Re: Sendmail Question

> Using the m4 macros, how do I convince my Sendmail to greet as
> mail.rebel.net.au instead of my local hostname?

I think the answer is define(`confDOMAIN_NAME', ``mail.rebel.net.au'')
in your .mc file.  Many people don't maintain the .mc file, they just
edit the .cf file instead.  If you want to do that then replacing the
Dj line with Djmail.rebel.net.au will do the same thing.  I don't think
you really want to do this, though.  I suspect that any site that looks
up your hostname will notice that you aren't really mail.rebel.net.au.
You could leave Dj undefined, and redefine your hostname when you bring
up your ppp link.  Do a reverse DNS lookup of your dynamically assigned
IP address, pump that into hostname and then restart sendmail (kill -1
will probably do the trick.)  When you bring down your ppp link put back
your old hostname and restart sendmail.  Perhaps a better solution might
be to configure your sendmail to always pass outgoing mail to our mail
relay (mail.rebel.net.au), which is nicely configured to permit dialed
in hosts to use any name they want.
