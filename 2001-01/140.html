Re: From RISKS digest 21.18

Mark Newton wrote:
> 
> Don't confuse "Flash Card" with "CompactFlash Card", though.
> CompactFlash media is different in that it actually speaks ATA
> protocols;  The CPRM stuff has nothing to do with MemoryStick,
> SmartMedia, or any of the other non-CompactFlash flash media
> technologies.

I wasn't confusing the technologies.

I was using Sony's MemoryStick plans to demonstrate that
flash memory is becoming generic and pervasive data storage,
and thus plans like CPRM will have a noticable effect.

As far as I can tell, Intel are competing against Sony to
establish the defacto standard for this generic data storage
and the CRPM proposal makes Intel's flash interface more
attractive than the Sony flash interface to some designers
of consumer product.  That in turn might help Intel win
the VHS/Beta-type contest.  Or, if the public reaction to
CPRM is strong enough, to lose the contest.
