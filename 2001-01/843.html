Sure John, I should have told Dingoblue I did not need their poor business
model, I did not need their low cost or unlimited time or downloads.  Please
get a grip on reality, your points are fine, but cost, if you read my previous
mail, was NOT the primary reason for picking Dingoblue.  I stated that I would
be happy to pay more for such service. Don't you understand... I dont think
about what I D/L or how long I stay on for, that in itself is worth the money.
And while I'm on it, Internode's business strategy and others who still limit
hours or apply D/L charges are the ones who should be reviewing their business
models.  The Internet is and will be permanent part of our work and play
habits.  Limits of any kind are a model for disaster.

Simon H. once said to me, the business models for ISP's must be value adding
and include complementary services. I hope he hasn't changed his mind on this
as he was quite correct.  The ISP business model of just dial-up services is no
longer valid.

Steve
