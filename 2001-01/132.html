Re: From RISKS digest 21.18

Mark Newton wrote:
> 
> Can you think of any useful application of CompactFlash technology
> which would be hampered by CPRM?

Sony, with the positioning of their MemoryStick product, are
betting that flash will take the role the MS-DOS formatted
1.44MB floppy disk used to have as a sort of universal data
transfer media.

[ Of course, IRDA and Bluetooth and USB and FireWire all have
  that hope too :-) ]

With that sort of consideration, you could realistically
expect flash cards in all programmable platforms, doing
much the same role as the PC Card flash in Cisco routers
now but in devices ranging from microwave ovens to PC BIOSs.
If that's the case, then if a manufacturer fails you won't
be able to upgrade the software from a third party.
