> > Obviously the easy way to fix some of the hassle would be a second hard
> > drive to house the cache, mainly to cut down the disk activity, having
the
> > cache & the swapfile on the same drive isnt the best scenario.
>
> Yes. I have suggested having a dedicated disk for the squid cache could
> be beneficial.

To be of any benifit, the cache and swapfile partitions would have to be on
separate channels.  Not just separate drives.  If the cache and swapfile
partitions are on drives that share a channel (ie. primary and slave
drives), then having them both on the same drive is better than having them
on separate drives.  Ideally, you'd want to have each drive on a different
IDE channel.

See if you can get another small drive and drop it in there on the secondary
channel, and make it a dedicated cache drive; it should give you the best
performance if squid is going to thrash your swapfile.
