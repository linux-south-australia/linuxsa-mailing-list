>Dear Richard,
>
>To respond to your criticism (below), I would like to explain why we 
>haven't yet launched xDSL formally in Adelaide.
>
>Since people on the linuxsa list tend to enjoy details, I'll supply them.
>
>The core reason is that we are absolutely insisting that we _do_ get 
>it right before we open the doors. Yes, we've had to revise our 
>intended launch date by four weeks as a result.
>
>The reasons why its taking some time are manyfold, and I'm going to 
>lay them out here. They include resolution of our internal 
>provisioning procedures - and some technical issues - to ensure that 
>when we open the door, we can, and will, service our customers 
>professionally and promptly.
>
>The service works fine technically, at a low level. At a high level, 
>we're working to resolve a few issues which Telstra has imposed upon 
>us to limitations inherent in the technical choices that they have 
>made in providing us with infrastructure access to deliver the 
>service.
>
>Those technical limitations include some which make it impossible for 
>Telstra, at present, to offer ADSL as a business focussed and/or high 
>quality home service, including providing static IP addressing and 
>other features that we know our professional customers will expect. 
>We've nailed effective ways to provide those things despite the 
>limitations in the underlying service because we know our customers 
>want them.
>
>To resolve those issues, we have been developing our product around 
>the use of Cisco DSL customer premises equipment, instead of the 
>cheap and cheerful Alcatel boxes that Telstra pitches to their own 
>customers at present - and which are part of the problem.
>
>Unfortunately, yet again, Cisco haven't yet done what we need either 
>- there is a specific IOS feature change we need in order to deliver 
>the flexible and effective service we want to deliver, making it both 
>easier to use and more powerful than the options pitched to the 
>market by Telstra.
>
>I have been in weekly phone and email discussion with Cisco engineers 
>both in Sydney and in San Jose working on getting this issue 
>resolved, and the delays in doing so are the other major barrier to 
>our being comfortable in launching the service right now. I expect 
>that to be resolved Real Soon Now, as I've (just) found a workaround 
>that will get things moving in the meantime.
>
>To make it worse, to date, mysterious delays within Telstra mean that 
>Cisco have been unable to have their equipment certified to connect 
>to Telstra phone lines, despite _significant_ effort on their part to 
>push Telstra along to get this done. This is one of those 'any day 
>now' issues, that was anticipated to be resolved in two weeks, about 
>two months ago.  It was supposed to be 'definitely' resolved by today 
>- and it isn't. Mysteriously, Cisco just don't seem to be making it 
>to the top of the testing list. Strange, isn't it. The issue isn't 
>technical, I am 100.0% sure of that.
>
>In summary: While I am sure you didn't mean to imply incompetence on 
>our part in your comments, below, by saying we 'can't get it right', 
>I wanted to underscore for your benefit and that of others that it 
>is, absolutely, our drive to deliver a competent, supportable, and 
>effective service.
>
>To do that, we have insisted that this service is an example of a new 
>drive inside our organisation to make sure that when you ask us to 
>pick up a ball, we don't drop it again.
>
>In other words, my own quality focus is the issue, because I know 
>that if I don't insist on our organisation delivering a quality 
>solution in this realm, we might as well not bother at all. We have a 
>great technical reputation, and I'm insisting that ADSL is something 
>that underscores, rather than damages, this reputation!
>
>We are hugely, hugely aware of how important it is to get this out of 
>the door rapidly, and we're working on it as hard as we can, 
>commensurate with the roadblocks I've noted above (compounded with 
>the major one - that Telstra held back the access we needed to the 
>network itself until the day of the launch of their retail service, 
>which meant we've had to start from behind and run like the devil - 
>and that's exactly what we're doing)
>
>So to paraphrase your comment - we insist on not (knowingly) getting it
wrong!
>
>I'm sure you'd be less than impressed if we promised you a service 
>and it didn't work properly on arrival, and you'd be telling the 
>linuxsa list all about _that_ as well.
>
>Be assured that I'm less than happy about the delay, too - and 
>acutely aware that we're letting people down in having to wait a bit 
>longer before we can open the doors.
>
>I ask for your patience, and for your continued (and appreciated) 
>support of a local company doing great things in the face of the 
>mediocrity of the Telstra offerings in general, and in this space in 
>particular. I believe you'll be impressed with the results.
>
>The actual low level service stability during our test deployments 
>has been excellent - I'm sending this via an Agile ADSL service now, 
>in fact.  So we expect the outcome to be very good indeed, Real Soon 
>Now.
>
>I am asking you to be patient, and I know that isn't a happy thing to 
>ask.  But I'm asking it anyway. Please wait.
>
>Cheers,
>Simon Hackett
>Internode / Agile
