Re: [OT]on the net, on the road?

At 11:24 PM +1030 20/1/01, Michael Fewings wrote:
>I've had a brief look at using GRIC through Internode but at $6.60/hour
>before phone costs I was hoping some of you jet-setters may have a
>better solution. The astronomical number of ISPs in Europe makes it
>difficult to look into them all - I'd still be going through the A's by
>the time I left!

This is a simple trade-off. The $6.60 per hour is composed of $0.60 
per John Howard, and the rest is a very close match to our true costs 
(which are charged to us in US$, so we also wear exchange rate 
variation just to make it more exciting).

For most of our customers that use this service (which tend to be 
roaming business people), $6.60 per hour is a trivial expense to 
avoid the hassle of finding and joining an ISP in another country, 
while still in Oz, and then cancelling the membership two weeks after 
you leave that country). It provides a true 'casual' access to 
thousands of ISP's around the world.

If you think that works out cheaper than $6.60 per hour for your 
intended usage pattern, I'd suspect that you aren't valuing your own 
time sufficiently highly :)
