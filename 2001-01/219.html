Re: port scanner

Andrew!

> does linux log a port scan from an app such as nmap. assuming that the scan
> was not stealth?

The symptoms of a port scan tend to show up if you log things using your
firewall (netfilter, ipchains). There are also a number of loggers
available at http://freshmeat.net/ which claim to be able to detect port
scans. Because ipchains doesn't log packets by default (you have to add
an -l) - and I suspect netfilter will probably be the same but the flag
to get it logging may differ - you may miss a port scan.

> if so, where is it logged?

If you're using netfilter/ipchains, they tend to log to syslog at level
info. That is to say, look in /var/log/messages. A port scan should show
up as an inordinate amount of logs, or weird things being logged. Mine
should log all scans to ${MYIP} 2049 which is where NFS servers normally
listen. If I see that go off I assume that I'm being scanned from
somewhere...

But scans are script kiddy stuff so I don't normally fuss too much
unless they get more often and often...
