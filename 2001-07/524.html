All that does is create another entry point on the graph of options. 
There's ORHH, Telstra ADSL, other ADSL offerings, and each of them

>128k is probably fast enough, 64k I'm not so sure about.
>But... if I want ORHH at 128k that means I need another PSTN
>line for voice calls which adds roughly another $20pm to the
>cost of the ORHH option - making adsl even more attractive.
>(since my original comparison assumed 64k ORHH)

Actually it doesn't have to mean that at all.

First, you can configure your router to run 64k with a dynamic 
decision (based on sustained traffic load) to punch up the second 
channel.

Second, a good ISDN router with voice ports can let you run a phone 
and a fax and your net link on the pair of ISDN channels, with 
dynamic setup and teardown of channels as required.

e.g.:

- you are on the net at 128k and a call comes in. The router drops 
one ISDN channel to let the voice call in, and you are at 64k to the 
Internet until you hang up the phone, when your router either 
immediately or when you pull more than 64k worth of traffic (your 
choice) punches the second channel back up to your ISP.

- you pick up the phone and dial an outgoing call while on the net at 
128k . Again, the router drops one 64K channel out to let you place 
the call, and you hang up and the same scenario happens as noted 
above.

Non theoretical, there are boggins of ISDN routers with analog voice 
ports in them for -exactly this reason-.

So you can get what you want without paying an extra $20pm, at the 
expense of a few more local calls to re-establish the second ISDN 
channel.
