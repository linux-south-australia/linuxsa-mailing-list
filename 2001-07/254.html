That sounds like fun!  I should join. :-)

Of course it's quite obvious that GPL is the dominent licence, having
displaced all of the incumbent free licences.  BSD did a great job last
century, but in modern times the GPL is the only one that we should
support.  MIT almost stuffed everything up with the licence for X,
but fortunately FSF and GPL and galloped in like the cavalry, showed us
how to do it and why, and saved the day.  If we don't rally behind GPL,
if we don't expose the many glaring deficiencies in the other licences,
such as the fact that they don't *truly* support our continued freedom
of expression, use and improvement, we'll be selling our own future short.
That's why we're LinuxSA not some freakin' also-ran FreeBsdSA, and that's
why GPL is going to surplant BSD, SCL, MPL and all of the others.
