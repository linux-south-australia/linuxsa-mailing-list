Re: Routing overheads using linux

Evan,

(Going OT a bit here...) Linux routers are potentially very good, You can pick 
up a book from good book shops called "Linux Routers" by Tony Mancill (Havent 
got the isbn no. here I can get it if you like...). With something like a 
celeron 500, a traverse Netjet ISDN card and good access equipment at the ISP, 
you can go as low as 20Ms pings to thier equipment, some thing like a cisco 
2500 (once again, more than likely overkill) will have similar performance.

Even still I use a 133 with my netjet/isdn and get low 30ms pings to machines 
on the other end. The 500 celery/2500 cisco may be overkill if your not into 
gaming, then a 133 may well suit. Keep in mind, that the 133 can also act as 
the DNS/Proxy/Mail/Web/Ftp/This/That and the other server, whereas a cisco 
cannot.

Also to keep in mind, for ISDN a low end cisco (800 through 1000 series)can be 
had for 4 to 600 dollars on sold.com.au or ebay.com.au, but may need a software 
upgrade to IOS 12.1 if you are looking for DOV isdn.

have fun!
