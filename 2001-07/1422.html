Hi, it should be easy to set up, but there are other issues that nobody
has mentioned. The cards use the public 2.4gz range, so its shared
bandwidth. There are also power/output limitations, and you wouldnt want
to breach these and then knock out someone else's network and get busted
by the ACA. I was checking this out on the wireless list, and got some
answers. Note that I was talking power amps, and havnt done the maths
for low power with large DB, but heres the info:

Ant:
>> I have just been talking to a HAM which I know about wireless 
>> and using
>> power amps to extend range. He seems to think that the use of 
>> poweramps
>> (like the 250mw and 400mw that some company who recently advertised on
>> this list have) are illegal to use in Australia. 

Tony Langdon:
> If the power amps are designed for wireless service and are type approved,
> they are legal... BUT, only as long as the total effected radiated power
> (EIRP) is less than 4 watts.  So, if you have a 24dBi dish fed from one of
> these amps, you're well into illegal territory, but if instead, you're
> feeding a 10dBi omnidirectional, a 400mW amp would put you at maximum legal
> power.

Then he explained how to calculate it:

> EIRP (dBW) = Tx power (dBW) + antenna gain (dBi) - coax losses (dB)
> Simple as that


> So, if you have a 1 watt transmitter into a 20dBi gain antenna and 3dB of
> antenna loss, then the EIRP is...

> 0 + 20 - 3, = 17 dBW, or 50W.
> FYP, 4W = +6 dBW, to save having to convert back to linear values.

> Also, if antenna gain happens to be expressed in dBd, add 2.14 to get dBi
> (doubt you'll see this, but it can happen).
