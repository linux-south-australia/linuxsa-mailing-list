Re: Cisco vs Linux

On 08 Jul 2001 23:59:34 +0930, Richard Russell wrote:

> When connecting a LAN to the internet, is there really any advantage
> in
> using a dedicated Cisco (or other brand) router between your firewall host
> and the internal network? My way of thinking tells me that I would really
> prefer IP straight to a server that I can get to do all sorts of things
> (webserving, etc), with an ISDN or ADSL "modem" connecting this pretty much
> directly to the internet... I guess there is an advantage of having two
> layers of protection, but does this really help?

That depends on what you're trying to do, how much bandwidth you've got,
how many upstream connections you've got etc.

Having the ISDN connection plugged in to the back of a Linux box has the
advantage of gaining an address to play with, seeing as a Cisco ISDN
router (If you can call them routers :-) swallows an IP address for the
PPP connection.

Cisco equipment does more than just terminate the link though. They do
sell Firewalling equipment and other neat boxes that perform other
tasks.

For most SMEs, I'd say it'd just be easier (and cheaper) to drop a Linux
box in, instead of a Cisco. I'd not use the Cisco unless I had a reason
to. In the case of most SMEs, I can't see one.

Simon provided a good example of where a small Cisco can be used (see
the thread "[OT] ISDN vs ADSL and Cisco vs Linux".
