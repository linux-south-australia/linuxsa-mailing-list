> Hi list,
>
> I am the volunteer IT Support for the Salisbury North Neighbourhood
> Network. We are a community group working under the auspices of the
> City of Salisbury. We run a unique program to refurbish computers,
> train volunteers in their use, provide them with a system / MS Office /
> Internet / Printer etc and then allow registered members of the public
> to access the systems in the volunteer's home or in a couple of public
> places such as a Footy Club or Neighbourhood House.
>
> The aim of the Project is to break down the serious digital divide in
> our community. Latest survey shows about 7% of the 12.5K residents in
> our suburb have access to the Internet either through work or at home.
>
> OK, enough background. Up until now we have been working with older
> systems and have had enough copies of the MS 95 OS to install and run
> with. That source of licensed OS's is drying up faster than we can fit
> them to computers so we are looking to use Linux as the OS as well as
> Star Office as the application package. We hope to be able to sell off
> some of the lower spec donated computers to fund the rest of the
> Project.
>
> Our average system is a P166 with usually 64 or 32 M RAM, 1.2 G drive
> and floppy/CD/keyboard/mouse etc. Some of the systems are P133's, a few
> are P100's
>
> So far I have tried Red Hat 7.1 and Mandrake 8.0 with limited success.
> Shortly (once it dl's) I will be trying the Peanut distro to see if
> their claimed 148M space is enough to work the rest in. Fitting the OS
> onto the small space as well as leaving enough room for Star Office is
> the major problem.
>
> The other biggie is that the users are in many cases going to be
> computer illiterate and will need to get basic computer training as
> well as training in Linux and Star Office. We can do that but the
> systems will still need to be stable and dead simple in operation.
>
> Any constructive advice is welcomed and appreciated.
>
> If there are any experienced Linux users in the vicinity of Salisbury
> North reading this your physical help to get this project under way
> would be much appreciated. I can offer free coffee and loads of 'fun'
> with old computers <G>
>
> I am also on the lookout for 2nd hand 72pin EDO RAM to fit into these
> machines so they can all have a minimal level of 64M RAM.
>
>  Ian Loxton,
>  on behalf of the
>  Salisbury North Neighbourhood Network
