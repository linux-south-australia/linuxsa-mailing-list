As much as it pains me to say it I get more GPFs using
Gnome or KDE or Mozilla than Windows :-( The upside at
least is they dont take the whole system with them...

Even the latest RedHat 7.1... it detects your USB
mouse but you still need to manually edit gpm.conf to
get it working properly after a reboot; the fonts suck
unless you are capable of deciphering info from
multiple sources on getting TTF to work properly; my
digital camera doesnt work properly with it because no
one has written a driver yet...; I cant fax from Linux
using my MFC; and I still get word documents that
_crash_ KWord and just wont load on SO5.2. I cant
print colour graphics to my BJC4000.

This doesnt mean the above are not cabable of being
fixed in Linux. I just dont have time to bother fixing
these things at the moment, it is easier to use
Win4Lin or reboot.

I can cope with these kinds of problems, but its a bit
much to expect a newbie or a person whose life doesnt
revolve around a PC to do so.

Hence the need to try and set up a Linux system, which
will provide robustness and zero-licensing problems,
that is also capable of providing an extremely user
friendly environment for learning and actual
usefulness. 

At least we can run SO on both Windows and Linux
platforms, providing some commonality across all of
