Re: http auth from pam db

You can, but its a Fairly Bad Idea(tm) unless you implement the login
procedure in a very strict, limited, way.

Remember all those statistics about how many web pages per second
Apache/Linux can serve? Remember the stats about how many possible tries it
takes to brute force common passwords? Now, do the math ;)

There is a reason why scp/telnet/su/etc have delays in them, to prevent
brute force attacks.


For a second reason, remember you're probably sending these logins and
passwords over an insecure network, possibly allowing someone to snoop and
snaffle them. Also, there are various ways you could fool someone into
entering the data into a trojan page.

Although these are true regardless of your password source, the exploit is
made worse when you could follow up by impa/sshing into the box using what
you obtained. Generally its a bad idea to teach your users to use a single
password for all accounts
