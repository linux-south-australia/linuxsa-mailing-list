Re: [OT] Formal Education [WAS: Connection Speeds question]

This is an interesting discussion, not!

Having recently sat the LPI102 exam (with LPI101 done over a year ago), 
and then having been involved on the team that constructed the next 
level of exams, it is interesting to think about the exams from the 
point of view of what they are intended to achiev, and how to short 
curcuit them.

I found that there were questions in the LPI102 exam that were possible 
to finesse. As long as one knew enough about the general area, one could 
often get the right answer without knowing the exact answer.

Of course, what one is trying to do is assess the candidate's knowledge 
in an area, and if one only has 60 or so questions to assess it in, a 
lot of care has to be taken. It would be prefferable to do a hands-on 
exam, but this is just not possible in many cases.

In setting questions, I found myself preffering those where two pieces 
of information had to be given. Then I could provide the following sort 
of questions:

   Which of the following frobs are required to fubar a froboz?

    a) Richard Stallman could do it very quickly

    b) X and Y  (correct answer)

    c) Y and Z

    d) X and W

    d) Richard Stallman doesn't know what he is talking about

Clearly, you have to know what you are on about, and be able to ignore 
Richard Stallman, to get the right answer, If you only know half the 
answer, you only have a one in three change of getting it right.

As to these bozos with certification, an MCP is not all that good. An 
MCSE is better. But one does hear of 2 year olds who have passed their 
MCSE's.

All I can say is that prospecive employers should insist on the shaving 
test. If they don't cut themselves shaving, they can probably be trusted 
near a computer :-)
