Re: internal modem

adrian.grantham@dsto.defence.gov.au wrote:

> i am after recomendations for what brand / model of an INTERNAL modem to
> get for a red hat box.  main points is that it has to be internal and
> obviously that linux recognises it.  


One of the best known modem chipsets that work under Linux is the Lucent 
"LTWin" chipset.  EverythingLinux 
<URL:http://www.everythinglinux.com.au/>; stocks a few modems based on 
this chipset including:

* Dynalink 56K PCI internal modem
* NetComm Inmodem56

These sites on internal "winmodems":
<URL:http://linmodems.technion.ac.il/>;
<URL:http://www.linmodems.org/>;
suggest that modems built on the Motorola SM56 and the Rockwell HSF
(NOT HCF) chipsets should also work.

Having said this, I would still stongly recommend an external serial 
modem: after changing from an controllerless internal PCI modem 
(winmodem) to an external modem, I experience far fewer dropouts to my ISP.
