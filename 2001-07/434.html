Re: configer red hat

I find taking the PC out of the box, and putting it on a desk, or table of
some form, is a good start.

I know it seemed like a good idea at a time, but I think you should remove
the dictionary from underneath the monitor, and find something else to raise
it up.

Note that although Linux has an increasing number of GUI tools, you will
stil find many cases where you will be required to type on a command line.
Although most Linux distributions come with a spell checker, this does not
mean you will be able to get away with typing things like this:

./configer --wiv-gnu-ld --nabl-pthreads
mak menuc onfig
cp[ sytem.a mp ]\boot/system.nap-2.4,6

etc
