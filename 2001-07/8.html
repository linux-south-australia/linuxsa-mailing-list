Hi.

Look, I like a whole lot about Macs and use them extensively, but this
whole PowerPC is twice as fast as same speed Pentium is a load of old
washing. Marketing smokescreen. I have had a 233 mhz G3 running LinuxPPC
sitting next to a 233mhz Pentium with equivalent memory and disk, and the
pentium is just plain faster. I am sure that the Photoshop tests that
Apple proudly displays with each release are correct, but they are not
translatable to your daily computer use unless you spend all day doing
unsharp masks on large image files.

Sure the new powerbooks are seriously cool appliances, and not too bad
value compared to the price differences we saw only a few years ago
between 'equivalent' Mac and PC Laptops. They just don't 'feel' as fast
as a current PC. Load OSX on them and they take a speed hit from that as
well, hopefully Apple will get into optimising OSX and give us back the
speed we had on OS9...

Oh yea, don't pick that Titanium Powerbook up by the corner, aparently the
frame twists and the battery loses contact with it's connectors resulting
in an immediate power-off (oops)

Michael
