using linux as a hub

I want to add another pc to my network, but my hub is full and don't really
want to buy another hub just for 1 pc. I have plenty of network cards and
could put the other pc on it's own network and use one of the linux boxes as
a bridge using ipchains. Can I instead use the linux as a hub and have the
new pc on the same network?
For example, could I have eth0 as 192.168.0.1 (which is plugged in to the
existing hub) and eth1 as 192.168.0.2 and the new pc as 192.168.0.10 (cross
over cable between the new pc and eth1), with packets from and to the new pc
going through eth1 and then out to the rest of the network on eth0?
