[Interview] Reaction to OSCON's Microsoft-Red Hat Debate

Found the following article on java.sun.com, which starts off with the 
introductory paragraphs below:


"
MAY THE BEST CODE WIN
Reaction to OSCON's Microsoft-Red Hat Debate
by Kammie Kayl

July 27, 2001-- On Thursday, July 26, Microsoft Senior Vice President Craig 
Mundie and Red Hat CTO Michael Tiemann faced off in consecutive keynote 
addresses.

In between hushed murmurs from the crowd, Mundie made the case for his 
company's shared-source software, in which code is made available to 
programmers but the company maintains intellectual property rights. 
Tiemann, whose company sells a version of Linux that rivals Microsoft's 
Windows, took the opposing side and discussed why open source is better.

In the wake of the keynotes and debate, java.sun.com caught up with Danese 
Cooper, a.k.a. Sun Microsystems's "Open Source Diva," to get her 
interpretation of what it all means to the larger free and open source 
community.
"
