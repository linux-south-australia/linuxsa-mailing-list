Re: Linux PDA's.

David,

Upon further inspection I must say that I am WAY impressed with the QT
paltop environment.
If you are interested in taking it for a test run before installing it onto
a PDA visit:

http://www.trolltech.com/products/qt/embedded/qpe-disks.html

I tried it last night (it runs from floppy on Linux or DOS) and I truly
believe that it could become the WindowsCE killer!!!! It looks, feels,
smells and tastes better! And it runs on many platforms.

The handwriting recognition is BETTER (imho) The software keyboard is very
nice and it also includes picksheets for letters and commonly used words
(like the auto complete that some Nokia phones have for text messages) for
example, I write "appli" and it offers "appliance, application... etc" Very
smart stuff. Choose the word your after and one click puts it into the
document / note / whatever.

You also have the advantage of developing applications on your favourite
platform and cross compiling to the processor of your PDA There are
indstructions for installing it onto many of the top models of PDA
including Cassiopia and the iPAQ.

It's a full graphical environment, no command prompts to deal with.
Your mum wont notice this, but hackers will just install a ported rxvt,
xterm or friend.

I'm sold, there is a free GNU version that suits my budget.
Otherwise you can go commercial for $3000 odd US and not give away your
source code.

I may even start using the full Qt and teach myself C/C++, maybe.

Jay

PS: Let me know what you think of it if you give it a try.
