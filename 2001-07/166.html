On 05 Jul 2001 10:43:54 +0930, Nik [TM] wrote:

> so why cant the "gurus" write scripts that filter the mail?? I beleive
> someone offered to do the work in a earlier email.

The offer was to write a Procmail recipe to filter out HTML. I stated
quite clearly above that the 'The idea of a seperate list ... is not to
prevent "newbies" from bothering the "gurus"', but to provide a more
helpful and supportive environment[1] for "newbies". It's not that hard
to write filters (even easier in Evolution with its graphical filter
configurator :-), but I don't see the point.

> maybe it could be accepted so the mail isnt lost and unanswered but then
> replies a warning to the sender to use plain text next time?
> and what may be useful is from reading the X-Mailer tag reply with the
> relevant instructions to show the "newbie" how to change their mail client
> to send plain text...

The 'X-Mailer:' header doesn't tell you whether or not the MUA is
sending in HTML or not. When I'm at work, I'd hate to receive an
automated message just because my 'X-Mailer:' tag includes "Outlook".

Instructions on how to disable HTML mail should be provided by the
vendor of the MUA. As far as I'm concerned, our responsibility stops at
telling them to change their settings (NOT how to change the settings).

> so as I said earlier, make the computers do the work with filtering
> scripts.. I always thought thats what we use computers for, to make our
> lives easier... or would that leave the  "gurus" with nothing to complain
> about ?!?  ;)

point in this email -- it was (and remains) rather obscure...
