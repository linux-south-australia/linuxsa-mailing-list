Re: internal modem

Hi Adrian,

Dynalink still make ISA internal modems:
http://www.dynalink.com.au

PCI is OK, as long as it is based on the Lucent Venus chipset:- this
chipset emulates a com port, albiet with a high i/o address & IRQ. The
ActionTEC modem is an example of such:
http://wwww.actiontec.com

Other Lucent chipsets, (Mars/Apollo) are WinModems.

I would *strongly* suggest going for an external USB modem:- whilst
internal are neat-&-tidy, if they latch-up due to telephone-line
interference you have to cold-boot the PC & if lightning hits the phone
line, it is likely to take the whole PC out, not just the modem.

An external modem is the weak link in the system, I will usually be
destroyed by lightning:- not the whole PC.

Without exception, I have nothing but trouble configuring internal
modems, even from reputable manufacturers, once done, (often hours
later), they work fine, like I said, I'm wondering if it is really worth
the tidy factor.

On that note, can people suggest hardware-based USB modems?
