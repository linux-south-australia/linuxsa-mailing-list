Re: Shared email across multiple OS on one system

> The problem appears to be that you want to use your isp as a mail store.
> I'm sure there are a lot of people on this list who collect their mail
> from the isp and store it on their own mail server. That way it is
> accessible and you only have to delete it once. If you wanted, you could
> run imap on your own mail server...

Yep, I get the email for all my domains forwarded to 1 of my 5 Optus@Home
email accounts and have fetchmail download the mail and distribute it to
the appropriate local email account (running qmail) every 2 minutes.

Why not just download it from Optus instead of using fetchmail? The mail
will be more secure (yes, it's all transferred using plain text
anyhow, but...) if it is kept on my mail server than Optus. There will
also be no quota problems if I keep emptying my Optus account every 2
minutes.

I am currently running pop3 but will move to imap because then I can view
my mail anywhere without it being permanently downloaded to any MUA. That
way I can check my email using pine (telnet-ssh2), my usual MUA
(evolution) on X windows, and IMP (https) or some other web based email
client.

My 2 cents. GST inc.
