My three MSI boards run Linux/BSD like a charm, however:

1) The Riva TNT2 Ultra
 - will stuff Windows 98 (whichever version) requiring about 15 reboots
to get the settings right

2) The AMD-K6II
 - is likely to cause Windows (whatever version it was that buggered up
with them) to just crash

3) The MSI6167 K7 Irongate Board
 - is, although it's not MSI's fault, as buggy as hell
 - takes about 10 reboots to get it to behave if you're installing Win
98

the drivers/board BIOS that causes Windows to not recognise the AGP bus
correctly, thereby forcing my [then] state of the art, fantastically
expensive video card to run at 640x480 with 256 colours; I'm lucky I
didn't get confounded green and black mono)

On the other hand, I've found MSI boards to be inexpensive. Or should I
