If we had to believe what Adam has just written then me and most of my
colleagues would be out on the street looking for somewhere else to go and
play :)

It is quite easy to oversimplify things, however the answer is that you will
find that Telstra is running just about everything everywhere, and just when
you thought they didn't you'd be proven wrong !.

Adam seems to have made the mainframe class machinery in Telstra's Sydney
and Melbourne data centres redundant by placing dome of their biggest
mainframe applications onto a MidRange platform :)  FlexCAB under Unix - I'd
like to see that :)

Telstra has an internal central data network (or WAN).  This integrated
network spans the entire continent, and more than likely extends over the
entire planet and even into space.  It is comprised of thousands of
fileservers, many thousands of workstations (> 40,000), hundreds of
mid-ranges, and a few mainframes.  This WAN is integrated into the
communications network that Telstra makes it's bread and butter from.  Now
consider Telstra's entire network of literally millions of devices, diverse
exchange technologies, network switches and routers, and then just for a
moment come to the conclusion that Telstra is running alot of different
stuff !!!

And Yes, Telstra whether they know it or not is running Linux boxes
somewhere in the organisation, umm maybe in a CISCO router or two - or
maybe, just maybe, on an OS/390 Mainframe :)

Finally, consider this - earlier this year a company (the parent company of
the one I work for) announced a greater than one $US billion dollars
investment in Linux, a company that is the major supplier of IT services to
Telstra.
