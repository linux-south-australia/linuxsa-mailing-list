I suggest a committee-based approabh to list filtering. Emails to Linuxsa
should be automatically forwarded to a select group of individuals (mainly
consisting of David Lloyds[1]), who collectively vote through an automated
system as to whether the mail is ok or not. If more than half[2] of this
group respond positively within a reasonable time limit[3], then the mail
message is entered into a CVS repository.

The LinuxSA Email Guru Squad (LEGS) then have half an hour to edit the mail
collectively to be correctly formatted[4][5] and then forwarded of to QA[6].
At this point, emails that have potential to be misintepreted[7] should have
a lenthy disclaimer attached to the top.

Finally, emails should be sent to a beta group of list users (selected for
their long-standing usage of the list) and comments received back to the
LEGS. If this group of beta users provides mostly positive feedback after a
one day beta cycle, the email can enter the full circulation of the user
group.
