Honestly, a 1.2GB HDD should be buckets of space for
what you are trying to achieve.

One thing you can do with the Distros, is just not
install eveything. The trick is knowing what to leave
out. The trouble with RedHat et al is the installers
give you some choices between types of systems & then
load tons of stuff a lot of which (esp. in your case)
will never be used. 


If you can work out what to put on, which I would
assume would be the core system, X/Windows, KDE or
Gnome (but only the bare bones), StarOffice and some
internet connectivity, you may be able to choose the
custom install selecting individual packages. This
leads to your next point...

> 
> The other biggie is that the users are in many cases
> going to be 
> computer illiterate and will need to get basic
> computer training as 
> well as training in Linux and Star Office. We can do
> that but the 
> systems will still need to be stable and dead simple
> in operation.

The less stuff on there, the less stuff a newbie can
mess up (at least until they get more experienced).
You can still ship a CD of other apps, just dont have
them installed to start with. Your average user in
this scenario will be able to achieve most things with
Star Office.

One area you may have problems with is printing, esp.
if the printers are InkJets that dont have LInux
drivers avail.
