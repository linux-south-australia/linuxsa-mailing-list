> Or am I missing the point of what a firewall is... I've always
> assumed it to mean effectively an IP filter, but as I think now, I
> do wonder... Would I be right in saying that in such an application,
> you would have a router to route packets and a separate firewall to
> block access, or would you just have a router with lots of ACLs?

A packet filter is only part of a firewall.  It's a really good tool
to have but you need other things like application proxies.  Depends
on what you want to get out of your firewall.  Security, Control and
watchfulness are the main things that should be considered.  Security
that fits in with the overall security policy of your organisation
(ie. don't spend a million $ on you firewall and have no physical
security).  Control so that people inside can only do what you want
them to [1].  Watchfulness so you can see if you have any
misconfigured internal machines [2] or if someone has broken into your
network etc.

Router == firewall..... maybe...  again, depends on what your
requirements are.  If you're a typical SME then merging the two is
probably OK.
