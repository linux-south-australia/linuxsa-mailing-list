Re: Hostname

On 28 Jul 2001 12:00:04 +0930, Andrew Williss wrote:

> what file, sets the hostname and domainame of the machine on BOOT?

Well, seing as you've not specified your distribution, I'll assume
you're using RedHat or a derivient.

RedHat stores the hostname, as well as some other basic network
information in /etc/sysconfig/network. Open it up in your favourite
editor and check the 'HOSTNAME' field.

> I thought my /etc/hosts would do this but it doesn't...

Er, nope. /etc/hosts just maps certain IPs to certain names. The
principle of /etc/hosts was, in fact, the earliest form of Internet
names, before DNS came into being.

> I have to manually set my hostname ie
> root@pikachiu root$ hostname [myhostname]
> why is this so?

Because you've not set it. The INIT script that sets the hostname
probably doesn't have any information to use.

> I found this out because samba wouldn't work and it ended up being a hostname 
> problem...(samba works fine as long as you change the hostname!)

Er, what distribution /are/ you running? RedHat and friends tend to set
the hostname to localhost.localdomain if you give it nothing else.
