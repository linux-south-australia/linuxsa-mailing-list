"Richard Russell" <richardrussell@internode.on.net> wrote:


> (note of course, that running `nmap localhost` is completely different to
> running `nmap <hostname>`, as localhost refers to the lo (127.0.0.1)
> interface, whereas <hostname> is (usually) your eth0.

Also bear in mind the type of nmap you run - if you're only blocking (for
instance) initial TCP packets with SYN set, and you're running something
other than a "connect" (SYN) type scan, it will report accurately the ports
that are open from RSTs sent back (but there are ways around this too).

> (I always wondered why 3128 is the standard -- is that a pre-squid,
> cache-standard thing?)

I think that's just a squid thing - 8080 was traditionally used for non-root
owned web servers (back when running web servers was less fashionable).
