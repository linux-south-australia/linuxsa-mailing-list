Re: SCSI driver causing kernel crash!

No.

But i can point you to linux.adaptec.com which has drivers.

Some of them are avaiable as rpms but only up to rh7.0. You cn also get
them as disk iamges which you can dd onto a floppy and use to install with
under expoert mode rh install(type "expert" at the lilo prompt when you
boot from the install cd or floppy).

I suspect many of the cards explicitly supported under linux are new and
are I20 based cards - whatever that is.

With my older adaptec scsi card( a 2940 U?| W?| UW? something or other) at
home, it was automatcially detected when I installed rh7.1 and just worked

I would assume that if you have just installed a scsi card into an aleady
running rh7.1 system, that it would simply be a matter of rebuilding the
kernal to get he newly installed older card working, or hitting the
website above if it is a newer card.

Hope this is slightly helpful.
