256kbps is 32kB/s. (divide by 8)

Of course, no matter what connection you use, you also pay extra for
protocol overheads -- sometimes up to 20% -- and you rarely get the full
connection speed anyway, for various reasons (bottlenecks all the way along
the pipe, from the server application you are talking to right through their
ISP, the backbone, your ISP, the link between your ISP's backbone and your
router at the ISP, and then your actual link.)

Remember that whatever speed your cable is, it's still got approximately the
same constraints on it, and it will be quoted in kbps, not kB/s.
