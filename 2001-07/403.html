The official word from Linus & Alan is that all new development should be
done with the new kernel. They also said they wouldn't accept new drivers
and stuff for 2.2 unless they are also produced for 2.4.
Plus, 2.2 just doesn't scale up as well, with filesystem, process, threads,
memory, etc. limits. 2.4 is faster too, has more features, better design,
better support for new hardware, and has been nice and stable for me.

Check out the various webpages detailing the differences between the two
kernel branches, and you'll see what i mean ;)

basically, if 2.2 works for you, you may as well stick with it, but as soon
as you need something nifty, just swap to 2.4 -- you'll want to do it
eventually  anyway.
