Re: Sendmail over dialup

Glen Turner wrote:
>David Newall wrote:
>> Your ISP should be less demanding of you; they should accept and
>> forward your email regardless of who you think you are.
>
> No they shouldn't, that allows customers to send e-mail with a known
> bad From address.  That is, they send but can't receive.  That
> functionality is only good for spamming.

I understand the point, but disagree about the source.  Sendmail was
rejecting James's mail because of an improper sender's envelope address,
not an improper "from" header.  The envelope address is not particularly
needed beyond delivery to the next relay.  If James cannot give his
machine a correct external domain name -- and there are plenty of reasons
why he might not be able to, even if he wanted to -- his ISP should be
willing to relay based on the pre-confirmed credentials that he used to
log on.
