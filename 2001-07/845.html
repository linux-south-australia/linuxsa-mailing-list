Re: [OT] Drive letter weirdness - Solvered?

On Mon, 16 Jul 2001, Brian Astill wrote:
 
> In WinNT  Drive 1 C E (G=CD) H I J K  Drive 2 D F ;(ext2 area)
> In DOS     Drive 1 C E F (G=CD) H I J  Drive 2 D K ;(ext2 area)
> 
> Furthermore, DOS thinks K is empty, whereas WinNT sees all the files in
> F (The same partition given the letter K in Drive 2 by DOS).
> Wierd.

Looks like I might have found the answer using Linux (Ahh! Rahh! Linux!)
Fdisk shows me that the extended partition on drive 1 is a type f -Win95
extended partition - with fat16 logicals therein.  ie plain DOS cannot
see inside the extended partition without Win95 &/or Partition Magic
help.  DOS 6.22 plain vanilla (no CD driver) sees only C D and an
empty E, which might well be a recognition of the presence of the
extended partition on Drive 1 (maybe??).

If I knew how, and was brave enough, I could probably change the type
of the extended partition on Drive 1 from f  (W95 extended) to 5
(Extended).  Otherwise it's going to be a matter of backing up all the
data and re-partitioning using DOS fdisk.

Thanks for your ideas which triggered the investigation.
