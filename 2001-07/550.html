[OT] PCI/AGP etc (was: RE: Cisco vs Linux)

> a co-incidence.  Now you tell me that they're putting RAM on an AGP bus?

I've always wondered why the AGP bus was for graphics only... I guess that
you have no need to go fast than PCI for most cards (eg sound, winmodems,
100BaseT etc), but if there's an appropriate application, why not? I'm kinda
surprised to see RAM on AGP though -- I thought that was normally on the
northbridge, with a dedicated, extra-fast bus of its own, whereas PCI and
AGP were on the southbridge (I could be wrong with AGP here though)...

I remember when AGP first came out, I wondered why call it Advanced Graphics
Port... why not Advanced PCI or something...  (marketing, I guess)...
There's nothing particularly Graphics about it apart from the fact that
that's the main application that was dirving the need for speed...

Certainly, if I wanted 1000BaseT, I'd be questioning the ability of PCI to
keep up...
