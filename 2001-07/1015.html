>P.S.  I've seen a V92 standard.  Question(s):  can I firmware 
>upgrade most/some V90 56K modems to this standard, and what does it
>offer?

Whether you can upgrade depends on your vendor.

V.92 has a number of cute enhancements. Improved compression (html 
optimised); Ability to use call waiting creatively (modem call can 
'park' itself and then come back from the dead when you've finished 
with that incoming call while you were surfing); 10 second-ish 
handshake time if you dial the same V.92 server as you did in your 
previous call (cacheing of the spectral analysis of the line from the 
previous call).

All pretty nice. Of course, ISDN DoV or ADSL blows it away for speed, 
but V.92 is the 'last best upgrade' of what you can do on an 'analog' 
phone line.
