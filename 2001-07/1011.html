Hey dude.

Bit of info : There are 8 bits in a byte.  So if you're converting kilobytes to kilobits (vice-versa) the multiple/divisor is 8.

A 56.6K modem is actually a 33.3K modem with compression algorithms (V90/56KFlex, which sucks anyway..) for incoming data (outgoing,
i.e. uploads, is still only 33.3K).

So a 56.6K modem is approximately, depending on compression, about 56.6K/8 = 7.075kb/sec.  Probably less.

Telstra ADSL on connections I've seen is capped to about 60kb/sec... so you do the maths.  They probably quote it in "bits" as it
looks bigger.

I've also used Telstra Cable connections in Sydney and Melbourne.. and they seemed pretty much the same.  Both are supported under
linux (pppoe for adsl, bpalogin for cable).  There might be slight differences but I'd say get whatever its the last
problematic/expensive.  You dont want a service going down on you more than your girlfriend ;-)

In my personal experience South Australia sucks for Internet Connectivity.  Full stop.  Broadband included.
