Re: EXT2FS, HPFS, JFS, NTFS, XFS shootout.

Daryl!

> Whats the better filesystem?

Put quite simply, the one that suits your criteria best.

> EXT2FS, HPFS, JFS, NTFS, XFS?

EXT2

- stable
- no journal
- no fall back
- slow fsck

HPFS

- don't know

JFS

- just gone release 1
- proven on AIX

REISERFS

- mature as far as Linux ports/creations go
- claims to be fastest
 - actually, mongo.pl, which is reiser's way of working out where it's
weakness' lies, tends to be tweaked to make it look the fastest
 - i.e. the "benchmarks" on the ReiserFS site are benchmarks for Reiser
vs Reiser and virtually all the other supposed benchmarks are
particularly invalid
  (for example, a ReiserFS expert might test a tweaked ReiserFS against
a default XFS)

NTFS

- oh please?
- why?

Hasn't anyone told you that it's main drawback is that:

a) it runs stably only on Win2000 or NT 4 service pack 1000000000

b) that the Linux kernel code to write to it basically says it WILL
PROBABLY crap your file system out


XFS

I like XFS. There's a RedHat Installer and XFS V1.0.1 is a total no
brainer to install except for two extra disk swaps. It's noticebably
faster than ext2 and it reboots unclean in no time. It apparently
understands Samba ACLS and it supports terabytes of storage.

It works well with NFS - unlike ReiserFS - and it has a dump program
already unlike ReiserFS.

It's crap at deleting lots of files, but who cares?

Generally, you have to make a choice on:

* stability
* features
* durability

They are not always able to be included at once...
