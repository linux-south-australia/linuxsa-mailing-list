RE: EXT2FS, HPFS, JFS, NTFS, XFS shootout.

> Whats the better filesystem?

depends on needs...

> EXT2FS, HPFS, JFS, NTFS, XFS?
>
> What are the pros & cons of each?
> What would be good for video editing?

You probably want something that is fast for both reading and writing, and
deals with large files well... fscking shouldn't matter so much here
(although given that you have massively large binary files, that you are
chopping and changing, you probably want to be reasonably confident that if
it all goes to custard, you are less likely to lose your file...)

Spose it also depends on how big your files will be, and how high resolution
etc... if you are editing web streams, then most file systems will handle it
find. If you are editing three hour HDTV epics, you may need something
special. I would suggest that if you are in the higher end, you will be
wanting to think about RAID more than file system choice...

Try to define your needs in terms of file size, read speed, write speed,
read/write ratio, perhaps even locality of likely reads/writes etc... Then
define your means in terms of $, time, space, power availability etc...  :)
