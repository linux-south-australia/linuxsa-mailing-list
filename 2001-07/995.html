Re: vietnamese fonts for linux? (chu+~ Vie^.t)

www.vps.org or www.vnisoft.com or www.trichlor.com uses
VIETNAMESE fonts.
you may ask questions for those sites. they may help you
to have dynamic fonts or fonts conversion software that
allows you to read vietnamese fonts in Vietnam. The tcvn
fonts only use in Vietnam only and it works for windows
platform. I did not know any one use UNIX either. Most
oversea websites use VNI fonts, VPS fonts and VISCII and
all are convertables.
I have tried VISCII and VPS for Solaris. I'm able to read
VISCII and VPS on oversea websites. ( notes they have
source code you may have to compiles for linux ).
Also you may try OPEN BSD or FREEBSD .These come with
VIETNAMESE fonts .

Good luck.
