RE: How to get best results from LinuxSA

Dale Long wrote:

> On the other hand. HTML in MIME in an email is a lot simpler to strp,
> leaving the actual plain text of the message.

Two points:

1) There's no requirement to send the plain text version along with
HTML version (even though most MUA's do it, apparently).  They're
both sent as separate MIME encoded parts.

2) Stripping any parts may corrupt any digital signing that someone
may elect to use (yes, no one uses it now, but that will prevent
anyone from using it).  Mind you, tacking the LinuxSA .sig on the
end probably breaks that too.
