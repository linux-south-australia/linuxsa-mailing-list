Re: Why do we hate HTML mail?

Rendering HTML in a text-only terminal isn't very satisfactory.
For those of us with shell accounts, there no alternative to text-based
terminals -- well, not unless hellstra & co want to offer up mega-fast adsl
with no bandwidth limits.. *grin*

Also, HTML has become associated with cluebies and gits. I have at least two
retailers who have me on a mailling list and spam me with absolutely
horrendous html emails.. we're talking about 10 (windows) fonts on a page,
all huge, with bold, underline, and colours changing every second word.

Needless to say i now filter them straight into my spam folder since its
almost easier to read the html source than their mess, but the point is that
i believe the message is more important than the medium.

If you have something to say, you can say it perfectly well in text without
requiring the ability to put annoying backgrounds and a variety of garish
colours. I believe things like that are mainly used by people who have a
poor grasp of language skills and need to distract the reader from realising
this.


Toby
