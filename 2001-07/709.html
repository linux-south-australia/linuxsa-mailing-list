try running `netstat -pl` as root -- it tells you the pids of the programs
that are listening on each port....

alternatively, try `lsof -i [tcp|udp]:<port>` (the tcp or udp is optional,
the : isn't, if you specify a port.

man is your friend. (man netstat and man lsof, that is...)
