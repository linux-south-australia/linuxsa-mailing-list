Re: RedHat 6.2 works OK with Linux 2.4.0-test9

David Lloyd! wrote:
> If you find that your system "bitches" even at make zImage, use make
> bzImage. bzImage uses the bzip2 compression algorithm which can make all
> the difference between a kernel that is too large and not (seeing you
> only need 1 byte out to have one that is too big)....

I don't think that's true.  A bzImage is a zImage that is capable of
being loaded above 1MB and has a maximum kernel size of ~2MB.  Both
bzImage and zImage use gzip; 'bzImage' just stands for 'big zImage'.

> Why the makers of bzip chose to give it an interface almost wholly
> incompatible with gzip is beyond me...on a really fast processor it's
> the best compression utility to use

Incompatible?  How so?

Richard Sharpe wrote:
> Ummm, David!, I did a 'make bzImage' for my 2.4.0test9 kernel, and it wound
> up being 960kB. Is that too large?
