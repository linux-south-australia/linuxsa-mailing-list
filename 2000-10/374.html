Mark Phillips wrote:

>> Signal 11 is usually a CPU or RAM problem. I have never ever seen SIG11
>> from software problem. Check your hardware (could also be motherboard).

> I second this.  I had signal 11 problems when the memory circuitry of
> my motherboard had problems.

Signal 11 is Segmentation Violation - most often caused by faulty
programs (whose default handler is to cause a core dump).  However,
it has been known to be caused by dodgy hardware, especially in
the early days with 4 meg o' RAM and 12 hour kernel compile times.
