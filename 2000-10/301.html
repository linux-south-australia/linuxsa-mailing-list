Re: Magicpoint ROCKS! We should have a meeting on it ...

I find the line-number orientation very sucky.  Sometimes
you don't want the first line on the slide to be the heading.

I think the ultimate technical presentation package is yet
to be written.

I noticed that someone has a XML DTD for slides that looks
very promising -- using "fop" you can convert the presentation
to PDF and use the fullscreen mode in the latest xpdf to
give the presentation.

I'll give it a go for my next presentation.

I've tried MagicPoint, Applix, StarOffice, PowerPoint, SliTeX
and a few Tcl packages and have been very disappointed to date.

If you look at what books on presentations say is best practice,
and what these packages provide, they're all pretty dreadful.

For example, a best practice is to provide on-going context.
So you might have a topic heading slide, and then repeat that
heading in small text at the top of each slide within that
topic.
