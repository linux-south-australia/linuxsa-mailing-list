Re: Wireless LANs and the law

Darryl Ross wrote:
> Basically, if a company wants to set up a WAN between sites, and they ARE
> NOT providing service to the public (any third party), and it is only for
> their internal use, then you DO NOT need to become a carrier.

Or, to make sure that your particular circumstances suit, check out (for
starters) Sections 26
(http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/ta1997214/s26.html),
27
(http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/ta1997214/s27.html)
and 42
(http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/ta1997214/s42.html)
of the Telecommunications Act 1997
(http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/ta1997214/index.html).

Then, seek out legal advice :-)
