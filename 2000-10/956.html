Re: Script behaving badly

> No but.  I was just highlighting the fact that in RH 6.2, /bin/sh is
> symlinked to /bin/bash, so if you put #!/bin/sh at the top of your shell
> script, you're not actually executing sh, you're executing bash.
> 
> Do you not think that this is worth pointing out?

Of course it is except that it had already been done. However I made
my point fairly clear last time, that saying "yes, but..." is not an
appropriate thing to say to a statement like "sh != bash".

It is this very subtle but important statement that so many people
make that breaks stuff.

I make this point beacuse that fact that sh is symlinked to bash in
some distributions of Linux means that many people put /bin/sh at the
start of their bash script.

It is a regularly occuring annoyance that breaks portability.

/bin/sh is not /bin/bash

#!/bin/sh
echo -e "$(echo -e "this is $(echo -e "wrong")")"

#!/bin/bash
echo -e "$(echo -e "this is $(echo -e "right")")"

#!/bin/sh
echo -e `echo -e "so is this"`
