Re: Bandwidth Throttling

On Wed, Oct 25, 2000 at 03:20:42AM +0930, David Lloyd wrote:
> 
> Is there any way to stop an active [1] ftp get from hijacking almost all
> of my bandwidth? Currently almost everything else simply goes awfully
> slow, whilst the ftp transaction appears to be going as fast as my link
> (over a modem) will allow it.

David,

You could use Packeteer:
http://www.packeteer.com/products/index.cfm

Or Fort Knox:
http://rogets.ind.alcatel.com/enterprise/products/id/proseries.html

Or the Xedia:
http://www.xedia.com/
-- which has been so thoroghly absorbed into Lucent that I just
can't find it on their website.
Maybe it's this:
http://www.lucent.com/ins/products/accesspoint/ap450.html

There's also the CheckPoint software:
http://www.checkpoint.com/
which runs on Solaris and NT.
They offer the Floodgate software:
http://www.checkpoint.com/products/floodgate-1/index.html

The prices of these sorts of customer premises shapers
are in the range US$2800 to US$28,000, according to
the November 1998 edition of "Data Communications".

It's clear to me that all of this could be done for free
in the linux kernel.
But I don't know what shaper projects are running right now
for linux.
Such software would be a natural extension to packet filtering.
Filtering by volume rather than just by things like
IP addresses and port numbers would be a logical extension.
And then the high-price dedicated hardware product market
would collapse.
Linux could take over the shaper market in addition to the
server and desktop markets.
And this would not be that difficult.
I bet it's been done already!

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.
