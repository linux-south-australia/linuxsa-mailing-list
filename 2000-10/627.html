You would plug your 56K modem into one of the analog ports on the NTU(*),
and go from getting 28.8 connections to 56K connections (you have a digital
line into the NTU, so virtually perfect line, only analog bit is the 2M
cable between your NTU and modem) without changing anything else.

If you use the ISDN port on the NTU, then you pay $1/hr for the phone call,
if you use one of the analog ports, then you get charged 18c flatrate for
the phone call. Question is, is that extra 8K worth $1/hr?? Especially since
you'll be nearly doubling your connection speed as it is!

DR


* NTU = network terminating unit
