>
> Now here's the rub - the majority of residents in Greenwith and Spring
Hill
> have their phones serviced off the Greenwith AXE RAU, this is an AXE
Remote
> Access Unit which is parented off one of the major Adelaide CBD exchanges.
> In itself this is not a problem, but Telstra then decided to use RIM's to
> provide the phone services to the end user, which means most residents
> cannot get higher than a 28.8K modem connection and can dream about such
> things as ADSL and ISDN.
>
> Here's how it works, the entire development is full of fibre optic cabling
> (just dream about the Gigabits of capacity) and this is used to cable the
> RIM's to the GWTH RAU.  From each RIM, standard copper twisted pair is
used
> for the local loop to each house.  Sound's great doesn't it, lots of clean
> digital channels between the RAU and each RIM and then literally metre's
of
> new copper cable to each house.  Think again, Telstra decided to use non
> integrated RIMs which means that the local copper loop is converted to
> digital at the RIM, the signal is then transmitted through the fibre optic
> cable to the RAU where it is converted back to analogue and fed into the
> standard analogue exchange ports of the RAU.  These two analogue to
digital
> conversions are what restricts the modem to a theoretical limit of 31.2K
but
> more realisticly 28.8K.  If Telstra had used integrated RIMs then the RIM
> would be digitally connected to the RAU, thus eliminating the second A/D
> conversion and allowing 56K modem operation, also ISDN services would be
> available right now and ADSL would only require adding new cards to each
of
> the RIMs and not changing all the interfacing infrastructure at the RAU
end.
>
> Back to ADSL, Telstra wasn't planning to provide ADSL to Golden Grove -
the
> service was going to stop at the Modbury exchange.  However, according to
> their web site, Telstra is now reviewing the provision of ADSL from the
> Golden Grove exchange.  This will make most residents of Golden Grove and
> Wynn Vale happy, but it still leaves the residents of Greenwith and Spring
> Hill out in the cold.  Perhaps somebody should inform the investors on
> Spring Hill who are building their 300K plus mansions of the situation.
