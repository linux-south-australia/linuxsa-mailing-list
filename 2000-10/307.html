Re: fsck stuff (longish)

Andrew wrote:
> In short : How do I run fsck, so that the machine will reboot no matter
> what fs problems (within reason) ?

The only time I've personally had problems with an ext2 filesystem not getting
fixed by the standard on-boot fsck is when the superblock gets corrupted.  You
then need to specify one of the redundant superblocks as a param to fsck,
something which I guess is hard to script (since the first redundant superblock
may also be stuffed etc), and hence why the login script encourages fsck be run
manually.

Not an expert in this area by any means - more enlightened comments welcomed,
