Don Collins wrote:

> I've done the `shutdown -F -r now` trick, and it did do the fsck'ing at
> reboot, but still gives a warning message anyway.

What did the dumpe2fs show you?  Had the mount count been reset to 0/1?

> The only thing I've noticed is that the warning banner is being
> displayed immediately after a notice regarding a ramdisk.  Could it be
> that it's referring to a ramdisk, not a physical partition?

Not likely.  Ramdisks don't exist across reboots, so they never live
long enough to require a fsck.

> Is it necessary to have a ramdisk anyway?

Depends on your circumstances - your most likely not using one during
normal boot (but I could be wrong).
