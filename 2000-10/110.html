>On Tuesday,  3 October 2000 at 23:07:08 +0930, John Edwards wrote:
>>
>> Remember that Telstra suggest that 2Mb/s shared among 200-300 ADSL
>> (<1.5Mb/s) users is a workable oversubscription level, so there
>> won't be much load to deal with. Most ISP's in Adelaide are offering
>> more bandwidth than this to 200-300 modem users .. do the math and
>> work out why you'd want to pay twice as much to possibly end up with

>Where do they suggest this?  What line are they talking about?  After
>all, ADSL is a dedicated line between the exchange and your premises.

Well, given that they will have a bunch of modems (an DSLAM?) in their POP,
which will be connected into their infrastructure, it may be that this
connection is a 2Mb (frame relay) link. Thus, each of the group of users
who are on the same DSLAM are sharing this link. It is the same as a normal
ISP. It they only have 2Mb connectivity to Telstra/Optus, then all their
customers are 
sharing that bandwidth.
