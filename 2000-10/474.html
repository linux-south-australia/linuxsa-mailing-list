Re: Kppp problems RH6.2

OK, here is yet another try on this topic.

After thinking about things, and realizing that the routing may well be
correct, I recalled that I have seen this sort of problem several times
before in my Linux classes when we use PPP and modems etc.

When you tried to ping even the address on the other end of the link, what
command did you use? 'ping' or 'ping -n'?

ping by itself tries to translate the address into a name, and if your DNS
setup is wrong, it will take a long time to time out, and will only print
one response in like 10 seconds or so.

Secondly, have you used tcpdump to see if packets are actually flowing down
the line and responses coming back?
