Re: telnet slowness

bmonten wrote:
> 
> telnet and ssh are the same. I pinged the remote server - avg. time was
> 472ms.
> I doubt compression will help - most of the work is command line stuff.

Compression does help on lagged links, even for command line stuff.
Packet loss is what makes lagged links annoying more than latency
itself. By compressing data, sshd is sending smaller packets, or less of
them so packet loss becomes less of an issue. 

The other thing that really helps with long-distance telnet is an input
buffer (sometimes called a chat window), where you type on the local
screen but data is not transmitted until Enter is pressed. This makes
the session much more like command-and-response, rather than the painful
lagged 
i n t e r a c t i v e   k e y s t r o k e s. This is a feature of the
telnet/ssh client, although there used to be a terminal mode/ansi thing
you could do to make a standard telnet client act like this. I can only
recall it being refered to as "scrollies" implented in the chat module
on the TSX BBS system.
