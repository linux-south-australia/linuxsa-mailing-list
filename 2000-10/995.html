Re: telnet slowness

> The other thing that really helps with long-distance telnet is an input
> > buffer (sometimes called a chat window), where you type on the local

> Can you do this with SSH ? How ?

For those who were left wondering about this thread, you can do it now
with Telnet.

Telnet (possibly over SSH for encryption/compression) to the remote host
as usual, then do a ^] to enter the telnet escape mode. Type "mode line"
in there, to enter line-at-a-time mode, where all keys are buffered and
echoed on the local system, and only transmitted when you hit enter.
Great for slow and lagged connections.

The downside is that ncurses type applications won't work with this, as
well as hotkeys, tab-autocomplete, and cisco ? help. You can always
switch back to normal mode without losing the connection.

As always, RTFM, this info was in the man page for telnet :)
