Okay. I can't match the 1200 bps modem.
In 1986, I used 300 bps, but that was just borrowed
from the Kentucky comp sci department. So I had to give
it back.

However, in 1989, I did buy a 2400 bps modem from someone
who went to the US and offered to get everyone a cheap
modem in the US. This one cost only A$100, even though the
current 1200 bps modems in Australia cost more like A$200
or A$300 at the time.

This further free modem is a "basic time" BT2400E,
still in excellent working condition, although it is
almost certainly not "Telecom Approved" or whatever
it's called now. It has a US 120V transformer too.
So I'm not expecting a rush for this one.
And 3 of the 9600 bps modems are still available.

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.

PS. I guess that in 2 years, even 56k modems will
