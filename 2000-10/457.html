That's absurd.  The place to be paranoid about disk I/O is most certainly
not in an application such as cp or cpio.  Did any of my staff or proteges
do something like that and I would feel obliged to beat such person around
the head and shoulders.  Severely.

The place to be paranoid about disk I/O is in the disk I/O driver, or
better yet, in the disk I/O hardware.  I have no idea if Linux offers
read after write.  If it does I predict it would be an option that is
off by default.
