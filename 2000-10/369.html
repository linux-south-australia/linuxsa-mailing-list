>Is it just my perception, or is Red Hat decidedly less reliable since
>version 5.2?  Every 6.x and now 7 issue has had lots of quite
>significant problems.  Is the "RedHat is the M$ of the Linux
>world" comment being proved correct?  I actually only use

I think it is your perception, and a fact about the RedHat release process.

Historically, all x.0 releases of RedHat have been poor, and by the time
they get to x.2, they have improved markedly.

Allied to that, however, is the growing trend for security bugs to be
discovered in all and sundry programs that all distros release, and you
have a high rate of release of patches.

I personally will not use RH 7.0 on production machines, and will stick
with 6.2. Once RH7.1 comes out, I may consider it, especially if I need a
2.4 kernel for some of the things I need to do.
