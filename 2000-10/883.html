Re: Bandwidth Throttling

Alan Kennington wrote:

> It's clear to me that all of this could be done for free
> in the linux kernel.
> But I don't know what shaper projects are running right now
> for linux.
> Such software would be a natural extension to packet filtering.
> Filtering by volume rather than just by things like
> IP addresses and port numbers would be a logical extension.
> And then the high-price dedicated hardware product market
> would collapse.
> Linux could take over the shaper market in addition to the
> server and desktop markets.
> And this would not be that difficult.
> I bet it's been done already!

It has!

If you turn on "Prompt for development and/or incomplete code/drivers"
in the "Code maturity level options" when configuring a 2.2 or newer
kernel, a "traffic shaper" option appears at the end of Network devices.

Also, at the end of "Networking Options", there is a submenu containing
QoS kernel options, which are probably more useful with the FTP problem.

Disclaimer: I havn't tried any of the above in a production system.
