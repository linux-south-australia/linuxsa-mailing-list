Re: diagnosing packet loss...

Ian Hogben wrote:

> I had a similar problem when I moved from one ISP to another. Try the modem
> init string. Reading through the MODEM HOWTO (linuxdoc.org), I found a good
> string that is very reliable. Try something like this:
> 
> Located in /etc/sysconfig/network-scripts/chat-ppp0
> OLD: "ATS0=0Q0V1"
> NEW: "AT&K3E1S0=0Q0&C1&S0V1"

The &K3 part is the most important; if you're running the DTE speed way
over the connect speed of the modem (eg, 115200 vs 33600), then without
flow control you stand a good chance of losing larger packets.  The
3 options enables hardware flow clontrol on the modem, which lets it
drop CTS when unable to keep up.  Couple this with modem retraining
(eg, MNP4/5) usually causes a lot of people a lot of grief.
