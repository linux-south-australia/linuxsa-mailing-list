> I'm about to embark on learning C as well as teaching it to my nephew he's
> 11. Is there a good book out there like Teach Yourselft C in 24 hours etc?
> This is not my first programming language as I've done Basic, DBase 111+,

> my code.

Compiling uses GCC.. if you have built a kernel you'll be right.

As far as editing.  Xedit, Vi, gnp, etc are OK.

Good editors for C-code are Emacs and Grasp.

Emacs comes with most Linux distros, and i have some good config files here
somewhere that I can send to you.  Grasp is free from
http://eng.auburn.edu/grasp  or something like that.  Grasp has the
advantage that it can build a CSD (tree showing what belongs in what) and
coloug hilighting out of the box.  Emacs can be configured to do
brace/bracked hilighting and to call your compiler.  I'd recommend trying
both and choosing the one you are most comfortable in.
