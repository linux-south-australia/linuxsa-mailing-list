Re: ADSL

Richard Sharpe wrote:

> It is the same as a normal
> ISP. It they only have 2Mb connectivity to Telstra/Optus, then all their
> customers are
> sharing that bandwidth.
> 

Normal ISP's are not just limited to their upstream connectivity, they
also employ caching proxy servers, mail servers, news servers/caches,
peering, game servers and other content (like tucows) to give their
customers more data than they could download through an upstream link
alone. With a Frame or ATM link to a virtual POP, you don't have this
advantage, even though telstra's inferior method of forcing IP
connectivity for ADSL would allow it.

If you play peer-to-peer Quake on a Telstra ADSL system with your buddy
who also uses the same ADSL provider, the data must travel up and down
the same frame link via the ISP's router, along with all of the data
being used by all other customers. This means that there's no speed
advantage to play against someone using the same ISP as you. ISP's that
own their own infrastructure do not suffer from this degradation of
service.
