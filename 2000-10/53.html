Noooo -- don't do that!

Perl is great for people who understand about how computers work, and can
choose the best way of acheiving something. This is when TMTOWTDI (There's
More Than One Way To Do It -- the Perl slogan) is an advantage. However, if
you are learning, it becomes a curse, as you can get away with really bad
programming practices. I'd recommend Java or Ada (even though it sucks and
no-one uses it) for teaching disciplined programming, that can then be
applied to C or Perl. In many ways, C is also a good language to learn in,
because while you can get away with many poor programming practices
(uncheckd typecasts, etc), it is relatively close to the underlying
hardware, and will increase your understanding of how computers really
work... IMHO, the two best languages to learn are Java and C... Then move to
Perl, C++, and whatever other language you need to know...
