Re: IP Accounting

  Damien Wilson wrote: 
> I have a client that would like to do accounting for the internet time 
> of their clients.

Glen Turner wrote:
> Firstly, Internet "time" is a dodgy measure for permanently connected
> machines.  You might want to charge by traffic or apparent bandwidth
> instead.
> 
> Secondly, you need to decide what level of aggregation is suitable.
> In general, more accounting infrastruture is needed for more
> detailed accounts.  I'd strongly encourage you to keep it
> simple.

I agree with Glen - time is a hard to measure when the clients are
permanently connected...

For clients needing this sort of solution, I have implemented SQUID and
a few addons to record what sites they have gone to, and how much they
have used. I was part of the team that wrote it, so I have a resonable
understanding.

We called the system "Alfred". Don't ask why.. The package contains
the SQUID stuff, a quota management webpage, an Internet Token generator
[1] and a NDS Authentication Module (PAM).

http://alfred.sourceforge.net   [the website needs a bit of updating :)]

It works quite well for us. You might find it wise to flush the log file
every now and then (Every 6-12 months) depending on usage. One that I
work with is 1.4GB and takes a few days to generate HTML logs for users
on a PII-333
