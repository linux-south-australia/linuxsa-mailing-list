OK, my understanding for (part of) the process e-mail to fax is:
1. The gateway receives an e-mail that is to be sent by fax 
2. The text of the e-mail is converted to fax graphics.
3. Using fax-modem the gateway dials the destination number 
   and tries to send the fax (cost = 25 cents if the line is not busy)
4. If step 3 fails - repeat N times(*25 cents) or until success

I will be quite surprised if step 3 could be executed over the perm
connection. On the other side of this connection there is a modem,
router and the Internet. There is no access to the public telephone
system so the destination fax number can not be reached.

However if somebody wants to sell ad space in the fax cover page he
could give it a try. There might be advertisers ready to pay 30 cents or
more for about 200 square centimeters. Just have in mind that receivers
sometimes get upset when their fax paper is wasted for advs.
