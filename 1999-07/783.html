> The more distributions a company supports

Let's turn this around:

I agree that there would be more Linux support
work around (some for each Linux "flavor")...

But I see oppportunity in that... since, with an in-
crease in demand, there will be an increase in
service-providers.

My point:  Who says ONE company must take
all that work on themselves.  Isn't it at least the
hope of SOME of the (say, younger) Linux SA
members that there growing skill-levels will be-
come - at some point - great enough to make
them interesting to consulting firms, ISPs &/or
end-users of Linux or *BSD systems?

And - even if you think ONE company *should*
win most of the support work (e.g. for organis-
ing a users group & doing deals with vendors,
etc.)...

- who says that Co. couldn't *sub-contract* out
the less lucrative and/or (for any reason) less
interesting (to them, given their preferences,
e.g. for application area, etc.) work to others?

If they can do this for less interesting app'n-
areas, they can do it for less interesting fla-
vours of Linux or *BSD...

Finally, even if they were to stay with a few
distributions (the ones they know best, we
hope, rather than the ones with the best
margins...), the old Intel model says:  sell
the boxes at around cost...

... so that MORE people will try it; if it's as
good as it's believed to be by its mission-
aries, this will lead to MORE people & or-
ganisations using it...

...which, of course, means MORE Linux or
*BSD work for people (e.g. on the respec-
tive mailing-lists) to bid for, either directly
or via a contracting firm.

Cool, eh?
