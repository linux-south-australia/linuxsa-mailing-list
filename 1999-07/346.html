redhat 5.2 Perl no good!!!?

Further to my earlier note about Perl/Tk not working
on Redhat 5.2, I've had a communication from the author/maintainer
of Perl/Tk to say that the Redhat 5.2 version of Perl 
is no good. To quote:

"The perl bundled with RedHat 5.2 is NOT perl5.00404 but a pre-alpha
hack on the way to 5.00405 which was never intended for wide release.
It is broken."

This is very disheartening, and is the first I've heard of it.
This has cost me some 20 hours of hack-work trying to find
bugs. Most frustrating, but not as frustrating as the
infamous Cisco router modem retraining bug.....

Question:
Is this a generally known thing?
The serene entity who communicated this info to me
said that Redhat 6.0 Perl should be okay.
Any confirmation of this?

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.


PS. If anyone else has a Bigpond permanent modem (33.6k)
service to the Internet, as has encountered the infamous
Cisco router modem retraining bug, I finally resolved it
by writing a Perl kludge-script to do pattern recognition
on ping results and then reset the PPP link when the modem
goes bad. The script is at
http://topology.org/src/pingdog/pingdog2.pl
(If you missed the traumatic discussions of this matter
in Dec 1998-March 1999, just be grateful.)
