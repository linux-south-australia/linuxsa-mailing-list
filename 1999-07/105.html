The funniest thing

 << How MS plans to remake Windows.url>>  
 
This is just the funniest thing I have seen in a while ...

The link is to a zdnet article that is discussing the next version of
Windows. No - not Win98++, not Win2000, but beyond that, to something
codenamed 'Neptune'.

The article is based on internal documents from Microsoft. - A practice that
MS has been up to for years, where they leak far flung plans at the press,
with vague promises about some near-future digital nirvana. Anyone on this
list remember Cairo ?   How 12 months after the shipping date for Cairo had
expired, MS was telling people 'but YOU got it wrong - Cairo was never a
product, it was just a bucket of ideas that we have picked peices out of and
applied to all of our products ... hmmm'.

Anyway, the really funny thing about this article is what happens when you
scroll down the page and have a look at the feedback column ...    It seems
that the old press-leakage tactic that MS has used so well in the past does
not translate at all well to the internet - where real people are free to
provide instant rebuttal.  Yet another sign that the Internet is completely
over MS's corporate head.
