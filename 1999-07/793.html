On Thu, 29 Jul 1999, C4IS wrote:
> Even if he leaves behind the ones who don't want to

Please don't misunderstand me.  It's not a question of training
anyone else's employee.  People who seek work experience can be
no-hopers.  It can take substantial effort to babysit such people.
I think it's easy to understand why a company would be reluctant
to spend that effort when they're not even looking for staff.  (If
they were looking for staff then one wouldn't be cold-calling for
work experience, one would be responding to an advertisement.)

In my personal experience, getting great skills in IT needs nothing
more than a computer and a genuine interest.  I think people who
have a computer and still need somebody to teach them are probably
never going to be great.  Somebody who has a computer AND has the
enthusiasm might still need some formal experience, but when they
come they'll be so bright and switched on that "work experience"
would be the last thing on anyone's mind.

As an aside, none of what I wrote is intended as a comment on
whoever it was that started this thread.  I have and express no
idea about her (?) skills, enthusiasm or ability.
