> gcc -g -c myclient.c -L/usr/lib/mysql
> gcc -g -o myclient myclient.o -L/usr/lib/mysql -lmysqlclient -lsocket
> -lnsl
> ld: cannot open -lsocket: No such file or directory

	Erm, I take it you're compiling under Linux?
	If so:
	libsocket & libnsl are perculiar to Solaris only (AFAIK),
	you shouldn't really need to specify them at the linking stage
	at all (in Linux, pretty much all the socket stuff is part
	of libc)....

	Try commenting out the '-lsocket -lnsl' bit from the Makefile
	(these libs are probably tacked onto LD_FLAGS somewhere).
