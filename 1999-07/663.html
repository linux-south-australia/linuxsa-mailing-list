Re: MODEMS & COM PORTS

On Sun, 25 Jul 1999, David Lloyd wrote:
> I normally use modemtool to connect the modem to the correct com port.
> However, what does modemtool actually do?

This illustrates the problem with "black-box" administration tools.
You should know what really goes on under the hood, otherwise when the
tools let you down (and there will come a time when they do) you are
stuck.  Fortunately modemtool is (so I just discovered) a bourne shell
script.  Actually it's a Python script encapsulated in minimal bourne.
A really good start, when you feel the urge to ask questions such as
this, is to look at the program.  If it's a shell script you can just
read it; and while that may not be a complete answer (as is the case
with modemtool) it can certainly point you in the right direction.  If
the program is compiled then UTSL (Use The Source, Luke.)

Knowledge is power.  Power to the people.  And phui to people who won't
see the power in front of them.
