I've probably read fewer man pages than you have, David, so I don't
lay claim to any real expertise, but as I understand it, startx does
not involve xdm.  However both startx and xdm should use xauth, as it
provides good security.

`startx' is just a script which probably includes lines similar to this:
   serverargs="$serverargs -auth $HOME/.Xauthority"
   mcookie=$(mcookie)
   xauth add $display . $mcookie
   xauth add $(hostname -f)$display . $mcookie

(/usr/bin/mcookie generates a 128-bit random hexadecimal number for
use with the X authority system.)

Check out chapter 6 of the Linux Remote X Apps mini-HOWTO at:
  http://sunsite.unc.edu/LDP/HOWTO/mini/Remote-X-Apps
(or somewhere in your /usr/doc/ filesystem)

Are the permissions on your $HOME/.Xauthority set to 600?

Does 'xauth list' generate output something like this:
   toby.caemrad.com.au:0  MIT-MAGIC-COOKIE-1  661045517b6c701b63520f2824093565
   toby/unix:0  MIT-MAGIC-COOKIE-1  661045517b6c701b63520f2824093565
?

"begun failing again".)
