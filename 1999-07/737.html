Mark Newton wrote:

> 3 years?  That's a bit of a stretch.  A kick-ass computer bought today
> will be somewhat inadequate running the kinds of applications we'll
> be taking for granted in three years time.

Actually, a recent Communications of the ACM article identified a three
year purchasing cycle as the optimal for both price and performance,
so Alan's gut feel is spot on.


I'd post to the OSISP list (asking for off-list replies) as most ISPs
have exactly your problem.  Which basically boils down to "I want
a very-standard PC".

As Mark says, if you want to specify exactly what goes into the
computer you will pay more.  Be careful that this `more' doesn't turn
out to be in excess of buying a PC from Dell with Linux preinstalled.

Even if the Dell parts are non-standard (and they usually aren't)
their market-share is enough to ensure that Linux will run on it.
[Which I'm sure is the only reason that Linux runs on some really
shocking hardware from Acer :-) ]
