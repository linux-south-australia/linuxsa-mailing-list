	Urgh. I just reread this, and it doesn't make any sense.
	Inetd creates a UDP socket and adds the relevant file descriptor
	to the select() mask.
	Inetd doesn't listen() or accept() with UDP sockets, but 
	you don't ever use either of those syscalls with UDP (ie 
	neither inetd nor the server itself calls listen() or accept()).
