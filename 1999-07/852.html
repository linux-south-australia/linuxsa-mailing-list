>C4IS wrote:
>> 
>> Your UNIX Youth Mentoring program is fascinating!
>> 
>> Would your tests be on-line and/or available (e.g.
>> by return e-mail attachment), so that others might
>> see how theirs compare to yours?
>
> Sorry, it is not.
> The test is given once a year,
> twice if there is sufficient growth in membership
> [enough to start a new class].
>
> The goal is to test knowledge and not memory.
>
> We do not want the youth to study the test,
> we want them to study UNIX:-)
>
> There are 100 questions, 3 points each.
>
> When you join <snip> you are given a white cord.
> If you get an 80 on your test you get a red cord,
> if you get a 180 on your test you get a blue cord,
> if you get a 280 on your test you get a green cord,
> if you get a perfect 300 you get a gold cord.
>
> There is only one gold cord in Ohio and
> just a hand full in the USA.
>
> We have found that UNIX Sys Admin's with 5 years
> of on-the-job experience tend to get a blue cord.
>
> Those with 10 years tend to get a green cord.
>
> All white cord members can take the test when it is offered.
>
> Red and above cord members can only take the test
> if they are "active" have written an article for our news letter
> "The Adventure" every month, and responded to all nominations
> for "The Scouting The Web Award".
>
> Unfortunetly, we currently have no red cord members that are
> eligible to take the test over to get a blue cord:-(
>
> When a youth achives their blue cord,
> we sometimes allow them to
> shadow the advisors at work,
> help them to become inturns,
> get them jobs...
>
> We tend to let them come to Advisors' meetings:
> [social events :-]
>
> Youth Membership is open to anyone between the ages
> of 14 [and in high school] to 20 years of age.
>
> We are extremely picky on adults though.
> They must be able to teach. [UNIX, Camping, Leadership...]
>
> Though it is a bit premature, we have two churches asking
> to copy our program. One in Denver Colorado and one in Utah.
>
> It is our goal have our program all over the world.
> This will happen soon:-)
>
> 369 was in Adelaide a few years back. It is a great city.
> I know that I would love to find a teaching job for the
> months of January and February in Adelaide.
> Kind-of a working holiday...
>
> There was a group in Queensland that tried to start a
> UNIX Youth Mentor "Rover" program. It did not last :-( 
>
> To my knowledge there are only 2 maybe 3 working
> UNIX Youth Mentor Programs in America:
>
>   us, AT&T, and NBC-TV. I think we are the best :-)
