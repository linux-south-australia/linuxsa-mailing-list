Re: NY Times Circuits section (Commodor 64 I'net access! ;)

Hi.

Not wishing to fuel another heated debate, but there are people out
there C41S, who cannot afford to buy that neat 32bit cash converters
PC. (No, I'm not one of them)

This is not an ideal world. In percentage terms, less than 1% of the
worlds population has a home computer yet. If someone wants to run a
C64 for the net, or a Z80 or TRS-80, let them go for it. All power to
them. I'd rather some of them get connected to the information via
outdated technology than not at all. If C64 access is limiting, exactly
how limiting is no access? Unfair? would these types rather be in an
'Internet underclass' (a bit harsh) or a 'no-internet underclass'?

Don't forget that the power is in the information, not the gee-wiz
graphics. Lynx is cool for cutting to the chase and forgetting about
the eye candy. I'll bet you could use lynx from a C64. Gaelyne?

Michael
