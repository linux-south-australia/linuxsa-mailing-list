linux weekend course in Adelaide?????

A recent couple of posts conjure up the following idea:

A linux training course in Adelaide.

My apologies is this is a tired, old, obvious idea,
which it is. But it occurred to me that the once-a-week
1-hour talks could be concatenated into, not a conference,
but a full weekend course, possibly semi-residential at some
hide-away in the hills that has a good internet connection
and lots of room to set up, say, about 20 PC, ethernetted.

A weekend, organized in time similarly to a choir rehearsal
camp, would be 3 formal sessions of about 3 hours each per day,
plus limitless informal sessions in the main room, with people
learning the way computers have always been best learned -
by asking the person sitting next to you.

The timetable would be Fri 6-9 p.m., Sat. 9-12,
2-5 p.m., 7-10 p.m., Sun 10-1, 2-5, or something like that.
That would be about 18 hours of intensive formal time --
i.e. 18 1-hour talks by specialists in each topic.
That's equivalent to about a year of linuxSA meetings.

This is such an obviously good idea, I'm certain I'll
be told that it's been done already.

But _please_ don't let this be a stimulus for
a long thread of dilute postings. 

The name of the thing could be Linux Fest 99,
or LinuxFest99a for the first one, LinuxFest99b for the
second one etc. Or LinuxFest Adelaide 0.0, LinuxFest Adelaide 0.1
and so forth.

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.
