Re: Sound Card

> Hi all, 
> 	I bought a new sound card (creative pci 64 labs). Linux found a AudioPCI
> card in my PC and I can hear the sound testing. Unfortunately, I can't play
> music CD. If anyone know how to fix, please let me know. Thanks

The sound aplications can control what the CD is doing.  And hence you
press play on xplaycd or whatever and the CD starts to play.  Most
have a jack on the fronth for listening.

For the soundcard to be able to control the volume and balance of the sound
(and to play it through external speakers) the CD-ROM must be connected to
the soundcard.

If you sound card didn't come with a cable, contact the hardware provider
and try to find one that is suitable.

BTW I have a SB PCI64 and am quite happy with it under Linux.
