> On Tue, 20 Jul 1999, Reynolds, Steve (DAIS) wrote:
> > What's the best way to remove Linux, so that I'm left with a totally
blank
> > unformatted disk
>
> In a technical sense it's not possible.  You can no more unformat a disk
> than you can uneat a steak.  When a disk has been formatted, it's been
> formatted.  However: in the sense that you're left with an unbootable
> disk with no easily accessible filesystem, and assuming you mean you're
> running on a Wintel PC, use fdisk to delete the partition.  I think
> recent DOS/Windows fdisks will allow you to remove 'foreign' partitions.
> Alternatively, try
>
>   # dd if=/dev/zero of=/dev/hda
>
> Let it run for a few seconds and then switch off the machine.
>
> Beware, kiddies:  We're trained professionals: Don't try this on Mommy's
> or Daddy's machine because you'll probably break it.
