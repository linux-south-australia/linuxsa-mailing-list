Jake,

>>>>> "Jake" == Jake Hawkes <jake@eclectic.com.au> writes:

  Jake> basically: fopen64 fn_format __lxstat64 are missing. the '64'
  Jake> seems to me like it may be refering to 64bit things. This
  Jake> machine is only an i386 machine (p90), so is this wrong?


These symbols are in libc.a if you're running glibc-2.1.  They are not 
in glibc-2.0.

nm /usr/lib/libc.a | egrep "(open64|lxstat64)"
         U __open64
iofopen64.o:
00000000 T _IO_fopen64
00000000 W fopen64
freopen64.o:
00000000 T freopen64
         U __open64
         U __lxstat64
lxstat64.o:
00000000 T __lxstat64
open64.o:
00000000 T __open64
00000000 W open64
         U __open64
         U __lxstat64


Sounds like you probably need to upgrade your libraries.  If you are
on glibc-2.1 already, try adding -lc to right at the end of your
compilation line (though I wouldn't expect this to be necessary given
the options you're using).
