> How do I boot without any modules (or with only some selected modules)?

When lilo comes up type press tab to find out the boot names then type:
linuxbootname init=1 root=/dev/hdxx  (where xx is the drive and partition
					where linux is installed ie
                                        /dev/hda1) 

This forces the kernel to go to level 1 on the specific linux partition
and to ignore /etc/fstab (whcih may be the problem)

Also try:
linuxbootname init=1 root=/dev/hdxx,ro

then once at the prompt:

mount -o remount,rw /dev/hdxx 

to get back to readwrite mode.

> I don't have a clue now.

If this fails and you can still access the drive in another PC then
mv /lib/modules/ /lib/oldmodules

This will move all the modules to where the can't be found. And you should
be able to boot.
