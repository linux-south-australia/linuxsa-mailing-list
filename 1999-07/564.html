Evolution in action

With lots of you banging on the slides for last night's presentation
over the last 24 hours, I've found a bug or two in the server. 
Specifically, it's prone to dying due to SIGPIPE when you hit the STOP
button in your browser when it's part-way through sending something
to you.  Obviously this behaviour sucks, especially when you stop to 
consider the fact that anyone who is half way through viewing the 
presentation when I restart the server will be sent back to slide number
0 when they hit the "next" button :-(

I've also noticed in the logs that most of you are leaving the index
turned on (down the left-hand-side of each slide), and that must be
*VERY* annoying considering the size of the index compared to the size
of each slide.  It occurs to me that perhaps most of you don't know 
that there's a button you can click to turn the index off; perhaps you
haven't seen it because the button is at the bottom of the index, and you
need to scroll your display to see it.  I've therefore moved the button
to the TOP of the index display - Thanks Gaelyne for pointing that out.

I've just updated the ftp server with a version of the presentation server
that doesn't have the problems outlined above; Those of you who grabbed
a copy today might like to revisit the server to grab the new release.

ftp://slash.dotat.org/pub/present/present-1.1-current.tar.gz
