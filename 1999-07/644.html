> May I ask why it's running every minute? You only need to stop the deamon
> once.
It's part of a longer script that stops the daemon on a remote machine,
uploads a new copy of the database (synchronising between 2 machines)
then restarts the daemon when it's finished uploading.  I was originally
doing it every minute, but I found that every 10 minutes was more
reasonable in that if there's a lot of updates in a one minute period,
it could take a little longer than the cron job allows for.

Once again, thanks to all those helpful souls who replied, without whom
I would have been lost many times in the past couple of years.
