David Lloyd wrote:

 > "If you're running X make sure the network daemon starts at boot time".

Corollary: "If you're having trouble with a standard part of system
functionality which works for everyone else, don't get into the habit
of thinking you can `solve' it by applying fudges and hacks which
might have flow-on effects to other subsystems."

Every time you need to break-out of the "standard" system configuration
methodology (e.g.: by inserting a bogus ifconfig command for lo0), stop
and think, "Hang on... why do I need to do this?  What is it that's
broken enough to stop the standard methods from working?"
