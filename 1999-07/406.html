I believe that, no matter what other reputation I might have earned, I
am held in exceptionally high regard with respect to my computing skills.
Here's a hint: I learned by reading manuals.  Obviously I don't keep
all of the manuals in my head; I find that every time I write a socket
program I have to have the man pages for socket, inet_aton, bind and
accept in front of me.  What I got from reading the manuals was not
eidatic recall of all the return values for the yacc command, what I
gained was a "big picture".  I should say, too, that the big Unix picture
is simply gorgeous.  The real reason why I grizzle about Windows is that
Microsoft lock their big picture in a darkened vault so that nobody --
probably not even they -- can appreciate it.  Also because it's crap,
but let's not get into mutual admiration ;-)
