Re: Spot survey

My 5c worth.

I don't believe that reading all the pages over a couple of nights to be too
helpfull. By the time you have finished say the D sections then most of A
would be pushed out of the brain. As the scientist say we can only hold on
average 7 bits of information in the brain at one time.

If this is true then reading all the documentation at once is basically akin
to reading the last sections anyway.

I recommend, read what you need. If you have some spare time maybe peruse
another couple of command and USE them. Reading retension is less than doing
retension (oops sorry about the lack of any form of grammar here).

Read a page as Jakes says, yeah, usually there are bugs and caveats down the
bottom which might be missed, but don't be fooled into going where you don't
need to go.

As a home sysadmin, yeah is fun to know all the commands, but if you don't
use them everyday, you will be looking them up again anyway.

Rob.

P.S. Please feel free to disagree with anything said here.
