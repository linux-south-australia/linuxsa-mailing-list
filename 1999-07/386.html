RTFM from A to Z ..WTFiRTFM :)
Seriously, ummm ..weeeeellll, do you really want an honest answer...ummm
RTFM...hmmm OK.. I confess... As a generalisation, I only RTFM when I
can't figure something out or think the command could be useful/might do
more :)

My very first linux install was on an 40Mb hdd .. I left the man pages
out to save valuable space :)) cos I found the --help option :)) I got
my network going & X & various amateur radio apps without the aid of man
:) (took a while though!)

I suppose not a lot has changed between then & now ;) ... these days I
do the following..
If I see a command that attracts my attention cos:
1)I have never used it before
2)Dont know what it does
3)Wonder what else it can do etc
I will do a --help or  -h  Then, if I can make sense of it I just DO IT!
If I cant make sense of a command/command_option or I want to learn more
then I will do a man [command] but usually man doesnt come first!! 

I have, however, worked my way from A to Z through /bin /sbin /usr/sbin
/user/bin using MC and hitting enter on every command I wasnt familiar
with to see what happened & then did --help/man [command] if it looked
interesting :)) Does that count?! :)

I will now don my pointy hat & head for the nearest corner!!
