Re: Who's Listening?

On Sun, 4 Jul 1999, Steve Fraser wrote:

> When I do  "netstat -a" on several of my Linux machines, I see entries I dont
> expect. In particular, there are entries for Localhost ports, with numbers not
> names. This suggests to me that they aren't in /etc/services.
> 
> The entries for tcp are numbers like 687, 2049 and show as LISTEN status. The
> udp entries are numbers like 684, 2049 etc.
>...
> So, how can I identify what process is using these ports (and why)? And,
> subsequently, identify if they are a security risk.

Use the program "lsof" (list open files) which will show you which processes
have what files *and network sockets* open.  Should be available from all
the usual linux places... maybe even on your distribution CD.  On my
debain system, it's installed in /usr/sbin.
