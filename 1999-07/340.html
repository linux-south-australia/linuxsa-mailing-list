It's not just the Internet that gets bad press...
a person was hauled into court for taking &
keeping his own library of photo's of a child
in his care (in Adelaide), eventually fined, etc.

In each of the two articles, he was described
as a member of <a specific> church.

In another article, the story was told of a group
of people who were apparently responsible for
a crime of some sort (e.g. break & entry); the
article ended with "the people were described
as aboriginals"

Of course, these 3 story-bits are very inconsis-
tent with the Press Council's Code of Conduct
for newspapers... I'm not aware of any com-

each and every time it happens... AND

keep those stories & letters about the GOOD
side of the Internet flowing in the competition
of ideas... not just to your (apparently deaf) MP's,
but to editors, local "free" papers (as articles),
etc.

Internet Information Meetings (Show & Tell's)

help balance the scales...
