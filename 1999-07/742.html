Re: hardware for linux questions

Just a point - I think the software that seems to require constant
hardware upgrades always seems to be written for Microsoft Windows...
Unless you're doing particularly i/o or cpu intensive stuff, old
pentium-class PC's can do most tasks without even breaking into a
sweat..
A p200mmx with 32mb at home displayed television, and did icq, irc,
email,  ftp, played mp3s, video capture and cd ripping and conversion to
mp3.
After upgrading it to a celeron 366, the only time I notice a difference
is when actually encoding mp3s, everything else runs the same. Except
the video capture, but I believe the main factor in speeding it up was
the new harddrive being significantly faster due to ultra DMA and raid
striping, which I could have implemented on the old PC if I'd wanted to
buy more harddrives then ;)

However, a p200MMX isn't 3 years old, so maybe this isn't a fair
comparision. However, if you look at the difference between how windows
95 ran on the p200mmx compared to how it ran on a 366 celeron, it was
significant. if you compared the performance of redhat 5.0 (plus
upgrades) on the p200mmx compared to redhat 6.0 on the celeron, it's
insignificant.

The problem is - do you know what you want to do with your machine over
the next 3 years? If your needs won't change much, then upgraded
applications will probably continue to work quite well on a kick-ass
today's machine quite well then (for linux products, of course..) But if
you realise that you need to be developing full-on Virtual Reality sites
in 2 years for your company, then hopefully it won't come as a surprise
that you need to upgrade to the latest 16 processor AMD K8 or quad
processor Hexium II, so that you've got the 4 GHz of power and VR video
cards neccessary, and the 10Ghz ethernet adaptors to carry the bandwidth
to your customers.


TC
