Who's Listening?

When I do  "netstat -a" on several of my Linux machines, I see entries I dont
expect. In particular, there are entries for Localhost ports, with numbers not
names. This suggests to me that they aren't in /etc/services.

The entries for tcp are numbers like 687, 2049 and show as LISTEN status. The
udp entries are numbers like 684, 2049 etc.

I havent been able to find any way to determine what process is using these
ports. netstat -ae only tells me that they are "root", and I cant find any
reference to them in config files in /etc. Telnetting to them gives no
information (other than the fact that they really are there, listening).
Shutting down each running process in turn seems like a rather crude way of
checking them!

So, how can I identify what process is using these ports (and why)? And,
subsequently, identify if they are a security risk.

TIA

Steve
(Slackware 3.?, kernel 2.0.35, lots of modules inc amateur AX25, 486, 20MB
Ram, fairly old version of netstat)
