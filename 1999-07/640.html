Re: 2.2.x kernel, rh 6.0 & stability the Q answered!

I can testify to the kernel in RH6 being unstable RE: networking.


We have a machine here playing MP3s, and if we pummell the network (like
resizing an xterm for constantly for 30 seconds) the player will die,
and in fact,the onvoard NIC has a very new driver which caused the
machine to kernel panic. A new NIC (DEC) and an old driver (etherwork3)
makes it more stable.

Benjamin Close wrote:
> 
> Just a general comment on the 2.2.x kernel & rh6.0
> 
> Kernel
> ------
> The 2.2.x kernel still has some bugs in it. They affect the following
> areas:
>         - networking
>         - filesystem
> 
> The networking is not on the high priority list.
> The filesystem is! The reason on some highlevel 64bit machines it can kill
> the filesystem. Chances of this happening are VERY remote but it's being
> fixed as we speak.
> 
> (Aren't you glad i386/486/586/686 are 32 bit not 64!)
> 
> Networking will be fixed later.
> 
> RH6.0
> ------
> Remeber redhat 6 uses egcs as a compiler. This is a C compiler that is
> designed with optimisation for pentiums,686's,ppros and the like.
> Even without optimisation on (no -O) this compiler is still NOT endorsed
> as a stable compiler (talk about your kernel not working with egcs on
> linux-kernel list and you'll get flamed). Hence since most of RH6 has been
> compiled with egcs it isn't as stable.
> 
> I noticed this when I went from a stable 5.2 system running kernel 2.1.121
> (which became 2.2.0) to rh 6. I used the same kernel, just recompiled it
> in rh6. I noticed I was getting more sig 11 errors that day. (Overclocking
> is not the cause. My P133 has been running at 188 for 3 years fine).
> 
> So just remeber next time you complain with rh6 that you system is
> unstable it might be due to egcs.
> 
> Quote from /usr/src/linux/Documentation/Changes (kernel 2.3.11)
> ----------------------------------------------------------------
> 
> "   Note that the latest compilers (egcs, pgcc, gcc 2.8) may do Bad
> Things while compiling your kernel, particularly if absurd
> optimizations (like -O9) are used.  Caveat emptor.  Currently, the only
> C compiler available in a binary distribution is egcs.  Version 1.0.3
> seems okay; if you have to have a binary, you may be successful using
> that.  In general, however, gcc-2.7.2.3 is known to be stable, while
> egcs and others have not been as thoroughly tested yet."
>
