Re: Commentary: The Greatest Flaw In Linux

David Lloyd wrote:

> No operating system will survive a serious hard disk failure. You should
> know that.

I'll go and rip out those wasted disks doing RAID then.

Seriously folks, consider mirrored disks on your desktop machines.
For your typical 2 port IDE PC the cost is just a second disk.
Most CD-ROMs now support all the PIO modes that a hard disk does,
so there's no real performance hit in having

  ide0 (controller) --- hda (master, hard disk) --- n/c
  ide1 (controller) --- hdc (master, hard disk) --- hdd (slave, CD-ROM)

This won't save you from "rm -rf /".  You can save yourself
from most typos by archiving /etc and /home/* to tar files
on a partition which you usually leave unmounted.

That's probably adequate for home, and much cheaper than a
tape drive.
