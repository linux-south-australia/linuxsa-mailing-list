Richard Russell wrote:

>I have a new phone (end of contract, new plan, free phone (6610), etc),
>and it has, among other things, GPRS. Does this give my phone an IP
>address on the Internet? Can I do Internetty kind of things on my phone,
>or is it just WAP? I can't find much other than WAP at present, but it's
>got a lot of functionality, and I can add to this by downloading Java
>apps as well (ooer). So, any ideas, comments? Anyone got a link or a
>brief explanation as to what exactly GPRS is (at the network level
>rather than the physical or link levels)? Can I ssh from my phone?

Check 
http://www.telstra.com.au/mobilenet/network/gprs.htm#pricing_options 
first. Unless you've opted for prepaid GPRS usage, they're slugging you 
22c every time you use it, plus 2.2 cents *per kilobyte* (that's $22 per 
megabyte, makes 19c look good!). The prepaid plans have no connection 
charges.

The good news is that it's not time charged, so you don't need to rush 
too much using ssh one character at a time as you fumble through the 
10-digit 'keyboard' :) If you really want to do mobile ssh, it might be 
worth looking for a PDA that integrates neatly with your new phone.
