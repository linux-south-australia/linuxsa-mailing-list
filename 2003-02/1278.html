Quoting John Edwards <isplist@adam.com.au>:
> To list a few, although I'm still not sure how .NET is anything but SOAP

.NET is really a "2nd generation Java" - java + lessons learnt from the java 
experience.  It includes the development environment, the run-time environment, 
and just about everything Sun thought of from J2SE and J2EE (but strangely 
ignored most of J2EE's deployment stuff :P  There isn't much innovation AFAICS, 
mainly just evolution.

SOAP is just an XMLification of data and services over HTTP.  It's a subset of 
the whole web services shebang, which .NET is one end-to-end implementation.

Of course that's just my take on it all :)
