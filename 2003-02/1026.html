Re: redhat 8, 2 duplicate hdd's error on boot

David Newall writes:
> The problem is that when you duplicate your disk using DD you also copy
> the filesystem labels.  Thus mount is unable to uniquely determine which
> disk to mount, and probably chooses the wrong one.  Put /dev entries in
> place of the LABEL values in /etc/fsck and I expect it will work again.

John Edwards writes:
> In your case it sounds like both disks have the same label information,
and
> that the boot sequence does not handle the duplicate labels gracefully.
>
> Perhaps you could change fstab to look more like a traditional setup,
> using /dev/hdaX as the root partition.



I kind of suspected it was that, but wasn't really sure.... thanks guys.

I'll change FSTAB to /dev/hda* entries and see if it then plays ball. :)
