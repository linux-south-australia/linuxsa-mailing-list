stephen, an ISP counts the bytes it sends to you. full stop, end of story.
it doesn't care if the data is ASCII text or a bzip2'd ISO, it just counts
the bytes it carries for you.

link compression allows you to receive those bytes at speeds greater then
previously possible on the POTS. it doesn't actually alter the number of
bytes you send and receive. therefore, i think your point is moot.
