On Thu, 27 Feb 2003, Matthew Western wrote:
> and it works too.   they get a bounce message from your server and inside
> the bounce contains a copy of the spam.  very rude.  had us worried for a
> while as we thought we we're an open relay but all the online checks passed.

You don't have to include a copy of the original message, however
it's hard to make the decission not to.  If the email is genuine, but
couldn't be delivered for some reason, then it seems polite to return
the message to sender.  On the other hand, if the message is a forgery
then you really want to silently drop it.  How to tell the difference
is obviously an ongoing research project, for which significant Ph.D's
are in the offing, and surely a Nobel prize or two, too.

I'd suggest that Alan's approach, of vigorously maintaining accurate
anti-spam blacklists is appropriate.
