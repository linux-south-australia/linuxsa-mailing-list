> I have a feeling that it's a problem of terminology here.
>
> UML COW has:
>
> base fs (ro) + some number of rw COW mounts that are persistant.
>
>
> LVM Snapshots has:
>
> base fs (rw) + some number of historical ro snapshots.
>
> So, in both cases, you have a fixed-in-time ro copy of the fs. with
> snapshots you can have many of these. Both cases also have some sort of
> rw live filesystem over the top. with uml cow, you can have many of
> these.
>
> I suppose that in uml, reverting to the old fs is almost trivial,
> whereas with snapshots, it's not really designed for throwing away the
> changes...
