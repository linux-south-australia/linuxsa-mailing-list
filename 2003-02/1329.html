Declaring variables in for loops like above is also new in the C99
standard (Section 6.5.8.3). It is meant to have the scope of the loop.

IIRC C++ at some stage allowed declarations like above, but that the
scope continued beyond the loop. During one of the revisions of the
C++ standard it was changed to only have the scope of the loop. I
remember having to change a whole lot of code at one stage. I guess
microsoft are still catching up with their C++ standards compliance
and they share a bit too much with their C parser :-)
