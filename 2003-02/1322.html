Re: [O/T] Tape backup capacities

On Thu, 27 Feb 2003, Michael Selge wrote:
> Can anyone advise on tape capacities with regard to hardware/software
> compression.

The advertised compressed capacities are nominal, and depend entirely on
the data being written.  Highly compressable content can yield a much
higher capacity (e.g. I tried 'cat /dev/zero > /dev/tape' and two days
later it was still running.) whereas content which is already compressed,
for example GIF and JPEG files, probably will see little, if any, gain
over the raw tape size.
