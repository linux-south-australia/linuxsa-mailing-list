> Heres what FSTAB looks like:
> 
> LABEL=/                 /                       ext3    defaults        1 1
> LABEL=/boot             /boot                   ext3    defaults        1 2

Redhat 8 appears to make use of the Label functionality in mkfs, so disks are 
mounted based on their label rather than their startup position.

This is handy in the case of a disk failing, as under 'normal' use /dev/hdb 
might become /dev/hda on a reboot and cause all kinds of different headaches. I 
imagine that it's also rather handy for removable media.

In your case it sounds like both disks have the same label information, and 
that the boot sequence does not handle the duplicate labels gracefully.

Perhaps you could change fstab to look more like a traditional setup, 
using /dev/hdaX as the root partition.
