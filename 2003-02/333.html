Hmm. You'd need a rather large underground loop to use the water alone. Are you not thinking of the GeoThermal heat-Pump systems? These can use an underground, sealed, water loop that interfaces with the heat-pump via a 'water to coolant' heat exchanger. (instead of the more normal 'air to coolant')

These things are in popular use in the US and Europe, but have never caught on here, probably because the capital costs to install are higher than the standard split system, and the savings pay-back sits out at 3-5 years.

Not common in Adelaide, but there are some around, and a couple of brands on sale. There's one in our basement, and I can say that it rocks!, but the cooling is not free.
