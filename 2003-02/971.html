Re: Data security - any thoughts?

jonathan soong wrote:

> However, i can't think of a better way to do it - encrypting the data 
> isn't really much of a security measure here - it will just make it 
> slightly harder for anyone who gained access to to read the files...
>
>
> Has anyone got any other ideas on how this could be done?

Have you looked at client-side SSL certificates? That's where both the 
client and the server identify each other in the https transaction, so 
that you don't have to allow just anyone from the Internet to read the 
data. Most modern browsers support them, and it offers an extra degree 
of authentication over a username/password combination.

You could then store data on disk that has been encrypted such that the 
information presented by the client-side certificate is the key for 
decryption.
