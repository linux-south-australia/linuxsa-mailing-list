New Scientist published a short article about whether or not it was
possible to live entirely on a diet of beer, following a publicity
campaign by the Guinness people which satirically drew attention to
the nutrition you can draw from their product.

On a lark, some academic somewhere performed a chemical analysis
of Guinness, and determined that it contains lots of protein, fats,
sugars, and all kinds of other things required for sustaining life.
It was, however, deficient in Calcium, and contained almost no 
Vitamin C.

So the researcher concluded that on balance you could receive all 
the chemical compounds required for life from Guinness if you supplemented
the diet with some smallish Calcium and Vitamin C sources.  After doing
the math he decided that you'd need a daily supply of one glass of
orance juice, three glasses of milk, and 47 pints of Guinness.
