We've been looking at this around the office today:

  http://www.netcraft.com/survey/

The first chart is very interesting:  You can see that MS market share
for web servers has been in almost monotonic decline for nearly a year
There's a mirror-image in peaks and troughs between Apache and Windows
for the same period too, indicating that people have swapped-and-changed
between Windows and Apache servers over time. I'm betting that the peaks
on the red line correspond to MS service packs and new XP revisions;
the troughs would correspond to new viruses and worms. :-)

The second graph is even more interesting:  In absolute numbers, IIS
servers have been flatlined for nearly a year and a half, while Apache
has jumped ahead.  I'd be very concerned about flat sales for MS 
servers over the last 12 months if I was a Microsoft Vice President in
charge of Web Services.
