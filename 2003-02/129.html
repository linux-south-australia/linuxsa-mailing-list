Re: gzip vs bzip2

stephen white wrote:

> Link compression should take care of some of that, but ISPs have the 
> nasty habit of charging by uncompressed volume transferred, not actual 
> bytes through the wire. Just another little shaft from your local 
> ween0rz.

What makes you think that a megabyte of Internet traffic is worth less 
once it's been compressed?

The ISP still had to get it into their own network, and it's simply not 
practical to run compression on backbone links. Charging for compressed 
bytes would make it nearly impossible to perform any sort of 
differentiated accounting.

If you feel you're being hard done by with white-space in IP packets, 
perhaps look for applications that work with compression at a higher 
layer (ie: apache mod_gzip). Attempting to compress every packet past a 
point is never going to be the most efficient solution anyway.
