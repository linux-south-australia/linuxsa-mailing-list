Re: Fetchmail + Debian Sarge + Spamassassin + Procmail + Exim

On Wed, Feb 26, 2003 at 10:29:53AM +1100, Robert Thorsby wrote:
> On 2003.02.26 09:54 David Lloyd wrote:
> >I have the following setup:
> >
> > 1) fetchmail gets the mail from gw [i.e. gateway]
> > 2) exim gets the mail from fetchmail
> > 3) exim delivers via procmail
> > 4) procmail filters it through spamassassin
> > 5) eventually mail gets appended to /var/spool/mail/lloy0076
> > 6) then I get it through uw-pop
> 
> I can't help with your filtering problem. But since you may have to 
> bypass applications to isolate the problem why don't you eliminate one 
> unnecessary step (Exim) by having fetchmail deliver straight to 
> procmail.

With the "mda" procmail command. That works.

But, there are good reasons for keeping an MTA in there. An MTA has
lots of checking that usually works in your favour. But if the only
thing you want procmail for is to pass it to SA, you can do that
directly from Exim. In fact, you can do that as fetchmail is talking to
Exim using the Exim runtime SA at http://marc.merlins.org/linux/exim/ .
Portable code but Debian-compliant. You've still accepted the message
(because fetchmail already has it) but eliminating one piece does reduce
complexity.
