Although I am not the most experienced here, it looks to me as if you 
have been r00t3d. That means your server is being controlled by a 
cracker so that spam mails can be delivered.

*If* this is the case, the only thing I think you can do is reinstall 
and patch your machines as security alerts come up.

How big is your company? Do you need to accept incoming mail on that 
server instead of an ISP?

My company is small enough that I am lucky enough to not have to put my 
server directly on the Internet.

I do have a mail server that I set up mostly to learn. It is part of the 
lan, but can send outgoing mail, and it is a wee bit faster that the 
smtp server provided by the ISP. But the real answer is that it is 
probably not worth setting up for a small speed increase in send outs. 
1/10 of a second. :D

I have a firewall on my server, but I feel a heck of a lot better being 
shielded by a firmware only router in addition to the firewall.

Sorry I can't come up with a better answer, but I am sure someone will.
