Re: gzip vs bzip2

Quoting David Lloyd <lloy0076@adam.com.au>:
> I've noticed that most of the time, bzip2 is slower than gzip but makes
> smaller archives. Given the speed of machines these days, therefore,
> wouldn't it make more sense to use bzip2 rather than gzip?

Things to consider:
  o bzip2 is slower
  o bzip2 produces smaller archives (important for people with slow bandwidth)
  o bzip2 can recover corrupted archives (gzip can't)
  o gzip and bzip2 are both integrated into tar
  o zlib (gzip) is far more prevalent than libbzip2, meaning that applications 
are more likely to be gzip-aware (i.e. gimp, abiword etc)
  o bzip2 has a BSD-like license, while gzip is [L?]GPL
  o gzip is GNU, maintained by more than a single person
  o gzip is far more prevalent, available as part of cygnus (hence Win32), not 
sure about bzip2

Pros and Cons :)
