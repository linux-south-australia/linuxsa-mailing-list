What he said. Remember that if you are doing something like TCP/IP
transmissions, there are a lot of variables. If the network happenned to
be busy, there may well be a lot of retransmits, which would not only
slow the total elapsed time down significantly, it would also increase
the amount of computation needed. To reduce the dicrepancy, try timing
it for a few consecutive repeats:

time for i in `seq 1 100`; do <command>; done

Of course, this adds some extra overhead in the process startup and in
the for loop (and in the seq), but that won't matter too much when
averaged out. Alternatively, try modifying your program to send
1,000,000 packets or so... Really depends what you are trying to measure
-- the performance of a program starting, sending 1000 packets, and
quitting, or are you trying to discover the overhead in creating this
network traffic?
