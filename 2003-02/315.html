Re: gzip vs bzip2

On Thu, Feb 06, 2003 at 02:11:04PM +1030, Glen Turner wrote:
> 
> Since the bzip2/gzip tradeoff varies according to individual
> circumstance, it's considered polite to also provide .gz files
> if providing .bz2 files.
> 


Glen,

Generally with all my on-the-net software I provide gz and bz2 formats.
What I find is that significantly less than 10% of users
download the bz2 file. Maybe this is partly because people
with MS operating systems seem to never, ever download bz2 files
(in my experience). And more than 90% of people visiting my site
have MS OSs.

But when I provide only .gz and .bz2, I get complaints from people
saying they can't decompress the .gz even. 
So now I get into the habit of providing the .zip and .bz2 formats.

The problem with providing multiple compression formats is that
so many (not very bright) people just activate some browser
function to download the whole directory because they can't work
out which file they need.
(I used to always provide tar.Z, .zip, .tar.gz and .tar.bz2. Not any more!)
So then you defeat the purpose of providing the choice of formats -
because so many people download _all_ the formats.

The most famous case where providing .bz2 is really beneficial
would be downloading the linux kernel.
There it makes a difference to download burden - even though it
takes a long time to uncompress the thing.

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.
