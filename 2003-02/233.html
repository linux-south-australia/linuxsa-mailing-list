Re: bigpond are spammers?

On Thu, Feb 06, 2003 at 11:13:45AM +1030, Matthew Western said:

> non-delivery bounce message that looks like the mailbox doesn't exist.  but
> it sends back some woffle (by default, obviously you can change the wording)
> saying you naughty people stop it, or we'll report you to your ISP.    does

It really isn't a good idea, because most return addresses on Spam are used
to collect live addresses.  If your system bounces back a message their end
might be able to interpret that as detecting a valid address.

Secondly, one of the main problems with Spam is that it 'clogs up' the
Internet.  Let's say you bounce back to a spam's reply address, you are
sending out more unnecessary traffic to the wire which will most likely be
ignored anyway.

In the long run, it's more likely just going to encourage spam.
Unfortunately the best thing to do is either take real affirmative action
and contact the mail server's netblock owner, or report it to an anti-spam
group, or ignore the email completely.

In my opinion, it's best to just ignore it, but there are times when I will
do a whois on the IP address of the originating IP address and forward the
spam (all headers included) along to the Netblock owner, especially if it's
a large corporation.  I usually ignore anything based in Asian countries
because I'm afraid I'll get myself in deeper and just end up getting more
spam.
