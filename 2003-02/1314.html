Re: Fw: Re: linux from dos

On Wed, 26 Feb 2003, I wrote:
> For the benefit of those amongst us who don't read C++, the "int
> j=0" part of each for statement introduces a new identifier, j, which
> is initialised to zero.  I think the scope of that identifier is the
> scope containing the loop, and that j should continue to exist after
> the loop completes [...]
> Unfortunately I don't have an authorative C++ reference handy to look up
> the defined scope of identifiers declared within the initialiser of a for
> statement.  I did find http://www.iplusplus.com/CppRef/For_Statement.html
> however they might also be wrong.

Being dissatisfied with the one reference that I found on the web
(i.e. iplusplus), I took the time out of a truly busy schedule to pop into
a bookshop today, to see what Bjarne Stroustrop had to say on the matter.
In his book, "The C++ Programming Language," Stroustrop specifically
says that an identifier defined in the initialiser of a for statement
is only in scope within the for statement.
