6 cyberproxy.com mails per minute!!

I'm really preplexed by what I'm seeing in my sendmail log file.
It's not pretty; so I'll append it to this message.

ABout 6 times per minute, I'm getting e-mail attempts from various parts
of the world, all pretending to be from checker@cyberproxy.com.
They sort of come in storms. They go non-stop for about 30 minutes
and then stop for hours.
But they seem to be synchronized from various real source IP addresses.
I reject all of them with STMP status code 550.
And yet in every storm, they keep coming back, usually the same
source IP addresses keep trying.

My questions are these:

1. Is there anything I can do (e.g. by using an appropriate STMP status code)
to stop these e-mail attempts?

2. Are they getting anything which is valuable to them? E.g. is there some
sort of relay effect going on here whereby I'm sending something
on (like spam) to innocent victims or something?

3. Has anyone else seen this effect?

Cheers,
Alan Kennington.
