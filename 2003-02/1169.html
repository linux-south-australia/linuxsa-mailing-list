RE: Public Housing (CNN.com - Low-income housing goes wireless - Feb. 24, 2003)

It isn't so much that public hosing is placed in ghettos, it's that they
become ghettos.

South Australia has the highest proportion of public hosting per capita
in the Country.

The South Australia Housing Trust (and others) have learnt a lot about
Public Housing in the last 50 years, specifically limiting the density
of public housing within areas (specifically to avoid another Elizabeth)

I'd prefer to see public housing which is built on a needs basis, I
can't imagine Wireless is on the top of the list for people seeking such
accommodation.

Innovation in design (including taking into account the aspect of the
property), energy efficency, access for people with disabilities,
variability in location, density of construction, placement of
properties based on access to local shops, schools, transport, libraries
would all take precedence over Wireless. (This also includes
consultation with LGA's and other Government departments on long term
strategies to manage other resources including Hospitals etc)

Matt
