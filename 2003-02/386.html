Linux/CSDN, burning CDs and the CD tax

There is a lot of fuss going on about piracy and a tax/royalty on blank CDs to return funds to the artists and recording companies.

IMHO it is nothing but a money-grabbing exercise but it could push the price up for legitimate Linux and CSDN activities. Yes the proposal says you can claim the royalties back every 6 months but to me this is all so much bullshit. You have to list all the things you have burned onto CDS and then get it all Stat Dec'd.

We are in almost all cases burning CDs with software that is legal to burn, distributions, community software such as OpenOffice etc. Why should our legitimate activities be considered as piracy activities until we jump through bureaucratic hoops just to prove we are legit and can get our money back?

I think this is something that Linux Australia and AUUG etc should be speaking up about. The Linux/OpenSource world doesn't have to pirate software, we get it legally and pass it on legally.

Cheers,  Ian
