RE: [O/T] Tape backup capacities

The main consideration is what sort of data you are storing. If it's
already compressed (or a large portion of it is), then you will have
little compression on tape (hardware vs software doesn't matter).
Similarly, if it's mostly random, unstructured data, you won't get much
compression (try `dd if=/dev/random of=/tmp/testfile bs=1k count=100`
and then try compressing testfile with whatever you want, and you won't
get much compression). If it's text, or highly structured data with
significant repetition, you'll get a lot of compression (try `dd
if=/dev/zero of=/tmp/testfile bs=1k count=100` and then compress
that...) ...

So, take a look at what's on your filesystem. Bear in mind that most
media formats (GIF, JPG, PNG, MOV, AVI, MPG, MP3, etc) are pretty
compressed already. Similarly with many software distribution packages
(.rpm, .deb, .cab etc)...
