Re: gzip vs bzip2

stephen white wrote:
> On Wednesday, February 5, 2003, at 09:39 AM, David Lloyd wrote:
> 
>> But what about sending files over email, downloads and so forth?
> 
> Link compression should take care of some of that, but ISPs have the 
> nasty habit of charging by uncompressed volume transferred, not actual 
> bytes through the wire. Just another little shaft from your local ween0rz.
> 

I think the problem with that is three-fold:

- You shouldn't be sending large archives via email.
- Link compression is never as good as pre-compression using bzip2 or gzip
- Relying solely on the transmission mechanism for compressing data is 
terribly wasteful as each node which buffers or spools the data needs to 
both uncompress it upon recieving it and recompress it upon retransmission.

I think the other thing to note about bzip2 is that (IIRC) it can take 
much, much longer to compressing data at the maximum compression rates, 
compared to gzip. However unbzipping is usually quite snappy. This works 
well for software distribution as you typically only compress the 
archive once but people download and uncompress it many times.

It makes a lot of sense to compress non-trivial archives for 
transmission across the 'Net, prior to actually doing so. The better the 
compression algorithim, the better the result.
