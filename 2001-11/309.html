ntfs, and 2.4.14 with ext3

howdy all,

i managed to get ny ntfs-read-supporting-2.4.9-7 kernel to compile and
install properly on redhat 7.2 - my first ever successful kernel
compile! very exciting. anyway. the issue now is, the ntfs support
sucks. i can mount any of my ntfs partitions, but trying to do anything
on them (such as 'ls') will often freeze the computer totally - no
keyboard input accepted at all (or mouse). a hard reset is the only fix
:( it's a tiny bit weird too - 'mount /mnt/c' is fine, 'ls /mnt/c' is
fine, but 'ls /mnt/c/WINNT' crashes the box (reliably). is this just
because ntfs support isn't the best? or is there some other issue
perhaps?

working with ext3? i have little idea about this stuff, but i downloaded
the latest 2.4.14 from kernel.org and unzipped it. doing 'make

stopped. richard sharpe, i remember you saying "after a marathon session

to vfat under win2k. fun.
