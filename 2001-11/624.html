If you have the original Nimda, your hard drive will have a _large_ quantity
of .eml
and .nws files scattered all over it.
http://www.antivirus.com/vinfo/virusencyclo/default5.asp?VName=PE_NIMDA.E

This is of course presuming that you have your local drive shared (very 
bad practice on internet connected PCs I might add).

The second incarnation of Nimda is a little different, info is here:
http://www.antivirus.com/vinfo/virusencyclo/default5.asp?VName=PE_NIMDA.A-O

Trend Micro have an online scanner you can use:
http://www.antivirus.com/vinfo/security/nimda_form/

If you don't want to use the online one (understandable), download their
free 
anti-nimda tool:
http://www.antivirus.com/vinfo/security/nimda_form/
