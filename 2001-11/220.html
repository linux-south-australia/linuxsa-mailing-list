Re: Unsetting variables in Python

Andrew Pullin wrote:

>     Why do you need to "unset" a variable? In most other
> languages that I have programmed in, if you need to "unset"
> a variable then you usually give it a null value, ie stringv
> = "".

Under Python, most likely for reasons of orthogonality.  Namespaces
are implemented using dictionaries, and they have a standard access
arrangement of get, set and delete.  Other than the reason I
mention before (forcing garbage collection), I haven't had a real
need for it.
