The usual developer response to such as question is:
please upgrade to the latest version.

This page lists a serious bug in said version which you should be aware
of:
http://kmail.kde.org/bug.html

If it's at all possible, I would suggest upgrading, as the KMail that
ships with KDE 2.2.x (KMail 1.3.x) is very nice.  It fixes many bugs,
introduces IMAP support, and I think it has some enhanced filtering and
message flagging.  There's also some encryption features that are in
this version or will be in the next version.

On the other hand, you may be unwilling or unable to upgrade.
