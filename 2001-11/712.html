Re: Routing - Best Practices

Dale Long wrote:
> 
> On Fri, 23 Nov 2001, John Edwards wrote:
> > If you have to add static routes often enough that you consider it
> > 'painful' then maybe you should consider a routing protocol like RIP or
> > OSPF. Zebra or Gated will do the job on linux, and most routers will
> > support something to propogate information.
> 
> On this topic, does Linux have any elegant solution to provide redundant
> network card functionality? For example, the main NIC fails, the secondary
> NIC takes on the ip details and MAC address of the failed card
> automatically.

There are a few linux high-availability projects that do this sort of
thing.

Another way you could do potentially do it is is by binding your
services to an IP address on the loopback interface, and then using
routing or bridging protocols to make that address visible to the rest
of your network.
