> If a Microwave uses 2.4GHz spectrum, and it cooks our food, what
> will the 5GHz 802.11a do to us?

Nothing much.  802.11b also uses 2.4 GHz.  We recently did some
consideration of this matter:

1.  In a 1000 MW microwave oven, it takes 25 minutes to cook an
    average size cat.

2.  The average 802.11b card generates 100 mW of energy.  Assuming you
    can keep the cat still long enough, and at the same time avoid
    heat losses, it would take 6 months to cook the cat.  We came to
    no conclusions about whether bacteria might not have eaten the cat
    luke-warm before this happened.
