You gotta remember a couple of things - evolution is in feature freeze right 
now (ie release candidate 2 is out) awaiting the fabled 1.0 release.  Right now 
the developers are probably very tired of late nights and sick of chasing down 
the hard to find bugs.  It's probably not the right time to say their hard work 
sucks because it doesn't work seamlessly with exchange.

As the evolution hacker mentions, you or anyone else is free to reverse engineer 
the undocumented exchange protocol.  Once that's done it's relatively simple[1] 
to implement it.

This is an opportunity for fame and glory.  Want to be the next 'Tridge'?  
Then reverse engineer the calendar protocol in exchange and publish a reference 
implementation.

Of course Ximian/Red Hat/Someone else could pay someone to do it, but only if 
they can see a profitable business case to do it, since it'd be quite a bit of 
work.
