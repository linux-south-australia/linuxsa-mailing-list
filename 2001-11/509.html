the problem with these sort of comparisons is they never
really work for home users.  Either people get the OS and
other odds and ends bundled with a new PC so that it kind
of "feels" free (which might hopefully change with the
XP licensing pay-by-the-month thing), or they ripped off
a copy from a friend or from work or ... etc.
I don't know anyone who went out to a shop and paid the
full price for M$ stuff, except for companies of course.

And this is the problem.  While it feels good from a
"subverting and ripping off M$ for a change" point of
view, in reality by assisting/giving someone a pirate
copy of windoze we are helping M$ maintain their
dominance - if everyone had to pay full price for windoze
and office, how many people would go seek alternatives?

Therefore I believe it is your duty as a linux user to
dob in anyone using pirate, or helping to pirate, M$ stuff.
