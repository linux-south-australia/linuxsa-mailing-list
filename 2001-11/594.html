Re: Naming Conventions (again)

Michael Wardle wrote:

> Also, some companies are going with firstname.lastname or similar.  Is
> there anything wrong with using periods/dots in usernames?  How does
> BIND handle it if the administrator is my.name.server.com?

BIND doesn't care, since it never sends email to the contact in the SOA
record, it just stores a string there.

> > What good reason is there for standard character length usernames???

Do you make backups of mailboxes onto a DOS filesystem somewhere? :)

RFC822 doesn't specify a maximum length for an email address, but does
suggest that mail headers should not be longer than 72 characters to
ensure that headers are readable.
