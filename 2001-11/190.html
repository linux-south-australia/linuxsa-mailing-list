Debian bugsquash partay

this is an email from Conrad Parker on the Debian-au list... for those
into Debian... Perhaps if people are super-keen, a parallel event
could be called (at a house or someone's business premises) at the
last minute for a few people... 

------


Hi all, debheads,

Apparently there's a global debian bugsquashing party this weekend.
It's
a bit of a free-for-all, coordinated via IRC. However if you're in
Sydney
and you'd like to get together and participate with help and jostling
from others in a real room, here's your chance:

  UNSW Compsoc has arranged a venue for Sydney's Debian developer
  community to participate IRL in Bug-Squashing Party #6 [0].

  This event will be held in the seminar room of the School of
Computer
  Science and Engineering, Building K17, UNSW Kensington campus,
during
  the following hours:
  
        10am - 10pm  Sat Nov 10 2001
        10am - 10pm  Sun Nov 11 2001
  
  It is open to both Debian developers and non-developers.

  [0]
http://lists.debian.org/debian-devel-announce/2001/debian-devel-announce-200111/msg00000.html

So, it should be a whole lot of fun. Even if you're not a Debian
developer,
fixing bugs and testing software is a great way to learn about free
software development. If you're a coder, you can benefit from the
karma
of fixing some of the bugs in other people's programs ;-)

see you there,

Conrad.
 -> a bugfix a day keeps the daemons away.
