    about the Massachusets Institute of Technology MediaLab



    on



     Thursday, November 8th, at 11.10 am,



     in



      Flentje Lecture Theatre, Plaza Building.

    University of Adelaide



       Title:

    Research and Playful Discovery in the MIT Media Lab



    Speaker:

    Dr Barry Vercoe

          Professor of Media Arts & Sciences, MIT

             Founding Member, MIT Media Lab

           Assoc Academic Head, MIT Media Lab

                 http://sound.media.mit.edu/~bv/



    ABSTRACT



    Since its opening in 1985 the MIT Media Lab has been about people,

    computation, and quality of life in a digital age.  It focuses on the study,

    invention, and creative use of enabling technologies for learning and

    expression by people and machines. The Lab is unique at MIT in comprising

    both a degree-granting academic program and a world renowned research

    program, where 30 faculty supervise the work and play of 175 master's and

    PhD students, and where 90% of its support comes from 150 corporate sponsors

    spread around the world.



    This will be an illustrated lecture about the various research groups within

    the Lab, about the research they do, and the style in which they do it.  It

    will look at the work of some groups in depth, at how all the groups and all

    the disparate disciplines are woven into a single academic and research

    program, and how this attracts 250 MIT undergraduates each year to do much

    of their learning here too.  The talk will close with examples of how this

    style of learning and discovery is currently being duplicated in other Media

    Labs in both Europe and Asia.



    All interested are welcome.
