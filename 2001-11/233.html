[OT] External ATX PSUs

Hi...

I was thinking about PSUs, particularly in regard to noise, heat and
case packaging (as you do), and I had a thought -- why not mount the
PSU _outside_ the case, having some sort of plug to connect it to the
case, similar to those found on SunRays and the like. The three
advantages to this approach would be noise (PSU can be further away,
and there are other options for cooling), heat (heat from PSU won't
affect case temperature, so PSU can be made to run hotter), and
packaging (you can have a nicer looking, smaller case)...

Why isn't this done? Is there a reason I don't know about? Perhaps
your average 250W PSU can't be effectively cooled without big fans
anyway, and would end up melting if it were packaged like a laptop
PSU... What if you used less power-hungry components, and could get
the consumption down to 150W? Does anyone know of any practical limits
on the wattage of external PSUs?
