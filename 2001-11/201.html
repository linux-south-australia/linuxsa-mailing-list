> I presume when burning the image file is expanded into normal files onto the
> CDROM which is them readable as normal -no?

ummm... no

An ISO is nothing more and nothing less than an exact bit-for-bit
copy of the contents of the CDROM. When you are burning it, you are
simply burning those bits onto the CDROM as they appear in the file.
No "expanding" takes place. You can actually mount an ISO image as a
loopback filesystem if you wish (because it is identical to what you
find on a CDROM).

Note that the only reason you see files on the CDROM but just one big
file as an ISO image is that when you mount the CDROM (or when you
loopback mount the image), you are telling the computer to interpret
that sequence of bits as an ISO9660 filesystem.
