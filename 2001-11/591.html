RE: Naming Conventions (again)

Hi Patrick.

Patrick Browne wrote:

[Email variations shortened]

FWIW, just set a convention and stick to it.  Some larger orgs.
also use:

1)  Firstname.Lastname@domain
2)  Lastname.Firstname@domain
3)  Variations on '.' (eg, '_')

There's a sendmail site that I look after that does all permutations
of the above, plus Firstletter.Lastname, FirstletterLastname (sans
'.'), and aliases (eg, all roberts map to bob).  But they are an
exception, rather than a rule (i.e., when you can't decide on one
policy, pick all of them).

> Do some MTA's barf at really long names?

Only the broken ones.  I can't remember what the RFC mandates as
a maximum, but your Greek friend should be way under.

> What good reason is there for standard character length usernames???

Old Unix convention - used to be an 8 character limit on usernames
(still is on some Unices).  But this opens another can of worms.
Basically, if your email account users don't have Unix accounts
(ie, authentication/authorisation data is kept elsewhere, eg LDAP)
then this shouldn't be a concern.
