Steve,

Exactly the point of my article
(http://www.acnsnet.com/~slydder/stories/op/storiesView/sid/47/).  I have
even added some more information since the last time I posted about it here.
Including the possible use of CIPE, which I will be testing as soon as I can
get the wifi cards back from a friend, which uses encrypted UDP instead of
encrypted TCP as in IPsec.

The point of using SSH across an already working IPsec/VPN connection was
due to the possibility of the encryption being busted in the first place (
as far as I have been informed ).  Although I have yet to experiment with
this type of connection I hope to do so in the near future.

If anyone has any actual cases of IPsec being busted and/or the benefits of
using SSH or some other method to further secure a connection I would be
very pleased to hear of it.
