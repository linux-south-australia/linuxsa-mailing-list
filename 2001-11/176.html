No they won't - the pollies shouldn't even be considering such micro

There is a real issue with support though.  Once upon a time, I worked as 
a Desktop Support Operator in a Windows NT environment.  In this 
environment, there were these stupid automatic gate thingies that were 
running DOS.  I could never get PC Anywhere for DOS working properly so 
whenever there was a problem, I had to jump into my car and go and have a 
look.  Now, my suggestion was to port the C program that was running on 
these gates to Linux.  They considered this especially when coupled with 
the fact that Tuxedo wasn't supported on DOS anymore and it was supported 
on Linux.  However, the biggest issue was still support.  I managed to 
convince them that it wasn't a big an issue as they thought.  Afterall, 
out of the DSOs that we had, half of them preferred Linux (or Solaris) 
anyhow.  Well, it all fell in a big hole when I left and all the other 
DSOs that preferred Linux left as well *sigh*.  This is the problem with 
Open Source Software.  Management don't know where to get the support 
unless it's sitting under their noses.  They don't (generally) make the 
sensible decisions that their IT Departments want... they make decisions 
based on their own experiences!  They use Windoze or they have one app 
that can't be replaced easily that uses a closed source application so 
that's the way they go *sigh*.  Is it political?  Yes.  Does it have a 
place on this list?  Absolutely.  Do we approach this problem by talking 
to political parties? Nah... that's just senseless - if the IT Department 
can't support it, it would be suicide.

Explaining that if we find a bug in Open Source Software, we don't have to 
pay nearly $300 to be told that it's not going to be fixed until the next 
release?  Well, managers understand that...
