 > The only thing that I don't know is if they can be used in Australia. The
 > frequency range at 2.4Hz allows them to be used in just about every other

2.4 GHz is also the legal range for .au

One thing to keep in mind, though, is that 802.11b isn't the only
user of the 2.4 GHz band.  More specifically, there are some brands
of digital cordless phones which use the same frequency range.

Ideally spread-spectrum applications are supposed to hop around 
each other so that multiple devices can share the same spectrum, but
in practice this doesn't always work correctly.  I've spoken to a 
couple of people with cordless phones who find that their WaveLAN 
home networks stop working whenever they make a telephone call (!)
