Re: Eu passes directive *against* software patents

On Thu, Sep 25, 2003 at 04:01:33PM +0930, Peter Hutterer wrote:

> so a quote (orig. in german):
> IT-inventions shall only be valid for a patent, when they provide a 
> technical contribution - such as computer-aided brakes.

I saw that. And I think it is reasonable. The point is, if any physical
device is patentable, then a physical device with a computer stuck in it
should be patentable too. However something which is "pure software" as
it is put cannot be patented. As the second article I linked said, the
mere fact a piece of software makes electrons move and disc heads move
etc does not constitute it somewhich which has a "physical effect". So I
don't think what you observe is in fact a problem.

This has lots of problems for the future as firmware and hardware and
not-quite-eitherware develop. And where does configurable computing fit,
where the computer often doesn't actually exist until the problem has
been defined by another computer? And so forth. But nevertheless it lets
ABS be patentable and CIFS not. Hooray.
