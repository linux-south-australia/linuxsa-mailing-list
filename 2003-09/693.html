Romana,

Since you don't have control of the destination mail server, I guess there's
not too much you can except filter out stuff after arrival.
But if timelady.com resolved via MX to your always-on-the-internet
home machine in your lounge room, you could just iptables these things
away or bounce them in some other way.

I'm always surprised by how few people have their mail server at home.
I can see advantages in the client-server approach, but there are advantages
in the mail server being on your own machine too.

Cheers,
ak.
