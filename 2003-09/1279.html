Another alternative is to even look at a OpenBSD box to do your packet shaping
- altq/pf leave any other offering in Linux dead, unfortunately (I've never
got completely reliable shaping working with iproute correctly; for simple
stuff, it's fine, but throw more than a few rules in for several subnetworks
[especially with lower bandwidth stuff] and it seems to choke).

You can even do it completely transparently; you configure the OpenBSD to
bridge both network cards (so it doesn't even have an IP address on your
network), do packet shaping on the traffic going through it, physically place
it in the correct location, and things will happen without anybody even
knowing about it.
