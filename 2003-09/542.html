Re: Linux, ADSL, TCP, Window Sizes, download times

On Wed, 2003-09-17 at 03:59, Chris wrote:
> It's like this.  1500 down 256 up.  I don't want to go to some website
> and start downloading a file expecting to get 1500 when all I would
> really get is 256 because TCP would rate limit me(flow control) because
> of mp upload speed.

It doesn't work like that. That is why 56k modems, and satellite
download links with very different download/upload speeds work.

The upload speed needs to be around 10%-15% of the download speed. 256
should rarely be the limiting factor when downloading, being ~17% of the
capacity of the download link.

For example, a download that saturates my 64k inbound, will only use
about 15% of the outbound capacity.    :-( I cant get ADSL.
