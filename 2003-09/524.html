Re: SCO CEO Posts Open Letter to the Open Source Community

On Saturday, 13 September 2003 at 16:29:26 +0930, David Newall wrote:
> Ian Loxton <snnn@airnet.com.au> wrote:
>> I didn't reject his comments because of his attacks on Linux. I rejected
>> them because they were without substance and mostly hyperbole. Did you ask
>> Romana why she rejected his statements or are you only responding to me
>> because I felt your stance was misled?
>
> Romana, like you, missed the point, which was not that IBM stole from
> SCO, but that the open source community needs to be careful about
> how it obtains its works.  Even quite innocent actions can be wrong.
> For example, at the AUUG conference SCO showed an email from one IBM
> engineer to another explaining, very briefly, how he had solved a problem
> in AIX. 

A number of people were concerned about this.  I don't know why.
First, it has nothing to do with SCO, and secondly the content was a
brief summary of basic SMP principles, something that I knew in my
very first job, a little over 30 years ago, and which is described in
much more detail in books such as Schimmel and Vahalia.

> SCO claim that IBM's UNIX contract assigns all derivative works back
> to SCO, and that therefore that idea is owned by SCO.

They claim lots of unsubstantiated things.  If you look at the IBM
countersuit, you'll see the voice of reason.

> The point is not that IBM's UNIX derivations belong to SCO, rather
> that one IBM engineer, having had access to AIX source (which
> undeniably is a UNIX derivative) gave a hint, in all good faith and
> probably in ignorance of the implication, to someone working on
> Linux.  It's could be that easy to violate SCO's rights.

No, this is a red herring.  It shows SCOs (and apparently your)
misunderstanding of the material.  If SCO claims that they invented
the computer, do all hardware vendors have to get worried?
