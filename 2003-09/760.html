Re: [OT] IBM "boot linux faster"

At 2003/9/19 11:02+0930  David Lloyd writes:
> Windows takes just as long, it's just that you don't get to see all the boot
> processes.
> 
> If one were to replace the startup scripts with a nice progress bar (use the
> linux progress patch and such) then lusers^WWindows Users wouldn't notice
> the difference.

I believe there are patches around that can do this (for the whole
boot process). "Perceived time" is important - there were a lot less
complaints about the speed of e2fsck after a progress indicator was
added.

However, boot times are important for many people eg large machines,
or consumer devices (how long does your vcr take to boot?).  Richard
Gooch has had a parallel start service implementation for a while -
IIRC dependencies are specified by comments in the startup
scripts. However, service startup time is just one of a few areas
which could be sped up.
