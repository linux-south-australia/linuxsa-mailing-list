[ANNOUNCE] Reminder - LinuxSA September 2003 Meeting

Hi all,

Time for the LinuxSA September Meeting reminder (it's tonight!).

Here are the usual details:

  When:   7:00pm (doors open 6:45pm) on
          Tuesday, 16th September, 2003
  Where:  2nd Floor
          Compaq Computer
          139 Frome Street
          Adelaide SA
  Cost:   FREE
  Who:    Anyone and everyone.
          No pre-registration necessary.

Presentation:

  Glen Turner will be talking about "TCP performance and the Web100
  project".

  TCP is the transport protocol which carries almost all of the
  traffic on the Internet.  With Gigabit Ethernet becoming widely used
  we are starting to reach fundamental limits to the performance of
  the TCP protocol.

  The talk will discuss:

  - The factors which limit TCP performance.

    These factors make TCP performance worse in Australia than in most
    other countries.

  - Network design changes to avoid web performance in Australia
    becoming many times worse than web performance in the USA.

  - Steps which can be taken by users to increase the limits on TCP
    performance.  Including:

    - new purchasing criteria for PCs and network equipment.

    - tuning the Linux kernel's TCP implementation and other wizard
      stuff.

  - The Web100 project -- automating TCP tuning wizardry for the
    masses

    - demonstration of Web100 tools on Linux.

    - exploration of Web100 implementation in the Linux kernel.

  Glen Turner is a network engineer with the Australian Academic and
  Research Network.  He is a contributor to the Web100 Project and an
  advocate for larger MTU sizes.

Extras:

  installation, feel free to bring your machine.  Make sure to bring
  everything necessary to make your computer work (eg. monitor,
  keyboard, etc).
