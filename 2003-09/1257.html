On Mon, Sep 29, 2003 at 12:40:24PM +0930, Ben Williams wrote:

 > > I don't see /why/ you need drivers?
 > 
 > Things like:
 >   - multimedia buttons (8 on my current keyboard) and alternate 
 > navigation buttons (there's 10 on my current keyboard, plus a scroll 
 > wheel on the side of it that i use constantly now)

The fact that your X server doesn't have those buttons mapped to 
any functions doesn't mean that it doesn't know you've
pressed them.

 >   - making X to realise that there's more than 101 keys on standard PC 
 > keyboards nowadays.

/usr/X11R6/lib/X11/XKeysymDB

 >   - supporting extra buttons on mice (a lot of higher-end mice have more 
 > than 3 buttons now).

Five buttons and a scrollwheel on mine, and they all work in X.

I think you're overrating the importance of drivers for "Generic"
devices.  I've never quite understood why MS OS's need drivers for
modems, keyboards, mouses, USB and firewire mass storage devices, etc,
given that all of those devices have pretty clear-cut standard
functionality with well-defined standardized APIs.  

Perhaps it's a branding thing:  The manufacturer of your Firewire
hard disk drive would rather it come up on your desktop as "Seagate
Firewire hard disk" than "Firewire hard disk" (or "Hard disk")
