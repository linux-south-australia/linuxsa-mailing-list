i think your a little confused.

if i'm downloading a 100MB file, the packets coming in bring 100MB worth of
data (plus some overhead for packet headers). the packets going out are
very small with the data consisting of the next expected TCP sequence
number.

(if that still isn't clear, have a read of
http://www.cisco.com/univercd/cc/td/doc/cisintwk/ito_doc/ip.htm)

hence running at 1500/256 you will still receive packets at up to 1500 even
if what you are sending in reply is equal to or less than 256.
