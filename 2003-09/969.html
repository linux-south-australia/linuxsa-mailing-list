Pete,

Everyone else will chip in, if course. But basically...

Verisign controls the root servers, which are the resource where
the .net, .org, .com, .everything are stored and accessed by all the
computers in the world.

But the control of all but .com and .net has been delegated to
others for retailing.

The root servers are like the resource.
Verisign is the wholesaler for .com and .net names.

The registrars are the retailers for .com, .net, .everything
So when you rent a .com name from a registrar, they pay a cut of the action
to the wholesaler Verisign. Confusingly, Verisign is also a retailer for
.com and .net.

If you buy a .org, the "cut of the action" goes to a wholesaler other than
Verisign. I don't remember who.

You can't get a .edu or .gov.
