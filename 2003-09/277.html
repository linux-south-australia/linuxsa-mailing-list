There are several reasons why the software is the way it is:

Firstly, we have to make our own cars. :) No company alone, except for 
Microsoft, has the resources to build a car the way most people want it. 
However, if a community is able to work as a group and partner together, it 
has a chance to build a car just like the one you want. 

Secondly, the community is working together as a group to break Microsoft's 
monopoly, as we are the last hope to do so. We need everybody's help because, 
as you know, a lot of complicated issues go into enabling a driver to merely 
turn a key to start his car.

Collaboration helps pay for our car, but we need people to learn about how the 
car works to help us.  Just like building a car, developing and maintaining 
an operating system, is a very complicated task, and our experience has shown 
us that it takes a certain amount of stubborness and curiosity to be an 
effective helper. 

In addition to collaboration, an understanding of the system allows people to 
actively participate in the community by forming solutions to problems that 
arise, which makes the software more useful for everybody.

Thirdly, we sometimes have to build our own pieces of the car, so we have to 
leave certain parts of our projects unfinished such as not to prevent other 
parts of the car from being developed.
