Re: Web100 will clobber VoIP? Was Re: [ANNOUNCE] LinuxSA September 2003 Meeting

> On the face of it, unless you can tell me something contrary, it looks like
> Web100 will clobber VoIP.

On the contrary, if the web100 guys get their wish of higher MTU's on the
greater Internet, then routers will potentially be able to carry 6x more
traffic with the same CPU power. At 100Mb/s, the serialisation delay of these
larger packets is negligable, and reduced CPU usage on routers will possibly
result in reduced jitter and packet loss for VoIP.

Of course, it may also mean that people can run a lot more bandwidth through
cheaper routers and put off upgrades longer resulting in greater vulnerability
to DDoS floods and other abnormal conditions, but those sorts of networks don't
tend to be around for long.
