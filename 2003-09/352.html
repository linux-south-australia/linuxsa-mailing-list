Re: [ANNOUNCE] LinuxSA September 2003 Meeting

On Wed, Sep 10, 2003 at 04:12:58AM +0930, Alan Kennington wrote:

 > I would like to suggest (perhaps a little provocatively, but in all
 > seriousness) that tuning up TCP stacks to do 100 Mbps on the open
 > Internet is about as socially responsible as optimising spam software
 > to send 10 times faster.

<sarcasm>
Yeah, I think you're right, Alan.  I think we should definitely make sure
that as technology and capitalization improves, and the Internet evolves
into something based on 10 or 100 or, let's face it, 1000 gigabit per
second wide-area bearers, we should make sure that the users of the 
network are constrained to the same throughput rates they were able to
achieve when the standards were laid down in the 1970s.
</sarcasm>

If we'd been using IMP protocols for the last 30 years, and the world's
first implementation of TCP/IP was going to be launched today on a 
10 Gbit/sec link between Adelaide Uni and Melbourne Uni, what kind of
throughputs do you think the users of that network would expect, and
what kind of expectations would be carried through to the eventual
commercial users of that network as it evolved and grew?
