well let's see:
according to the following article
http://www.heise.de/newsticker/data/jk-24.09.03-000/
the following bits are interesting:
(heise is one of the most trustworthing media agencies in germany)

"In der Schlussabstimmung am heutigen Mittwoch in Straßburg votierten 
364 Abgeordnete für deutliche Revisionen an dem Konstrukt und nur 153 
dagegen."
In the final poll on Wednesday in Strassbourgh 364 delegates voted for 
_significant changes in the directive_ and only 153 against it.
(Note: they only voted about having changes, not about passing the whole 
thing)

"Schutzansprüche auf reine Programme, die in einen Computer oder ein 
Netzwerk geladen werden, soll es nicht geben."
There is no right to get a patent for pure software which can be loaded 
into a computer or network.

"Die revidierte Richtlinie muss nun aber noch der Europäische Rat für 
gut befinden, bevor sie das Parlament in 2. Lesung verabschieden kann."
The changed directive has to be accepted by the Council of the EU before 
the law can be passed by the parliament.

that means that at the moment everything can be changed again...

the rest of the article is mainly about which political party claimed 
that it was their credit that theses changes were made.

i hope i got the translation at least partly correct...
