Re: Banks vs. Browsers

Taylor, Corey (AHA) wrote:
> The names 'David' and 'Goliath' come readily to mind.

I wouldn't be too quick to diss the person.

It's likely that the bank has no idea how accessible
its website is.  It's simply paid people to design
it and the result looked good.

This sort of bad PR means that the next contract will
give "HTML standards compliance" a mention.

There are people at banks in charge of winning new
customers.  They spend massive amounts of cash.
You shouldn't underestimate how pissed they get
when non-strategic actions make them loose those
hard-won customers.

The problem is that those people currently know
a lot about physical banking facilities (they'd
never allow a branch with orange walls) but
currently know bugger-all about web banking
facilities.

The bad PR might inspire them to learn.
