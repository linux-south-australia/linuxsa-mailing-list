[OT] Virus Scanning from CD?

Hi,

What do people here use for scanning/repairing viruses on a Windows box, but
from a boot medium?  That is, independent of the OS?

I used to use Vet's bootable Linux rescue disks, but modified them to all fit
onto a bootable CD; when I wanted to update the virus definition files, I'd
just reburn the CD but with the one file updated.

Thing is, they're up to VET 10.5 or something now, and the Linux bootable
floppies haven't been updated in a long time; neither has their DOS RESCUE
tool, strangely.  It's almost assumed that everybody scans for viruses on
infected machines within Windows these days, which I sure hope isn't true.

I then thought I could use good old Norton Antivirus to make a Rescue disk,
but it seems they've completely failed to put this functionality into NT/2K/XP
installs.

What do people here use/recommend?  Cost isn't an issue, I just want something
that works; I've seen talks of modifying a Knoppix cd to put F-Prot or another
free scanner on it, but I'm not entirely convinced how effective scanners like
these are.
