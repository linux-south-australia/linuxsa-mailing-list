Ximian Evolution would be what you're after as an Outlook clone. It comes
with pretty much any Linux distro you could think of (Red Hat, Mandrake,
etc.).
 
As for book-keeping, GnuCash maybe? I'm not an accountant so I'm not sure.
Either way, it's been on the front of most Linux magazines for a while now.

Subject: Linux newbie looking to migrate to this OS...



Hi everyone,
 
  I am a Linux newbie so be nice ;-)
 
  We are looking at doing a full migration to Linux from Microsoft's Windows
XP. Over the years we have briefly looked at different distributions from
Mandrake, Corel & Caldera. What we want to do now is completely switch over
to Linux permanently without the dual-boot safety net that has seen us
return to Microsoft on so many occasions in the past. What I need to do
prior to the migration is enusre that certain areas of software are covered
by reliable, stable Linux apps. We already have some areas of software
covered (for instance Open Office instead of Office XP, Konqueror instead of
IE6 and one of many different e-mail handlers instead of outlook express).
 
  Is there a Linux equivalent of Microsoft Outlook? We use the calendar &
task features of it for booking appointments. One other area we need to
address is our accounting package. Currently we use Intuit's Quickbooks Pro.
If there is some form of Linux app that can do this job of bookkeeping
including GST tracking? If so then let the migration begin ;-)
 
  Thankyou to all who have taken time to read this message and bigger thanks
in advance to those who post any helpful hints or suggestions.
 
  Andy.
