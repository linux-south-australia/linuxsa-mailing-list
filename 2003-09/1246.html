Re: SMTP callouts for email really work

On Sat, Sep 27, 2003 at 12:18:17PM -0400, Anithen wrote:
 
> My .mailfilterrc file consists of rules that delete mail that is not 
> addressed to any of the three pop accounts it checks.  Also, it deletes 
> mail from *microsoft, *ms, and *patch, and from likewise. What kills 90% of 
> the garbage is the "<>^(To|Cc):.*naota@tampabay\.rr\.com" rule (deletes 
> mail that isn't addressed to me).
> 
> How is an SMTP callout different from mailfilter?

It is realtime, and you don't have to maintain it. You are checking in a
static manner for what kills much of the rubbish for you, today. Better
to check for someone who is behaving as if he is a spammer, no matter
what kind of a message he is attempting to send.

Same for Michael's Postfix regexs (a very nice feature of Postfix BTW,
it uses pcre.org from the Exim project.) They have to be maintained and
they only fit the profile of spam that one particular site/person is
getting.
