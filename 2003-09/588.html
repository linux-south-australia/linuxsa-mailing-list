RE: Linux, ADSL, TCP, Window Sizes, download times

Chris,

TCP will not rate limit your download to the slowest "direction" at all.  As
others have explained, the amount of data uploaded while you are downloading
a file is a tiny fraction of the amount you are downloading, hance the fact
that your upload speed is slower does not have an impact on the download
speed.  Example...

Server <--- 100meg Ethernet ---> ISP <--- 1500/256 ADSL ---> You

In this example you'll be able to download at 1500kbps because the server
has a faster internet connection than you do, and the 256k upload channel
won't be filled up by the small amount of data going back to the server.
You may find some instances where you can't download at 1500kbps though,
because you will however be limited to the maximum speed of the slowest pipe
between you and the data you're downloading.  You can only download at
1500kbps is all of the pipes between you and your data are of at least
1500kbps...  Take this example...

Server <--- 128k ISDN ---> ISP <--- 1500/256 ADSL ---> You

Even though you have a 1500k ADSL connection when you try to download data
from the server you'll only be able to download at 128k because thats the
maximum speed that the server can push data through its ISDN link.  Now for
another example...

Server <--- 1500/256 ADSL ---> ISP <--- 1500/256 ADSL ---> You

In this instance you have 1500kbps download, and 256kbps upload, and so does
the server.  So what speed can you download at?  256k, the reason being that
the server can only upload data to its ISP at 256k, therefore the best speed
you can download at from that server is 256k.

Hope that helps.

Jim.
