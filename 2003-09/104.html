European software patents

As of September 1 the Europeans have delayed debate on the introduction of
new software patents for the second time. Many Internet sites joined the protest by
shutting down and alerting readers to the problem about to be created. This well organized,
publicized protest paid off. Good on them. They showed some guts. 

As I watched the news on Slashdot today I was interested in the fact that
more people felt inclined to post about delays in the introduction of
Longhorne than the important fight in Europe.

I wonder about Slashdot sometimes, and how relevant it is to Open Source. Is
it still geeky to browse Slashdot? or under its current owners does
mediocrity rule? A lot of Microsoft pundits seem to hang around the site, that
cut its teeth on the techie, nurdy, alternative, not your average open
source type. 

Certainly they admit to being America focussed. Hmmmm not sure.

Are there other sites that do a similar job ?
