Re: Linux, ADSL, TCP, Window Sizes, download times

This is probably stating the obvious but 
addressing this
>> hence running at 1500/256 you will still receive packets at up to 1500
>> even if what you are sending in reply is equal to or less than 256.

ADSL is Asymetric
So If you are uploading at full speed your download speed is lower 
consequently it really comes down to how many acks get sent up as to how
fast you download.
Seeing TCP is a sliding window protocol it will maximise itself to attain
full bandwith (in theory)
So I would suggest serial downloads rather than simultaneous ones would be
faster aggregate time wise.

But we're really splitting hairs.

The other thing is variables associated with the respective transfers play
a part.
If Site A is close and Site B isn't 
You have 2 downloads happening
you will be sending acks to Site A at a much faster rate simply because
Site B data takes longer to come down, you then will be using more of your
upload in backchannel for Site A furthur impacting Site Bs data coming down. 

I may be wrong but this is the way I see it. - Correct me if Im wrong please.
