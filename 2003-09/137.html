Re: whois blacklist puzzle...

On Tue, 2 Sep 2003, Michael Firkins wrote:

> Today, I was trying to track down a problem one of our suppliers was
> having sending mail to us, and in the process I ran a whois search
> against their domain with the following result:
>
> "% Copyright 2001 auDA.  Terms of Use at
> http://www.aunic.net/copyright.html
>
> The object shown below is NOT in the AUNIC database.
> It has been obtained by querying a remote server:
> (whois.ausregistry.net.au) at port 43.
> To see the object stored in the AUNIC database
> use the -R flag in your query.
>
> BLACKLISTED: You have exceeded the query limit for your IP address and
> have been blacklisted. This restriction will be removed in 24 hours.
>
> %%% End of referred query result"

The problem is that ausregistry is blocking requests from aunic's
whois server.  What you really want is to query ausregistry directly:

i.e.    whois -h whois.ausregistry.net.au $domain

and for IP addresses and AS numbers, use whois.apnic.net
