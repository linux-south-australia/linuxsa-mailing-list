Speaking as part of a panel session at the Australian Unix Users Group (AUUG)
annual conference in Sydney yesterday, OShaughnessy faced a hostile audience,
including FreeBSD developer and AUUG president Greg Lehey.

At the event O'Shaughnessy was forced to admit the legal threat against Linux
users remained.

With the audience clearly fuming at what they were hearing, OShaughnessy
pointed out that the companys legal pursuits are not targeted at end users, but
did make a reference to businesses that use Linux.

There may be problems with commercial Linux users, he said. The SCO licence for
Linux will be available in Australia and New Zealand within the next couple of
months. O'Shaughnessy said that, despite popular belief, "it is our desire to
share information among interest groups".
