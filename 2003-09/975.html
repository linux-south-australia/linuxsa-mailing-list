Re: verisign ?

On Tue, Sep 23, 2003 at 02:50:01PM +0930, Alan Kennington wrote:

 > Verisign controls the root servers, which are the resource where
 > the .net, .org, .com, .everything are stored and accessed by all the
 > computers in the world.

No they don't.

They control the .com and .net servers.  The root servers are controlled
by ICANN, and their day to day administration is carried out by over
a dozen different organizations all over the world.

Verisign administers two of those root nameservers -- very professionally,
with people who have clue.  They do a good job.

The SiteFinder stuff comes from a wildcard A record in .com and .net --
Not the root.  The .com and .net nameservers are administered by Verisign
Global Registration Services (VGRS).  They're the people who will are 
likely to require the future assistance of the witness protection programme
to enable them to live out their day to day lives without people saying  
things which make their kids cry at school.  They are also not the same
people who run the two Verisign root servers.

There is a lot of FUD and propaganda on this topic, caused by deliberate
conflation of issues in VGRS press releases, by journos who don't 
know what they're talking about, and by random uninformed conjecture from
people who really should know better.
