Re: Web100 will clobber VoIP? Was Re: [ANNOUNCE] LinuxSA September2003 Meeting

Alan Kennington wrote:

> On the face of it, unless you can tell me something contrary, it looks like
> Web100 will clobber VoIP.

Bandwidth increases at a rate which exceeds Moore's Law.

In practical terms, that means, "If congestion is present, add more
bandwidth."

Serious networks rarely deal with congestion by accepting high
utilization and using fancy queuing strategies to deal with its
inevitable presence.  Serious networks deal with congestion by
adding more bandwidth until congestion goes away.

I've been telling you this literally for years, but you still behave
as if you believe major networks operate in the presence of congestion.
They don't.  Congestion makes it all too hard;  It's much easier to
deal with it with bandwidth provision instead of queueing strategies.

If Web100 means that end-users can use more bandwidth, then more
bandwidth will be added to cope.

I'm mystified to understand why you think VoIP must necessarily suffer
just because end-users are permitted to use more bandwidth per TCP
session.
