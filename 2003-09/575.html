RE: Linux, ADSL, TCP, Window Sizes, download times

Chris, it isn't so much that we are getting confused at your question,
what you seem to be missing is that if you are downloading a file from
the Internet at 1500k/sec, then you aren't SENDING data back out to the
Internet at 1500k/sec as well!

Think of it as two separate lanes - if one gets congested, then it will
slow down. It doesn't affect the lane going in the other direction!
(Well, it does a bit but for the purposes of understanding this concept
assume it doesn't)

So in answer to your question, tcp won't slow the download to the speed
of your upload.

If you really want to understand this, you'll need to find a decent book
or site how how tcp works. Cisco books work well for this I've found...

Cheers,

Sam.
