Re: redundant firewalls

PIXOS 6.3 can do OSPF (finally dynamic routing) so you can do some 
rerouting, but as with all redundancy solutions you've got to know what 
the hell you're doing or it'll all either be in vain or go very wrong.

Netscreen do some very cool active/active failover [their higher end 
virtual firewalls will make your head spin] costwise it's more than the 
PIX though.

Question for Paul:
They say the want redundant firewalls but do they have any other 
redundancy (switches, ISP links, servers)?  People often _want_ 
redundancy but seem very reluctant to pay what it costs to make it 
worthwhile.
