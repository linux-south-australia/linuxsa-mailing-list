Re: SCO CEO Posts Open Letter to the Open Source Community

On Monday, 22 September 2003 at  0:53:45 +0930, David Newall wrote:
>
> Pity that Bell's own anti-trust suit didn't permit them to
> commercialise Unix.

Well, the divestiture did.  That's where System III and System V came
from.

> A marriage of Bell (Unix) and IBM (PC) would have made the current
> computer climate much more interesting than it otherwise has been.

They didn't need AT&T for that.  Microsoft already had a version of
UNIX when IBM approached them; indeed, it was their stated intention
to base all future products on XENIX.  The PC changed all that: it was
too small to run UNIX.

And when hardware did become cheap and powerful enough to run UNIX,
AT&T was there with the rest, showing a complete incompetence in
handling computer marketing.  Remember the 3B2?  A good machine, on
the face of it, but it didn't have IBM behind it.  See
http://www.obsoletecomputermuseum.org/3b2/ and Google for more
details.
