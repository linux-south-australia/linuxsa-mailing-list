I agree, I've got a few clients deployed with Windows 2000 servers (mainly due
to the fact they bought those $500 Microsoft packs, and insisted they use the
software).

They're fine if you set them up, then DON'T TOUCH THEM.  One problem I've had
with Windows machines is that occasionally you get clients that think they
don't need you, because "Wow, it looks just like my workstation, easy!" and
they get the impression they can administer it theirselves, and as a result,
they often stuff the server up completely - the Windows Server wizards are evil.

A few weeks ago one client was having issues with their Win2K server, which
has been running beautifully, and I found out that the guy's nephew "computer
genius" had told his uncle all about KaZaA and such, and the entire server was
covered with spyware, new IE toolbars - you name it, and many files on the
hard drive were corrupt - and the little shit had somehow killed the ASPI
layer in 2K, so the guy hadn't been doing his backups nightly onto CD.  He
lost a few weeks worth of work - wasn't happy :(

Which I guess is good to some people when you're charging by the hour, which
most Microsoft-guys are solely out to do (it seems, sigh), but I prefer to
have to have happy clients; most don't *care* how or what the server does,
just as long as it keeps on working.

And I've had no issues at all with Samba, minus one hard drive dying; but
everything was backed up the night before via rsync, so it only took a hour to
get the client back up again. :)
