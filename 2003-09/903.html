I remember looking for this once (would you believe, I'm not even an
Amigahead :-) as part of my simulated systems research. Amix is legally
available for binary download, as are scanned versions of the manual.
There is a story that the only copy of the source code was destroyed
years ago by accident.

However -- and this was the reason I was doing the research -- Amix
requires an Amiga 3000, and the UAE emulator only does up to a 2000, and
even then the only way you can get an MMU is by some hacks not in the
main tree. So for now UAE isn't the robust emulated boot environment for
Linux/BSD/SysV on 68k that I want, although you can get it to work a
certain extent. xcopilot works much better, although it is specific to
MMU-less Linux (see
http://www.uclinux.org/pub/uClinux/utilities/xcopilot-0.6.6-uc0.tar.gz)

Amix is SysV, BTW.

There is real commercial use for *ix on 68k, but you can just enjoy the
retro-computing goodness of it all if you'd prefer.
