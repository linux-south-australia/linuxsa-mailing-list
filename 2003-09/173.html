Re: Keeping Linux Off the Desktop

On Wed, Sep 03, 2003 at 05:40:59PM -0700, James Leone wrote:

 > This is going to be the showdown in my opinion folks. If we don't team 
 > up with Johnny Six Pack, we will be DCMA'ed into isolation, and 
 > destroyed as a community. 

Huh?  We've survived as a community for over a decade without any realistic
alternative to MS Office at all.  Why would we suddenly be under threat
just because MS decides to change the format of files we've never reliably
been able to read anyway?

(and forget the DMCA -- despite the headlines, it's stillborn, as proved
by the fact that you can play DVD movies on your Linux box and legally
purchase software to crack Adobe eBooks.  If MS's efforts at DRM are as
ham-fisted as all their other security efforts in other arenas I have no
doubt that input filters for OpenOffice and KOffice will show up which
crack their security, and no lawsuits will be able to stop it.  Most of
the legal action and panic surrounding the DMCA originates with people
who pretend it doesn't have a clause which says that breaking security
for the purposes of interoperability is a legal circumvention)

A sense of proportion never goes astray.
