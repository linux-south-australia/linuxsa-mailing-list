good workarounds for the verisign typo-hijack exploit?

The slashdot replies to the verisign type hijack caper don't seem to give
many really good workarounds for the problem.
Does anyone have some bright ideas on how to mitigate the harm?

The first thing I thought of was:
-	Use iptables to block input from 64.94.110.11.

Then I thought that this just means that if I make a type, I'll
have to wait for 30 seconds for my browser to tell me that there is
no response.

The second thing I thought of is:
-	Use iptables to reject (-j REJECT) _output_ to 64.94.110.11.

This should give me an instant message saying that the server doesn't respond.
But this is still not ideal.

The third thing I though of is:
-	Patch my local copy of BIND to interpret 64.94.110.11 as "not in DNS".

That seems to do the right thing almost. But it's very untidy. Then I'll
have to re-patch this IP address into BIND every time they move the
number 64.94.110.11 to something else. And what's to stop them replacing
a fixed IP address with a rolling set of 10 or 20 IP addresses and selling
the locations in their table?

I'm sure there are people on this list who must have stormbrained this already.

Cheers,
