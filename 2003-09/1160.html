Wow, collaboration in the Linux community; who would've thought? :-)

Good on Red Hat for taking the step that many others should. I get the

Just in case you hadn't heard, Red Hat is changing the way they develop
their OS.  The development process will be more Debian-like - more open,
allowing external contributors etc.  This new hacker-level version is called
"fedora" and info is available here (http://fedora.redhat.com/).


Red Hat won't be providing support for this OS, but will take snapshots of
it and for their "Advanced Server" product which support can be bought for.


An interesting move by them.


What caught my eye, was the latest release of fedora, "Severn r2", which
includes in it's release notes:


"The Red Hat Update Agent (up2date) now supports installing packages from
apt and yum repositories as well as local directories. This includes
dependency solving and obsoletes handling. Additional repositories can be
configured in the /etc/sysconfig/rhn/sources file."


So it appears that RPM and apt are coming together slowly...  A good thing
IMHO...
