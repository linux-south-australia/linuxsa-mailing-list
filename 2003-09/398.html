On Thu, 2003-09-11 at 12:00, Crosby, Bryan wrote:
> Also fundamentally I never understood Moore to have made a Law, it was only
> ever an observation with regard to transistor density. It turned out to be
> useful for road maps. Also you should use MIPS as a metric rather than MHz,
> as processing power has increased faster than clocking frequency.

Whoo, silver.  Moore's law is about density, not clock rates.
The two are closely linked but are not the same.  Which is
why we are seeing multi-core CPUs -- Moore's law has increased
the chip acreage but clock rates are not increasing at the same
rate.

It's a "law" simply because that is what it was originally
named.  Moore had provocative "prove me wrong" smile when he
named his contention a law.
