Geoffrey D. Bennett wrote:
> 
> >   I couldn't find out how to prevent the bootup process from looking
> > for this module but I finally found something that let me get rid of
> > the boot message by adding
> > "alias net-pf-5 off"   to my previously empty /etc/conf.modules
> file.
> >
> > This sounds like a bit of a hack to me, so where could I have
> altered
> > things so that the modprobe never looked for it in the first place?
> 
> That's the correct solution.  Adding that line tells modprobe not to
> bother looking for the module.  My understanding is that modprobe is
> looking for it because kerneld asked it to.  kerneld was requested by
> the kernel to look for it, and it was ifconfig/route that was
> originally
> talking to the kernel about all the protocol families that it knows
> of (eg. IP (net-pf-2), AX.25 (net-pf-3), IPX (net-pf-4), Appletalk
> (net-pf-5)).
> 
> Regards,

Alternatively you can let the kernel have the modules (by compiling

kerneld will eventually remove then if they don't get used.
