Hello :)

Another thing too..  are these mice you've tried two, or three, or both,
button mice..  if the mouse is set to three-button mode and X expects a
two-button mouse, it will jump all over the place and click in places it
probably shouldn't.

Like David said, also check to see if gpm can control your mouse out of X...
additionally, keep in mind (and please correct me if I'm wrong...  that has
been known to happen before...  occasionally...) that gpm doesn't control the
mouse in X.  ie you can safely kill gpm (with gpm -k).   Not that you'd need
to... but it's handy to keep in mind, in case you spend time configuring gpm
in the hope that X will start working.

linux again :)
