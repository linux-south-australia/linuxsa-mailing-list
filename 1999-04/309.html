It's syslogd, Was: Funny entry in /var/log/messages

Hi,
On Fri, 16 Apr 1999, Daryl Tester wrote:

!Someone has either explicitly or implicitly enable the mark facility
!in your /etc/syslog.conf file (look for something like mark.*).
!According to my manpage for syslogd, the "-- MARK --" message is emitted
!every 20 minutes by default.
!If no further activity is occuring in that particular log file, it
!is probably queuing it up for the "last message repeated x times"
!message.  If you login again (or any other activity that will log to
!that same file), it will force syslogd to write the pending messages.

Thanks Daryl, I think this could be it.
My syslog.conf doesn't say anything about mark.* but....I remembered
over lunch that I had updated my syslogd the last time I booted up. The
update was from redhat errata. Maybe they had implicitly enabled the
'mark' facility during their build. I came back after lunch and there
was a bunch of the entries in the log.

I'm relieved it's not due to some trojan program or hacking attempt as I
1st thought :-)
