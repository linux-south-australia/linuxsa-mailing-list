For what it's worth, for a while I used to have a 486-dx2-66, p120+
6x86, a p200+ 6x86, a p133 pentium, a p200 pentium, and a K6-200
available to me. (I didn't actually own 'em all, but worked with em
all.)
I tried out linux on all of, and compared kernel compiling on them.
The Cyrixs went significantly faster than the 486, but this wasn't a
terrible surprise, given their much higher CPU speed. Interestingly
though, a friend compared a Cyrix 586-100 and a 486dx4-100, and found
they were almost exactly the same. Comparing a 586-100 and a 686-120+
(actually 100Mhz) showed the 686 to be faster than the 586 in integer
math, but the same (slow) speed in FP math.
The genuine pentium 133 actually blew both Cyrixs out of the water.
Maybe not in windoze, but under linux where it's 32bit, the real pentium
ran everything faster, integer and FP. At the time the p133 was my
machine and the p200+ was a brothers, so serious testing when on between
the two.
The k6-200 was definately faster than the pentium 200 in most things,
way faster in integer math, and a bit faster in FP math. Interestingly
though, while rc5 benchmarked faster on the K6, Quake benchmarked a
little slower on the K6 compared to a p200. I never figured this out.
However, a good K6 or K6-2 is still pretty cheap compared to buying the
Slot1 board and pentium2 stuff, and they do put up very good results in
your system. if you want hardcore FPU abilities, still go with the
celeron or pentium2, but otherwise the K6 is certainly worth looking at
for the price.

Anyway, just my second 2c worth for the day(for a total of 4c, rounded
up to 5c), and back to work.
