I'll admit that the p200+'s were pretty poor 
(they were actually 150Mhz in speed anyway).
Furthermore, the 75Mhz bus-speed p200+s were absolute
rubbish (extremely unstable) and they had some major
overheating problems. In fact, we have already cooked, 
sold or disposed of any that we did have.

However, the earlier 686's (p120,p166) are quite reliable,
quick enough on the maths side and really only lacking in
"cool gameplay" aspects (which worries me little if I get good
uptimes on servers). At the very least, they were worth the
[1/3 cost of Pentium] prices we paid for them at the time.

And more recently, I have found the 300s and 333s to be a great
improvement on the 200 range and haven't had a problem with them
so far. (Once again, they're quick(er), they're stable and 
they're cheap)

I haven't yet had the chance to work with a K6 chip, but
they seem to have a good rep with most of the sysadmins
I know who have used them.

Just my $0.02 :-)
