Chris Cornish wrote:
> 
> yer but where is the FPU in the things
heh, yeah. The Cyrix processors are really chip, and stable too, but
when it comes to running 32 bit apps, and floating point applications,
they slow down to a crawl.
If you are looking for really decent, non-pentium II, power, check out
the high end K6-2's.
Toms Hardware page frequently benchmarks CPUs in various situations, and
I've run a lot of different CPUs myself in my time, and the K6-2 is
definately a lot faster than the pentium mmx, partially due to the
higher clock speed and partially due to being a more complex design.

In floating point intensive apps though, I'd only use a celeron A
(overclocked of course), or a pentium 2, or a pentium 3. Their FPUs are
still noteably better than the K6. A Celeron 400 running at 500 MHz will
beat down pretty much everything, except a pentium III 500, which gets
about the same speed. (In non-KNI/SSE accellerated software)

However, the AMD K7 will change all this.
