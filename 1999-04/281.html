If believe this is mentioned in the documentation that comes with
x11amp..
But basically, x11amp wants things installed like gtk+ 1.2.0 or newer,
and lets you know this.
However, these things aren't really established in rpms yet, and
installing them with tar won't add the files to the rpm database. Just
--nodeps the x11amp RPM, and it'll install. But make sure that you have
got recent versions of glib, gdk, gtk, audiofile, esd, imlib, libunjpeg,
etc.etc.
