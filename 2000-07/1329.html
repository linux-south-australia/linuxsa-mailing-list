Re: Plural of Computer Virus

I thought I had sent this message before, if  I have, my apologies.

1.    Octopus is a GREEK word, not a Latin word.  For some reason, which I
cannot understand, most Greek words ending in "os" end up as ending in "us".

2.    The correct plural of "octopus" is therefore "octopoda", from the
GREEK octos, meaning 8, and pos, podis, poda, meaning feet.  Do not confuse
this with the Latin pes, pedis, meaning foot.

3.     This also applies to "platypus", meaning "flat feet".  Therefore the
plural is platypoda.

4.     It also seems that in Greek, the "u" (upsilon) is translated into
'y", for whatever reason I cannot fathom.  Perhaps some Greek scholars could
enlighten us on that point.  I am aware a Capital "u" looks like a "y".

Cheers,   Rick McQueen-Thomson
