Re: Trace Route

Daryl Tester wrote:
> ... one reason for blocking ICMP echo or reply at
> a border router is to prevent becoming a "smurf" amplifier, where
> pings with bogus source addresses are directed at broadcast addresses
> on your network, causing your network devices to respond with a reply
> back to the forged source address.

Surely this could be solved better by blocking incoming packets that
appear to originate from your network and incoming packets that are
destined to any broadcast address(es)?

You could also block outgoing packets that do not appear to be from you
network(s), and prevent your network(s) being a source of this problem
too :-)
