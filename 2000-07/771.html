> At 08:52 17/07/00 +0930, bmonten wrote:
> >Hi,
> >     On my LAN I have a mixture of Win 95, Linux, Win NT and unix boxes.
I
> >have just converted my Win95 to Win2000, and am now having trouble
> >authenticating and making connections through samba, running on RH6.1. I
> >seem to remember some discussion on Samba (current generation) not
working
> >with Win2000, and Samba TNG not working with Win 95. Are there any
solutions
> >which will work with both ?
>
> Assuming you just want to share files and printers and so you don't need
> all of the domain stuff then Windows 2000 works fine with samba. All you
> have to do is turn on plain text passwords in Windows 2000. There is a
.reg
> file to do this included with samba, which I will include here since it is
> so small.
>
> REGEDIT4
>
> ;Contributor:   Herb Lewis (herb@sgi.com)
> ;Updated:       16 July 1999
> ;Status:        Current
> ;
> ;Subject:       Registry file to enable plain text passwords in Windows
2000
>
>
[HKEY_LOCAL_MACHINE\SYSTEM\CurrentControlSet\Services\LanmanWorkStation\Para
meters]
> "EnablePlainTextPassword"=dword:00000001
