Re: Burning audio CDs

On Thu, Jul 27, 2000 at 02:06:23PM +0930, Dean Gawler wrote:

 > Now I'm trying to create some audio CDs, but this
 > is where I'm hitting problems. I can play them 
 > back on the CD-RW drive itself using xplaycd and
 > other Linux CD players, but they won't play in 
 > normal CD players. Normal CD players don't find 
 > any tracks at all on the CDs.
 
Many "normal" CD players don't grok CD-RW media.  There are subtle
differences between the format of a "normal" audio CD and the format
of the data deposited by a CD-RW drive.

Some CD players don't care, others care a lot.  YMMV.

 > - do I need the special audio CD-R's that people
 > like TDK sell?

<shrug>  Give it a try.  They're cheap, so get a couple and
find out.

 > - has anyone burnt audio CDs on a CD-RW drive that
 > work in normal players? If so, what hardware,
 > commands, etc... are you using?

Yes, I've done it on a Mac with Toast.  I didn't use the "gold" blank
media, though.

The gold disks are pretty funny, really:  The recordable surface is 
actually the underside of the label, which is why it's so important
to use the right pen to write on it and avoid scratching it :-)
