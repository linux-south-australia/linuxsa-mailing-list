Opensource vs Commercial software - was "best way to write a large document?"

I have no concern with commercial software for Linux.  I respect a persons 
right to choice, but all-in-all I think commercial interest in Linux is 
health (as long as the open source and GPL philosophy is preserved as 
choice).  I have found that commercial Linux vendors (mostly) offer a 
superior service for a cheaper price than under the Windows platform.

I suspect that Applixware, WP and others are top of the range packages 
(although I have not used them) and we should encourage support for 
commercial ventures in Linux.

On the open source side, I have heard that there is a fantastic OS word 
processor (the name escapes me but is on the tip of my tongue) that will be 
improved over time and perhaps even rival the commercial offerings.
