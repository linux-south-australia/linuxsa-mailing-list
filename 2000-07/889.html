Re: LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]

At 07:57 17/07/00 +0000, michaeld@senet.com.au wrote:
> > Martin Sheppard wrote:
> >
> > > Why don't we just have an option when you subscribe for adding the
> > > mailing list name to the subject?
> >
> > Now there's a reasonable idea;  the only problem I could see with
> > that would be the noise leakage between the two domains (ie, someone
> > replying in the "[LinuxSA]" group would be echoed across to the non-
> > mangled-header group).
>
>The problem here is that messing around with subject lines could prevent mail
>threading occuring properly.  ie. modifying the subject line to add/remove
>[LinuxSA].

I don't think this would be a problem in the majority of cases. For 
example, if someone posts a message with the subject
"LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]".

Everyone on the normal list gets it as is, and the people on the mangled 
subject list get it as
"[LinuxSA] LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]".

If someone on the mangled list replies then the subject becomes
"Re: [LinuxSA] LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]"

This gets sent as is to the mangled list and the normal list has the 
[LinuxSA] remove, so it gets
"Re: LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]"

So, the original list gets:
"LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]" and
"Re: LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]"

The mangled list gets:
"[LinuxSA] LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]" and
"Re: [LinuxSA] LinuxSA mailing list subject line [THINGOS]"

This is the correct set of subjects, so threading works properly.

>That's enough for me to say that we shouldn't modify subject lines IMHO.

I agree that if modifying subject lines is going to cause problems then we 
shouldn't do it, but in this case I can't see how it would. The only issue 
I can see is that CC'd replies would have a mixture of subject lines.
