Re: Cyrus now open source

On Fri, 14 Jul 2000, Dan Shearer wrote:

> Following up on my talk a little while ago, the Cyrus IMAP server will be
> covered under an 'Apache Public License' style copyright from 1st July
> 2000. Commercial use of any form no longer requires a license.

Incidentally I think this illustrates what I just said about "more-or-less
open source" and "the overall effect". A large community of users of the
Cyrus mailstore product has developed over the years even though it was
non-free software with the source available.  The intention and practices
were close enough to open source for it to work reasonably well. The
maintenance team worked as if their product was under the GPL or some
other open license, with many contributions from the user community.

I'm very pleased and I'm sure that this action will really help Cyrus in
the long term, but it does show that an open license isn't needed to
either enforce or create a functional software user community in the open
source sense.
