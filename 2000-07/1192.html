Everyone else got the message without html,
without attachments. No problems at all.
I.e. there's something wrong with either
1. your e-mail (client) software, or
2. your local mail server.

In the past, I was once accused of sending e-mail.
I didn't. 
I just encapsulated my message in an attachment,
which was then converted to html by the recipient's local
e-mail server - some sort of Microsoft thing.

In your case, the sender did not even send
any attachment stuff in the headers or body of
the e-mail.
So something is really very wrong at your end.
I suspect you have some sort of dodgy
mail server converting things for you.

You can check the linuxSA archives to determine what
was send, and compare it with what you received.
That's a pretty good check.
