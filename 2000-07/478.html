Re: Debate on the issue of certification

Agreed,

As a lecturer for the Business Studies Component of the Advanced Diploma in IT
at Adelaide TAFE (Including Quality Management) I am heartily sick of the MS
centered philosophy.  Please note it is soul destroying to meet and teach
diploma qualified IT personnel who speak and believe there is MS and then there
is GOD. Sigh.

However, I can assure you all that I provide comprehensive BROAD views on
business and computing models and introduce moral and philosophical
implications of tying oneself up to any organization.

I think it is important that  all of us not promote one disto over and above
another but to promote "open source" free etc etc.  Remember the choice is, and
should be, for the individual.

LPI is the way to go and I point all of my students to open source projects as
a way to get experience and a track record for their own CV's

There is a great paper at:
http://www2.linuxjournal.com/articles/currents/017.html  for those who are
interested in the other war that is raging apart from the tech war we are
normally exposed to.

There are many in education who are fighting the MS tide with skills other that
technical skills but with a moral and ethical underpinning of freedom and
fairness.

</soapbox>

Really though our hidden war is just as important as the tech war that is much
publicized in the media.


Stephen Donaldson
