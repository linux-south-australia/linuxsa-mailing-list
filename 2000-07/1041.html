misbehaving internet connection

Hi All,

Firstly, a sincere thankyou to all those who gave
their time at the "installfest" to help those of us
who are just "linux newbies"...much appreciated.

At the "installfest" i obtained the latest copy of
mandrake, which i have since installed.

I then proceeded creating and\or modifying the files:

/etc/resolv.conf
================
search
nameserver <Primary DNS>
nameserver <Secondary DNS>

/etc/host.conf
==============
order hosts,bind
multi on

/etc/ppp/options
================
/dev/ttyS1
57600
connect '/usr/sbin/chat -v -f /etc/ppp/isp-script
noipdefault
defaultroute

/etc/ppp/isp-script
===================
ABORT BUSY
ABORT "NO CARRIER"
ECHO OFF
SAY "Dialling Internet Service Provider.....\n"
"" ADTD <ISP Number>
SAY "Waiting for connection.....\n"
ogin: <Username>
assword: <Password>
SAY "Username and password accepted.....\n"

Using the above configuration it seems to connect,
but soon after it automatically disconnects.

Looking in the /var/log/messages file it says:
"Peer is not authorised to use remote address..."

The ISP is "Chariot Internet" if that helps, I
also thought that it may be due to them using
PAP authentication. So I ...

added "user <Username>" to /etc/ppp/options
added <Username> * <Password> to /etc/ppp/pap-secrets
removed "ogin" and "assword" lines from /etc/ppp/isp-script

...but know luck.

Any ideas?

Thanks,
Richard Watkins
