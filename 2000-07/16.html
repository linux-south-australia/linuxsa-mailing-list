> You seem to have confused kilobytes with megabytes (kB MB).

Scans... Right.  Muscle memory: so used to typing MB.  In 10 years
time I'll no doubt claim that the PC maxed at 512TB of RAM :-)

All RAM sizes were intended to be expressed in KB, not MB, with the
exception of those for Lisa.

The Apple /// had 32KB bank switching.  The lower 32KB was fixed; the
upper was switched.  It also had a twisted bank-switched indirect
addressing mode.  I'm still unclear whether Apple used a modified
part, or if they did that by clever monitoring of the data bus.
Anyway, it was interesting, in the same way that their floppy disk
controller was interesting (a kludge.)
