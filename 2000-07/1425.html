On Wed, Jul 26, 2000 at 04:12:11PM +0930, David Newall wrote:

 > > I'm a beginner at these things, but it seems to me that
 > > an incorporated organisation (such as a suburban choir
 > > or table tennis club) has unlimited liability.
 > 
 > I think members of incorporated bodies generally have limited liability.
 > That would be the reason why one incorporates.  Right now all members of
 > "LinuxSA" are liable for LinuxSA debts without limitation.  We're liable
 > jointly (all of us together) and severally (each of us individually), so
 > there's lots of ways that a debtor can try to recover monies. 

See, that's exactly the stuff I was talking to David Lloyd about yesterday.
That statement is simply not true.

At the moment, LinuxSA is not capable of running up a debt because it
doesn't exist.  But if LinuxSA needs to obtain goods or services to
do something, and those goods or services need to be paid for, the
debt actually lives with the individual who ordered them -- not the
organization, and not the members of the organization.

Let's say Compaq started charging rent for the meeting space.  The
individual who signed the lease document would be liable, not the members.
Quite a favour to perform for the group, eh?
