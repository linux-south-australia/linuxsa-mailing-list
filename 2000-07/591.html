> I'm a little confused...

We all know, from Spamming 101, that the To and From headers can be
bogus.  This is because the headers are part of the mail message,
and the sender and recipients are part of the mail envelope.  Mail
transports communicate the sender and recipients amongst themselves.

I'm going to tell you how Bcc can work, but you'll kick yourself:
Your mail program removes the Bcc header from the message before
passing the messages to the mail transport.  (Your mail program
tells the mail transport what addresses to deliver the message to.)
