STUDY REPORTS LINUX A SIGNIFICANT THREAT

STUDY REPORTS LINUX A SIGNIFICANT THREAT
by Dave Murphy, member@itrain.org

Market research firm International Data Corp (IDC),
reported today that Linux will pose a significant threat
to Microsoft for market share among server operating
systems (OS) over the next few years.

That report's conclusion is good news for Linux system
administrators and consultants. It's especially good
news for cash-strapped IS managers looking for an
alternative to the potentially high costs associated with
installing and maintaining multiple Microsoft
Windows NT/2000 servers.

Since 1999, when Linux based Novell NetWare to
become the second most popular server OS, the talk
amongst techies in the know has been whether Linux
has a chance to unseat Windows NT/2000 as the most
popular OS for file, print, and other network services.

With a 28 percent growth rate, Linux is expanding into
the arena of managing critical systems.

Linux is well known as a stable platform for websites
and online services. Because it has its roots in UNIX,
it's a very secure OS for e-commerce transactions. It's
also a snap to set up for small office workgroup
computing that requires file and print services without
the overhead of high end equipment. Low cost Linux
server installations start at about $1,000, everything
included.

On the flip side, industry power hitters such as IBM are
banking on Linux' ability to power the industry's super
computers. Clusters of hundreds or thousands of low-
cost Linux PCs can be harnessed to run large-scale
data processing operations such as Google, one of the
net's largest search engines.
