I use IBM's toppage for wysiwig editing AND (more importantly) site
navigation. It runs under Linux via wine.

IBM are, naturally enough, pushing the Linux marketplace. But, I have to
say, that using Wine is a disappointing first step. It does not gel well
(cut 'n paste) with the KDE environment (or for that matter gnome), and the
forced C:\ D:\ style folder navigation is clumsy and irksome.

As a tool, and, as it relates to your overall query, toppage is a fine
product (better than anything else out there currently). But it really does
need to move on and be truly ported to the *nix glibc library and widget
sets before it is a truly productive and satisfying 'experience'. It's early
days, and I assume will get better, but Wine has to go.
