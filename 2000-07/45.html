While this sounds like good advice, I think that it is hard to achieve in
practice.  SCSI disks have for a long time presented a linear array of
sectors and you often have no real idea where on the disk those sectors are
going to land, or even if what you think are contiguous sectors are going
to be contiguous on the disk.

Similarly, (E)IDE does lots of different translations and a partition that
is logically in the middle of the disk may not be physically (contiguous
and) in the middle of the drive.

Of course, I could be wrong about it.

Back when we had RA80's and the Fuji equivalent, we could put things near
the middle of the drive, but not, I fear, any more.
