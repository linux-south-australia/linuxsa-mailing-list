Re: apache +dns problems

He's talking about the actual file that your webserver is trying to serve.

Usually, these are in ~httpd/html/

use `ls -al /home/httpd/html/` and have a look at the permissions on these
files...

will look something like:

[rarussel@nova rarussel]$ ls -al /home/httpd/html/
total 44
drwxrwxr-x   7 root     wwwadmin     1024 Jul 14 12:12 .
drwxr-xr-x   7 root     root         1024 Mar 17 16:47 ..
-rw-rw-r--   1 root     wwwadmin      359 May 16  1999 index.html
...

the string of chars on the left are the permissions.

anything starting with "d" is a directory, and should have "r" and "x" where
mine does.
anything starting with "-" is a file, and should have "r" where mine does.

`man chmod` will tell you how to change permissions.
