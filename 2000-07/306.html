I know someone who puts the string "+++ATH0" in his mail headers. Quite
mean!

   (That's a modem 'guard tone', or, 'stop what you're doing and drop back
    to modem commmand mode ready to receive AT commands.' The AT command
    H0 hangs up the modem immediately. So someone who has left their modem
    with the common default guardtone setting on will have a dropped line
    every time they try to receive a message from this person. And since
    SMTP servers normally retry quite a few time before giving up on a 
    message the recipient is likely to have quite a bit of grief. Ouch)
