> I wanted to reignite the debate on certification by injecting 
> my opinion.
> 
> There are several certification routes that one can go:
> 
>   - the Red Hat certification program, a vendor-specific certificate, 
> 
>   - the SAIR/GNU Linux certification program, now part of Wave,
> 
>   - The Linux Professional Institute's (LPI) certification program.
> 
> Of these, the LPI certification program has broad industry 
> backing, and is
> trying to avoid vendor specifics (in anycase, all Linux 
> distros are very
> similar to each other, with maybe 2% differences).
> 
> However, I feel that those of us who are into certification should
> demonstrate that we want an independent program that it not 
> tied to any one
> vendor, as much to demonstate that we want a vialable Linux 
> industry out
> there, not tied to any one vendor's marketing needs.
> 
> For that reason, I suggest that those who are interested in 
> certification
> should take the currently available LPI exams and support 
> their aims. They
> can be taken through VUE, and are relatively in-expensive.
>
