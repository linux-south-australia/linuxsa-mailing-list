No, that's a quite normal looking routing table. pppd generally adds a
route for the exact box on the other end of the ppp link (the top line),
and a default route for everything else at the bottom. On a Redhat box
(not sure of others), route resolves dns names by default, you can do
route -n to get nameless IPs in your routing table, which can be useful
if your connection to your nameserver isn't up.
