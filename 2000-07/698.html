Good psql/mysql comparison article

Since the topic was thoroughly debated here...

http://www.phpbuilder.com/columns/tim20000705.php3?page=1 talks about the
experiences one of the sourceforge/geocrawler designers has had with both
databases. He did some benchmarking and discusses the results. He
obviously really likes MySQL for a lot of good reasons.

A rough summary of his conclusions:

   - either will do for many people 
   - mysql is much more fragile than psql for long-term running
   - psql is much slower than mysql
   - mysql is unsafe for critical applications such as 
     ones dealing with money
   - psql has many features that most people won't care about but that
     heavy-duty database designers coming from Oracle will be glad to
     see
   - mysql is easier to run
   - psql has some database corruption problems that mysql doesn't, 
     even though under load mysql crashes more frequently
