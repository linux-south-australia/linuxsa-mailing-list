yes -- We need some sort of system. After some time, I instituted a queue,
so that people who got in first could get a table before people who got in
late, but we should have had that organised at the start. Also, we needed a
table numbering system, and a whiteboard for writing who had what problem
where, so people who are helping could look there to see where they were
needed.

> Also all that hardware leaves some concerns in the security 
> and power surge 
> department.  I do not fully understand what arrangements were 
> in place, so I 
> am not position to comment, but even so..

yes, security was tough. We had a few people keeping their eyes peeled, and
we tried to keep hardware labeled and checked. It wasn't the best solution,
but it worked OK for us.

Power wasn't good. We didn't have time to check that we weren't overloading
any circuits, and the circuits themselves weren't protected (AFAIK), and we
daisy-chained random powerboards. We had one powerboard overload and shut
off, but it didn't cause problems. Next time, we will do better in this
department, _somehow_... :)
