Interesting.  I see this as a recurring theme among people who are
new to Linux (or, for that matter, UNIX in general):  The idea that
a high load average, or a busy system in general, will end up triggering
a crash.

Really -- If you ask a system to do lots of stuff, you should see the
load average increasing and response times getting slower.  There's no
rational reason at all to expect load to "kill the box", though.
