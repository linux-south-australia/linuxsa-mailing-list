Re: Trace Route

Dan Shearer wrote:

>> Some sites filter out ICMP messages in the mistaken belief that this
>> improves security.  Traceroute does not work through such filters.

> It isn't always quite that simple.
> http://www.faqs.org/faqs/computer-security/most-common-qs/section-18.html
> covers "Should I block all ICMP at my firewall/router?"

In addition to Dan's fine reply (and lengthy!  Someone's had their
weetbix this morning), one reason for blocking ICMP echo or reply at
a border router is to prevent becoming a "smurf" amplifier, where
pings with bogus source addresses are directed at broadcast addresses
on your network, causing your network devices to respond with a reply
back to the forged source address.  Can cause people undue stress at
the receiving end :-(

Personally, I hate having to disable ICMP echo/reply, as it is a useful
network diagnostic tool, but abused trust is a funny thing ...
