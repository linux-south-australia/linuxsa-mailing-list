Re: Samba and Windows 2000

Martin Sheppard wrote:
> 
> At 08:52 17/07/00 +0930, bmonten wrote:
> >Hi,
> >     On my LAN I have a mixture of Win 95, Linux, Win NT and unix boxes. I
> >have just converted my Win95 to Win2000, and am now having trouble
> >authenticating and making connections through samba, running on RH6.1. I
> >seem to remember some discussion on Samba (current generation) not working
> >with Win2000, and Samba TNG not working with Win 95. Are there any solutions
> >which will work with both ?
> 
> Assuming you just want to share files and printers and so you don't need
> all of the domain stuff then Windows 2000 works fine with samba. All you
> have to do is turn on plain text passwords in Windows 2000. There is a .reg
> file to do this included with samba, which I will include here since it is
> so small.

Turning on plain text passwords opens up a security problem. It is
better in most situations to turn encrypted passwords on in Samba
('encrypt passwords=yes'). You will also want to have 'security=user' if
you don't want to use any of the NT-DOM stuff.
