On Mon, Jul 03, 2000 at 10:50:00AM +0930, Greg Lehey wrote:

 > You're talking positioning here.  In my experience, average
 > positioning times are nowadays in the same order of magnitude as

Just a quick note to anyone here who doesn't know Greg Lehey:  He's the
guy who wrote the "vinum" logical volume manager for FreeBSD and several
books on UNIX and system administration, and he's also worked in a
system engineering role at more OS vendors over the last 25 years than
most of us have even heard of.  As such, when we're talking about disk
I/O and he starts a sentence with the words, "In my experience," it's
probably a good idea to avoid discarding his comments lightly :-)

Comments earlier in the thread have left me questioning some of my own
assumptions, and searching around for reasonably modern machines with IDE
disks which I can use to do some testing on (I can't find any newer 
than 18 months or so, though:  We use all SCSI at work these days, and
my only IDE device at home is a CD-ROM drive);  I'd suggest a similar
degree of open-mindedness in others :-)
