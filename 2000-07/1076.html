The advice I give everyone is to install a distribution that you know you can
get supported. ie. if you know people who run Linux, and they are prepared to
help you out when you get stuck, install whatever they're running.

As for LinuxSA, most people seem to be Red Hat/Debain users with a reasonable
sprinkling on Mandrake/Corel/Caldera people around as well.

The good thing is you can try one, then another, etc until you find one you
like.  It's all Linux, so there lower learning curves each time.
