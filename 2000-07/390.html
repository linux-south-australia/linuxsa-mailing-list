Re: IDE timeouts

On Tue, 11 Jul 2000, Robyn Manning wrote:
> Hi Michael,
>  From my own experience ide timeouts are a hard drive fault.

Or, the PC power supply may be faulty or not have enough capacity for the
attached equipment. For example, one power supply I had, had three devices
on one of the drive power cables, and the drive would 'power down' and
then back up occasionally when it was not meant to. Changing the drive to
another power cable fixed that. I also have a DEC Lpc 486 with a 145W
power supply, which is very quiet and used for routers, but some of the
hard disks on it started showing time-outs. I belive it is because I had
two hard disks, a DDS tape unit, a CDROM and a floppy drive running off
it. The disks themselves were fine.
