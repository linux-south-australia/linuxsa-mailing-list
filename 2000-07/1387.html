Dear Bruce

Australian Taxation Office - etax

You sent an email to the Australian Taxation Office on 17/07/2000.

The text of your enquiry was:
   -----------------------------------------------------------
When will a version of e-tax for other
operating systems be available? I use
Linux
and wish to lodge my return
using the e-tax
software. Does the ATO
or Federal Gov't
generally have a
policy of forcing people to
use only
one proprietary system type to be
able
to deal electronically for tax?
References
please.

Postal address: 30 Afric Street
Largs North SA 5016
   -----------------------------------------------------------

e-tax 2000 only supports Windows operating systems.

The ATO has concentrated in the first instance on making e-tax available to
those people with the most widely used operating systems. This has been done
to maximise the availability of e-tax in the community, yet at the same time
work within the boundaries of a cost justified solution.

The ATO has not ruled out the possibility of making future versions of e-tax
available for users of 'Linux' computers.

The ATO is aware of concerns of users with operating systems other than
'Windows'. As part of the ongoing development and improvement in the range
of electronic services available to taxpayers, any opportunities to increase
the availability of e-tax will be investigated.


Thank you for your enquiry. Please quote reference number E20000717-582 in
any future correspondence on this matter.

Yours faithfully,


Jennie Granger
Deputy Commissioner of Taxation
