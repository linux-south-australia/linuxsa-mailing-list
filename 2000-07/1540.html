Re: what version of linux for a crusty mac laptop?

Hi, I have no idea what will actually run, but have recently, quickly,
done some searching for someone else.
To get _your_ search started...
go to http://www.everythinglinux.com.au on the left look for
"distributions"
below this, click on "Power PC Linux".  This will show two
distributions:- LinuxPPC as well as yellowdog.

Go to the web site for yellowdog,   http://www.yellowdoglinux.com
     and find the page that shows "support" and then the "FAQ for
hardware".
    http://www.yellowdoglinux.com/support/faq_hardware.shtml .

Go to the web site for LinuxPPC,   http://www.linuxppc.org
     and find the page that shows the hardware.
http://www.linuxppc.org/about/hardware/
     I learnt that LinuxPPC has had more time to be made to work on a
larger variety of hardware.


For a broader/further education, go to http://www.linux.org
   on the left is a button for "Distributions"
   on the distributions page, click on the "English-Language Linux
Distributions" link,
   and look down the page that lists all the known distributions.
http://www.linux.org/dist/english.html
   I discovered there, that the Apple company was involved with a
third distribution. http://www.mklinux.org

During an exploration of the turbolinux site,
http://www.turbolinux.com
   I discovered that they are developing a version for mac hardware.

I also learned along the way, that Debian http://www.debian.org
   has an aim to port to as much hardware as possible, including macs
of various shapes. http://www.debian.org/ports/
