My interest in the gui is solely the dialog box. A list of selectable in your
face options. One of THE design rules for dialog boxes is that they have no
pull down pop up menus of any kind. Not really relevant to what you are saying
but thought you'd be interested. This 'rule' incidentally came from the 1990
edition of Gui Desgin Rules for OS/2 warp. The prinicples laid
down then are still repeated by KDE.

Incidentally, the gui interface is not just the graphical user interface. It
stretches into the keyboard. IBM pioneered these consistency rules and were
enshrined in their Systems Application Architecture handbooks. SAA 599 before
the MAC or Windoze (tm) were 'invented'. If you've ever wondered where ALT F4,
ALT TAB and all the others come from, look no further.
