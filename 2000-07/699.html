Re: Which distribution ?

I have try Slackware 7.0 and Redhat 6.0. Both are good.
Readhat package and Slackware package are easy to convert.
Slackware is quick to install by choosing default
installation of all packages. Slackware is more stable
than redhat 6.0 I have more reboot on redhat system than
any other. BSD is good too.FreeBSD have a lot of porting
software already compiled on the net. OpenBSD is the most
secure OS for the free OS. NetBSD is good to consider.IF
you choose GUI base , you should go Redhat or FreeBSD. I
personally choose Redhat myself if  I do not have
experience. With Slakware you may have manually change
some configuration files to make it work properly. eg .
config the netcard. or sound card while redhat mostly
detectable those devices. 
Have you ever try sco unix? why not it's free too.
www.sco.com . With Sco, you can config the system manually
or by GUI. My suggestion is to try a few systems first to
know which one fits your taste.
