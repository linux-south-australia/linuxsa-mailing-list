Re: Server Hacked.

Tristan Ball wrote:

> Not running UW-IMAP, and certainly not world visible.

Not world visible in what sense?  If it's accessible from the Internet,
it's world visible to any number of scanners that the script kiddies
use.  I had a sendmail box probed to see if it was spammable (no,
tweren't ORBS) less than 24 hours after it had been put up (and it had
no DNS entries).

> Current most likely culprits are sendmail and httpd...

Not likely, unless you're running a really old of sendmail (pre 8.8) or
Apache (don't even know of any exploits for Apache, and certainly not
rootable ones).  You're most likely culprits are wu-ftpd, bind or pop
server (nothing but the _very latest_ - a new wu-ftpd was released only
a couple of weeks ago).  One nasty bind exploit I've seen actually
blows away /etc/hosts.allow and /etc/hosts.deny (I was on the box at the
time, and only noticed because it screwed up qmail); luckily it left the
ipchain rules in place ...
