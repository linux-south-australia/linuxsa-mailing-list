Re: IP Ranges

> Is there any way I can get access to how IPs are mapped?

You can find the owners using whois, but there is no prefix indicating the
country or other geographic area.  Early addresses were handed out
generously and I don't know the basis but I guess it's as good as random.
Later, as it became apparent that there weren't enough addresses to go
round, and that the "random" approach was both wasteful in use and
inefficient in routers, CIDR was devised, which is an attempt to provide a
geographic prefix.  Thus addresses are organised into blocks and randomly.
