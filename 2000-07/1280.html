Re: Trace Route

Richard Sharpe wrote:
> Detecting incoming packets that are destined to broadcast addresses is
> hard. Without the netmask, you cannot tell if the address is broadcast or
> not. So, you cannot do this unless you know the broadcast addresses of all
> downstream networks. Similarly, checking for incoming packets where the
> source address is a broadcast address is hard.

*thinks*

Okay, help me out here. Why is denying incoming packets to a broadcast
address hard? (Disclaimer: networks are not yet my forte. :-)

Is it really necessary to know the broadcast address of *all* downstream
networks? Wouldn't it be possibly to simply drop incoming packets to the
broadcast address that each router knows about? That way, if a network
is subnetted down stream, it's the job of the router doing the network
subnets to drop packets to any extra broadcast addresses created as a
result.

Or am I missing something about the way subnets work?
