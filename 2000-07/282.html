Hi,

You can create your own environment variable with your IP...  This shell
script works on my RH 6.1 box, and should work for you...

---------- Cut Here -------
#!/bin/sh
## Get the host name of our login session by grep'ing the w output for
## our login session.  Characters 19 to 35 contain the host name of our
## login session.  NOTE: Assumes you won't log in from two different
## hosts simultaneously.
LOGINFROM=`w | grep $USER | cut -c19-35`

## Now that we have a host name, use nslooup to get the IP,  head and tail
## are used to isolate the second last line of the nsloopup output (which
## all being well, is our IP.  Then we cut the line to remove the
## "Address: " bit from the front.
LOGINIP=`nslookup $LOGINFROM | tail -n2 | head -n1 | cut -c11-`

## Do whatever you want with the ip here...
echo $LOGINIP
