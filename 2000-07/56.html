Re: Hardware

Mark Newton wrote:
...
> Downsides:  Again, think of it as something akin to an Ethernet:  It
> doesn't cope well with bus bandwidth saturation.  Collision domains
> don't work well under load.

Not quite right.  Although the channel is shared the responses
upon the channel being in use is radically different.

SCSI asserts a signal to gain control of the bus one immediately
it is free.  Competing signals are resolved in SCSI ID priority
(which is why the controller should be the highest ID).

Ethernet exponentaially delays further attempts to gain control
of the bus, as it has no signal that it can raise to indicate
a pending bus request.

The cost of bus occupancy is *much* higher under Ethernet than
under SCSI.
