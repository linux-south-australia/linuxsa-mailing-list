> You need to load the driver. Without the driver, the card will not be
> visible. You might also like to configure the card.
>
> You can load the driver manually with something like:
>
>   modprobe ne io=0x300
>
> You might need to change the io address. Choose addresses like 0x340,
0x280
> and so on. If you get the IO address from Windoze, use that address.
>
> Once you have got the driver loaded manually, edit /etc/conf.modules and
add:
>
>    alias eth0 ne        # That might be eth1 if you have another eth card
>    options ne io=0x300  # or whatever you found
>
> Then configure the card using netcfg and the next time you boot, the card
> will come up like magic!
>
