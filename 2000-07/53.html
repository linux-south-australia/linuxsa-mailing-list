Re: Performance with Raid 0 using IDE drives

Robyn Manning wrote:
...
> Was I mistaken or did Glen talk about placing the swap partition at the
> middle of the disk for better performance?

Another disk is better.  But in a workstation single disk, dual boot scenario
the best partitioning plan appears to be:

  1: /boot
  2: other OS
  3: extended
     e1: swap
     e2: Linux /

My observation about the location of swap was to make two points:

 - people forget to optimise swap performance, and thus rob their
   machine of under-load performance [the "you should never swap"
   myth]

 - you do want to think about what is at the center of the disk.
   Or more to the point -- don't put performance critical data
   at the ends of the disk

Even if the disk topology can't be known (and EIDE's logical block
addressing makes that hard) the filesystem will sort blocks and
service them in an elevator fashion.  This always improves performance
on rotating disk media with significant head movement times.
