Re: what version of linux for a crusty mac laptop?

I am not sure about 1400s, I have only ever installed them on desktop macs
and on a G3 laptop but you could try mklinux, or linuxppc.

http://www.mklinux.org/
http://www.linuxppc.org/

MkLinux is not a pure linux, it actually runs a CMU Mach kernel with a
Linux personality server on top.

It is binary and src compatable with other ppc linuxes

Both Mklinux and linuxppc are rehdat based.

There may have been some problems with 1400s but my memory does not
stretch far enough back to recall - I am not usre if ti was only with
certains versionof the 1400s, or if it was a general problem and I cna't
recall if it was resolved or not.

I believe the CD/floppy bay works under linuxppc and  mklinux.

It certainly does with later powerbooks, such as the g3.

I dont know anyhting about yellowdog.

I have a copy of linuxppc and mklinux, if you wnat to give them a try and
maybe burn a copy.

You will have to organise the burn and you will have to pick them up and
return them.

Linuxcare did have some burn to order Cd scheme going, but I am noti sure
if they still do.

Try www.linuxcare.com.au (I think) to find out details or if they still
do it at all.
