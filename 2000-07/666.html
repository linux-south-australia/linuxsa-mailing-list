Cyrus now open source

Following up on my talk a little while ago, the Cyrus IMAP server will be
covered under an 'Apache Public License' style copyright from 1st July
2000. Commercial use of any form no longer requires a license.

The license change coincides with a major version number. The 2.0.x series
are still in testing, but a lot of interesting things have been added.
Scalability is being addressed (Murder IMAP clustering sees the light for
the first time, better databases and more) in case 150 000 users wasn't
enough for you or you wanted better failover characteristics. There's
quite a lot more useful things in the management department, and replacing
the TCL cyradm with a perl version will help anyone trying to do things in
the quick-hack category. Not that TCL isn't a fine language, but there is
better IMAP support built into Perl. And there's many more changes too.

sasld and acapd have also been updated and if you're into mail products
from that part of the world the public beta of the non-free binary-only
Mulberry IMAP client is available for download.
