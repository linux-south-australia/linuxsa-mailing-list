 > I changed my modem today to a Netcomm wave sp56 and everything is absolutely

Check your /etc/ppp/options file (or wherever else command-line options
for pppd are being set).  Make sure the "crtscts" option is present, 
otherwise there's no guarantee that flowcontrol is being correctly handled.

Someone else suggested it might be an MTU problem.  If you're sending 
packets larger than your ISP is able to receive, you can reduce the size
of packets you transmit with the "mtu XXX" problem -- XXX should be a "small"
number, e.g., around 512 or so.  MTU means "maximum transmit unit," and
is the maximum datagram size your PPP interface will transmit to your
ISP.

If your ISP is sending you packets larger than the ones you're willing
to receive, you can change the Maximum Receive Unit (MRU) size with the
"mru XXX" option.  I'd suggest "mru 1500" to make sure you can hold an
ethernet frame (you won't ever get anything larger in practice).
