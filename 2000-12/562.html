Re: Word document password cracker...

<sigh>

This has got to do with probability mathematics. The only real reason
why RSA et al is so hard to crack is that we don't have anything
powerful enough to brute force them. Last time I looked MSWORD documents
were encrypted with ASCII characters supplied by humans. Ignoring, for
the moment, humans:

* if it's one character, of only ASCII, then

I need 256 iterations to break the code. That takes less than 1/2
second...

* if it's two charactgers, of only ASCII, then

I need 256*256 iterations

Not terribly difficult on a local machine. The person who asked is
working on a local machine. So, a 10 letter password would be 256^10
iterations of testing, which in computer terms doesn't strike me as
being too difficult...

The point of the matter probably should be any idiot wouldn't use
something as stupid as an 8 character word password to protect
EXTEREMELY important data.
