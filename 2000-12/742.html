Re: Boot command

On Wed, 20 Dec 2000, Matthew Geddes wrote:
> Martin Stacey wrote:
> > 
> > I come from a Solaris background and are new to Linux. In Solaris they have
> > a command "boot -s" to boot a machine into single user mode from a halt
> > position. Does Linux have a similar command?
> 
> init 1 changes to runlevel 1.

I think what you want is this. LILO is sort of the equivelent to Sun's
OpenBoot (for the purpose you require). At the LILO prompt enter:

linux single

Where 'linux' is the name of the lilo.conf section you wish to boot.
'linux' is a well used default.

'single' is passed to init. From memory, -s also works.

The boot prompt HOWTO will best describe what you are looking for:

http://www.linuxdoc.org/HOWTO/BootPrompt-HOWTO.html

Also try the man pages for:
init
lilo 
lilo.conf
