On Tue, 5 Dec 2000, Moore, Alastair (DETE) wrote:
> What I'd like to start off with is just a console/command line installation
> and then work up to an Xwindows interface later on when I've got some sort
> of understanding of the linux command line interface. Can I download just
> the kernel and whatever is necessary to boot up rather than downloading a
> whole distribution?

A mini single-disk distribution would be the best for that. But it wont
give the the feel of a complete 'standard' install. You could do a minimal
Debian or Turbo Linux installation. 

The following is a good minimal installation based on Slackware.
http://www.tux.org/pub/people/kent-robotti/looplinux/rip/

The Linux Router Project could be equally useful. But bear in mind they
all have their own methods of configuration and process control (init
etc...), especiall when compared to Redhat and so on.
http://lrp.c0wz.com/
