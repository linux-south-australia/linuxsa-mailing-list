Cracking down on copycats: enforcement of copyright in Australia.

http://www.aph.gov.au/house/committee/laca/copyrightenforcement/contents.htm

Lots of less-than-positive stuff.

We have until February until the next meeting of the committee
if we wish to comment.

Of particular concern:

 - employers are held responsible for software on employees
   machines.  So you can expect a corporate crack down on
   installation of any software, free or otherwise.

 - Stating that your machine shouldn't be ceased because
   it is running Linux and thus can't possibly infringe
   doesn't cut it -- you still lose the machine and need
   to go to court to get it back.

and I'm sure there is more.

I'd suggest people read it and comment with specific
objections to the list.  I will collate these into a
document and send it to the committee.
