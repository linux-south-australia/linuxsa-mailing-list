Re: PDF

On Tue, 19 Dec 2000, James Mclean wrote:

> 
> What is a good PDF viewer for Linux? i have receved a PDF, but I am unwilling to
> open it on my winblows box...
> any ideas?

gv is good, although it has an annoying habit of defaulting to foolscap
paper size, despite the size of the age being embedded into the document
itself...

xpdf is _very_ slow at rendering complex pages, especially those with TIFF
images on them. It also doesn't handle image rotations very well (lines
are dropped). The author is very responsive to bug reports though.

acroread is reliable and fast, but I have had it crash on me a couple of
times.
