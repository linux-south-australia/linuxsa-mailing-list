Re: Tape Drives

Martin Stacey wrote:
> 
> I need to install a tape drive onto a Red Hat Version 7 machine? What is a
> good tape drive and how do I go about installing one?

It depends a bit on your pocket.  DDS3, DLT or LTO from an estalished
manufacturer like Compaq, HP, etc.  There's a fair bit of
relabelling.  When the price sticker hurts, you've bought a
large enough drive.  If you really don't mind changing tapes,
then there might be some old DDS2 (6GB) stock going cheap.

Search the LinuxSA mail archives for the installation instructions;
I posted what I had to do to get one working a few months back.
