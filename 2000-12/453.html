Re: Samba + Automount

On Tue, 12 Dec 2000, Dan Shearer wrote:

> On Tue, 12 Dec 2000, Adam Hawes wrote:
> > I want a system where the disk can be inserted and mounted on insertion

Didn't read the question, did I. My solution was just wonderful at solving
another problem. You shouldn't have to do anything to Samba, it is a
question of having the local filesystem automatically mounted when the
media is changed. Samba can share out any local filesystem, and doesn't
care if the local filesystem comes and goes.

You have a choice of autofs, amd automount or supermount. You can read
about supermount at
http://www.geocities.com/alexismikhailov/supermount.README.txt. There is
an amd HOWTO somewhere. Autofs is available from kernel.org.

The difference betweeen amd and the other two is that amd is a very
portable solution. The other two are Linux-specific.
