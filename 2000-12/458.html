Wrong David, but anyway:

1) securetty
In Redhat 6.2 you just add plain numbers. There's a bug in PAM (I think)
which may or may not have been fixed in RedHat 7.0. If the plain numbers
don't work, try prefixing them with pts:

EITHER
0
1
2
3
4
5
6
...

OR
pts0
pts1
...

2) usertty
There is no usertty on my system, but I suspect it has the same format
as securetty and you could just clone them.

Incidentally, the r-utilities are generally quite, well, evil. You
really should be using ssh; current DNS is hopelessly insecure and the
most common port scans look for r-utility holes.
