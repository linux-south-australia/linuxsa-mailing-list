Re: samba speed

I've snipped a heap.

I've taken a look at your PS postings and I've noticed:

* 2MB of swap is already taken
* there's at most 5MB of RAM

Now, from recolleciton, SAMBA will spawn a new process for each user who
decides to open a share. My guess is that between SAMBA, normal file
system operations and serving MP3's your machine is probably swapping
out at some stage...

My advice would be this:

* find a spare stick of memory even if you have to `borrow' it from
another computer
* stick it in there
* see what happens

I would assume this will help.

Furthermore, seeing you have a legacy machine I suspect you have a
legacy drive. You wouldn't BELIEVE how slow legacy drives are in
comparison to modern drives - try doing hdparm -tT on it and see what
you come up with...

Whilst this may be a SAMBA speed problem, I suspect that it may be just
as much a hardware speed problem too.
