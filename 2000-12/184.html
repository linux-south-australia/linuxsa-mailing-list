> My fathers family lives in a small town near Trier in the former
> West Germany. The town only has a population of about 2000 and in
> Germany is termed a Village or "Dorf" thats where the name

Hahn is a rooster.  In the case of Hahndorf, it was the name of the
bloke who founded it.

> Anyway despite the fact that this town is small, they have fully
> fledged ISDN. My uncle has his entire house ISDNified. He runs it
> all through his computer and can monitor or pickup any line in the
> house.

Yes, but that's a private PABX (pretty normal stuff in Germany).  How
much did he pay for it?

> He can use 2 B Channels to access the internet through T-Online the
> equivalent of BigPond here, ie. the biggest telco DeutscheTelecom's
> Internet arm.  He gets charged roughly 1DM per 30minutes I
> think. Thats using 128K.

If that's the case, prices have dropped rapidly.  But I suspect you're
talking about the access prices.  Even then, that's cheap for Germany.
The latest prices I have seen for Internet access in Germany are round
DM 0.049 per minute.  You used to have to add to that the cost of the
local call (DM 0.08 per minute), but I'm not sure they haven't made an
exception now.
