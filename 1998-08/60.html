Re: serial bamboozle

I've set up dumb terminals (eg 286's with telix) over null-modem cables,
and modems, before. The main problem I had was just making sure
everything was talking to each other at the right speeds, etc.
Have you tried loading minicom on the linux machine, and telix/minicom on
the dumb terminal, and tried typing stuff and seeing if it turns up at the
other end?

If your inittab is correct, have you checked to see what it is doing with
syslogd, etc? I havent used the xmodem config util, and my modem is
external, so I'm not sure there. 

Depending on how new the PC that is acting as a dumb terminal is, you may
or may not have FIFO buffers on the uarts. Internal modems usually come
with a decent one, but I found my 286s could not cope with very fast
serial speeds at all. I had to run them at 19200 in the end, or else you
dropped characters every now and then. Still, that was far and away
enough for most applications. (eg. IRC, remote debugging.)

Make sure you dont have gpm or other mouse apps loading, since they will
be trying to use the same serial ports. Check /etc/rc.d/rc.<yourrunlevel>
for them. Since I sometimes wanted to have a mouse, and sometimes run
remote terminals, I set up a runlevel with no mouse drivers, and inittab
loading for remote axs, and another runlevel with no remote axs, and mouse
drivers. a quick telinit can switch between them.


good luck,
Toby
