Re: Significance of MX numbers

On Sun, 30 Aug 1998, Steven Bolbot wrote:

> I run my own SMTP/POP server on my linux box under my own domain name, and
> i've set up MX records like so:
> 
> @                       IN      MX      20      smtp.tne.net.au.
> @                       IN      MX      10      mail.thunderbolt.net.au.
> 

the Lower the number the higher the priority, mail is sent to machines in
order of priority for example this is my MX record:

ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 10, mail exchanger =ukraine.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 20, mail exchanger =kiev.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 50, mail exchanger =uqmail.humbug.org.au
ukraine.humbug.org.au   preference = 100, mail exchanger =mail.humbug.org.au

So mail is first sent ukraine (my comercial dial up) itself, if that does 
not respond then kiev is tried (my student dial up at uni), if that
doesnot respond(and unless your in the uq net it won't) mail goes to
uqmail.humbug also known as adams.humbug is tried, this will accept as it
is on a permanent link if for some reason it is not up then mail goes to
mail.humbug also known as zerlagal.humbug 

The thing that conceernis me is that your MX seems to be pointing at your
machine in both cases as the @ tells the DNS that it is talking about the
host machine.
