ethernet woes

hi people.

I have a problem, it is to do with etherrnet. It takes a bit of 
explaining, so unless your really enthusiastic, stop reading now.

Ok.

If I telnet to the box in question (redhat 5.0/kernel 2.0.32/SMP dual p90) i 
can log in OK, but as soon as I send a large amount of data over the 
link - it either stops straight away, gets realy jerky and then stops, 
or sometimes its ok.

the best example of the brokeness is ftp.
as soon as it goes to open the other connection (meaning the DATA 
connection) it dies instantly.

Other times, the FTP DATA connection will work ok, but only sne 1024 bytes.

Here we get to (i hope) the important bits.

1024 is the magic number. Transfers bigger then this are probably not 
going to work.

The kernel has been compiled with Masquerading, Firewalling, IP 
Accounting, IP aliasing, ... i think thats it...

Oh yeah - web pages dont work either.

Now - could it be a MTU problem?
Should I try testing it isolated from the rest of the network?
Could it be a router somewhere causing the problem?
