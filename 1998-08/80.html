Printers

While on the topic of printers.

The best way to find out if your printer is supported is to consult the
manual that should have come with your printer and look for the section
that describes what printers your is compatible with.

Then check to see if *ANY* of the printers are supported under Linux.

Most of the printer support under Linux comes from ghostscript.  That is, 
that most unix progams talk text or postscript.  if your printer
supports text then it will do that under Linux.  If you printer supports
graphics and doesn't support postscript then you need to convert from
postscript to your printers native langauge.

To check compatibility with your printer (and it's compatibile modes)
check out 

	http://www.comp.lancs.ac.uk/~damon/builder/index.html

And then you will know if your printer is supported.

(So yes the BJC-4000 is supported :).
