Presentation:

    Internet attack case studies
    ============================

	This talk analyzes (approx) 10 attacks made on a web site
	(2 linux hosts + 1 solaris host) over the last 3 months.
	These `internet hacker attacks' ranged in strength from
	mild to medium, and they all seem to have failed.
	However, they provide a useful starting point for
	understanding the activities of the sea of disgruntled
	humanity which knocks constantly on the doors of
	unwary sites looking for an unguarded entrance.

	Comprehensive defence strategies will _not_ be presented,
	although some elementary defensive tactics will be discussed.

	The emphasis will be on understanding TCP/IP and UDP/IP
	through analysis of individual packets with linux
	tools such as `tcpdump' and `ethereal', and responding to
	attacks with the aid of the `dig' and `whois' tools to
	find a responsible person to inform about the attack.

	Much of the material is necessarily in text form.
	So preferably bring binoculars if you arrive late.

	If you wish to prepare in advance for this talk, see
	http://www.topology.org/attacks/
	which contains logs of most of the attacks to be discussed.
