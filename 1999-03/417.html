Yes, and to FIX the problem, which is probably what he want's to know,
you need to do this:
boot up your PC, as per usual, and when you get the lilo prompt, enter
linux single

instead of hitting enter for the default, or just typing linux<enter>./
If you call linux something else, obviously type that instead.
(newlinux, linux223, or whatever. linux is the default though from a new
install)

This will now boot up and place you into a root shell.
type
passwd root
and enter a new root passwd in.
type reboot or shutdown to restart the PC, and this time load linux as
normal.
Now, you should be able to log in as root with the new password.
In the future, to adjust a users password, type "passwd username" as
root, instead of su-ing to them. If you do need to su to a user to do
sometihng, I reccommend using the -l option to do a full login. man su
for more information.
Alan kennington seems to have forgotten about this.
When su-ing to root, you retain your local real user ID, unless you su -
or su root -l, which does a full root login.

Oh, indeed. But would u rather have a system where anyone could hack
into it remotely, and do whatever they liked? If you've got a non-online
system, maybe it's not a problem, but these days more and more computers
are being hooked up to LANs or the internet.
Security is a good thing to get into a habit about. Maybe not extremes,
but hey, an analogy:
Do you ever forget your PIN number for a
bankcard/keycard/securitysystem/etc ?
Now, wouldn't it be much easier if you could just walk up to an ATM and
put your card in and get your money?
Do you ever get locked out of your house? Ever locked your keys in your
car?
Wouldn't it be soo much easier if you just didn't have any locks?
"All this 'security' stuff has traded off the functionality of my
car/house/bankaccount!"

But, what happens when you get home, and find someone has stolen your
lockless car, and entered your lockless house, and taken your PC, with
full access to all your precious data? THey could even drive away up to
the local nuclear power plant, walk right in, and meltdown the core,
without ever needing any of those bothersome keys or PINs or security
badges, that just get in the way of everyday 'functionality'. 

Now, sure, there's a balance between the two extremes. But even if you
have a good lock n bolt on your front door, it's no good if you've left
a window wide open around the back, and have your four digit PIN numbers
the same on all your bankcards, and left written on a scribbled piece of
paper in your wallet.

but hey, go on, leave your root password as <enter>, because life and
