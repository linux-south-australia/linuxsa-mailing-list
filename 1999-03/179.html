gxTar 0.1.0
Chris Rogers - March 10th 1999, 22:00 EST
gxTar is (yet another) Gnome/Gtk+ front-end to common archive utilities
under unix. The goal is to make it the winzip of the unix world. Currently
zip, gzip, tar, bzip2, and arj are supported. Current functionality
includes the ability to open and create archives, and to add, delete,
extract, and view files. Drag & drop support for adding and opening files
from gmc and other Gtk+ apps.
Changes: Multiple files can be added at once, and the path to all helper
applications is now user configurable. Also other misc bugfixes, see the
Changelog for a full list.
