Importing Books - Duty & Taxes - Update

Customs tells me there are:

  No Duties and
  No Taxes

on Books imported into Australia

BUT (as another correspondent has noted)

If the value on the Invoice exceeds:

  $ 250.00 (if it's by Sea or Air Freight)
  $1000.00 (if it's by Australia Post)

one must "Formally Clear" the goods thru
Customs - with a formal lodgement *and*
a fee of

  either $ 51.40 (Sea Freight)
      or $ 44.55 (Air  " or Post)  (sic)

FedEx said they charge $ 32.49 to clear
a shipment - IF they're transporting it

(DON'T put shipping charges on Invoice!)

An order for 31 books would, even without
shipping on the invoice, be above $ 1000.

Their FIMS service corresponds to DHL's
WorldMail service (prices vary with the
US customer, i.e. the book seller...).

So the cost - per book - could rise to:

  $ 49.00  (saving $11 or $21)

Replacement cover may not be needed, if
the transport company includes some ($9
per Kg was FedEx's parameter).

It would be best to see what the bookie
has to offer on shipping rates, I'd say
