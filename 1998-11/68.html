    >2) i have the rh powertools 5.1 cd, and it says it has kernels up to
>2.0.24, and experimental kernels. i cant find them on any of the six cds,
>(except for 2.0.34), and i want to try out experimental kernels. i dont
>have enough money/time/etc to download > 6 meg often, and if that was the
>only way, i wouldnt bother. does anyone know where they are?

    The current experimental kernels are the 2.1.x range. If you can't be
bothered downloading them, don't bother with experimental kernels - the
recent ones need alot of new software versions that don't come on the RH5.1
cd.

    If you do feel so inclined to get them, check out
ftp://ftp.kernel.org/pub/linux/kernel/v2.1
    Take a look at Documentation/Changes after you gunzip and untar them.
Specifies what stuff you need for the new kernels. Good luck :)

    >3) how do i put a program i wrote into the GNU GPL?

    Take a look at http://www.gnu.org/copyleft/gpl.html#SEC4.
