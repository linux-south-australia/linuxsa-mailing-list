One thing which not very many people understand is the EDS 
business model. EDS
> arrange Service Level Agreements (ie. guarantees of availability) with
> customers ... the minimum SLA I have seen is 98% uptime, 0800 - 1800 business
> days, 97% all other hours. Higher is quite common. For a 98% service, this
> means that if the service is not available (eg. a file service) for more than
> 49 / 50 hours during business , EDS will pay the customer penalty rates. There
> is also a limit to the number of outages within a month ie. within a 98% SLA
> there cannot be one outtage per week for that 50th hour (ie. 49/ 50)
>
True, and as far as I'm concernerd the network has been just fine (see my comments on previous mail re stability)
