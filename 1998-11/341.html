I disagree. MS did one good thing, that thing that it orignally set out to
do (before it discovered money), was to bring PC's to the home user. Yes,
MS now really bites, but what they did 10 years ago was rather good.

Personally, I think if MS didn't come across, we'd be sitting on
$5000-$6000 Alpha machines using some rather expensive Unix variant.
Microsoft may be warping standards to hell now, but atleast back then it
defined itself as a popular OS and managed to get the PC to the home
market.

Linux would have probably come along anyway, and we'd all be using it
(being the naturally born and bred computer people that we are *grin*),
but when you said something like "I'll email it to you" or "Yeah, just
check out my webpage, the details are there" people would either wonder
what the hell you are talking about, or tell you that they've got better
things to do than trek all the way down to the Uni just to get the file.

Yes, Microsoft is definetely a bad thing on the whole, but if it weren't
for them who would have brought the PC to the home consumer market? Apple
failed in its early stages because it didn't support a uniform low
resolution. Unix is just too goddam complex and expensive for your average
Joe Bloggs. And Linux... back then Linux was just an idea in some poor
students mind.

Don't interpret me as a Microsoft lover here - I hate their policies with
every core of my being. But it would be nice if people stopped and thought
"Hey, without Bad.Company.From.Hell, we wouldn't have this, this, or this,
would we?"
