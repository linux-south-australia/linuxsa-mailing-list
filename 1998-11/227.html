Re: SCSI Controllers for a Hard Drive

Heyas Kirsty,
I have used the following SCSI cards under linux, all with No Hassles (tm).
Adaptec 1535 (ISA)
Adaptec 1542CP (ISA)
2 different Future Domain cards. (sorry was a long time ago. One ISA, one
PCI)
A Seagate ST-02 (ancient 8bit scsi card.)
Adaptec 2940UW (PCI, ultra wide, my current card.)
Symbios NCR810 (PCI, icky!)

When buying a SCSI controller, I definately reccommend Adaptec. Except for
the cheapo/freebie cards like the 1515's, or the like, which are designed
for Scanners, Zip drives, etc., they are all of good design.
The Future Domain cards seemed fairly good, but not as good ;)
The Seagate was so old as to be silly.
The Symbios was a fairly budget card, but it did the job...With very minimal
options, or anything.
