I don't use it myself, but I put it in front of people who have never
used UNIX before and find that it *dramatically* reduces their learning
curve.  There's no place like home, right?

A note of warning, though:  Either later today or tomorrow some 
postings will be made to bugtraq regarding some rather large security
holes in kppp and klock (the screen lock utility), as well as all the
.kss screensavers.  Suggestion:  Remove the setuid bit from those
files.

Holes include buffer overflows and over-entheusiastic trust given to
$PATH and $HOME.  It's relatively simple to end up with a setuid shell
by spending two minutes writing a shell script and getting one of the
setuid KDE programs to run it.

It's fine if you turn off those setuid flags;  Brilliant for newbies.
