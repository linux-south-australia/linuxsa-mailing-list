You just described 'par'.  'fmt' happily reformats paragraphs to '-n'
columns, but 'par' has the advantage that it keeps the message quoting
(multiple levels, different chars all work) on the left, out of the
reformatted text.

In vi (the one true editor), you then just pipe the current paragraph
through fmt/par (eg. }!fmt or V!fmt in vim).  BTW, I'd be most amazed
if Emacs (and pine) don't have the capacity to turn on/off line wrapping.
(Of course vi(m) does ;-)
