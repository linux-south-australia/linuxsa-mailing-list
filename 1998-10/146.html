Yep, it shouldn't be hard at all.  You can authenticate using PAP just
like most other ISPs around the place.

Here are the simple instructions; let the list know if they work for
you or not.

Start X (either as root, or 'su' to root).
Start 'modemtool'.
Select the COM port that corresponds to your modem.
Start 'netcfg'.
In the 'Nameservers' box put in just one line corresponding to the IP
address of the Camtech nameserver.
Click: interfaces, add, ppp, ok.
Fill in the blanks (Camtech dialin number), your login name, and your
password.
Turn on 'Use PAP authentication'.
Click 'Customize'.
Turn on 'Allow any user to (de)activate interface' (if you so desire).
Click 'Networking'.
Turn off 'Restart PPP when connection fails'.
Click 'Done'; there should now be two lines in the 'Interfaces' window
('lo' and 'ppp0').

That should be it!  (Hopefully I didn't forget anything)

To test, click on the PPP interface, then click 'Activate'.  The modem
should start dialing in a few seconds time.  Run:
tail -f /var/log/messages
to see interesting messages as they happen.  To hang up, you can use
the 'Deactivate' button.  If things don't work, you can get more info
out of the messages file by turning on 'Debug connection' (under
edit/customize -> communication in netcfg)

If you want to start/stop the PPP interface without using netcfg, you
can use either 'usernet' (the graphical push-button interface) or
ifup/ifdown.  I recommend ifup/ifdown:

To start: /sbin/ifup ppp0
To stop: /sbin/ifdown ppp0
