Re: Time and Daylight Saving

Hi Darryl,

I use ntpdate from the xntp package.  I understand this is in the contrib
stuff for redhat, although I don't have any personal experience of redhat
distributions.

You can also get it from ftp://ftp.udel.edu/pub/ntp/xntp3-5.93.tar.gz and
other miscellaneous patches in this directory.  I'm not sure if this
actually writes into the cmos clock, but you could always run "clock -w" or
similar after ntpdate runs successfully.

The package also has a daemon that can be an ntp server for your local net
if required.  I decided not to do this as it requires some additional
memory, and as we are on the end of a fairly heavily loaded modem
connection, the times would probably not be that stable anyway.

If you then run samba on the linux box, you can also do a "net time
\\linuxserver /set /yes" in startup scripts for win95 type boxes to keep
them all in sync.

Cheers,
Martin
