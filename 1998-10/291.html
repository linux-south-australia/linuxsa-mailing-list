Assuming that you *have* to connect to an ODBC database (there are a plethora
of options under Linux, free and otherwise).  Then PHP is a really good way 
to go.  PHP is kind of like a typeless C.  Very easy to program in.

There is also interfaces for C and Perl for most databases, depending on what 
your particular forte is.

> Even if someone knows of a way to convert an Access database to something
> that will allow me to do this under linux.

Although I don't know what sort of export formats ACCESS has, but if you can export
it as an SQL dump with CREATE/INSERT commands then you should be able to bring it
in (with a little massaging) into any of the free or proprietary databases available.

Alternatively, you can write a simple program that would read in from ODBC as rows and
then re-insert the rows into the other database.  A bit of playing would be necessary
to get the schema and types just right.
