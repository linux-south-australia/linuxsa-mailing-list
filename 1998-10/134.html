Re: a small libc6 anomaly

Geoff Bennett Writes:

> AFAIK, with the switch to glibc and associated portability stuff you
> shouldn't be including anything from /usr/include/linux unless you're
> writing kernel code.

in theory i agree with you, and try to make programs as system independant
as possible. but practically, you have to sometimes refer to the system
dependent stuff. 

i need to be able to find out how many bytes a network device has
tranferred. /proc/net/dev does contain transfer information, but it's in
"packets", which is virtually useless. linux/if_*.h is the only place
where network interface definations are defined.

what i've ended up doing to remedy this, is copy the structure definations
in linux/if*.h to my distributions h files. this is extremely messy and
unelegant, but it's the best solution i can think of (unless somebody less
can figure out a better way).
