RE: Wanted to swap: 5.1Gb Maxtor HDD

Wow, I find this hard to believe. The AOpen is a good motherboard
and the Maxtor isn't bad either. Their is no reason for them to be
incompatible.

What sort of errors are being generated?
Can it be auto detected by the BIOS?
Is it on the primary or secondary controller?
Is it set as the master or slave drive?
Are their any other IDE devices in the system?
(eg Zip, CDROM, Other HDD)

I have been in the business a long time now and IDE
compatibility problems are almost unheard of these days.
If there is a problem it usually comes down to a hardware
failure or incorrect configuration.

The reason I have posted this to the group is because
I feel that there is "a little something in this for all of us"
Don't accept the "incompatibility" excuse without finding
out why! Never settle for "it just doesn't work".
In this day and age hardware is of such a quality (in
general assuming you buy reasonable brands) that this
sort of thing should not happen. Of course the same can
not be said for software unless it is Linux. :)

Steve if you can get that info to me I may well be able
to help you out and save you quite a bit of time and
hassle.

Nick Stock
