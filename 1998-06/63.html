Re: Now it's Quake's turn.

You seem to be running quake under sh.

Try

	./quake2 +set vid_ref gl 2>&1 > /tmp/quake.out

and open with your favourite editor quake.out and
do a search for reg_gl.  Look for the open line for
ref_gl, and see where it is looking (and failing) for
ref_gl.so

You will probably find that ld is searching your ld path
as given by /etc/ld.so.conf, and not looking in your
current path. 

A couple of options.

	Copy ref_gl.so to somewhere that /etc/ld.so.conf mentions
	(most likely /usr/local/lib).  You would probably not
	want to include a third party (untrusted) library in
	a location that it could get used by an unsuspecting ap.

	Set LD_LIBRARY_PATH to be equal to '.'.  This may break it
	elsewhere.

	Create a /usr/local/lib/games, copy ref_gl.so to that 
	directory, and then add that directory to /etc/ld.so.conf
	and then run ldconfig.

Of all the options I prefer the third.

BTW does readme.linux or readme.txt say anything about this?
