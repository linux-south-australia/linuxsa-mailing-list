Timothy Aslat <timothy.aslat@usa.net> said:
>I can relate to that problem, I found that Pico was as close to a simple
>text editor I could find on Linux, it's usually bundled with the Pine

Joe can be anything you like. You just have to spend a day and a night 
writing your own table. It compiles in three flavors. I'm old fashioned 
and use jstar. Pico is basic but you need to be bilingual. ^K deletes 
and line, not ^Y ! Pine uses Pico by default. Pico was developed in 
Seattle by the guys who wrote Pine. However, you can nominate another 
editor. Anything is better than vi. The point is Joe's table can be set 
up for the keys you are used to using, or would like to use. Make it as 
simple or complex as you like. Make you own help screens.

I am not sure how wysiwyg fits into this. What you see in a text editor 
is not necessarily what the other guy sees. Proportional fonts seldom 
work. You can make money by betting that in most fonts a lower case "m" 
is wider than a cap "M".
