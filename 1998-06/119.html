pop.lmp is contained in ./id1/pak0.pak (shareware) or ./id1/pak1.pak
(commercial) The file is just there so Quake can tell if it's shareware or
registered. Try re installing Quake onto your system. If that doesn't work
then i'd say going through the contents of the PAK files with unpak (get it
from id's ftp site) or some sort of similar util. to see if pop.lmp actuall
exists there. If it doesn't then you've got a screwy (probably pirated) copy.
I can't really think of much more, and this reply is pretty pointless - but at
least you now know what pop.lmp is and where it is.
