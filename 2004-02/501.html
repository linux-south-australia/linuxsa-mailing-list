Re: [O/T] Cat 5 cable length - repeaters

Hey,

Yes, the theoretical limit for Cat5 cabling is 100 metres, although due to
surrounding conditions, cable quality, and a range of other issues, these
cables often don't perform up to this standard, and for example, will maybe
work up to 80 metres on one day, 60 the next and maybe 90 the day after.

Good news is that you can get dedicated repeater devices. I havent had the
opportunity of buying or using one because a) apparently not that cheap and
b) there is no distance from any point in my house that spans 100 metres.
Yes, you could use a hub/switch. That would mean 2 distances of 50 metres,
or whatever, because the hub/switch effectively 'repeats' the data in much
the same way as the repeaters will.

Yeah, fibre was my other suggestion, but good point. Give it a try with the
hub, and see how it goes. This is a better shot than just straight Cat5
cable.

Good luck.

- Adam
