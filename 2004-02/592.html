RE: Boosting NFS performance (bottlenecks?)

> I've also heard that with point to point tcp transfers across gigabit,
> that you will vary rarely get full 1000mbit throughput (more like
> 200mbit). In point to multipoint you can but not point to point.

Not a bug.  Driving 1Gbps of 1500byte packets is quite taxing.  If you can
do large ("jumbo") frames, you can more easily sustain it.  PCs especially
suffer from the PCI bus not being massively efficient.

> Is this still a problem or has it been fixed? (or was I told porkies?)

Not so much a bug as just a limitation of the hardware.   You may do better
if you have a 64bit/66mhz PCI bus and decent GigE cards with some offload.

Glen Turner knows all about this.  I bow to his knowledge on this as mine is
a bit out of date and relates to getting 622Mbps ATM cards working on
SBUS/VME and PCI in various Unix machines.
