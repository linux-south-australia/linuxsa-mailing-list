Not necessarily. If you have a fast link like dsl then keeping both Redhat and Mandrake up to date is simply a matter of reading the man pages for up2date (for redhat) and urpmi (for mandrake) which are the tools for automating the installs of any errata updates that may be released from time to time. Of course you can do that on dialup too (like I do) but it gets painfully slow for several boxes on a lan.

> 
> In the expert opinions of those that have 'played' with these distro's for
> more years than I've been aware that Linux even existed - what is the
> easiest to learn on as well as keep up to date?  Mandrake or Redhat?

IMHO and I've only been playing with linux for 8.5 years, both Mandrake and Redhat are pretty much the same as far as complexity. They both have lots of gui admin tools so you can be productive immediately and take your time learning more about linux and command line tools.

As far as what you want to do with your network, I'd actually suggest fedora, as nearly everything you describe as "requirements", comes standard with fedora, or fedora versions can be found easily. Fedora is in reality "the next generation" of Redhat. It's also an excellent choice for newbies and is probably the most windows-like distribution.

Fedora also has excellent hardware detection and support, and ships with about 1700+ applications including web servers, mail servers, desktop office suites, games and much much more. But then again, so does every distribution, just some have more than others.

Fedora ships with up2date and yum, although you can also install apt-get, giving you 3 choices of automated package management tools, as well as the synaptic gui frontend for apt allowing you to visually browse all the various package trees. It also promises to provide more cutting edge package releases than earlier versions of redhat with major releases every 4 to 6 months.

Basically, everything you need/want can be be answered by fedora (or Mandrake), but it finally just comes down to "your call". Any linux distribution can too.
