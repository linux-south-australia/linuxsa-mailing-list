If it is of any use, those who possess a Microsoft .net Passport (the second
time I have had to refer to one of Microsoft's attempts to rule the
electronic universe tonight - AARGH!!) are able to 'enable' other users to
contact them via SMS through the MSN Messenger program. I am unsure of
whether this works in alternative chat clients, but it may be worth
investigating.

This is only of use, however, if you are the recipient who wishes other
users to contact you - you are unable to type in any old number and message
them. However you could tell your friends to register with Microsoft?? :-P


On a slightly different note, if you don't mind spending a little time in
order to give yourself the ability to send SMS messages, it might be worth
visiting Australia Post's http://www.pureprofile.com.au/ website, where you
earn a minimum of $1 in SMS credits every time you respond to a survey. You
can then send SMS messages at a cost of $0.20 per message from their web
interface.

Hope this is of some use...
