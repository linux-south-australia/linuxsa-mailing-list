Another option (if you can figgure out a way to make the following programme
run on your Linux distro) is to install the ICQ programme ( www.icq.com )
and send all SMS messages to phones on any network (Optus, Telstra, Voda and
Etc) anywhere in Australia, absaloutly ***FREE***.  (ICQ allows you to send
ICQ's, E-Mail's and SMS's directly from your desktop.)

My thinking is that IF you can figgure out how to port the programme to your
Linux distro then you'll escape the cost of sending E-Mail's...
(Particularly usefull if you're going to be sending a few on a regular
basis.)  Now... That said, it's only 160 charrachters you can send in each
ICQ - so after writing your E-Mail, you'll have to cut and paste it into
ALOT of seperate ICQ's.  Consequently, I guess it just depends on whether a
free method that takes time is better (to you) than a pay per each use
method that is quick...

If you have no way of installing ICQ onto your system and can't afford
whatever programme the list can suggest to make it work - you're also in
luck if you own any of the following handheld platforms that you can connect
to your Linux box in some way, because the following handheld formats have
ICQ versions ready to go for immediate install...  They include: Palm m515,
m500, 505; Visor Handspring, Sony Clie, Compaq iPaq, Casio Cassiopeia or a
HP Jornada 568.
