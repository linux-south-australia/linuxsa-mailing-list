Re: Unsolicited Email

Spam is an enormous problem and frankly the spammers are winning.
Filtration is the main method of control nowdays but that's no
substitute for being careful where you submit your address.  My main
address is fairly clean but my yahoo spam attractant address is nasty.
The yahoo filter gets about 80% of it and only buggers up 1 in 100
emails or so.

Spam seems awash with 'v', male appendage or female mammary enhancement,
and issues related to money.  I assume it's because these are the most
successful.  What drives me right up the wall is that there must be
people out there who actually believe this stuff and cough up.  It
should go past the point of irritation though and into the joyful realm
of apathy, spam is that pervasive and persistent you'd go mad otherwise.

If these messages are arhived publicly I hope they're protected from
spiders.  Many spiders are quite stupid but there are some which can
pull an address even masked by tricks like somedude@NOSPAMsomewhere.com
, javascript or encryption.  Theoretically as long as a viewer can view
your email address publically the spambots can extract it.

Hotmail is not good.  They even admit to spamming you in their agreement
with you.

Aaron
