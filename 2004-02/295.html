If it is doing a lot of I/O, does it get snappier under the anticipatory
I/O scheduler in 2.6? 

There's an article in the latest Linux Journal showing the benefits of
the new scheduler in two situations that effect 2.4.x badly; 

Test 1: Writes starving reads. 

Test 2: Effects of high read latency. 

Their benchmark results are stunning, with improvements in execution
time of the tests by a factor of 10 for Test 1 (45 Seconds down to 4.6
Seconds), and over 100 for Test 2 (30 Minutes, 28 Seconds down to 15
Seconds).
