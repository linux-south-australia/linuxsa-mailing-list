Re: RPM question

<quote who="Allan">
> I typed in (as root): rpm -U pan-0.14.2-1.src.rpm
> The response I got was:
> warning: pan-0.14.2-1.src.rpm: V3 DSA signature: NOKEY, key ID aa6f367a
> and then it dropped back to prompt.
>
> WTH am I supposed to do about that? Do I use:
> rpm -U --nosignature pan-0.14.2-1.src.rpm  instead & is that likely to
> generate other grief?


That RPM isn't signed.  Meaning you can't verify the origin of the
(source) RPM.  There isn't a technical reason why you can't install it,
but there is a matter of trust.

Can you assume the RPM doesn't have a "root kit" inside it?  Can you trust
where you got the RPM from?  If you're just messing around at home,
perhaps it's ok.  For a production server that needs to be up 24x7 and
can't afford a reinstall, I wouldn't.  It's your call.
