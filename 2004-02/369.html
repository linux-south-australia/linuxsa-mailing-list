In this scenario, the recipient is charged for each and every message
sent to them from Messenger (that's how it was when I looked at this
about a year ago)  The "free SMS" market is constantly shifting.
Free-SMS web sites have come and gone, ICQ-SMS worked perfectly for
quite a while, and then suddenly messages couldn't be delivered to
certain providers on and off.  Some British and other telcos have
blocked SMS messages from Telstra due to Telstra taking a piece of the
English SMS pie

Personally I think the prices some telcos charge for SMS are outrageous,
especially when you consider that the cost of delivery is so low and
delivery isn't even close to being guaranteed.
