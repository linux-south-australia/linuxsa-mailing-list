Re: [O/T] Cat 5 cable length - repeaters

> Yes, the theoretical limit for Cat5 cabling is 100 metres, although due to
> surrounding conditions, cable quality, and a range of other issues, these
> cables often don't perform up to this standard, and for example, will maybe
> work up to 80 metres on one day, 60 the next and maybe 90 the day after.

Err - no.  Cat5 works at 100m (actually 90m plus 10m for patchleads).  It
may work beyond this.  If you have a cabling run that doesn't work at 100m
then it was installed badly or has been damaged or isn't actually Cat5.
