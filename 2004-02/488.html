RE: Need help from intrepid SUSE users re: changing kernels

Okay I nutted this one out. Hyper threading on the chip was causing SUSE to
assume I had a second chip when obviously I don't. So I ran the install,
deselected the SMP kernel and installed the standard kernel. It now works a
treat. 

It's a bit annoying that SUSE didn't offer a choice of optimised kernels
like Slack but at least now I know, right? ;-)
