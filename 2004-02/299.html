> Forgive me for asking, and I know this won't help you with your problem,
> but is there a reason you chose Raid0 for this setup? I've always
> considered Raid0 as a neat idea that delivers some speed improvement at
> (for me) the too high expense of reducing your redundancy by half. I
> like the sound of Raid10 (or 1+0) which is supposed to deliver
> redundancy and speed, but at the cost of 50% of your drive capacity.

No harm in asking.  In truth it was recommended to me by the guy who 
built this machine.  I looked it up and like you said it has better 
speed and puts your data at the risk of both hard drives with my setup. 
  There are many other possible raid setups some offering both speed and 
reliability.  basically the more disks you have the better and more 
options you have.  Ultimately speed is nice for all the usual reasons 
and data reliability means far less to me because I don't run a server 
or anything mission critical on it and backup quite regularly.  So for 
my case I decided to take it.
>
