Telstra claimed 99% coverage on their web site. Then they revised the figure to
90%, so that they could claim 95% once the minimuxes are rolled out.

The reality is more like an 84% probability of having ADSL installed if you
apply for it in South Australia (through Adam Internet anyway). Areas near the
city have near 100% success rates because they're not big coverage areas. The
same good fortune affects those who live in adsl-enabled country towns -
everyone lives within a stone's throw of the local exchange and there's rarely
enough density or growth to justify RIMs.
