At Panasas we use Perforce.

Perforce makes it very easy and cheap to create branches, which is 
something that CVS doesn't.

We run many branches, in a tree-structured manner. Indeed, a lot of 
developers work in their own branch and submit as a way to ensure their 
changes are backed up.

Merging is also very easy.

I have heard that subversion also makes it easy to create branches, but I 
have no experience with it.
