Alan Kennington wrote:

 > I looked at the TX/RX lights on the modem, and
 > telstra router is to blame!!!!!!!!
 > That is, 8 pings go out, and the telstra router then
 > sends back 8 packets at once.

It might be the phone line too:  Your modem might be in the process
of retraining when you send the 8 packets, so it'll buffer them 'til
it has finished and send them in a big bunch.  If it retrains for
too long you might see the CTS light switch off.

This can happen on poor quality phone lines.  Hanging up and 
redialing can (temporarily) fix it.

[ incidentally, "poor quality" is relative to the requirements of
  high speed modems, not relative to Telstra's technical standards.
  If you get them to test your line they'll no doubt say it's perfect. ]

One thing that can help here is switching off error correction in 
your modem (consult the manual; they're all different).  Let the higher
level protocols take care of it; they assume they're running on 
unreliable media anyway, and they don't need to block data during a
lengthy retraining procedure to fix broken CRCs.
