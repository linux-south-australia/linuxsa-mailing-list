gimp versus photoshop jpeg compression

Graphics cognoscenti (e cognoscente):

I've just read a JPEG file created by Adobe photoshop into
GIMP, and then written it out again.
The original was 8 kBytes.
The GIMP version was 2.5 kBytes (at 75% quality).

The two photos look identical to me.

I'm astonished at this very large difference.
The 75% quality number doesn't seem to be able
to explain this.
Both pictures were 124648 bytes uncompressed, 288x216 pixels,
16-bit, RGB true colour.

Question:

Is this typical?
Does it mean that GIMP has better compression algorithms
than Adobe photoshop?
Or is there a simpler explanation?
