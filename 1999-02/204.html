Just finished an e-mail conversation with a cdrom.com tech support guy. It was
very interesting. I was quizzing him on the stories that have been doing
the rounds around about a suggested NT alternative to the BSD run
ftp.cdrom.com. It goes something like this...

cdrom.com had 6 million logins and pushes out 20 Terrabytes of data every
month making it "the worlds biggest, fastest, busiest, public ftp archive in
the world" The NT solution consisted of over 20 clustered PII machines, and it
couldn't push out nearly the amount that cdrom.com does.

But here's the cool thing...

ftp.cdrom.com is still, to this very day, one single BSD machine. All 500Gigs
of it, and it's all on one single PPro 200. It is never overloaded and it's
only limitation is it's 100Meg connection to the local switch.

For all the jucy specs you simply HAVE to read...

ftp://ftp.cdrom.com/archive-info/wcarchive.txt
