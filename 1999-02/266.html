Re: Attack?

On Sat, 13 Feb 1999, Mark Newton wrote:

Not too long ago, we tried one of the scanner/sniffer files intending to
test only our own site, and inadvertantly ended up scanning the subnet as
well (how embarrassing).  

Something that I learned from the experience though, is if you do query
the postmaster at the origin site, be sure to include a snippet of the log
file with the date, time, and IP number making the request.  It really
helps tracking down the source of the problem/culprit/mistake much faster.   


> Darryl Ross wrote:
> 
>  > I noticed this following in my web log this morning. Is this an attack?
>  > min-mn14-08.ix.netcom.com [12/Feb/1999:09:53:53 +1030] GET /cgi-bin/phf
>  > min-mn14-08.ix.netcom.com [12/Feb/1999:09:53:55 +1030] GET /cgi-bin/test-cgi
>  > min-mn14-08.ix.netcom.com [12/Feb/1999:09:53:56 +1030] GET /cgi-bin/handler
> 
> Yes, it's an mscan probe.  They're testing your web server for a
> couple of known holes.  If you look at your syslog you'll probably
> see messages from pop3, portmapper, telnet and finger.
> 
> The log messages mean they didn't get in, so you can ignore it if you
> want (the ones you worry about are the ones you can't see in the logs
> because the logs have been edited to remove traces of their breakin :-)
> 
> If you feel entheusiastic you can punt an informative message to
> postmaster@netcom.com (include your timezone to add context to the
> logs) but by and large you can expect to get those sorts of things
> all the time, sometimes several times per day.
