Re: Grabbing X-Screens

On Tue, 2 Feb 1999, Adam Hawes wrote:

> Hi all.
> 
> How do I grab the entire X display and save it to a file. I have
> tried XV but that will only take a window or rectangular area.
> I just want a simple command-line or X-based utility that will capture
> the current display and dump it to a file.

XV does - XV does everything :-)

Try clicking the root window after selecting your grab options.

You will see the coloured line around the whole screen for a moment to
indicate it's capturing it. Then (and this is the disconcerting bit) your
desktop will be replaced with an image of your desktop :-)

Right click somewhere to popup the XV controls and save it.

On a related note, a good practical joke for any windows user is to
capture their desktop (alt-printscreen), make it their windows wallpaper,
then move the real icons around. They will have a devil of a time working
out why the icons have been duplicated and why only half of them work :-)

Serves 'em right :-)
