RE: Onboard Acer SCSI problems

Its nice for this relative Linux novice to be able to answer a question
(sort of). Hope it helps.
Having set up 2 scsi cd writers under RH Linux, this is what I did. 
Before I scrapped Bill's Finest I took note of what settings the scsi card
had under Windows. Then when installing Linux, it gets to a point where it
asks about scsi support (and probably doesn't detect your card) and asks
about any special commands required for the card. The reason that the card
isn't detected is that (I think) there is no boot rom onboard to tell the
world what it is. You mention aha1520 from the manual so we can assume it is
an Adaptec 1520 compatible device. You will need to enter a command like
'aha152x=0x340,12,7,1'
The exact entries after the equals sign you will have to find out your self
but the irq is probably 10 not 12 as in my example. Refer to the windows
settings if you can.
The scsi howto at http://www.linux.org/help/howto.html will help. 
If you can find enough info to make this command string up yourself and
don't want to re-install, let me know. I can tell you how to get running (I
think) another way but its getting late this end for my little brain.

Just for interest, just up the road from LinuxSA is ASLUG. Yes a user group
exists in Alice Springs. Kind of puts things in perspective when a town of
27000 in the middle-of-bloody-nowhere has an active LUG.

Hope my rambling makes sense to you.
