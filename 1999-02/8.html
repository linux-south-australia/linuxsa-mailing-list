Final Q on AMD :) and hot chips

Hi,
OK, thanks for all the replies but..when the K7 comes out and
motherboards finally support multiprocessor K7s, will one be able to run
dual K7s straight away or do we have to wait for the kernel experts to
patch the kernel (I read about PII's using APIC and K7 uses OPENPIC or
something).

Regarding hot running video and other chips, I assemble my own computers
and I usually fix an extra cooling fan on the casing and direct
the air flow across the video card and processor. Even in tropical
Malaysia, I find that is sufficient.

If you connect the fan to the connector on the m/b ( as opposed to the
extra connector from the casing power supply), be careful not to
use a high powered fan and overload the m/b power supply.

I remember when working for my PhD, of my colleague and I using the
vapour from a dewar of liquid nitrogen to cool the insides of an Apple
II that was running rather hot in our laboratory :-)
