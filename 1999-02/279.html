I think you'll find that ulimit is responsible for many things other
than limits..
Have you checked what option you are passing ulimit?
It'll most probably be called in .bashrc or.bash_profile or /etc/bashrc
or /etc/profile.
Some options passed to ulimit are priveledged and can only be called by
root. Trying to call them as a normal user will result in that error
message. Does this message occur when you log in as root?

My main suggestion is to find where ulimit is being called in your login
scripts, and then to check the relevant documentation to see what that
option is trying to do.
