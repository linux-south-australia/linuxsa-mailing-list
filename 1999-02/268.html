! > min-mn14-08.ix.netcom.com [12/Feb/1999:09:53:56 +1030] GET /cgi-bin/handler
!
!Yes, it's an mscan probe.  They're testing your web server for a
!couple of known holes.  If you look at your syslog you'll probably
!see messages from pop3, portmapper, telnet and finger.

Yeap, and as Mark puts it; it's a probe (not an attack...yet)to find out
the vulnerabilities of your systems to a checklist of holes and
exploits. Check your maillog as well. You'll find a connection
to your smtp port and your MTA being queried for aliases for root.
(I've got a really cute alias for root :^)

I got really tired of these attacks, specially those from netcom.com and
sent an email to them. The correct email addy is security@netcom.com.
Include relevant lines from your messages, secure and maillog files and
the relation of the timestamp of your log files relative to GMT (eg GMT
+ 9.5 hrs)

Netcom actually replied to my email and said they've identified the 
account responsible and have taken action.

I don't know if that's a standard reply, but I felt much better after
that and the probes stopped.

!logs) but by and large you can expect to get those sorts of things
!all the time, sometimes several times per day.

You could just ignore them but sooner or later, they'll have an updated
list of holes and your system could well be a target for a real attack.
