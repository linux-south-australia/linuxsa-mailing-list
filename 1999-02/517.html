Re: no who?

Timothy Aslat wrote:

 > but I'm getting nothing at all from who, and finger tells me there's
 > nobody logged on which is strange cause I'm logged in as root.

If you're logged in through xdm, make sure you're starting your xterm
processes with -ut (which makes them insert records into utmp to say
you've logged in -- utmp is the file which "who" and "finger" read).

The important thing here is that an xdm login isn't a unix "login" 
in the traditional sense, but the sessions running inside terminal
windows are.

kvt and Eterm have similar command line options.
