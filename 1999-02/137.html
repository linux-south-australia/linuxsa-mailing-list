Re: Squid performance question

On Wed, 3 Feb 1999, Daniel Callan wrote:

> At 10:13 3/02/99 +0000, you wrote:
> >Hi. A friend of mine asked me advice on this:
> >
> >Would a Celeron, 64 MB RAM, be apropriate to run squid web/ftp/... proxy
> >server for about 250 people? (over Linux, of course)
> 
> Sounds pretty good but I'd up the RAM (squid loves RAM and that tends
> to keep the cpu load down). I'd also suggest to setup their cache dir as a 
> mounting point for a seperate drive/partition so you can expand it easily.

Also mount the seperate partition/drive for the squid cache with NO_ATIME
options.. (Since access times are complely useless for a cache)
This will save on some disk IO on reading files.
