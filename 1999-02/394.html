On Fri, 19 Feb 1999, ARA wrote:
> More to the point at hand, how do we get people to
> make more waves when:
> 
> - ACCC "says" the laws it enforces would
>    not preclude OEM's from including a par-
>    ticular operating system with each computer,
>    even when the Buyer has indicated a preference
>    for quite another legitimately licensed replacement?

To demand that OEMs sell computers without Windows would seem like
demanding that the corner deli. sell a flan without custard.  Sure
you might not like custard, and there would be many people who
would agree with you that coconut creme is nicer; but they only
stock flans with custard and if you don't like it you can lump it
(pardon the pun.)  You don't have to buy a flan from them if you
don't want.  How could you expect ACCC to start dictating what
stock computer OEMs and resellers should carry?

As it happens, many corner delis. *do* stock plain flans; and many
computer shops are quite happy to sell you a computer without
Windows.  If you ask they might even sell you one with Linux.
