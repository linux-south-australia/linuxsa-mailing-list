Re: Simple question...how do I change cua* entries to ttyS

On Thu, 11 Feb 1999, Matthew Mills wrote:

> hi, I am in the process of upgrading my kernel and it keeps coming up with
> a message  about my cua* (equivalent to com port devices in DOS) being old
> and changing entries to ttyS* which is the new format.  the changes and
> readme for 2.2.1dont seem to tell me how to do that.  Please point me in
> the right direction or reply to this address

The cua devices have been depreciated for some time, but in the release of
the 2.2.x kernel's the kernel will now actively complain if software uses
them. I'm not sure if you can turn this off or not.

The most likely culprit on a RedHat box is gpm - it will use a link which
RedHat made from /dev/cua? to /dev/mouse (incorrectly I guess). Simply do
something like this:

rm /dev/mouse
ln -s /devttyS0 /dev/mouse

and from then do a 

/etc/init.d/gpm restart

and all should be happy.
