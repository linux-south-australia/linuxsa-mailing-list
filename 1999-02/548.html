Re: Changing default font face in emacs

>>>>> "JC" == Joao Campos <jcampos@icat137.icat.fc.ul.pt> writes:

  JC> Hi.  What do I need to write in my .emacs to change the default
  JC> font face?

- Out-of-the-box Emacs uses the `fixed' font in
    /usr/X11R6/lib/X11/fonts/misc/fonts.alias 

- You can modify the default font in your ~/.Xdefaults file:
    emacs.font:  -b&h-lucidatypewriter-medium-*-*-*-15-*-*-*-m-*-iso8859-1

- Or you can modify it in your ~/.emacs' file, e.g.:

    (setq default-frame-alist '((font . "-schumacher-clean-medium-r-normal--16-*-*-*-c-*-iso8859-1")
				(foreground-color . "white")
				(background-color . "slateblue")
				(cursor-color . "black")
				(user-position . t) 
				(user-size . t) 
				(height . 60) 
				(width . 80)))

- For major modes that use font-lock mode, you can change the faces, 
  usually by modifying the default "face" variables:
	font-lock-comment-face
	font-lock-function-name-face
	font-lock-keyword-face
	font-lock-string-face
	font-lock-reference-face 
	font-lock-variable-name-face
	font-lock-number-face
	font-lock-type-face

- Or you could try asking the question on the appropriate list:
       news:gnu.emacs.help 
       news:comp.emacs
