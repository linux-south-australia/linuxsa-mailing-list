Neil Ridley wrote:

 > I have 6 phone lines pooled to one number, and if I dial in 7 times they
 > rotate properly and the seventh is engaged as it should. However if one
 > modem is turned off, and I try dialing in, the call dials out instead of
 > rotating to the next modem, "has anyone encountered this problem
 > before"?

I know this is late;  I've been interstate.

Telstra offer two types of rotary pools.  A "type one" rotary sounds
like what you have:  The last line in the group is the "directory number,"
and incoming calls to that line are despatched to the first available
line in the group.  The scan for a free line always starts at the same
place.

Telstra can also provide a "type two" rotary.  It works in the same
way as a type one rotary, except that the scan for the free line 
steps forward by one line for each call.  That means if you have 
"n" lines only one call in "n" will ring out if one of the modems
is turned off.

Talk to your rep;  they should be able to help you out.
