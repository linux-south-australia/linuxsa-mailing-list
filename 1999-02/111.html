Re: Installing a new kernel (2,2,1)

Matthew Mills wrote:

 > Partition Check:
 >      hda: hda1
 >      NTFS version 990102
 >      VFS cannot open root device 16:05
 > kernel panic: VFS: unable to mount root FS on 16:05

I don't think you can have your root filesystem on an NTFS volume
(in fact, I'm almost certain of it).

Is your first partition on your first hard disk NTFS?

Boot your kernel with "root = /dev/hdc1" (or wherever you've installed
Linux) and see if that helps.

 > normally they should look Like this
 > 
 > Partition Check:
 >      hda: hda1
 >      hdc: [PTBL] 1023/64/63 hdc1 hdc2 <hdc5 hdc6>
 > and so on

I'm guessing from that that Linux is on hdc (since you wouldn't 
have Linux root+swap on a disk with only one partition, would
you?)
