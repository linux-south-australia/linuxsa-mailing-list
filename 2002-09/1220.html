Ditto: Read all the way thru the contribution from Greg to find BSD take
front and centre in the last para - the NOIE is interested in Linux
because: -Centrelink
-Dept Vet Affairs
-Air NZ
-IBM
.... all of whom have adopted Linux because of "solid business reasons"
and "not ideological ones".

The business reasons for attraction to Linux stem from the 'order from
chaos' of Linux development (at least as compred to the 'closed shop'
proprietary code approach). It is the no-one owns it but everyone owns it
dichotomy that business analysts and strategists see as its significant
advantage - it does not rely upon the marketing hype of a single vendor,
nor the innovative drive of an individual or small cartel - it is its
genericness which ensures its ongoing "value proposition".

AUUG will need to represent Linux with equal vailidity that is if they
wish to leverage continuing relevane within the climate of a rising
adoption of Linux.
