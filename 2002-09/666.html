Re: [OT] anyone know of any second hand ADSL modems? wireless gateway for ADSL?

On Tue, 17 Sep 2002 11:34:53 +1000
"Mcleod, Ian" <ian.mcleod@eds.com> wrote:

> Does anyone know of any second hand ADSL modems or the best place to get
> them?  The cheapest Ethernet modems I have found are for around $300.
> 
> Is it much cheaper and just as reliable to construct a wireless router from
> an old Pentium motherboard and a wireless NIC than purchase a wireless
> router to make a wireless ADSL gateway?
> 
> Wireless routers go for around $400 - $600.
> 
> So the total cost of setting up and obtaining the equipment for a wireless
> ADSL connection is around $900 - $1000 - surely there must be a cheaper
> way??
> 
> How about a PCI ADSL modem on an old Pentium motherboard with a wireless
> NIC?
> 
> Any ideas?
> 

Ian, 

Yes you can use a wireless network card and a ADSL card in the same machine as a router. You need to get a card that uses the Prism chipset. You then reflash the card to allow the card to support Access Point. Note: make sure you firewall your router... Restrict DHCP if you use it, etc..

Have a read through some of the pages at air-stream.org.
