You know this just occured to me...

I completely forgot until you brought it up again....

Here at my small company we have a Netgear Firewall Router that provides

1. # of web site hits in a time period
2. Bandwidth use by ip address
3. Bandwidth useage by service

It doesn't say exactly who did what, however.

It cost around $250 US, if I remember correctly.

It has NAT, but I only use it to put my routers on different subnets...

It is a Netgear FR 314, and can be found at: http://www.netgear.com

We also have a DSL router/modem.

Nice web interface.

Obvious downside is that it can't be customized to the extent Linux or
Free BSD is.

Hope this helps....
