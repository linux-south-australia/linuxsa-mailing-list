Re: [OT] Adelaide Uni - "The Microsoft Way"

List,
I don't think people really expected the uni had become a sub-branch of MS. 

If you look at it rationally though the management of the
network/services/support in an organisation of this size is not something
that can or will change overnight about the only thing that can is the
location of the respective boxes. 

In all instances and I think at some point someone will stand up and say we
need to standardise our setup for economies of scale. The CS and
Engineeering will probably be exempt being allowed to run their own setups
providing variety for teaching purposes whereas the users with PCs whose
sole purpose is WP/Spreadsheets/Calender will benefit from one common
approach. Be it MS or Linux or (insert flavour here) for those of us in the
'business world' we have all seen this. 

The choice of an individual to use an alternative O/S setup will probably
be at their discretion and if they are cluey enough to say I need to use an
open source based system then they are probably in a better position to
look after themselves than the person who wants to use the office based
software.

I also feel no matter how much we hate/like MS there is a degree of comfort
for management chosing MS rather than a technically superior alternative
(similar to the IBM mentality 20 years ago - no one got fired buying IBM)

Evan
