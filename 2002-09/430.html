Re: DNS Question

My understanding is this:

- the actual numbers really don't matter.  you can use 0,1,2 etc.
- however, originally, I belive it was to show the relative 
"cost"/"distance"/"usefulness" of delivering to a secondary mailserver.
- but, like a lot of things, this has been lost in time.

To be honest, the ONLY reason to have secondary MXs these days is if you 
experience very high loads or you have an unreliable link.  Otherwise 
they're not very useful as it's better to leverage a tiny bit of spool on 
many computers than overloading the spool on one.

(I know different people have very different ideas on this, but this is my 
opinion, and I have some experience to back it up).
