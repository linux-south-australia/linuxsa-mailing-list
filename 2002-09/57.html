Re: X-ray machines and high-tech hardware

Toby Corkindale wrote:

> Does anyone have solid evidence about what this does to your hardware?

X-rays are electromagnetic radiation, which has zero effect on magnetic
storage media, and isn't likely to affect electronics at the power
levels used.

Ironically, you are more likely to cause damage by not putting your data
through the x-ray system, as the airport metal detector that it will
otherwise pass through uses a magnetic field to determine if you are
carrying lethal nail clippers or not.

To top it off, the most dangerous place for your laptop is in the air
itself, as at 10000m cruising altitude, you're likely to be exposed to
all sorts of interesting radiation that the atmosphere normally protects
you from at sea level.

Have a nice flight :)
