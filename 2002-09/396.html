RE: mounted floppy

Yes, and similarly you need to watch out for opening extra shells, such
as with su. If you change user whilst in the directory the new shell
stacks on top and the old one stays in the directory you were in when
you did the su. So if you were in /mnt/floppy and did an su to root,
then you change dir out and cant unmount the dir because of the old
shell.

You can also do an "lsof |grep floppy" (lsof = LiSt Open Files) to see
if any process has any open files there that you may have missed.
