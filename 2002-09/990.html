David Fitch said:
> On Mon, 2002-09-23 at 15:35, Darryl Ross wrote:
>> One way is to put into a crontab a job to ping something on the net
>> every 5 or 10 minutes.
>
> or just fetch/check your pop/imap mail every 15 mins or so
> (what I used to do)

I think something other than pinging a website to keep the connection
alive is a good idea also. Some ISPs have got wise to the fact that people
are just pinging servers to keep their connection alive and the ISP's
inactivity timers have been set up to not count pings as activity.

You might want to check your mail regularly as was already suggested or
perhaps fetching a small web page regularly. You can just keep a web
browser open to a page that refreshes itself regularly. There are such web
pages available on the web for just this purpose, or you can easily make
your own with a few lines of javascript or a html meta tag in a html file
and upload it to a web server.
