[ANNOUNCE] LinuxSA September 2002 Meeting

Hi all,

Time for the LinuxSA September 2002 Meeting...

Here are the usual details:

  When:   7:00pm (doors open 6:45pm) on
          Tuesday, 17th September, 2002
  Where:  2nd Floor
          Compaq Computer
          139 Frome Street
          Adelaide SA
  Cost:   FREE
  Who:    Anyone and everyone.
          No pre-registration necessary.

Presentation:

  Dan Shearer will be giving a talk on "Running Linux on IBM
  mainframes":

  Step 1. Buy a mainframe.

  Step 2. You can't afford that, so install Hercules from
          http://www.conmicro.cx/hercules, which implements mainframe
          architectures in software

  And from now on, this presentation is about running Linux on simulated
  architectures under either Unix of some kind or even Windows 2000. There
  are three questions we'll look at:

  1. How (Getting started with 64-bit mainframe Linux)

  2. Why this particularly one simulation? (In addition to the main
     fact that it is very nifty)

  3. Why emulation in general?

Extras:

  As per usual, if you are having a problem with your Linux
  installation, feel free to bring your machine, but please warn
  organisers@linuxsa.org.au beforehand.

For more information:
