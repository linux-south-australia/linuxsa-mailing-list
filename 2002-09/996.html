I too have the same degree, and it was 1/2 way through 2nd year of my 
course when they dropped the stats/maths requirement. Contrary to you 
guys though, I was extremely happy when this happened, because it meant 
I could do virtually all of the available CS subjects and avoid doing 
any more "several variable calculus", which I hated, did not understand, 
(somehow passed), and have never had any use for in real world programming.
Obviously logic / binary etc is required for computing, but these should 
be in CS subjects if they are required - I have no desire to be a 
mathematician. If a customer needs some insanely complicated maths 
coded, I ask for an algorithm and I code it. I have enough basic 
knowledge of Maths to do this, with my skill being in the coding part. 
There are much more useful/relevant things to my current career that 
could have been made available at uni than maths.
If you like maths - do them as your optional subjects, if it only bores 
you and you will get nothing out of them (like me - I cram and forget 
stuff like that), study something more interesting and useful to you.

obviously IMHO,
