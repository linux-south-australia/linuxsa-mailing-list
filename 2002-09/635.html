free software?

Hi,

The question has been raised once again on my local linux mailing list
about mp3 licensing, and free software encoders and decoders.

I have had a thourough look at your site to try and find this exclusion
for free software that many people tell me exists. I found an exclusion
for private use/free streaming, and for small scale games and for mp3
storage, however nothing about free encoders/decoders.

I note that you can stream mp3s for private/non commercial use 
<http://www.mp3licensing.com/royalty/emd.html>;

However, software encoding & decodeing doesnt carry the same note:
<http://www.mp3licensing.com/royalty/software.html>;

So, as I understand streaming, you usually run a software encoder and
then feed the encoded data in to your streaming app. For non-commercial
use, does this require a license?

Also if I am to encode a cd I legally own for my private cd jukebox and
I am going to use a free encoder as well as free decoder, is this legal
without licensing?

I'd like to get a definitive answer for this once and for all.
