I am sure that the point of the article is that all modern modems run at 
2400 baud (signals per second) and use a combination of phase and 
amplitude modulation within and trellis coding within that to achieve 
anything from 4.8kbps to 33.6kbps and above.

So Telstra guarantees 2400baud. The line quality determines what you can 
get out of that. And the Gov't is saying that the line quality should 
guarantee a min of 19.2kbps.
