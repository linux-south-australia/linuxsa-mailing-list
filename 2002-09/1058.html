Ian,

> Red Hat is more secure too right? 

You can build a good house out of plain dirt, water and straw. You can
build a bad house out of bricks and steel. You can, if you really try,
make Smoothwall and IPCop totally insecure. You could also make OpenBSD
totally insecure although you'd probably have to make a concious
decision to do so.

My advice?

a) stick with a distribution you are comfortable with
b) stick with tools you are comfortable with OR
c) make sure you understand the tools you will use by reading, asking
questions and experimentation

If you were setting up a business gateway or firewall, I would counsel
you not to run any extra crud -- including X itself -- on it. Because
you're not, I'd suggest you set such a machine up, it won't hurt, and
see what performance you get.

When you get your box up, you might want to talk to someone on IRC
(irc.openprojects.net) and get them to port scan you to see how your
firewall is standing up.
