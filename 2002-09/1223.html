Re: Will AUUG represent Linux with equal validity?

> On this list there has been little distinction between, Linux and the
> BSD's.  For all intents and purposes, we have been treating them as one
> (mostly).

I don't know why we have to draw irrelevant distinctions.  We held
ourselves back for years by arguing that one unix was better, or even
significantly different, to another, with the most obvious example
being BSD vs SysV.  Even though the distinction has never really been
so important, by bickering about it we confused and scared the market
so much that they looked to Redmond instead of to us.  Now that we have
Linux (which is good) we seem to be re-inventing that old war.  Enough!
Let's not divide ourselves lest we make it easy for "them" to conquer us.
