RE: DNS Question

David,

> I agree in principle but what about the brain-dead default 
> sendmail setup where if it can't send a message for 4 hours 
> it sends a "warning" back to the sender, which is annoying and 
> can make the receiver look like an incompetent.

Which is why you would have a secondary MX accept the mail. Being your
own, you could set it to a 24 hour (insert any other value) warning
message as opposed to 4 hours. Also you can make your second MX queue
the message for longer periods before bouncing, just for those customers
that like to turn their dialup mail link off over a long weekend :-)

Considering some of the kooky routing on the net, having geographically
seperated mail and DNS servers still isn't a bad idea. However it days
add a bit extra load in the things to manage basket.
