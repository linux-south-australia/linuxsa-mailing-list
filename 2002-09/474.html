Re: Shadow Passwords

On Fri, Sep 13, 2002 at 10:54:39AM +0930, David Lloyd wrote:
> 
> Ok,
> 
> Can we all become devil's advocates here? Why would I disable Shadow
> Passwords on:
> 
> * RedHat 7.1, 7.2 or 7.3?
> 
> I can't think of a justifiable reason.

I've seen plenty of stupid software that doesn't use the "standard" means for authentication (mostly older apps). They do a "crypt and compare" themselves. shadow+MD5 passwords will break this.

Most of the apps I refer to weren't native apps, though.

The option's probably still there in to provide compatibility with older systems/networks too. For example, if you had a magic password synchronisation script that copied the contents of the passwd file(s) from a RedHat 4.2 box (which couldn't / didn't need to be upgraded), you'd have to again turn off shadow passwords.

Hope it helps
