Michael,

> So do I, I have always used the Redhat Kernel RPMs, mainly because
its quick and easy....

Also, if you are in a business and production environment the less you
have to change from an original distribution the better you'll be. You
don't want to have to find kernel-2.4.2-foobar.special.patch and then
have to recompile it with foobar.special option if you really have to
get the system back up and running again.

Not to mention that special patches and other kernels can really play
up with your system. (Which of course is why I run an XFS non-Linus
supported kernel ;-P).
