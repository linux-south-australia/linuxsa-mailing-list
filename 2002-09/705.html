Re: Wireless PCI cards as cheap access points?

Quoting "Mcleod, Ian" <ian.mcleod@eds.com>:

> I am trying to set up a wireless ADSL connection on the cheap.  If I have a
> ADSL modem / router - can I plug that in to an old Pentium machine with a
> $90 PCI wireless card - will this allow my wireless laptop to use the ADSL
> connection?
>
> Will this allow sharing with other machines via wireless or will this only
> allow one machine at a time - i.e. can the PCI wireless card function as a
> access point?  I am not sure if I need to purchase a $400 access point if I
> can make do with a $90 wireless PCI card and an old machine?

Welcome to the world of wireless!  Simple answer, yes.  Multiple machines, and 
quick-and-dirty pentium, linux and cheap card will do.  Complex answer, yes, 
but there's lots of different ways to skin a daemon.  Ad-Hoc, IBSS, BSS modes; 
updating to newer drivers and kernel rebuilds; external omni and directional 
antennae, pigtail connectors etc. etc. etc.  There's lots of room for making 
things bigger, better and more useful - in hardware and software.  Fortunately 
there's plenty of open-source people doing this stuff.
 
> I presume the security is all in the software - like locking down the MAC
> address, proper firewall, etc - so no one else can access my ADSL connection
> via wireless.

This is a whole 'nuther email in itself.  All of what you said, plus using WEP, 
IPsec etc etc etc.  There are a bunch of guides out there on the web about this 
stuff - go and look at joining http://lists.samba.org/listinfo/wireless and 
have a look at http://www.adelaide.air.net.au/ and http://www.air-stream.org/

If I ever get a round tuit, I might just give a dummies guide to wireless talk 
sometime.  Or someone more knowledgeable could.  I'm finding my adventures in 
wireless is giving me the same buzz as when I first started playing with 
Linux :)
