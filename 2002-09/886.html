Re: Fw: How to install an additional harddrive in Linux

Well, as with any OS, you'll need to put at least 1 partition on it, and
then make a filesystem on it.

fdisk or cfdisk to make the partitions. 

eg fdisk /dev/hdx
   cfdisk /dev/hdx

for help: man fdisk 

Assuming you are using the secondary master, it'll be /dev/hdc. Assuming
you made one big partition for the whole drive, that'll be /dev/hdc1

To make a filesystem of type ext2, do something:

mke2fs /dev/hdc1

There are many options you can use, or different filesystems. man mke2fs
would be a good start.
