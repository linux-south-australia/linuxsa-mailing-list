Have a look at DVD:RIP and also K3b if you want GUI frontends. I've used
DVD:Rip to encode dvds, it does the whole process step by step in one gui,
no stuffing around with 5 different programs like Windows.

http://www.k3b.org/

http://www.exit1.org/dvdrip/

I installed the new release of K3b over the weekend, but haven't had time to
play with it yet, will post if I get anywhere with it, nice GUI tho, for all

the GUI-inclined ppl out there.

Both these are targeted at encoding dvds only, but I noticed in DVD:Rip you
get the options to encode to vcd, svcd and other formats, plus it outputs
all
the command line parameters in the log window, so a bit of copy & paste to
the CL,
and you could probably hack up your own scripts to convert avi to other
formats.....
