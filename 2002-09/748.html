Andrew,

> > It doesn't remember all of the pictures I download.
> 
> Why not? Isn't that what it's supposed to do?

They probably have no cache set. It's weird, but I know it doesn't.

Also, I have tuned my squid cache to save anything up to 0.5Mb of a
download and I suspect that Mozilla and Netscape may only cache things
less than 4Kb. If you turne a cache to save huge files it will slow it
down [because it needs to search more of its cache to find if the object
exists] but it will also save you from getting stuff from the Internet.
I have a slow connection, but a fast squid...it's much more beneficial
to me to spend time searching my squid caches than download stuff at
0.8kb (icck) per second.

> What i am saying is that if both squid and your browser get the data (which
> it had cached) from the same place (be it memory or a filesystem) then there
> should be no speed difference.

What I'm saying is they are different things, with different codes. Also
squid is much more tunable.
