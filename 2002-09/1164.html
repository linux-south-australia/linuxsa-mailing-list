>short, I have a free phone line that goes through a PABX here, but no
>decent net access. So, I'm thinking of either getting a second phone
>line plus ADSL (the second phone line would be used for nothing but
>ADSL), or doing something completely different, if the added cost of

How friendly are you with the PABX provider? :)

Because ADSL through an ISP is billed directly to the end user, you 
could have it provisioned on a line downstairs, assuming they're not ISDN.

Then it may be remotely possible to get 3 POTS splitters to take the 
ADSL signal from the analogue line 'outside', to your copper line on the 
'inside' of the PABX, then the last one in your accomodation so you can 
still use the phone.

>

>Internode, etc. Not the same thing as a community network.
>
Just to satisfy some of the lurking nitpickers here who would 
undoubtedly bring it up later, Adam does not provide wireless links. It 
does have access to a lot of roof space that some customers have used to 
setup their own wireless networks. They usually use MIMP to install and 
manage the equipment.
