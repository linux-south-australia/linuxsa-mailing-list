Will AUUG represent Linux with equal validity?

On this list there has been little distinction between, Linux and the
BSD's.  For all intents and purposes, we have been treating them as one
(mostly).

I have to wonder though, is AUUG, the most appropriate body to be
representing a combination a OS software when negotiating with the
government. Take Greg's mail below for instance. The thrust of the
article was Linux based, and more than likely the Linux phenomenon is
making waves in all government departments and hence rational thinking
people will evaluate its effectiveness for their needs.

Yet at the end Greg clearly indicates that his push is for BSD and
making it an attractive option for filling government roles, which I
must say is great in itself, but will AUUG, work for The "Australian
Unix Users Group" commercial Unix's included, to the detriment of Linux.

Will Linux, considering it is Linux that has turned the world on its
head get fair representation from AUUG?

Lastly, my reason for this post comes from my attendance at the AUUG
security symposium last year. It was quite apparent from vendors and
AUUG attendees, that Linux was seen as tolerable, but in many aspects  
not what was wanted. They would much rather have seen one of the BSD
going great guns, rather than Linux.

And do I really want BSD being funded for improvement by government
departments,(public money)  so that corporations can take the best bits
and make them propriety?

Have Linux groups dropped the ball on this, should we have Incorporated
across the country?

Just my rambling thoughts

Steve D
