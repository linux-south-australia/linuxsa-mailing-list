Re: mounted floppy

In the immortal words of Luke Vanderfluit <luke@chipcity.com.au>...

> I have a problem with a floppy drive that won't umount.
> I mount it with: 
> mount -t msdos /dev/fd0 /mnt/floppy
> 
> then when I've cd'd into the directory, done my stuff and cd'd out
> again I try to do:
> umount /mnt/floppy
> 
> but it tells me the device is still busy. There are no applications
> accessing /mnt/floppy.
> 
> How can I force a umount,
> I've tried umount -f but it doesn't unmount the floppy.

if you are using the automount daemon, it will not let you umount the
device since it's technically in use by the daemon.  I believe mainly
redhat/manduck use this but I could be mistaken.
