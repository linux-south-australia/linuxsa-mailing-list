Nod - increasing the power output isn't everything, investing in some good antannae is the way to go.

A few months ago we (work) tested two cabletron cards (30mW) in laptops with 18dbi 8slot 180" waveguides in adhoc mode.  No amps were used.

We managed to get a bunch of good vantage points up on a hill - I think we managed 41km or something LOS, but only at 2mbit.  At around 25km we could get full 11mbit, and we transferred over an ISO at around 600kb/sec.

(Note: It was only a test - in a pernament situation, we'd have directional antannae on the clients, else we'd have noise levels skyrocketing.)

I've since gone to Prism 2.5 cards (I was using the DWL-520's, but no longer know of a wholesaler in SA that can supply these - anybody know where?) for their hostap support, and the fact that they do seem faster.  I can transfer stuff over at 800-900kb/sec, but haven't tested it at long distances yet.
