Re: A Toshiba story similar to Geoffrey B's experiences

>Although I lament Toshiba's stated position, I can't really find legal
>fault.  They have offered a full refund, but not for the software alone.
>They say that they are selling a product comprised of both hardware
>and software, and that they can't (or won't) sell their product in
>separate parts.  That's surely their right.

I think our constitution discourages the forcing of anything on others (it's
the vibe), but IANAL;

The trade practices act of 1974 (section IV) specifically prohibits
'exclusive dealing' which includes the third line forcing of related
products.

http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/cth/consol_act/tpa1974149/s47.html

See item (7).

Note that some of these laws apply to making an offer, not just selling, so
giving a refund isn't really a way out.
