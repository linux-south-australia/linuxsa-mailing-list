Now like DSL, I don't particularly care what others may think. I state
my own opinions and stand by them. I do however spend a great deal of
time on an irc #linuxhelp channel offering advice and support for our
much beloved operating system, and I'm aware of the many and varying
levels some newbies have of expressing their frustration and
difficulties at coming to terms with it. Sometimes the biggest obstacle
to resolving a linux problem is understanding what the question actually
is.

Now had DSL been paying attention to the list, (for his main focus
appears to be his own wit), he may have noticed Rob's apparent
trepidation at becoming a new computer user at the ripe old age of 60.
An age in fact I'm sure David would likely one day wish to achieve, but
his own conduct sometimes places in doubt.

The problem with David's answer was not his desire to help, but his
intention to consolidate his own position of superiority. Hoist him on
his own petard? I think David already has a far too elevated view of the
world. He needs to come down off his pedestal imho and see how "the
little people" live.

Now finally, I'm sure DSL will respond with some more of his witticisms,
but before you do, I don't care! You have your opinions which you feel
compelled to share with this list. So be it. I merely wished to say,

distasteful those views may be for some. It would be much nicer though
if we all tried to refrain from being deliberately hostile and
antagonistic. At times I think this list is more like an unruly irc
channel than a helpful forum where newbies can get the assistance they

the crap. I've felt that way myself many many times.
