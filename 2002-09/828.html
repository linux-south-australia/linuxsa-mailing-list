funky bash tip

All you hardened unix gurus out there probably know this, but I just
discovered something very cool about bash, and thought I'd share it with
the group.

I was copying a stack of files from different locations to the same
place, over and over. To save on typing I did an:

export dest="/very/very/long/path/name"

then moved with:

mv file $dest

So that saved on a bit of typing, cool. Then I needed to navigate to a
nearby directory, so I did:

mv file $dest/../otherdir <tab>

..and to my suprise, bash expanded the $dest, worked out where I ment,
and filled in the command line with the whole path, direct to the final
destination! cool! ;)
