Re: IMAP and Windows clients

Dan Shearer wrote:

>
>Not forgetting the storage expansion, synchronisation and non-IMAP 
>access arguments, which all favour filesystems. The last one is slightly
>circular though :-)
>
Why do you say that synchronisation favours filesystems?

Near-realtime synchronisation is easy with database replication 
features, whereas similar tricks with a filesystem require application 
awareness, or a pretty fancy filesystem.

MySQL at least uses a log file for its replication, which makes it easy 
for a failed server to 'catch up' with synchronisation without having to 
compare the entire database. Most filesystem synchronisation tools 
require a comparison of some sort at best.

If you know of an open source filesystem that can perform similar 
log-file replication of Maildir-style messages across a network on two 
servers at once, I'd like to hear about it! (Software RAID over 
Network-Block-Devices doesn't count).
