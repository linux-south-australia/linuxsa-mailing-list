Re: The VM

Quoting Richard Sharpe <rsharpe@ns.aus.com>:
> As a by the by, FreeBSD's VM has been better than Linux' for a long while. 
> When testing both under FreeBSD 4.3 and Linux, I have seen none of the 
> embarrasing long pauses on the console under FreeBSD when the system is 
> under heavy load (60-100 Samba smbd processes running doing things).

I can't compare the FreeBSD VM against Linux's, but I understand some of the 
work in 2.5 has reaped significant improvements in process startup, and heavy-
load scenarios[1].  Should be good for us mortals once 2.6|3.0 comes out.

[1] Like work on the Native Posix Thread Library (NPTL) and the Next Generation 
Posix Threading (NGPT) comparisons.  There's also the deadline I/O scheduler 
work going on.  Of course these are all branches and not in Linus' official 
kernel yet.  But it all looks pretty promising.  As you can see, I can trawl 
summary web sites too :)
