Re: hope maybe?

> > However you twist it, 2400 is slow :-)
>
> I am sure that the point of the article is that all modern modems run at
> 2400 baud (signals per second) and use a combination of phase and
> amplitude modulation within and trellis coding within that to achieve
> anything from 4.8kbps to 33.6kbps and above.
>
> So Telstra guarantees 2400baud. The line quality determines what you can
> get out of that. And the Gov't is saying that the line quality should
> guarantee a min of 19.2kbps.

2400 baud is used in v.32 up to 14,400bps (v.32bis) (although v.32ter[1]
allowed up to 19,200bps).

Everything above that demands symbol rates of 3200+ (v.34 -
28,800/33,600bps) or more (e.g. v.90/v.92 @ 8000 - ~56,000bps[2]).

I've often wondered why Telstra's guarantee is expressed as baud? Perhaps
because it allows them to equate it to the lowest corresponding bit rate
that uses it (4,800bps fallback in the v.32 standard) but allowing them to
say that 2400 baud provides data rates of up to 14,400/19,200bps? i.e.
MarketingSpeak[tm].

[1] Did anyone ever see this used in Australia?

[2] 56,000 being the theoretical and practically unobtainable maximum.
