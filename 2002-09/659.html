> I get the feeling that there are many more all-in-one-box firewall
> solutions available based on BSD than the relative popularity of the two

Licensing is a big reason for companies picking one over the other. A lot of companies like the BSD license for "black box" firewalls, as it means that they can make changes and not have to share.

Also (like I said earlier), the BSD networking code used to be significantly better than what was found in the Linux kernel. That has changed fairly recently (depending on who you talk to).
