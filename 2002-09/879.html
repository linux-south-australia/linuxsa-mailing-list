On Thu, 19 Sep 2002 14:15:21 +1000 "Mcleod, Ian" <ian.mcleod@eds.com> wrote:

| Basically what I want to do is hook up my notebook to my network - I'm
| wondering if I need an access point, or just stick a card in my linux box
| and get that to act as a router. Access points act as bridges... I wonder if
| I can get linux to do bridging?

If you want an access point, you want a board that uses the Prism 2.5 chipset - these have a driver developed for it in linux called 'hostap', and its brilliant - I'm emailing right now over wireless, with one of my boxes acting as an AP.

I've found the best/cheapest cards I can find are the Dlink DWL-520's - not the 520+, this has a completely different chipset, by Texas Instruments, and they're being rather anal by not releasing any information about the chip, so there's currently no linux/bsd drivers for them, unfortunately.  The 520's retail for around ~$180, you can get them even cheaper if you can whoresale them.

Pagedata did stock the 520's - but yesterday when we went to grab a bunch they no longer stock them, only the 520+'s - anybody know of any other wholesalers in SA that sell PCI based prism cards?

I'm in the middle of a wireless rollout, but it seems halted for the moment until I locate some AP-capable cards :(
