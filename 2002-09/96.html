Equipment and airlines

Toby, 

I fly regularily around North America, and I can say this.  Try to keep the amount of stuff in
your carry on to a minimum.

This has nothing to do with x-rays, but everything to do with jarheaded security guards blindly
following rules.  Anything sharp, or actually, anything not blunt, will be scrutinised.  American
Airelines for example will confiscate bike tools.  COnfiscate.  Not take and fly them seperately
from you.  They will throuw them away and you will never see them again.

If you batteries are dead on any electronic device, they will confiscate it.  Doesn't matter how
expensive it is.

X-rays machines will only harm your stuff if the jerk behind the machine is not paying attention,
and he shoots your laptop out the other end at near light speed.

All this has either happened to me, or to the guy or girl in the line in from of me or behind me.

Try your best not to look scruffy either, otherwise you will be "randomly" selected for security
search.

Allow twice the normal amount of time at airports.

Do not wear steal toe boots.

That's all I can think of right now.
