> Are there any good guides for configuring X-Widnows for multilinugual use?

Yes. google knows about them. But first just a comment on the term
"multilingual".

Just about any default Australian X setup can be trivially customised for
Western European keyboard input. Output should already be working. If all
you want is some basic diacriticals then search google for "XFree86 dead
key" or get distribution-specific and try "Debian X dead key" or similar.

Bi-directional and multibyte character sets require some reconfiguration,
and extra fonts. Search google for "XFree86 Arabic" or "SuSE X Arabic"
etc. You may need to do your homework to know which Chinese encoding you
want, for example.

Another good trick is just to look for pages supporting the linguistic
groups you're interested in. If you are going for Spanish for example
switch on the Spanish filter on Google and search on "Linux".

The question was maybe a bit too broad :-)
