I'd agree that you will learn more if you build your own, however if you
need something NOW that will work and provide protection get IPCop and
install it. Its only a 25 Meg download - burn the ISO and install it. Given
that it will install and run on a low end pentium or even a 486 it is quick
and easy to set up.

Step 2 : The guys who put IPcop together have done a good job of an "off the
shelf" install product. Have a look at what they have done. See some of the
nifty tools they have integrated.

Step 3 : try and aquire another PC !! and on this one load up
Mandrake/Redhat/Debian or whatever and then try and " roll your own". Think
about what IPcop does and whether you need less or more functionality.

For a newbie ( myself included ) trying to get a Firewall running under
Linux is daunting. Your first step would be to make your work area secure
and secondly to learn about what makes it tick.

David said

"I don't want to sound harsh, but consider that most people whom I know
who setup firewalls as their living generally don't install nice GUI's
to administer them. Why?

* bloat
* X can be seen as a security issue"

Yes, but IPcop ( or Smoothwall or Clarkconnect ) Dont run X windows. They
run a Local Webserver that you connect to from another PC. You can also
connect to your Ipcop box from your main windows or Linux PC using SSH if
you want to edit files or look around in Command line mode.

A LOT of people on this list will bag the "ready made" firewall products and
most of them have NEVER installed any of them. They have their place IMHO
and should not be disregarded so lightly. Ive got friends who run IPcop and
Clarkconnect that have absolutley NO idea about Linux, but they are more
comfortable in regards to their LAN security behind their Net connection.

A good analogy is like buying a car.

1) You can just buy one from a dealer and use it to drive from A to B. When
it needs repairs you take it to the garage.
2) You could go to a Wrecker and buy all the bits to make a car and put it
together yourself ( that would be fun - NOT ). Yes you will learn all about
the workings and probably get very frustrated. It might take many months
before you have a safe car to drive.
3) You could buy a car from the dealer and then make some of your own
modifications to it to suit yourself and carry out repairs. It gets you from
A to B safely whilst you can still learn about it.

P.S. regarding pretty graphs - Smoothwall and IPcop generate basic traffic
graphs ( using MRTG ).
ClarkConnect generates lots of pretty graphs showing
uptime/usage/loads/traffic etc etc.
