window / display manager performance

Last night I thought I would try something radical - break out of my Gnome /
KDE Windows inherited comfort zone and try IceWM and BlackBox and
Windowmaker - all ran about 10 times faster than KDE and Gnome (P4 1.7Ghz
512MB DDR RAM) - it was like I was finally seeing the effects of my
expensive upgrade.

Why are these Window managers so much faster?  They seem quite capable
although I could not run 3D accelerated apps or video applications on them
(I thought a box with IceWM or something similar would make a great
networked video / music player).

What is windowmaker?  Even that seems faster - and there are still plenty of
apps even if one can not run Gnome / KDE apps any more.

How can I run KDE on one terminal (default) and start another window manager
in another console?  I tried xinit -- :-1 and got a basic X screen but I
can't work out how to start KDM or GDM with that or just go straight to a
designated display manager?

Could one run VMWare under another console to have two operating systems
that one can switch between?
