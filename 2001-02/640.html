Re: Modem Speeds

Andrew,

I understand you to mean that you have an external 56K modem on one
machine, and a PCMCIA 56K modem on the other.  You will achieve a
maximum connect speed of 33.6Kbps with two 56K modems, thus you are
quite correct when you say you get about the same speeds.  The speed
reported by modern modems can be either line speed or port speed.
Line speed is the speed of the connection between the two modems, and
port speed is the speed of the interface between the modem and the
computer.  There does not seem to be a standard for the command used
to select which of these two speeds is reported, so you will have to
read your modem manual to find the AT command to use.
