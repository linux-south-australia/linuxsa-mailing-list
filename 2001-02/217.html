David,

I would consider myself to be a typical Windows developer that
may, in the future, like to move across to Linux. The majority
of us have spent many years using Visual C++ and Basic, FoxPro,
C++ Builder, JBuilder and Delphi etc. I'm confident that if tools
are made available for Linux that are very similar to what we
are use to will assist in the migration from Windows.

I'm sure the tools you mentioned are quite good...however I have
my doubts they enable the same ease of development as the above
programs (have you used them?). I may be incorrect...if so, please
demonstrate the RAD capabilities at a future user group meeting.

I agree that in some circumstances Linux may be a better solution
...what I don't agree with is ignoring Microsoft products purely
on the basis that it comes from Microsoft. To answer your question
...since I am more familiar with W2K and IIS..and provided those
tools can achieve the outcome adequately...I would implement them.
However...since your example stated that I had knowledge in RH6.2
and Apache (which I don't)...cost would definitely be a factor...
and yes I would probably choose this option instead.

I apologise if I gave the impression that I was accusing some on
this list as following the "Linux" religion. I welcome healthy
debate on why Linux products should be used rather then Microsoft
alternatives...but it needs to be unbiased...not just a forum for
Microsoft bashing...IMHO.

Sure a number of factors do need to be considered when determining
the software a business should use...but to suggest that by using
"non-free" software the IT staff have no idea...and management is
incompetent is absolute rubbish.

Why be wary of Borland's motives...it is obvious...they want to
create a niche market...for the purpose of profit (how nasty!!!).
If they weren't in business to make a profit then I think as a
shareholder you would be in your rights to lodge that claim. I'm
not sure what will be the licencing arrangement...either way how
does this stop you developing open software?

Regards,
Richard Watkins

Do you know how to:

* program gnome or gtk+?
* program Qt?

In turn, have you ever:

* used GLADE?
* used KDevelop?

Both these widget sets contain drop in components that range from the
simple combo boxes, to whole pre_made default menus, to editors that
will highlight and indent C programs automatically...

Not only that, but providing you have the right GLADE module - really an
XSL translator - you can turn GLADE into:

* gtk+ under C
* gtk-- for C++ (yes, gtk-- is a C++ widget set)
* gtk+ under PERL

And even more...

> I will not be drawn into a discussion about the so-called

box all on its own, if you had the knowledge to drive IIS + Windows 2000
or RedHat 6.2 and Apache, would you:

* recommend RedHat 6.2 and Apache?
