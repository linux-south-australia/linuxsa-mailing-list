Re: Find help

[root ddrury]# find -atime 7 -print -exec rm -f '{}' \; >& log

You're looking for the "exec" option. That command (and it's not even a
script!) will:

* find all files accessed in the last 7*24 hours
* print the names of the files
* run rm -f '{}' \;
 - rm
  - -f (don't ask)
 - '{}'
  - substitutes for the filename found
  - the quotes, I think are not necessary but if your shell escapes {}
for some reason they'll stop them from escaping
 - \;
  - ; is the delimeter but it requires a \ to stop bash or another shell
from considering it a shell thingo
* redirect output to log

I suggest running it without the -exec...\; to find out what you'll
delete BEFORE deleting it the first few times :-)

If I haven't chosen the write "atime" or modification option, I don't
find the "find" man page too difficult to read. I generally scroll past
the waffle at the top and look at the options.
