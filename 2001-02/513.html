Re: Video Capture under Linux

Jason Tan wrote:
> 
> >
> > Firstly I was interested if anyone knows of any video capture devies that
> > would work with a laptop, eg pcmcia. Also USB cameras we believe may be
> > unsuitable for the job, as the cable may have to run up to 1km away from the
> > laptop.
> >
> 
> I have a suspicion that not many devices are going to run off the shelf
> with a 1km cable.
> 
> Any chance of two laptops and some sort of network,(once againthis might
> be awkward, most LAN technologies wil probably require a repeater I woudl
> think)?

Ethernet over fiber will easily reach 1Km without a repeater.
A 10BaseF interface run at full duplex will easily handle video
output.  The 10BaseF is usually implemented by attaching a
Fiber Optic Transceiver to the AUI port on a 10Mbps-only ethernet
card.  The network interface cards might only be available for
ISA buses.

You could also run either twisted pair or fiber optic modems that will
accept the 1V P-P composite video output from the camera.  This would
be much less prone to failure than using a remotely-located PC.

There are also fiber optic and twisted pair USB extenders.

The Black Box catalogoue is a good starting point for this sort
of weird stuff.  Be warned that they are expensive, so don't use
them for sourcing stock items.  For the stuff that's only slightly
wierd, talk with the industrial controller and security suppliers.
