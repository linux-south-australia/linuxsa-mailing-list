On 15 Feb 2001, Andrew Reid wrote:
> I've overclocked before, and mostly it works (for the newer AMDs anyhoo)
> but I get a bad feeling in the pit of my stomach about doing it on a box
> that I might depend on. I know a guy who runs an ISP where three of his
> servers are Celery 300s (or something round that) overclocked to 450~.
> That scares me. You just cannot muck around with servers. Unfortunately,
> temptation can get the better of people :-)

The 300A was a 'sweet spot' in over-clocking. The internal chip design was
based on those that would be running at higer frequencies. Except for
several serialy identifiable releases of the Celeron 300A, it is safely
over-colckable to 450 with no (if not minimal) extra cooling or voltage
changes required. It was as if the chip was designed to run at 450.
