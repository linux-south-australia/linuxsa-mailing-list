Re: ps question?

Alain Satre wrote:

> Can someone tell me how to change back my /bin/ps program to use 'ps
> -ef' properly?
> Im running redhat 6.2, and for most other machines I have, this is not
> the same.  I have to use ps -aux to gather all process info.

The last time I saw this happen, the box had been "rooted",
and a trojan ps (amongst others) put in its place.  The
trojan ps is to stop it from listing "alien" processes.
Whoever put together the root kit used a really old version
of ps.

If that's the case, it most likely has a freshly compiled
ps on the box.  ls -l `which ps`  may show a recent date.

FWIW, my RedHat 6.2 laptop shows the following -
[dt@slave dt]$ rpm -qf /bin/ps
procps-2.0.6-5
[dt@slave dt]$ md5sum /bin/ps
5e1725f2734365fef9e55398785f3033  /bin/ps
