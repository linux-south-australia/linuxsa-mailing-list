>Hi Michael;
>	Do you wish to also make this machine a DNS server, even if caching
>only?  I would suggest you have some kind of caching DNS server on the
>network, and going by your email you plan on making this PC the only one
>with a valid, usable (routeable) IP address (on the PPP interface).
>Given that, and because of the other specs you've supplied, I'd suggest
>as much RAM as possible; 128 MB being a minimum, 256 MB or more
>recommended.  For an extra $50 or so you can get a 20 or 30 GB HDD, so
>why not go the little extra for the unknown future needs and
>requirements?
>
>Security will be of greatest importance to you, so ensure that
>unnecessary services like lpd will be removed from startup.  I would
>recommend that you not use the box as a print server as this will place
>an extra load on the machine and open up another port for crackers to
>try to compromise.  Get yourself a port scanning tool to try to crack
>into the box (inside and outside) yourself once it is bought up.
