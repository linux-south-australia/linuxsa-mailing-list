Re: ISDN vs ADSL

At 10:27 PM +1030 5/2/01, John Edwards wrote:
>
>More specificly, PPPoE supports fixed IP, ADSL by itself just passes bytes
>end-to-end.

<pedantry>

PPPoE implements PPP over Ethernet frames.

Whether PPPoE (or PPP in geneeral) supports a fixed IP assignment 
depends on the server end's configured negotiation arrangements. In 
the Telstra ADSL 'network', as it is today, Telstra have configured 
devices internal to their 'network' to complete any attempted PPPoE 
'circuit' before it escapes from their network to anywhere that we 
can influence or control its outcome.

This provides us with no flexibility to provide end to end customer 
circuit handling, at all [though I'm a week or so away from fixing 
that via my own nefarious means with smarter CPE equipment, as 
previously indicated]

They actually have the gall to claim that this decision is 'value 
adding' their wholesale service to us, saving us the terrible 
hardship of having end to end management of our own customers.

My wholesale rep tried valiantly to say this with a straight face.

It didn't work. I didn't believe him either.

ADSL doesn't necessarily use or require PPPoE, though using it is 
common. Like most other things, it has swings and roundabouts. One of 
the roundabouts is related to serious annoyances that PPPoE causes in 
respect of MTU handling, when trying to communicate with hosts that 
have a brain-dead idea of MTU path discovery (which everything in the 
world gets right, except - oh god - Windows NT - small issue, that)

</pedantry>
