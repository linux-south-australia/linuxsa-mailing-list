which ever tool u use, if someone really wants to (all it takes is 
access to the raw read on the drive and a Digital Cro with the ability 
to 'mask' the signal) reconstruct the drives previous contents, they'll 
find a way.

In England, the Home Office rules for Hard drives was:

	remove the drive, dissassemble (steffanie), grind the platters
	to a powder and retain for 12 years.

	Pretty effective, i'd say.

US Dod (something like):

	Write the drive with inverted bits, all 0's, all 1's, random
	bytes, then zeros on each pass and repeat 6 times over entire
	drive

fairly good against raw read technique.


Ref:  www.viacorp.com/crypto.html
		Jim Heath has a good text only site with the low down.
