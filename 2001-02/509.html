Re: downloading isos and md5sums

David!

I've tried that trick a couple of times. I've found ftp'ing on a local
network to be quite unreliable. For example, I downloaded XFree86.4.0.2
onto my Linux box. I then FTP'ed it to a Window machine where it failed
to read under WinZip or under tar -xzvf. Very annoying.

So, I buggered around and got Samba to work on my Linux machine.
Surprisingly, a copy via Samba actually worked. There must be more
stringent check summing or something under Samba.

Personally I think the md5sum's placed on ftp sites to verify that the
files are real are as much to ensure you got a correct download (as in
no bytes accidentally changed) as that you got a real download (as in
it's not a trojan).
