Re: Linux Web Farms

>My company is thinking about moving from NT to Linux for it's web serving
>needs. I am looking for any information on setting up web farms using
Linux.

If you don't have anyone with serious linux experience on board, may I
suggest the Cobalt RAQ products. They'll let you and your staff ease into
linux from NT, without having to know the details straight away. The result
will likely be more proffesional from your customer's point of view while
you learn the differences between the two hosting platforms.

A Raq3 product will host around 200 average business web sites without
breaking a sweat, although there are some issues with the logging system
once you pass 123 virtual domains. The newer Raq4 servers can also handle
Active Server pages, and (apparently) ODBC and MS-Access, all on a linux
platform, which also assists with the recovery from NT.

If you still want to roll-your-own server, don't discount FreeBSD. It is
excellent as a fast and reliable server platform.
