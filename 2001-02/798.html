RedHat 7 Up2Date

Has anyone had a positive experience of updating kernels with Up2Date?
I've tried using it a couple of times for general packages with some
success, 
it didn't seem to work properly when I updated from 6.2 to 7.0 but it's 
worked well with a fresh install of 7.
Last night after updating all my packages I decided to get all brave and use
it
to update my kernel, I should have taken heed of the clanging warning bells
in
the background.
I down loaded the kernel and thought about downloading the source but at 79
Meg 
was a little beyond my bandwidth before bed time (warning bells yet again).
Installed the new kernel (Redhat default *shudder*) and re-booted.
While watching my machine boot I noticed that some modules weren't being
found.
This was my first hint that all wasn't well.
I went to use sndconfig, but was told that loadable module support wasn't
enabled in the kernel.
By now I've got a terrible sinking feeling in the pit of my stomach as I
remember I didn't download source.
So without source I can't re-compile in module support,
and without module support I don't seem to be able to use my modem to
download source.
Catch-22 for newbies.
So after cursing Redhat for supplying kernels without modular support
enabled by default, I ended up just
re-installing and vowing to never use Up2Date to get new kernels again.

David Creed
(learning by my mistakes)
