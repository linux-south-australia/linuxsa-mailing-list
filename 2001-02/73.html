RE: Suggest a Hardware Configuration for a Gateway, small webserver, etc

Hi Michael,

AMD Duron would be the best processsor you could use for a low powered
server. They are higher performance than Intel Celeron, almost the same
performance as the Pentium III, if not the same, and they've been
benchmarked at 90% of the speed of the AMD Athlon, yet they are the cheapest
in price available CPU on the market.

As much ram as possible is good. With current ram prices, it may be a good
idea to stock up on 256mb sticks, and use these as an alternative to 128mb
in the servers.

If the computer is being used for email, pop3 style retreval only then a
10gb hdd would be suitable. However, if you were to be using an IMAP setup
where all user email is stored on the server, then I would suggest 10gb may
be not enought. Even in the small business of 5 to 10 staff, I have seen
total mailbox sizes going over 20gb (eg, pam and tommy movies being passed
around the office ;-).

The video card would be adequate. I saw Tim recommended using a PCI card, if
you were to find a brand new PCI card for sale, it would cost more than
double an AGP, and if you were going to look second hand PCI, your already
shooting yourself in the foot !

Intel Pro/100+ NIC are good, high performance network cards .... if you use
them with that other operating system ;-(
Using them with the available Unix drivers, you will find it to be a low
performance card. Try looking at 3com 10/100's. A litle more expensive,
however definatly worth the extra $$$.

Using a netcomm modem is probably gonna send your cost up a little. I would
recommend a generic brand modem say a discovery modem from leader. Same
chipset, half the price. Very good quality modems.

Hope this helps.

Jeremy
