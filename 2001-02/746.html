Re: Wiping a drive

Don Collins wrote:
> 
> As a branch from another thread, I'm now curious.  How would
> those on the list go about overwriting every physical
> byte/sector of a linux drive/partition, using linux tools?  This
> is something that is often done in situations involving data of
> a confidential or secure nature, and I was curious as to the
> methods that might be used in a linux environment to achieve
> this action.

Software can't reliably wipe the previous contents of a hard
disk drive.  The basic problem is that the drives are reliable,
and this means that a huge amount of magnetic flux is aligned.
Because of the scale of this, the previous flux alignments are
also readily discerible on the drive, although at a few orders
of magnitude below that used by the heads.

The usual routine in a confidential environment is to disassemble
the drive, take a belt sander to the platters, and remove all the
magnetic coating.  This somewhat lessens the drive's resale value :-)
