Most of the "info" documentation is all right, especially the more
"tutorial" style. "info" lends itself to tutorials and it's especially
easy to use with the Gnome (and possibly the KDE) help browser. Browsing
"info" in a terminal can be painful.

However, the "info" pages that have supposedly replaced the "man" pages
generally show dreadful to no structure and are little more than lists
of options. "man" pages generally contain information such as return
values, files needed, when the command/utility appeared and much more...

Personally, I prefer the "man" pages for finding information unless the
"info" pages have a good tutorial. An example of this is "gcc". man gcc
is ok, but info gcc is much more navigatable...
