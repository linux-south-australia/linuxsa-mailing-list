Re: Modem Speeds

> note that 56K is just 33.6K with V90 compression.

This is totally wrong.  V90 is a standard that describes a transmission
speed in one direction of up to 56kbps, with a transmission in the
other direction of up to 33.6kbps.  It relies on a totally digital
link between the two ends other than one segment which may be analogue.
It also makes assumptions about the AD converters used in the exchange,
and so, for example if a telephone switch used a significantly different
converter the 56K direction probably would not work.  If a connection
fails to meet the requirements (ie has more than one analogue link,
or a different AD converter) the 56K transmission degrades to 33.6K.

If both modems are connected to the standard PSTN (ie normal telephone
lines), which I believe to be the case, then there must be at least
two analogue segments in which case the connect speed will be no more
than 33.6kbps.

Who made up that toss about "V90 compression"?
