With all the energy spent on the huff and typing over the past year on
HTML email, we could have coded the suggested filter/notifier 10 times
over by now. It would:
1) Detect HTML message content.
2) Either convert the HTML message to text, or remove the HTML part of a
multipart alternative section of a MIME sub section (leaving only the text
without the sub part, then put the message on to the list. There may be a
first line indicationg that this has happened and that any PGP/GPG may not
work with this message.
3) Notify the 'offender', indicating that the LinixSA mailing list does
not welcome HTML messages, and suggest ways to turn on text only mode.

Otherwise, we should only send complaints to the person who mailed the
HTML email and not to the whole list.
