Ah, yes.  The days of Digital Equipment Corp. -  the Oldsmobile of personal
computers.  They don't make 'em like that, anymore!  (There's a RISC
workstation that's been running since 1991 at my old job.  I think it was
shut down once, to move it to another building... otherwise, it's been
running 24/7/365 for ten years straight...)

There were several models of the Venturis 466.  I found info for the
following models:
   FR-854AA-AD
   FR-854AA-WB
   FR-854AA-WC
   FR-862AA-AD
   FR-862AA-WB
   FR-862AA-WC

These models have 4mb on-board and two available expansion slots.  Maximum
memory is 68mb (4+32+32).  You can mix and match to obtain any memory size,
such as 4+8+16=28mb.

The manufacturer's part# for the 16mb is FR-PCVAM-AC.
The manufacturer's part# for the 32mb is FR-PCVAM-AD.
You can locate compatible memory using the manufacturer part #'s at places
like Kingston Memory http://www.kingston.com and Viking Components
http://www.vikingcomponents.com/.

Purchased new, you'll probably pay from $50 to $60 for 16mb modules, and
$100-$120 for 32mb modules.  At those prices, of course, most folks would
just toss it and buy a new PC.
