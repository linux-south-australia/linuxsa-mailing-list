Redirecting nameservers to ficticious sites is "old hat", anyway (either by
breaking into the box, or fiddling with manual or automated backend
processes that update the name server entries).

Since the penultimate round of Bind flaws, I've switched to djbdns
(http://cr.yp.to/djbdns.html), by the author of qmail.  It's like a Lego
DNS server/resolver - you get to build what you want from the
parts provided.  It does, however, perform a lot more nicely under
load, and as it only queries "authorative" servers, is less prone to
cache poisoning.
