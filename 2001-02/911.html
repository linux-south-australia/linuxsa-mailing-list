I needed to bring up a new link the other day and ran tcpdump on a
single (unused) class C to determine if the connection was accepting
traffic or not, and honestly saw a portscan at least once a minute. Most
of them were from Korea. I wouldn't get any work done if I bothered
looking for this stuff.
 
On the same box I had to change from RH7.0 to RH6.2 to get a driver
module to work, and the box was cracked and root-kitted before I'd even
finished installing it. I guess the lesson there is don't leave a box
attached to the Internet while installing an operating system with known
remote exploits! The story has a happy ending, the attacker chose this
box as a point to launch other attacks and made the mistake of choosing
a US-military system as a target. We tracked it back to dialup ports on
a couple of Kuwait ISP's, and it's probably not going to go well for him
