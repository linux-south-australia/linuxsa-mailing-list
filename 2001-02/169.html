Re: Remote access

On Wed, Feb 07, 2001 at 02:12:57PM +1030, Richard White wrote:

 > >I want to be able to allow employees to work from home if they wish. I
 > >want them to be able to use Word and Excel on their home PC's and
 > >allow them to have access to their files on my Linux server. Is this
 > >possible? If so, how can it be done? If not, is there another
 > >solution?
 > 
 > I guess the simplest (and safest?) way to give your users remote access
 > to their files is via FTP.  There may also be an option with Samba to
 > allow remote access but I am not aware of it.

Samba's entire purpose in life is remote access, so you don't need an
"option" to do that, but it's not recommended that you open your samba
servers to the world :-)

If you run a VPN server on your firewall box (whether it's a Linux box
or a dedicated firewall), your employees can use a VPN client to gain
access to your internal network over IPSEC tunnels.

Windows comes with an IPSEC VPN client, and there are lots of free
ones available for other platforms, so there shouldn't be any problem
with software availability.
