RE: Dialup modem speed

Hello Nathan,

Apart from dodgy cabling...  You may find your line is connected to a RIM
(sorry - forget what it stands for), which is in turn connected to your
exchange.  Telstra uses RIMs to avoid excess copper cabling, so they run
fibre out to a RIM, and run copper from the RIM.  The RIM restricts your
dialin speeds to 28.8k.  I have the same problem in QLD, as have many others
on the list.

It seems the only way to get a faster than 28.8k connection, is to get
Telstra's Onramp Home Highway, and put an analogue modem on one of the
analogue connectors on it.  Results in an untimed 50k connection.  (BTW -
Telstra refuse to install ADSL over a RIM :( )

Hopefully the information about the RIM is correct, otherwise I'm sure
someone here will correct me :)

~Rhett
