Re: [OT] Alt. Remedy proposed for M'Soft

ilox <ilox@telstra.com> wrote:
> CNET News.com, December 7, 2001
> "... requiring Microsoft to develop a version of Office for Linux."

> Are these and other proposed remedies "extreme", as Microsoft believes?

I would love a Linux version of Microsoft Office, but yes, I think it's
extreme.  It does impose a huge development and support burdon on them.
I would hope that they could recoup the cost from sales in that market,
but there is no guarantee of that.  Without wishing to seem inflammatory,
Linux users often show a mean disinclination to pay for software,
so the real market is far smaller than Linux's install-base suggests.
Microsoft would probably do better if they port Office to VMS, or to
Unix SVR4 in iBSC2 format.

I really would love to be able to buy a Linux version of Microsoft Office,
but I know it's a big ask.  Actually, I think I'd prefer to buy it for
FreeBSD; and there lies the rub: release it for one free platform and
you'll have the others screaming foul.
