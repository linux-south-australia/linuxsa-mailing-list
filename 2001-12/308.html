I have two points to make:

1) This can only go sa far - you can put DRM into computers, DVD players etc, 
but you are going to have free reign at the speakers/screen/final output 
device.  There'll always be a mechanism to grab the content, but as you 
mention, it is an issue of convenience due to volume.

2) The IP (intellectual property) net is closing and is a major concern for 
open source software.  As has been mentioned previously here, Netscape would 
not have been decimated if they had an IP portfolio to barter M$ with.  I was 
reading Linus' book and he discusses the IP issue quite well.  The only winners 
are big corporations with armies of lawyers, and the small inventor / company / 
open source project just cannot compete.  *sigh* Why do I raise IP?  Because 
these ideas get patented, groups like the W3C/hardware manufactors are 
faltering and letting standards *depend* upon patents, which effectively locks 
out anyone Microsoft / US Music Industry / Hollywood doesn't want to deal 
with.
