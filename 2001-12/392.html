OpenBSD is a fine OS, however its forte is security. Not to say that it's 
stability is bad, but FreeBSD may suit better. FreeBSD is also an *excellent* 
OS for the purposes you describe. Seeing as this is LinuxSA, i will suggest the 
token Linux distro. Debian is excellent for the required tasks, and if you are 
used to the filesystem heirachy of linux, then the learning curve is non-
existant. FreeBSD has a different filesystem heirachy, and things are not in 
the same place as under Linux. Having said that, the learning curve is well 
worth it, for the power and stability of FreeBSD.
