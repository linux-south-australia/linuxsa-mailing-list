Licq has an option to specify a range of allowable ports.  I think I've
seen that option in the Winbites client too.

What you do is open 10 ports (minimum required is 10) 4000-4009 for ICQ
then tell the client it can accept direct connections on those ports.

If you do open the ports, make sure nothing is listening on them when
ICQ is not running (netstat -l).  It's pedantic, I know, but better to
know you're safe than end up sorry.

You can do this if you're forwarding (masq) too because you can forward
10 ports to your box and tell your client to listen on those.  It gets
around the direct connection problem of ICQ behind a masq/firewall.

But like I said, you need at least 10 consecutive ports open for ICQ to
work.  There's a howto on it somewhere in the LDP.  That's where I got
the info for a long time ago when I was setting up my masq box.
