RE: Windows DRM

> Without a doubt DRM is going to make life less rosy for those 
> who want to steal content, or even just make fair use of it, 
> but even with Microsoft's best efforts, I do not believe that 
> Windows will be the only platform that can play DRM protected 
> media.  RealNetworks already support free unix, and probably 
> will continue to do so.

Point is though, that without control over the OS, you have even less
protection of your media content -- you could make as secure an
application as you like, but root can still copy the unencrypted memory
segments, or get the key or something... MS's DRM way, the only way you
will be able to get that access is via hardware, or analog line out-line
in methods... Much much much harder... Then, when a content producer
decides to release secure content, they will ensure that it is only
playable on a DRM platform, which would _have_ to exclude anything open
source... Unless they approved specific binaries (which themselves could
be open source) that had DRM in them (kernels, libraries, applications,
etc)...
