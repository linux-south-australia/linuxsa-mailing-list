Farewell ...

Hi,

Tomorrow, I fly to the US to start a job working on Samba at Panasas, in 
the Bay Area.

While I will be working primarily with FreeBSD, I will not be abandoning 
Linux.

Much of my focus will be on making sure that the good things in Samba 
that work under Linux also work under FreeBSD (like winbind), and that 
Samba occupies a smaller footprint and so on. The goal is that 
Samba-based storage servers (made by Panasas) can support tens of 
thousands of clients with petabytes of storage, along with other 
features. A lot of that will flow down to normal Samba installations.

To a small extent, that has already started, as I uncovered some 
performance problems in the TCP/IP code under FreeBSD which Matt Dillon 
fixed on Sunday. FreeBSD now gets the same sort of numbers as Linux when 
it comes to the NetBench-based benchmark I was interested in.

While I will be back in Adelaide briefly in January, I fondly hope that 
this will be a long-term move.

I wish everyone well in the coming festive season and for the future as 
well.
