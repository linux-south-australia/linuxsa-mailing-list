RE: up2date

Quoting David de Groot <david@viking.org.au>:
> Wednesday, December 12, 2001, 10:26:58 PM, Robyn wrote:
> 
> > In Konsole in kde I change to root and run up2date, it then starts a gui 
> > frontend and lets me log in to the RedHat network and I can get my updates.
> 
> > What is it that I don't have permission to do?
> 
> Because root is running under a different shell, or somesuch, it does
> not inherit the X-Display authority that your user has, thus, before
> you change to root, run:
> 
> xhost localhost
> 
> Which will allow "remote" x connections to from localhost.

Or more expressively, xhost +yourhostname (and - does what you might think) from 
a login user xterm.  If this still doesn't work, do a "export 
DISPLAY=yourhostname:0.0" in your root shell so it 'knows' where your display 
is.

Is this under Mandrake or RedHat?  Normally RH takes care of this for you via X 
cookie herding and rangling (not sure about Mandrake).

You can read all about this stuff in section 1 of the man pages under Xsecurity.
