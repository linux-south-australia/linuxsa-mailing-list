Re: hostnames

Joshua,

> If I have a server which is going to be an internet gateway, webserver,
> Primary
> MX server, and Primary DNS server, all on the one machine...
> 
> ....if the servers hostname is snorky on domain bogus.com e.g.
> snorky.bogus.com
> which is assigned during installation, what do I do if I want the mail
> server
> to be known as mail.bogus.com and DNS as ns.bogus.com?? Do I need to
> assigns
> these as ailieses to snorky.bogus.com and if so how?

You need to do all these in the zone file for the domain. Read the BIND how-to, 
it provides all the insight.

It can be a bad idea to have the primary DNS on your gateway, it is better to 
pay an ISP for the hosting. They have better equipment, and do all the leg 
work. You can run a caching-only dns on the gateway with no problems, but if 
you have your primary dns on your gateway, and your link goes down for some 
reason for a few days, then your domain will fall out of the dns, and take a 
few days to be visible again.

You call the machine snorky with an A record, and you can CNAME the other names 
to the main A record. Alternatively, you can A record all the aliases. CNAME is 
good, when you want to point to a hostname that you may not be in control of 
and may change IP, whereas an A record cannot be pointed to an hostname. Read 
the bind how-to and make up your own mind.

Happy DNSing
