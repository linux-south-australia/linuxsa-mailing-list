Executive Summary

This letter promotes the adoption of Open Source software by 
governments and government supported instrumentalities in Australia.  
It is argued that the free operating systems of Linux and BSD with 
their associated application programs are more secure than the current 
ubiqitous commercial alternative, and would by that security and 
significant virus impregnability save not only initial and licensing 
costs but also maintenance costs.  In this way, public monies can be 
used to the maximum benefit of the purpose for which they are intended 
and not be spent unnecessarily on computer software.

It is also argued that by adopting international standard document 
formats, rather than the current commercial offerings, documents 
intended for public use will be available to all members of the public, 
regardless of their preferred choice of software.
