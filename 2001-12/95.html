Re: opensource.org.au

Richard Russell wrote:
> 
> Linuxsa isn't _that_ organised, and they have a .org.au :)

LinuxSA has a structure, there is a convenor of the meetings
and attendees at the meetings.  The meetings are regular.
The attendees know the organisation as "LinuxSA".
That's all Robert really needs.

The proposed opensource.org.au is not describing an organisation,
but is an generic phrase being used for advocacy purposes.
As such it's unlikely to get approved without providing
some information to support the claim that Open Source
Advocacy Australia is not a front.  Even then Robert would
probably prefer the name opensourceadvocacy.org.au as
that wouldn't gazump a common phrase.

This might seem a bit nit-picking, but consider if
Microsoft created a organisation named Open Source
and Innovation Australia.  You wouldn't want it getting
opensource.org.au, and Robert's rules go some way to
preventing that.
