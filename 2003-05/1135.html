Ack!

Unstable on a production server is like sticking a fork in your eye.  
Do NOT recommend it - things get broken all the time, I remember once a 
year or so ago they managed to stuff up something in fsck, so on a 
reboot, it rendered a friends renderfs partition completely useless.  
He was really impressed.

I run stable with my production servers with a few third party apt-get 
repositories, and packages I make myself for things absolutely needed 
(php 4.3.1, etc).  My web development box at home runs unstable, but 
I'm really not happy with it.  I love Debian, almost religiously, but I 
really think the maintainers of testing could do a better job of making 
a stable world with more up to date packages.  Testing has more issues 
then unstable that go unfixed for weeks; at least with unstable, 
they're fixed within a few days.
