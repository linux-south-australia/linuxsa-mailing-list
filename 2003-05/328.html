RE: [OT] Microsoft's prototype computer and other discussions.

The "athens" PC seems really seems like a great piece of integration.
There's really nothing there that is *that* complex. It's all just
pretty normal technology, and for the most part, Linux could do it.
However, the integration is something that is a long way off for Linux
-- it's something that Apple and MS do really well (Apple particularly
so). Of course, there's a bunch-o-problems, like security, reliability,
and all that -- I can't imagine real life going quite as well as the
demo... However, this integration is something of huge value, even if it
doesn't work 100%...

Not that I've really said anything significant there, but point is that
Linux is sorely lacking in this area. Stephen White once emailed a rant
about packages not integrating with each other, in that if you have
mailserver X and you spamfilter Y, they don't automatically configure
themselves for each other... This is taking that idea to the end user --
I have phone X, and email application Y, and they just work together. Of
course, they are all MS taxed, but hey -- that money is considered worth
it for the businesses who are paying for it...
