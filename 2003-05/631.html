Re: satellite connection

On Tue, 2003-05-20 at 08:15, Kevin Waterson wrote:
> ISP says link is up and working, they do not support linux.
> I am sure this is just a routing settup. The IP of the card can be pinged
> from outside, I imagine traffic coming through ppp0 do the card.

Does yout Sat connection have a different ip to the ppp0 link?
If so, try binding squid to your sat interface, using the ppp0 as the
default route, then point your lynx/ie/moz/konq at the proxy.

All packets will then go out the land line, and the only route back to
the sat ip address will be by going to the satellite and back. Thus
introducing your typical satellite lag. ;-(

traceroute can also be used with the -s option to force packets to come
from the sat connection.

The other way is that the ISP will give you a ppp address that they do
not advertise anywhere except at the uplink site, and they accept it
from their dialups. If this is the case, and you dont have a different
ip for the sat card, then i'd check your sat card setup.

You should be able to tcpdump on the sat card, and see stuff streaming
in if its configured correctly.

HTH, its been a while since playing with that stuff ;-)
