MIME.  I'm not sure I understand the help text, though.  MIME is a set
of standards which describe how to subdivide a mail message into
"attachments".  The individual attachments can have one of a number of
formats.  For example, your image attachments have a base64 encoding,
and your text attachments are 8 bit clean (i.e. plain text).  Here's
what mutt says about the attachments of your most recent message with
an image:

> [-- Attachment #1 --]
> [-- Type: text/plain, Encoding: 8bit, Size: 0.1K --]
> Content-Type: text/plain; charset=iso-8859-1
> Content-Transfer-Encoding: 8bit
> 
> Ok, so this time without going through the mailing lists, here is the
> file I sent before.
> 
> Cheers,  Ian
> 
> 
> [-- Attachment #2: LCA buttons.jpg --]
> [-- Type: image/jpeg, Encoding: base64, Size: 86K --]
> Content-Type: image/jpeg; name="LCA buttons.jpg"
> Content-Transfer-Encoding: base64
> Content-Disposition: attachment; filename="LCA buttons.jpg"
> 
> [-- image/jpeg is unsupported (use 'v' to view this part) --]

Brian's text attachments are quoted printable, which is a little
obsolete, but that's what KMail does by default:

> [-- Attachment #1 --]
> [-- Type: text/plain, Encoding: quoted-printable, Size: 1.5K --]
> Content-Type: text/plain;
>   charset="iso-8859-1"
> Content-Transfer-Encoding: quoted-printable

These are all MIME.  It was my understanding that uuencoding was an
obsolescent alternative for, say, base64.  I think most MTAs can
handle 8 bit clean text, though.  I note that my MUA (mutt) also
encodes with base64, though, so maybe I'm incorrect there.
