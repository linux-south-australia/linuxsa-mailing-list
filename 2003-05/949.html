Well, I've been collecting mail messages for over 20 years.  In that
time you get to know a lot of people, and you accumulate a fair amount
of mail.

> I have many mail folders, each folder dedicated to a different list

That's approximately the number of messages I receive every day:

  On 23 May 2003 you received 2171 mail messages and sent 42 mail messages.
  On 24 May 2003 you received 1321 mail messages and sent 17 mail messages.
  On 25 May 2003 you received 1639 mail messages and sent 37 mail messages.

> and cannot comment on your experience except to say that maybe Kmail
> is designed to work the way I do, and not the way you do.

MUAs shouldn't make assumptions about how people work, nor how
important they consider their data.

>>        It's limited.  I can't thread, I can't sort by size.

OK, for the sake of argument, I've checked this stuff.  Yes, I can
display threads and sizes.  I can't sort by size.  To change from
threaded to non-threaded displays (a thing I do several times a day)
appears to require reconfiguration.  With mutt it's two keystrokes.

I tried to RTFM.  What I get was (expectedly):

  === grog@sydney (/dev/ttyp5) ~ 13 -> man kmail
  No manual entry for kmail

All there is is this help facility.  It doesn't appear to be very
good: I can't find any mention of threads in it, for example, so I
can't say with any certainty that I can't easily change the message
display.  I can say with some certainty that it's difficult to find
out how to do it.  For example, I found a reference to using mail
files instead of directories, but I couldn't find any details.

While searching the help files, I *did* find a chapter (7) on
importing data from other MUAs, all of them apparently from the
Microsoft space.  But the idea of importing and exporting data is the
Microsoft way, not the UNIX way.
