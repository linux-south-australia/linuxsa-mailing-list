HP to move Australian jobs to India
By Online Staff
May 1 2003

 More Australian IT jobs are moving to India, with HP
 deciding to cut staff at its support centres in both Sydney
 and Melbourne, news reports say. 
 The support provided by these staff will be taken over by
 the Bangalore-based Digital GlobalSoft, which is 51 percent
 owned by HP. 
 Jobs from the rest of the Asia-Pacific region will also be
 shifted to the same location. 
 There are two Victorian operations in Blackburn and Forrest
 Hill and two NSW operations in North Ryde and Rhodes. 
 Last December, HP's services chief, Ann Livermore, indicated
 that the company would relocate a major part of its IT
 services to India.
