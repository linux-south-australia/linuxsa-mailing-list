Re: Imminent exclusion of non-GPL kernel modules?

Mike Gratton <mike@vee.net> wrote:
> I think he is trying to protect users (giving them the right to fix and 
> otherwise modify the software they use) and developers (so that 
> redistributed derivatives both acknowledge and enhance their original 
> work), which is a noble persuit. However to say Satallman is doing this 
> is incorrect. He is responsible for the GPL and a lot of evangilism, but 
> it is the copyright holder who releases their code under the GPL that is 
> to "blame".

It's going to be harsh for users when companies decide to produce no
driver for linux at all.  Some businesses clearly find it palatable to
give away a binary kernel module but quite unacceptable to give away the
source (and thus to give away the bulk of their intellectual property
rights.)  Force them to choose between giving source or giving nothing,
and some will give nothing.  It's hard to see how users benefit from
that losing those drivers.
