The biggest gripe with OS X users, once you get over how pretty Aqua looks, 
is how damn slow some things are - at least, compared to other popular UI's.  
I'm probably biased, because I have an older Powerbook (500mhz, without 
Quartz Extreme), but all the fancy crap really gets old quickly.  I love the 
OS X interface; it's a much more efficient workspace than Windows-type 
mentalities, once you get your head around it.  Its brilliant to work in, and 
you end up getting a lot more work done.

10.3's coming out soon; we're hoping things will be faster (10.2 made a huge 
difference), but Apple seem more intent on whacking in new fancy UI features 
than fixing annoying Finder bugs.
