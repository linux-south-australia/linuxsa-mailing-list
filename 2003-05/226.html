Re: Redhat 9.0 vs 8.0, Comments?


Michael,

> I think you'll see some more product differentiation soon - versions
> of distros that are aimed at desktop users verses back office systems.
>  Whether this will  work (from a business sense) for free software is
>  another thing (i.e. I want it all).

True.

I think the one thing that the companies tend to misunderstand is that
Linux system administrators don't *need* the vendors to package
everything up too nicely for them. In fact it could be a very bad model
to actually do so.

A balance between customisations that the system administrator does on
the one hand, and whether it works straight out of the box on the other.

When it comes down to:

 1) It works straight out of the box with RedHat
    - but costs a mint
    - and sort of makes me redundant

 2) It works with a small amount of documented fiddling with Debian (or
     something less expensive)
    - and costs less
    - and makes me have a job

It's a difficult call to make.
