Re: What do you do when you're banned by someone else's spam?

Richard Russell wrote:

>>yes, the whole of 202.* is banned due to the amount of spam 
>>from thos domains Sign of the times..
>>    
>>

That sounds more like a sign of ignorance than of the times.  From 
http://ftp.apnic.net/stats/apnic/apnic-2003-05-20 you can see that the 
202.* range covers virtually the entire Asia-Pacific region, and that 
blocking it entirely is tantamount to claiming 'no-one from that part of 
the world has anything interesting to say to me'. It's like using a 
tractor slasher to clear the aphids off a rose garden.

If a network must hold the xenophobic belief that all asian Internet 
users are spammers, use something like the blocklists available at 
http://countries.nerd.dk/

>Banning 202.* is surely overkill. Anyway, regardless of that, what can
>be done? I only have control of a /29 within 202, and no spam comes from
>there... Sigh. What to do, what to do?
>  
>

Use a relay or smarthost that is available to you and might be on a 
different IP address, possibly not affected by filters. If this guy is 
getting mail from linuxsa ok then 203.* probably isn't being blocked (in 
some places it is).
