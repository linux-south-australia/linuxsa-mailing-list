Are you trolling? :-)

You should set it up for reason that others can more eloquently explain.

Having said that, my site, msdavies.net, is hosted by a company that doesn't 
set up reverse-DNS.  What does this mean?  Web appears to work fine from 
anywhere.  Email works almost anywhere, except for sites that check there is a 
reverse DNS entry and drop email otherwise.  This is annoying because I don't 
see that there is a problem, people just don't respond.

What happens in my case, is the hosting company I use keeps a list of domains 
that check reverse-DNS on email, and punch SMTP through another host that does 
have a reverse-DNS entry for them.

Is that bad?  Well, annoying at least.  But it seems a common practice.
