if you are using NAT/IPMASQ to get access to your ISP for each of the 
private networks behind your router, check that you NAT/MASQ setting
will allow traffic from one private network to the other.
 ie;
  net1 -> net2
  net2 -> net1
  net1 -> ISP
  net2 -> ISP

check you ipchains or iptable rules, to see if the traffic is *only*
going to be NATed/MASQed to the external ISP interface on ppp0.
if it is, then make sure you add two rules each allowing traffic to
the other network.

also have a look at your rules to see if you have and "DENY" or "REJECT"
rules. this could also be part of your troubles...
