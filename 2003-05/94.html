P2P Networks

When I used to use Windows on the desktop, I used to enjoy browsing the
Peer to Peer networks to find new music (and yes, I even went off and
bought it!!)

I miss using SoulSeek and could never get Pyslsk to work properly on
FreeBSD 5.0.  I was introduced to mutella, which connects to Gnutella and
Limeware and Bearshare and all that other stuff, but the program itself is
can never see very much data across these networks, and as a result I can't
find anything!

Apparently the Gnutella network protocol is what Bearshare/Limeware/etc
use, and the protocol KaZaA uses is licensed through whoever created it,
but the KaZaA network appears to be extremely broad.  Because of this
difference in networks the actual peer-to-peer networks aren't totally
compatible to connect to each other, which is a shame.

Who here has ventured into the world of Peer to Peer networks in Unix, and
what have you been using?  Is it GUI based or console based, or both?  What
kind of networks does it connect to?
