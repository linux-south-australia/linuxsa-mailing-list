On Mon, 2003-05-19 at 14:03, David Creed wrote:
> Next problem was a message saying /usr/src/linux/include/linux/modversions.h 
> didn't exist, I had a look and there was a modsetver.h, so I did 
> ln -s modsetver.h modversions.h

This is likely your problem. I dont think modsetver.h is a valid
substiture. On my redhat 8 system:

[ant@ant ant]$ locate modversions.h
/usr/include/linux/modversions.h
/usr/src/linux-2.4.18-14/include/linux/modversions.h
/usr/src/linux-2.4.18/include/linux/modversions.h
/usr/src/linux-2.4.18/include/config/modversions.h

I's suggest doing a similar check on yours and using modversions.h from
your kernel instead.
