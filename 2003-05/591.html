Re: Debian Bug Tracking System vs Mozilla

dlloyd@microbits.com.au wrote:

>I'm trying to weigh the pros and cons of using the Debian Bug Tracking
>system versus the Mozilla Bug Tracking System.
>
I just filled out a Bugzilla bug report for GnuCash: 
http://bugzilla.gnome.org/show_bug.cgi?id=113196

Bugs.

They get me in a sarcastic mood. :-P

What I hate about bug reports is this: Not only did my program not work, 
but I also have to do some more junk. Gaaaaaawd! :-(   Now that's just 
me, and I am extraordinarily lazy today, so it may not be a problem. :-D

Bugzilla seems to be reasonable, except for that damn password and email 
verification crud. I also didn't like the way the keyword field didn't 
let me use "Year End." I mean if someone is going to do a search, they 
will look for "Year End", not "useability" or "1.x-parity."

**** In my opinion a bug reporting system should be fairly close to 
bugzilla, but it should allow for amendments, amendments that are 
emailed to the poster.

People should be able to say, "yeah that happened to me too!"; Guru's 
should be able to come by and say, "you guys suck, change your config 
file" or whatever and when its fixed, the fixer should post a short 
message on the page saying "It's so hard, being this good!" or whatever.

The name sucks too. Before I used Bugzilla, I assumed that it was a bug 
reporting system best fit for Mozilla, ans by implication, less fit for 
other programs. But that was pure prejudice on my part, based solely on 
the name.

So I recommend calling yours "Bugbits"
