Re: Democrats introduce legislation to support Open Source Software

Barney Wrightson wrote:
> 
> The problem with the wording is a big one IMO. There shouldn't be 
> legislation to mandate the use of Open Source over proprietary software. 

You may be right, but the important thing is that out Government knows 
that it's electorate supports the use of Open Source software, and 
getting behind this Bill is one way (the best way?) of demonstrating this.

The Bill may not make it through as-is, but it (or something like it) 
will never get passed unless we show our support.

I read something interesting the the other day (I'll see if I can find 
the link); FOSS marketing works on a "pull" model - someone in the IT 
department, procurement department or whatever, needs to be clueful 
enough to look at FOSS. No one is going to sell it to them. Commercial 
software works on the "push" model - companies actively promote their 
software. At least a Bill like this, even if it is toned down greatly, 
will help that "pull" marketing model - people will have to look for 
alternatives rather than just decide which company has the best glossies.

/mike
