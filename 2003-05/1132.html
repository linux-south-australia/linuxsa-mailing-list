You're sounding like an end-user.  A person with your technical prowess
should use a distribution as a starting point, and download whatever
source updates you think you need from each separate 'package' maintainer.

I've previously complained about point-n-click, that it takes away the
need for deep knowledge of the system, and leaves a knowledge vacuum in
return.  Your complaint with Debian pretty much illustrates what I said.
(Obviously you do have a deep knowledge of your system, but your using
it less and less.)

Let's face it, Gentoo and Debian are almost entirely, but not quite,
identical.  Same goes for FreeBSD.
