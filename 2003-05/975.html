Anyway, on the subject of Gentoo, I'd like to know if Gentoo is
particularly bug prone or just very good at finding and/or reporting bugs.
My slashdot account is configured to show linuxsecurity.com news at the
top right of the screen, and most days there are 2 or 3 Gentoo security
advisories there. This morning is a rare exception. But here's the past list:
http://www.linuxsecurity.com/advisories/gentoo.html
I count 86 advisories so far for 2003.

So if I finally take the plunge and put Gentoo on a sacrifical machine,
will I be in security heaven because Gentoo is so good at reporting
problems that other distros don't, or a security nightmare because
their packages are dodgier than the average distro's?
I know that SuSE is a real slug when it comes to fixing bugs which
other people have discovered. And they make you use FTP to get your
fixes, which is not good if you happen to hate FTP. (The iptables
facility for permitting FTP still makes me wake up in a sweat in the
middle of the night...) So lack of security advisories doesn't mean 
necessarily that a distro is more secure, obviously.
