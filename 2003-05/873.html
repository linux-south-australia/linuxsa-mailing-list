I suggest working on your web server configuration skills ;-)

Seriously though, take a traineeship if you can get one.  At least you
will be PAID to study.  Poo-poo the study bit all you like, but you'll
find that you'll learn more by doing (ie. working) than by most
traditional "training" techniques.

The best way to get a good job in IT to have already worked in IT.  If
you can get a traineeship in a field you're interested in - great.
Study (read and practise) in your own time, and become really good at
what you want to do.  You'll be in a much better position to get the job
you really want later.

In my experience, IT job agencies won't look at you unless you have lots
of experience and/or lots of qualifications.  It is, after all,
practically the only way they can ascertain who is best for the job.
Check the paper every Wednesday and Saturday.

Spell-check any requests for work.  You made three errors in as many
sentences in your email to LinuxSA.  Employers (and nerds like me) pick
up on that sort of thing :-)
