Changing DSL broadband providers is braindead; Telstra treat *any* 
change of connection as a new connection - that is, you first *have* to 
be disconnected from your current provider, then your line has to be 
activated again with the new provider.  That's another activation fee, 
on top of the usual 3-4 week wait (with no DSL).

Problem ended up being solved; Airnet insisted on Dlink modems, which I 
tried this morning (they kindly lended me a DSL-300), which so far (4 
or 5 hours) hasn't had a line sync problem yet.  Strange - I fail to 
understand why, because as far as I can see, the modem's configured in 
the same way as the others were, but the problem's solved now.
