Re: Constellation 3D and FMD

On Thu, 2003-05-08 at 01:26, Andrew Reid wrote:
> Several years ago, I read a little article about a company that was
> seemingly being successful in producing clear, multi-layered compact
> discs that could hold anything up to 100GB. The company was called
> Constellation 3D. Wired News have an interesting background article[1]
> on the company.

> Recently, I had cause to start investigating them again. For a
> spit-second, I thought there was something wrong with my Internet
> connection, as the company's website, http://www.c-3d.net/ didn't want
> to load.
> 
> However, I found some more information[2] today that states that the
> company filed for bankruptcy in December of last year.
> 
> I'm a little surprised that they went under in some ways (and not, in
> others), due to the fact that they seemed to have some rather
> remarkable technology in their possesion.
> 
> Does anyone know what's happening with the technology, or have any
> background on the "how's" and "why's" of their demise?
> 
I'd completely forgotten about this technology. Indeed remarkable that
none of the major companies like Sony & Matsushita didn't snap it up.

Pure speculation,but...

Maybe there's too much money already invested in DVD technology by these
companies, & so development would annul any return on these investments.

Perhaps the majors couldn't agree on a suitable licensing system. I
remember reading somewhere that C3D were in negotiations with Lite-On
whereby Lite-On would pay a licensing fee for every FMD-Rom drive they
produced to use the technology. I've yet to see Lite-On produce even one
of these drives.

Whatever the reasons, it amounts to a crying shame.
I like the idea of the technology being introduced into hard drive
platters. At a starting point of 100GB per disc (50GB per side), & an
average of 8 platters in most HD's these days, that would be a fantastic
800GB hard drive.

I live in hope that some day one company will pick up on this.
