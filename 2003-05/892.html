Re: X video Problems

Mike,

I don't have any direct experience with the S3 you describe.  Some things you 
could try...

Examine the output from startx.  On my Debian system this goes to 
/var/log/XFree86.0.log.  If you don't find it there, just tee the output from 
startx to a file.  Somewhere in all this information, Xfree will tell you 
that it has rejected various modes and why.  

Examine the Xfree configuration file, mine is at /etc/X11/XF86Config-4.   
Find the section "Monitor" and ensure that the Horiz and Vert sync matches 
your actual monitor.  I hope that this is not telling you to suck eggs but 
you reported:
> the monitor reports Out of Range, frequency of 26Hz.
This is a clue that the entry in this section is wrong and if so, maybe the 
top end of the range has not been correctly set either and that would explain 
the inability to go higher than 640x480.

Check that the Xserver which you are using is able to access the video clock. 
Prior to version 4 of Xfree86, this was partly the responsibility of the sys 
admin, in those days, the clock generator was separate from other things and 
getting it wrong would exclude a whole bunch of video modes not unlike the 
symptoms you describe.  The older X86 server had an option to probe the 
clocks but I am unable to find it.  Perhaps someone on the list knows what 
has happened to it.

If you can load M$ Windows AND the drivers that came with the 
motherboard, examine the the video settings including the clock frequency.  
This will give you a benchmark.  Try creating a modeline in XFConfig which 
uses the same clock and resolution.  I once used this technique to 
discover that if I overclocked my monitor just a tweeny-weeny little bit, I 
could push the frame rate to 85Hz and reduce eye strain.

Good Luck,
-- 
Branko
