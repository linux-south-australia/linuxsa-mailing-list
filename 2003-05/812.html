>  cdrecord -dev=0,0,0 -v -speed=24 -data thing.iso

> This is on Debian Woody...any ideas of why this might be happening?

My 48x IDE CD-RW never goes at 48x either... I don't know why, and
both man cdrecord and /usr/share/doc/cdrecord are annoyingly silent on
the subject. I have a couple of hypotheses:

1) specmanship: it may be that at high speeds the CD drive goes into
constant-angular-velocity mode and only achieves its full speed on the
outer part of the CD. cdrecord, on the other hand, reports the writing
speed achievable on the inner part of the CD, where the recording
starts. (This is speculation; I don't know if cdrecord behaves this
way or not.)

2) speed of system: perhaps cdrecord makes some estimate of how fast
it can continuously transfer data from HD to CD-RW and adjusts the
maximum speed accordingly... though either it's being very
conservative or you have a very slow system. Hmm. You haven't got the
CD-RW and the HD on the same cable have you?

for comparison, I have: CyberDrive CW-088D 48x IDE CD-RW, SCSI HD for
which hdparm -t reports around 12.5MB/s transfer rate (which is
roughly what the data sheet says for the raw transfer speed off the
actual platter), 1.533GHz Athlon XP, 512MB 133MHz SDRAM, 50MHz PCI
bus, writing speeds max 40x.
