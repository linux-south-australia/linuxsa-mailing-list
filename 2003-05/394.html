I well recall the days of CB radio. Many of us ran hacked sets to get 
extra frequencies, hundreds of them.  It was push a toggle this way, 
turn a switch that way. Sure we took a risk with the Red Indians but 
very few got caught. I recall getting a visit from the RI (Radio 
Inspector) back in the days of working across the emergency frequency 
as part of the volunteer support folks. All friendly but the warning 
was there ;-)

But my HyGain 5 set was clean, fine tuned onto the car by a good tech 
(remember the days of Gawler CB? No out of band emissions from me. 
Legal power too. Still worked more skip than most others :-P

User modifications are one thing. Modifications properly made to stay 
in band and focussed require a high degree of technical ability, also 
requiring antennae to set up properly and cabled with care, a high wall 
if you like to use Glen's terminology.
