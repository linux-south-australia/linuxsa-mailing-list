Re: Democrats introduce legislation to support Open Source Software

On Wed, 30 Apr 2003, James Leone wrote:

> >>17A. (1) A public authority must, in making a decision about the
> >>procurement of computer software for its operations, have regard to
> >>the principle that, wherever practicable, a public authority should
> >>use open source software in preference to proprietary software.

This is, I think, poor wording. It should be about open standards and 
a couple of other things. However, Open Source Software is one way to 
achieve the goals.    

> Here is a draft of a bill that seems to have broad support in the US 
> State of Oregon:
> 
> http://www.leg.state.or.us/barnhart/home.htm
> 
> And some limited information about the fight taking place between 
> proponents:
> 
> http://portland.indymedia.org/front.php3?article_id=61531&group=webcast
> 
> The key point to the bill is not a mandate of open source use, but a 
> mandate that it be considered for use.
> 
> Not excluding proprietary solutions is a  level of fairness that seems 
> to have pushed the bill forward despite heavy lobbying by the opposition.

This is a very important provision, since there will not always be Open 
Source Software for every thing that government needs to do.

However, there should always be a requirement that software that is 
purchased by government departments on our behalf, should, without 
exception, conform to open standards!
