Yes (but) if the other mailserver is down, it doesn't matter if the mail
sits in your queue or the ISP's queue. It will either eventually get
through, or bounce.
'Braindead' spamblocking is all we really have, because (big picture)
there is no end-to-end authentication between all the parties involved in
mail transport. Welcome to the Matri^H^H^H^H^HInternet... :)
If we knew for sure that the sender of the email was authenticated and
traceable, spam control would be a simpler case of verification of
credentials, and the problem would not be the issue it is today.
Anyway, I understand your reasons, I'll stop telling you to smarthost now,
except to say that if you would like a simpler life, there are time and
