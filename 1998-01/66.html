Well surprisingly rpm (or dpkg) or whatever packaging program you use
tell you a lot about what is needed to get a program going.  If you 
use Red Hat you can find out a lot about a program by doing the following.

Say for example you are wondering what sendmail is... 

To find out what package it is from

	> rpm -qf /usr/lib/sendmail
	sendmail-8.8.7-12

To find out what is in the package

	> rpm -ql sendmail
	/etc/aliases
	/etc/aliases.db
	/etc/mail
	/etc/mail/deny
	...

To find out related packages

	> rpm -qa | grep sendmail
	sendmail-doc-8.8.4-3
	sendmail-8.8.7-12
	sendmail-cf-8.8.4-3

Also have a look around /etc.  And don't forget man and man -k.
Do a man man if you don't understand what man -k is.

These are very hands on ways of learning, but it how I learnt most
of what I know.

There are also Linux courses that crop up from time to time on the 
Internet.

Otherwise get a few books, I don't have any so I can't recommend any.
What sort of level do you want to go in at? Dummy level or reference 
level.

Have fun, and hope the group really helps you.
