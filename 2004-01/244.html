Borland has a Delphi for Linux, called Kylix 2.

It is sourcecode compatible with Delphi for Windows (above v6) to a 
degree that leaves me wondering why it isn't more widespread than it is.

As of a few months ago, a free version was available from Club Borland 
http://community.borland.com/ (you have to sign up as a member, after 
which they spam you just a little), with a few components in the library 
and a splash on all executables letting the endusers know that the 
program they're trying to execute is made using free software.

There are no database components packaged with Kylix2 Free Edition and, 
as far as I know, no free ones available from third parties either.

Other than that, great package, especially for hardened Windows crims 
like meself, who often have a hard time regressing to ....   eh, getting 
used to the many fine features of the commandline compilers in Linux.
