If you like, I can get you a small hard disk (like a 1.2GB or something)
with Linux pre-installed. Boot off that, and that circumvents needing to
boot the CD.  I could also organise another hard disk (500MB or
something) to use just for swap space, and I reckon you will be in
business.

For you choice of distro, depends on what you are hoping to run. Dont
expect to be able to install a full RedHat with Gnome/KDE & OpenOffice
on that system (not enough room or grunt), and bootable live-CD's seems
to be out of the question for you as well.

If you just want to setup a text based web/file server, then SoL is a
nice lightweight distro, very slick and optimised for your hardware. It
should also just fit. If not, you can easily make your own ISO based on
SoL by creating a new tarball without the bits you dont need. I really
like SoL :) and recommend it.  Otherwise, have a look at some of the
tiny distros like LRP, and DamnSmallLinux.

For a desktop distro, Lorma Linux is looking pretty good (Fedora based,
chopped down to 1CD, i686 optimized, includes multimedia apps like
MPlayer and MP3 support, and no server apps. But I dont think your
hardware would be able to fit even this on it.

If you are up for a real challenge, gentoo would fit on your hardware,
but you would have to go for a stage-1 install (again - no room for
stage 3), and compile everthing, which should take a while on that
little box.
