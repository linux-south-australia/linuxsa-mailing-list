On Mon, 2004-01-05 at 12:08, Peter Ballard wrote:
> Thanks to all who have replied. Here are a few things I have learned:
> 
> 1. There seem to be 3 types of modems: hard (real) modems,
> controllerless modems, and soft modems. Controllerless modems have
> more hardware than soft modems but are still in reality soft modems
> because they need special software to run them. You need to be careful
> what you ask for because to some suppliers seem to think a
> controllerless modem is a real modem.
> 
> 2. Anything with a Rockwell or Conexant chipset is probably (perhaps
> "certainly") a controllerless or soft modem. 
> 
> 3. Several people mentioned free drivers from linuxant.com. They might
> have been free once but not anymore. (They do supply a free version,
> but it is limited to 14k bps). 
> 
> David Fitch mentioned 3 cheap, apparently hardware, modems he found:
> 
> >"Discovery 56K Hardware Int PCI  Rockwell chipset" RRP $35
> >"DLink DFM-560I 56K PCI Hardware Modem" RRP approx $50
> >"Dynalink PCI 56K F/D Hardware Modem" RRP approx $40
> 
> This URL for the DLink modem says it uses the Rockwell chipset and
> requires Windows:
> 
> http://www.dlink.com.au/products/modems/dfm560i/
> 
> Based on that, I suspect all 3 modems mentioned above are
> controllerless, not hard, modems.
> 
> It seems it is hard to get a hard modem for under about $150. 
> 
> In case anyone else has to go through the same procedure, here is what
> I did:
> 
> Rather than fork out for an expensive modem (since it'll only be for
> work once I get ADSL going), I downloaded the 14k-limited driver
> from Linuxant. It worked! And while it did indeed seem to enforce an
> *average* limit of 14k bps (I did a whole pile of downloads and
> averaged 1500 bytes/s), this was in fact faster than the same set of
> downloads in Windows on my supposedly 45k bps line. So I suspect I
> have a crummy throughput with my ISP (well it is only $1/hour with Big
> Button), so I might as well stick with the (free) 14k bps limited
> version. 
> 
> I had to do one thing to get it going: after dialing, the modem must
> have had trouble with the dial tones because it kept returning the
> "BUSY" signal after dialing. I fixed this by changing the dialing
> string from "ATDT" to "ATX1DT". This causes the modem to not try to
> determine whether the phone at the other end is busy.
> 
> (This was with a controllerless modem. Linuxant have drivers for soft
> modems also).
> 
> 
> I must say this whole process has made installing Linux a pain and
> would dishearten many novices. Since the linuxant 14k versions are
> free, perhaps they should be included in the standard Linux
> distributions (perhaps they are).
> 
> Thanks again for everyone's help.
