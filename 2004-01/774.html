I am not sure what category of belief I fall into but I do believe in "buyer
beware".  In the sense of if I buy something I take it upon myself to be
honest/responsible as to why I purchased it.  If I purchased it knowing full
well that I am going to use it in a way not intended (which includes quite a
number of possibilities) then being honest with myself I would know that I
could incur some sort of consequance (regardless of whether it is good or
bad) for it and therefore take that responsiblity.  I am not at all saying
that the penalties that currently exist for this type of scenario are the
correct penalties, but I think getting penalized for doing something that
you know isn't right is only fair.  Do I wish for a penalty everytime I
break the speed limit?  No I don't.  I just think that knowning the penalty
allows me to make a decision to either follow society (government) or not.
Also I don't think that most manufacturers (does a package of meat state
what it is for?) state on their product what the intended use is for, and
because of that then there should be no penalties.  I don't believe in the
statement of 'ignorance is no excuse'.
