Re: OT: Dead Pixels on LCD

From: "David Fitch" <davidf@parachilna.com>
> yes it's interesting the way people just accept the
> manufacturers line about X number dead is ok, more than
> that and they will fix/replace it.  So X-1 dead is somehow
> not covered by warranty?  Who gives the manufacturer the
> right to decide how many dead is and is not acceptable?
> If it happens to my laptop (within warranty) no doubt
> I will find the answers to these questions.

The manufacturer has a right to decide the manufacturing tolerances.  Dead
pixels and subpixels are only created at manufacture time.  If I declare
that my car is meant to be built to 0.01mm tolerances, while the
manufacturer guarantees 0.1mm, I have no right to demand the item to be
fixed.  The customer can shop around for the LCD warranty policy that suits
their needs.  It only gets sticky when unsuspecting customers who fork out
the extra dollars are suprised by dead pixels (I doubt anyone savvy enough
to be on LinuxSA is unaware of LCD technology limitations).

I have avoided dead pixels/subpixels by viewing my screens at the store
before purchasing.  I wouldn't buy a screen through mail order without a
guarantee as provided by Acer or Philips.
