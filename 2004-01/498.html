Bored hacker cracked state's network secrets
By Court Reporter MICHAEL OWEN-BROWN
14jan04 
OUT of boredom and curiosity, Darren Paul Kruse repeatedly exposed
significant security flaws in the State Government's main computer network.

The Adelaide Magistrates Court heard that the computer security specialist
could have caused havoc in various government departments and agencies, but
had not been motivated by malice. 
The 35-year-old did not use his hacking skills to gain access to restricted
data. 
While contracted to work at the Department for Environment and Heritage in
2002, Kruse was able to use a program from the website News For Nerds to
exploit flaws in the StateNet computer network. He was able to crack poorly
chosen passwords and gain access to accounts in the Office of Consumer and
Business Affairs, Department of Human Services, Department for
Administrative and Information Services, SA Water and ETSA Utilities.
