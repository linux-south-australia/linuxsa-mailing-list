> Nothing about crypto prevents it from being a broadcast medium.

True but I wasn't referring to cryto here. I was referring to how limiting
public networks limits the speed at which information can be exchanged. For
example, in a recent operation, a website updatable by any of the coppers on
duty was set-up so they could update details changing constantly whilst the
operation was in effect. It was viewable by all involved at once so that if
you needed an update on the situation as a whole (spanning three states at
any one time), you coudl get it without going through comms to find out the
latest info told by someone who might not have the whole picture having gone
through his s/sergeant and sergeant to do so. 

Sure it wasn't terribly secure but it was quick. I don't deny that it's
insecure by nature but several incidents which would have blown up in the
past weren't allowed to do so because people were updated with all the
latest info as quickly as possible and decisions could be made must faster.
