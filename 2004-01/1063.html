RE: continued exploration of small livecds

Actually im in a dilemma concerning this - I have a bunch of old
PII/PIII class machines that are being converted to a dedicated Point of
Sale terminals.

Now, I tried loading the latest Fedora on one of these, and its just too
big, and runs like a snail in mollasses.

So, I have done the right thing and started rolling my own distro. Its
rally easy once you start -  All you need to do it write a monster bash
script for the installer, and then write another bash script to replace
the antiquated SysV init process.

Im starting with VL 4.0, knocking stuff out, adding other stuff in, and
streamlining the boot process.

Now, the old machines boot straight to the GUI app, running full screen
with no window decorations or window manager. On a P200 with 64MB of
RAM, its way fast and responsive - MORE responsive than running the same
app on my development workstation (which is an Athlon XP2100+, 1GB RAM,
but with a bucketload of redhatware running in the background).

I have so far created an ISO of POSLinux  (PointOfSale or PeiceOfShite -
take your pick), but I need somewhere to mirror it.  Anyone recommend
somewhere where I can host 250MB of ISO image, with associated upstream
bandwidth ??
