I completely disagree with this article.  As with any community, you'll find there's 
subcommunities; this is especially evident with open source.

I find there's at least two types of Linux users; the ones that actually care about making a 
difference, and are the real developers/users, and understand the concepts of what freedom 
Open Source really provides; then you get the "I must be better then you because I run Linux" 
type, who often barely can even compile an application.

The arguments presented in that article apply only to the latter, which unfortunately seem to 
domineer.  There's subcommunites out there that actually do give a shit; this mailing list is 
definitely one.  The guy who wrote the article needs to look a bit harder.
