RE: Interesting concept for govt oss development

Freeman, Peter (ERHS) wrote:
>Good point, but for politicians in particular, they're always going to be
>more interested in the political/economic/social benefits of OSS, rather 
>than the (obvious to us) technical benefits.  The fact that Samba kicks 
>butt over NT/W2k as a file & print server doesn't really rate on the agenda
>of any bureaucrat whom I've met...

I was talking with a MLC about Ian's OSS bill two days before the vote.
He had NO technical understanding whatever.

So put the benefits in terms of:
. Lower cost of software to Government
. Better for the community because of easier, cheaper access to technology
. Better control of internet nasties (Spam/Porn/Viruses)
. Better security
. Better local (SA) employment opportunities
. Profits from IT more likely to be kept locally
etc.

These things made sense and he therefore voted for the bill.

The fact that, say, SAMBA outperforms Win 2003 is irrelevant.
