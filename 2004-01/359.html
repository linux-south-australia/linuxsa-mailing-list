Re: smbmount of a subdirectory of shared directory

Bill,

Nope.  I had the same dilemma not long ago.

The way I got around it was to (your share details substituted) smbmount
//winfileserver/b as /mnt/.b and then symlink /mnt/mydir ->
/mnt/.b/mydir.  By mounting as '.b', the directory is hidden from
unadorned ls, hence directory listings look a little cleaner and all you
see is 'mydir'.

- Brad.
