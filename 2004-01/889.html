List,
Im going back a few years 20 or so in one of my jobs I was asked to modify
versions of customer OSs to remove the Phrase Logon Please to "Logon" with
words to the affect before that This system is for authorised users only
blah blah. The security wasn't beefed up just the notice was deemed adequate
for ensuring no one had a claim to an invitation.

This came about from the fact a hacker got let off as 'he interpreted the
message he found on some mainframe as an invitation' the court ruled in his
favour so we had to put these warnings up not to make it more secure but to
reinforce the fact that unless you were authorised you were not allowed to
enter, it's the same as someone coming to your front door if you refuse them
entry with a sign that's as far as they are allowed to go any effort to
enter the house/system is then an illegal act.

This was explained to us by our company lawyer some years ago hence the
instructions.
