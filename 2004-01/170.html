Re: Modems and 'Soft' Modems under Linux

> you can still get cheap external serial modems though,
> which is easier all round (IMO).

Yes and no.  Although I'm not keen on internal modems, I've found over the 
years that they have one advantage and that is modems require a good quality 
powersupply that the plug packs just don't supply.

I've used a Rockwell Chipset ISA card (56kbps) for years (now a friend of 
mine uses it) which works a treat and never drops and never locks up.  
Whereas external modems all seem to drop/lockup anytime there is a slight 
power fluctuation.  UPSes (except for really good, online ones) don't help 
because we're often talking only slight variations.
