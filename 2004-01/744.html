> I'm advocating that much of the information that may be kept on the

Well considering the speed at which criminal networks function these days,
advocating something like that lessens the speed of communication between
and within agencies which lessens the effectiveness of Intelligence-led
Policing. In effect, you put your own needs to get to publically access
networks above catching smarter than usual crims for example. That means
your view is unrealistic and compromises have to be (and have been) made.

And anyway, since you want to push the 'reality' angle, here you go; banks
DO put personal details on networks and SAPol DOES put sensitive files on
networks. That ain't about to change any time soon so they have a
responsibility to protect stakeholders in the information and have been
given the mandate to punish those who try to breach any security measures
put in place, benevelent or not. Arguing that Banks and other places
shouldn't put info on networks is about as effective as arguing that
crackers just shouldn't try to breach security in the first place.
