There's no doubt that you'll learn more about Linux in 1 hour with 
Gentoo than you can with 1 year of Fedora. Gentoo has no guff in the 
way, so you use the real commands to administer your systems instead of 
some bodged up "administration tool".

This isn't unique to Gentoo... LFS and Slackware and a few others also 
share this property, but Gentoo does have the advantage of easy to read 
ebuilds so you can see how to compile and install various packages. Do 
the same thing on your LFS systems, and you're set.
