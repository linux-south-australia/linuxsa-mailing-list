> On the other hand, if passwords fly by in the clear on a government
> network, and I find that out just by looking at the wire, I don't think
> I should be severely punished. 

But do you have the right to look at the wire?  Do you have permission?  
What is your intent?  If you connect to the network with or without
permission and copy out and then misuse information you see why shouldn't
you be punished?

As Mark keeps saying, it's about how you gain the information and how you 
use it or intend to use it.   If you accidentally find passwords then you 
report that, you don't use them to break into machines you have no right to 
use.

It's about intent.  Lots of people here have privleged access to systems 
because of their jobs.  But MOST of us, I'd say, don't misuse the 
information we have access to.  Otherwise, we'd lose our jobs and/or be 
prosecuted (criminal or civil) [see: Darren Kruse]
