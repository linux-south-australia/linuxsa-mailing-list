Yep - Online's typically HTML.  But PDF rocks for printing or transporting.  
I deal with lots of PDF documents (most vendors like Cisco that I deal with
barely print anything anymore) and so being able to have a document as a
single unit that prints the same is great. I can save them as a single file
on my laptop to refer to when onsite.  I can print them, they have indexes

when not online.

PDF is that you as an author get to set how you want it to print.  It's NOT 
an editable format at all (well not much).  Usually however, I have success 
cutting and pasting - you just have to ask the PDF reader to do that.

> The fact that it is a proprietary format is the icing on the cake.

Well, as I said above, propose a decent format to me that does the job as

for what it's intended to do - which is as a readonly portable document
