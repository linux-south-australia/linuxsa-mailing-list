> >A friend of mine is installing SUSE 9.0 and wants a FAT32 partition to
> >share documents between Windoze and SUSE. Simple, right? Well he goes
> >and mounts the FAT32 partition as /home and has had no end of bother. I

Ugh, indeed.  Permissions are going to go right out the window, which is what /home is all 
about.  This is not a solution at all.

If he wants to share documents, just mount the windows partition under /mnt/c or something, 
and symlink ~/Documents to /mnt/c/Documents\ And\ Settings/Username/My\ Documents.  
Then just tell him when he wants to use his documents, go to home, go to Documents, and 
wha'la.

If he's one of these users that's going to whine that it isn't exactly like Windows, tell him that it's 
a good thing :-)  What baffles me is people, when wanting to try an alternative desktop, expect 
things to happen in the exact manner that it happens under their previous desktop - you tell 
me, what's alternative about it then? :-)
