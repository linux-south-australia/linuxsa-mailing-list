Daryl Tester <Daryl.Tester@iocane.com.au> wrote:
>>> Bash from csh?

>> It borrows in other ways, too.

> This is what I'm curious about; as far as I'm aware, it doesn't
> share a lot in common with csh, syntactically or semantically
> (e.g. "setenv" is a no go, file redirection clearly follows sh
> behaviour).

The bash >& file redirection comes from csh.  Likewise job control was a
csh feature, although since ksh picked it up from csh, I think it would
be more fair to say bash picked it up from there.  All in all, I'd say
that the diagram was wrong at least to the extent that it showed bash as
influenced by sh, ksh and csh.  I'd have said that sh and csh influenced
ksh, which influenced bash.
