On Wed, 21 Jan 2004 01:16 pm, Ryan Verner wrote:
>  then you get the "I must be better then you

That is an astonishing comment.  Has it escaped your notice that with 
only one or two exceptions, Linux distros depend on installing binary 
packages and NOT compiling applications?
However, even for BSD users I would NOT say that compiling applications  
necessarily equated with sound thinking.

The comments posted by Corey Taylor seem to me to be valid and tend in 
my experience to derive from a sort of siege mentality which is common 
among minority groups.  The more mainstream Linux becomes, the less 
there will be silly "why should you" (want to do what I can't do) 
comments.

I think that the BSD community suffers less in this regard because Un*x 
is well established and well regarded, so no "apology" is needed.
