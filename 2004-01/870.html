I actually used to do this quite alot when I was building my home "lab" so
to speak and I found that the most common thing I was doing over and over
was dealing with software configuration issues among others of course.  For
instance I would move say /home to /1 (bad naming perhaps but it is my brain
:) and then when I logged in of course it was pointing to /home which well
nothing existed anymore.  So what I came up with was a very simple solution
of `ln -s /1 /home`.  I wouldn't have done this for work but I can easily
say it was acceptable to me at home.  I start getting lazy by using the bash
shell's '~' for my home directoy and forget that most apps I use don't
understand the '~' until they start complaining.
