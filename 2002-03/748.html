Hi + large removable media

Hi all
Until now I have been sitting in the background reading all your troubles,
opions and most interestingly ideas.  It is about time I made a contibution.  

I have a few ideas on removable storage for linux boxes.  I had an interest in
smartmedia adapters (for reading my digital camera files) that were linux
friendly, this led me to discover the flashpath smartmedia -> 3.5' floppy disk
adapter.  This not only allows smartmedia  to be read in, but can act as a (32,
64 and maybe upto 128MB) removable drive for your computer.  Great for legacy
laptops or those who don't have CD re-writers in the home and office.  I
believe there is no other technology (ie. zip disks or external hard drives)
for file swapping that can be so portable without having to install a non
standard drive for each computer.

Let me know if anyone has an interest in this area or has had any success with
smartmedia under Linux.

Verbatim are the distributers for Australia.
