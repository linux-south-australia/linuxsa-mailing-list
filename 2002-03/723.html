Re: [ot] amd cpu?

Hiya all,

The video that Tom did was indeed enlightening, but it seemed to of been
done for kicks rather than a controlled scientific experiment.

The material is several months old though. A few weeks after that was
released another site mirrored a video allegedly from AMD showing a
similar experiment between an Athlon XP system vs a Pentium 4 system.

Tom's video apparently was done with an Athlon motherboard that didn't
have thermal protection. AMD's video did. and it was shown to behave
similarly to the Pentium 4 system. In that video both systems shutdown
immediately upon the motherboard detecting an overheat.

My system at work now is a dual CPU Athlon MP box, and the ASUS A7M266-D
motherboard manual does stress that it has a motherboard-based thermal
protection circuit. I'd say that for many new motherboard over the past
few months, they would offer better protection to the CPU.

These 0.18u Athlon XP/MP CPUs are bloody good desk warmers when they are
running in dual CPU config. I'm hoping that for the 0.13u Athlons (due
very shortly) they will make it thermally acceptable for my use at home
where the ambient room temperature can reach 40 degrees Celcius (I need
to get an air conditioner).

Cheers,
Joseph Tan
