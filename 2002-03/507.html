Hmmm... according to my calculations...

Modeline "640x480" 55 640 664 776 856 480 503 509 1066 -hsync -vsync
- 64.3KHz horizontal, 60.3Hz vertical

Modeline "800x600" 118 800 1136 1360 1840 600 813 816 1053 -hsync -vsync
- 64.1KHz horizontal, 60.9Hz vertical

Modeline "1024x768" 118 1024 1240 1448 1840 768 894 897 1054 -hsync -vsync
- 64.1KHz horizontal, 60.8Hz vertical

Modeline "1280x1024" 108 1280 1296 1456 1672 1024 1032 1035 1076 -hsync -vsync
- 64.6KHz horizontal, 60.0Hz vertical.

Those look closeish to me (presuming you got the 60/64 around the
wrong way), but you could make 1280x1024 a bit closer by trying:

Modeline "1280x1024" 108 1280 1296 1464 1688 1024 1030 1033 1066 -hsync -vsync
- 64.0KHz horizontal, 60.0Hz vertical.

I have no idea how close "precisely" must be for your monitor (or for
any fixed-frequency monitor in fact :-).  The numbers you had before
are within 1-2%.  The new 1280x1024 numbers are within 0.1% I think.
