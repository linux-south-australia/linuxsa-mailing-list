Re: Transparent Proxying

> Having said that though, my ISP doesnt have a transperent proxy and when
> the shoes on the other foot I prefer it that way. I guess I just dont
> trust other people :)

And I think ultimately that's what we're looking at:

- ISP people look at it from a cost saving point of view (quite valid)
  and performance benefit (maybe good for modems, less so for higher speed 
  links - probably overall a benefit)
- Users of the network look at it from a "monitoring/trust" point of view 
  (also quite valid).
- Some users (eg. me) look at it as something they'd like to get around 
  occasionally to be able to diagnose problems ('cause its what I do!)
  (valid to a degree).

Who's right?  Who knows.  I personally like my TCP streams unmodified, but I 
guess when it comes down to it am willing to wear it to pay less per month.
