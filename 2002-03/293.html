Re: FW: zlib security advisory

Quoting Anthony Symons <ant@sa.pracom.com.au>:

> Seems a bit wierd, redhat being one of the first

If you read the advisory you will discover that the bug was originally detected 
by the GNOME community, and verified by a Red Hat engineer who is part of that 
community.

So it is certainly not weird, "Red Hat being one of the first", since they 
verified the bug after discovery, contacted CERT, and now all distributions are 
releasing fixes just about simultaneously.

This is exactly how most people would like Red Hat to behave.  They are pretty 
good at security - a lot of other distros don't get security fixes out very 
quickly at all.  If you don't believe me, go and look at lwn.net and see which 
distros/OSes post fixes to problems quickly.  And if you don't think your 
distro/OS doesn't have problems go and visit 
http://www.linuxsecurity.com/advisories/index.html or any other of the 
multitude of sites that look at security.
