up2date is one of the tools used for configuring your box for the Red Hat 
Network (RHN), which allows you to manage the software installed on your box, 
or if you're an admin, possibly across a large number of boxes automatically.  
This is one money making machansim that Red Hat use.

Red Carpet is part of the Ximian GNOME desktop, allowing you to do much the 
same as the RHN, but this time you give Ximian money instead.  The advantage 
that Red Carpet has is that there are a number of third party channels 
available, like Star Orrifice, Loki Games, VMWare etc which allow you to 
install on your box software that isn't part of GNOME or Linux distro, and may 
not be free software (so you need to subsequently pay for a licence key).  Red 
Carpet also works on most linux distros but not slackware (tgz too hard to 
handle dependencies, but debs and rpms no problems).

Both services have limited "free use" capability and an option to pay money to 
get access to a fatter download pipe.

I used to use Red Carpet, but no longer.  Haven't tried RHN.  I manage my boxen 
manually now.
