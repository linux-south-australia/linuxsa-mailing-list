David,

I have several AVM A1 (Fritz!) ISDN cards available, all fully supported 
under Linux. They are all ISA. $50 each if you are interested - as far as I 
know they are virtually identical to the Netjet cards.

The website uses frames, but the card info is available from 

http://www.avm.de/

I believe the cards I have are the Fritz! classic (non PNP) cards, but I do 
not think there is too much difference.

Let me know if you (or anyone else on the list) is interested. For a long 
time I used these with Linux and an Internode ISDN account - now no longer 
needed as I have ADSL. The AVM cards support Data over Voice (DOV) under 
Linux so you only need to pay for the cost of a local call for an unlimited 
64Kbps if your ISP supports ISDN over DOV.
