Re: Squid behind firewall

  > I was wondering what port on the firewall I need to open to let squid
access the outside world ?
Squid acts like any other client on your network, none of it's server ports
need to be accessed by the outside world for it to function, unless you're
using it for http acceleration or some kind of reverse proxying.

If you're doing transparent proxying, all you will probably need to open is
port 80 outbound access. If it's being configured in the end-user web
browser, you may also need to allow it access to 443 for SSL requests, and
then any arbitrary port at your discretion for URL's like
http://www.something.com:8000/

For maximum flexibility, you might just want to keep a list of tcp ports you
don't want it to connect to, like 22, 23, 25 or 110.
