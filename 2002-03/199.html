[Article] Cox: Cheaper chips give new life to Linux

Find below an excerpt and link to a recent on-line article...


"
Cox: Cheaper chips give new life to Linux
By Matthew Broersma
ZDNet (UK)
March 6, 2002, 6:05 AM PT

The advent of affordable 64-bit computing could be the best thing to happen 
to Linux in a long time, opening up a new market potentially as important 
as the original PC market, according to Linux "kernel hacker" Alan Cox.

Cox, an employee of Linux vendor Red Hat, also criticized new guidelines 
suggested by the Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF) covering the 
reporting of software security holes.

New processors emerging from AMD and Intel--whose main focus has until now 
been desktop chips--will allow many companies currently locked into 
expensive computer systems to switch to mainstream chips and open software 
like Linux, Cox said. The new Itanium line from Intel and the upcoming 
Hammer range from AMD offer similar performance to the RISC processors made 
by the likes of IBM and Sun Microsystems, but aim to achieve desktop-level 
prices.
