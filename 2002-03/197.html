Re: 'find' help

Just for your understanding of the reason why we have a \ and -exec and ; in the find command.

The -exec operator allows you to execute any command even another find (I'm sure u realise this).
If you think for a moment, you may come to realise that find needs to distinguish the
command _its_ executing from _its_ own arguments. The obvious solution would be to use
the same end of command character as the shell ( ie semicolon). Since the shell uses the
semi colon itself it is necessary to escape it with a back slash OR even quotes.
Therefore every -exec operator ends with \; or ';'.

Just in case you were curious Ben ;->
