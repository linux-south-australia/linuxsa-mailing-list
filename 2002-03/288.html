I'll wait for an official response from Internode before making jumping
to any conclusions. However, I will say this:

If you've got your DNS managed by Internode, they won't change DNS
records for you over the phone. They'll ask for a written request in the
form of something such as an e-mail, fax or whatever.

If that *has* happened (it may not have in this case, but I don't know
that), and the e-mail or fax was not noticeably fraudulent, I wonder
what else Internode could have done. It's unfair to blame them entirely
for this, and to call it a "security breach" is more than a bit
misleading.
