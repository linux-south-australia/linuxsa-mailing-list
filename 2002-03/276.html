Bash Programmable Completion

I came across this web page recently (via Freshmeat), which gives some
general tips and tricks regarding bash:

http://www.caliban.org/bash/index.shtml

However the standout feature is programmable completion. It extends bash to
do "contextual" completion. If I type "ssh *tab* *tab*", it will give me a
list of hosts I have recently ssh'd to as possible completion matches.

It is good in a "this is smart thinking" way. Type "rpm -q" and a list of
packages that can be queried are given. And many more, including "chown"
(users/groups completion), "kill/killall" (pid/process), "man", "ipconfig"
(interfaces), "make" (valid make targets in Makefile) - the list just goes
on. It seems (to me anyway) to be quite effective.

I know others probably read Freshmeat, but just thought this was worth
mentioning as it really seems to be worth a look.
