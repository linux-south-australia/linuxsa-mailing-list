It's also worthy to note that many motherboards ( well mine at least ABIT
KT7 ) has inbuilt protection that will not allow Power to Motherboards if it
detects no fan rotation. This doesn't help however if the Fan "falls off",
but given how hard is is to get the clips on you'd have to be doing some
serious knocking around to dislodge a fan.

> On Sun, 24 Mar 2002, Andrew Pullin wrote:
>
> > Hi All,
> >     I hate to say this, but you probably have fried your
> > Processor. A few weeks ago, slashdot had a link to a
> > hardware test on processors (I cannot find the actual link
> > after a brief search, but it could have been Tom's Hardware
> > page or some such) done by an independent tester. The tests
> > were up against a PIII, a P4 and 2 AMD Processors, all were
> > high end, ie + 1Ghz clock speeds, and basically were to run
> > some software that gave the processor a real workout (like
> > quake etc.), and then remove the processor fan and see what
> > happens. The results were a bit sobering.
> >
> >     Now I don't want to start a Pentium vs. AMD war, but it
> > seems that AMD has very little thermal overload protection.
> > The graphics and streaming video were pretty conclusive; all
> > processors shut down within a few seconds, but the AMD
> > processors died spectacularly, one even melted through the
> > motherboard. Both these processors were definitely DEAD. The
> > Pentium processors however, when refanned and rebooted came
> > up as if nothing happened. The final conclusion of the
> > tester was that AMD might perform better, but Pentiums were
> > more reliable with cooling accidents.
> >
> >     I wish I could remember the link as the site was
> > definitely worth a visit. I know that this message hasn't
> > helped your problem, but it has hopefully made you aware of
> > how fragile AMD processors are.
> >     Cheers!
> >         Andrew.
