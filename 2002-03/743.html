Re: Transparent Proxying

Maybe on this topic then, ISP's that use transparent proxying such as
Internode could put a setting or something into their customer interfaces
(in an advanced settings area) with a checkbox which turns on and off
transparent proxying with a disclaimer, or warning about slow content, more
megs (overheads), etc. that way, for the normal average users it still acts
as a catch all (dont even know what proxy means kinda people) whereas for
the gurus and grizzle gutzes they know what its for, and can disable it if
they wish.
