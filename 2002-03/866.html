The point is Matt that the exchange is Telstra equipment not Primus. I was 
advising Telstra of a fault with their equipment. The fact that I am a 
Primus customer does not remove the fault from the Telstra exchange. 
Telstra needed to fix the fault not just for my benefit but also for their 
own customers. Primus could not fix the fault. So my reporting it to Primus 
only delayed Telstra being notified and delayed the correction of the fault.

If we extend this to its logical conclusion then if we have a black-out I 
should ring AGL because they sell us the electricity rather than ring ETSA 
utilities who manage the distribution network,.
