RE: flex atx motherboards etc

> Has anyone had anything to do with the very tiny motherboard 
> formats such as flex or micro atx? ie linux compatibility, 
> models available in country, cases etc I want to build a very 
> portable divx, mp3 type box with bits available from my junk 
> box. And yes I know I could use my laptop but it's too 
> expensive to break.

Yep -- I have a micro ATX one here that has successfully run both
FreeBSD and Sorcerer Linux... I don't think there is anything special in
regards to compatibility about the Micro/Flex ATX format -- they are
pretty standard, just a little more compact (and tend to have more
integrated stuff, and less expansion capacity)... the main thing to look
out for is physical compatibility with "stuff"... your case needs to fit
your mobo, your psu needs to have enough capacity and cables to run all
your devices etc -- I used a "mini" case (AOpen H300), and the psu only
had 2 HDD/CD power cables, and I wanted 1 cd and 2hdds... PITA. You may
also find that if you use a slightly unconventional case, your IDE
cables etc will be too short... there's a whole lot of stupid things
that can go wrong here, and there's no sensible way to avoid them,
really...

Anyway, good luck :)
