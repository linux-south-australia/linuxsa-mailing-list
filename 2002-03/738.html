Re: Transparent Proxying

I dont have a problem with them. Here I run a squid proxy, that is
transperently proxying any port 80 traffic on our gateway. I have never
had any problems with it, and the gains are significant. We once had a
machine with a buggy netscape 6.2 that grabbed 3gb of data out the cache
reloading an image over and over. Without the proxy that would have cost
us fortune! Also, when the nimda worm came out I was able to use a regex
to stop infected pages getting through and protect all the windoze
users. Plus you can graph your traffic usage stats from the logs. I say
no problems so long as it works. Having said that though, my ISP doesnt
have a transperent proxy and when the shoes on the other foot I prefer
it that way. I guess I just dont trust other people :)

Ant
