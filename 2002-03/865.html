RE: Carrier changes

Bill, I'm not really sure where you're going with this.

In your instance, Telstra's customer is Primus, not you, they have a
responsibility to respond to Primus in regard to issues with the
provision of network services.

When Primus sign an agreement with Telstra for the wholesale provision
of services they agree to provide service to manage their customer base,
i.e billing, customer enquiries and fault reporting. 

If Primus are your provider then you need to report it to Primus who
then follow it up with their provider (Telstra) Just as you wouldn't
ring Telstra about a billing issue with your provider Primus.

If you are having difficulties with your provider (Primus) and their
inability to manage fault escalations then perhaps you should choose a
full service provider.

Unfortunately it appears we expect customer service from business's even
when we aren't their customers.

Matt
