Your english is fine!

Assuming your tape drive is scsi, you need a scsi host card, a kernel
that can talk to that card, and you should be going. You need to take
care of scsi id's for the drive, and termination of the scsi bus.

Watch the kernel boot messages for something like:

Nov 28 16:07:07 sandwich kernel: Detected scsi tape st0 at scsi0, channel 0, id 
2, lun 0
Nov 28 16:07:07 sandwich kernel: scsi0: Target 1: Queue Depth 3, Synchronous at 
10.0 MB/sec, offset 15
Nov 28 16:07:07 sandwich kernel: scsi0: Target 2: Queue Depth 3, Asynchronous
Nov 28 16:07:07 sandwich kernel: scsi : detected 1 SCSI tape 1 SCSI cdrom total.
N

Once booted, I can address my scsi tape via the mt command and use tar
and dump to copy files. Official Redhat also comes with BRU which is a
good backup program.

The devices on the system for addressing the tape (in my case) are:
  /dev/st0    (rewinding)
  /dev/nst0   (this one is non-rewinding - the tape stops at the end)
