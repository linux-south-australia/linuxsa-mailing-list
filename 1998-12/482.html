One man's treasure

G'day,

On the subject of computers and Linux use, I just wouldn't be myself if I
didn't mention that cheap Commodore C64's and C128's go well with Linux (and
other *nix flavours).  

Pine, Elm, Pico, Tin, Lynx, Emacs, Vi, telnet, ftp etc are all utilities
can be used by a C64 or C128 connected to the system, and for some people, this
does the job for them when it comes to Internet access. 

I have a mail list with 140 subscribers for TIFCU (The Internet for Commodore
Users) and my book (same title) has sold enough copies for us 1) to afford our
Internet connection, and 2) <grumble> to pay taxes on the income earned. 

I know for many who want graphical web browsers and such it wouldn't be the
right system for them, but I also know (from a few hundred customers) that it's
the perfect match for other people.   

We're hosting another mail list for C= users who are using postscript printers
and we've been investigating ways to use shell accounts to enhance the types of
items that can be printed. Such as using djpeg and gif2ps to convert JPEG
images to a postscript file.  

Anyway, if you're interested in low-tech (and inexpensive) Internet options,
this is another option.   


Cheers,
Gaelyne
