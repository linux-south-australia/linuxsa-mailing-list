I should clarify (to hopefully avoid starting a religous war :-)

GNU software has certainly contributed to the success of Linux.  But GNU
did not birth Linux, in fact it outright resented it at first (as a
competitor to Hurd).  Linus made Linux a success by adopting GNU tools
and building Linux to use those tools, but these days you could quite
easily have a Linux box without anything from the GNU project on it.
(It is especially worth noting that putting your project under the
GPL does not make it part of the GNU project.)  Should BeOS be called
'GNU/BeOS' because it uses GCC?  Should your product be called 'GNU/Frod'
because you built it on Linux with some GNU tools?

This is, of course, my view and other people's 2c is worth just as much
as mine (with rounding :-)
