This could go on forwver (or until early 2000 anyway). Ever designed control
circuits? Most of them have only a time of day clock or a primitive calendar.
And anyway if my toaster stops I'll eat raw(?) bread.

I am concerned about the the GPS issue. I thought this was a triangulation deal.
What has the time of day got to do with it? Ah... or is the real problem in the
satellites, but the planes will have to compensate?

And if I remember the Airbus airshow crash, I think the deal is that the pilot
these days can only operate manually to the extent that the computer will let
him. A bit like having the teacher looking over your shoulder all the time.

I agree with those that think this is an overrated subject. I also believe that
there are thousands of professional and competent software and electrical
engineers who are applying the same care to this problem as they are to all the
other problems that could cause disasters.

I had to make my software 2000 proof 5 years ago, I have already sorted out the
GST bit (thanks to NZ experience), and I'm back at the real stuff in
applications.

At the end of the day my conclusion was that it isn't hard, rather boring.
Requiring painstaking, meticulous review of firstly data structures, then
calculations using the date components of the data structures, and finally the
collection and display of that data. - my I hope helpful contribution to this
debate. If you've got a better plan perhaps we could talk about that instead of
speculating on planes?
