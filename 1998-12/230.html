Re: IP Masquerading questions

>The conclusion of all of this is that if your program works by listening
>at an address (I suspect ICQ does this) so that other hosts on the internet
>can connect to you, that program will be of no use to you if your
connection
>is through a masquerading gateway.
>

Both ICQ and mIRC have the ability for the user to specify a range of ports
to listen on. When you have chosen these ports it is just a simple matter of
forwarding them from the ipmasq box with ipautofw or similar program.

I have been doing this for a few months now and ICQ and DCC
incoming/outgoing works fine.

In ICQ:
Preferences -> Connection
X I'm using a permanent internet connection (LAN)
X I am behind a firewall or proxy.

Firewall Settings:
I don't use a SOCKS Proxy server....
Use the following TCP listen ports for incoming event xxxx  - xxxx
