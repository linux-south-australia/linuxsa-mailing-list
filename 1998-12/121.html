There is something even worse - called a smurf attack, where the "hacker"
sends ICMP echo packets to a broadcast address on some router on some
large network (where the network operators are clueless) and fakes the
source address as from being on of your machines.

Then all the computers on that broadcast network reply :-)
There is a Cisco IOS command that prevent you being used in this way .

That is also interesting when talking about the BTR charge from Telstra -
all of those outbound packets !
