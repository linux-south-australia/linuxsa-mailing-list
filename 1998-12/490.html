Re: 3COM Network Cards.

Well, easiest method is to make sure your kernel has the appropriate drivers
compiled in, or available as modules.
then load the relevant modules, and see if you get an appropriate response.
if so, and you get a 'real-looking' hw address, and you can at least ping
yourself or something via it, then that's the best sign you can get that it
is working, short of getting two cards and actually talking over a network.

Some cards come with (usually dos-only) software that runs self-tests in the
card, but i haven't seen any generic ones for linux.

Toby
