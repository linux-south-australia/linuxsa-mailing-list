> I guess I just got a little hurt when software developed by volunteers is
> referred to as "Stallman on world domination". And as before my whole

> go without credit. I'm not really a GPL evangelist, more of a GNU

I to am regularly offended by the many anti-FSF/GNU sentiments made by
various (often anonymous) linux enthusists.  There are few (if any)  
people who can match Richard Stallmans contribution to free software.  And
no organisation has done as much as the FSF to promote and maintain free
software (although there are organisations who have contributed more
source code).

However Linux isn't The Free Software and visa versa, and while the FSF
have made a signifigant contribution to linux, by no means are they the
only ones.  The FSF go to great lengths to explain that not everything
under the GPL is GNU, I consider it rather predatory and oppitunistic for
them to make an exception to this rule for linux.

> I understand what you're saying with the embedded systems stuff, and yes,

> with Stallman/Co, not with the FSF's license. I know it didn't sound that
> way, but that's the main reason for my vehemence.

My prefered license is the LGPL.  I don't like the infection clause of the
GPL, however I don't like the fragmentation of the various BSD licensed
source-trees.
