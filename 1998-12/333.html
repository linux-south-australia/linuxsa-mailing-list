Re: Header in Mailing List

Andrae Muys wrote:

>>> Newall's law of computing: If there are two, or more, ways to solve a
>>> problem, pick one; don't pick all of them.

> And when two solutions present themselves, the more general of the two is
> typically the better.  In this case the prefix is more general as it
> dosn't prevent the use of procmail, while providing support to those who
> (for whatever reason) don't have access to filtering.

But you are fixing the wrong problem.  The "more general" solution
that you are advocating is to replicate information that is already
available within the mail header to make up for the deficiency in
your MUA.  I'm subscribed to another mailing list that supports this
practice, and it's visually distracting to say the least (to the
point that I now filter the subject lines through a sed script to
remove the list tag).  The subject line should be reserved for the
subject only, otherwise I can envision the day where you have the
author and date sent in there as well.  :-/
