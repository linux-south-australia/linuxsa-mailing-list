In the US, the answer to the "Sell RH5.1" challenge is:

  Mark it down by maybe 50% (to or even *below* cost)
  to keep your Cash Flow up

  Result:  - People KNOW they're getting an old version -but-
           - People who might (otherwise) NOT try it... DO
           - More LINUX Users

By contrast, locally, if not all over Australia:

  Shops seem to be quite happy to use the Customers' ignorance
  against them... HOLDING prices at the same level as when the
  product was current...

  Result:  - People aren't quite as likely to try LINUX
           - Those who DO try it and find only OLD versions in shops
             may well feel disappointed when they realise the facts
             (contrast this with the competition's same world-wide
             release dates policy...)
             ...and, with the inherent strangeness & difficulty in
             finding LINUX software on the same shelf as they found
             the op sys, no great lust in generated for LINUX...
           - Less return-business for the shop(s) that act so...
           - Less LINUX Users
