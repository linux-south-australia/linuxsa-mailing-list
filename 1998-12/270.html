Hiding Source... Like Copy Protection of Days Gone By?

 Who remembers way-back-when Lotus 123 was copy protected?

 (And a head-crash could mean not being able to re-install
 it after the faulty drive hard drive was replaced...)

 All the copy protection mechanisms of that not-so-distant
 past time lead to the rise of tools, capable of defeating
 such protective mechanisms.

 When people realised that the effect of overt copy protec-
 tion schemes was the reward of those who could build toys
 that defeated them from the sale of their backup software
 copy protection went away...

 These days, source protection is *still* more-or-less the
 in vogue... giving rise to disassemblers like IDA Pro and
 Sourcer (both commercial products from what I can gather)
 and rewards for those who help those who reverse engineer
 non-open-source software.

 Well maybe it's about time for the wheel to turn again...
