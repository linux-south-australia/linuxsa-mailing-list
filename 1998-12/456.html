Re: netcfg

>2)      Authentication is not allow allowed.
>        a) Use host based authentication and open things up doing
>                'xhost +'

I strongly recommend you _never_ get into the habit of using this
command without any extra parameters.  Whilst it is safe to use on your
box whilst it is not connected to a network, once you /are/ connected,
your box is more open than a system with blank passwords in /etc/passwd

If you _must_ use xhost, then use it like this:
	xhost +localhost
	xhost +machineIallow

But in general, ssh (which does X forwarding automatically) is the best
way to go.  It has the advantage of encrypting your session.  xauth (as
detailed by Matt) is a bit more cumbersome, but is also a lot better
than xhost.

Sorry if I sound a bit strong, but xhost exploits was the sole source
of bemusement for half of computer science during my time of studies :-)
