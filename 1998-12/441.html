Re: SCSI: Same Disk, Different Controllers ==> Need Low-Level Format?

For what it's worth, I work with many servers in commercial environments,
and there have been many times that I have swapped harddrives between
different types of SCSI controllers. 90% of this was done with NT4 machines,
and I never once had a problem that required a lowlevel format! I would not
expect to ever need to low-level-format a harddrive, under any circumstances
anyway.
Plus, I just wouldn't trust anything Microsoft say, just on principle.
I know you said you didn't want 'comments from just ordinary people who
don't have anything at risk', BUT I do commercially maintain servers,
working in a company, and so I and my company are at risk in not heeding
this MS Warning (tm). However, I don't know of anyone in the field who DOES
actually lowlevel format their harddrives on swapping host adaptors.
(Note: If you are dealing with RAID arrays of harddrives, running off a
proprietry controller (as opposed to a software implementation, like NT
striping or Linux raid filesystems), then I certainly would consider
low-level formatting the drives after switching to a different type of host
controller. HOwever, I would plug all the drives in and see if they do still
work first, and if so, run some good tests too.)

Toby
