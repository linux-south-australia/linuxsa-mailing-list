Heyas Again,

Also, I am running NT4 and RH5.2 on my machine. I *was* running NT on an NTFS
partition, and when I installed RH5.2 LILO stuffed up NTs boot info. If I remember
correctly, it recognised the NTPS as an OS/2 HPFS. Hence that was probably why it
stuffed it up for NT.

I am now running NT4 on a FAT16 partition, and LILO had no trouble installing
without stuffing the system up for NT. This may not be the most secure way to run a
system, but in many cases, FAT/NTFS isn't really that much of a system.

FYI, my system is a PII-300 with a 6.4Gb LBA HDD. I did have some fun trying to fix
the screwup thanks to the IDE ZIP which wouldn't let me "fdisk /mbr" the harddrive
from a dos boot floppy. But removing the drive from the CMOS setup solved that. I
seem to remember reading something about that somewhere...

I have two partitions for NT, for a total of 3Gb and the rest I left for RH, and I
did a "workstation" install straight off the CD. I have the main C: mounted on /nt4
and the d: mounted on /nt4data
