Re: IP Masquerading questions

> ipfwadm -F -p deny
> ipfwadm -F -a m -S 192.168.1.0/24 -D 0.0.0.0/0
> ipfwadm -F -a m -S 0.0.0.0/0 2000:4000 -P udp -D 192.168.1.2/32

First: what is this "-a m"?  You must have a different version of
ipfwadm than me.  Mine permits only "accept", "reject" and "deny".
Perhaps I could read that as "-a accept -m"...

How do packets, outside of the 2000 - 4000 UDP port range, get from
the outside world back in?  You allow any packet to get out from
within your LAN, but not back.  Further -- and here I expose my
lack of experience with masquerading firewalls -- packets sent to
you (from the Internet) will never be addressed to 192.168.1.2;
they will always be addressed to your gateway's external IP address.
This is the point of masquerading gateways.
