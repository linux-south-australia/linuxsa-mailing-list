Hmm... to me, a dongle sounds like a generic name for a thing that dangles
-- ie, a synonym for "dangly thing" ... sort of like "dangler", but sounding
better. It's a bit like "song" and "sang" -- "I sang the song", and "I
dangle the dongle" ...

also see: http://www.jargon.org/html/D/dongle.html

Thing is, language is dynamic, and dictionaries are merely descriptive of
current usage, as opposed to being prescriptive. "dongle", in the sense of a
copy-protection device is obsolete (because the devices themselves are no
longer in common use), and it would seem to be a perfectly sensible
re-application of the word to mean what we all think it means now...
