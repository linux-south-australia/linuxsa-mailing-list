South Australia has a law which says that an agent of a sporting or
community group carrying out the functions of that group is shielded
from individual liability (subject to the usual reasonable due care,
etc).

If the club/association/whatever is incorporated then they have limited
liability too.  And presumably insurance.

There's a Government office (whose name escapes me, but someone on this
list will know it) which exists exclusively for the purpose of 
assisting community groups, clubs, associations, etc by helping them
through the legal framework they need to navigate to do what they need
to do.  If someone else on the list can post the name of that office
then I'm sure a couple of well placed phone calls will yield useful
results.
