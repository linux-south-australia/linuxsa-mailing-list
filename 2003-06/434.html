Hi All,
    I don't know if it has been tested since, however as I recall about 2-3
(maybe longer) years ago when this came out, I contacted the Trade Practices
Commission (or whatever they call themselves now) and asked if this was
legal. According to them - it isn't, but they have not so far taken any
action. The reason that they had not taken any action was (in the words of
the person I spoke to - unofficially and off the record and not legally
binding in court), that apparently these sorts of things were not pursued
unless there was a groundswell of claims or that there was a blatant
flaunting of the Law that had some significant potential of blowing out or
it affected a significant proportion of the population. In his off the
record opinion, of the 5% of the population who use Linux (or any other OS
OS) and of the (maybe) 10% of those who use Toshiba Laptops, only a very
small proportion of those were interested in pursuing the matter. His advice
was basically either get a whole bunch of people making noise about it to
them AND Toshiba or try a small claim for breach of contract or whatever the
appropriate point of Law was (I forget as it was a while ago). If you tried
a small claim, Toshiba could not bring any Lawyers into it at all, just you
and a company Rep, and you could sue them for the sum involved (OEM License
is about $150) plus any costs incurred up to $1000 total claim. Basically
Toshiba were/are hiding behind peoples laziness of pursuing the matter and
trying to frighten them off and not pay if they threatened any other type of
legal action. I am not sure if it was you or someone else, but someone who
has talked about this in the last few days said they were a lawyer and would
be willing to test it so why not start a class action and pursue it, it
could be worthwhile. At least check up on the current state of the Law and
see if it is viable.
    Cheers!
        Andrew.
