Re: Squid Auth. question

All the systems that we use at university request your username and 
password, then click "ok" in order to access any sites that are outside the 
"unisa.edu.au" domain. When you close the last browser, it ends your session 
of browsing. If you open ie again after that, you will need to log on again. 

Is it really that hard to click an ok button? It's not as if they are going 
to get rsi or anything from 1 extra mouse-click every now and again. 

Maybe you should focus on more important things, and tell your rather picky 
users to just live with it. As I said, it is only one button!

> You could set it up as a transparent proxy, although it would need some
> rules added to your firewall/router to redirect all web traffic through
> your proxy.  The disadvantage to doing it this way is that your accounting
