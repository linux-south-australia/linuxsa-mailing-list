> Broadband and ADSL slow rollouts are the least of the average person's

> I live in a fairly new housing development in the North of Adelaide.  
> I had a strict budget, a need for a one level house (for family 
> medical reasons) and had my house built - they are still building the 
> last few houses in the development and still have a few empty lots.  
> The land prices are ***reasonably*** (eg not $150K+ but between $50K 
> and $100K - but started out at between $20K and $70K) priced for 
> people on the one income or low income.  Each time I asked Telstra 
> when my exchange would be ASDL enabled (easier for work access and 
> homework access) they couldn't give me a definite answer - even their 
> handy website checker said ASDL isn't enabled in your area yet, please 
> check back later - I wasn't planning on broadband for various reasons.
>
> I eventually discovered, a few years after I moved in, and after many 
> calls to Telstra, and deciding to change my expectations, to my shock, 
> that I cannot get cable (they wont lay it in my area and have no 
> intention of doing so at any time in the future), nor can I get ADSL 
> (they didn't lay the correct type of phone lines for that - they say 
> the original owners of the property development didn't specify it 
> being needed (so they picked the cheapest phone cable around - 
> regardless of the fact that the housing development is situated right 
> near a main arterial road with 20 minute access to the cbd and has 
> quite a few small and large businesses in and around it)).
>
> My only option is to go satellite and pay big bickies for the install 
> and then more big bickies on monthly satellite access with any ISP 
> (Telstra were very helpful with lots of other options - No, I don't 
> want to go ISDN - why bother - the cost isn't worth it and they 
> couldn't ensure it would work properly on the cheap phone lines or 
> give me more than 66K - not the 128K they advertise - and, No, I don't 
> want Foxtel - they only do satellite link - they can't/don't/wont do 
> cable in my area - who has time to watch lots of TV - to get it is a 
> further waste of money on re-run crap - No, I don't want BigPond 
> Satellite access, I already have an ISP I am happy with, also the cost 
> involved in putting in the satellite comes in yet again).
>
> So I have to put up with my somewhere between 21K and 33K modem link 
> (the phone line wont handle anything higher than that even if I could 
> tweak the settings of my 56K modem - another reason to forget ISDN) 
> until something else comes along or I move or (I doubt it would happen 
> in a brand new housing development) they upgrade the phone lines.
>
> This leads me to the conclusion that what I would like to see is 
> Telstra and the Parliament, tarred and feathered, drawn and quartered, 
> and then hung up for the vultures - or a royal commission with teeth 
> (is that an oxy-moron) into their supplying reasonable services to 
> ***all*** Australians (the government thinks if you can get 14K or 
> above, this is ***reasonable*** - try it sometime - you might last 5 
> minutes).
>
