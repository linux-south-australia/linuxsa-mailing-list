ISDN only uses a single copper pair (phone line) to run a digital service, 
usually replacing an existing phone line. On this digital service you get 2 
usable channels, which can be used with the NT1+ that comes as part of the ISDN 
home product. Because it is a digital service, you're guaranteed 64K per 
channel. Due to analogue-digital conversion issues, a 56K V90 modem on each 
channel will get a 49333 connection nearly every time like having the exchange 
in your house.

You can use a real ISDN device to use the full 64K of each channel, bonding 
them together to get 128K. If you don't like the price Telstra charge for that, 
you can use DoV, and get the 64K at the same price as a voice call if your ISP 
permits it.
