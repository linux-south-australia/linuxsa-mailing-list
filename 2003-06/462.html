Why not mention Moore's law as the basis (the # of transistors per chip
would double every 18 months) -- this could be translated into lay
person's terms as "CPU power doubles, on average, every 18 months"...

http://www.intel.com/research/silicon/mooreslaw.htm

Someone with a good memory (or more time for googleing) might like to
find the speeds the processors on that graph were released at...

It could be worth (depending on the age and interest level of the
students) inventing an analogy to describe the relationship between
clock speed, # of transistors, and the resultant CPU "power". You could
say something like with a bicycle, clock speed is how fast you pedal,
having more transistors is like having more gears (which it isn't, but
it's a weak analogy I made up on the spot), and the total "power" is
like how fast you actually go...
