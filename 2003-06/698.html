APANA, in conjunction with the Adelaide Computer Club,  is pleased to
announce a free seminar covering ADSL and Virtual Private Networks (VPN) for
people with a technical interest in computer networking. The seminar is to
be held on Saturday June 21, from 12.45pm-3.20pm, at the Henley and Grange
Town Hall (cnr Seaview Road and North St). It will cover technical aspects
of ADSL, how ADSL is implemented, how and why VPN's are used, and why an
economical VPN is useful for a hobbyist.

The seminar is free, and will have a technical emphasis. Light
refreshments will be provided. Ample parking is available near the venue.
This will be an interesting and informative seminar, and should be attended
by anyone who has an interest in the technical aspects of computer
networking and the Internet. Visitors are most welcome, but registration is
essential. Please phone 82325365 or email pr@sa.apana.org.au if you wish to
attend.

APANA is a not-for-profit hobby group providing internet access, technical
experience, and a hobby community for people who want to learn about all
aspects of computer networking. See http://sa.apana.org.au for more details.

The Adelaide Computer Club is a club which promotes the sharing of interest
and knowledge in computers amongst members.  The clubs activities are
centered around IBM compatible computers but the club also has a group with
an interest in older Tandy computers.   See http://ching.apana.org.au/~acc/
for more details.
