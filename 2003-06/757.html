Playing RevolutionOS DVD in SuSE Linux 8.2

I am very happy to announce that I am Independent from using Mplayer.
  Happy days are here again! Xine is a PITA too. But Noatun and Kaboodle
are excellent and reasonable to use for me.  Yeah, it doesn't show you a
play list, but I really don't care. I just want to watch my DVD of
Revolution OS, damnit!

Per the usual, I found this solution on accident, and it is realitively
easy one. This is exactly what I did. I am going to give all of the
possible details related to DVD playing, as my gut says any factor still
may apply.

Here are the DVD related RPM's I have installed on my desk as provided
by SuSE Linux 8.2:

1. dvdrtools 0.1.3-13 i586
2. dvd+rw-tools 4.1.3.0.3-23 i586
3. k3b 0.8.1-15 i586
4. libdvdnav 0.1.4-11 i586
5. libdvdread 0.9.3-86 i586
6. kdemultimedia3-video-xine 3.1.1-13 i586
7. kdemultimedia3-sound 3.1.1-13 i586
8. Mplayer 0.90rc4-25 i586

The following RPMs must be obtained elsewhere:

1. libdvdcss 1.2.6-1 i386
2. libxine1 1_beta12-0.pm.1 i686
3. xine-ui 0.9.21-0.pm.0 i686

And can be obtained from here:

#'s 1 & 2) http://packman.links2linux.org/index.php4?action=124
# 3) http://packman.links2linux.org/index.php4?action=125

How do I play the DVD?

1. Mount the DVD by clicking on the dvd icon on the KDE desktop
2. Click on the DVD again to view the contents of the DVD.
3. Click on the VIDEO_TS directory to expose the contents of the folder.
4. Right click on the VTS_01_1.VOB and select open with
5. Choose Kaboodle.
6. Repeat to watch all of the .VOB files.

Hope this helps!
