Re: gentoo vs freebsd

On Monday, June 2, 2003, at 07:23 PM, davidn@rebel.net.au wrote:
> They make it easy to stay up to date if you don't want to have to know
> the details.  I *do* want to know the details.

Then switch to Gentoo.

	emerge -p world

will tell you everything that can be updated, along with all the 
dependencies. You can then emerge just the ones you want to update, 
leaving the rest of the system alone.

You can see precisely what patches are being applied, control all the 
compile options being used, or extract the code so you can customise 
stuff with your own alterations.

It's a control freak's wet dream, without the soggy underwear 
afterwards.
