RE: Mounting drives on remote workstations

Trevor,

I assume you want to mount the slave drive across the network, so: Share
the drive in Win2k as you normally would (giving full permissions to
some user we'll call 'X').  In Linux, use "smbmount" to mount this
windows share.  Check the man page for smbmount for details of its use.
For the smbmount options "username=???,password=???", give the details
for the Windows user 'X' that you gave permissions to on the shared
drive.  Once mounted, you can read/write to your slave drive as you
would a local filesystem.
