Hi Richard,

Sorry for top-quoting.

Well, I guess you have a couple of options:

1. Fix from source, and suffer the loss of apt-gettable upgrades (until the fix is
added into the official package).

2. Talk to the maintainer. My experience is that often the maintainer has a fixed
package available in a non-official apt repository that you can add to your
sources list.

3. Swap from Courier to something else that isn't broken in the official package.

4. Do nothing. (I'm guessing you are experiencing too much frustration to do
nothing, but I'm adding it here for completeness)

I've never used courier, so I cannot comment on it's benefits or otherwise.
