RE: Turning a doorstop into something useful...

Well, it appears NetBSD won't run on it, so I doubt much will...

http://mail-index.netbsd.org/netbsd-ports/2000/07/15/0002.html

However, try:

http://elks.sourceforge.net/
http://elks.sourceforge.net/faq/FAQ-English.html

The Embeddable Linux Kernel Subset... Looks fascinating, though not quite
ready yet... still, if you want to be a kernel hacker, this could be a good
place to start learning... :)

Alternatively, try MINIX (it appears to be free-ish -- "For all practical
purposes, MINIX can be treated as if it were in the public domain.")

http://www.cs.vu.nl/~ast/minix.html

Yep, that's Andrew S Tanenbaum's home page -- the same who said the
following:

----
I still maintain the point that designing a monolithic kernel in 1991 is a
fundamental error. Be thankful you are not my student. You would not get a
high grade for such a design :-) (Andrew Tanenbaum to Linus Torvalds) 

Linux is obsolete (Andrew Tanenbaum) (in 1991)
----

And the famous response:

----
Your job is being a professor and researcher: That's one hell of a good
excuse for some of the brain-damages of minix. (Linus Torvalds to Andrew
Tanenbaum) 
----

Enjoy... :)
