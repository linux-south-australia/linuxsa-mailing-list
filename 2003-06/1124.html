acroread vulnerability

I was just thinking: "Isn't it amazing that there have been no
significant linux vulnerabilities that I've had to panic about in
the last couple of months."
And I always remember Daffy Duck saying: "Where are all the hunters today?"
(You may remember that this was in a film whose script was written by
Bugs Bunny, and that the hunters showed themselves immediately after
Daffy said this line.) I often rememeber this when I say: "Where are
all the linux bugs today?"

Well, surely enough, I look at the linuxsecurity.com site and see this:

http://www.linuxsecurity.com/advisories/gentoo_advisory-3399.html
http://marc.theaimsgroup.com/?l=full-disclosure&m=105555332025253&w=2

It seems that acroread on linux will execute arbitrary shell commands
if you click on an external URL in a PDF file.
This sounds pretty serious to me, although no one's made a fuss about it.
Am I imagining things or is this a serious problem that needs an acroread
update download pretty quick?

Anyway....
