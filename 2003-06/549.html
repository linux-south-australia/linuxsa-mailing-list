"Dongle" was probably originally defined by function, as
you suggest.

But I think "dongle" is now defined by form factor.  It
seems to me that anything that hangs out of a port with
a bulge on it now gets called a dongle:

  - parallel port software anti-copying devices (KEY-LOK, etc)
  - USB/RS-232 serial adapters
  - USB/Bluetooth adapters (Zeevo, etc)
  - and those RJ-45/propietary cables for PC Cards.

As support for the last I quote the 3Com parts list
on their web site:

    3Com® 100BASE-TX Fast Ethernet RJ-45 Cable

    Replacement dongle for connecting 3Com®
    Megahertz® 10/100 LAN and LAN+modem PC
    Cards to your network

    Product #: 3C-PC-TX-CBL
