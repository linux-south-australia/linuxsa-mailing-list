What are you trying to work out, exactly?  Your response to DL's previous
post suggests you're after nothing but the IP address, default gateway and
DNS servers that the DHCP server feeds a DHCP client.

Why not take a DHCP client of some sort, make it get a lease, and then
examing the client's settings?

I doubt there is a "right tool for the job".  I would suggest the "right
way" to find out what the server is telling your client is to ask the
client, or ask the server.  If you think they're lying to you, use a
network troubleshooting tool, like, say, ethereal.
