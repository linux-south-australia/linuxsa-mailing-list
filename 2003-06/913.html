Re: Coda & AFS

Richard,

You might want to look into enbd and drbd (network raid1).

We use drbd - on failover of the master, the secondary will have a 
replica of the data
and takes over the master's functionality via heartbeat.

These will give you redundancy, and can be set up for load balancing 
(tho this is a little ugly).

Apparently it is also possible to do it on WAN's or big LAN's, so you 
could have your master
in a server room somewhere and its drive mirrored on your desk top 3km's 
away. Backups
are then not needed :).

Cheers
