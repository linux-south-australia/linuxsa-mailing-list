So you're frustrated at people packaging applications differently on
different unix (or unix-like) operating systems? There's documentation
around that explains the logic behind this, but that doesn't force
application vendors or software packagers to conform to it. It's also a
little harder with Linux given that there is no base user-land part of
the operating system as there is with FreeBSD et al.

Does that make Unix illogical?

In the Hands-On Unix course I instruct, people often complain that vi
isn't logical, whereas most people on this list (even those who despise
vi) can probably see the logic behind most of the editor's features.
It's just not all that intuitive for the uninitiated.
