Re: gentoo vs freebsd

On Sunday,  1 June 2003 at 14:47:00 +0930, Daryl Tester wrote:
> Richard Russell wrote:
>
>> FreeBSD has two levels of partitioning -- the DOS Primary partitions,
>> which FreeBSD calls slices, and the FreeBSD partitions, within those
>> slices (sort of like DOS Extended, but not readable by much else).
>
> You also have logical volumes pretty much built into FreeBSD under the
> guise of Vinum.  On a FreeBSD machine I typically set up a root
> directory on ad0s1a, then configure the remainder of the disk(s) on
> ad?s2, and vinum that (well, set up the e partition to cover all of
> slice 2).  Then carve the remainder of your filesystems out of Vinum,
> which gets you over the 7 partition limit and allows for future
> expansion, not to mention several varieties of RAID configuration.

Vinum now supports the root partition as well.

>> SMP is rumoured to be more mature and faster under Linux, though
>> this doesn't matter much to me anyway. I understand that FreeBSD
>> 5.0 brings major improvements, though I don't know if that means
>> it's equal to Linux.
>
> I think kernel level threads were also an issue that 5.0 was meant
> to start addressing, but I haven't delved too deeply into FreeBSD
> as a development platform yet.

Kernel level threads are still under development.  I believe that 5.1
(appearing on an ftp server near you Real Soon Now) will include some
support for it, but there's more to come.
