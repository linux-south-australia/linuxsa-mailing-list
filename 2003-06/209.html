>Why not just tell everyone that they're attending the event entirely
>at their own risk, and get them to sign something to that effect?

Because under Australian law you cannot sign all of your rights away.   If
you do attempt to make attendee's sign away their rights, it is as good as
an admission that there was something dangerous in what they were doing
when the brown stuff strikes the reciprocating fan at high velocity.   Any
such contract will not stand up in court, it will be dismissed very
quickly.

David A's email was a very good dissertation of the way the law stands.

David L is correct with his assertions, bite the bullet and get insurance,
full stop.
