> On Mon, 16 Jun 2003 06:32 pm, Jason B. Standing wrote:
> > Hi folks...

> > Not linux-related, but anyway - what's the actual name of the thing that
> > plugs into a PCMCIA Ethernet card that you then plug a LAN cable into ?
> ive always had them referred to/referred to them as dongles

Nope,
Aint a dongle. As someone else stated, a dongle is purely a copyright
protection device. CISCO call a similar device a tranceiver, which is an
acronym for transmitter/receiver. This tranceiver converts the signal to use
another medium, ie BNC to RJ45. So, if you had a NIC, Network Interface Card
with a BNC socket on it, and you wanted to connect a cable using an RJ45
plug, you would use the appropriate tranceiver for the task. They are a
layer 1 device, except if they are onboard the NIC they are a layer 2
device. So, a change from PCMCIA to RJ45 would be possible.
