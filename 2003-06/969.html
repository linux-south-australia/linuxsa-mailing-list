Linux desktops may go past Apple?

FLIPPING THE SWITCH
Linux's new popularity may hurt Apple more than Microsoft.
By Paul Boutin
Posted Slate, Monday, June 23, 2003
<http://slate.msn.com/id/2084727/>;
 
 In the latest of his legendary keynote stage shows, Steve
 Jobs kicked off Apple's Worldwide Developers Conference this
 morning in San Francisco by showing off the company's speedy
 new aluminum G5 desktop Mac.
 <http://www.apple.com/powermac>;

 But while listing the new machine's impressive specs, Jobs
 left out a related, eye-popping statistic: Business Week
 columnist Alex Salkever dropped the bomb last week that next
 year, "Linux should pass Apple in market share for desktop
 operating systems on computers."
 
<http://www.businessweek.com/technology/content/jun2003/tc20030618_7983
