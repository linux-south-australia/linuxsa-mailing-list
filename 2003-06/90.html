Sometimes it is possible to close the hole without upgrading by adjusting
the configuration. I just take each threat on it's merits and decide what
to do when I understand the implications for my systems. A lot of holes
are closed very quickly by the distributions, so sometimes it is as easy
as updating.

You're right about the implications of a patch vs update approach. If you
patch, you quickly get to the point of making update irrelevant for many
important packages, and probably broken bacause you can't use update to
fix packages that are not 'distribution-native' packages (unless someone
is hiding the secret somewhere), so you land up having 2 processes to run:
patch the source based stuff and update the distro based stuff. That could
get painful after a year or so...
