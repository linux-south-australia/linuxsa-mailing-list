Re: Playing RevolutionOS DVD in SuSE Linux 8.2

This is how I set up DVD playing in Linux:

1) Install ogle
2) load ogle
3) click file->open disc
4) watch DVD

:)

Seriously, ogle has no competition in the DVD playing arena on Linux, it is 
the best. It has full support for menu's (I have *never* seen it fail), it 
has 4.1/5.1 channel support (I use it on my Logitech Z560's, very nice). You 
can skip to various chapters/menu's from the GUI, it has subtitle support, 
etc etc... it's at version 0.9.1, I installed the red hat 7.x rpm's on 
mandrake 9.1, it worked fine...

For using Mplayer, download kmplayer for a nice front end to it (as well as 
for embedding playback in Konqueror). Then install all the codecs (quicktime, 
etc) from the Mplayer website. Works like a charm for me, haven't touched 
Xine since (xine is the _worst_ player *ever*).

Cheers,

Damien

> 5. libdvdread 0.9.3-86 i586

> How do I play the DVD?
>
> 1. Mount the DVD by clicking on the dvd icon on the KDE desktop
> 2. Click on the DVD again to view the contents of the DVD.
> 3. Click on the VIDEO_TS directory to expose the contents of the folder.
> 4. Right click on the VTS_01_1.VOB and select open with
> 5. Choose Kaboodle.
> 6. Repeat to watch all of the .VOB files.
>
