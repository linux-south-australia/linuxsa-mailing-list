Re: Wireless PCI adapter

> could any one tell me what PCI adapter that will work with LINUX
> I want to set up a wireless home net work using Linux
> Thankyou

As an access point?  Your only luck's going to be Prism based cards - the only
ones I know of at the moment that sell in Australia are the Sparklan 360's,
and they're only stocked in Adelaide at the moment (although, there may be a
local distributor soon).

As a client - an orinoco/hermes style card (Cabletron, Orinoco, Lucent, etc)
will run fine in a pcmcia cradle.

Personally, I think your best bet in terms of making it simple will be to buy
a wireless router from Dlink, and for the client machines, the orinoco card in
the pcmcia cradle.  I think netgear sell this gear reasonably cheaply (it's
fine for indoor stuff; I don't use netgear because most my wireless installs
are outdoor, and netgear seems to suck at this).

Be careful - all (afaik) the 22mbit cards won't work in Linux, at least not
without binary Linux drivers that'll most certainly make your kernel lock up /
freeze intermittedly.  This isn't Linux's fault, its the makers of the
wireless chipsets being pains in the rear.
