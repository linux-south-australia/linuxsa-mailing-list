Please forgive me if this has been said already but fairly recently (within
the last 2 months) Telstra have changed their policy regarding folk on RIMs
vs. ADSL.  When they receive an order for ADSL, they will now automatically
check to see if there are any spare copper pairs running between the
exchange and the RIM.

If there is, they will change your line over to the copper (thus bypassing
the RIM) and perform a line loss test, which, if passes will result in the
ADSL service being provisioned on your line.

My advice to anybody that has previously been rejected for being on a RIM
prior to around April/May, reapply as you may now be successful.

Graham
