[OT] Toshiba and bundled software

I know Geoffrey and possibly others have managed to obtain a refund from
Toshiba/Microsoft for their bundled copy of Windows by not accepting the
Windows license agreement and returning the software as suggested in the
EULA.

I just unboxed a brand new Toshiba notebook and found a big yellow
sticker reading:

"IMPORTANT NOTICE

The software products pre-installed on or bundled with your computer are
copyrighted works.  Before opening this package, please read the license
agreement furnished with the computer.  By opening this package and
turning on the computer, you indicated that you have read and accepted
these software license terms.  In order to maintain the highest quality
standards under the Toshiba brand name, TOSHIBA committed to delivering
computing solutions to the customer as a total PC system.
Notwithstanding anything to the contrary in the documentation
accompanying your computer, TOSHIBA does not accept the return of
components parts, or bundled software, which have been removed from the
PC system.  Pro-rata refunds on individual PC components or bundled
software, including the operating system, will not be granted.  If you
wish to return a complete PC system, contact the TOSHIBA dealer where
you purchased the product, and comply with the dealer's standard return
policies and procedures."

Looks like you'll be stuck with your Windows license next time (assuming
you still want to buy a Toshiba, that is.)
