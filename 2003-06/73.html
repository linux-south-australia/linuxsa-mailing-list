Also agreed, and it does for some combinations of packages and platforms.

My point is: don't upgrade without a reason.  If you wait until you
have a reason, perhaps you need a new feature or you've become aware
of a security concern, the additional effort to upgrade dependancies
is usually too little to worry about.  It tends to be quickly clear
when the extra effort will be large, and then I do a new install; that
happens really infrequently.

All this worry about which distribution is easier for automatic upgrades
is rather like looking for a way to let you upgrade without having to
know the reason why.  As I said, I don't do that.  And I save time by
not worrying about distributions.  I can't see any advantage in doing
unnecessary work. ;-)
