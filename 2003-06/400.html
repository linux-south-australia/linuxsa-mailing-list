> 1) Correct configuration of your MTA (mail transfer agent).
>    This should be setup to ONLY allow relay from within your
> organisations private address space (eg 192.168.1.x etc etc)

Alternatively, pop-before-smtp...

> 2) Correct configuration of you Firewall (iptables/ipchains).
>    I prefer to take the attitude here as to first look at what
> absolutely NEEDS to be allowed in (ie 25/tcp for mail, 80/tcp for web
> server etc) and then generate a script that sets a default drop/block

I generally agree with this; but I've seen many, many poorly configured
systems where the daemon's open plain as day, with the *only* protection being

Securing your daemons is an Important Thing to your system's security policy,
IMHO.

Ack, they don't seem terribly 'experienced' if they left an open proxy *AND*
