Re: Telnet on my DSL-connected system: Connection refused

On Mon, Jun 09, 2003 at 07:28:29PM +0200, Marcus Minzenmay said:
> Hi ,
> 
> I am running a Suse-Linux 7.2 System as Internet and Mailgateway to my 
> private lan. These days I made it to register my system to dyndns.org - 
> works fine so far.
> I just have a problem to access to the system from the Internet:
> 
> While ssh and telnet from my private lan works fine, the access from the 
> Internet ist denied with (telnet) " telnet: connect to address x.x.x.x : 
> Connection refused" or
> (ssh) "Secure connection to x.x.x.x refused." . The only thing that 
> works is "ping".
> 
> The "hosts.allow" and  "hosts.deny" - files are left empty. Also the 
> Suse firewall-stuff ist deactivated like my own iptables.
> 
> Any idear ? Thanks in advance,

Is the DSL device configured for NAT?  If it is, you'll need to tell it to
forward connections from the Internet to port 22 and 23 (or whatever ports
you choose) to your internal machine(s).  That's sometimes called "inbound
nat", sometimes "pinholes", sometimes "network servers", and often
something completely different.

Best of luck.
