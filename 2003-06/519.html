* Matthew Western <mwestern@affairs.net.au> [2003-06-16 14:04]:
> I also agree.   there was a lot of noise a while ago about smoothwall going
> non-GPL but there must have been enough stink to make them rethink not going

Actually, the problems most people had with smoothwall was not the non-GPL shift
that happened with the commercial product they released. At the time, there was a
particularly abrasive founder and lead developer who would abuse users at the drop
of a hat. Just asking a question the wrong way would get threats against your
character and removal from the users mailing list.

There was also an awful lot of noise about 'have you donated yet?' and how the
family were suffering because of the lack of donations etc. That was a bit rich,
but I would have donated if the abuse had not been present.

IpCop grew out of that abuse. Eventually, there was enough people with development
abilities to fork the smoothwall GPL code and go their own way. The project is
active, and is running on current 2.4 series kernels.

I'm sure smoothwall is an effective system. I think it may be slightly behind
IpCop for the GPL version (I haven't looked recently), but that won't be a problem
for it's intended audience. Personally, I wouldn't touch it because the wounds
have not healed over yet - I can't support something created by an abusive git,
even if he's left the project now.
