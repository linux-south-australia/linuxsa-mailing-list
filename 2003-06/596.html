OK, so after sifting through all of those replies (thanks !!), we can
summarise by saying:

   A dongle was previously the term given to a passthrough device to
   plug into a port on the back of a PC, containing circuitry to
   activate/enable software packages, such as CAD.

   As of approximately 1999, the term now refers ALSO to a short cable/
   socket arrangement used to interface between PCMCIA cards and other
   connectors, such as RJ45 or RJ11.

Cool.  I think that'll placate the argument :)
