In regaurds to the partitioning, put your swap in the middle of the drive
with / on one side and /usr on the other. Make / as small as possible (I
have 15M), make /var, /tmp, /home, /usr, /usr/src and /opt all individual
partitions. Having swap in the middle is optimal as the head spend most 
of it's time going ove that part of the drive and having / and /usr close
to it is also optimal as those are the partitions are where the heads
spend most of their time :) The size of your partitions is up to you, but
like I have said make / as small as possible 'coz if a unice gets bad
sectors in / then the show is over.....a bad sector in any other
partition and it will recover well but / is a special case. I recomend
/usr be at least 1GB.
