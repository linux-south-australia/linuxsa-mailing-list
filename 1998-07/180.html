Re: firewalling, accounting etc

> We have a 'customer' who is located in our building who would like to
> get a direct cable to our bandwidth ( twisted pair ethernet ). I have
> been thinking about different ways of how to account for this. I know
> that a firewall can account for packets going to a certain ip address,
> but am not sure how it does this or in what format. I know routers can
> also do this, but once again I am not sure in what format. Can anybody
> enlighten me, make sugestions, or point me in the right direction.
> Thanks in advance,

If you set everything up right you can use a switched ethernet backbone
(such as a cat 19xx) then use snmp stats to do the billing. There are also
security issues if you put that customer on your main collision domain !

Another way to do it is to slide another ethernet card into a linux box
and then do byte accounting on the interface - this gives you the byte
counters as well as putting a routed interface in the way that you can
realistically do firewalling on and by nature filters out broadcast
traffic from your production network incase it leaks onto their network.
