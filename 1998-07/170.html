Hi Franco,

you wrote:

> I am trying to get an application to talk to a specific port.  I've
> edited
> /etc/services 
>   >po  3002/udp   PO
> and /etc/inetd.conf, 
>   >po  dgram  udp  wait  root  internal
> then restarted inetd (killall -HUP inetd) [in fact even rebooted since
> then].

(presuming that the ">" is not actually in the file...)

Does the inetd you are running document an internal service called
"po"?  The keyword "internal" means that inetd will handle the service
itself, and not spawn another program to do so, but the inetd I have
here only lists echo, discard, chargen, daytime, and time.
/var/log/messages might have a complaint or two from inetd about this.

> I'm finding that the application still wont talk to this particular
> port.
> I'm not sure if this is a Linux problem or an application problem.  Have
> I edited all the necessary files under Linux?

If your application tries to open a connection to the po/udp port,
then yes (except that inetd probably doesn't know what to do with it).

> When I try  "> telnet {hostname} po " I get "po: bad port number"

Telnet uses TCP, not UDP, and you defined only po/udp, not po/tcp.

> but when I try ">telnet {hostname} 3002" I get 
> Trying aaa.bbb.ccc.ddd...
> telnet: Unable to connect to remote host: Connection refused

Similarly, that's because inetd is handling po/udp, not po/tcp.

> Shouldn't I get this in both cases ?

No.

> Hope somebody may be able to shed some light,

I hope this helps, but knowing what is the application is, and what
are you trying to make it connect to would help in figuring out what
you are meant to be putting in those files.
