Americanisms and relational DB's

Hi all, 

I have received the following questions from a friend, regarding an
American correspondence course.  

The first part of her assignment says:
--------------------------------------------------------------------
(1) What is the role of the end user in the dev of Info Systems (IS)?
(2) What is End User Computing ?
(3) What are the Advantages & Disadvantages of End User Computing as one
    of the alternatives in IS development ?
(4) When is it appropriate to make use of End user computing in an Org.?
(5) How will an end user ensure (i) quality of and (ii) a secure system
    is being developed.?
---------------------------------------------------------------------
What do they mean by end user computing? I get a little bit concerned
when I see things like this as;  1 - I don't understand what they mean,
and 2 - I'm not sure what they know what they mean either.

It's actually for an MS Access course, so who knows what they're trying
to get at.  Is it a Client/Server verses Standalone programming
model difference, or are they just trying to say "that it's up to the
user to design a system that works for them and if they stuff up it's
not our fault because we only supplied the tools." ? (Sounds like the
MS Access model to me.)

PaulS
