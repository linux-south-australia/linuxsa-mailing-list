Video For Linux (and Asus RIVA TNT2 Ultra Cards)

Adam Hawes writes:
 > 
 > I am looking for information on the Asus video cards under Video For
 > Linux... At the moment I am unable to find any information about capture
 > devices.  The card runs beautifully under XFree86 (3.3.5).
 > 
 > Does anybody have one of these cards running under Linux and use the
 > video capture parts of it?
 > 

I haven't heard of anyone writing a driver for V4l or v4l2 for this
sort of card. The only Display card that I know of that has support
for Video Input on its frame grabber is the Matrox (Marvel ? ). The
rest of the drivers are for stand-alone cards.

The web sites to look at are :

http://roadrunner.swansea.uk.linux.org/v4l.shtml

There are additional V4l2 drivers (but that wasn't the question asked
).

http://millennium.diads.com/bdirks/v4l2.htm

I also have some additional references to some weird and wonderful
frame grabber drivers around :

http://apasun.atlantek.com.au/USERS/wes/linux/frame/


The last I have heard, the frame grabber driver is being rewritten by
Alan Cox and others (for 2.4) to allow among other things :

- usages of the Xfree4.0 xv features (they finally made a port from
X11R6 xv ;-)

- scatter gather DMA in user space pages (for efficient transfers and
having very large buffer spaces  - the present drivers are limited to
using kernel memory space or using the big phys. area patch  - see the 
zoran drive).

- better interfaces to other devices for example differents sorts of
codecs (hardware processing ).
