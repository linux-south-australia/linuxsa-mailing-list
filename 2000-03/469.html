Brian Astill sent this to linuxsa around the middle of January (I only 
have a hard copy of the message he sent - minus the mail headers). Me 
paraphrasing:- "You enable the keyboard mouse by pressing <CTRL>, <SHIFT> 
and <NUMLOCK> keys at the same time. Apparently the speaker should beep, 
but mine never does. 

The numeric keypad is the control. The even numbers move the cursor in 
the direction indicated by the arrows painted on them (and the odd 
numbers give the diagonals - Me). [He went so far as to have a little 
drawing - I'm not].  The "/", "*" and "-" become the 1st (left?), 2nd 
(middle?)  and 3rd (right?) mouse buttons in that order.

The number 5 key:
While pressing any of 1-4, or 5-9 keys speeds the mouse cursor up.
Pressing the number 5 key on its own does a single click of the 
current button. (?)

Pressing the "+" on it own does a double click of the current button.
Pressing the "0" (zero) clicks & locks the current button.
Pressing the "." key releases the "0" lock."  End paraphrasing & 
inserted comments

That's the basics of it,  He also referred to a file I don't have, you 
might:
/usr/doc/WindowMaker/README .
