 > Have you ever been shopping with an autistic child, tried to receive

No.  I do, however, sing in a choir with an Autistic man.  During the 
first rehersal for every concert when nobody knows the music he'll 
say, "It doesn't sound very good, does it?  I don't think I'll sing
in this concert because we're not very good at it."  At every rehersal
he'll have the same smalltalk conversation with each of the two or
three people he's "connected" with ("What do you think of the music?
Will you be singing in this concert?  It's hard work, isn't it?").  
Believe me, I completely understand the frustration of dealing with
someone who can't learn from the past, has very little perception of
the difference between past, present and future, and who can't perceive
things from perspectives other than their own.  I can't begin imagine
the difficulties of living it day-in-day-out with it for years.

However, one of the ways that people make sense of difficulties is
by laughing at them;  I found the Bill Gates stuff funny *because* I
spend three hours per week with an autistic man who exhibits many of
the same traits lampooned in the joke.  And, incidentally, that doesn't
mean I enjoy making fun of autistic people, it means I enjoy making fun
of Bill Gates (whether or not he happens to be autistic too).

So who, exactly, was being made fun of by the Bill Gates posting?  
I thought it was Bill Gates, but you seem to think it was the entire 
subset of human beings who suffer from autism.  I find this difference
in perspective interesting, if perplexing, and it seems clear that it
goes a long way towards understanding why the posting "hit a raw nerve."
Can you identify the difference between a slur on an individual, and a 
slur on a group to which that individual belongs?

 > I can have fun and a
 > good joke, but not at the expense of others, it's not needed.

Again, I challenge you to find any joke at all which *isn't* "at the expense
of others".  Perhaps you have your own personal idea of what that phrase
means, but I guarantee that someone, somewhere, will have an opinion about
it that is different enough that they'll be mortally offended by a joke
you think it perfectly innocent, leading them to demand that you "take 
responsibility" for the joke... Or, in other words, "Shut the hell up."

So you're left with two alternatives:  Go through life being terribly,
terribly serious about anything and everything lest you offend someone
(That's called "taking responsibility for your words"), or relaxing,
accepting that you can't please all of the people all of the time, and
saying what you feel good about saying (which is often called "harrassment,"
"vilification," or, in extreme cases, a "hate crime").
