Re: simple questions

> i have a few simple questions for you.
>
> 1. how can i find the drive space on a specific drive?

df(1) will tell you a lot about mounted drives (device name, total space,
used space, free space, % use, and mount point)... try the man page for more
info.

> 2. how can i mount a second phisical hard disk when the first is
> partitioned? i don't know what each partition is currently called.

If you are using IDE disks, they are named as follows:

Primary Master:    /dev/hda<n>
Primary Slave:    /dev/hdb<n>
Secondary Master:    /dev/hdc<n>
Secondary Slave:   /dev/hdd<n>

where <n> is an integer referring to the partition (on Intel boxes, 1-4 are
primary partitions, 5 onwards are logical partitions)

SCSI disks are similarly named: /dev/sd[a-p]<n>

have a look at the man pages for mount(8), fstab(5), fdisk(8) and
/usr/src/linux/Documentation/devices.txt (also available from
http://www.kernel.org/pub/linux/docs/device-list/ )
