Ummm, I was under the impression that BlowFish was a symmetric encryption alg.

A question like that tends to indicate a lack of understanding about what
public key and symmetric encryption techniques are for.

Because public key encryption is very much slower that symmetric
encryption, it tends to be used for authentication (ie, ensuring that the
two parties know who they are talking to), and exchanging a randomly
generated session key for a symmetric encryption alg.

Public key encryption also tends to get used for things like signing
certificates.
