> how do I kill a specific port so I can convince Netscape to try again?

You can't kill a port.  You can kill the process that has a port open -- and
there might be more than one of them!  When no process has a port open the
operating system will close that port.  Closing a port is a (straining my
memory here) three part procedure; don't think it will be instantaneous.

I doubt that closing a port would convince Netscape to do anything again.
Most likey you'd get a warning, "Remote server has closed connection." or
similar.
