> Actually, you need to deal with them _now_.  You cannot route private
> IP address over the Internet proper (you can tunnel, but that's a
> different kettle of hamsters).  Your internal LAN needs to either
> masquerade behind the IP address of your dialup connection (ipmasq),
> or use the appropriate proxies (http, etc) on your router (which still
> amounts to a masquerading of a sort, as you are using your ISP assigned
> IP address to talk to the outside world).  If you try to inject a
> 172.16.0.0/16 packet onto the Internet, chances are good that it won't
> come back to you.
