Your BIOS supports a maximum of 8GB. I would not have thought Disk Druid
would have complained, but LILO will probably have a hissy fit. You
should read the BIG Disk HOWTO (called Large-Drive-HOWTO or something).
It's on the CD I think.

You might find using Linux fdisk better. It will complain, but let you
do 'bad' stuff anyway.

Linux fdisk time. Be very careful. just boot off the redhat CD
(whichever version) and when you get to the bit about Disk Druid, select
fdisk. You are just looking. Do not change anything. You may end up
killing everything (else). type 'p' to view which partitions you have.
Take down what it tells you. E-Mail the details to the list or myself as
it should help find the problem

Good luck.

Matt

P.S. If you can avoid it, try not to access the drives too much from
Windows, as I have seen it do some nasty stuff when it isn't sure.
