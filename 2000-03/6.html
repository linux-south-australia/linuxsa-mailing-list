Re: The future of Linux

On Tue, Feb 29, 2000 at 10:42:06AM -0500, Sulis, Adam wrote:

 > To retouch on the Open Source problem again: we started out with Linux,
 > created by Linus and a few peers. Then the concept of the distro came, and
 > the distros grew and evolved. Where will this lead; will Linux distros
 > evolve so far from each other that they actually cease to be the same OS?

No, the GPL seems to be doing a  good job of stopping that:  People have
been foretelling doom and despair over this for years, but it hasn't 
happened, and it won't happen for as long as one distribution can get
the source code from another.

I really don't see the problem:  Why do you need to care about how many
distributions are on the market if the apps work with all of them and
your organization only really needs to get one?  This isn't an MS world,
where "lots of different operating systems" means "lots of incompatible
systems";  The Linux marketplace is a different beast, which can enjoy
diversity without sacrificing compatibility.

 > In the commercial world of Unix, SCO people and Soraris people are not the
 > same. Will we someday be so divided that SUSE-certified people won't be
 > up-to-date on Debian, and so forth?

No.
