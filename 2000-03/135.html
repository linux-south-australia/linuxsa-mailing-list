Re: linux pre-installed

Hi -  I do this sort of thing all the time and although I have only ever
done it with caldera, suse & slakware - cant see why rh or mandrake etc
would be any different ;)
ram is irrelevant basically, there are a few gotcha's but other than NOT
starting X until reconfiguring for the new video card (unless its the
same as was in the machine the hdd came from) - its no big deal!

Scenario = someone wants to play with linux - they give me a hard drive
(usually 1.2 or 1.6gb)
I jumper it as a slave & hang it off a 486dx2/66 I have with a standard
install (trident 8900 card, sb16 sound card, ne2000 isa network card
etc) and then I fdisk, mke2fs mkswap & then use MC to just copy
everything (except /mnt & /proc ;) )
then mkdir /mnt/mnt & /mnt/proc - edit /etc/fstab & /etc/lilo and a few
other minor things & then umount it.
rejumper it to master if needed then give them a boot floppy &
instructions to run lilo after mounting ...
sometimes I write a little script file if they are really "challenged"
:)

there are the odd little probs but as long as its a standard generic
kernel no major drama... you need to rerun lilo, change network cards
maybe, but it works well.
NB: if you had recompiled the kernel for 586+ and you stuck it in a 486
it WONT work !!
