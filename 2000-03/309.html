Hi again,
Many thanks to you all for the many and varied suggestions.  A closer look at
the file revealed that there was one small piece of text on the second line of
the second part of the file, that was unique and always present.
With this in mind, the answer as part of a larger script was as follows:

#!/dos/command.com   ;c)
csplit -s -f splitfile. filename /unique-text/-1             <--- this is the
bit I needed
dostuff splitfile.00
dostuff splitfile.01
rm -f splitfile.*

This gives me the two files I need, to process as I need/want.
If nothing else, it goes to show that there are many, many ways to accomplish
things with the tools available.
All I need now is time and grey cells to learn them all.  *sigh*
