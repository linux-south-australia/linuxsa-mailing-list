Re: once an hour

I had a similar problem a few years ago, when I was a student at Adelaide
Uni, in the CS department. The CS dept instituted a mailbox quota, and if an
incoming mail caused your mailbox to exceed this size (1 or 5 Mb, from
memory), the mail would be left unsent, and another attempt would be made a
period of time later (I think it was less than 1 hour, from memory). A
number of CS students and staff were on the LinuxSA list at this time, and
one or more of the mailboxes belonging to these people exceeded this limit,
causing those messages to be resent every hour. Unfortunately, the mail
program they were using at the time did not keep track of who the message
had been sent to already and who it hadn't been, so the rejected messages
got resent at regular intervals to everyone at Adelaide who was on the list.
Of course, the original overflowing inbox that precipitated the resending of
all this mail caused all the other mailboxes on the list to fill up rapidly,
making the problem worse... Also, as time went on, the number of mails being
resent regularly increased rapidly.

The possible solutions to this (if this is actually your problem...) are as
follows: somehow get the offending inboxes cleared -- rather difficult,
unless you are root on the mail server (note that you can't just send mail
to the offenders asking them to clear their mail, as your mail will be
rejected until they have cleared their mail themselves). Alternatively, you
need to get the mailbox size policy changed -- again, difficult, as there is
usually a very good reason for instituting these restrictions in the first
place. I believe that the solution used (eventually) in the CS department
was to install updated mail software that could handle this better.

HTH
