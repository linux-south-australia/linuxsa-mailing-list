Re: Lilo

Yes there is. You need not to copy suse kernel to /boot directory of redhat.
Just comment out the "boot=/dev/hda". Because the boot parameter is ommited,
the boot sector is read from the device currently mounted as root.

Ray Chin

On Fri, 10 Mar 2000, Brian Marr wrote:
> Re setting up Lilo to boot RH, Suse and win95.
> 
> Thanks to Matt, Jake & Richard for your suggestions.
> 
> My attempts to twig Lilo so that is could find the Suse kernel were to
> no avail. In the end I followed your theme and copied the Suse kernel to
> the /boot directory of RHat. I ran Lilo and Suse booted up ok with its
> current 2.2.13 kernel.
> 
> There are probably other ways that you can do this.
> 
> Brian Marr
> 
> boot=/dev/hda
> map=/boot/map
> install=/boot/boot.b
> prompt
> timeout=60
> #Boot RedHat
> image=/boot/vmlinuz-2.2.12smp
>         label=RedHat
>         root=/dev/hdb1
> 
> #Boot Suse
> image=/boot/vmlinuz.suse        
>         label=Suse
>         root=/dev/hdc2
> 
>                 
> #Boot old kernel         
> image=/boot/vmlinuz-2.2.5-15
>         label=linuxOld
>         root=/dev/hdb1
> 
> #Boot Windows 95
> other=/dev/hda1
>         label=win95
>         table=/dev/hda
