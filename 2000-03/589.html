comp.talk.ietf -- a week of seminars by IETF meeting attendees


The 47th meeting of the Internet Engineering Task Force is being held
in Adelaide from the 26th to 31st of March. The meeting brings
together the people that built the Internet.

Some attendees at the meeting have graciously offered to give one hour
presentations to staff, students and the public. The planned times and
locations are:

Mon 27th 1-2pm

  Horace Lamb Lecture Theatre
  Level 1, Horace Lamb/Plaza Building

Tue 28th 12-1pm

  Flentje Lecture Theatre
  Level 2, Plaza Building

Wed 29th, 12-1pm

  Horace Lamb Lecture Theatre,
  Level 1, Horace Lamb/Plaza Building

Thu 30th, 12-1pm

  Horace Lamb Lecture Theatre,
  Level 1, Horace Lamb/Plaza Building

Fri 31st, 12-1pm

  Horace Lamb Lecture Theatre,
  Level 1, Horace Lamb/Plaza Building

                --------------------------------------

                             MONDAY 27th

                             E. Paul Love
               Chair, Internet2 Topology Working Group

                              INTERNET 2
                      http://www.internet2.edu/

Internet2 is developing the next generation of Internet applications.
It also operates a gigabit-speed optical network linking research
facilities and universities across the United States.

All are welcome to attend.  Free admission.

                --------------------------------------

The presentations for the other days will be announced next week.

Hopefully they will include large e-mail servers, and performance of
TCP/IP protocol implementations.
