Re: help needed with sed problem

Hi Michael,
don't know about the i \ option, but you could try:

sed 's/search.*/&(Insert here)/' <existing file> > <new file>

This wuold search for lines that contain the word "search" or whatever you specify, 
and place the phrase "Insert here" at the end of that line. It would copy the 
file with changed lines into the new file.

The 's' switch at the beginning of the command means search (and replace) mode. It
replaces lines that contain the something in the first "/ /" with something in the
second "/ /". 

hope this helps,
Andrew O'C
