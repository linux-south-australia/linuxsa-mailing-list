Re: Fw: [mostly OT] Cable Internet access

> What is ADSL ? I have only heard of satelite and cable

Asymmetric Digital Subscriber Loop.  Also known as xDSL because there are
a number of different DSLs with various characteristics.

ADSL uses the existing copper between the exchange and your house to send
high speed data as well as voice.  Data is sent at frequencies outside of
speech.  The speed of the forward data path need not be the same as the
reverse path, so for example you can receive data at millions of bits per
second but only send at thousands of bits per second.

Everyone is excited about ADSL because your computer is permanently
connected to the internet (but note that you pay for data) and at very high
speeds (eg 56k is very slow.)

I don't know how slowly Telstra will go over rollout of xDSL.  It might
well be years before it's available in your home in Adelaide.
