5 years of LinuxSA in 6 days :-)

Hi all,

Just in case you haven't been listening to Michael's posts about
Linux.Conf.Au :-)  here's a reminder:  South Australia is soon going
to host Australia's Premier Linux conference.

In just a smidgen over 10 weeks time, we're going to have:

- 8 tutorials - NSA Linux, Exim, PostgreSQL, Cairo, Augmented Reality,
  PHP, GNOME and Annodex.

- 36 papers - BIND, uCLinux, DBUS, SCOvIBM, AOL|7, Exim, NetBSD, LSB,
  GStreamer, Linux Evangelism, TeX, Linux Device Drivers, umlsim,
  reverse engineering, distcc, LVS, MySQL, Vinum, GNOME and python,
  Undergraduate open-source curriculum, RCU, C-Bus, Perl 6, GNOME,
  KDE, Simulated systems, libsysfs, Linux power management, Tridge's
  junkcode, php-apd, storage architectures, managing lots of machines,
  GCC, and PIC micro-controllers (whew!),

- 3 keynotes,

- 7 miniconfs - Debian, Audio, educationaLinux, IPv6, OSS in
  Government, GNOME.conf.au, and python,

- LPI exams at a greatly reduced price,

- Penguin Dinner,

- Professional Delegates Networking session,

- The Hackfest competition,

- FIXITs (BOFs with a purpose),

- Speakers Coaching Session,

- Works in Progress sessions,

- and some extra special surprises which we can't announce publicly
  (you'll just have to come and find out for yourself :-)

It's going to be like 5 years of LinuxSA crammed into 6 days (but even
better :-)!  So don't miss this opportunity to hear and meet lots of
excellent speakers from all over Australia and the world.  It won't be
back in Adelaide for a long time.

So go to http://lca2004.linux.org.au/register/ -- if registrations
fill up and you miss out, you *will* be disappointed!

Have fun,
