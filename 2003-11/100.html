There's nothing really new here though - Red Hat have had sunset clauses
on support for their OSes for a couple of years now at least, and the Red
Hat Linux Project (now transmogrified into Fedora) which was announced 6
months ago also said it was replacing "standard Red Hat Linux".

There's no margin for _companies_ to provide QA'd free downloadable OSes. 
There never has been.  I think you'll find that "enthusiasts" will start
running community distributions (Debian, Fedora) more and we'll end up
paying for full-on 24x7 supported distros which we'll roll out to our
clients.  Or rely upon Debian-stable :-)
