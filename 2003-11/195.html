I dunno about everyone else, but I didn't fancy Fedora Core 1 as much as
Redhat 9.  The installer has been dumbed down.

AFAIK (I may have missed it) you can't specify exactly what packages you
want.  Ie, if you select text based internet, you can't specify you want
wget but not lynx, etc.

Also, if you happen to control+alt+f1 while the bootup splash screen is
active, it'll sit on "initialising swap space [ok]", and control+alt+f7
won't get you to the initial Fedora setup wizard.  I can't remember how
I ended getting around this, might have been control+alt+f8 or
something.

I guess it's going to take a few months before we know exactly what to
expect from Fedora.  I understand we now have yum and whatnot for
updates, but I've yet to see a decent howto with how everything fits
together. If I work it out, I might write something up. =)
