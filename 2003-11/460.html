Re: Can I (externally) host my own domain?

David WOLVERTON wrote:

> I don't mind the extra $ - provided that I have a dependable, reliable
> Service that costs less than $150 per month (what I'm currently paying) and
> is 'unlimited' in the bandwidth that I pull down - as well as unmetered in
> the bandwidth that goes out.

If you truly want a reliable, dependable service, you wouldn't be doing 
this on DSL in the first place :-)

You may also crash into problems hosting multiple sites on your DSL. 
Oh, you can do it, but seeing as ADSL's upload is somewhat capped, you 
may find once you get enough users accessing your sites at once, things 
get somewhat crippled.

You'd be far better investing your money getting a server colocated at a 
dedicated facility; at least, IMHO.  I'd never, ever host business 
mission-critical stuff on a home DSL connection; especially not 
multiple.  If it was my personal domain, and the only thing I really 
cared about was email, I would; but I'd make sure I'd have somebody 
doing my DNS, and secondary mail.
