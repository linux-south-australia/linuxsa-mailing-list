Re: Diskless Workstations

On Mon, Nov 03, 2003 at 11:15:51AM +1030, Paul Shirren wrote:
> 
> These mini-itx usually run off a brick that supplys 12VDC and have a 12V 
> to ATX converter in the case. I would need a higher voltage and current 
> limiting to trickle charge an SLA.

Chances are the "brick" isn't very well regulated and probably puts
out well over 12V on a light load, the 12V rating signifying "it
doesn't drop below 12V at rated full load"... I'd be surprised if the
"12V to ATX converter" couldn't take 14.4V input.

So, you could provide a "brick" that supplies 14.4V, tightly regulated
so as to be suitable as a float charger for a lead-acid battery, and
simply hang the lead-acid battery across the "12V" line... if you need
to limit the charging current of a fully-discharged battery, stick a
suitable resistor in series with the battery, bypassed in the
"discharge" direction with a Schottky diode.
