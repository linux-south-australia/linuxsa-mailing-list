Re: [OT] Openoffice.org mentioned in The Advertiser!

Damien Uern wrote:
>Lotus 1-2-3, was the first "spreadsheet" application, and appeared 
>on the scene as far back as 1983.

Uh, what about VisiCalc by Dan Bricklin & Bob Frankston? It first 
appeared on 1979 and has a much more serious claim to being the 
world's first 'electronic spreadsheet.'

Lotus 1-2-3 was a VisiCalc wannabe that had one significant advantage 
over VisiCalc: it ran on the XT before VisiCalc did. (VisiCalc 
started life on the Apple II.)

For more info see <http://bricklin.com/visicalc.htm>;.

This may appear to be nit-picking but, when scolding others (ie The 
Advertiser*) for their innaccuracy, it fatally damages your 
credibility when you make basic errors in your scold.
