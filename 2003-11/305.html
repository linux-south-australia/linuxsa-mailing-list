Printing via serial port

Hi,

I have an old inkjet I'm hooking up under linux and have succeeded  in 
installing the drivers for it (Stylewriter II) and getting it to print from 
the command line without trouble.  

eg.  

enscript --output=- /home/andrew/test.txt | gs -q -dBATCH -dPARANOIDSAFER 
-dQUIET -sDEVICE -dPARANOIDSAFER -dQUIET -sDEVICE=pbmraw -r360x360 
-sOutputFile=- lpstyl -f /dev/stylewriter

Problem is I can't print from my GUI (KDE).

I have set up the printer under a second 'title', via the GUI (KDE printer 
wizard) and upon selecting the 'serial port' option, am presented with 
"serial:/dev/ttys0?baud=115200".

My printer only takes data at 57600 baud, however, so I'm guessing that I need 
to change the rate at which CUPS feeds data via the serial port ?????  Very 
much guessing.  Any clues here would be appreciated. 

Under the KDE Printer Wizard, I've tried altering the port 
"serial:/dev/ttys0?baud=115200" to "serial:/dev/ttys0?baud=57600", but the 
Wizard insists that such a change is not OK . . . . not accepted.  I get "The 
local URI doesn't correspond to a detected port".

When I attempt a test print under CUPS, I get:

	Printer State: processing, accepting jobs. 
	"Sending print file, 167904 bytes..." 
	Device URI: serial:/dev/stylewriter 

then:

	925k completed at Sun 16 Nov 2003 10:04:32 PM CST

So CUPS thinks that the document is printed.


I'd be grateful for any tips as to what I should be looking at now.  Perhaps 
changing the rate at which CUPS sends data via the serial port ?
