Re: Multi-boot

On Mon, 2003-11-03 at 12:01, Squareyes wrote:
> Hi all,
> A neighbour of mine would like me to install Win XP on his machine.
> I have allready installed Win 98SE, and Mandrake 9.1 on this machine,
> but am not sure how the boot loader will accept WinXP.
> Win98SE and Mandrake 9.1 are already on separate partitions, and have a 
> spare fat 32 partition
> XP could be installed on. Do I have to put another line in lilo?
> Any help would be appreciated, thanks.
> Winton

Well, as this is a linux list I'll keep short as possible, but AFAIK
windows (all versions) need to be on the first partition on the first
drive. Well, at least without special hacks. Thus you might have more of
a problem with the windows side of things (not surprising heh). I think
your best option would be to install XP on top of 98, and then leave the
selection of those OSes to the windows boot loader. XP should detect 98
and leave it mostly alone, and add a boot option. Then as mentioned in
the other posts, you'll need to revisit your main boot loader (lilo or
grub).
