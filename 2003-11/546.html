Re: Stuffed XFS filesystem

Brendan,

Believing that effect follows cause, I'd suggest you ask yourself if it
wasn't the move to 2.6 kernel that corrupted your XFS.

As to how to fix it, you probably can boot your filesystem with just
a shell by using the lilo option "vmlinux init=/bin/sh".  NB replace
vmlinux with your actual kernel.  You'll get a root prompt, and nothing
else running.  Then you can mount any required filesystems, but do it
read-only, and run mkfs.
