Red Hat plays it safe when it comes to the legal aspects.  The owners of
the MP3 technology, when questioned by Red Hat, did not say that Red Hat
didn't have to pay royalties.  My guess is that SuSE and Mandrake are
taking the calculated risk that they won't be issued an invoice or taken
to court.

Similarly the mplayer codec thing.  mplayer itself is GPL, but it's not
clear that the codecs needed to play a lot content are legally
distributable due to patents, DMCA etc.  RH plays it safe but not
including it in their official distros.  Of course apt-get and
freshrpms.net are your friend here :-)
