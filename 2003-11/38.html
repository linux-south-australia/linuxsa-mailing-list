Mini-box: 200mm(W)x44mm(H)x220mm(D)
Everything Linux: 295mm(W)x63mm(H)x272mm(D)

I have a mini-itx from Everything Linux. I kind of like the roominess of 
the case. It was easy to add a PCI card or get to the hard drive for 
example. But yes, we all want smaller.

Sadly, I looked around the case interior and thought about putting a 
small sealed lead acid battery in there somewhere. Add a powerboard with 
some MOVs in it and a charging circuit and we have an UPS. It wouldn't 
have much run time, but I could add a socket to plug in extra batteries 
externally, and it would be enough to send an alert and shutdown.

These mini-itx usually run off a brick that supplys 12VDC and have a 12V 
to ATX converter in the case. I would need a higher voltage and current 
limiting to trickle charge an SLA. My laptop power supply for example is 
rated at 18.5VDC, and higher amps(!). I could add two regulators - one 
for the PC and one for the battery charging.  The closest thing I could 
find googling was a UPS for a circuit cellar project at 
http://pages.infinit.net/clavette/hcs.html . Before I get a soldering 
iron out, does anyone have a better idea?
