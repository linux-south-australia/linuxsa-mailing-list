Debian Packages (Was: IMAP folders question)

FYI, I currently use on most my servers:

deb http://debian.moolfreet.com ./

Which has things such as php4, mysql, etc.  It's the most stable, rock solid
repositry I've used.  Just to be sure, I usually mysqldump -aAc & GPG encrypt
the mysql databases on most my installed systems, but I haven't seen any
broken packages yet.

In most cases, 'broken packages' just muck the system up in some irritating
way; i.e. apache breaks due to a module not being updated properly, or
something, and it is fixable, but occasionally it'll break in some horrid way
that'll kill your entire system.  I try and avoid any chances of either :-)

There's plenty more at http://www.apt-get.org/, but again, you're trusting
your system in the hands of the maintainers; something which I'm not totally
keen on.

There's also backports.org, but they replace things such as coreutils, libc6,
and I'm not a fan of replacing these with unofficial sources (they break, oh
boy, fun galore).  Apparently they're meant to be good, but I personally don't
know, and again, I'm suspicious.
