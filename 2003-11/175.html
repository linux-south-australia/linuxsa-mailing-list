Re: [news] Open source group wins grant

I take your point Alex. But I think Linux has great broad representation
through Linux Australia, our own fairly informal LinuxSA and AUUG.

I think OSBN-SA would be better served if it didn't try and do too much.
If it became another excuse for beer and pizza (as worthy as that is) it
might lose some kick.

I think I would like to see OSBN-SA lobbying business and government to
take open source seriously and breaking down barriers where they exist.

But I think individuals employed in the use of open source and free 
software should have a voice as well. Perhaps OSBN-SA could have 
memberships for business, employees and associates. Associates being for 
enthusiasts and students. Employees allowing people working under the 
radar in their company to have a voice.
