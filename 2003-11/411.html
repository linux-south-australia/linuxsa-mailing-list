Re: Can I (externally) host my own domain?

David WOLVERTON <david.wolverton@internode.on.net> wrote:
> My question is this:  Since I know that I can use my Linux Distro
> (Redhat 9) to act as (amongst other things) a DNS/Proxy/Web Server -
> I would like to know if I can use a non-static IP address to host the
> domain that I own on one of the four boxes on my home network...

Short answer: You can to some degree.

Longer answer: You can't host your own DNS (because when your IP address
changes nobody will know where to find it.)  There are third parties who
will host your DNS and permit you to change your IP addresses frequently
(ie as freqently as your dynamic address changes.)  Even so, some of the
time your address will change exactly between the time somebody looks
it up and the time they try to connect to your machine.  Obviously they
won't find their way to your machine, which is unfortunate at the least.
Your old address might even be assigned to a new computer at the same
time as you are assigned a new address, and so the person trying to
contact you might find their way to somebody else.  (Imagine your email
going to a random third party.)
