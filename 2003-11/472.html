Remote Graphic Editing

Thank you all those who made suggestions relating to my last post.

I found many helpful and I have already done a batch of files by remote
editing.

I describe the process I used below for you interest and criticism.

The quality of the final images were somewhat lower than I have been
getting by editing them in photoshop but I hope to be able to train
myself to close the quality gap over time.


   48 files in last batch

   12 were quark files which are tiny files anyhow, I download, render
to eps and edit in photoshop per past practice, no benefit in remote
editing these

   36 pdf were pdf which I previewed as follows.  I installed
Ghostscript in my account on the server, (imagemagik was already
installed) and wrote and ran the following script

#!/bin/sh
/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r50
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/thumb01.png
/home/ncah/ncah/??01.pdf
/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r50
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/thumb02.png
/home/ncah/ncah/??02.pdf
/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r50
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/thumb03.png
/home/ncah/ncah/??03.pdf
/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r50
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/thumb04.png
/home/ncah/ncah/??04.pdf
.... etcetera

This produced a set of low res thumbnails so I could decide individually
how to handle them
I previewed them from a browser as per the sample at
http://www.ncah.com/work/999.php

I then could individually size/resolution all images not requiring
cropping by editing the following file, or produce a rendered image for
later cropping by commenting out the second and third lines

#!/bin/sh
/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r200
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/01x.png /home/ncah/ncah/??01.pdf
#convert -size 600x2400 /home/ncah/public_html/work/01x.png -resize
600x2400 +profile "*" /home/ncah/public_html/work/01.png
#rm /home/ncah/public_html/work/01x.png

/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r200
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/02x.png /home/ncah/ncah/??02.pdf
#convert -size 600x2400 /home/ncah/public_html/work/02x.png -resize
600x2400 +profile "*" /home/ncah/public_html/work/02.png
#rm /home/ncah/public_html/work/02x.png

/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r200
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/03x.png /home/ncah/ncah/??03.pdf
#convert -size 600x2400 /home/ncah/public_html/work/03x.png -resize
600x2400 +profile "*" /home/ncah/public_html/work/03.png
#rm /home/ncah/public_html/work/03x.png

/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r200
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/04x.png /home/ncah/ncah/??04.pdf
convert -size 700x2400 /home/ncah/public_html/work/04x.png -resize
700x2400 +profile "*" /home/ncah/public_html/work/04.png
rm /home/ncah/public_html/work/04x.png

/home/ncah/gs/bin/gs -dSAFER -dBATCH -dNOPAUSE -sDEVICE=png256
-dGraphicsAlphaBits=4 -r200
-sOutputFile=/home/ncah/public_html/work/05x.png /home/ncah/ncah/??05.pdf
convert -size 400x2000 /home/ncah/public_html/work/05x.png -resize
400x2000 +profile "*" /home/ncah/public_html/work/05.png
rm /home/ncah/public_html/work/05x.png
.... etcetera

This left 11 images requiring cropping

So... from the comments received I think I still have to ftp this 11
files to a server where I can run X-Win32 (which I have used) or
tightVNC/VCN (which I have not tried) and Gimp.
I could place the crop marks using the screen view from Gimp, re-save
and re-run imagemagik from a script to size etc the foles

I have not yet tried doing so, but installing Gimp on my web server may
be a bit much for my host and diskspace. Ghostscript was huge in web
server shell account terms!

Again, thanks for comments
