Hey,

It might be a power supply issue. The way I figure it, the hard drive itself 
attaches the ECC bits as it writes the data, and so surely that could not be 
interfered with by the motherboard. Remember, that in the S.M.A.R.T tests, 
the drive itself is checking the data, and the same errors occurred on a 
different machine. This points to the fact that it's possible that the data 
is written out corrupted in some cases and so the ECC bits will show an 
error. This may be due to the drive being unable to draw enough current, or 
perhaps some spikes or dips in the power supply could be causing "weak" 
writes, corrupting data.

Either that, or the drive is dead. Of course this is all speculation, I don't 
know enough about how the hard drive works to be certain.

I'm going to swap out some components, reformat the hard drive, and see how it 
goes.

Cheers,

Damien
