Re: Policy Makers' Positions

On Tue, Dec 23, 2003 at 04:53:36PM +1100, Christopher Yeoh wrote:

 > > That's not the case.  ACCC v Sony was lost on appeal.  So
 > > circumventing region coding falls under the criminal clauses
 > > of the Copyright Act.
 > 
 > So region free dvd players are illegal then?

No.  Sigh.

The outcome of the original case was that it was considered an
illegal restraint of trade for someone to block region-free players.
So contractural terms which forced manufacturers to only make 
"regioned" players, or which prohibited importers from bringing in
region-free players, was illegal.

Now it isn't.

If you can find a region-free player then it's perfectly legal to 
buy it, use it, sell it, do whatever you want with it.  But it's
no longer illegal for (say) Sony to sue (say) Grundig for manufacturing
and importing region-free players.
