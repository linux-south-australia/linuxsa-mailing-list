And I'm dissapointed that the measure of worth for a community,
development system, Operating System, etc etc has to be the subjective
level of innovation associated with it. Worth has more to do with
ultimate quality and reliability IMO. It's no good having loads of
innovation if the implementation is off.

For me, it's innovative enough that the OS loads and doesn't crash.
Everything else is a bonus!

For the record, Silicon Beach's 'Digital Darkroom' predated Adobe
Photoshop by about 2 years, even though both landed up in the hands of
Adobe through the Aldus merger of 94. 

Having an app or os fail because a better alternative came along is a
'risk' of competition, and is core to having better ones. That
competition even happens in OSS, look at KDE vs Gnome. Where would they
both be by now without the competitive tension of the last few years?
