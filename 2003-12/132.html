The portable telephone will almost certainly not be Austel approval but it
will probably work, although you will have to change over 2 wires 3&4 ( I
think) at the socket. The Uk system is a 4 wire system, whilst the Oz is a 2
wire system. However, the portable systems are often so smart that they know
how to deal with both (or so I have heard).

"David Drury" <idavid@atdotdotnet.net> wrote on 12/02/2003 11:41:07 AM:

> I am shortly to return to Oz from the Uk. I have obtained over here, and
> Alcatel Speed Touch USB ADSL modem, and a portable Telephone (not a
mobile),
> and I'm wondering whether these devices are likely to work on the
standard
> phone systems in Oz, or whether I'm up for new hardware on my return ?

Providing the modem is marked with "Austel Approval" then it can be used,
if not then its not wise to attach it to the PSTN here.   Unless marked
with "Austel Approval" you cannot assume its the same as the Alcatel modems
sold within Australia, many people get caught by this.   The fines if
damage is caused to the PSTN are quite simply hideous.

The portable telephone if RF based will need to have either ACA approval or
meet the relevant LIPD class licence.  It too will require "Austel
Approval" markings.
