Re: SCO has to show infringing code?

On Sat, 2003-12-13 at 11:26, Ian Loxton wrote:

> I just spotted this article and am left wondering just how SCO will 
> comply without blowing their present bluff?

Recall that there's a Protective Order in place.  So
anything SCO hands to IBM won't be public until April
2005 (the current trial date).

The Protective Order can usually be contested by a
third party (such as a media organisation) on a
per-document basis, exactly how is up to the particular
court.

I wouldn't hold you breath on this, as the trade press
coverage of SCO v IBM has been pretty woeful.  They
only look half decent because the industry analysts
have been worse.

The only reason we are seeing anything now is that IBM
and SCO differed over the adequacy of SCO's discovery
responses and that difference was deep enough for
the magistrate in the case to be called in by IBM
to make a ruling.

Groklaw <http://www.groklaw.net/>; is the best resource
if you need details.
