Also the 'killing innovation' is a BS argument, as the best ideas are 
often created by people that are playing with a thought and then simply 
start coding to see if it can be done. These projects most commonly end 
up in the OSS domain, after which a big company scoops up the idea, 
expands on it (or kills it with features that are not needed, as M$'s 
usually does) and then markets it as their great innovative product.
If you're lucky, they reference you, or at least write their own code to 
built a similar thing... Some companies will just simply take that OSS 
code and adapt it for their own use and then sell it...

Also he seems to think (at least that is what I read from above article) 
that OSS is not copyrighted, or at least the copyright cannot be 
enforced. I think the OSF has been proving that assumption incorrect for 
a while now...
