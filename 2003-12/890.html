On Mon, Dec 22, 2003 at 01:54:40PM +1030, Ryan Verner wrote:

 > If you've got one of those floppy drive cables with only one drive
 > connector on the end, it's possible to put it backwards (so the few pins
 > that are reversed for fd0 are on the wrong side).  End effect is that
 > the floppy light isn't on, but the drive appears completely dead.  I've
 > done it quite a few times :-)

If you've plugged the cable in wrong-way-up and the activity light is
turned on, you might find that the write head is permanently energized
as well, and that any floppy disk you insert into the drive while 
it's in that state is instantly corrupted -- Regardless of whether it's
write-protected.
