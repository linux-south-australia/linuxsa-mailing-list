That's not what you said.  You said, and I'm reading from the top line of
this message, "the ability to build on existing work _is_ the innovation."
People have been doing that since they invented the wheel.  Universities
work on that principle.  Even the patent system incorporates that principle,
although when you build upon a patented work you need to pay a licence fee
set by the patent owner.  There's absolutely nothing innovative about being
able to build on existing work.

restrictions.  There are other precendents, too.
