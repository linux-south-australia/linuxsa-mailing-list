Re: IP addresses (was RE: internode adsl pricing OT)

> (Ignoring the stuff about internode dishing out 4 IPs for a business adsl
> service because I don't understand why they do it).

Because it's a bridged service - so rather than running the ugly PPPoX stuff 
it appears as though you're just connected to an ethernet point - thus you 
need a /30 subnet:

.n	= network
.n+1	= your machine
.n+2	= internet router (actually telstra)
.n+3	= broadcast
