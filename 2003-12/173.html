Re: 486 8MB + Linux

At 21:46 04/12/2003, you wrote:

>I used tinylinux on a 486 machine with 8MB of RAM before. I could load up
>fvwm, but it was dreadfully slow. I got it to work as a thin client to my
>1.4GHz athlon, so I could use KDE remotely on it :) However when it cached
>the fonts, it started to get _really_ slow as it ran out of memory.

Seriously give Whitedwarf a go (http://www.whitedwarflinux.org)... I've had 
it running on a 486dx33 credit card sized PC with only 4Mb of RAM.  Being 
based on Slackware 8.1 you get a much better selection of prebuilt binary 
packages.   It's default foot print is less than 25Mb.  Its a bit strange 
in certain areas but with a bit of work its easy to make it 'feel' like 
normal slackware again.

There is also pebble linux which is based on Debian, but I've not used this 
with machines with less than 8Mb of RAM.
