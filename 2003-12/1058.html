Re: Modems and "Soft" Modems under Linux

For hardware modems, all you need is a Hayes-compatible modem (supports
AT commands & nearly all do now), that connects to your serial port. No
external drivers are required.

We use a 3Com US Robotics 56k hardware modem that is very common &
readily available in Adelaide (around $150-200 at last check).

If your deadset on using the cheaper PCI software modems, anything with
a Lucent chipset is well supported on Linux using the lt-modem Lucent
drivers (works in kernel-2.6 also).

Netcomm is a highly regarded Australian company that use Lucent chipsets
in all their PCI software modems. These are extremely easy to find,
nearly all Adelaide PC shops are flooded with Netcomm gear. I even
recall seeing one in Big W.
