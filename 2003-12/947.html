I think Linus has handled many of those cases. He created them from 
scratch, and as Mark says, ideas and methods cannot have copyright placed 
over them, only the combination of words in a document can.

Moreover, you cannot copyright a list of numbers or words, and most of the 
content of include files are just that. Lists of numbers.

However, some of the macros etc are a little more, but Linus has dealt 
with that as I say.

In my mind an ABI is a specification that states what system calls will be 
used, what stack layouts will be used, what values and structures etc will 
be used and their precise layout etc, just as Mark said earlier.

The spec can have a copyright placed over them, as can the files that 
implement the various specs.

However, I think Linus is on good ground when he claims that the files in 
Linux are not derived from UNIX files, which is the claim being made.

> Do SCO have the "copyright" and IP licence rights to Solaris?

Solaris most likely has multiple copyright notices on it, including the 
original AT&T/USL copyrights. However, Sun paid large sums of money a long 
time ago and recently for the rights to do what they want with that code, 
I believe.
