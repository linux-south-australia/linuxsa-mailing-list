RE: internode adsl pricing OT

Please explain??

As part of a business service you are assigned a real world IP address.
BADSL uses direct IP connectivity not PPPoE or PPPoA. Casual dial-up people
only use an IP when connected, that IP is the reallocated to another dial-up
user when the first disconnects, which is what causes the old problem of
occasionally when you connect you still get connections destined for the
previous user. Static services occupy that IP all the time, even when the
service is not connected... Hence the point of a static IP.

Besides, the main thrust (oooo thrust) of my previous post was that it is
the service and reliability that costs the additional money, and static IP's
don't help although they are in reality a drop in the ocean as far as DSL
costs go IMHO.
