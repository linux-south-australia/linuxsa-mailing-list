Re: Passwords

On Tue, Dec 16, 2003 at 09:58:37AM +1030, David Lloyd wrote:
> 
> Brian,
> 
> > The below is cut and pasted from an Linux Today article on security.
> > The bit I am interested in is the comment about passwords. How can we
> > make our passwords more secure ? or how do we avoid passwords being
> > easily cracked/sniffed ?
> 
> One needs to setup company policies for passwords. For example, a policy
> may be:
> 
>  * that the password not be based on a dictionary word
>  * that the password not be similar to the previous password
>  * that the password be more than 6 characters long
>  * that the password must contain at least one letter, number and non
>    alphanumberic character

And password expiry.  Then there are more extreme measures like one-time
passwords, challenge/response, time-based protocols that need to be in
sync...

> There are pam modules about which will enforce the above.
> 
> Lookup the pam documentation for the "cracklib" stuff.

It can be quite instructive to set up a few test accounts with various
levels of weak to strong passwords and run crack on them.  (On an
isolated home machine at least :-)
