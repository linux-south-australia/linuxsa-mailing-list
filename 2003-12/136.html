"Michael Haarsma" <mhaarsma@adel.tafe.sa.edu.au> wrote:
> Please explain??

I'm not talking about internode or adsl specifically just
responding to your comment "IP addresses are scarce".

My points were that although we are running out of
IPv4 addresses, we've got another 20 years or so, so
no immediate hurry (search for proper papers/research
on the subject).  And secondly that if a machine is
connected to the internet all the time then it is
"taking up" an IP address, regardless of whether it's
static or dynamically allocated.

Therefore so long as you are connecting permanently,
you are not consuming more IP resources by having a
static IP than by having a dynamic one.  (Ignoring
the stuff about internode dishing out 4 IPs for a
business adsl service because I don't understand
why they do it).
