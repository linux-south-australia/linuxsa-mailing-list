Re: Linux on Mac: Is there a point?

On Thu, 2003-12-11 at 10:12, Kim Hawtin wrote:
> I was wondering about the point myself. What is the options for adding
> loadable modules for things like IPIP, GRE tunneling, PPTP and other 
> funky network options into the MacOSX kernel?

GRE/PPTP is already there :-)

The MacOSX kernel is a strange beast.  Wade through the source code
(yes, you can even roll your own kernels from Darwin's CVS, which work
perfectly, although there isn't really a point), and you'll find bits of
FreeBSD, bits of NetBSD, bits of Mach, bits of NeXt.

You'll be surprised how many goodies are already under the hood,
actually.  One of the reasons why I use OS X.
