Hmm.  See, this is what I'm trying to avoid with Debian; being almost forced
to use non-official repositries.  My issue with this is that there's a higher
chance of a package being broken, which does occasionally happen, and it can
turn into a complete nightmare; especially when you have to deal with a
bucketload of servers, and another admin goes and upgrades things unknowingly.

backports.org, unfortunately, I found to be shockingly unstable and to have
lots of broken packages; I managed to half-kill a installation the other day
by attempting a dist-upgrade with it (They haven't really 'backported' as
they're using later versions of core libraries such as libc6; defeats the
entire purpose of having backports, IMHO).

The repositries you're using; are they more for desktop stuff, or are you
using them as you need a later MTA, a later version of php/apache, etc?  The
latter tends to be an issue with Debian stable.
