On Thu, 2003-12-04 at 01:11, officem wrote:
> Hi all,
> well I would want to know if you have some experience with Linux on notebook 486/25 8mb ram, 120mb hdd and NO cd-rom. I have red about several distributions, but I want to know your opinion.
> The use>>> apache, mysql, php, vi, latex, fluxbox.
> 

Depending on how keen you are, you could probably build your own. I've
got a Linux distribution here that runs a router from a floppy that was
home-grown (it supports packet filtering, nat, ipsec and QoS). We use
uClibc (http://uclibc.org/) a fair bit for small appliances and we've
found that 8MB with a 2.4 kernel and an X server isn't out of the
question.

You could also do a search and see if you can find some of the
documentation from the Linux Router Project. LRP used to take files from
your installed distribution and copy them to a machine with little in
the way of hardware.

There's also a 4MB laptop HOWTO (or similar) at places like
http://tldp.org/

Alternately, if you're not doing anything particularly Linux-specific,
the installers for OpenBSD, NetBSD and probably FreeBSD require very
little in the way of RAM...

The opportunities are almost endless :-)
