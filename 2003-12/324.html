Re: What is innovation? (was: Re: We got mentioned in parliament)

Richard Sharpe <rsharpe@richardsharpe.com> wrote:
> By the same token, a lot of what propietary software organizations produce 
> is also not innovative. Much of the work is preceeded by University-based 
> research that the propietary orgs are simply leveraging off of.

I agree with that.  It's easy to understand why open source software
tends to be an immitation of proprietary software: cheaper, more robust,
can add new features, and so on.  Also, the research for true innovation
is usually quite time consuming, and if you're not being paid for it it
can be hard to justify the expense in time.

Do you think that fear of open source immitating results might discourage
businesses from doing research?  Or encourage them to seek strong
intellectual property protection on what they do?
