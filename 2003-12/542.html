Passwords

The below is cut and pasted from an Linux Today article on security.
The bit I am interested in is the comment about passwords. How can we make
our passwords more secure ? or how do we avoid passwords being easily
cracked/sniffed ?
Brian


What most CTOs need to understand, said Stacey Quandt, principal analyst at
the OSDL, is that most attacks happen when end-users don't protect their
passwords, not from an inherent flaw in the kernel that lets attackers get
in. 

"At the level of the systems administrators, they need to be more careful
with their passwords," Quandt said. "Security's easier to do in Linux than
what you have in a Microsoft operating system, with some of the remote
vulnerabilities that are possible in a Windows system, or at least the
remote attacks that are successful."
